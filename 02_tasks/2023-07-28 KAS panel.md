	- the concept of universal employment is relatively new
		- by saying "employment," I mean someone paying you in exchange for your labor
		- pre-industrial times - farmers -- wasn't "employment" in today's sense as you and your family or village are able to attend to your shared survival needs, or put it more simply, feed yourselves most of the time by yourselves 
		- pre-agricultural times - hunter-gatherers
		- industrialization: deprive one from the material and social conditions to sustain one's life through their direct participation in the ecosystem's metabolism
			- not saying that the quality of life during pre-industrial time is better than today
			- but this will provide some crucial hints to why, hundred years ago, people in the south were able to live a decent life even without much money, yet now so many of them even struggle to make their most basic ends meet
		- putting employment back into its socio-historical context, can help us to comprehend our this question more holistically
		- What is the purpose of jobs? Our what are the social conditions that demand us to be employed?
			- to make a living while contributing to the functioning of the society
			- or more ideally, to make a flourishing life possible by contributing to the flourishing of all
			- in other words, "employment" a way to both sustain our lives and other interdependence with others, ideally in a healthy way
			- so the fundamental aspect of "future proofing" your job or career is to achieve this
			- the jobs and careers as we know today are only some manifestations of this given the existing conditions
		- So the ways to achieve such healthy interdepedence or mutual flourishing can take many forms, and getting paid by fiat money is only one of the many forms
			- e.g., in ancient China, 
				- often the salary of government officials are grains, instead of money;  
				- whereas farmer or peasant feed themselves by their own harvests, but tribute their harvest to the government for infrastrcture (e.g., canals), defense, and redistribution
				- exchange of goods and services occur more easily when there is surplus
				- such form of interdependence continued for at least 3000 years
				- not saying this is better than today, but it is one of the many viable forms
			- today: 
				- the problem is, while such interdependence may take many different kinds of forms, or we can say that there are many kinds of capitals, not only economic capital,
				- most of us today reduce all kinds of interdependent social relations to transactional relations, and reduce all capitals to economic capitals
				- but from engineering or network theory, we know that if too many things are too dependent upon one kind of thing, the structure becomes very fragile
					- this is how unemployment becomes a huge problem, because today most of us has lost the ability to live without money, or maintain healthy social relations without transaction
			- future: 
				- for us to imagine, design, and actualize
				- not just "jobs" but "social relations"
	- segway to "learning by caring"
