學生：
- don’t compete for a school that is rigorous in an unsuitable way; compatibility is in many ways*, more important than selectivity/ranking
	- * Symbolic capital: depends on relevance/irreplacibility regarding your goal, and you need to survive till that point, which means you’ll be making trade offs 
	- * cultural capital: also depends on compatibility with culture— if the culture you’re in is suffocating, might want to think twice even if you’re able to make these connections, whether that's a culture that you can live healthly and happily for a very long time
- Opt in for alternative schools-> this will support them and also, with multitudes, bring pressure to change the overall landscape (Taiwan as example)
- if possible, build reciprocal relations with faculty at less selective/lower rank institutions, to create win-win situations (a form of [[德中學 Learning by Caring|learning by caring]]) --
