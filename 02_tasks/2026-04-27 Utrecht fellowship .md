I think instead of generically talking about the problems of academia, I want to highlight that the current schooling system up to academia structurally resembles the linear, extractive, exhaustive, and polluting structure of the material economy.

If you take a look at the references I previously mentioned, you can see that the exploitation and extraction of resources, the consumption of resources, and the waste and pollution of unused resources is structurally overlapping or highly similar between the academic training pipeline and the material resource pipeline (or process). It doesn't only happen with humans' utilization or exploitation of material and biological resources, but also to the process of exploiting human resources. 

this is the transfactual characteristic of allocation dependence. 

The current decline or crisis of academia in the sense that highly educated PhDs are becoming structurally vulnerable and marginalized in the economic system resembles the increasing waste produced in the process of creating highly sophisticated final products in a shrinking production line: only a fraction of the raw material can be utilized, while everything else turns into waste. 

At the same time, the global brain drain resembles the hierarchy of the global supply chain: the finest goods are extracted to the core of the world system, while those of less quality are distributed or remain in the peripheries.

You can find more resemblance of these kinds from the reference folder and the previous proposals.

However, some of the needs of academia remain. For example, there is the need for credible knowledge production.

The question then is:
1. How do we identify the key features we still need from academia?
2. How should they be redesigned to prevent the reproduction of existing extractive structure that is antagonistic to a circular economy?

Note that we shouldn't be defending academia or the rights of academics and students as they have been in the old model, because the old model itself resembles the unsustainable, extractive, exhaustive, linear model.
