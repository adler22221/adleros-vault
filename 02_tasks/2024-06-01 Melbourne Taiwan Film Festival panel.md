1. Can you briefly share your experience studying in Humanity (RenWen) Junior High School and what your classmates’ and teachers’ responses were to your filmmaking? 
2. What challenges did you face when filming the documentary? [Prompt: Parental, Teachers, Classmates support] 
3. Why did you choose to film your classmates’ study experiences? Can you give an update on what your classmates are doing now? 
4. Could you give us some insights into why parents choose to send their children to an alternative school like Renwen? 
5. You began filming in 2009, how do you think Taiwan’s education system has changed since then?
6. East Asian schools are known to be super competitive. There are several fiction films and television dramas that have explored the pressures of the entrance exam system in Japan, South Korea, and Singapore, such as “The Family Game,” “Asassination Classroom,” “Sky Castle,” and “I Not Stupid.” Why did you choose to use the documentary format rather than produce a scripted feature film to explore these educational issues?
7. Our research suggests that a key feature of the cultural concept, _guai_, is for children to achieve academically. Scholars like Andrew Kipnis have also argued that the emphasis on academics is driven by an educational ethos and that this ethic continues to be lived in Chinese communities around the world. Based on your experiences making this film and being a student in Taiwan, did you find this to be true? Were there any other factors that you found were shaping student experiences and parental expectations at Renwen?
8. We came across your idea of “Allocation Dependence.” Can you please explain what this term means and how your film illustrates the issues associated with Allocation Dependence? 
9. Renwen is rather unconventional in the Taiwanese educational landscape. Is there a difference between how success is conceived in a school like Renwen and wider Taiwanese society? How does your film try to challenge conventional understandings of success?  
10. Can you share with us what projects you are currently working on?
