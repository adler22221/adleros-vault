So, the direct effect together with the indirect effect on GDP leads to an increase of GDP of 500 billion yen. 100 initial increase divided by 0.2 is 500. How is this possible? Because extra schools or roads will generate more jobs. So households have more income and will buy more goods. And this, in turn, will allow producers to produce and sell more goods. With more workers, and so on. It's this knock-on effect that turns an initial injection of 100 into a final effect of 500 billion yen. 

So in conclusion, the multiplier effect is a blessing for policies trying to get an economy out of a recession.
