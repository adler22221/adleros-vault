Two provocations (~10 min each), grounded reflections from each speaker's work and context. A case, a tension, a design question they're sitting with. The two provocations approach trust from different scales and lenses, creating a productive tension that opens the group discussion.

Provocation 1 — Jed
Jed will look at trust in shared infrastructure: how decentralised communities coordinate through standards when no actor has the mandate to impose rules. Jed leads Radiant Earth and has helped convene open communities that develop data standards used across many organisations. The provocation will treat standards as institutions that shape collaboration through protocols, norms, and adoption pathways.

Guiding question: When no one has formal authority, what design moves help a standard, or any new institution, earn trust and spread across contexts?

Where does trust sit in the institutions you work with? Is trust closer to lived experience and local signals, or to protocols and shared infrastructure? What does that suggest about which design features matter most?
