# sonnet

This is exactly the right tension to confront directly. Japan, the Dojo vision, the wellbeing learning, and your financial goals are potentially complementary—but only if you design the Japan year around a specific income architecture from day one. Without that, the savings burn, and you come back with a rich soul and an empty wallet, which immediately destroys the stable home life you're trying to build for your partner.

Here's how the pieces can actually fit together.

## The core tension

The Japan working holiday is worth ~12 months. Your savings are roughly NT$500,000 (~US$15,000). Average monthly costs in Japan for a solo person run roughly US$1,500–2,250. If you spend nothing strategically and just "farm and write," you run out in 7–10 months with nothing to show financially—and return to Taiwan with *less* stability than you left with.[1]

So the Japan year only makes financial sense if you design **at least one income stream that runs during it**, and ideally **two**.

***

## What actually generates income on a working holiday visa in Japan

The working holiday visa in Japan permits part-time work without the constraints of a specialist work visa, so your options are wider than many realize.[2][3][4]

The three most viable income streams for your profile specifically are:

### 1. Remote consulting / facilitation (your highest-leverage option)

You already have active relationships with TIAL, Ecoversities, IACR, JASA, and international education networks. None of these require you to be in Taiwan to serve them. Specifically:[5][6]

- **Systems thinking facilitation and workshop delivery** (via your Cornell certification) can be done as remote contracts at competitive international rates.[7][5]
- **TIAL fellow contributions**: even if unpaid, maintaining this actively while in Japan signals continuity and opens doors to paid associate work through Geoff's network.
- **Short consulting stints** for education innovation organizations you already serve (Only Experimental Education, TabiFuku, KAS-connected orgs) can be invoiced internationally.[5]

The realistic floor here is probably 2–3 small remote engagements per quarter, each worth US$500–1,500, adding up to roughly US$500–1,000/month part-time without disrupting writing or farm learning time.

### 2. WWOOF Japan: eliminate the cost of learning farming

WWOOF Japan (membership ~¥5,500/year) connects you with organic host farms that provide **free room and board in exchange for ~6 hours of work/day**. This means:[8][9][10][11]

- You learn the agricultural modules you want (糧食自足) with zero tuition cost and zero housing cost.
- You preserve savings for phases where you're not WWOOFing.
- You meet farmers with alternative philosophies—exactly the Ecoversities-adjacent network you want to build.[11][12]

This is not a financial strategy on its own—it generates no income—but it **massively reduces your burn rate** during the months you're doing it, potentially cutting monthly costs from US$1,500+ to near US$200-300 (personal discretionary spending only).

### 3. One small international Dojo cohort (the pivotal move)

Here's where the crystallization you just had becomes financially meaningful. With the Dojo's value proposition now clear—**whole-person support for changemakers**—you have something you can actually sell to a global niche immediately, without a finished product.

A beta cohort of 10–15 participants at US$300–500 each = US$3,000–7,500 per run. Run one or two during the Japan year—online, distributed across time zones—and you've covered 2–4 months of living costs while simultaneously:

- Building your proof of concept,
- Creating material for the book,
- Cementing your positioning as the person who addresses what TIAL, Ecoversities, and adjacent ecosystems' people *actually need* but can't get from those platforms alone.

Your Cornell systems thinking credential, your Modes of Dependencies framework, and your TIAL/Ecoversities network all serve as pre-existing credibility with exactly this audience.[13][5]

## How the financial goals sequence from here

Given this architecture, here's how your six financial conditions from the midnight notes map onto the Japan year and what follows:

| Financial goal | Japan year role | When it becomes real |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Research/writing without financial anxiety | WWOOF periods cut costs; remote consulting covers gap | Achievable during Japan year if designed |
| 2. One-bedroom for two, two-person office | Japan year is preparatory; return to Taiwan or move to affordable Japan city after | 2–3 years post-Japan, once PhD or book anchors more stable income |

| 6. Support 1–2 children | Requires stable family base (housing, health, income) | Post-PhD / post-book; 5–10 year horizon |

Goals 1–4 are partially achievable within and immediately after the Japan year if designed well. Goals 5–6 are downstream of the book and PhD—nothing you do in Japan will shortcut those, but nothing you do in Japan prevents them either, *as long as you don't burn your savings by having no income*.

***

## The one design decision that changes everything

The Japan year's financial viability hinges on one decision you need to make **before you go**:

**Commit to running at least one beta Dojo cohort during the Japan year.**

Not "think about it." Not "once I'm settled." Commit now, design it in the next 4–6 weeks, and open applications before you leave Taiwan.

Why now?
- Your positioning is clear enough today to write a compelling one-page description.
- Your network (TIAL, Ecoversities, critical realist circle, alternative education networks) is warm and global.
- Running it while in Japan adds the narrative of "I'm building this from the ground up, walking the talk"—which is *more* compelling to your target audience of changemakers, not less.
- It generates real income during the year instead of being a "later" plan that never happens.

This single move transforms the Japan year from a "soul-building sabbatical funded by savings" into a "field research + embodied learning + first Dojo proof-of-concept year"—which is both more financially sustainable and more strategically powerful for everything that comes after.[14][13][5]

# Adler's Integrated 10-Year Roadmap

The three threads — where to live, what to publish when, and how your partner's trajectory unfolds alongside yours — are not parallel but *load-bearing* on each other: the locale choice directly shapes the healthcare security and income floor your partner needs to pursue her own path; the publication timeline determines when you generate enough symbolic capital to support a better visa or fellowship application; and your partner's growing autonomy progressively frees up your attention for deeper work.[1][2]



***

## The Binding Constraint: Partner's Care Role

Before designing anything, the most consequential fixed constraint is that your partner carries the primary caregiving responsibility for two post-stroke parents in Taiwan — and this is not merely logistical but psychologically and physically taxing.  Any locale plan that asks her to be entirely alone in that role while you live abroad, or that leaves her in financial precarity, will erode both her wellbeing and your relationship regardless of how intellectually attractive your destination is.  The plan must therefore treat her care burden, her own vocational development, and her dependency needs as *co-equal* design parameters — not afterthoughts.[1][2]

Taiwan's NHI Long-Term Care 2.0 (LTC 2.0) provides home-care and respite services that can reduce out-of-pocket expenses for stroke rehabilitation and personal care, but the system is underfunded and wait times are significant.  Aggressively applying for and maximizing LTC 2.0 benefits — including caregiver respite subsidies — is the single highest-leverage financial action available in 2026 that does not require Adler to be physically present.  This makes 2026 "LTC infrastructure year," not a year of major geographic moves.[1]

***

## Phase 1 (2026–2028): Anchor, Write, and Build the Care Infrastructure

**Locale:** Taiwan remains the primary base — not as a fallback but as a deliberate strategic choice. Taiwan's NHI provides Adler's own cardiological care for NTD 300–800/visit, which for someone managing a cardiac condition is existentially important.  Monthly living costs of approximately USD 1,200–1,800 mean that income variability during the platform-building phase carries minimal financial stress.  The "plateau" Adler feels is a *social capital plateau within the Taiwanese ecosystem*, not a ceiling on global income — a Substack newsletter or book written in Jiaoxi earns the same subscription and advance income as one written in Amsterdam.[1]

The key locale action in this phase is registering a **Wyoming single-member LLC** (approximately USD 100/year) to invoice all US-sourced income — speaking fees, consulting, Substack, and eventual book advances — cleanly and professionally from Taiwan.  This gives Adler a US corporate identity on book proposals and grant applications without bearing US healthcare or living costs. In parallel, the groundwork for a **Dutch Stichting with ANBI designation** should begin: identify three trusted international board members (one each in Japan, UK, and the US), and commission a Dutch notary to draft the foundation deed (approximately EUR 500–1,000).  The ANBI application takes 2–6 months and can proceed remotely.[1]

**Publication:** This phase has one non-negotiable priority: **finish the book manuscript.** Given that "Beneath Modes of Exchange" and adjacent working papers already exist, the manuscript is closer to 60–70% complete than it is to zero.  The documentary's 10th anniversary campaign should launch in this window as a *time-bounded amplifier* — a curated series of 6–8 screenings and dialogues, each one channeling attendees into a mailing list, a Substack newsletter, or a waitlist for the first Dojo cohort.  This is not a separate project; it is the manuscript's first distribution channel and the Dojo's first recruitment funnel. The Substack should begin publishing working sections of the book and dependency-framework reflections at a rhythm of 1–2 substantial pieces per month.[3][1]

**Partner's pathway:** The most important thing Adler can do for his partner in this phase is **reduce her solo caregiving load structurally.** This means: maximizing LTC 2.0 home-care hours; hiring part-time caregiving support using income from the Wyoming LLC; and — crucially — actively *holding space* for her to articulate what she wants to explore vocationally, without pressure to decide immediately.  If she has latent interests in counseling, art therapy, somatic practice, or social work, this is the phase to begin 1–2 days per week of exploratory activity, even informally. The goal is not a credential yet; it's keeping her self-conception alive alongside the care role.[1]

***

## Phase 2 (2028–2031): Global Platform + Amsterdam Pivot

**Locale:** Once the book manuscript is complete and Adler has a stable Substack audience and income rhythm, the **DAFT (Dutch-American Friendship Treaty) sole proprietorship in Amsterdam** becomes the optimal next step.  The DAFT requires only a ~EUR 4,500 deposit (no points scoring, no business plan approval) and grants full Dutch residency and self-employment rights within 8–12 weeks.  Amsterdam is the single most concentrated hub for the post-growth, degrowth, and social innovation discourse that is the natural intellectual ecosystem for Adler's work — home to Ashoka Europe, Impact Hub Amsterdam, the Presencing Institute Europe (Otto Scharmer's Theory U network), and the DOEN Foundation.  Dutch health insurance via the mandatory Zorgverzekeringswet system costs approximately EUR 150–200/month and provides full cardiological coverage.[1]

This locale move also activates the **Stichting Awakening Coop Lab** as a live entity: once Adler has Dutch residency, the Stichting can apply for ANBI status, opening access to Horizon Europe grants, Dutch Postcode Lottery grants, and EU philanthropic networks that are structurally inaccessible from Taiwan.  The Wyoming LLC continues to receive English-language commercial income; the Stichting receives grants, donations, and EU-sourced project funds — and can contract the LLC for Adler's implementation work, creating a clean channel for grant income to flow toward personal earnings.[1]

**Publication:** The book should be **published** in this window — with an English-language primary edition (targeting a publisher like Polity, Routledge's social theory list, or a US university press) and a Chinese co-edition negotiated simultaneously, leveraging Adler's existing Taiwan publisher relationships.  Publication anchors the **Unschooled PhD / PhD by Published Works** application: Birkbeck (University of London), Goldsmiths, or the University of Edinburgh are realistic targets, given their track records of accepting non-traditional candidates with strong publication portfolios.  The PhD application should be submitted in this phase, targeting completion by the early-to-mid 2030s.[1]

**Partner's pathway:** Phase 2 is the natural window for the partner to shift from survival mode to *development mode*, as LTC infrastructure has matured and income has stabilized.  If she has been exploring counseling or somatic practice, this is when she pursues a formal certification or supervised practice hours. If her interests have crystallized around something aligned with the Dojo (facilitation, lived-experience research, peer support), she can begin co-facilitating Dojo cohorts — which gives her a meaningful professional identity that is *her own work*, not merely "Adler's partner helping Adler's project." The key design principle: **she should have a distinct role and credit**, not a supporting one.[1]

The question of whether she relocates with Adler to Amsterdam is a genuine decision point. If the parents' care is well-covered by LTC 2.0 and hired support, and if she has a vocational direction that can be pursued in Europe (many counseling and art therapy programs accept students from anywhere), then a partial relocation — spending 4–6 months per year in Amsterdam and the rest in Taiwan — may be the most sustainable pattern.  This avoids forcing either full rootlessness or full sacrifice.[1]

***

## Phase 3 (2031–2036): Institutionalization + Prefigurative Home Base

**Locale:** By this phase, Adler's intellectual platform should be self-reinforcing. The UK's **Global Talent Visa (Exceptional Talent/Promise)** in research and innovation is a realistic pathway given his award history, TEDxTaipei recognition, Presidential Education Award, and international board positions.  A base in **Bristol or Edinburgh** (not London) reduces living costs by 35–45% compared to London while maintaining full access to UK intellectual life, NHS cardiological coverage supplemented by private cardiac insurance (~GBP 120–150/month), and proximity to the publisher and PhD supervisor ecosystems.[1]

Earth-regrounding becomes more structurally possible here: a semi-rural base with a garden or small plot, integrating the agroecological practice that Adler's own dependency framework calls for, becomes both personally coherent and intellectually generative — a living laboratory for the Dojo's "cosmic-terrestrial" mode of dependence.  This does not require buying land; it could mean a long-term rural rental in Pembrokeshire (Wales), the Scottish Borders, or — if the Amsterdam base is retained — the Dutch countryside near Leiden or Groningen.[3][1]

**Publication + Dojo:** The second book, a Dojo methodology volume (toolkits and playbooks from 5–6 years of cohort delivery), becomes the primary output. The Dojo itself should by now have 2–3 co-faculty and a governance structure that does not depend solely on Adler's energy — this is the transition from "Adler's project" to "a federated ecosystem."[1][3]

**Partner's pathway:** If the parents' care situation has shifted — either through Taiwan LTC 3.0 maturing, or through bereavement — the partner may have significantly more freedom. This is when her own credential or practice can fully flower, whether as an independent therapist, a Dojo co-director, or her own creative/social work practice.  The design goal for this phase is that *she is not defined by proximity to Adler's work but is a peer practitioner in her own right*, with her own professional network and identity — which is not only ethically important but also, practically, makes the partnership more resilient over decades.[1]

***

## The Interlocking Logic

The three threads are not just parallel timelines; they have specific *dependency relationships* that make sequencing important:

| Decision | Depends on | Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Staying in Taiwan 2026–2028 | Partner's care burden; Adler's cardiac NHI | Book writing; Stichting groundwork; LTC 2.0 maximization |
| Amsterdam DAFT (2027–2028) | Wyoming LLC income stability; Stichting ANBI progress | EU grant access; Horizon Europe; global symbolic capital |
| Book publication (~2029) | Manuscript completion 2026–2028 | PhD by pub. works application; speaking fee tier increase; Dojo legitimacy |
| Partner's vocational development (2027+) | Care load reduced via LTC 2.0 + hired support | Her own credential/practice; Dojo co-facilitation; relocation optionality |
| UK base (2029+) | PhD supervisor relationship; book publication; income stability | PhD completion; second book; NHS cardiac cover; Earth regrounding |
| Earth regrounding (ongoing → deeper post-2031) | Income reliability; reduced travel burden | Dojo embodied lab; health; Modes of Dependencies lived research | [3][1][2]

The through-line is this: **Taiwan buys the time and health security to write the book. The book buys the global credibility to move. Amsterdam buys the EU funding ecosystem and symbolic capital upgrade. The UK buys the PhD credentialing infrastructure and the Earth-regrounding context. And throughout, the partner's pathway is not "after all this" — it is woven in from the beginning, with her care infrastructure, her exploratory time, and her own vocational identity built incrementally alongside every one of these steps.**[1][2][3]

## 整合起點：你真正的問題是什麼

台灣薪資天花板（無論受僱或自營）大致鎖死在每月3至5萬之間，而你與伴侶目前的合計收入幾乎無法在扣除生活成本後累積任何財務緩衝。 伴侶父母現階段尚未需要全額扶養，父親仍在工作並承擔主要照護責任，但這是一個時間問題——一旦他再次惡化，每月需要匯回家的金額可能從接近零急速跳升至兩至四萬台幣以上。 這代表你們現在擁有的，是一段不知長短的**緩衝期**。問題的核心不是「如何在台灣活下去」，而是「如何在這段緩衝期內，建立一個能夠長期支撐扶養需求、同時貼近你的研究使命的生計架構」。[1]

## 財務脊梁：三條收入軌道的分工

單一收入軌道對你已被證明不可行——接標案壓垮身體、受僱收入觸頂、補助計畫落空都是前例。 整合計劃的財務基礎需要**三條同時運行但風險不互依**的軌道：[2][1]

- **軌道一：高單位收入、低時間成本的「身體技能」服務**（太極教學、穴位按摩、靜坐引導）——時薪相對高、不需管理下屬、不需回應客戶關係、可遠端或移地授課，適合在研究寫作的空檔即時補充現金流。[1]
- **軌道二：英語市場的知識型自雇收入**（Substack訂閱、顧問諮詢、工作坊促進、演講費）——這是從公共知識人身份衍生的「長效型」收入，初期少但時間複利效果強，且目標市場（歐美）的單價是台灣的3至10倍。[2][1]
- **軌道三：任務型收入作為財務安全閥**（有選擇性地接少量、高單價、清楚範圍的諮詢案，或限時的研究合作合約）——不再接低價、管理複雜的政府標案，只接能保護寫作時間的合約。[1]

**目標不是每個月最大化收入，而是三條軌道加總後穩定超越生活成本加扶養備用金，同時保留你每週至少20至25小時的不受干擾研究寫作時間。**

## 公司現況：暫停的台灣公司怎麼辦

台灣公司（覺醒合作實驗室）目前暫停營業，這是正確的選擇——保留而不解散，品牌資產仍在，未來如果有台灣業務可以復活，行政成本幾乎為零。 但這個主體的作用僅限於台灣業務報帳，**無法**用來申請歐美的補助、慈善資金或國際平台收款。[2]

關於**台灣政府補助**（如經濟部中小企業處或文化部相關計畫）：以你目前暫停營業的狀態，大多數企業補助**需要公司為正常運作狀態**才能申請。如果要重啟申請路線，需要先恢復營業，並設計一個讓台灣評審能理解的商業模式包裝——例如把Dojo的工具包和短課程包裝成「跨域人才培育服務」或「青年探究工具開發計畫」，主打中學到大學年齡層的素養教育需求，這是台灣教育部和文化部目前有明確預算方向的領域。 但這條路需要花相當多時間準備申請文件，且通過與否高度依賴評審對「非學術、非傳統的教育創新」的理解能力——成功率不穩定。建議**不要把它設為主要策略，只作為機會性嘗試**。[2]

## 遠端開發歐美市場：你目前缺少的是什麼

你的問題是**市場連結與市場資訊的缺乏**，而不是產品或能力不足。你的理論框架（依賴模式、前身學術）、紀錄片聲望、以及跨域的實踐履歷，在歐美進步知識圈都是稀缺的組合。 以下是具體的遠端切入路徑：[3][2]

### 第一步：讓你「可被找到」（0至6個月）

- 建立一個**英語長文章發布頻道**（Substack最適合，因為它有付費訂閱機制、搜尋能見度、以及社群分享功能）。不需要每週發，每月1至2篇有深度的英文文章，主題圍繞「dependency modes」、「unschooling research」、「lived-experience inquiry」——這些在英語學術圈和社創圈都是活躍話語，但缺少像你這樣有東亞田野背景的聲音。[2]
- 同步把文章分享到**LinkedIn**（針對社企、智庫、基金會人士）和**Twitter/X**（針對學術推文社群）。你目前的LinkedIn個人資料需要重新定位為「公共知識人 + 前身學術倡議者」，而非單純的台灣社運背景。[3]
- 把你紀錄片的**英語版clip或字幕版片段**剪成2至3分鐘的介紹影片，放在個人網站上作為社會公信力的快速展示。[3]

### 第二步：進入歐美圈子的邊緣（3至12個月）

- 你目前的**IACR（國際批判實在論協會）理事會席位**是進入歐洲學術社群的正式管道。 2026至2027年的年會通常在歐洲舉辦——爭取在年會做一場與你書稿直接相關的發表，這是接觸英語學術和跨學科出版社的最直接機會。[3]
- 透過**Ecoversities聯盟**、**TIAL（倫敦大學學院跨學科行動實驗室）**這些你已有連結的機構，提出**remote research affiliate**或**visiting fellow（遠端）**的合作請求——這類非正式身份雖然通常不帶薪，但會提供機構信箱、平台背書、以及引薦給相關出版社和基金會。[3]
- **Ashoka Fellows network**是你最值得投入時間申請的全球獎項。Ashoka在台灣有辦事處，申請流程你可以用中文溝通，但入選後就自動接入全球網絡，包括歐美基金會連結和媒體能見度。[2]

### 第三步：創造付費需求（6至18個月）

- 在Substack有穩定的100至300名免費訂閱者之後，推出**第一個付費工作坊或小型課程**，定價150至300美元，主題可以是「如何從真實生命問題出發設計自己的跨域研究旅程」——針對歐美有高動機的獨立研究者、另類教育工作者、或社企創業者。[1][2]
- 演講邀請的突破口通常是**Podcast訪談**，而非主動投遞演講申請。瞄準3至5個與你主題相關的英語Podcast節目（系統思考、教育創新、後成長經濟、另類學習），主動投稿訪談邀請。一次好的Podcast訪談可以帶來數百名新訂閱者，以及後續的演講詢問。[2]

## 伴侶父母扶養的財務規劃

現階段的重點是**事前備糧**，而非立即輸血。 以下是分層的財務保護設計：[1]

| 情境 | 每月估計需求（台幣） | 你的財務目標 |
|---|---|---|
| 當前（父親仍工作）| 幾乎為零 | 累積緩衝儲蓄，每月存入3至5萬台幣等值備用金 |
| 輕度照護（父親需部分協助，媽媽不變）| 2至4萬 | 英語市場軌道月入達US$800至1,200後可覆蓋 |
| 中度照護（需聘外籍看護，一位）| 4至7萬 | 需英語市場月入達US$1,500以上，或加上身體技能服務 |
| 重度照護（兩位父母均需全額支撐）| 8至12萬 | 需多軌道合計月收入達US$3,000以上，或有部分出國工作 |

伴侶本身的薪資現在以犧牲健康為代價撐到5萬多，長遠不可持續。 **整合計劃的財務目標，應該是讓你們兩人加起來在不犧牲健康的前提下，合計可用收入達到每月15至18萬台幣**——這樣才能在輕中度照護需要啟動時不慌亂。[1]

## 回歸大地的策略性位置

你想透過農耕與清潔能源來降低對金錢和分配的依賴，這在理論上是你「依賴模式」研究的「走路印證」。 從務實角度：[3]

- **現在（2026至2028年）**：在宜蘭找1至2天/週的農地協作機會（不需要自己買地或租地），或加入既有的社群農業計畫。這讓你維持身心接地的實踐，提供書稿的田野素材，但不消耗你有限的資本。
- **中期（2028至2032年）**：若英語市場軌道已有穩定基礎，可以評估以宜蘭或花東為生活基地（生活成本更低），同時結合太陽能板等基礎能源自足投資（一次性成本15至25萬台幣，5至8年回本）。
- **長期（2032年後）**：若Dojo有穩定的共同參與者和收入模式，可以考慮設立實體「實踐場」（類似小型生態村的研究居所），把農耕、能源自足、研究工作坊整合為一個有機生活實驗室。
