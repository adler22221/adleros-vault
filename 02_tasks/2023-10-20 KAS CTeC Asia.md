9:10 Keynote: Digital Media: A New Era of Blind Participation?

Prof. Jörg Matthes, Professor, Department of Communication, University of Vienna (Austria)

  

Populism is taking hold. Looking for solutions.  
Digital media open an era of “blind participation”

  
Original Idea: Digital media as a new instrument of informing and engaging

  

Why does the use of digital media make us more politically active?  
The intertwined mechanism:  
Communication: political discussion and expression  
Competence: political learning  and political efficacy (> theses rejected by speaker in the end)

Motivation: Goal formation, Affective, Polarization

  

Evidence:  
Social media use related to: 

Online and offline political participation (Ekstrom & Shegata 2018)

Success of social movement

Political protest

Affective polarization

  

Optimistic View: “Never before has so much information mostly without extra financial costs, been so close at hand for citizens (Boukes 2019 p39)  
  
Pessimistic View:  
1. information overload (van Erkel and van Aelst 2021)

2. distraction (Matthe, 2022)

[Matthes 2022 Social media and the political engagement of young adults  
](https://www.degruyter.com/document/doi/10.1515/omgc-2022-0006/html)Informed? Mobile experience sampling study of Austrian youth: 6.16% political news: majority a-political 

Those with high entertainment exposure had their political interest decreased with more political exposure  
3. Filter bubble

4. Misinformation

  

Summary  
Social media use 

- activates use politically
    
- does not foster objective political knowledge
    
- can even negatively affect knowledge
    
- leads to one sided learning
    
- and to the learning of misinformation
    
- creates an illusion to be subjectively informed 
    

  

Solution?

Incidental exposure could have positive effect?  
2022 [Democratic consequences of incidental exposure to political information](https://academic.oup.com/joc/article/72/3/345/6549884) 

2022 [Seeing political information online incidentally effect of first and second level incidental exposure](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0747563222001078)

  
Conclusion: The enhanced political participation associated with use of social media is NOT based on “learning,” but more on “Motivation”  
Dilemma on democracy**

11:30 Panel 2: Going Beyond Misinformation: Faking the Future

Misinformation on digital platforms is shaped by a myriad of elements, encompassing peer effects and platform features. This panel will offer a multi-method perspective into the study of these factors, as well as approaches to exacerbate or mitigate their effects on attitudes and behaviour. The panel will conclude with a research agenda for the future of misinformation research into the uncanny valley effects of new modalities of misinformation, with a keen focus on its regional nuances.
