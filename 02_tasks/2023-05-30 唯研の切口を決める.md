the polical, economic, ideological structures have all changed.
but what is the constant?
the dependency on resource allocation, and the problems that follow.
if the structures have all changed, how can we blame the structure for determining [[Allocation Dependence 配分依存|allocation dependence]]?

of course, if we abstract from the actualities of the political, economic, ideological structures -- we might see the unequal distribution of (access to) resources. But is this situation necessary human-made? Or would it be geological? (my contribution to this would probably mainly lie in [[Rationality Dependence 理性依存]])

If such dependency has been constant since history, it might tell something about human nature (at least, when conditioned by the fact of being part of the mass). e.g., risk aversion, desire for certainty/linear thinking/control. Of course, the psychological drivers of such dependency are worth studying. But operational-wise, it is practical to say that allocation dependence is a decent and operatable level of analysis.
