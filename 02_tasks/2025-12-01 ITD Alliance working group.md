### About the Workshop Series

A cornerstone of this year’s activities for the Organizational Change for ITD working group is a set of similarly formatted workshops shaped by our inaugural sessions in September and participants’ feedback.

### Format of This Session

This workshop follows our Common Room model - an interactive space for connecting, sharing, and exploring organizational change practices.

The session includes:

- A brief thematic presentation on a current success or dilemma
    
- A respondent reflection
    
- Open discussion
    
- Small-group conversations on related topics
    

### This Session’s Theme

**Developing an Interdisciplinary Workforce for the Institutionalization of Interdisciplinarity**

Interdisciplinarity depends on individuals with the skills to implement it meaningfully. Competency frameworks can help guide the development of those skills, but they must be adapted to the diverse roles and experience levels within universities.

To spark discussion, Marie-France Gévry will present the _Sentinel North Training Program Conceptual Model_ and its associated _Competency Framework_, including insights from its implementation and evaluation at Université Laval.

**Speaker:** _Marie-France Gévry_, Interdisciplinary Integration Advisor and lecturer at Université Laval, Quebec, Canada.  
**Respondent:** _Bald de Vries_, Professor of Interdisciplinary Legal Education & Director of the Center for Academic Teaching and Learning, Utrecht University

### Interactive Component

Participants will work in breakout groups to examine one of the five core competencies from the Sentinel North Framework and consider what mastery looks like for:

- Researchers (including postdoctoral fellows, research associates, and group leaders)
    
- Professors
    
- Administrative personnel
    

### Overview of Additional Ongoing Projects

- **The Library Project:** Curating and discussing resources to build our ITD knowledge base
    
- **The Impact Project:** Mapping interest and developing action plans to advocate for ITD in policy, funding, publishing, and institutional assessment
    

More information on the working group and previous events is available here:
