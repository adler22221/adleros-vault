Previously, I thought that the healthiest human condition is that of [[無欲]]/[[知足]]/self-sufficiency/absence of deficiency, and such healthy condition antagonistic to a [[market economy]] where all people's survival/livelihood are directly dependent on the money/purchase power they possess.

However, listening to [[Shiller, Robert]]'s [[202212311627 Coursera-Yale-Financial Markets-W2-Invention Takes Time]] lecture in his [[Coursera-Yale-Financial Markets]] course reminded me that I have to take volatility into account. In other words, even if one can achieve health through the peace from internal [[homeostatis]]/[[equillibrium]](?)/balance, since the external environment/world is not static/is volatile, the internal peace can be disrupted. This is when adaptation is called upon. 

We can logically infer the following types of adaptation:

| 無為(nonexertion)  |  正德, 修行, 順天應時 |  animal evolution, 養生, 順天應時 |教化, 順天應時 | animal evolution, appropriate technology, permaculture, 利用, 厚生, 順天應時 |

[[money]] is useful when:
- the extra effort required for adaptation exceeds the agent's capacity if combined with the effort for immediate survival (e.g., the effort required for one to solve problems renders him/her unable to sufficiently feed him/herself)
- and, 
	- such extra effort also exceeds the total capacity of the local potential immediate beneficiaries (e.g., the amount of people required to resolve their common adaptation challenges exceeds what the community can afford to feed)
	- or, there isn't enough agents in the local community who value the effort for adaptation, thus the adaptors (innovators) cannot get sufficient support locally (e.g., not enough people in the community see the point of the problem-solvers effort, so for their endeavor to continue, they need to seek external support)

In [[East Asian Philosophy]], the commandment to follow the heaven/nature's order (順天應時) seem to be the underlying view when facing adaptational challenges. This is possible because East Asians held a non-linear/cyclical view of the timespace -- The future is partially knowable as it is just different manifestations of the same cyclical order ([[輪迴]]/[[陰陽]]/[[五行]]). Their approach in principle, can be characterized as [[無為]]/nonexertion upon the agent and its system. When adaptational challenges arise, people had to seek ways to reconcile with nature (this implies that actions will be done within existing capacity), instead of exerting extra/additional control upon nature. At the agent level, this is manifested through the emphasis on personal [[修行]] nonmaterially and [[養生]] materially (even though the material-nonmaterial distinction might not exist at the time), and [[教化]] and 利用 厚生 at the nonmaterial and materal dimension at the system level, respectively.  

Since the commonly valued principle is to follow the nature's order instead of exerting power against it, the necessity of international trade or money transactions/market economy was not high. This should probably be viewed together with China's approach to governance. [[China]] historically seeks unification, maybe because it seeks to grow the capacity of its community to a self-sufficient level, which is to sustain its system's capacity to feed the population which is focused on adaptational challenges (+ those managing this very system) without farming? One evidence might be that historically, the material life necessities such as crop harvests, land, cloth, and labor play a fundamental role in the pay of imperial officials (俸錄)[^1].  Money for most of the time, was considered as something extra, which is for other miscellaneous expenditures.
