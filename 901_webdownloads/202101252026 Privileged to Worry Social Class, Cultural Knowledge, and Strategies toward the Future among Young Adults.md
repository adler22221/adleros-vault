# Privileged to Worry: Social Class, Cultural Knowledge, and Strategies toward the Future among Young Adults
In the face of uncertainty and structural constraints, differences in cultural capital manifest themselves in unique capacities. This article demonstrates that the profound status anxiety of the upper middle class acts a protective force. ==When their status is threatened, specifically by widespread structural forces such as those reshaping the current economic landscape, the highly advantaged draw on their cultural capital to become aware of this threat, and subsequently to activate resources to protect their privileged position.==

Using the Great Recession and the changing transition to adulthood as a shared context of uncertainty and structural obstacles, this article considers the perspectives toward dealing with the economic downturn as expressed by 106 young adults of different social class backgrounds. Despite the difficulties introduced by the Great Recession, I find that ==working and middle class young adults are generally optimistic that their futures will be unscathed because of the economic downturn and they invoke individual failings to explain mishaps related to the recession.== In contrast, the upper middle class youth feel uneasy when considering how the recession would affect their long-term trajectories. This concern prompted distinctive strategies in coping with the economic downturn—strategies that were likely to result in greater economic security in the long term.

This article demonstrates one important ==mechanism by which social privilege is reproduced: those with more cultural capital are more likely to develop a sense of anxiety—defined here as overt concern about the maintenance of their social status—toward structural constraints, such as the recession, as well as strategies that result in reproduction of their privilege.== In contrast, those with less cultural capital express more hopefulness and resilience when considering the future. In certain circumstances, unease about the uncertainty of future privilege can be advantageous, as it spurs the upper middle class to action.
