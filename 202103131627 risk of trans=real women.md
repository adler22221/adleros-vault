I think that this is where we are in practice. Recently in the UK, the Ministry of Justice
announced that 50% of transwomen prisoners are sex offenders, who can claim a right to move to a women’s prison (Reality Check team 2018)^[see [[202103131624 How many transgender inmates are there]].]. Children are being given sex hormones to block their natural progression through puberty, without the medical ethics of this being resolved. Those querying the wisdom of this are accused of demanding ‘conversion therapy’ and so are cast pre-emptively in the role of wrongdoer.

Women organizing peaceful meetings to discuss sex and gender are meeting bomb
threats from trans activists. Women announcing such events are being punched in
public. Art exhibitions, contemptuous of TERFS, are displaying bloodied baseball bats. ‘Trolls’ on social media are using phrases like ‘Kill a TERF!’ or ‘Punch a TERF!’. A dark cartoon of a woman hanging from the gallows is sometimes helpfully supplied, with the caption ‘Dead TERFS’. Gender-critical feminists wishing to speak publicly about this topic are being ‘no platformed’. In the febrile self-righteous world of identity politics, and its competing versions of epistemological privilege and virtue signalling, a serious and legitimate discussion of sex and gender now seems to be afforded the same dubious status as the promotion of fascism.

This is the politically brutal and commercially-inflected world of trans politics that I have begun to understand in the past couple of years. If we ask the question ‘what would the world have to be like in order for this scenario to arise’, then the answer lies in my original paper, which Summersell wishes to criticize narrowly for its alleged faulty form of inference. But talk of wedding invitations, tea parties and wish lists about different types of concrete singularity to describe real women do not answer this question.

Instead, the answer lies in male power, the contradictions of identity politics when subjectivity is hyper-sacralized, drug company profits, healthcare professional careerism, the emergence of technology that permits body manipulation absent in the past, social contagion in children about a simple and permanent fix for their transitory existential woes and misplaced liberalism across the political spectrum in current times about everyone being free to be who they want to be and act accordingly. The last of these factors goes unnoticed until identity assertion causes offence in its behavioural expression. The freedom of gender-critical feminists to express that offence is currently being suppressed by both legal and illegal means.
