## 3. Committee comments → before → after

### 馬 (Ma) — conceptual clarity · evidence-grounding · comparative breadth

**1. Make the squeeze the core, and explain its machinery.** *The squeeze is the thesis's most original and compelling idea, yet in the draft it had paradoxically receded — stated briefly rather than developed and carried through.* — **Fully**
- **Before:** the squeeze appeared only as a short closing section of "signs," under-theorised.
- **After:** a full chapter develops it as a recurring, evolving figure and a **4-type typology on one shared 5-axis scale**, framing the abstract, the introduction, and every chapter (Ch VI §A; abstract; Ch V §I).

**2. Comparative/cross-cultural breadth; interpret Needham, don't name-drop.** *The argument leaned on Western modernity without enough comparative grounding; the pre-modern contrast was thin; and Needham was cited in passing ("he is a chemist — make his voice heard").* — **Substantially**
- **Before:** "disposition to domination" framed mainly through Western modernity; Needham name-dropped; weak pre-modern contrast.
- **After:** Ch III retitled to "universalization"; Needham **interpreted as a reciprocal "web, not a chain"** (verbatim across four works + comparison table + a critical break from his teleology); Brague carries "not every tradition has a unified concept of *world*"; a concrete pre-modern contrast for the destitution sign (Ch III §E; Intro §D fn16; Ch VI §D). *(Poskett, Fuchs considered then set aside — §5.)*

**3. Ground the "evidenced by…" claims.** *Several claims were asserted as "evidenced by…" without the evidence following.* — **Substantially**
- **Before:** key diagnostic claims rhetorical/asserted.
- **After:** sourced to specific literatures — the body sign to Wilson; singularization/destitution to Illich, Ellis, Hussein & Nelson; anti-fitness to Crosby, Levis et al., Holling/Gunderson, Taleb, Ponting, the 1970 corn-blight case (Ch III §G; Ch VI §D).

**4. State your position; situate vs governance/ownership; answer the acceptance objection.** *Locate your view against the established readings (Montesquieu on governance, Marx on ownership); and answer the obvious objection — if artificiality saves lives (fewer die, fewer locust plagues), isn't its spread simply proof it is good/fittest?* — **Fully**
- **Before:** position implicit; not situated; the "why isn't it just good?" objection unanswered.
- **After:** a three-term ontology + a stated contribution, situated **"beneath"** Montesquieu and Marx; the objection met by *fitness-to-a-world vs fitness-to-the-universe*, conceding offloading's real goods ("chosen daily by people who are not deceived") before showing it manufactures fragility (Intro §D–E; Ch III §G; Conclusion §B).

**5. Is the heavy philosophy actually important?** *Is such philosophical apparatus necessary for understanding social development, or is it ornament? Make it earn its place.* — **Substantially**
- **Before:** the apparatus risked reading as ornamental.
- **After:** it does operative work — reframes the live AI-alignment debate (*"alignment is the most advanced symptom, not the cure"*), brackets the sentience question, and converts into a concrete prescriptive turn (Intro §C; Conclusion §C–D).

**6. Guard against essentialization.** *The framework risks essentializing its key terms — "the West," "the human," "modernity," "technology" — as if each named a fixed nature.* — **Fully**
- **Before:** "disposition to domination" could read as an essence ascribed to the West, or to the human as such.
- **After:** every key term is reframed **dispositionally, not essentially**: the chapter is retitled "the **Enlightenment**, *not* Western/modern," treating the disposition as a trans-historical *program* rather than the essence of a people or a period; *"sovereignty is not domination"*; hypotheticals are recast as **Weberian ideal types** (tendencies, not natures); the **animal→human continuum** refuses to essentialize "the human"; and Needham is read as a reciprocal *"web, not a chain"* with an explicit break from his teleology, so neither "East" nor "West" is handed a fixed essence (Ch III §C, §E–F; Ch I §B–C).

### 米 (Mi, supervisor) — argument structure · logical derivation · terminological precision

- **After:** ~75,000 words / 226 pp across 8 chapters; every stub now argued — the single largest structural response.

**2. Develop Chapter I; the rest must build on it.** *Universe/world/human are introduced in Ch I, but it was too thin to bear the later argument.* — **Fully**
- **Before:** Ch I a brief stub.
- **After:** full chapter — GOE feedback, niche construction, Obara's dual ecosystems, and the **AOS specified by five working criteria** reused throughout (Ch I §D–E).

**3. **4. Clarify metaverse vs world.** *Is the metaverse merely one of the worlds? Does it replace the world — and if so, does the human disappear, and is there still a world?* — **Fully**

**7. Consider opposing viewpoints; justify your choices.** *You pick a few favoured positions; weigh rivals, justify why you sustain yours, test whether the cited positions are tenable. And: too many hypotheticals.* — **Substantially**

- **After:** *"Why Bhaskar rather than the more famous critics?"* weighs Adorno/Horkheimer, Derrida, Habermas; Conclusion §C engages the strongest opposing program on its terms; hypotheticals reframed as **Weberian ideal types** (Ch III §F; Conclusion §C; Intro §C).

### 劉 (Liu) — scope discipline · section architecture · sufficiency for a master's thesis

**1. Rigour for an MA; no trailing sentences.** *Enjoyable and not mere sci-fi, but free-wheeling; is the argumentation rigorous and sustained enough for an MA? Some sentences simply trailed off ("laws of their own…" with nothing after).* — **Substantially**
- **Before:** associative discourse; thin argumentation; trailing sentences.
- **After:** rebuilt with a stated retroductive method; the trailing "laws of their own" is now a full Polanyi-grounded section (Intro §C; Ch I §D; Ch II §C). *(Global "MA-rigour" is the committee's call.)*

**2. What is new beyond philosophy of technology?** *Phil-of-tech already treats existence as technologically mediated; Marcuse's* One-Dimensional Man *concerns adaptation; Feenberg's primary/secondary instrumentalization is on the table. The metaverse looks like your real proposal — state the contribution.* — **Substantially**
- **Before:** the contribution wasn't clearly differentiated.
- **After:** stated explicitly — *"beneath the familiar readings… a structural condition they do not name — the squeeze"* — earned via "artificiality not technology" + triangulation; the metaverse defined as the thesis's own object (Intro §C, §F; Ch II §B). *(Marcuse/Feenberg not named — §5.)*

**3. What is the contribution *beyond critical theory*?** *Frankfurt critical theory already diagnoses domination, reification, and "second nature"; what does this thesis add that Adorno, Horkheimer, or Marcuse have not already said?* — **Fully**
- **Before:** the surplus over critical theory was not stated; "second nature" appeared only in its inherited Frankfurt sense.
- **After:** three distinct contributions are made explicit. **(i) The squeeze** — a structural, *recurring and accumulating* ontological figure (the 4-type typology on one 5-axis scale) that critical theory diagnoses symptoms of but never names. **(ii) Second nature made ontologically literal** — where the Frankfurt tradition means reified social relations that merely *appear* natural, the thesis argues the metaverse becomes a genuine **second given** (the forged Dao, 偽道): substrate-real and self-completing, not a false appearance to be unmasked. **(iii) The three faces of the one disposition** — critical theory (Adorno, Horkheimer, Marcuse) could explain only the **controlling** face of the Enlightenment; the thesis reframes the disposition as the **sovereignty of the subject** and shows it wears *three* faces — **control / mastery** (domination), **benevolent control / stewardship** (control exercised as care, which *appears* to deliver freedom and autonomy), and **withdrawal** (disengagement, resignation, *tangping*, nihilism). All three are flights from givenness realized by offloading the given to artificiality, and all three tighten the squeeze — so the thesis can diagnose precisely what a domination-only critique misreads: that benevolent stewardship and quiet withdrawal are **not escapes** from the disposition but further expressions of it. The contribution therefore sits *beneath* critical theory's critique of ideology, at the ontological stratum (Ch III §C; Ch IV; Ch VI §A; Ch V §E–I; Conclusion §A).

**4. What are the metaverse's own laws?** — **Fully**
- **Before:** "laws of their own" unspecified.
- **After:** the **forged Dao (偽道)** — *"opaque, mutating, perpetually self-rewriting"* organizing principles (Ch V §E; Ch VI §E).

**5. The squeeze: not always negative; how is it different from past squeezes; it is dynamic; relief became the squeeze; cases must be vivid; it may show in identity.** — **Fully** (with the identity case **Partial**)
- **Before:** the squeeze under-theorised; its novelty vs prior "world's squeeze" unargued; the Frankfurt-school squeeze not distinguished; cases thin.
- **After:** *"the squeeze admits of more than one ending… not all of them are losses"* and is framed through the **pharmakon**; the 4-type typology measures the difference — **Type 4 is the first to cut at the organizing-principle (Dao) stratum**; it is presented as a recurring, *accumulating* figure; the relief→squeeze paradox is internal to artificiality (*"they tend to create the very problems they were meant to solve"*); cases thickened (Ming collapse via Brook, the eight-legged essay, Illich's automobile, the 2025–26 "agent internet"). **Identity/cosmetic-surgery is only indirectly covered** (diseases of civilization, self-petification) — §4 (Ch VI §A–G; Ch II §E; Conclusion §G).

## 4. Self-initiated substantive elaborations (beyond the committee's comments)

*Genuine intellectual additions the author developed on his own initiative — verified as substantive, new vs the viva draft, and not prompted by any committee comment. These show the expansion was not merely reactive.*

1. **The animal→human continuum and the dual human existence (人間 / ヒト).** Grounds "worlds" in niche-construction across the tree of life (ant, beaver, coral) and derives the human's dual existence — the anthropological human (人間, ningen) coupled to the biological Homo (ヒト, hito) via Obara's auto-artificial selection — to **defuse anthropocentric bias** and place the human on one continuum with other world-builders. *(Ch I §B–C)*
2. **The Adaptational Operating System (AOS) as a formal ideal type with five working criteria.** A new analytical primitive placing animal niches and human worlds on one continuum of collective survival strategies — specified across evolutionary, systemic-functional (Polanyi), semiotic, and phenomenological (Uexküll *Umwelt/Umgebung*) criteria — from which "world" and "universe" are then defined as special cases. *(Ch I §D–E)*
3. **The three-articulation stratified ontology: 道/理/相 ↔ real/actual/empirical ↔ complex-adaptive-systems levels.** Welds critical realism, CAS theory, and a coined East Asian triad (道 principles / 理 structures / 相 phenomena) into one stratified spine carried across universe, world, and the metaverse (as forged 偽道/偽理/偽相) — the cross-referencing grammar the whole thesis runs on. *(Intro §C; Ch I §E; Ch V–VI)*
4. **The four-cell typology of artificiality + the convention→structure migration.** A 2×2 map of how the made compels (material/nonmaterial × conventional/structural), read at three depths, with a directional migration from convention toward structure whose far end is self-completion — turning a static taxonomy into the **engine of the squeeze**. *(Ch II §D)*
5. **The regeneration base and "ontological monoculture" as the deepest cost of universalization.** Introduces the "regeneration base" (the surviving stock from which a collapsed world rebuilds) and argues the species-level base just *is* the plurality of worlds — so universalization's gravest harm is the erasure of that plurality. A new irreversibility argument. *(Ch III §B, §G; Ch VI)*
6. **Self-completion located at the "forged Dao": the one genuinely new move.** Argues that almost nothing in the metaverse is new — it inherits discretizing/grammatization/concretization (Stiegler, Simondon) from the world and adds exactly **one** move, Hui's exosomatized tertiary protention, which closes the loop. A disciplined claim about what is and isn't novel. *(Ch V §C–F)*
7. **The "forged Dao" as a counterfeit of constancy; mortality recast as a programmable parameter.** Develops Xunzi's 偽 (made/forged, *not* fake) into a precise metaphysical claim — the metaverse's ground sheds activity- and concept-dependence but not substrate-dependence, mutating faster than adaptation can follow — and reframes death under a counterfeit authority. *(Ch V §E; Ch VI §E)*
8. **The science-fiction / AI-alignment / "mind-as-being" rebuttal cluster.** Disposes of AI-doom and transhumanist-succession scenarios on the thesis's own terms: a perfectly aligned AI *tightens* the squeeze; a disembodied "mind" that wants to persist still needs organs in the universe — hence a body, hence inherits its own squeeze. *(Conclusion §C)*
9. **The three faces of the sovereign subject — explaining what a domination-only critique cannot.** The decisive surplus over critical theory: where Adorno, Horkheimer, and Marcuse could theorise only the **controlling** face of the Enlightenment, the thesis reframes the disposition as the **sovereignty of the subject** and shows it wears three faces — **control / mastery**, **benevolent control / stewardship**, and **withdrawal** (resignation, *tangping*, nihilism). Unifying all three under subject-supremacy-realized-through-offloading lets the thesis diagnose benevolent stewardship and quiet withdrawal as *further expressions* of the disposition rather than escapes from it — phenomena a domination-only frame structurally cannot account for. *(Ch III §C; Ch IV; Conclusion)*

> Beyond these, two **comment-responses in §3 also went substantially past the literal request** — the five-tradition "convergence is itself the evidence" synthesis (Ch II §E); and the **two-engines** synthesis welding the value/instrumental pair onto Bhaskar's ontic/epistemic fallacies (Ch IV §C). These are substantive elaborations that exceed the comment they loosely touch.
