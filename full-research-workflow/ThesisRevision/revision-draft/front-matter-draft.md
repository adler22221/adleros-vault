## Abstract (English) — DRAFT (~300 words)

This thesis conceptualizes the metaverse not as a technology or a virtual-reality product but as an emerging *background environment* — a second "given" that artificiality becomes once its self-completion is sufficiently advanced — and argues that its emergence reconfigures the basic situation of human existence. The central claim is the **squeeze hypothesis**. The human has always survived by building a *world*: an adaptational operating system nested within the universe, a made habitat that buffers its makers from the raw pressures of the given. As artificiality self-completes into a second background environment, the human comes to be situated *between two environments* rather than within one — between the universe, the realist substrate indifferent to human meaning, and the metaverse, a self-revising artificial order that runs by principles of its own. The world that once mediated between the human and the universe is thereby hollowed, and the human is pressed between two givens, neither of which answers to it.

The argument proceeds in six chapters. It reconstructs the *world* as a built habitat (Ch. I) and analyzes what worlds are made of — *artificiality* in its technological, virtual, and law-irreducible aspects (Ch. II). It then asks why one mode of artificiality, the modern Western "Enlightenment mode," came to predominate and to universalize, tracing its disposition to both a material basis and an epistemic one (Ch. III). Two engines drive the present: a human-side pursuit of a *life-as-intended* and the *self-finishing* (自己完結化) of human life (Ch. IV), and an apparatus-side *self-completion* (自足完成化) of artificiality that closes into a circuit able to run without us (Ch. V). Chapter VI draws these together as the *evolution of the squeeze* across four types. The thesis locates a structural condition beneath the familiar discourses of governance, ownership, and liberty, and reframes the task: from asking how to master what we have made to asking what we have come to depend on.

**Keywords (EN):** metaverse; artificiality; virtuality; the squeeze; world; self-completion; self-finishing; critical realism; philosophy of technology; the Anthropocene

本論文主張：「元宇宙」不應被理解為一種技術或虛擬實境產品，而應被理解為一種正在浮現的**背景環境**——當人為性（artificiality）之自我完成（自足完成化）達到足夠程度時，它所成為的「第二個所與[?]（the given）」。本論文的核心主張為**擠壓假說[?]（the squeeze hypothesis）**：人類向來透過建造一個「**世界**」而存活——世界是嵌套於宇宙之中的一套適應性運作系統（adaptational operating system）[?]，一個人造的棲所，將其建造者與所與的原始壓力相隔開。當人為性自我完成為一個第二背景環境時，人類遂從「處於單一環境之中」轉變為「夾處於兩個環境之間」——一邊是宇宙，對人類意義漠然的實在基底；另一邊是偽境，依其自身原理自我改寫的人造秩序。曾經中介於人類與宇宙之間的世界因而被掏空，人類遂被擠壓於兩個皆不回應於己的所與之間。

全文分為六章。第一章將「世界」重構為一個被建造的棲所；第二章分析世界由何而成——即人為性之技術、虛擬、與法則不可化約三個面向。第三章追問：何以人為性的某一種模態，即現代西方的「啟蒙模式」，得以取得主導並普世化，並將此傾向溯源於物質條件與一種知識論條件（巴斯卡的知識論謬誤）。當前的局面由兩具引擎所驅動：人這一端對「**如其所願之生（life-as-intended）**」的追求，及隨之而來人之生的**自己完結化**（第四章）；以及裝置那一端人為性的**自足完成化**——閉合為一個無需人類即可運行的迴路（第五章）。第六章將二者合觀，視為**擠壓的演化**，貫穿四種型態。本論文於治理、所有權、與自由等既有論述之下，指認出一個結構性的處境，並重新框定問題：從「如何主宰我們所造之物」，轉為「我們究竟已依賴於何物」。
