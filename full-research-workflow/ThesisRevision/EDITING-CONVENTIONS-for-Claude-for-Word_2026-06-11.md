## 1. CJK / multilingual presentation — the rule we keep returning to

**This is a rule of FORM, not just order.** At **first use** of any non-English term:

> **English gloss FIRST → original in PARENTHESES with romanization → bare English thereafter.**
> Pattern: `English meaning (漢字, romanization)`.

- ✅ `Heaven and earth are not benevolent (天地不仁, tiān dì bù rén)…` → later just "the impartiality of heaven and earth."
- ❌ `Laozi wrote — 天地不仁 — they treat the myriad things as straw dogs` — **bare CJK fenced in em-dashes**, presented as the primary token with English draped around it. This is a violation even though English appears nearby.
- ❌ `時空間的自立性 (spatio-temporal self-subsistence)` — CJK-first inside the parens / CJK leading.
- ❌ bare `偽道` with no gloss; ❌ `wu wei (無為)` — romanization-first.

**Specifics:**
- **Romanization is required at first use** and goes *inside* the parenthesis after the script: `(漢字, romanization)`. Many first occurrences in the Introduction and Conclusion drop it — add it.
- **Romanization styling:** italic is the dominant body style (`(有所待, *yousuodai*)`). Make it uniform — some chapters left it roman.
- Applies to **Chinese, Japanese, and Sanskrit** alike (Sanskrit with diacritics: *pratītyasamutpāda*, *anātman*).
- This holds **even for coined/canonical terms** (偽道, 天人合一, 君子不器, 無為) — none is an untranslatable token; each gets an English gloss first.
- **Obara's typographic contrast is load-bearing — preserve it:** 「モノ」(katakana) = the whole of human-made artificiality (incl. language, religion, ideology); 「もの」(hiragana) = concrete material artefacts only. Never normalize one script into the other.
- **Classical quotations:** give the English first; the original may sit in parentheses (short) or as a block quote (long), but never bare-CJK-as-headword.

## 2. Locked terms — never silently rename

| Term | Locked form | Do **not** use |
|---|---|---|
| universe / world / metaverse | universe (宇宙) realist given · world (世界) human meaning-space · metaverse (偽境) made second given | blurring them; metaverse as **元宇宙** (collides with 宇宙) |
| artificiality | **artificiality (人為)** — umbrella for the made | technology · technics · cosmotechnics · neo-artificiality |
| metaverse-stage | **metaverse-stage artificiality** (positional) | "neo-" / "neo-artificiality" |
| virtuality | "the **causally efficacious nonmaterial aspect of artificiality**" (verbatim) | "a computer-based reality" / VR-only / "structure" |
| human vs apparatus pole | self-**finishing** (自己完結化, human) vs self-**completion** (自足完成化, apparatus) | swapping/conflating the two |
| register axis | **structural** [TERMINOLOGY-PENDING], paired with "conventional" | necessitarian · causally-binding (both superseded) |
| the 偽- triad | forged Dao (偽道) / forged structures (偽理) / forged phenomena (偽相); strata 道/理/相 | writing "real/actual/empirical" as the thesis's *own* terms (CR glosses only); 偽象 |
| mode characteristic | **supremacy / sovereignty of the subject**; mechanism = offloading to artificiality | "controllability" / "the subject's intent over the given" (Rosa's pole) |
| Enlightenment mode | the trans-historical **metaphysical revolution** (Adorno & Horkheimer "widest sense") | the 18th-century period (Aufklärung) |
| squeeze | the **existential figure**, recurring and **un-numbered** (GOE→Homo-origin→universe↔world→universe↔metaverse) | "Squeeze₁"; bare "squeeze" for Bhaskar's "primal squeeze" |
| tekhne → phusis | a **non-literal critical provocation** against phusis/nomos/techne | reading it literally |

Also preserve precisely (viva-locked): technosphere, "disposition to domination," "fully actualized," "world-completeness," the four squeeze types, squeeze ≠ collapse.

**Disambiguate the bare words "made" and "given" by context.** Both carry the thesis's technical contrast — the **made** = artificiality (human-made); the **given** = the universe's natural substrate, with the metaverse becoming a *second* given — but as bare participles they read as ordinary English and blur the argument. Where the technical sense is intended and the bare word risks an everyday reading, expand it:
- **"made"** → "artificial" or "human-made" (whichever the sentence wants).
- **"given"** → "given by the universe" (or, when the metaverse is meant, "given by the metaverse" / the "second given") — or an equivalent that names the source.

Apply **by context, not as a blanket find-replace** — leave "made"/"given" alone where the ordinary sense is meant or where the surrounding sentence already makes the source unmistakable; expand only where it aids the reader.

## 3. Citations & references (Soochow Journal of Philosophical Studies style)

- **In-text:** author-date, colon before pages — `(Author, Year: 68–69)` or, name in sentence, `Author (Year: 68–69)`.
- **One citation at the sentence's natural seam** — never dropped mid-clause, never the same cite repeated in one sentence.
- **EPUB without stable pages → cite by chapter**, e.g. `(Chalmers, 2022: ch. 10)`.
- **Split a cite across distinct works** rather than collapsing years: Baudrillard `(1994: 3, 6; 2005: 13, 33)` — *Simulacra and Simulation* (1994) for dissimulate/simulate + the four phases; *The Intelligence of Evil* (2005) for integral reality / the virtual as "final solution." Keep all four Searle entries distinct (1969, 1995, **2008a** = book *Philosophy in a New Century*; **2008b** = article "Language and Social Ontology").
- **Reference list:** new page after the body; **one entry per paragraph**, alphabetized, **hanging indent**. **Chinese references first** (ordered by surname stroke count 筆劃), then **Western references** (A–Z). Same author → chronological; same author + year → append a/b/c.
- **CJK-named authors cited by romanized surname in text** (Obara, Uegaki, Miura, Kawamoto) must be **locatable in the list** — either cross-reference (`Obara, H. — see 小原秀雄`) or list under the romanized surname with 漢字 in parentheses. Right now they appear only under 漢字 and are unfindable for a reader following "Obara."
- Entry shapes (English): *Author (Year). Title of the book. Place: Publisher.* / *Author (Year). Title of article. Journal, vol(no): pp.* / chapters: *…In Ed. (Ed.), Book (pp.). Place: Publisher.* Chinese entries use 〈 〉 for article/chapter titles and 《 》 for book/journal titles.

## 4. Punctuation & mechanics

- **Em-dash:** the house style is the **spaced** em-dash ` — `. Do not leave tight `word—word` dashes, and use a **lowercase** continuation after a mid-sentence dash (not `anaerobic—To them`).
- **Quotation marks:** Chinese inline `「…」` (nested `『…』`); English inline `"…"` (nested `'…'`). A **displayed (block) quotation carries no quotation marks** — indent ~0.5 cm L/R, blank line above and below. (The Heraclitus Fr. 30 passage, "This kosmos, the same for all…", is currently body text — make it a block quote; sweep for others.)
- **Footnote marker** is superscript and sits **after** the punctuation mark.
- **Numbers:** Arabic for dates, statistics, decimals, percentages, fractions, legal article numbers; otherwise spell out "expressive" numbers.

## 5. Voice / register

- **Write as the author** — measured, first-person scholarly, **public-facing but rigorous** (MIT-Press register). No meta-commentary, no "as requested," no AI cadence.
- **De-jargon the body; push abstruse material and long quotations into footnotes.** Argue each source in its own voice; don't reduce an interlocutor to a parenthetical name.
- **Hedge overclaims and contested superlatives** where the strong version misrepresents a source (e.g. soften "the AI-safety program presupposes an artificial agent whose decisive act lies in the future"; the GOE is not uncontroversially the "largest mass extinction").

## 6. Fact / citation integrity — and what **not** to "correct"

- **Verify before changing a citation.** Flag in-text↔reference mismatches as comments (both directions) rather than auto-fixing; distinct works that *look* like duplicates stay distinct.
- **Do not revert these verified attribution locks** (a careful copyedit can accidentally "fix" them into errors):
  - *pharmakon* = the word **Derrida revived** and **Stiegler extended** (not "Stiegler revived").
  - Polanyi **coins and endorses** "dual control" — the *reservation* in the thesis is the author's own, against the deterministic two-tier reading.
  - Bhaskar's **ontic fallacy** = determination of *knowledge by being* (don't invert); "Eurocentrism / extreme-form" is Hartwig, not Bhaskar.
  - **Uegaki** = entrustment that *alleviates* burden (freedom/equality/autonomy/conviviality) — **never** Adorno-&-Horkheimer "domination." Keep 切断 (severance) ≠ 亀裂 (fracture).
  - **Obara**: secondary altriciality is an *effect* of self-domestication, not its cause.
  - "monotechnology" = **Hui 2024** (*Machine and Sovereignty*, pp. 225–226), not 2016.
- **Author's own / unpublished works:** don't invent citations — leave a comment asking for the titles/keys.
- **Chinese abstract & Chinese register are the author's domain** — apply only mechanical fixes he's approved (e.g. 掘空→掏空); do not rewrite the 摘要.

## 7. Layout (Soochow spec) — mostly Word-layout, may need a macro or the author

A4; margins L/R **3.5 cm**, T/B **3.0 cm**; **1.5** spacing; **Times New Roman 12 / 新細明體 12**; chapter titles **≥20 pt, centered, new page**. Watermark **校徽 on abstract / TOC / body / bibliography — NOT the cover**. **Dual page numbers** (lowercase roman front matter; Arabic from the Introduction). **Refresh the TOC** after structural edits.
