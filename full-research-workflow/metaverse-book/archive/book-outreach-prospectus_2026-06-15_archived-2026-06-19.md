# From thesis to book — a one-page note

> Forwardable summary — impersonal by design, for passing to an editor or agent. Per-person asks live in the individual outreach emails, not here.

**Working title:** *Between the Universe and the Metaverse: Surviving the Adaptational Squeeze*
**Adler Yang (楊逸帆)** · adler.yang@thesouledu.org · expanded from an MA thesis (Department of Philosophy, Soochow University, 2026) into a ~75,000-word manuscript · embargoed, pre-publication

---

**The argument.** Humanity is arriving at an unprecedented condition: living pressed between two background environments whose organizing principles share no common measure — the **universe** (the realist given that answers to no intention of ours) and the **metaverse** (not headsets or virtual worlds, but what artificiality *becomes* once it self-completes — a *second given*). Between them sits the **world**: the human-built habitat, an "adaptational operating system" that has always buffered us from the universe and is now being hollowed from within. What makes the metaverse a *different kind* of given is that its organizing principle won't hold still: where the universe's is relatively constant — slow enough to learn and adapt to — the metaverse's is opaque and perpetually self-rewriting, so the very ground one would study in order to live well keeps mutating as we build more artificiality (an *anthropogenic three-body problem*, after Liu Cixin). This structural predicament — the **adaptational squeeze** — lies *beneath* the familiar debates over governance, ownership, liberty, and AI alignment, which is why naming it changes what those debates are about.

**Why it wants to be a book.**
- It **names a condition the current debates don't** — one structural diagnosis under the scattered anxieties about AI and the "metaverse," rather than another commentary on them.
- Its method is a genuine **East–West synthesis** — critical realism (Bhaskar) welded to Xunzi's 偽 (*the made*, not the merely fake), Needham's comparative history, and Japanese synthetic anthropology — a cross-traditional ontology, not a Western frame with an Eastern garnish.
- It is **timely but built to outlast its examples**: today's systems (the agent-internet, the alignment problem, the AI-plus-big-data apparatus) appear as *instances* of a structural tendency, not as the subject.
- It has **crossover reach**, and ends constructively — turning the question from how to *escape* dependence to how to *attune* to it.

**Where it stands.** Just completed; the deposit is embargoed to preserve first-publication rights; being developed as a **scholarly trade (crossover) book**. The full manuscript is available on request — pre-publication, please treat as not for circulation.
