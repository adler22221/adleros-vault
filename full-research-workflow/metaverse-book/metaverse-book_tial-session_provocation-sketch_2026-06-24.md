# TIAL Fellows provocation — *Institutions between two givens*

Links: [[metaverse-book]] · folds in Geoff's reframe (institutions "like magic," new alienation) · constructive end from Conclusion §E (re-embedding / attunement / the four openings)

**Guiding question:** when a second, *self-rewriting* given takes over what institutions used to do, can we design institutions that **re-embed** us in what we depend on rather than **offload** it?

**Format:** ~10 min spoken (one of 2–3 provocations), then group discussion. Talking-point outline — expand in your own voice. Keep it actualism-safe: define the metaverse by the *real* (a tendency, not today's machines); gravity = the **universe's** constancy, never the metaverse's. Lead with the institutional stakes; hold the CR machinery (Dao / Li / Xiang, 偽境) in reserve unless asked.

---

### 0 · Open on Geoff's image (~1 min)
Take his handoff directly: institutions, like our technologies, becoming "like magic — impossible to explain or understand," breeding new alienation. Agree — and offer that the alienation has a *cause we can name*, and that naming it changes what we'd do about it.

### 1 · The fork: representation, or a second reality? (~2 min)
The instinct is to treat this second world as a *representation* or imagined variant of the real one — a model, a mirror, a simulation. Resist that. What I call the **metaverse** — not headsets or XR, but what artificiality becomes once it *self-completes* — isn't a picture of the world; it's a second **given**: a real layer with its own causal grip that we increasingly must adapt to the way we adapt to the universe.
- **Why the fork decides everything:** if it's a representation, the cure is *transparency* — explain the magic, open the black box. If it's a second reality we depend on, transparency isn't enough — you can't explain your way out of a given you're embedded in. The task becomes *re-embedding*.

### 2 · The twist that makes it hard (~2 min)
The universe is a given too — but a **constant** one: gravity doesn't change, so we could build institutions, sciences, and cultures that slowly attuned to it. This second given's organizing principle won't hold still: it self-rewrites faster than we can study or govern it — a kind of **anthropogenic three-body problem** (after Liu Cixin). So institutions are caught between two givens — one stable, one mutating — and the old move of "study the ground, then build on it" stops working.

### 3 · The stakes for institutions (~2 min)
Institutions were how we buffered ourselves against the first given and stayed *answerable* to what we depend on — bodies, ecologies, one another. Now a second given absorbs those very functions — judging, coordinating, remembering — and rewrites its own rules underneath us. The buffer is hollowed **from within**. That's Geoff's alienation — but *structural*, not just epistemic: we're estranged not only from understanding the apparatus, but from the things the apparatus now stands between us and.

### 4 · The design question — re-embedding / attunement (~2 min)
So: can we design institutions that **re-embed** rather than **dis-embed** — that keep people in working relationship with what they actually depend on, instead of offloading that dependence to a self-rewriting system? Four levels of opening (not a program — a menu to argue over):
- **Claim less** — institutions that don't try to absorb every function; that leave dependence visible and reciprocal.
- **Re-embed** — conviviality, commons, 共生 / permaculture-style design: keep the loop between us and what sustains us short and legible.
- **Keep the made from totalizing** — appropriate technology, polycentricity: stop any single order from becoming the only ground.
- **Hold open plurality** — technodiversity, the pluriverse, Audrey Tang's plurality: keep enough alternatives alive that the apparatus can't pre-empt the future.

The aim I call **attunement**: dependence organized well enough that life isn't squeezed between two givens at once.

### 5 · Hand to the group (~1 min)
Two seeds:
- Which of these openings is actually *tractable institutionally* — and which is wishful?
- Where does the "magic / alienation" bite hardest in your own domains — and is the right response there *transparency*, or *re-embedding*?
