# Publisher-readiness rubric (v2)
> Recreated 2026-06-12 for the pre-submission review run; the precise, scored companion to [[publishing-strategy]]. Applied result for this thesis is in [[master-thesis_editorial-review_2026-06-12]] §7 (verdict: proposal-ready now; full-MS ~3–4 months). Backlink: [[metaverse-book]].

**Purpose.** Grade the gap from *this draft* to a manuscript an acquisitions editor + peer reviewer would advance, calibrated per target press. Two gates: **proposal-ready** (proposal + 2 polished sample chapters) and **full-manuscript-ready**.

**Scale.** Per dimension score **Current 0–4** (anchors as in [[top100-rubric-v2_2026-06-12]]) and state the **Target** for the chosen press; the **Delta + the work that closes it** is the output.

| # | Dimension | Wt | Notes for this manuscript |
|---|---|----|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Big idea / carry** | ×3 | "The squeeze between universe and metaverse" — a thesis a reader carries. |
| 2 | **Significance & timeliness** | ×3 | Deep philosophy of technology on a live anxiety; stakes legible to an educated general reader. |
| 3 | **Originality / scholarly contribution** | ×3 | East–West synthesis (critical realism + Daoist/Buddhist/Xunzian + Needham) as a genuine differentiator within the Hui/Stiegler/Bratton lineage. |
| 4 | **Architecture & length** | ×2 | Monograph ~80–110k / crossover ~70–90k. Shape (hook-first opening; chapter arc that reads, not defends) matters more than size. |
| 5 | **Register & readability** | ×2 | Public-facing rigor — remaining: shed thesis scaffolding ("this thesis", "in Chapter X", lettered sub-heads, hedging). |
| 6 | **Evergreen framing** | ×1 | Frame topical examples (agent-internet, named platforms) as instances of a structural tendency. |
| 7 | **Comp-title positioning** | ×2 | Differentiation vs Hui, Bratton (*The Stack*), Floridi, Rosa, Crawford (*Atlas of AI*), Vallor — stated, not implied. |
| 8 | **Author platform / credential** | ×1 | MA→book is the structural headwind; mitigate via 1–2 journal placements + the award + the supervisor's standing. |
| 9 | **Apparatus & rights** | ×1 | Notes; permissions; **deposit embargo (延後公開)**; English-first w/ later Chinese edition? |

**Per-press calibration** (different identities → different bars):
- **MIT Press** — crossover big-idea, the natural home; inquiry-first email, then ~¼ of the work on invitation; bar = idea + register + comp fit.
- **Polity** — Stiegler's/Rosa's English publisher; accessible-academic, fast, global; the register is already theirs; lowest conversion risk.
- **Minnesota *Posthumanities* (Cary Wolfe)** — best intellectual home for the nature/biosemiotic/ontology material; wants ≥2 sample chapters + CV; **discourages unrevised dissertations** → the proposal must narrate the rework.
- **punctum** — OA, antidisciplinary; completed MS + brief proposal as one PDF; narrow May 1–Jul 31 window (verify current-year status); lower prestige/reach, fastest to an open book, best voice match.

**Reporting.** A Current/Target/Delta table for the most-likely press (MIT crossover benchmark) + a one-line read per alternative; an explicit verdict on each gate; the **top 5 gap-closing moves in priority order**; and the campaign path (embargo at deposit → de-scaffold Intro + Ch VI → proposal to MIT + Polity in parallel → full-MS pass + 1–2 journal placements → punctum/Minnesota as routes). Distinguish "the idea is book-worthy now" from "the manuscript is submission-ready."

**Why v2 is more precise than the strategy notes:** numeric Current/Target/Delta scoring with weights; two explicit readiness gates; and per-press bars rather than a single list — so the gap is actionable and sequenced.
