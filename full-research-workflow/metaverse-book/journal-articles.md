# Chapters → journal articles — working map

> AI-draft (2026-06-10). Recommendations from knowledge of journal scopes — **verify current aims/scope, word limits, and competitiveness before submitting**; aim a couple realistically + one stretch. A self-contained article ≈ 7–10k words, one contribution. Build on this freely.

## Principles
- Extract arguments that **stand alone** without the whole apparatus.
- Balance **novelty / track-record** vs **book-overlap** (publishing the book's spine as an article overlaps most; introducing a discrete sub-argument overlaps least).
- Manage overlap: make each article self-contained; the book later incorporates a **substantially revised** version and **cites the article** — presses accept this when revision is real.
- Realism: peer review is largely credential-blind, so it's about quality; still, aim a mix of **realistic homes** + one **stretch**.

## TOP PICKS (do these first — high novelty, low book-overlap, clear homes)

### A. Uegaki & the self-finishing society — **from Ch IV**
- **Argument:** introduce Uegaki Takahide's diagnosis to English readers — the *as-intended life* (意のままになる生) vs the anti-intended; the social apparatus; *self-finishing* (自己完結化); the **two engines** (soteriological/Platonic + instrumental/Aristotelian) and their convergence; domination/liberation as two sides of one coin.
- **Why strong:** introduces an under-translated Japanese thinker → clear scholarly contribution with its own audience; the most *separable* from the book's metaverse spine.
- **Angle/title:** "The As-Intended Life: Uegaki Takahide's Self-Finishing Society and the Two Engines of Modernity."
- **Journals:** *Philosophy East and West* (flagship, stretch) · **European Journal of Japanese Philosophy** (ideal home) · *Comparative and Continental Philosophy* · social-theory angle: *Thesis Eleven*, *European Journal of Social Theory*.
- **Book overlap:** moderate; the "introducing Uegaki" framing keeps it distinct.

### B. Dao / Li / Xiang ↔ critical realism — **from Ch I §E (+ method)**
- **Argument:** a critical-realist, East-Asian *stratified* ontology of worlds-as-**adaptational operating systems**; the 道/理/相 = real/actual/empirical reinterpretation; complex-adaptive-systems / self-organization grounding.
- **Why strong:** a discrete methodological/ontological contribution with an obvious home; the East-Asian rendering of CR is genuinely novel.
- **Angle/title:** "Dao, Li, Xiang: An East-Asian Stratification for Critical Realism" (or "Worlds as Adaptational Operating Systems").
- **Journals:** **Journal of Critical Realism** (prime home) · *Philosophy East and West* · *Dao: A Journal of Comparative Philosophy*.
- **Book overlap:** low–moderate (foundational, but the CR-rendering is a clean standalone).

## STRONG SECONDARY CANDIDATES

### C. Artificiality, not technology: virtuality + the typology of the made — **from Ch II**
- **Argument:** why "artificiality" supersedes "technology"; **virtuality** as causally-efficacious nonmaterial (semiotic *genus* / biosemiotic *subset*, Uexküll + Peirce); the **typology** (register × aspect) and its new structural-virtual cell = **self-completion** (自足完成化); Hui's *preemption*.
- **Angle/title:** "Artificiality, Not Technology: Virtuality and the Typology of the Made" — or narrow to "Self-Completion: When the Made Becomes Structural."
- **Journals:** **Philosophy & Technology** (stretch) · *Techné: Research in Philosophy and Technology* · biosemiotic sub-angle: *Biosemiotics* · media-theory angle: *Theory, Culture & Society*, *Media Theory* (OA).
- **Book overlap:** moderate–high (central to the book).

### D. The squeeze hypothesis (flagship) — **from Ch V–VI + Conclusion**
- **Argument:** the core — caught between **two givens** (universe + metaverse); the four squeeze types; self-completion → a second given; the openings.
- **Why:** the calling-card piece that drives book interest — **but overlaps the book most.**
- **Angle/title:** "Between the Universe and the Metaverse: The Squeeze Hypothesis."
- **Journals:** **Theory, Culture & Society** (prestige, public-facing, stretch) · *AI & Society* (accessible, realistic) · *Philosophy & Technology* · *Angelaki* (continental theory) · *Media Theory* (OA).
- **Book overlap:** HIGH. **Strategy:** either publish a *compact* version as the calling card (common, and it markets the book), or hold it for the book. Don't give the whole spine away in an article.

### E. The Enlightenment mode, the sovereign subject & the two fallacies — **from Ch III**
- **Argument:** why the modern Western mode universalized; the **sovereign subject** as one configuration; the ontic/epistemic fallacies + the primal squeeze (Bhaskar); the East–West **material conditions** (Needham, command-and-control, "why not China").
- **Angle options:** (i) "Universalization and the Sovereign Subject" (critical-realist genealogy); or (ii) split off the **Needham / material-conditions** comparison as its own STS piece.
- **Journals:** *Journal of Critical Realism* (the fallacies) · *Thesis Eleven* / *European Journal of Social Theory* (modernity/Enlightenment) · **East Asian Science, Technology and Society (EASTS)** (the Needham material — Taiwan-based, Duke UP; strong fit) · *Dao*.
- **Book overlap:** moderate.

### F. Harmony as squeeze-prevention / re-embedding (constructive coda) — **from Ch VI §H + Conclusion**
- **Argument:** the normative openings — harmony as the *prevention* of the squeeze; re-embedding (Illich conviviality, Hui technodiversity, permaculture, 和, Audrey Tang's plurality).
- **Angle/title:** "Harmony as the Prevention of the Squeeze: Re-embedding Technology."
- **Journals:** *Environmental Values* · *Worldviews: Global Religions, Culture, and Ecology* · *AI & Society* · *Techné*.
- **Book overlap:** low (constructive coda, less central).

## Suggested order
1. **A (Uegaki)** — highest novelty, lowest overlap, clean home. Start here.
2. **B (Dao/Li/Xiang ↔ CR)** — discrete methodological contribution; *Journal of Critical Realism* is a near-certain fit.
3. *Then* **E** (Needham → EASTS) or **C** (Philosophy & Technology) as the track-record grows.
4. **Hold or compact D** (the flagship squeeze) — most book-overlap; use only as a deliberate calling-card if going hard for the book.

## Caveats / to-do
- Confirm each journal's **current aims & scope, word limit, and reference style** before drafting.
- Articles run **1–2 years** to print; plan accordingly relative to the book and the thesis embargo.
- Decide how aggressively to publish the spine (D) given book-overlap.
