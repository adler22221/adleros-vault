## 2. The Human-Animal Differentiator: 加工の二重性 (Recursive Tool-Making)

Obara's foundational move is to locate the human-specific mode of world-construction in **recursive externalization** — what he calls **加工の二重性** (the duplicity / double-structure of fabrication). Animals use the immediate environment; only humans make *tools with tools*, fabricate fabricating-instruments, and thereby construct a second-order artificial layer that comes to mediate all subsequent interaction with the primary natural layer. [1 — UUID 7ef94356]

This recursive structure generates five nested consequences that, taken together, constitute the 人工生態系 (artificial ecosystem):

| # | Japanese term | Gloss | Thesis mapping |
|---|--------------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | 加工の二重性 | Recursive tool-making (tools made with tools) | What generates the `world` stratum in the first place |
| 2 | 人工食物連鎖 | Artificial food chain | Material infrastructure of the world-stratum |
| 3 | 自己人為淘汰 | Reflexive anthropogenic selection | Humans selected by rules of their *own* artificial system |
| 4 | サンゴ礁スケール超個体 | Coral-reef-scale superorganism | The collective organism = world-stratum at civilizational scale |
| 5 | 自己ペット化の軌道 | Trajectory toward self-petification | Terminal degradation when 2–4 are over-extended |

**Thesis use:** Obara's 加工の二重性 supplies the *genetic account* of what a human world is — an independently derived, biologically grounded argument that the human mode of existence requires constructing a secondary artificial stratum to be distinctively human at all. This complements Stiegler's 三次的把持 (tertiary retention / tertiary protention) and Searle's status-function ontology at the empirical/phenomenological level. Obara provides the *evolutionary-ecological* warrant.

## 3. The 人工生態系 (Artificial Ecosystem) as World-Stratum

Obara's **人工生態系** is not a metaphor but a technical concept: the totality of fabricated conditions — food chain, habitat, social regulation, symbolic order — within which the human organism is embedded and through which it reproduces itself. [3 — UUID 83918040]

Key features:
- **Second nature without remainder**: humans cannot exit the 人工生態系 without ceasing to be human in the full sense; it is constitutive, not optional
- **内なる自然 (inner nature)** as the biological heritage that the 人工生態系 must remain *adequate* to — Obara's criterion for a healthy vs. pathological ecosystem [UUID 7ef94356]
- **風土 (fūdo / climate-habitat)** as the locally differentiated, historically accumulated form that healthy 人工生態系 takes — parallels Watsuji's concept (see ANNOT-Watsuji, forthcoming); Obara's 風土 functions as a normative concept: a world that still articulates with inner nature
- **カプセル化 (capsulization)** as the pathological form: total sensory enclosure from natural fluctuation, severing the feedback loop between 内なる自然 and the artificial ecosystem

**Thesis use:** The 人工生態系 names *the world* in Obara's vocabulary. It is the `dr`-level real stratum (in Bhaskar's terms) that the thesis calls "world" — human-emergent but irreducible to individual minds, possessed of its own causal powers. The squeeze hypothesis operates *on* this stratum: the metaverse-layer's absorption of the 人工生態系's functions is precisely what Obara's framework predicts as the terminal direction of 自己ペット化.

## 4. 自己家畜化 vs. 自己ペット化: Spectrum, Threshold, and Three Crossing-Markers

### 4.1 The Value-Neutral Baseline: 自己家畜化 (Self-Domestication)

Obara's **自己家畜化** is explicitly **没価値的 (value-neutral)** — it is the *necessary condition* of being human, not a pathology. [UUID 4d5785ee; UUID 0604a0ec]

The domestication analogy: 庭イヌ (garden dog). A domesticated dog living in a yard: dependent on human provision, shaped by selective breeding, no longer a wild animal — but still physically active, behaviorally complex, in contact with ground and weather, capable of social friction and purposive movement. This is the normal human condition: irreversibly embedded in the 人工生態系, shaped by 自己人為淘汰, but *still adequate* to 内なる自然.

Markers of healthy 自己家畜化 (庭イヌ mode):
- Bodily engagement with tools (道具の体からの密着)
- Friction with other social actors (社会的摩擦の存在)
- Exposure to natural fluctuation, however mediated
- The 人工食物連鎖 remains *tractable* — the human can trace the chain

### 4.2 The Pathological Extreme: 自己ペット化 (Self-Petification)

**自己ペット化** is not simply "more domestication." It represents a **qualitative threshold crossing** — a state-change in the relationship between the organism and its 人工生態系. [UUID 7ef94356; UUID 36bd6af7]

The domestication analogy: 座敷イヌ (parlor / lap dog). An indoor lapdog: total enclosure, passive reception, atrophied motor-behavioral repertoire, zero contact with soil or weather, social experience entirely mediated by owner-provision. This is 自己ペット化: the organism is *cushioned to the point of atrophy*.

Obara identifies **three crossing-markers** that together constitute the threshold:

| Marker | Japanese | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | カプセル化 | Complete sensory enclosure from natural fluctuation — climate control, processed food, screened environments |
| 2 | 道具の体からの切断 | Severance of tool from body — push-button / screen operation replacing embodied skill; the hand loses its generative function |
| 3 | 社会的摩擦の滅菌化 | Sterilization of social friction — conflict management, algorithmic recommendation, frictionless UX removing all resistance from social interaction |

The three markers are **jointly sufficient**: when all three obtain simultaneously, the organism's 内なる自然 can no longer find adequate expression within the 人工生態系. The result: **転位行動 (displacement behavior)** — blocked natural drives finding distorted exits (compulsive gaming, parasocial attachment, chronic low-level anxiety, psychosomatic symptom clusters). [UUID 7ef94356]

### 4.3 Is It a Spectrum or a Binary?

The relationship between 自己家畜化 and 自己ペット化 is **both**:

- **Spectrum in degree**: the three markers each admit of gradation; one can be partially capsulized, partially tool-severed, partially friction-sterilized. Individual and cohort variation is explicable on this axis.
- **Threshold in kind**: when all three markers cross their individual thresholds *simultaneously*, a **qualitative phase-shift** occurs — the organism's 内なる自然 can no longer adequately express within the 人工生態系. This is not just "more domesticated" but structurally different.

The spectrum/threshold duality maps directly onto the critical-realist stratification: the degree-of-crossing is an `da`/`de` (actual/empirical) phenomenon; the phase-shift in the organism's structural relation to its ecosystem is a `dr` (real) phenomenon. Geographic and cultural variation in *which* marker crosses first (カプセル化 in urban-Global-North contexts; 道具の体からの切断 in screen-first South Korean PC-bang ecology; etc.) does not vitiate the underlying mechanism.

## 5. Normative Stance and Corrective Vision

### 5.1 没価値的 and the Denial of Nostalgia

Obara's normative stance is **not** pre-modern nostalgia. The 没価値的 (value-neutral) characterization of 自己家畜化 is his anti-nostalgia move: he does not claim the pre-artificial was better. There is no exit from 人工生態系; the question is only whether the specific 人工生態系 remains adequate to 内なる自然. [UUID 4d5785ee]

**Thesis use (D2 anti-nostalgia safeguard):** Obara is explicitly immune to the "romanticized past" objection that committee member Ma flagged. 自己家畜化 is not lamentable; 自己ペット化 is pathological — but the distinction is functional (adequacy to inner nature), not temporal (now vs. then).

### 5.2 内なる自然 (Inner Nature) as Functional Criterion

The evaluative criterion throughout Obara is **内なる自然**: the biological heritage — evolutionary, developmental, somatic — that the 人工生態系 must remain adequate to if the organism is to remain fully functional. This is not a Romantic concept but a biological one: evolutionary time-scales of adaptation vs. the acceleration of artificial ecosystem change. [UUID 7ef94356]

**Thesis use:** 内なる自然 provides the *functional warrant* for the squeeze hypothesis's claim that real harm is occurring. It grounds the thesis against the objection "humans have always adapted to new artificial environments." Obara's answer: yes — but adaptation has time-scales. When the 人工生態系 changes faster than evolutionary adaptation can track, 内なる自然 is structurally mismatched. This is the squeeze at the Obara level.

### 5.3 風土 (Fūdo) as Normative Baseline Form

**風土** in Obara functions as the historically accumulated, locally differentiated *healthy* form of 人工生態系 — the form that has had time to co-evolve with 内なる自然 and local ecology. It corresponds to the world-stratum's full expressive capacity: the accumulated symbolic, institutional, and ecological resources through which the human mode of existence reproduces itself. [UUID 83918040]

Cross-reference: Watsuji's 風土 (in *A Climate*) uses the same term for the climate-habitat as the field of self-objectification of human existence — a convergence that strengthens the combined Obara-Watsuji citation.

### 5.4 Corrective Vision: 高次の自然さ and 第四世界

Obara's constructive response to 自己ペット化 is not to dismantle the 人工生態系 but to redirect it toward what he calls **高次の自然さ (higher-order naturalness)** — an artificial ecosystem that is *designed* to remain adequate to 内なる自然. This is operationalized in his concept of **フィールド / 第四世界** (field / fourth world): concrete proposals for re-embedding bodily skill, natural fluctuation, and social friction within a transformed 人工生態系. [UUID 7ef94356; UUID 36bd6af7]

**Thesis use (D3 novel contribution):** 高次の自然さ / 第四世界 anticipates what the thesis calls the *navigability* dimension — the possibility that the world-stratum can be restructured to resist squeeze-induced pathology. This is Obara's positive program, and it parallels Yuk Hui's 技術多様性 (technodiversity) / cosmotechnics as the corrective to algorithmic monoculture. Both provide the same structural move: not "reject the metaverse" but "insist on adequate multiplicity within it."
