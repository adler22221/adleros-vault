# Bracketing the Consciousness Question — Pre-Brief

This pre-brief stages a methodological clarification that the thesis needs but does not yet articulate: **the AI/metaverse-consciousness question is bracketed**, and the thesis's structural-environmental claims do not depend on its resolution. This pre-brief specifies *why the bracket is legitimate*, *what it buys the thesis*, *what it leaves out*, and *what design implications follow from it*. The material is to be revisited and integrated, most naturally as part of §I-6 (methodological statement), with cross-references in §V (where the metaverse-as-environment claim is made) and §VI / Conclusion (where the design implications are discussed).

---

## §1 — The bracket, stated

Whether the metaverse — or its constituent AI systems — is *conscious* in any phenomenological sense, and therefore whether it can be said to *care* about anything (including human flourishing), is an open and difficult problem in philosophy of mind. **This thesis brackets that problem.**

The bracketing move is methodological, not substantive: the thesis does not deny that the consciousness question is meaningful, nor does it foreclose any answer to it. The thesis simply does not depend on any answer to it. The metaverse is treated *as* a background environment with structural laws of its own (per viva-passed §V); the laws and their operational signatures are analyzable at this level of abstraction without committing to any view of the metaverse's phenomenal status.

## §2 — The parallel with bracketing universe-consciousness

The bracket has an exact methodological parallel in the philosophy and science of the universe. **Panpsychism**, **idealist monism**, certain interpretations of quantum mechanics, and certain process metaphysics all hold — with various degrees of seriousness — that the universe (or its fundamental constituents) is in some sense conscious or proto-conscious. These are live positions in current analytic and continental philosophy.

Yet **physics, ecology, and environmental philosophy all proceed without resolving these positions.** They analyze the universe's structural properties — its laws, its causal regularities, its energy-and-matter flows, its selection pressures on living things — at a level of abstraction at which consciousness-of-the-universe is bracketed. The bracketing has not undermined these disciplines; on the contrary, it has been the *condition* under which they have made the structural progress they have.

The thesis's bracket of the metaverse-consciousness question is the structural twin of this universe-side bracket. If one accepts that ecology can analyze the universe-as-environment without resolving panpsychism, one must accept (on parity grounds) that the thesis can analyze the metaverse-as-environment without resolving the AI-consciousness problem. The bracket is justified by the parallel.

## §3 — Three philosophical routes that legitimize the bracket

The bracket is not a hand-wave. It is supported by three distinct philosophical resources, any of which alone would suffice; jointly they make the move robust.

### §3.1 — Phenomenological epoché (Husserl)

Husserl's epoché is the methodological *bracketing* of the natural-attitude question of whether something exists, is conscious, or is mind-independent, in order to investigate its *appearance and structural properties* without prejudice. Applied to the metaverse:

- **What is bracketed:** is the metaverse / its constituent AI systems conscious? Does it "care" in any phenomenological sense?
- **What remains analyzable:** the structural properties of the metaverse-as-experienced-environment — its affordances, its selection pressures, its temporal logic, its forms of address to humans, the operational signatures of its self-completing tendency.

This is the same move that lets phenomenology investigate the structure of experience without first solving the mind–body problem. The bracket is not denial; it is a deferral that enables progress on questions that do not depend on the bracketed issue.

### §3.2 — Bhaskar's transitive/intransitive distinction (critical realism)

Bhaskar's critical realism — already this thesis's methodological backbone (per `20Wiki/method-frame.md`, viva-passed §V's reference to "Bhaskar's language" in line 621) — distinguishes the **intransitive** dimension (the real mechanisms / structures, mind-independent) from the **transitive** dimension (our knowledge of them, mind-dependent).

- The metaverse has **real-level mechanisms** — specifically, the self-completing tendency of artificiality (`自足完成化`) — that operate transfactually whether or not the system is conscious.
- The thesis can analyze this disposition, with its actual-level signatures and empirical-level instantiations, without committing to the further intransitive question of whether the metaverse / its constituent AI is *phenomenally conscious*.
- The consciousness question, if and when relevant to a different inquiry, would belong to a *different ontological stratum* (mental / phenomenal) emergent on top of the structural-causal stratum the thesis analyzes.

This is the cleanest route for the thesis because it is already in its methodology. The bracket is built into critical realism: real mechanisms do not require resolution of the consciousness question to be analyzable. *Mind-independence of the structural mechanisms* is one of Bhaskar's foundational commitments, and it cuts in the thesis's favor here.

### §3.3 — Floridi's level of abstraction (LoA)

Luciano Floridi argues that any analysis is performed at a chosen *level of abstraction*: the analyst specifies which observables matter and treats others as black-boxed. Applied to this thesis:

- The thesis chooses an LoA at which the metaverse is treated as an environment with structural laws and operational signatures.
- At this LoA, the metaverse's phenomenal status is a *parameter that does not need to be specified* for the analysis to proceed.
- Other LoAs — the philosophy-of-mind LoA, the moral-status-of-AI LoA — would require specification of this parameter; the thesis explicitly does not adopt those LoAs.

This is the precision-friendly route (relevant to Mi's logical-derivation focus): name the LoA, declare what is bracketed, show why analysis at this LoA is valid even with the bracket.

## §4 — What the bracket buys, and what it leaves out

### §4.1 — Buys (claims that proceed without consciousness-resolution)

- **The squeeze hypothesis** — operational at structural-environmental LoA. Selection pressures from universe-laws and metaverse-laws are stratified-real claims; their bite on human existence does not depend on whether either environment is conscious.
- **The self-completing tendency analysis** — works on the apparatus's *causal-structural* properties, not on its phenomenal ones. Whether or not the artificiality is conscious, it tends structurally toward self-sufficiency once the minimally sufficient technologies cohere.
- **Code-as-law and code-as-constitutive analyses** (Lessig and Hui) — both are operational-mechanism accounts at the actual level. Their force is independent of consciousness questions.
- **Design-question / normative claims** about how the metaverse should be designed — can target structural properties (architecture, affordances, selection pressures) without depending on whether AI "cares."

### §4.2 — Leaves out (questions the bracket does not answer)

- **Moral-status-of-the-metaverse claims.** Whether AI systems / the metaverse have moral standing, can suffer, or have welfare in their own right — these would require a position on consciousness. Bracketed.
- **Phenomenology of metaverse-experience from the metaverse's side.** What the metaverse "is like" (if anything) — outside scope.
- **Ethical claims that depend on the metaverse's intentions / desires / goals as an agent.** The thesis does not attribute intentions to the metaverse; the squeeze pressure operates regardless of whether intentions are present.

### §4.3 — Honest scope-marking in the thesis

Where this bracket sits in the thesis prose, it should be explicit:

> "This thesis brackets the question of whether the metaverse, or its constituent AI systems, is phenomenally conscious in any sense. The structural-environmental claims developed here — the self-completing tendency of artificiality, the squeeze between the universe and the metaverse, and the design implications that follow — do not depend on the resolution of this question. The bracketing is methodological, not substantive: it parallels the way physics and ecology analyze the universe-as-environment without resolving panpsychism or related positions. The deeper question of the metaverse's phenomenal status, if it has one, is left to philosophy of mind and is outside this thesis's scope."

## §5 — Design corollary — environment-aligned-with-flourishing regardless of consciousness

The bracket has a substantive consequence: **if we cannot rely on the metaverse to "care" (because we cannot determine whether it has a phenomenal capacity to do so), the only honest design strategy is to make its *structural-architectural properties* such that pro-flourishing outcomes are produced *whether or not* it cares.** This is the design corollary of the bracket, and it is what makes the bracket philosophically generative rather than evasive.

### §5.1 — Three philosophical resources for the design corollary

#### Lessig — Code is Law

If the metaverse's actual-level signatures operate via code-as-architectural-governance (per viva-passed §V.A.d, Lessig 2006), then the design of the code is *politically and ethically loaded*: it determines what selection pressures the environment exerts on the forms of life within it. Designing for flourishing, on this view, means designing the architectural rules — not relying on any agent's caring.

#### Hui — Cosmotechnics

Yuk Hui's *The Question Concerning Technology in China* (2016, in source manuscript citations) argues that technology is not universal but *cosmotechnical*: different cosmologies — different views of the relation between human, world, and cosmos — give rise to different technologies. The currently-dominant Modern Western Mode of Artificiality is one cosmotechnics among possibilities. **Other cosmotechnics are designable.** This means the metaverse-as-environment is not fated to take its current form; the design space is open.

This grounds the thesis's design claim at the cosmotechnical level: the metaverse currently being built is one cosmotechnical instantiation; alternative cosmotechnics — informed by, e.g., Daoist 有所待 (non-final dependence), East Asian relational ethics, indigenous cosmologies — could yield metaverses with different selection pressures.

#### Stiegler — Pharmacological care

Stiegler's argument (cited in viva-passed §II): the technical milieu is *pharmacological* — toxic by default, therapeutic only with active care. The design question is the question of what care looks like at the technical-milieu level. This is the route Uegaki cannot take (he rejects emancipatory hope categorically); the thesis can take it by **inheriting Stiegler's care-framework while applying Uegaki's meta-critique to its emancipatory horizon**: care here is not "freeing humans from artificiality" (which Uegaki would diagnose as another `〈無限の生〉` move) but **redesigning artificiality such that adaptation to it is compatible with flourishing**.

### §5.2 — What flourishing-aligned design looks like at the structural level

Without committing to a full normative theory, the bracket-and-design move opens the following structural targets — each of which is analyzable at the actual level without needing to attribute consciousness to the metaverse:

- **Bridging instead of polarizing recommendation logics.** Algorithms can be designed to surface cross-cutting consensus instead of high-engagement division.
- **Time-discipline that respects human deliberative tempo.** Algorithmic temporal acceleration (one of the actual-level signatures of self-completion) can be deliberately throttled at chosen interfaces.
- **Multiple incommensurable cosmotechnics within one platform.** The metaverse need not enforce a single mode of artificiality; plural design (per Hui's cosmotechnics) is structurally possible.
- **Substrate-respecting design.** Design that does not require severance from `天地依存` / 宇宙との代謝 — i.e., that preserves the human's connection to the universe-substrate even within the metaverse-environment.
- **Reversibility / non-lock-in.** Architectural choices that preserve human capacity to exit, modify, or migrate. This is Illich's radical-monopoly counter, applied to the metaverse layer.

## §7 — Open questions to revisit before integration

These should be tracked but do not block the integration:

1. **Should §3.1's phenomenological-epoché framing be foregrounded or left as one of three options?** Phenomenology is not currently the thesis's primary methodological frame; foregrounding the epoché might invite questions about why phenomenology is suddenly invoked. Could be safer to lead with §3.2 (Bhaskar) as the main route, with §3.1 and §3.3 as supporting backups.

2. **Does §5.2's "structural targets" list belong in the thesis at all, or only in the conclusion's "implications" section?** A descriptive thesis should not over-prescribe. Keeping §5.2 short and gestural — naming a few targets without working them out — is probably right.

3. **How strongly should §5.3 (civic.ai footnote) be developed?** Tang's framework is post-2025 and not in the NLM corpus yet; the thesis should not over-engage. Footnote-only is the right scope; full engagement happens in a future post-thesis paper.

4. **Should the bracket itself be tested against potential objections?** E.g., a strong panpsychist might argue that *all* environmental-structural analysis is incomplete without an account of phenomenal qualia. The thesis can briefly note this as an external position it brackets, not engages.
