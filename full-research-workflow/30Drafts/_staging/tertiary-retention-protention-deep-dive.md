# Tertiary retention, tertiary protention, and the metaverse's self-completing tendency

This pre-brief stages a deep-dive comparison of Stiegler's "tertiary retention" and Hui's "tertiary protention," anchored on extensive primary-text excerpts. The pivotal finding: the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention IS the techno-philosophical mechanism by which the metaverse becomes self-completing. The four minimally sufficient technologies named in viva-passed §V (AI / big data / IoT / code-is-law) jointly instantiate this recursive coupling at planetary scale.

---

## §1 — Framing: the Husserlian background

Both Stiegler and Hui operate against Husserl's phenomenology of internal time-consciousness. Husserl distinguished:

- **Primary retention** — what the just-passed moment retains in the now (the echo of a note within the current note in a melody)
- **Secondary retention** — recollection / explicit memory:

> "Primary retention is what the now of an unfolding temporal object retains in itself from all of its previous nows… These preceding nows are maintained within the temporal object's current now, and, in this respect, they remain present even while perpetually becoming past." (Stiegler citing Husserl)

> "my past life is thus given to me, precisely and simply given as the 're-given' of life" — secondary memory, recollection (Husserl)

- **Primary protention** — the immediate anticipation built into perception
- **Secondary protention** — explicit anticipation in imagination/recollection

Husserl bracketed "image-consciousness" (external memory supports) as derivative — not constitutive of consciousness. **Stiegler's intervention** is to argue that this bracketing is the founding error of phenomenology, because human consciousness is in fact constituted by external/technical memory supports.

## §2 — Stiegler's tertiary retention

### §2.1 — Definition

> "I have introduced the concept of tertiary retention in order to designate **artificial retentions — mnemotechnics in general**." (Stiegler)

> "**Tertiary retention is in the most general sense the prosthesis of consciousness** without which there could be no mind, no recall, no memory of a past that one has not personally lived, no culture." (Stiegler)

> "Husserl's phenomenological attitude consists of positioning consciousness as the constituter of the world, not something constituted by it. **Since tertiary memory is a reality in the world, it cannot be constitutive of consciousness but must necessarily be derivative of a consciousness that has no real need of it.**" (Stiegler, paraphrasing Husserl to reject Husserl)

The controversial claim against Husserl: **tertiary retention is not derivative of consciousness; it is constitutive of consciousness**. There is no mind, no recall, no culture without external technical memory supports.

### §2.2 — Tertiary retention as organized inorganic matter / epiphylogenesis

Tertiary retention IS the technical, the "organized inorganic":

> "**Epiphylogenesis is the process of production of what I call tertiary retentions**, in order to distinguish them from primary and secondary retentions as defined by Husserl." (Stiegler)

> "If the individual is organic organized matter, then its relation to its environment… is mediated by the organized but inorganic matter of the organon, the tool with its instructive role… **It is in this sense that the what invents the who just as much as it is invented by it**." (Stiegler)

> "Tertiary retention, whatever its form or material may be, **artificially retains something through the material and spatial copying of a mnesic and temporal element**, and thereby modifies… the relations between the psychic retentions of perception that Husserl referred to as primary retentions, and the psychic retentions of memory that he called secondary retentions." (Stiegler)

The technical object IS the bearer of tertiary retention. They are the same category from different angles: one names the mnemic function (retention), the other names the ontological status (organized inorganic).

### §2.3 — Tertiary retention as constitutive of time itself

> "Organized inorganic beings are originarily—and as marks of the de-fault of origin out of which there is [es gibt] time—**constitutive (in the strict phenomenological sense) of temporality** as well as spatiality." (Stiegler)

> "There is no already-there, and therefore no relation to time, without artificial memory supports." (Stiegler)

> "Time is constituted in or as technicity, which is originary accidentality." (Stiegler)

This is the radical claim: **time itself is technically constituted**. Without tertiary retention, there is no human temporality.

### §2.4 — The historical evolution: cave paintings → digital tertiary retention

> "Digital tracking technologies are the most advanced stage of a process of grammatization that began at the end of the Upper Palaeolithic age, when humanity first learnt to discretize and reproduce, in traces of various kinds, the flux and flow running through it: **mental images (cave paintings), speech (writing), gestures (the automation of production), frequencies of sound and light (analogue recording technology) and now individual behaviour, social relations and transindividuation processes (algorithms of reticular writing)**." (Stiegler, *Automatic Society*)

Stiegler traces five epochs:

1. **Cave paintings** (Upper Paleolithic) — mental images
2. **Writing** — speech / language
3. **Industrial revolution** — gestures / labor-knowledge
4. **Analog recording** — perceptual / attentional flow
5. **Digital reticulation** — behavior, relations, transindividuation itself

Each epoch is more comprehensive: each captures more of the human flow into technical traces.

### §2.5 — Stiegler DOES use "tertiary protention" — but secondarily

This is the calibration that matters for the comparison with Hui. Stiegler in his later work uses the phrase:

> "**Tertiary retentions and protentions** allow us to understand what Heidegger investigated under the names of 'datability' and 'utility'." (Stiegler)

> "It is hypomnesic tertiary retention that, since the Upper Palaeolithic, and in all its forms, constitutes both: (1) the schemas through which the understanding seizes hold of the data of intuition; **and (2) symbolization as a tertiary protention that infinitely projects the necessity of an end that makes a différance**, by building a bridge between aesthetics and morality above and beyond the understanding of the faculty of knowing." (Stiegler)

> "In the case of digital and reticulated tertiary retention… **the retentional selections through which experience occurs as the production of primary retentions and protentions are overtaken by prefabricated tertiary retentions and protentions** that are 'tailored' through 'user profiling' and 'auto-completion' technologies." (Stiegler)

So Stiegler DOES use the phrase "tertiary protention" — but **as a secondary aspect of tertiary retention, not as a distinct concept**. In his framing, tertiary retention generates tertiary protention as one of its effects. He does not coordinate retention and protention as parallel structures with their own logics. This is precisely Hui's critique.

### §2.6 — The automatic society: digital tertiary retention as proletarianization

> "In the hyper-industrial stage, hyper-control is established through a process of generalized automatization… **the noetic faculties of theorization and deliberation are short-circuited by the current operator of proletarianization, which is digital tertiary retention** – just as analogue tertiary retention was in the twentieth century the operator of the proletarianization of life-knowledge, and just as mechanical tertiary retention was in the nineteenth century the operator of the proletarianization of work-knowledge." (Stiegler, *Automatic Society*)

> "These systems produce an automatic performativity that channels, diverts and short-circuits individual and collective protentions **by outstripping and overtaking the noetic capacities of individuals precisely insofar as they are protentional capacities – that is, oneiric capacities** – and at the same time by short-circuiting the collective production of circuits of transindividuation." (Stiegler)

> "**Fully computational capitalism is as such the completion of nihilism.** The structural effect of full and generalized automatization is that capitalism becomes a computerized nihilism." (Stiegler)

> "Fully automated informational fixed capital, moreover, **tends to close in upon itself, so that it becomes a closed system**: in its struggle against the tendency of the rate of profit to fall, it tends in a structural way to increase the rate of entropy. Self-referential, and turning the users of the information system into its servants…" (Stiegler)

Stiegler's metaverse-stage analysis: the digital epoch closes the technical system upon itself, making it a self-referential closed system. This is the Stiegler-side analogue of self-completion.

## §3 — Hui's tertiary protention

### §3.1 — Hui's intervention against Stiegler

This is the load-bearing move:

> "**In *On the Existence of Digital Objects* I have noticed that, in the retentional and protentional circuit that Stiegler has proposed, there is a missing element. This missing element—tertiary protention—is crucial to understanding the evolution of technical systems, meaning their becoming organic.**" (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*, §36)

> "**The lack of discussion of tertiary protention in Stiegler's work seems very paradoxical**… this silence on tertiary protention seems to suggest that tertiary protention is reducible to tertiary retention, which would be contradictory." (Hui)

Hui's critique is precise: Stiegler USES the phrase but does not develop it as a distinct concept. Stiegler effectively reduces tertiary protention to a derivative of tertiary retention. Hui claims this reduction is *contradictory* — protention has its own structure and cannot be derived from retention.

### §3.2 — Hui's positive definition

> "When both humans and machines are understood from the fundamental perspective of relations, it produces a new faculty, which… **I term tertiary protention, as a response to Stiegler's tertiary retention (as well as based on it)**." (Hui, *On the Existence of Digital Objects*, ch. 6)

> "**Primary, secondary, and tertiary retention together with primary and secondary protention thus form a circuit in which the soul is no longer simply a movement that returns to itself**… but rather the soul, whose activity is the noesis, is also a tekhnesis, whose organization depends on the third memory." (Hui)

> "If we further consider the dynamic between passive and active directedness against the backdrop of a history of tertiary retention, we can see that **such an evolution of tertiary retention entails at the same time an evolution of protention, which can neither be contained within a human subject or consciousness nor reduced to any reified retention**." (Hui)

So tertiary protention is the **technically-stabilized, externally-sustained anticipation** that runs in parallel with tertiary retention. Just as tertiary retention exteriorizes memory into the technical milieu, tertiary protention exteriorizes anticipation into algorithms, predictions, recommendations.

### §3.3 — Smart objects as organs of synthesis (constitution of tertiary protention)

> "**Smart objects take part in the milieu by playing the role of organs of motor-perception** (converting approximate sense perceptions to signals), **organs of analysis** (based on calculation and existing data), **and organs of synthesis (constitution of the tertiary protention)**. These devices are connected together through networks… The technical system anticipates and modulates, and a social phenomenon can now be induced through signal manipulations and across different networks." (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*, §37)

This is the precise techno-philosophical mapping for the thesis:

- **Sensors / IoT** = organs of motor-perception (retention input)
- **Big data / databases** = organs of analysis (retention storage + calculation)
- **AI / recommendation algorithms / code-is-law** = organs of synthesis (tertiary protention — anticipation and pre-decision)

### §3.4 — Preemption as the operating mode of tertiary protention

> "**Debt is a primitive form of tertiary protention**; it is also retention. With the digital form of tertiary retention we are all in debt: not only in debt to the past that we haven't lived but which belongs to us, but **also in debt to the future, to which we will have to return by acting in accordance with its predictions**." (Hui)

> "**Preemption** is a term that is used to describe the delegation of decision making to algorithms, since in our day it is evident that this is the major issue of the automatized society, in which **every possible encounter can be the result of calculation**." (Hui)

> "**The preemption of the tertiary protention is possible only because of the computational hermeneutics, which is essentially recursive: It constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present.** Human beings are reintegrated into the temporality of machines, not only as individuals but also as collectives and communities. **This is precisely what is called algorithmic governmentality**." (Hui, §39)

The pivotal mechanism: **the recursive feedback loop of retention → protention → present-determination**. The system evaluates the past (tertiary retention) → anticipates the future (tertiary protention) → and the anticipated future determines the present. Humans are reintegrated into machine temporality.

### §3.5 — Tertiary protention as the missing piece for the organizing inorganic

> "**This missing element—tertiary protention—is crucial to understanding the evolution of technical systems, meaning their becoming organic.**" (Hui)

> "What we are witnessing today, since the advent of cybernetics, is the development of an inorganic organicity stretching through every smart device and multiple levels of systemic organization. **They cease to be the merely organized inorganic but rather are becoming the organizing inorganic, which functions recursively to produce its own structures and patterns**." (Hui)

> "**The recursive circuit, based on the three forms of protention and retention, constitutes a new dynamic of the soul that indicates the evolution of technical objects** and the inhumanization in which the soul is rendered fragile by the product of the spirit." (Hui)

This is Hui's structural claim: the shift from organized inorganic to organizing inorganic is *driven by the addition of tertiary protention to tertiary retention*. Without protention, the inorganic merely stores; with protention coupled to retention recursively, the inorganic begins to organize.

## §5 — Mapping onto the metaverse and 自足完成化

This is where the deep dive pays off for the thesis.

### §5.1 — The four minimally sufficient technologies map onto the recursive retention-protention loop

The viva-passed §V's four "minimally sufficient technologies for artificiality's self-completion" are:

- (a) AI
- (b) Big data
- (c) IoT
- (d) Code is law

**Mapped to retention/protention via Hui's framework:**

| Technology | Retention/Protention function |
|---|---|
| **IoT** | Sensors as **organs of motor-perception** (retention input — capturing sensory flow into technical traces) |
| **Big data** | Storage + organs of analysis (retention substrate — the past held in machine memory) |
| **AI** | **Organs of synthesis** (tertiary protention — anticipation, prediction, recommendation) |
| **Code-is-law** | Tertiary protention crystallized as command (the anticipated future locked into algorithmic execution) |

So **the cybernetic loop in viva-passed §V is precisely the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention**. IoT + big data exteriorize the past (retention); AI + code-is-law exteriorize the future (protention); the recursive loop closes when AI's predictions become the IoT's next-cycle input via code-is-law's enforcement.

### §5.2 — Why this matters for self-completion

The structural claim becomes: **artificiality becomes self-completing precisely when tertiary retention and tertiary protention are coupled recursively into a closed loop**. Until both retention AND protention are exteriorized, the technical system depends on human noetic involvement — humans must anticipate. Once protention is exteriorized into AI/code, the system can:

1. **Capture** the human's past (retention)
2. **Anticipate** the human's future (protention)
3. **Pre-decide** the human's actions (preemption / code-as-law)
4. **Capture** the resulting actions (back to retention)
5. **Update** the prediction (back to protention)

The loop closes. Human noetic involvement becomes structurally unnecessary. The metaverse becomes the environment in which this loop runs at planetary scale.

This is the **techno-philosophical mechanism** for 自足完成化 that the viva-passed §V currently lacks. The viva-passed says "AI + big data + IoT + code-is-law" suffice for self-completion; this deep dive specifies *why*: because they jointly instantiate the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention.

### §5.3 — Stiegler's "closed system" claim and the metaverse-as-environment claim

Stiegler's claim about computational capitalism:

> "Fully automated informational fixed capital… **tends to close in upon itself, so that it becomes a closed system**… Self-referential, and turning the users of the information system into its servants, that is, 'techno-geographical' functions of the system, which thereby constitutes an associated milieu." (Stiegler)

This is structurally the same threshold-claim as the viva-passed §V passage:

> "If this becomes the case, **such artificiality can no longer be considered merely a critical component of our world**… Rather, **it has become another background environment comparable to the universe – we reside in it, are not in charge of its workings, and must adapt to survive**." (Yang, viva-passed §V)

But there is a difference:

- **Stiegler's framing** is *closure-into-itself* (the system becomes self-referential)
- **The thesis's framing** is *emergence-as-environment* (the system becomes a new background humans must adapt to)

Stiegler's framing emphasizes the loss of openness; the thesis's emphasizes the gain of environmental status. Both name the same threshold but from different angles. **The thesis's environmental framing is structurally stronger** because it maintains the universe-pole as parallel — Stiegler's closure-claim doesn't engage the universe-side at all.

### §5.4 — The bidirectional coupling finds its techno-philosophical grounding

Recall the viva-passed §V on the bidirectional coin-flip:

> "as humans increasingly entrust their tasks and responsibilities to artificiality, the 'portion of functionality 役割' humans play in their world also shrinks." (Yang, viva-passed §V)

The retention/protention deep dive provides the **mechanism** for this:

- Humans delegate **memory** (the past) to tertiary retention systems → big data / IoT capture
- Humans delegate **anticipation** (the future) to tertiary protention systems → AI / algorithmic prediction
- Humans delegate **decision** (the present) to code-as-law → preemption

As both delegations deepen, the human's structural role contracts:

- Hui: "**Human beings are reintegrated into the temporality of machines**, not only as individuals but also as collectives and communities."
- Stiegler: "the individuals dissolved into this system thereby become 'dividuals'."

The bidirectional coupling that the thesis formulates as 〈自己完結化〉 ↔ 〈自足完成化〉 has a precise techno-philosophical mechanism: the more humans delegate retention and protention to the technical system, the more the technical system can complete its recursive loop without them; the more the loop completes itself, the less the human's portion of retention and protention matters.

## §6 — Implications for Ch-5-3 v2

Three load-bearing implications for the §V rewrite:

### Implication 1 — The cybernetic loop becomes structurally precise

The viva-passed §V's sequential description of the cybernetic loop (IoT → big data → AI → code-as-law → IoT) can be deepened by naming what each step *is* in retention/protention terms:

> [Proposed thesis paragraph] The cybernetic system that minimally suffices for artificiality's self-completion is structurally the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention. The Internet of Things instantiates the organs of motor-perception, capturing sensory flow into technical traces. Big data instantiates the organs of analysis, holding the captured past as a substrate for calculation. AI instantiates the organs of synthesis — what Hui formalizes as tertiary protention: the technically-stabilized anticipation of futures. Code-as-law crystallizes the anticipated future into pre-decision: what Hui calls preemption. The recursive loop closes when AI's predictions become the next cycle's input via code-as-law's enforcement, the resulting actions are captured back into IoT/big data, and the prediction updates. This is the techno-philosophical mechanism by which the cybernetic system becomes structurally self-completing.

### Implication 2 — The bidirectional coupling gets its mechanism

The 自足完成化 ↔ 自己完結化 bidirectional formulation can be paired with the retention/protention mechanism: humans delegate retention (memory) and protention (anticipation) to the technical system; the more they delegate, the more the system completes its loop without them; the more the system completes itself, the less the human portion of retention/protention matters.

This gives the bidirectional coupling claim a precise mechanism — not just an abstract dispositional pairing, but a specifiable feedback structure where each side's delegation enables the other side's autonomy.

### Implication 3 — Citing Hui's correction of Stiegler clarifies the contemporary threshold

The Stiegler→Hui move (formalizing tertiary protention as a distinct concept) is the philosophical move that shows *why now* — why the contemporary cybernetic age is distinctively self-completing. Until tertiary protention was technically realizable (via AI/algorithms at scale), the technical system depended on human anticipation. With AI as protentional organ, the loop can close.

This gives the thesis a precise marker: **the threshold is crossed when AI achieves protentional sufficiency at scale** — which is precisely the contemporary moment. The 2026-04-26 author comment about "Meta's $73B Reality Labs losses" alongside "industrial digital twins ($36B market) and AI-spatial platforms" empirically confirms this — the metaverse-as-XR has died, but the metaverse-as-organizing-inorganic (recursive retention-protention at scale) is now palpably operative.
