## 3. One-sentence formulation per diagnosis

- **(A)** The metaverse has been defined through and constrained by the historical contingency of its naming origin (Stephenson's "beyond the universe"), so the inquiry inherits that origin's framing as if it were the metaverse's overall disposition — and thereby fails to grasp the disposition itself.
- **(B)** Inquiry into the metaverse has been carried out by treating it as one specimen of "technology" (alongside others such as XR or AI) and exploring its intersections with closed disciplinary issue-sets, so that even pluri-disciplinary work is absorbed into the (sub-)disciplinarization process — and the categorial-framing-level move that would let the metaverse appear as a structural reorganization of the universe–world relation, rather than as a specimen, becomes unthinkable.
- **(C)** Inquiry has been conducted in the actualistic mode (collapsing the real into the actual, in Bhaskar's sense), so that what can be evaluated is restricted to what has already been instantiated, manifested, or empirically realized — and the dispositional structure that precedes and exceeds those manifestations is systematically excluded.

## 4. Body anchor (single-opening structure)

All three diagnoses converge on a **single opening: dispositional / causal-power analysis**, which is Bhaskar's *real* domain.

This is what the bridge sentence at DOCX line 233 already declares:

> "I will not take a positivistic, disciplinary, and specializing approach. Rather, I will attempt to uncover the dispositions or causal powers of the underlying anthropological, social, and cultural conditions that enable the development of the metaverse."

(The word "positivistic" in this sentence becomes "actualistic" in the writing pass per §2.)

The three foreclosures are *not* three independent openings. They are three different ways of failing to enter the same opening. The body delivers that single opening through Bhaskar dispositions, Uegaki self-finishing, Obara's evolutionary anthropology, and the F1–F5 squeeze framework.

## 5. Philosophical pedigree mapping

**Cabrera DSRP+LMAO applies across all three diagnoses** — the failures are *profiles*, not 1:1 letter assignments. DSRP (Distinctions, Systems, Relationships, Perspectives) is the corrective framework; LMAO (Linear, Mechanistic, Anthropocentric, Overly-ordered) is the failure pattern it diagnoses.

| Diagnosis | Dominant LMAO emphasis | DSRP failure profile | Diagnosis-specific anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| (A) | Linear (origin → present arrow) + Overly-ordered (one trajectory privileged as defining) | weak P (only the historical-origin perspective); weak D (disposition not distinguished from manifestation) | Bhaskar 1975 — dispositions/causal powers exist independent of actualization |
| (B) | Mechanistic (specimen-among-specimens) + Anthropocentric (anchored to human-disciplinary divisions) | weak D *at the framing level* (mis-distinguishing metaverse as specimen of XR); weak S, R, P downstream | Morin 2008 — complex thought + transdisciplinarity; Muntouri 2013 (already in draft); Cabrera DSRP+LMAO |
| (C) | Mechanistic + Linear (causation read off manifested events only) | weak D (real / actual / empirical conflated — Bhaskar's epistemic fallacy); weak P (one ontological stratum recognized) | Bhaskar 1975 (RTS), 1979 (PoN); optional Derridean footnote on undecidable referents |

## 6. Diagnosis-specific unsayable claims

One concrete claim each diagnosis's foreclosure makes invisible. Use as the live test for whether the diagnosis is doing real work in the body chapters.

- **(A) unsayable:** "The metaverse's overall disposition includes becoming-all-encompassing and blurring the artificial/natural distinction — a tendency that cannot be read off its current XR instantiations."
- **(B) unsayable:** "The metaverse is not a specimen of technology to be analyzed by interlocking disciplines; it is a structural reorganization of the universe–world relation that *uses* technology as one of its substrates. Treating it as a specimen — even pluri-disciplinarily — forecloses the ontological claim the thesis needs to make."
- **(C) unsayable:** "The metaverse may have no stable referent yet — the dispositional structure precedes its empirical manifestation."

**Note on (B) vs (C):** The (B) unsayable operates at the categorial-framing level (specimen-or-not). The (C) unsayable operates at the ontological-stratum level (actualized-or-not). They are adjacent but distinct: demanding stable referents (C) is *one consequence* of the specimen-frame (B), but not the only one — the specimen-frame also forecloses cross-substrate, cross-domain reorganization claims that have nothing to do with referent stability.

## 7. Contemporary case mapping

Each diagnosis gets one concrete 2023–2026 case in addition to the existing draft citations. These are queued for insertion into the DOCX writing pass, not inserted in this lock.

### (A) Apple Vision Pro / "spatial computing" rebrand (2023–2026)
- **Existing in draft:** Stephenson 1992; Mystakidis 2022; Smart 2022; Floridi 2022; Lamina1
- **New case:** Apple deliberately refused the term "metaverse" at WWDC 2023 and re-baptized the same headset class as "spatial computing." The framing is path-dependent on Apple's own pre-existing assets — Vision Pro is positioned as a continuation of the iPhone/iPad/Mac lineage (apps floating in the room, FaceTime, Keynote), not as an entry into a shared persistent world. Q4 2025 production halted at ~45,000 units (down from 390,000 in 2024); the device is now repositioned for surgical training and corporate collaboration — absorbed into Apple's existing enterprise-device disposition.
- **Why it fits (A):** Path-dependency operates *even in the act of refusing* the metaverse label. Apple's refusal is not neutral — it forecloses the persistent-shared-world dimension and substitutes a personal-computing extension. The market collapse then *vindicates* the foreclosure: "metaverse-as-spatial-computing" was tested and found inert, but only one narrow framing was ever tested.
- **Citation candidates:** Cointelegraph June 2023 (WWDC framing); Apple Newsroom April 2024 (Vision Pro for business); Matthew Ball, "9 Takeaways After 6 Months" (matthewball.co/all/applevision2024); Hypebeast / MacDailyNews / FT / IDC, January 2026 (production halt).
- **Reservation:** Need one explicit sentence acknowledging that Apple *refuses* the metaverse label and arguing that the refusal is itself a path-dependent capture (re-narrating the same hardware class through prior brand DNA).

### (B) Klarin 2025 + *Technology in Society* 2025 (academia/industry/regulator gap)
- **Existing in draft:** Muntouri 2013
- **New case:** Klarin (2025, *Systems Research and Behavioral Science*, DOI 10.1002/sres.3209, "Metaverse and Artificial Intelligence as Web 4.0") and *Technology in Society* 2025 (S0160791X25000429, "Reconceptualizing the metaverse") together document that academia conceptualizes the metaverse through technical-functional criteria, industry treats it as a marketing buzzword tied to immersive entertainment, and regulators (EU AI Act, WEF) operate from a third frame focused on consumer-protection and AI-Act applicability. Each closed system produces internally coherent statements that cannot be evaluated from inside any other.
- **Why it fits (B):** This *is* the Muntouri "thoughts not being thought" structure, but the (B) cut is sharper than mere cross-discipline gap: Klarin and *Technology in Society* both explicitly observe that the metaverse-as-autopoietic-emergent-form *cannot appear within any single one of the three discourses* because each closes its own boundary conditions. Even when these closures interact, the metaverse remains an object-of-inquiry (a specimen) rather than a structural reorganization. The (B) unsayable (§6) is what becomes invisible across all three closures.
- **Citation candidates:** Klarin 2025; *Technology in Society* 2025 reconceptualization paper; Frontiers in Virtual Reality 2025 "Metaverse in industrial contexts"; *Digital Society* 2025 "Are We Ready for the Metaverse?"
- **Reservation:** Mi may flag this as sociology of knowledge, not philosophical foreclosure. Mitigation: the (B) unsayable in §6 is a concrete *philosophical* foreclosure (the structural-reorganization claim), not a sociological observation. The Klarin/Tech-in-Society case is the empirical anchor; the unsayable claim is the philosophical move that closes the diagnostic loop.

### (C) Meta Horizon Worlds collapse + AI pivot (2022–2026)
- **Existing in draft:** Meinlschmidt 2023 (cybersickness); DID infrastructure; Lamina1 use-case agnosticism
- **New case:** Meta rebranded Facebook to Meta in October 2021 around a metaverse vision derived from its prior business — a social-graph-and-ads platform reskinned as VR. By Q3 2024 executives had stopped saying "metaverse" on earnings calls. February 2026: Reality Labs announced Worlds would shift "almost exclusively mobile." March 2026: the VR platform itself was shut down (then partially walked back within 48 hours). Cumulative Reality Labs losses: $83.6B as of early 2026.
- **Why it fits (C):** Meta's actualistic inquiry — what counts as the metaverse is what is *currently* being instantiated as a VR ad-platform — could not survive the moment when the empirical manifestation failed. Rather than concluding that its actualistic framing was wrong, Meta concluded that the metaverse itself had failed. The dispositional structure (which the thesis argues *precedes* and *exceeds* any specific instantiation) was systematically invisible.
- **Citation candidates:** CNBC March 2026 "Meta is shutting down Horizon Worlds"; TechCrunch February 2026; ALM Corp report on $83.6B Reality Labs cumulative losses; Gizmodo 2026 (700 layoffs).
- **Reservation:** Risks reading as a cheap shot at Meta. Frame structurally: Meta's actualistic framing — not Meta as a corporation — is what the diagnosis indicts.
