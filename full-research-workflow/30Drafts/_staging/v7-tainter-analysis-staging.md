## §1 — Tainter's structural claim (NLM-grounded)

> "**A society has collapsed when it displays a rapid, significant loss of an established level of sociopolitical complexity**." (Tainter, *Collapse of Complex Societies*)

> "**Collapse is not a fall to some primordial chaos, but a return to the normal human condition of lower complexity.** The notion that collapse is uniformly a catastrophe is contradicted, moreover, by the present theory. **To the extent that collapse is due to declining marginal returns on investment in complexity, it is an economizing process.**" (Tainter)

> "**After a certain point, increased investments in complexity fail to yield proportionately increasing returns. Marginal returns decline and marginal costs rise.**" (Tainter)

The thesis's "worlds" as modes-of-artificiality map cleanly onto Tainter. Each world (Roman, Maya, Chacoan, Ming) had its own complexity-maintenance equation; when it went negative, the world collapsed as an economizing return to lower complexity.

**Critical asymmetry the thesis exploits:** every previous world had a *floor* — a lower-complexity vernacular substrate that survived collapse. Per Ch-5-5's analysis (Stiegler grammatization, Obara petification, Uegaki dilution of relationality, Illich radical monopoly, Hui dividuation), **the metaverse-stage actively destroys its own collapse-floor**. This is the thesis's structural opening not present in Tainter.

## §2 — The Tainter threshold for the metaverse (three coupled dimensions)

**Energy cost.** Tainter extends his argument to high-tech/scientific systems:

> "**As science progresses within any of its specialized branches**… there is a marked increase in the over-all resource-cost to realizing scientific findings of a given level [of] intrinsic significance… **Exponential growth in the size and costliness of science, in fact, is necessary simply to maintain a constant rate of progress**." (Tainter)

AI scaling laws (compute doubling every ~6 months for frontier models) fit precisely. The metaverse's energy cost curve is Tainter's marginal-return curve in real time.

**Tightly-coupled vulnerability.** Homer-Dixon sharpens Tainter:

> "today our global social, technological, and ecological systems are so tightly linked together, and they now operate at such velocity, that **the duration of any future breakdown or collapse is likely to be dramatically compressed**." (Homer-Dixon, *The Upside of Down*)

The metaverse's tight cybernetic coupling (AI ↔ data ↔ users ↔ infrastructure) means that when the threshold is crossed, collapse propagates fast.

**Cognitive bandwidth ceiling.** The metaverse imposes growing maintenance demands on human cognitive bandwidth — more platforms, more passwords, more recalibration, more algorithmic literacy required. This is Tainter's "investment in complexity" running on human cognitive capital rather than material capital.

## §3 — How the squeeze is experienced *because of* the Tainter threshold

The Tainter threshold transforms the squeeze from a structural-environmental claim (Ch-5-3 v3 §1.4: humans caught between universe and metaverse) into a *temporally accelerating pressure*.

**Three-pole squeeze under Tainter pressure:**

1. **Universe-side pressure** (constant): biological/physical adaptation demands remain constant; the universe does not relax its laws.
2. **Metaverse-side proletarianization** (increasing): as grammatization deepens (Ch-5-5), the human capacity for savoir-faire erodes, making it harder to meet universe-side demands without metaverse mediation.
3. **Tainter-side maintenance pressure** (increasing): as the metaverse approaches its complexity threshold, it demands MORE maintenance from humans even as it returns LESS productive value. Cognitive load increases. Temporal compression increases (algorithm half-lives shrink). Error rate increases (system bugs, data breaches, cascading failures).

**Sharpened claim for Ch-5-7 v2:** the squeeze, as experienced subjectively by humans approaching the Tainter threshold, is the simultaneous failure of universe-side adaptation (because human biological capacity has atrophied per Obara) AND metaverse-side maintenance (because complexity demands exceed available cognitive bandwidth). This is the *experiential phenomenology* of being squeezed between two real-level environments where one is approaching its complexity threshold.

## §4 — The possible collapse of the metaverse as a Tainter manifestation

**Tainter's predictive claim:**

> "**Peer polities then tend to undergo long periods of upwardly-spiraling competitive costs, and downward marginal returns**… Collapse, if and when it comes again, **will this time be global. No longer can any individual nation collapse. World civilization will disintegrate as a whole.**" (Tainter)

> "**If civilization collapses again, it will be from failure to take advantage of the current reprieve, a reprieve paradoxically both detrimental and essential to our anticipated future**." (Tainter)

**Specific mechanisms by which metaverse collapse could occur:**

| Mechanism | Trigger | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Energy threshold breach** | AI training + inference compute exceeds grid capacity or energy ROI | Cascade: AI services → financial systems → logistics → food/water infrastructure |
| **Marginal-return inversion** | Metaverse maintenance cost exceeds productive value for majority of users/firms | Voluntary withdrawal, then forced fragmentation |
| **Synchronous infrastructure failure** | Tight-coupling propagates a localized failure (power, comms, finance, logistics) | Homer-Dixon's "deep collapse" — system's ability to regenerate is lost |
| **Recursive loop instability** | Adversarial inputs / model collapse / data poisoning destabilize the retention/protention loop | Cybernetic system can no longer close its own loop |

**Homer-Dixon's "deep collapse":**

> "The longer a system is 'locked in' to its growth phase, **the greater its vulnerability and the bigger and more dramatic its collapse will be**. If the growth phase goes on for too long, '**deep collapse**'—something like synchronous failure—eventually occurs. Collapse in this case is so catastrophic and cascades across so many physical and social boundaries that **the system's ability to regenerate itself is lost**." (Homer-Dixon citing Holling)

The thesis: the metaverse-stage destroys the floor that would make Tainter's "economizing return" possible. Without the floor, metaverse collapse is Homer-Dixon's "deep collapse."

## §5 — Consequences of metaverse collapse for humans

**The Tainter floor claim (applies to all prior collapses):**

> "Remaining populations must become locally self-sufficient to a degree not seen for several generations. Groups that had formerly been economic and political partners now become strangers, even threatening competitors. **The world as seen from any locality perceptibly shrinks**, and over the horizon lies the unknown." (Tainter)

The phrase "to a degree not seen for several generations" *concedes* that some degree of local self-sufficiency was preserved. Roman peasants still knew how to farm. Maya commoners still had subsistence knowledge.

**The regeneration-base claim (Schwartz & Nichols):**

> "**It is likely that a crucial factor in regeneration is the survival of institutions or ideas from the era before collapse, supplying a base for the eventual re-creation of complex societies**: the chances are good that regenerated complex societies did not have to totally 'reinvent the wheel,' literally or otherwise." (Schwartz & Nichols)

**The thesis's novel claim:** the metaverse-stage is the first "world" whose collapse cannot be Tainter's "economizing return to lower complexity" — because the lower-complexity capacity has been structurally dispossessed through proletarianization/petification/dilution-of-relationality. A metaverse collapse would be Homer-Dixon's "deep collapse," specifically because the human-side adaptation to the metaverse has destroyed the human-side adaptation to the universe.

**After metaverse collapse, the squeezed human faces:**
- Loss of metaverse mediation (the environment that was mediating survival is gone)
- Atrophied universe-side adaptation capacity (savoir-faire destroyed by grammatization)
- No vernacular floor (Illichean subsistence skills, embodied relational knowledge systematically dispossessed)
- Loss of the regeneration-base (no pre-metaverse institutions survive intact — they were absorbed into the cybernetic apparatus)

This is *not* Tainter's economizing return. It is structural dispossession.

## §6 — Comparison with Obara + collapse literature

| Theorist | Mechanism | Scope | What it adds for thesis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tainter (economic-administrative) | Marginal-return curve goes negative | Civilizational | Specifies *why* worlds collapse; the metaverse's energy/cognitive threshold |
| Obara (biological-evolutionary) | Self-petification → loss of adaptive capacity | Species-evolutionary | Specifies *what happens to humans* biologically when they adapt to metaverse |
| Homer-Dixon (systemic-cybernetic) | Tightly-coupled system propagates synchronous failure | Global infrastructure | Specifies the *speed and irreversibility* of metaverse collapse |
| Schwartz & Nichols (regeneration) | Regeneration depends on surviving pre-collapse institutions | Civilizational | Specifies what the metaverse destroys that prior worlds preserved |
| Diamond (multi-causal) | Five factors including response capacity | Case-by-case | The response-capacity (Diamond's fifth factor) is itself dispossessed |

**Convergent thesis claim:** the metaverse-stage is uniquely catastrophic-prone because every distinct vulnerability the collapse literature identifies is intensified by the same self-completing mechanism:
- Tainter's complexity threshold → AI scaling accelerates the threshold approach
- Homer-Dixon's tight coupling → recursive cybernetic loop is maximally coupled
- Obara's biological dispossession → petification is the biological face of grammatization
- Schwartz & Nichols' regeneration-base → vernacular floor destroyed by radical monopoly (Illich) + proletarianization (Stiegler)
- Diamond's response capacity → algorithmic governmentality short-circuits deliberation (Hui/Stiegler)
