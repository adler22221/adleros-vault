## The open question

The introduction currently sketches **three diagnoses** that explain why the metaverse has been misunderstood or misdefined:

1. **(i) Path dependency** — prior dispositions shape what counts as the metaverse by default
2. **(ii) Disciplinary reductionism** — single-discipline frameworks (tech, economics, media) fail to capture the ontological dimension
3. **(iii) Positivistic boundary-setting** — inquiry is constrained to empirically realized examples, missing the "yet-to-be-realized" nature of the metaverse

**The question is not:** are these three wrong?  
**The question is:** should they be reframed, merged, split, or renumbered? Final count could be 2 / 3 / 4 / other.

## Current state of each diagnosis (T1/T3 assessment — to be stress-tested by author)

### (i) Path dependency
**Where it does work:**
- Introduction's retrospective: Meta's "metaverse as VR extension of social media" is path-dependent on its advertising model
- §IV: "fully actualized" critique assumes a non-path-dependent understanding of what metaverse *could* be
- Introduction-4 contemporary-evidence: the entire failure of Horizon Worlds is a path-dependency story

**Risk:** This is the most empirically robust of the three but may be the weakest *philosophically* — path dependency is a well-known concept in STS/innovation studies that doesn't necessarily require Illich, Bhaskar, or Uegaki. Could be folded under disciplinary reductionism.

**Verdict to test:** Keep as distinct? Or subsume under (ii)?

---

### (ii) Disciplinary reductionism
**Where it does work:**
- §I: framing of why existing frameworks miss the ontological squeeze
- §III: the technosphere section's critique of tech-determinism and economics-first approaches
- §VI: why the squeeze cannot be captured by alienation / enframing / grammatization alone (anti-metaphorical safeguard)

**Risk:** This is the most *philosophically* load-bearing. The thesis's entire trans/antidisciplinary positioning depends on naming what single disciplines miss.

**Verdict to test:** Likely to stay. May need more precise naming — is it "disciplinary reductionism" or "category-boundary imposition" or "ontological flattening"?

---

### (iii) Positivistic boundary-setting
**Where it does work:**
- §I: the "yet-to-be-realized" character of the metaverse
- §II: universe/world/metaverse ontology as structural claim, not contingent on empirical success
- A.2 and A.3 reframe: the failure of Horizon Worlds *confirms* the diagnosis (the metaverse-as-VR was always a positivistic misconstrual)

**Risk:** "Positivism" is a contested term (logical positivism ≠ general positivism ≠ STEM positivism). The thesis needs to name precisely which version it is diagnosing. May need to be renamed.

**Alternative framings tested in T3 scaffolds:**
- "Empiricist foreclosure" — limits the metaverse to what can currently be instantiated
- "Realizability bias" — assumes something is only real when technologically achieved
- "Actualization fallacy" — conflates "actualized" with "real" (connects to §IV's "fully actualized" response)

**Verdict to test:** Probably keep the diagnosis, but the *name* "positivistic boundary-setting" may need refinement.

## Possible reframings (for author decision)

### Option A: Keep three, refine names
- (i) **Path-dependency constraint** — historical/dispositional framing carried over from prior technological paradigms
- (ii) **Disciplinary reductionism** — single-discipline ontologies flatten the metaverse's irreducibly multi-level character
- (iii) **Realizability bias** — the metaverse is only recognized as real when empirically actualized; structural-dispositional claims are excluded

**Pro:** Closest to current draft; least disruption to body chapters.  
**Con:** (i) and (iii) are related — both concern the limits of empirical/historical framing.

---

### Option B: Collapse to two
- (i) **Dispositional-structural misrecognition** — combines path-dependency + positivistic boundary-setting: the metaverse's yet-to-be-realized structural features are systematically excluded by dispositionally biased and empirically limited inquiry
- (ii) **Ontological flattening** — disciplinary reductionism: single disciplines impose category boundaries that make the three-level ontology (universe/world/metaverse) invisible

**Pro:** Sharper; easier to map contemporary evidence onto two clean categories.  
**Con:** Loses the distinction between "we carried over old frames" (path-dependency) and "we can't see beyond the empirical" (positivism).

---

### Option C: Expand to four
- (i) Path dependency (dispositional/historical)
- (ii) Disciplinary reductionism (epistemological)
- (iii) Realizability bias (ontological/empiricist)
- (iv) **Anti-ontological pragmatism** — the metaverse is only evaluated on functional/economic terms, making structural-ontological claims seem irrelevant

**Pro:** (iv) is genuinely distinct and maps well onto contemporary corporate discourse.  
**Con:** (iv) may be a subspecies of (ii), not a separate diagnosis. Adds complexity without clear payoff.
