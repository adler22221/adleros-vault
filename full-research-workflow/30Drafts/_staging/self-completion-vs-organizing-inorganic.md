# 自足完成化 vs. organizing inorganic — comprehensive comparison

This pre-brief stages the comparison between the thesis's named real-level meta-law (Yang's 自足完成化) and the closest existing analogue in contemporary technology-philosophy (Hui's "organizing inorganic"). The comparison is structurally important for Ch-5-3 v2 §1 because the convergence is significant (independent diagnosis of the same threshold from two distinct philosophical lineages) AND the divergence is non-trivial (Hui's organicist-fused ontology vs. Yang's environmentalist-stratified ontology). Both findings affect how the thesis should position itself.

---

## §1 — The conceptual chain: Stiegler's "organized inorganic matter" → Hui's "organizing inorganic"

Hui's coinage extends an existing conceptual chain in technology-philosophy. The chain runs:

**Bertrand Gille → André Leroi-Gourhan → Bernard Stiegler → Yuk Hui**

### Stiegler's "organized inorganic matter" — the inherited foundation

Stiegler (*Technics and Time, 1*) names technical objects as a "third genre of being" between inorganic minerals and organic life:

> "Between the inorganic beings of the physical sciences and the organized beings of biology, there does indeed exist a third genre of 'being': '**inorganic organized beings**,' or technical objects. These nonorganic organizations of matter have their own dynamic when compared with that of either physical or biological beings, a dynamic, moreover, that cannot be reduced to the 'aggregate' or 'product' of these beings." (Stiegler)

> "The singularity of the relation lies in the fact that the inert, although organized, matter qua the technical object itself evolves in its organization: it is therefore no longer merely inert matter, but neither is it living matter. **It is organized inorganic matter that transforms itself in time as living matter transforms itself in its interaction with the milieu**." (Stiegler)

> "**This inorganic matter organizes itself.** In organizing itself, it becomes indivisible and conquers a quasi-ipseity from which its dynamic proceeds absolutely: the history of this becoming-organic is not that of the humans who 'made' the object." (Stiegler)

> "Simondon analyzes the industrial stage in which conditioning and evolution are brought into play by a dynamism proper to the technical object **and independent of all human intention**—the technical object being of organized inorganic matter, **tending toward naturalization**." (Stiegler)

> "In the industrial age, **the human is not the intentional origin of separate technical individuals qua machines. It rather executes a quasi-intentionality of which the technical object is itself the carrier**." (Stiegler)

The structural arc Stiegler traces: technics begins as organized-by-humans, but in the industrial stage the technical system acquires "a genetic logic that belongs to itself alone." The technical object's dynamic operates *independently of human intention*. This is already a self-completion claim — but Stiegler frames it under the rubric of "organized inorganic matter" (passive participle).

### Hui's distinct move — from "organized" to "organizing"

Hui's specific contribution is to mark the contemporary cybernetic threshold by shifting the participle from passive (organized) to active (organizing):

> "**What we are witnessing today is a shift from the organized inorganic to the organizing inorganic**, meaning that machines are no longer simply tools or instruments **but rather gigantic organisms in which we live**." (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*, Introduction)

> "Modern technology has penetrated into different orders of magnitude. From micro to macro physics, technology now forms a gigantic '**organizing inorganic**' force or power. **The inorganic is no longer organized by the human body as was the case with simple tools, but rather constitutes an enormous technical system we can only live inside of, while submitting to its rules**." (Hui, *Art and Cosmotechnics*)

> "What we are witnessing today, since the advent of cybernetics, is the development of an inorganic organicity stretching through every smart device and multiple levels of systemic organization. **They cease to be the merely organized inorganic but rather are becoming the organizing inorganic, which functions recursively to produce its own structures and patterns**." (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*)

> "The modern earth—which, following Margulis and Lovelock, we can call an artificial earth or Gaia—**is the realization of a superorganism, a super Brain that is the collectivity of the individual brains**. This superorganism is in great tension with the concept of cosmotechnics… since the latter demands a locality and a multiplicity." (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*)

The structural claim: the technical system has crossed a threshold where it no longer awaits human organization but actively organizes the human environment. This is a *gigantic cybernetic system* that humans live inside; it operates by *recursive feedback* producing its own structures.

## §2 — Yang's parallel formulation: 自足完成化

From the viva-passed §V (verbatim, lines 599–609):

> "Building upon Uegaki's analysis, I see another tendency emerging in parallel with the self-finishing of human life, which is the '**Self-Completion 自足完成化**' of artificiality. If the routine operation and maintenance of artificiality are considered 'soul-sucking' or 'alienating,' which hinders the actualization of one's life-as-willed, the self-finishing tendency of human life will strive to make artificiality self-complete, **capable of operating and maintaining itself on its own**. On the flip side of the same coin, **as humans increasingly entrust their tasks and responsibilities to artificiality, the 'portion of functionality 役割' humans play in their world also shrinks**." (Yang, viva-passed §V)

And (lines 644–656):

> "If humans cease to – whether voluntarily or involuntarily – participate in the operation and decision-making process in a cybernetic system like this, **humans will become a mere source of data and have our lives directed by AI, regulated by codes**, and have our thought and behavioral patterns bounded by the affordances of IoT." (Yang)

> "If this becomes the case, such artificiality can no longer be considered merely a critical component of our world… Rather, **it has become another background environment comparable to the universe – we reside in it, are not in charge of its workings, and must adapt to survive**." (Yang)

The structural claim: artificiality has a real-level disposition to become self-sufficient, and once it crosses that threshold, it becomes a *new background environment* in which humans reside and to which they must adapt.

## §3 — The convergence — both diagnose the same threshold

The structural homology is striking:

| Element | Hui's "organizing inorganic" | Yang's "自足完成化" |
|---|---|---|
| **Threshold marker** | Shift from organized-by-humans to organizing-of-humans | Shift from operated-by-humans to self-operating |
| **End-state** | "Gigantic organisms in which we live" / "enormous technical system we can only live inside of, while submitting to its rules" | "Another background environment comparable to the universe – we reside in it, are not in charge of its workings, and must adapt to survive" |
| **Mechanism of self-organization** | "Functions recursively to produce its own structures and patterns" | Cybernetic loop: AI ⇄ big data ⇄ IoT ⇄ code-is-law |
| **Status of the human** | Lives inside, submits to the rules | Mere source of data, lives directed by AI |
| **Cosmotechnical alternative** | Plurality / cosmotechnics resisting the monoculture | "Alternative modes of artificiality" / non-Western cosmotechnics |

This is a **convergent diagnosis from two distinct philosophical lineages** — Hui via Stiegler/Simondon/Bergson; Yang via Obara/Uegaki/Bhaskar. The convergence is not coincidental: both identify the same real-level threshold where artificiality crosses from instrumentality to environment.

When two philosophical traditions independently arrive at structurally homologous claims, that is evidence the underlying real-level disposition is genuinely there.

## §4 — Verification of Yang's five additive contributions against Hui's primary text

The five-claim verification was performed via direct query against Hui's NLM corpus (notebook UUID 47ee1eac). Each claim was tested for: (a) presence in Hui, (b) contrary text in Hui, (c) honest assessment.

### §4.1 — Bidirectional human-side / inorganic-side coupling

**Status: PARTIALLY ADDITIVE — calibration needed.**

Hui has a general dialectic of *adoption / adaptation*:

> "The process of evolution is a dialectical movement between adaptation and adoption, since mere adaptation to the environment refuses the question of the will and mere adoption idolizes the will." (Hui)

But at the **organizing-inorganic threshold itself**, Hui describes **unidirectional human submission**:

> "**The inorganic is no longer organized by the human body** as was the case with simple tools, but rather constitutes an enormous technical system **we can only live inside of, while submitting to its rules**." (Hui)

So Hui has a general dialectic *across history* (humans adapt + adopt), but at the contemporary threshold he describes *human submission to the autonomous technical system*. He does NOT formalize a parallel **human-side tendency** (analogous to Uegaki's 自己完結化) that *drives* the inorganic-side organizing AT THE THRESHOLD.

**Yang's specific addition**: the two-sides-of-one-coin formulation, where human delegation drives apparatus self-completion AND apparatus self-completion shrinks human portion-of-functionality, **at the threshold itself**. Hui's adopt-adapt dialectic is across history, not at the threshold.

**Calibrated claim for thesis prose**: "Hui has a general adopt-adapt dialectic across human history, but at the organizing-inorganic threshold his account is unidirectional (humans submit to the autonomous technical system); the present thesis's bidirectional formulation at the threshold is a structural addition."

### §4.2 — Squeeze / double-bind structure between two incommensurable real-level environments

**Status: CONFIRMED ADDITIVE.** ✓

Hui has tension between the technical system and cosmotechnics, but this is **internal to the technological order**:

> "This superorganism is in great tension with the concept of cosmotechnics… since the latter demands a locality and a multiplicity." (Hui)

> "Technology is gradually 'organizing itself as a closed world,' and the process also eliminates nontechnical factors." (Hui, citing Ellul)

Hui does NOT have an **external real-level pole** (e.g., the universe-as-independent-environment) creating a structural double-bind. His account is **totalizing within itself** — the cybernetic system encloses everything; cosmotechnical pluralism is offered as resistance, not as a parallel real-level pole.

**Yang's universe ↔ metaverse squeeze is genuinely additive.** Confirmed.

### §4.3 — Bhaskar critical-realism stratification

**Status: CONFIRMED ADDITIVE.** ✓

Hui never engages Bhaskar's critical realism in the corpus. His ontological tools are **Simondon, Heidegger, Kant, Bergson, Schelling, and Chinese philosophy (Dao / Qi)**. The real/actual/empirical stratification is not in Hui's framework.

**Yang's Bhaskarian stratification is genuinely additive.** Confirmed.

### §4.4 — Minimally sufficient technologies specification

**Status: CONFIRMED ADDITIVE.** ✓

Hui describes layered technical infrastructures:

But he does NOT specify a **minimal set** that is jointly sufficient for the threshold. His account is general; Yang's AI + big data + IoT + code-is-law specification is **a falsifiable structural-conditional claim Hui doesn't make**.

**Yang's specification is genuinely additive.** Confirmed.

### §4.5 — Existential-anthropological generative anchor

**Status: PARTIALLY ADDITIVE — calibration needed.**

Hui *does* have an anthropological dimension via Leroi-Gourhan:

> "Technology has a universal dimension in the sense that it can be understood as **the exteriorisation of memory and the liberation of bodily organs in the process of hominization**…" (Hui)

But Hui's cosmotechnics project is built precisely to argue **this anthropological anchor is INSUFFICIENT on its own**:

> "**Antithesis**: Technology is **not anthropologically universal**; it is enabled and constrained by particular cosmologies, which go beyond mere functionality or utility. **Therefore, there is no one single technology, but rather multiple cosmotechnics**." (Hui)

So Hui has *exteriorization-of-memory* as a universal anthropological fact, but **DEPRIORITIZES** it in favor of cosmological grounding. Yang's Uegaki anchor (life-as-willed / 〈無限の生〉 as the existential demand that DRIVES delegation) is **specifically different in content** — it is a *demand-driven generative account*, not just an exteriorization-fact.

**Calibrated claim for thesis prose**: "Hui acknowledges Leroi-Gourhan's exteriorization-of-memory as a universal anthropological dimension but explicitly subordinates it to cosmotechnics; the present thesis's Uegaki existential-demand anchor (life-as-willed as the driver of delegation) is content-distinct from Hui's exteriorization-as-universal and serves as the *generative driver* of self-completion."

## §5 — The superorganism vs. environment distinction (LOAD-BEARING)

This is the most important finding from the Hui-corpus query and was not fully developed in the original comparison. It affects ontological commitments and should shape Ch-5-3 v2's framing.

### §5.1 — Hui FUSES superorganism and environment

Hui does NOT distinguish between (a) the metaverse as a background environment and (b) the metaverse as a gigantic organism. For Hui, the contemporary threshold is *precisely* when the environment becomes organismic:

> "**The mode of participation of technology is fundamentally environmental while at the same time transforming the environment**." (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*)

> "machines are no longer simply tools or instruments **but rather gigantic organisms in which we live**." (Hui)

> "Smart objects take part in the milieu by playing the role of organs of motor-perception (converting approximate sense perceptions to signals), organs of analysis (based on calculation and existing data), and organs of synthesis (constitution of the tertiary protention)… **constituting a new milieu characterized by an inorganic organicity**." (Hui)

Hui's milieu / environment IS the superorganism. The Simondonian "associated milieu" framing and the Teilhardian/Lovelockian "Gaia / superorganism" framing are not separate concepts in Hui — they are different scales of the same thing. The technical milieu, when scaled to planetary cybernetic level, IS Gaia / the superorganism.

### §5.2 — Hui restricts "organizing inorganic" to TECHNICS, not the cosmos

This is the critical scoping finding. Hui *explicitly restricts* "organizing inorganic" to **TECHNICS** and *contrasts* this with the older Schellingian/Lovelockian tradition that called *nature* the gigantic organism. Verbatim:

> "**In the time of Schelling**, and later in Hutton and Lovelock, **nature is considered as a gigantic organism, of which we are a part. However, this term general organism, which was attributed to nature considered as a source of contingency, seems more appropriately to designate the technological system that we now inhabiting**—for example, smart homes, smart cities, and the Anthropocene. Instead, we are observing the becoming of an 'artificial earth,' and we are living within a **gigantic cybernetic system in the process of forming**." (Hui)

This is decisive. Hui:
1. Acknowledges the Schellingian/Lovelockian view that **nature/the universe IS a gigantic organism**
2. **Reassigns the term** "general organism" / "gigantic organism" — formerly applied to nature — to the **technological system**
3. So in Hui's specific terminological usage: **"organizing inorganic" applies to TECHNICS, not to the universe**

This validates the philosophical observation that the universe self-organizes inorganic matter (Schelling, Lovelock, the older tradition would say so). But Hui *deliberately reserves* the term for the contemporary technological system. The universe drops out of the "organizing inorganic" category in Hui's specific usage.

### §5.3 — Implications: Yang's environmentalist-stratified vs Hui's organicist-fused

This is the LOAD-BEARING distinction. Hui treats the metaverse-stage as a **superorganism that is also an environment** — a single ontology where the environment is alive (cybernetically self-regulating). Yang's framework treats the metaverse as a **background environment with laws of its own**, parallel to (and incommensurable with) the universe-as-environment. The "laws of its own" framing is environmentalist-stratified, NOT organicist.

These are **different ontological commitments**:

| | Hui | Yang |
|---|---|---|
| Ontology of metaverse | Superorganism = environment (FUSED) | Background environment with laws of its own (STRATIFIED) |
| Status of the universe | Older organism, term reassigned-from in favor of technics | Independent real-level domain with universe-laws |
| Number of real-level environments | Effectively one (the cybernetic superorganism encloses everything) | Two (universe + metaverse) |
| Methodology | Phenomenological / organicist (Simondon-Bergson lineage) | Critical-realist / stratified (Bhaskar lineage) |
| Where humans sit | Inside the superorganism, submitting to its rules | Caught between two real-level environments (the squeeze) |
| Cosmotechnical alternative | Plurality of cosmotechnics resisting the monoculture | Non-Western cosmotechnics + design for flourishing |

### §5.4 — Why this matters: Yang's framing is structurally CAPABLE of generating the squeeze; Hui's is not

Hui's framework cannot generate Yang's squeeze hypothesis because:
- The universe drops out (its "general organism" status is reassigned to technics)
- The metaverse is the single totalizing organism-environment
- There is no second incommensurable real-level pole

Yang's framework keeps both poles real:
- The universe remains a real-level background environment with its own laws (transfactual, indifferent to data-generativity)
- The metaverse becomes a *new* real-level background environment with its own laws (its self-completing tendency)
- Humans are caught in the squeeze between two incommensurable selection regimes

**This makes Yang's environmentalist-stratified framing structurally STRONGER than Hui's organicist-fused framing for articulating the squeeze hypothesis.** This is not a defensive claim against Hui — it is an architectural advantage Yang's framework has that Hui's does not.

## §6 — Where Hui adds depth Yang doesn't currently have (potential imports)

In fairness, the comparison runs both ways. Hui has elements Yang could productively integrate without importing the organicist ontology:

### §6.1 — The "organized → organizing" terminological precision

Hui's shift from passive "organized inorganic" (Stiegler) to active "organizing inorganic" (Hui) is philosophically sharp — it captures the threshold-character of the shift. Yang's 自足完成化 emphasizes self-sufficiency (completion-as-state) but doesn't carry the active-vs-passive participle precision.

**Productive move**: in Ch-5-3 v2 §1, cite Hui's terminological move (organized → organizing) as a structurally homologous diagnosis from a different lineage. The thesis can mark the convergence without committing to the organicist ontology.

### §6.2 — Recursivity as the mechanism

Hui's account specifies cybernetic recursivity (the recursive feedback loop) as the mechanism by which the organizing inorganic operates. This is more precise than the current cybernetic-system specification in viva-passed §V (which lists components but doesn't formalize recursivity).

**Productive move**: a brief paragraph in §V.A could cite Hui's recursivity to explain *why* the AI/big-data/IoT/code-as-law cybernetic system has the structural property of self-completion: because it is *recursive* — the output feeds back into the input, producing self-stabilization. This is a mechanism-level addition, not an ontology-level one.

### §6.3 — Cosmotechnics as the design horizon

Hui's cosmotechnics is the most developed alternative-design framework in the lineage. The 2026-04-26 author comment ("non-Western cosmotechnics") gestures at this but doesn't develop the framework. Hui's framework is directly importable as the design horizon of the Conclusion (cf. consciousness-bracketing pre-brief §5.1).

**Productive move**: in the Conclusion, cite Hui's cosmotechnics explicitly as the resistance-framework parallel to the design question.

### §6.4 — What NOT to import: the organicist fusion

The fusion of superorganism + environment in Hui's framework is **NOT** something Yang should import. Adopting it would:
- Collapse Yang's environmentalist-stratified ontology into Hui's organicist-fused ontology
- Make the squeeze hypothesis incoherent (because the universe-side pole would drop out)
- Weaken the Bhaskarian critical-realist methodology

**The thesis can cite Hui's organizing-inorganic as a convergent threshold-diagnosis WHILE explicitly distinguishing the environmentalist-stratified ontological commitment from Hui's organicist-fused commitment.**

## §7 — Suggested thesis-prose move

A possible Ch-5-3 v2 §1 paragraph that honors the convergence while marking the divergence:

> Hui's "organizing inorganic" identifies a structurally homologous threshold where machines cease to be tools and become "gigantic organisms in which we live" (Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency*). The convergence between Hui's diagnosis and the present thesis's 自足完成化 supports the real-level disposition's robustness: two distinct philosophical lineages (Hui via Stiegler-Simondon-Bergson; the present thesis via Obara-Uegaki-Bhaskar) arrive independently at the same threshold-claim.
>
> The present thesis differs from Hui's framework in its ontological commitments. Hui's organizing inorganic is a superorganism that is also an environment — for him, the cybernetic age is precisely when the environment becomes organismic. Hui explicitly reassigns the older Schellingian/Lovelockian "general organism" term, formerly applied to nature, to the technological system; on his account, the universe ceases to be the relevant gigantic organism in favor of the cybernetic system that has supplanted it. The present thesis declines this reassignment. The metaverse is analyzed as a *background environment* with its own laws — parallel to and incommensurable with the universe-as-environment — rather than as a Gaia-style superorganism. The stratified-real architecture (after Bhaskar) requires an environmentalist framing in which the metaverse is a milieu humans adapt to, not an organism humans are inside of. The structural diagnosis of self-completion is shared with Hui; the ontological commitment about *what* the self-completing thing is, is different.
>
> This environmentalist framing is what makes the squeeze hypothesis articulable: humans must adapt simultaneously to two incommensurable real-level background environments — the universe's transfactual laws and the metaverse's self-completing tendency — rather than to a single totalizing superorganism. Hui's framework, having reassigned the gigantic-organism status to technics alone, cannot generate the squeeze in this structural sense; the present thesis's environmentalist-stratified architecture can.

## §8 — Summary takeaways

1. **The convergence is real and supports the thesis.** Both Hui and Yang independently identify the same threshold where artificiality crosses from instrumentality to environment. This convergence — from two distinct philosophical lineages — is evidence the real-level disposition is genuinely there.

2. **Yang's five contributions hold up under verification** — three are confirmed additive (squeeze, Bhaskar, minimal-tech), two are partially additive (bidirectional coupling, Uegaki-anchor) where Hui has weaker / differently-positioned analogues.

3. **Hui's organizing inorganic is FUSED superorganism+environment, NOT a stratified background environment.** This is a real ontological difference, and Yang's framing is structurally stronger for the squeeze hypothesis.

4. **Hui restricts "organizing inorganic" to technics — the universe is NOT organizing inorganic in Hui's specific usage.** The user's intuition that the universe might count as organizing inorganic is philosophically valid but does not match Hui's terminological scoping. This actually helps the thesis: it confirms that Hui treats the universe-pole as having dropped out of his analysis (or rather, having had its "gigantic organism" status reassigned to technics), which opens the structural opportunity for Yang's framework to reintroduce the universe as an independent real-level domain.

5. **Yang should import Hui's terminological precision (organized → organizing) and recursivity-mechanism, but NOT the organicist fusion.** Citing Hui as a convergent threshold-diagnosis from a different lineage strengthens Ch-5-3 v2 considerably; adopting Hui's organicist ontology wholesale would weaken the squeeze hypothesis.

## §9 — Implications for Ch-5-3 v2

This comparison strengthens Ch-5-3 v2 §1 in three ways:

1. **Cite Hui's organizing-inorganic as the closest existing structural analogue to 自足完成化** (rhetorical and philosophical strength — convergent diagnosis from different lineages).

3. **Mark the environmentalist-stratified vs organicist-fused ontological difference clearly** (this distinguishes Yang's framework from Hui's at the architectural level and demonstrates why Yang's framework is structurally capable of articulating the squeeze hypothesis while Hui's is not).

4. **Import what Hui adds productively** — terminological precision (organized → organizing), recursivity-as-mechanism, cosmotechnics-as-resistance — without importing the organicist ontology.

The combination of (1) convergent threshold-diagnosis + (2) explicit additive contributions + (3) ontological distinction + (4) productive imports yields a §V that is significantly stronger than either viva-passed §V alone or a Hui-uninformed §V revision.
