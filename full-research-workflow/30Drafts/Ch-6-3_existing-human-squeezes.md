# §VI.3: Existing Human Squeezes — The Anthropological-Experiential Precursors

## §0 — Framing

[SCAFFOLD] §VI.2 grounded F1+F2 in the empirical record of anthropogenic extinction at the species scale. §VI.3 returns to the human case but situates the existing critical-theoretic literature (Stiegler, Obara, Frankfurt School, Illich) as *anthropological-experiential precursors* — vocabularies that have been naming what the F1+F2 condition feels like from within human experience as the condition has been consolidating across the industrial-to-cybernetic era. The section's structural function is to (i) acknowledge that the canonical critical-theoretic literature has been describing real phenomena, (ii) position those descriptions as downstream from the structural F1+F2 condition (preserving the lock §4 anti-metaphor safeguards), and (iii) prepare §VI.4 to state the metaverse/universe squeeze proper as the structural condition those experiential signatures have been signaling.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Before §VI.4 states the metaverse/universe squeeze proper at the structural-ontological level, this section addresses a prior question. The critical-theoretic literature of the twentieth and twenty-first centuries has been producing extensive vocabularies for naming what is lost when humans live under industrial and cybernetic mediation — Marx's alienation, the Frankfurt School's reification and one-dimensionality, Heidegger's enframing, Stiegler's grammatization and proletarianization, Obara's self-petification, Illich's radical monopoly and modernized poverty, Hui's dividuation under algorithmic governmentality. Each of these vocabularies has analytical force; each names something real in twentieth- and twenty-first-century human experience. The question is what their relation is to the structural diagnosis this thesis offers.
>
> The answer this section proposes is that the canonical critical-theoretic literature has been describing the *human-experiential signatures* of the F1+F2 condition consolidating, *not* the F1+F2 condition itself. The squeeze, per §VI.1's structural definition and the lock §4 anti-metaphor safeguards, is the structural-environmental condition that would obtain even if no particular human subject experienced it as distress. Alienation, grammatization, reification, petification, radical monopoly — these name *consequences* and *correlates* of the F1+F2 condition that manifest at the human-experiential level. They are downstream effects of the structural-ontological cause this thesis identifies upstream.
>
> This positioning does two things. First, it credits the canonical literature with describing real phenomena — phenomena that contemporary readers will recognize from their own lives and from the past two centuries of social analysis. Second, it preserves the analytical distinctiveness of the thesis's structural-ontological diagnosis from the anthropological diagnoses it supersedes at the level of cause. The thesis is not in competition with Stiegler, Obara, Frankfurt, or Illich. It locates the structural cause that each of those traditions has been describing from a different anthropological angle.

## §1 — The relation between F1+F2 and the anthropological-experiential lineages

### §1.1 — F1+F2 is upstream from anthropological diagnoses

The squeeze-ontology lock §4 contrast table (lines 169–174) specifies the analytical relation:

> The squeeze begins from **structural niche conditions** that would obtain even if no particular human subject experienced them as distress. Alienation, domination, enframing, and grammatization name **consequences** or **correlates** of the squeeze that manifest at the level of human experience — they are downstream effects. The squeeze names the structural-ontological cause.

This positioning is preserved in §VI.3 without modification. Each of the four canonical anthropological diagnoses begins from the *human subject* and their relation to technical-institutional systems — they are subject-centered diagnoses that ask "what is happening to the human?" The structural-ontological squeeze begins from the *niche conditions* themselves — it asks "what is the structural condition of an organism that must satisfy two incommensurable selection regimes?" — a question that could in principle be asked of any organism in any environment.

### §1.2 — Why this matters for the thesis's position

If the squeeze were equivalent to alienation or grammatization, the thesis would be vulnerable to two objections that committee members might raise:

First, the *redundancy objection*: "Hasn't Marx (or Stiegler, or Marcuse) already said this? Why a new term?" The structural-ontological character of the squeeze answers this: the squeeze names the structural-environmental *cause* at a level upstream from any of the cited anthropological diagnoses. The cause is at the structural-niche level; the consequences are at the human-experiential level; the cause is not what the canonical literature has named.

Second, the *generality objection*: "Isn't your squeeze just a generic name for the modern human condition?" The F1+F2 specification answers this: the squeeze obtains *only* when two environments with incommensurable laws jointly constrain the same organism. Generic modern-condition distress that occurs within a single environment under transformed pressures does not satisfy F1+F2 and is therefore not the squeeze in this thesis's sense. The specification is structural and falsifiable.

### §1.3 — Ch-5-5's integrated framework as the basis for §VI.3

The Ch-5-5 draft (`30Drafts/Ch-5-5_proletarianization-and-the-shrinking-human.md`) has already developed an integrated framework treating Stiegler, Obara, Uegaki, Frankfurt, Illich, and Hui as *six convergent partial-perspective accounts of the same human-side dispossession* — operating at six different strata of mechanism. §VI.3 draws on Ch-5-5 but does not rewrite it. The function of §VI.3 within §VI is to *situate* those six lineages relative to F1+F2: they are anthropological-experiential precursors describing what F1+F2 consolidating looks like at the human-experiential level, from different angles.

The interested reader is referred to Ch-5-5 for the full integrated framework, the stratified mapping, and the assessment of which thesis contributions extend the integrated framework. §VI.3 here provides the abbreviated, F1+F2-aligned positioning of each lineage.

## §2 — Obara: self-petification as the bio-ecological signature of F1+F2 in formation

### §2.1 — What Obara names

Obara's analytical vocabulary is bio-ethological. He describes the contemporary human as the *lap-dog* (座敷イヌ) version of the previous human, the *yard-dog* (庭イヌ) — better-cared-for, less robust, structurally fragile. The mechanism is *capsulization* (カプセル化): the human inhabits a sealed, climate-controlled, conflict-managed environment that progressively sheds the natural stressors against which biological capacities developed. The lost capacities include autonomic regulation, metabolic flexibility, conflict-mediation skills, and — at the social level — the relational substrate that develops only through small-group friction.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Obara's analysis sits at the bio-ecological stratum. The human animal's biological-behavioral repertoire — what an earlier vocabulary might have called its Animal OS — is calibrated for environments with universe-originated stressors: temperature variation, food scarcity, predator pressure, infectious disease, inter-group conflict, small-group social negotiation. As the artificial ecosystem progressively shields the human from these stressors, the capacities that developed against them atrophy. The shielding is not malign; it is the achievement of the artificial ecosystem operating as designed. But the atrophy is structurally consequential: the human increasingly cannot function outside the shielded environment because the capacities required for that functioning have not been exercised.

### §2.2 — Obara as F1+F2-precursor signature

In F1+F2 terms, Obara describes what the human-side experiential signature of the F1+F2 condition looks like from the *biological-substrate angle*. The human's Animal OS retains universe-originated selection criteria (it is governed by biological law), but the human's daily environment increasingly satisfies the metaverse-side artificial-ecosystem criteria. The body is increasingly mis-matched to the environment it actually inhabits — F1 obtains at the bio-individual scale (two environments simultaneously demanding adaptation); F2 obtains because the criteria of the artificial ecosystem cannot be satisfied by the body's biological repertoire and vice versa.

What Obara calls self-petification is the structural correlate of F1 (biological substrate demanding universe-side competence while social-cognitive participation demands artificial-ecosystem competence) and F2 (the body's universe-originated capacities are not commensurable with the artificial-ecosystem's demands). The bio-ecological dimension of F1+F2 is what Obara has been describing. It is a real phenomenon; it is one stratum of the human-experiential signature; it is not the F1+F2 structural condition itself but its bio-ecological consequence.

## §3 — Stiegler: proletarianization as the techno-historical signature of F1+F2 in formation

### §3.1 — What Stiegler names

Stiegler's analytical vocabulary is techno-historical. Grammatization is the technical-historical process by which previously continuous human flows are *discretized* — broken into reproducible units, traces, "grammes" — and externalized into technical supports. Each epoch of grammatization captures more of the human flow: mental images (cave paintings), speech (writing), gestures (industrial production), perceptual flow (analog recording), and now behavior, social relations, and transindividuation itself (digital algorithms).

Proletarianization is the loss of *knowledges* that follows grammatization. Stiegler distinguishes three stages: industrial machinery operates the proletarianization of work-knowledge (savoir-faire); analog mass media operates the proletarianization of life-knowledge (savoir-vivre); digital algorithms operate the proletarianization of theoretical knowledge (savoir-théoriser). The threshold-claim, in *Automatic Society*, is that when grammatization reaches digital reticulation, the technical system short-circuits deliberation faster than human reason can catch up. *Fully computational capitalism is as such the completion of nihilism.*

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Stiegler's analysis sits at the techno-historical and technical-architectural strata. The technical system progressively absorbs human capacities into its operational substrate; what was once a living capacity of the human becomes a function of the technical system, and the human's living capacity withers. The mechanism is not coercive in the political sense; it is structural. Humans delegate functions to the technical system because doing so makes life easier, more efficient, more "as-willed" — and the technical system, having absorbed those functions, no longer requires the human to perform them. The functions migrate from human capacity to technical infrastructure.

### §3.2 — Stiegler as F1+F2-precursor signature

In F1+F2 terms, Stiegler describes the human-side experiential signature of F1+F2 from the *technical-architectural angle*. The human progressively loses the capacities that satisfy the universe-side selection criteria (savoir-faire is bodily; savoir-vivre is socially embedded; savoir-théoriser requires unmediated noetic activity) — at the same time as the human is required to operate within technical systems whose selection criteria are different in kind (platform legibility, algorithmic compliance, data-generative behavior). F1 obtains because the human is jointly constrained by both regimes; F2 obtains because the capacities required for one are not commensurable with the capacities required for the other.

What Stiegler calls generalized proletarianization is the structural correlate of the human progressively losing one set of capacities (universe-side) under conditions that simultaneously require a different set (metaverse-side). The techno-historical stratum of F1+F2 — the historical-mechanism stratum at which the technical system reconfigures human capacity — is what Stiegler has been describing across his career.

## §4 — Frankfurt critical theory: the politico-economic signature of F1+F2 in formation

### §4.1 — What the Frankfurt tradition names

Adorno and Horkheimer's *Dialectic of Enlightenment* and Marcuse's *One-Dimensional Man* describe a politico-economic mechanism converging on similar consequences. The commodity form, exchange-equivalence, and instrumental rationality become reified into administrative apparatus; the apparatus then absorbs even the administrators — including, eventually, those who would resist it.

The mechanism is politico-economic in that it operates through the commodity form and the rationalization of administration. Marcuse's *operationalism becomes the theory and practice of containment*; Adorno's *world tends to become the stuff of total administration which absorbs even the administrators*; Horkheimer's instrumental reason hollows out the substantive content of reason itself.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The Frankfurt tradition's analysis sits at the politico-economic stratum. Capital accumulation generates the imperative to rationalize all activity into commodity-exchangeable form; the rationalization process produces an administrative apparatus that operates by its own logic; the apparatus eventually no longer requires the human substantive reasoning that originally established it. *Self-propelling in its oppressive productivity and in its beneficial coordination*, the apparatus runs without genuine human noetic involvement. The Frankfurt-school analytical achievement was to name this as a structural feature of the capitalist mode of production, not as a contingent failure of particular institutions.

### §4.2 — Frankfurt as F1+F2-precursor signature

In F1+F2 terms, the Frankfurt tradition describes the human-side experiential signature of F1+F2 from the *politico-economic angle*. The capitalist mode of production progressively constructs an administrative apparatus that operates by its own logic — and that logic, increasingly, is the metaverse-side selection criterion (legibility, exchangeability, optimization). Humans are required to participate in this apparatus as the condition of livelihood, while their substantive reasoning (universe-side capacity) becomes increasingly unable to find purchase. F1 obtains because both regimes demand satisfaction; F2 obtains because substantive reason and instrumental rationality are incommensurable in the strict sense — substantive reason concerns ends, instrumental rationality concerns means, and the latter cannot evaluate the former without dissolving its own form.

What Marcuse called one-dimensionality and what Adorno called total administration are the politico-economic correlates of F1+F2 in formation. They are real phenomena; they describe how capitalism produces the apparatus side of F1+F2; they are anthropological-experiential signatures of the structural condition this thesis identifies upstream.

## §5 — Illich: institutional-professional signature of F1+F2 in formation

### §5.1 — What Illich names

Illich's analytical vocabulary is institutional-professional. Radical monopoly is the condition under which one industrial production process *exercises exclusive control over the satisfaction of a pressing need and excludes nonindustrial activities from competition*. Disabling professions are the institutionalized forms by which professional elites *claim control over human needs* through certification and credentialing. Modernized poverty is the loss of vernacular satisfaction-of-needs: *the modernized poor are those whose vernacular domain, in speech and in action, is most restricted*.

The mechanism operates through institutional-professional structures: schools monopolize learning; medicine monopolizes care; transportation infrastructure monopolizes movement; bureaucracies monopolize need-definition. Each monopoly destroys the environmental conditions in which vernacular alternatives would be viable.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Illich's analysis sits at the institutional-professional stratum. Industrial-professional institutions establish themselves as the necessary mediators of human activity, and in doing so destroy the vernacular alternatives that would otherwise be available. The mechanism is not malign per se — each institutional monopoly arose as a response to real needs (universal literacy, health-care access, mass transit). But each monopoly, beyond a threshold Illich names the *second watershed*, becomes counterproductive to its own stated purpose. Schools beyond a certain scale make learning more difficult; medicine beyond a certain scale produces iatrogenic harm exceeding the conditions it treats; transportation infrastructure beyond a certain scale slows the average travel speed by structuring all life around long-distance commuting.

### §5.2 — Illich as F1+F2-precursor signature

In F1+F2 terms, Illich describes the human-side experiential signature of F1+F2 from the *institutional-professional angle*. The institutional-professional apparatus progressively eliminates the alternatives through which universe-side capacities (vernacular skill, embodied practice, local knowledge) could sustain livelihood. The human is increasingly required to satisfy institutional-professional selection criteria (credentials, certification, compliance with professional categorization), which are not commensurable with the vernacular criteria that previously sustained life. F1 obtains because both regimes demand satisfaction (the institutional one for legibility; the universe-side one for biological-ecological functioning); F2 obtains because professional certification cannot substitute for the embodied competence it replaces.

What Illich calls radical monopoly is the *institutional-mechanism* by which the metaverse-side selection regime extends its operational reach. Modernized poverty is the *human-experiential signature* of the F1+F2 condition under institutional-professional mediation. Both are real; both are anthropological-experiential consequences of the structural condition this thesis identifies upstream.

§VI.5 will return to radical monopoly in F5 terms — as the operational feature of the universe-metaverse squeeze that intensifies most dramatically at the metaverse-stage. Here it appears as one of the historical precursor signatures of the structural condition consolidating.

## §6 — Convergence: what the multiple strata together establish

### §6.1 — Convergent but not identical

The four strata — bio-ecological (Obara), techno-historical (Stiegler), politico-economic (Frankfurt), institutional-professional (Illich) — converge on a common observation: across the modern era, humans have been progressively constituted as participants in structures (technical, administrative, professional, institutional) whose selection criteria differ in kind from the universe-side criteria the human biological substrate retains. Each lineage has named this from its own angle; each is partially right; none is wholly right; together they constitute a multi-stratum description of the human-experiential signatures of F1+F2 in formation.

The fact that *four independent intellectual traditions*, operating with different methodologies and across different periods (Marx in the nineteenth century; Frankfurt in the mid-twentieth; Stiegler/Obara in the late twentieth and early twenty-first; Illich in the 1970s), converge on this observation is itself evidence that they are describing a real phenomenon. They are not arbitrary or fashionable diagnoses; they are responses to a structural condition that has been intensifying historically.

### §6.2 — Why the structural diagnosis adds something the anthropological diagnoses do not

The structural-ontological F1+F2 diagnosis offered by this thesis adds three things that the anthropological lineages, taken individually or together, do not provide:

First, it *names the cause* at a level upstream from any of the named consequences. Stiegler's grammatization, Obara's self-petification, Frankfurt's reification, and Illich's radical monopoly each describe a *mechanism* by which the modern condition operates; the structural-ontological diagnosis identifies the *condition* that those mechanisms are operating *under*. F1+F2 is what the four mechanisms have been mechanisms of.

Second, it *integrates* the four anthropological diagnoses into a single framework. Ch-5-5 develops this integration in detail. The four lineages, framed structurally, are not in competition; they are partial-perspective accounts of the same condition. The structural diagnosis provides the architecture in which they can be held simultaneously.

Third, it *specifies what would resolve the condition*. Each anthropological lineage proposes a resistance prescription: Stiegler's pharmacology and contributive economy; Obara's re-engagement with biological naturalness; Frankfurt's negative dialectics and Great Refusal; Illich's convivial tools and vernacular re-engagement. None of these resistance prescriptions, taken alone, addresses the structural condition; each addresses one stratum. The structural diagnosis allows the §VI.6 productive-dimension argument to specify that any meaningful response must be multi-stratum and bidirectional — addressing both the apparatus side and the human-existential side simultaneously.

### §6.3 — From precursor signature to consolidated condition

The lineages discussed above were describing F1+F2 *in formation*. They were observing the human-side experiential signatures of a structural condition that had not yet fully consolidated. Frankfurt was observing the industrial-administrative apparatus producing F1+F2-style consequences in the mid-twentieth century. Stiegler was observing the digital-reticulation threshold from the late twentieth century onward. Obara was observing the bio-ecological consequences across the postwar Japanese transition. Illich was observing the institutional-professional consequences from the 1960s through the 1980s.

§VI.4 will then state the metaverse/universe squeeze proper as the *consolidated* F1+F2 condition reached at the metaverse-stage. The argument is not that the four anthropological lineages were wrong — they were right about what they observed. The argument is that what they observed was the F1+F2 condition in formation across the industrial-to-cybernetic era, and that the metaverse-stage is when the condition consolidates into the fully-realized structural-ontological squeeze the thesis names.

This developmental framing matters for the thesis's argument because it positions the thesis as *continuous with* the canonical critical-theoretic literature while specifying what it adds — the structural-ontological cause underneath the multiple anthropological signatures, plus the threshold-claim that the metaverse-stage is when the condition consolidates beyond what the canonical literature was observing.
