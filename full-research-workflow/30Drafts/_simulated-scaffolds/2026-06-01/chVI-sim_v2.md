## Opener — Where the chapter sits, and what it must do

Chapter I closed on a promise. It defined the squeeze, in its barest form, as an extension of the four conditions the Great Oxidation Event made vivid: that organisms shape their environments; that the direction of the shaping need not align with the shaper's adaptive trajectory; that such alteration can become a genuine survival pressure on the alterers; and that adaptation to the altered environment is not guaranteed. A squeeze, I said there, is what happens when an organism "is simultaneously pressured to adapt to its original environment and a new altered environment."[^op1] Chapter V then argued that the artificiality this mode has built is acquiring a tendency of its own — to operate, maintain, and renew itself without ongoing human direction — so that what was a *means within* a world begins to stand up as a *background environment around* it. The present chapter draws these together, asking what it is, structurally, to live as a being now situated between two background environments: the universe, the realist substrate that has always been there, and the metaverse, the second background environment the self-completing tendency of artificiality is bringing into being.

[^op1]: [VERIFY: DOCX [P0059] — the four GOE-grounded conditions; the squeeze as "an organism… simultaneously pressured to adapt to its original environment and a new altered environment." This chapter is the promised "unpack further in the following chapters."]

## §VIA — The squeeze, defined

The squeeze, stated at its most exact, is the condition of a being that must simultaneously satisfy the selection criteria of two environments whose organizing laws are not mutually translatable.[^a1] Two features constitute it, and both must hold for the word to do structural rather than decorative work.

[^a1]: [VERIFY: squeeze-ontology.md §1 — the constitutive definition, quoted verbatim.]

The first feature, **F1**, is a *between*-structure. The squeezed subject is positioned *between* two environments, not located *within* one environment that has merely intensified its demands — and this is where the squeeze parts company with the ordinary story of disruptive change. A villager meeting industrial agriculture, a clerk meeting computerization, undergoes a within-environment transformation: the substrate has shifted, but the organizing logic has not, and the universe's laws still set the terms. F1 obtains only when there are genuinely two environments, each with its own organizing principles, and the subject's success is *jointly* constrained by both — so that no single optimization resolves the situation, because optimizing for one leaves the other's criteria fully operative.

What is easy to miss, and what the locked definition makes explicit, is that F1 holds at **two distinct subject-levels**.[^a2] At **Level 1**, the subject is an *organism* — an individual human, or a member of another species — whose adaptive capacities are jointly constrained by two environments. At **Level 2**, the subject is a *world itself*: an operating system constituted by a configuration of *means* — institutions, technologies, practices, infrastructures, social arrangements. A world, too, can be squeezed between the universe and the metaverse. And the two levels are not independent; they are mechanistically coupled through the bidirectional pairing Chapters IV and V developed — the human-side tendency to delegate ever more of life to artificiality (自己完結化) and the apparatus-side tendency to complete itself (自足完成化), each driving the other.

[^a2]: [VERIFY: squeeze-ontology.md §1.3 + §F1 — F1 is constitutive at TWO subject-levels: Level 1 organism, Level 2 world-as-OS; the two are mechanistically coupled via 自己完結化 ↔ 自足完成化. This Level-2 squeeze is new relative to the 2026-05-29 scaffold, which did Level 1 only.]

The Level-2 squeeze deserves a sentence in its own right, because it is the form the thesis most often loses and most needs to keep. The *world* is a system of means humans built to serve human purposes. As those means are delegated to and self-complete, they gain operational independence; and once a means self-completes far enough, it ceases to be an *internal* means of the world and becomes a *background environment* of its own — the metaverse. The world's own components leave the world and become its pressure: the world contracts, the part humans play in running it shrinks, and the means that once nested *inside* the world now stand *around* it, a second background alongside the universe. So the squeeze is not only something that happens to people; it is something that happens to the world as such — the meaning-space, nested in the universe, that Chapter I named — as its means migrate from internal furniture to external environment.[^a3]

[^a3]: [VERIFY: squeeze-ontology.md §1.3 "Two-level squeeze" + DOCX [P0124]/Ch V §1.2 on the shrinking 役割 of humans in the world's operation. The "world's own means leave the world and become its pressure" formulation is the Level-2 squeeze.]

The second feature, **F2**, is *incommensurability* — and specifically incommensurability at the level of enduring principles, not at the level of how the two environments are experienced.[^a4] The universe operates through transfactual laws — thermodynamic, biological, ecological, geographic, chemical — whose generative structures are naturally given and run whether or not any human enacts them. The metaverse, at the metaverse-stage, operates through engineered laws: the meta-law of self-completion at the deepest stratum, and beneath it the architectural mechanisms Chapter V specified. These are humanly produced in origin yet compelling in operation. F2's claim is that these two sets of organizing principles are *not mutually translatable* — not different points on a common scale, but structures with no common measure that would license a single trade-off. This is incommensurability in the strict sense, after Kuhn and Feyerabend, but applied to environments-as-generative-structures rather than to scientific paradigms.

[^a4]: [VERIFY: squeeze-ontology.md §1 F2 — incommensurability is at the REAL/enduring-principle level (generative structures), NOT at the empirical-level signatures (which live in §VID's three signs) and NOT about which-laws-apply (gravity still applies in metaverse-mediated environments). "Incommensurability without incompatibility" tracks DOCX [P0081].]

Two clarifications keep F2 from over-reaching.[^a5] F2 is not the claim that gravity stops applying inside a metaverse-mediated environment — the universe's laws apply everywhere; what is incommensurable is *which generative structures determine fitness or integrity* in each environment. Nor is F2, at this level, the claim that competencies fail to transfer or that value-currencies fail to exchange — those are real, but they are *empirical signatures*, downstream of F2, and belong to §VID. F2 stays at the level of the laws themselves: the two sets of organizing principles are incommensurable as generative structures, prior to any observation of the consequences.

[^a5]: [VERIFY: squeeze-ontology.md §1 "What F2 does NOT directly assert" — the four guards against real/empirical conflation; empirical signatures belong to §VID.]

It is worth being equally exact about the *universe* side, because the whole architecture depends on its being real in a particular way. The universe is not a Kantian noumenon — not an unknowable limit-concept bracketed out of the causal order. It is, in Bhaskar's vocabulary, an **intransitive real**: a structure that "exists and acts quite independently of men and the conditions which allow men access to it," such that "if men ceased to exist sound would continue to travel and heavy bodies fall to the earth in exactly the same way."[^a6] Unlike Kant's thing-in-itself, the universe *is* causal, *is* spatiotemporal, and *is* knowable in its effects — through the real referents behind "gravitation" and "thermodynamics."[^a7] Its inaccessibility-as-a-whole is ecological and positional — every Umwelt is a part of the Umgebung — not transcendental. This is precisely what lets the squeeze operate: a noumenon could never bear the footprints of the worlds that act on it, but a real, intransitive universe can, which is why the universe-side of the squeeze is itself in motion (§VID).

Finally, the squeeze must be held apart from **collapse**, with which it is easily and damagingly conflated.[^a8] The squeeze is the *ongoing structural condition* of being jointly subject to incommensurable principles — a condition that can be lived with, for finite intervals, under conditions of recognition and navigation §VIF will name. Collapse is the *crossing of a threshold*: the endpoint at which the squeeze becomes irreversible and the squeezed being can no longer sustain dual satisfaction at all — extinction for an organism, dissolution of the operating system for a world, the breakdown of the dual operation for a person. The two come from different literatures and carry different urgencies; the chapter keeps them separate, analyzing the squeeze through §VIF and turning to the collapse-endpoint only in §VIG.

## §VIB — The animals were already squeezed

The structural condition just defined is not, in nature, new. Before turning to the human case it is worth dwelling on the fact that F1+F2 has been the operative condition of many non-human species for at least fifty thousand years — because that record anchors the diagnosis in phenomena no reader will dispute, and supplies the precedent against which the human squeeze will be measured.

For a non-human species, F1 obtains when its inherited operating system must satisfy the criteria of *two* environments rather than one: the universe-originated niche it co-evolved with, and the human-created artificial ecosystem that has displaced or invaded it.[^b1] F2 obtains because the two select for incommensurable things — the niche for predator-evasion tuned to natural predators, diet tuned to native chemistry, reproduction tuned to native phenology; the artificial ecosystem for tolerance of human proximity, foraging in modified landscapes, evasion of firearms and traps. There is no single dimension along which a species trades "niche-fitness" for "artificial-ecosystem-fitness." The parallel to the human case is exact: as humans now face a universe-side their own worlds have disturbed and a metaverse-side whose laws are engineered, so other species have long faced an altered niche and an artificial ecosystem incommensurable with their inherited criteria.

[^b1]: [VERIFY: Ch-6-2 §1; squeeze-ontology.md §1.3 "Other-creatures case." On the "Animal OS" terminology collision with the human-biological substrate, Ch-6-2 §6.3 recommends explicit qualification (species-OS vs human-OS) — keep the wording light in final prose.]

The empirical record of this squeeze is the record of anthropogenic extinction, in roughly five waves, each a different mode of human artificiality.[^b2] The Late Pleistocene wave removed large-bodied mammals on each landmass shortly after human arrival — Australia's *Diprotodon*, the Americas' mammoths and ground sloths, Madagascar's elephant birds, New Zealand's moa — the timing too consistent across continents to lay at climate's door alone. The Holocene agricultural wave worked through habitat conversion rather than predation, as cropland and pasture replaced wild ecosystems. The colonial wave globalized the artificial ecosystem, carrying commensal predators to islands whose endemics had no preparation for them — the dodo, the great auk, the passenger pigeon. The fourth, the one we are in, is the Sixth Mass Extinction, with vertebrate extinction rates estimated at a hundred to a thousand times background and wild terrestrial mammalian biomass reduced to a small fraction of the total.[^b3] The fifth, in formation, is the metaverse-stage's own contribution: data-center energy demand, extraction-site habitat loss, and the algorithmic optimization of agriculture, fishing, and forestry.

The structural lesson is not about cruelty but about form. The species that went extinct were, overwhelmingly, those whose squeeze was *symmetric* — those who could neither retreat to the universe-originated niche, because humans had altered it, nor inhabit the artificial ecosystem, because their operating system was incompatible with its criteria. A symmetric F1+F2 squeeze offers no stable fallback on either side, and such squeezes tend, absent intervention, toward extinction rather than recoverable transition. Humans have until now occupied the *causing* end — the apex niche-constructor that squeezes other species while not being squeezed itself. The metaverse-stage is when that asymmetry breaks: having built their artificial ecosystem so thoroughly that it stands up as a second environment for their *own* species, humans now enter the structural position they have imposed on others since the late Pleistocene.[^b4] What is new is not the squeeze; it is the identity of the squeezed.

## §VIC — The human precursors: signatures of a squeeze still forming

Before stating the metaverse-stage squeeze proper, one accounting is owed. The critical theory of the last two centuries has produced a rich vocabulary for what is lost when humans live under industrial and cybernetic mediation, and a reader may ask what the squeeze adds. The answer is that those vocabularies describe the *human-experiential signatures* of the F1+F2 condition as it was still forming — not the structural condition itself.[^c1] They name real things, from real angles; they sit *downstream* of the structural cause this thesis locates upstream.

## §VIE — How this squeeze differs from every prior one

Liu's question at the viva was exact and deserves an exact answer: how is this squeeze *different* from the squeezes the critical tradition has already analyzed? **F3 — stratified two-environment dynamism, with the conventional space shrinking.** The deepest correction the locked ontology makes to the naive picture is that the metaverse-stage subject does not stand between one stable environment and one moving one — *both* sides are moving, for structurally different reasons.[^e2] The universe-*laws* remain transfactually stable on civilizational timescales. ## §VIF — The productive dimension: answering whether the squeeze is "not necessarily negative"

Two members of the committee pressed the same hard point: that the squeeze is not necessarily negative, and perhaps even more positive than negative. The challenge is necessary, and the chapter would be dishonest to leave the squeeze as a description of crisis while saying nothing about the genuine goods the same conditions produce — goods people actively choose and value.[^f1] The answer is *not* to concede the point by weighing positives against negatives, and it is *not* to insist that the lived experience is really pathology in disguise. It is to refuse both framings and develop a third.

[^f1]: [VERIFY: Ch-6-6 §0 — answer 馬/Ma + 劉/Liu ("squeeze不一定都是負面的 / 正面的更多?") by refusing two framings and developing a third. The §VI-3/VI-4 three-item list (individual/cultural/civilizational) is explicitly deprecated per squeeze-ontology.md §3.]

The **first refused framing** is the positive-versus-negative *trade-off*, and F2 is precisely what forbids it.[^f2] To weigh benefits against costs requires a common measure in which both are expressible. But the universe-side goods (embodied resilience, biological attunement, vernacular competence) and the metaverse-side goods (platform legibility, capability extension, the reduction of as-not-willedness) have no shared currency: there is no rate at which "improvement in platform legibility" converts into "loss of biological attunement." A trade-off calculation would require exactly the commensurability F2 denies — and to frame the squeeze as a trade-off is to dissolve it back into a within-environment optimization problem, the very thing F1+F2 says it is not.

[^f2]: [VERIFY: Ch-6-6 §1 + squeeze-ontology.md §4 — the trade-off framing is structurally forbidden because F2 = absence of a common measure. "Not trade-offs or tensions" is one of the four anti-metaphor hard prohibitions.]

The **second refused framing** is *pathology-versus-recognition* — that the squeeze is simply a pathology recognition converts into something navigable.[^f3] This too fails, and Uegaki makes the failure precise. The bio-ecological "pathologies" — capsulization, sedentary life, attentional fragmentation, disembodied relationality — *are not experienced as pathological by many who live them*. ## §VIG — The prospect of collapse

The chapter closes, as the introduction promised it must, by looking ahead — not to predict, but to specify the *form* a collapse would take if the squeeze became irreversible.[^g1] The discipline of §VIA is reasserted: this section is about the collapse-endpoint, not the squeeze itself, and it draws on a different literature — Tainter's complexity-maintenance equation, Holling's adaptive cycle, Perrow's normal accidents, Taleb's fragility, Schwartz and Nichols's regeneration-base — none of which is the squeeze, all of which address the threshold-crossing the squeeze precedes.

[^g1]: [VERIFY: Ch-6-7 §0–§1 — §VIG is the collapse-endpoint, not the squeeze; squeeze-vs-collapse distinction load-bearing; prospective structural analysis, NOT prediction/eschatology.]

A symmetric squeeze becomes irreversible when its two dispossessions have *jointly* progressed past the point where ad hoc retreat to either side is materially possible — when universe-side competence has atrophied below the threshold for survival, metaverse-side maintenance costs exceed the resources available to sustain compliance, and the planetary manifestations have shifted beyond the range the residual competence is adapted to.[^g2] This is not a date that can be set; it is a threshold the squeeze approaches as both processes deepen. The collapse-and-fragility literature describes the *kind* of collapse a tightly-coupled, optimization-driven, instability-suppressing system tends toward: synchronous failure across coupled systems rather than slow decline, cascade across interdependent networks at thresholds lower than any single network's, and — when a system has been locked in its growth phase too long — the "deep collapse" in which the very ability to regenerate is lost.[^g3]

[^g3]: [VERIFY: Ch-6-7 §2–§3 — Tainter (negative marginal returns on complexity); Holling (late conservation phase → release); Perrow (tight coupling → normal accidents); Taleb (suppressed volatility → larger hidden volatility); Homer-Dixon citing Holling ("deep collapse… ability to regenerate itself is lost"). The thesis's distinctive contribution at this stratum is **Claim C**: that the metaverse-stage destroys its own regeneration-base *in advance*.[^g4] Schwartz and Nichols established that civilizational collapses are rarely total — what survives (institutions, ideas, vernacular skills, embodied knowledge) constitutes a regeneration-base from which reorganized societies re-emerge; Roman peasants kept the competence to farm, Maya commoners kept subsistence knowledge, so each historical collapse, however severe, left a floor. Claim C is that the metaverse-stage is different because the convergent dispossession mechanisms of §VIC — Stiegler's proletarianization, Obara's petification, Frankfurt's hollowing of substantive reason, Illich's destruction of vernacular competence — operate on that floor *before* any collapse occurs. A metaverse-stage collapse, were it reached, would therefore not be Tainter's economizing return to a lower-complexity equilibrium, because the floor such a return presupposes has already been removed.[^g5] The analogy with §VIB is exact: each species that went extinct faced a symmetric squeeze with no fallback on either side, and the endpoint was extinction, not recoverable transition. Claim C generalizes that form to the human prospect — not a prediction of imminent extinction, but a claim that the *form* of any collapse, were it to come, is extinction-class rather than Tainter-class.
