## §VA — From self-finishing to self-completion: naming 自足完成化

Chapter IV examined what universalization does to the inhabitants of the mode whose disposition has succeeded at planetary scale: the pursuit of life-as-willed, and the self-finishing tendency — Uegaki's 自己完結化 — by which a population delegates more and more of its tasks and responsibilities to the artificial apparatus that sustains it. This chapter examines the tendency on the *other* side of the same coin. As humans entrust their functions to artificiality, the routine operation and maintenance of artificiality become, in turn, hindrances to the as-willed life — burdens to be shed. The self-finishing of human life therefore drives the artificial apparatus toward a complementary condition: the capacity to operate, maintain, and extend itself without continuous human direction. This is the apparatus-side tendency, and I name it here for the first time: the **self-completion of artificiality**, **自足完成化**.[^a1]

[^a1]: [VERIFY: viva-passed §V, self-completion prose; Ch-5-3_v3 §1.1–§1.2 — 自足完成化 stated as the apparatus-side disposition; the coupling with Uegaki's 自己完結化 is the author's formulation.]

The two tendencies are two faces of one coin, and the coupling between them is bidirectional and self-reinforcing. As humans pursue the as-willed life by delegating, the routine running of the apparatus becomes structurally undesirable to keep performing, and this drives the apparatus toward self-sufficiency. And as humans delegate, the *portion of functionality* — 役割 — that humans themselves play in their world contracts: a contraction deepened, as Chapter IV argued, by the dilution of relationality at the collective level and the desomatization of life at the individual level. The shrinking of the human portion structurally reinforces the growth of the apparatus's portion. Each tendency, in actualizing, deepens the conditions for the other.[^a2]

[^a2]: [VERIFY: viva-passed §V lines 599–609; Ch-5-3_v3 §1.2 bidirectional-coupling mechanism. The 役割 / portion-of-functionality framing is preserved from the current main text.]

Before going further, the status of this concept must be fixed precisely, because it is the one place where the thesis's whole architecture is at stake. **Self-completion is the only enduring-principle-level feature the metaverse adds — and it is *not* a specific technology.** Chapter I distinguished three layers at which anything can be described: the enduring conditions or principles that organize things into structures or dynamics, which are then manifested in the context-specific phenomena a subject can experience or observe.[^a3] At the topmost of these layers — the layer of enduring principles, what is "real" in the precise sense the thesis gives the word — the metaverse contributes exactly one thing: the self-completing tendency as such. It is *substrate-neutral*. It is not artificial intelligence, not big data, not the Internet of Things, not code-as-law; those, as §VB will show, sit a layer down, at the level of structures and dynamics. This is the decisive contrast with the field. Where Lee ties the collapse of the natural/artefactual boundary to biotechnology, McKibben to climate, Floridi to information-in-general, and Latour to ecology — each binding the diagnosis to a particular domain or device — the thesis ties it to the self-completing tendency *as such*, which could be carried by different technical substrates and would still be the same tendency.[^a4] This is what makes the diagnosis genuinely general, and it is why the earlier draft's talk of "three real-level mechanisms" was an over-population of the real: there is one.

This also settles a terminological matter the earlier drafts left open. The condition this chapter describes was, in older versions of the thesis, sometimes called "neo-artificiality." That term is retired, and not reintroduced. "Neo-" marks *temporality*, not *substance*: it ages (today's "neo-X" is tomorrow's "neo-neo-X"), it carries a continuity-bias that hides the structural rupture, and above all it names lateness rather than *what is structurally new*. It is here that I want to introduce a provocation — and to flag, immediately, that it is a provocation, deployed non-literally, and that part of its force depends on argument this thesis does not fully discharge. The self-completing apparatus, I want to say, is a case of *tekhne becoming phusis*: the made acquiring the character of the given. Said flatly, this is false, and I do not assert it flatly. It is a critical reconstruction in the tradition's own vocabulary, and it does a specific job: it exposes the breakdown of the inherited categories and motivates the replacement apparatus the thesis has been building — universe, world, adaptational operating system, artificiality.[^a6]

[^a6]: [VERIFY: FRAMING note §1, §4–§5 — "tekhne becoming phusis" is a NON-LITERAL provocation against the inherited triad, NOT a literal claim that the made acquires φύσις's internal *archē*. [AUTHOR PROVOCATION — heuristic, not established.] It is the author's, NOT Brague's (the 2026-05-29 chV-sim's Brague p.14 attribution is corrected here). Under-argued portions footnoted per the governing instruction.]

To see why the provocation lands, recall the etymology. *Tekhne* descends from the Indo-European root \*tek-, "to build" — the same root that gives τέκτων, the carpenter; the word is, at its lexical core, *making-by-construction*. *Phusis* descends from \*bʰeh₂u-, "to grow, to arise, to be" — the word for what comes forth of itself.[^a7] The two roots are about as far apart as the language allows: *to build* against *to grow*. To say "tekhne is becoming phusis" is to collide the two most opposed roots in the inheritance, deliberately, in order to make audible that the build/grow opposition can no longer organize our world. And the inheritance itself names the exact hinge at which the distinction would dissolve. Aristotle, arguing that nature acts for an end, offers a counterfactual: "Art does not deliberate. **If the ship-building art were in the wood, it would produce the same results by nature.**"[^a8] I claim Aristotle's authority only for the *statement of the hinge*, never for his conclusion. The point is that the tradition's own marker of the difference between the made and the grown is whether the principle of its motion lies *within* the thing (phusis) or *outside* it, in its maker (tekhne) — and that under metaverse-stage conditions, talking *as if* the art were in the wood becomes unavoidable. At which point the distinction stops doing work and must be *replaced*, not literally crossed.

Corroborated by Cassin (ars=technē, root \*teks- "construct") and HWPh (\*tek- "bauen, zimmern").]

What licenses calling this a *reconstruction* rather than a discovery is that Chapter I already abandoned the divide the provocation plays on. The thesis rejected the nature/culture dualism outright — "no split is possible" between world and universe; "artificiality, my analytical unit that replaces culture, is what makes human human, and a world world."[^a9] The inherited triad *phusis / nomos / techne* maps precisely onto the *nature / culture / technology* divides the thesis has already discarded. So the chapter does not *discover* the made becoming given; it stages a provocation in the canon's terms to show *why the triad must go* — and then offers, in its place, the apparatus the thesis has built. The honest positive claim, weaker and safer than "the made now grows of itself," is the formula behind self-completion: the metaverse-stage apparatus is **nomos-by-origin** (engineered, instituted) yet **phusis-by-operation** (running with given-like, law-like authority that no longer traces to any human decision). That is a claim about operative law-like autonomy, not about a literal internal teleology.[^a10]

[^a10]: [VERIFY: FRAMING note §6a, §6c — nomos-by-origin / phusis-by-operation; this is the positive claim, distinct from "grows an *archē*." nomos-FINDINGS-v3 §2.]

Three interlocutors place the provocation. **Simondon is the closest ally.** His account of *concretization* is the nearest thing in the literature to the made acquiring the character of the given: the evolved technical object "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects … toward a closure of the system of causes and effects … within its bounds," so that "as it evolves, this object loses its artificial character," and — in a manuscript variant — "the object frees itself and becomes naturalized"; it "is thus its own condition."[^a11] Simondon is the precedent that makes it philosophically legitimate to re-describe the made in the given's terms in serious ontology — but he predicates this of the *discrete technical object*, where the thesis predicates a substrate-neutral *tendency* of an emerging *world-layer*, and draws the constructive conclusion (replace the triad) that Simondon did not. **Floridi is an ally on the mechanism and a rival on the level.** His *re-ontologization* — a technology so radical it "fundamentally transforms the intrinsic nature of the system itself, that is, its ontology," autonomously, with the human "not even … on, let alone in, the loop" — is a near-exact formal analogue of the thesis's self-completion (and, in Chapter VI, of the squeeze).[^a12] But Floridi and the thesis keep their realist floors at *different heights*: Floridi reserves realism for a bare inferred floor (the *dedomena*, the "recalcitrant and often grating presence of outside realities," never itself constructed) and lets everything meaningful above it become infosphere; the thesis cuts higher, keeping a thick, named realist *universe*. The disagreement is the height of the cut, not realism against anti-realism — which makes Floridi closer than the slogans suggest and the divergence sharper. **Hui converges from the nomos side.** Across the threshold he calls the shift from the *organized inorganic* to the *organizing inorganic* — where the inorganic "is no longer organized by the human body … but rather constitutes an enormous technical system we can only live inside of, while submitting to its rules" — he has technology itself acquiring law-like authority: "technology, as nomos … corresponds to a legitimacy that is prior to legality."[^a13] Hui's "technology becomes nomos" maps onto the *nomos-by-origin* half of self-completion; the *phusis-by-operation* half is the further step the thesis takes and Hui does not.

One predecessor deserves explicit engagement, lest the provocation seem unprecedented. Adorno's *second nature* — the made appearing given, the historical congealing into the seemingly natural, so that "second nature is, in truth, first nature" — is a genuine ancestor of this whole line of thought. But the thesis's contribution is to mark where it *departs* from Adorno. Adorno *re-values* the nature/culture distinction: the made merely *appears* given, as ideological semblance, an imprisonment to be seen through. The thesis does something more drastic — it *dissolves* the nature/culture frame Adorno still presupposes (Chapter I), and replaces it with universe / world / artificiality. The load-bearing claim is therefore neither "the made *looks* given" (Adorno) nor even "the made *operates* as given," but categorial: the made/given vocabulary itself no longer carves anything — which is why the apparatus must be replaced, not merely re-valued.[^a14]

There is one further argument to carry into this chapter — relocated here deliberately from Chapter IV, where the earlier drafts kept it, because it belongs with self-completion rather than with the self-finishing of human life. It concerns *causal efficacy*. A reader on philosophical precision will object that the apparatus is not yet self-complete — that consumer "metaverse" products have stalled, that no closed cybernetic loop runs the world, that the squeeze is therefore speculative. The objection mistakes the level. Chapter I's stratification distinguishes an enduring principle from its full manifestation: a generative tendency is *causally efficacious whether or not it is fully exercised in any given event* — it holds transfactually, and its effects precede its completion.[^a15] The self-completing tendency is operative *now*, shaping institutions, livelihoods, and attention, precisely because it is a real principle and not a finished artefact. The apparatus is causally efficacious *before* it is fully self-complete. This is why the empirical death of consumer XR is no evidence against the diagnosis: the tendency operates at the level of enduring principles, and is visible in its structures and dynamics — algorithmic governance, recommendation systems, automated credentialing — regardless of whether any headset sells.[^a16] The transfactual precedence of the tendency over its completion is the hinge on which Chapter VI's squeeze turns: one does not have to wait for the apparatus to finish in order to be squeezed by it.

## §VB — The minimally-sufficient instantiations, at the level of structures and dynamics

Self-completion is the enduring principle. It does not float free of any material realization; it operates *through* instantiations — and these sit a layer below the real, at the level of structures and dynamics. Four of them are jointly minimally-sufficient: enough, working together, to close the loop that lets the apparatus run without continuous human direction. They are already emerging, even though artificiality is not yet fully self-complete.[^b1]

[^b1]: [VERIFY: viva-passed §V.A four-instantiation specification; Ch-5-3_v3 §3.1. These are at the STRUCTURES/DYNAMICS layer (NOT "actual level"; NOT "the real").]

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

The four are: **artificial intelligence** — an artificial system capable of making decisions on its own; **big data** — the aggregate of exponentially growing information collected from almost every aspect of every individual's day-to-day life; the **Internet of Things** — a network of everyday objects that enables data collection, communication, and operation by and throughout the network; and **code-as-law** — codes that function as laws without third-party jurisdiction, unstoppable once executed by pre-programmed algorithms.[^b2] Improve these and make them work together seamlessly, and a cybernetic system becomes possible: every object people use becomes "smart," part of the Internet of Things; the Internet of Things automates the operation of objects and collects data from every interaction; the data is aggregated and delivered to an artificial intelligence that uses it to decide; the decisions are programmed into codes-as-law with regulatory and commanding power; and the codes, in turn, direct the behaviour of the artificial intelligence and of everything in the network. The loop closes. And if humans cease — voluntarily or not — to participate in its operation and decision-making, they become a mere source of data, their lives directed by the artificial intelligence, regulated by the codes, their thought and behaviour bounded by the affordances of the network.[^b3]

[^b2]: [VERIFY: code-as-law | Lessig 2006, *Code 2.0* (RCYT4KFH). The four are preserved from the viva-passed §V.A; phrased here as instantiations of self-completion, not as the thesis's distinctive level.]

[^b3]: [VERIFY: viva-passed §V.B closed-loop passage, preserved near-verbatim. Mi's "is the metaverse a world?" is answered in Ch-5-2: it is a different KIND of background environment, not a world — handle that boundary question in §VC, not here.]

The reason these four are *minimally* sufficient — neither arbitrary nor merely a list of fashionable acronyms — is that they jointly close a loop in time: the Internet of Things and big data exteriorize the *past* (capture and storage), while the artificial intelligence and code-as-law exteriorize the *future* (anticipation and its enforcement). The contemporary moment is structurally distinctive because it is the first at which all four reach operational sufficiency at once. Earlier epochs had retention without prediction-engines (writing without forecasting), or local anticipation without industrial-scale data, or analogue records with actuarial statistics but without the recursive feedback to close the cycle. Only now does the loop close.[^b4] When it does, the apparatus passes a threshold: it can no longer be regarded merely as a critical component of the world — the human-made habitat that supports survival in the universe — but has become *another background environment comparable to the universe*: we reside in it, we are not in charge of its workings, and we must adapt to survive. This emerging artificial background environment is what the thesis means by the **metaverse**: a realm (-verse) that emerges from the human attempt to go beyond the given (meta-) and turns into a self-referential underlying order.[^b5]

## §VC — The architecture: one enduring principle, several structures and dynamics

It is worth setting the architecture out explicitly, because the layers are easy to conflate and the earlier drafts did conflate them. At the **enduring-principle layer** there is exactly one item: **自足完成化**, self-completion. At the layer of **structures and dynamics** sit the mechanisms through which the principle operates — and these, unlike the principle, are not unique to any one medium. Two of them are worth naming, because together they specify *how* a background environment of this kind governs.[^c1]

[^c1]: [VERIFY: Ch-5-3_v3 — real level holds ONLY 自足完成化; the architectural mechanisms are demoted to structures/dynamics. The plan instructs: enduring-principle layer = self-completion alone; structures/dynamics = Lessig code-as-law + Hui retention↔protention.]

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

The first is **architecture-as-regulation**, the structural property Lessig identified and the thesis generalizes. A sufficiently rich architectural mediation regulates conduct not by issuing a rule to be obeyed but by *determining what is possible* — and it does so automatically, without an enforcing party. Walls regulate physical movement; code regulates digital action; the structural form is identical. As Lessig puts it, architectural constraints "work whether or not the subject knows they are working."[^c2] What is distinctive at the metaverse-stage is that this regulation crosses the physical/virtual divide that earlier architectures kept apart: smart locks implement code-as-law in physical space, automated traffic systems coordinate movement algorithmically, predictive risk-scoring operates code-as-law in the legal-institutional domain, algorithmic allocation does so in the financial. The constraint is everywhere, and everywhere it runs automatically — which is exactly the condition under which the apparatus needs no human in the loop.

[^c2]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Lessig 2006, *Code 2.0*, p.344 (RCYT4KFH) | "Architectural constraints … work whether or not the subject knows they are working." Page-verify exact phrasing.]

The second is the **coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention** — the temporal architecture that makes the loop self-closing. ## §VD — Grammatization, proletarianization, and the shrinking human

If §VA–§VC are the apparatus side, the human side runs in parallel, and the literature has described it under several converging vocabularies. The most developed is Stiegler's. **Grammatization** is the progressive discretization of continuous human flows — speech, gesture, emotion, attention, judgement — into reproducible traces the apparatus can store and operate on. It is an old process: the alphabet grammatized speech, the machine grammatized the artisan's gesture. What is distinctive at the metaverse-stage is its *scope* — affect grammatized into measured emotional response, attention into engagement metrics, relational labour into platform interaction, judgement into the acceptance or rejection of algorithmic recommendation.[^d1]

[^d1]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Stiegler, *Automatic Society* — grammatization passage | Ch-5-5 §1.1. Page TBD.]

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

Grammatization produces what Stiegler calls a three-stage *generalized proletarianization*, each stage marking the loss of a domain of human capacity to the apparatus. First, the loss of *savoir-faire* — work-knowledge, the artisan's hand-skill, externalized into machine-executable operation. Second, the loss of *savoir-vivre* — life-knowledge, the practices of dwelling, leisure, neighbourliness, moral-emotional response, externalized into commodified standard products. Third, the emerging loss of *savoir-théoriser* — theoretical knowledge, the practices of analysis, synthesis, and judgement, externalized into algorithmic systems "quicker than reason," which "short-circuit the work … in which any work of transindividuation consists."[^d2] The third stage is contemporary and contested, and I mark it as *emerging* rather than accomplished.

[^d2]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Stiegler, *Automatic Society* — three-stage proletarianization (savoir-faire / savoir-vivre / savoir-théoriser) + "algorithms quicker than reason" | Ch-5-5 §1.2–§1.3. The savoir-théoriser stage is flagged "emerging," per the author's guardedness note.]

The thesis's distinctive move is not to choose Stiegler's vocabulary over the others but to *re-frame* proletarianization as the human-side of the bidirectional coupling — and, in doing so, to answer a question the convergent literature does not directly address: *why* does the dispossession happen? Where Stiegler describes a one-way loss (the human dispossessed by tertiary retention), the thesis reads it as the structural correlate of self-completion: each delegation is at the same time a constriction; each thing the apparatus learns to do is something the human stops doing, and stops being able to do.[^d3] The point generalizes beyond proletarianization. "Shrinking portion of functionality" is broader than labour-economic dispossession: it names the human's reduced *role-share* in an environment regardless of whether a labour market is at stake — which is why it can hold together Stiegler's proletarianization, Illich's institutional disabling and "modernized poverty," Obara's ecological-biological fragility under capsulization, and Uegaki's existential dispossession as six readings of one structural shrinkage, each from a different stratum.[^d4]

## §VE — Algorithmic governmentality and the transindividuation counter

There is a third register in which the metaverse-stage's effect on human life can be specified: *governmentality*. Following Foucault, governmentality names not force exerted on subjects but the structuring of the field within which subjects act — the conduct of conduct, through the configuration of environments rather than the imposition of commands.[^e1] Digital platforms are governmentality machines in this sense: they govern not by directive but by shaping the choice-architecture — the recommendation, the engagement reward, the interface friction that guides toward the desired and away from the undesired.

[^e1]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Foucault, *Security, Territory, Population* (foucaultSecurityTerritoryPopulation2007), 1977–78 lectures | governmentality as the conduct of conduct. Confirm citekey in Zotero.]

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

What makes the metaverse-stage variant distinctive is that it operates on *dividuals* rather than individuals. Where classical liberal governance addressed the indivisible legal subject — the bearer of rights and deliberative capacities — *algorithmic governmentality*, in Rouvroy's term, addresses the disaggregated data-profile: the collection of behavioural patterns, engagement rhythms, and preference signals that the apparatus optimizes independently of any unified self.[^e2] The individual who deliberates is not governed; the dividual whose patterns are optimized governs the conditions within which the individual's deliberation occurs. The mechanism operates *around* deliberation, shaping its inputs at a level the deliberating self cannot fully observe or contest — and Deleuze's brief sketch of "control societies" already named this turn from the individual to the dividual.[^e3] This is why the squeeze is hard to negotiate individually: the governance is designed to bypass the very level at which individual negotiation would occur.

[^e2]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Rouvroy & Berns 2013, "Algorithmic Governmentality and Prospects of Emancipation"; relayed via Hui *R&C* §39 | the infra-individual (numerised relations) / supra-individual (machine-built profile). Confirm Rouvroy citekey in Zotero.]

[^e3]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Deleuze 1992, "Postscript on the Societies of Control" (deleuzePostscriptSocietiesControl1992, FXBQEQ2C) — the dividual. Confirmed in Zotero.]

Against dividuation, the literature offers a counter-concept that the thesis takes up carefully: *transindividuation*. Drawing on Simondon's account of individuation, Stiegler names by transindividuation the psychic-and-collective individuation by which subjects come to themselves *through* shared technical-symbolic mediation — neither the psychic nor the collective prior to the other, both produced through the technical milieu that carries the community's inheritance.[^e4] The contrast with dividuation is precise, and it is a contrast of time-horizons and aggregation-scales, not a slogan. Algorithmic governmentality operates at the horizon of the data-cycle — seconds to days — and at the scale of the single profile, never consolidating into the longer-term, deliberative, intergenerationally-inherited structures within which psychic-collective individuation occurs. Transindividuation requires exactly those longer horizons and relational scales. So the shrinking human of §VD is not merely deskilled in this or that domain; the shrinking human is *dividuated*, addressed below the level at which individuation has historically been sustained — and to that extent partly cut off from the conditions of its own becoming. Whether the contemporary technical-institutional landscape still holds resources enough to sustain transindividuation against an increasingly dominant dividuating apparatus is an open question the thesis registers without resolving.[^e5]

[^e4]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Simondon 1958/2020, *L'individuation à la lumière des notions de forme et d'information*; Stiegler on transindividuation | Ch-5-6 §3. Simondon likely NOT in thesis-sources — acquisition required (flagged in plan 1b#4).]

## §VG — Bridge to Chapter VI

The chapter has named the apparatus-side tendency — **自足完成化**, self-completion — and fixed its status: it is the one enduring-principle-level feature the metaverse adds, substrate-neutral, not a specific technology; it is best understood through the non-literal provocation that the *made* is acquiring the character of the *given* — *tekhne becoming phusis* — which exposes the inherited nature/culture/technology triad as unworkable and motivates the universe/world/artificiality apparatus in its place; and it is causally efficacious *before* it is complete. The tendency operates through four minimally-sufficient instantiations at the level of structures and dynamics (§VB), governed by architecture-as-regulation and the retention-protention coupling (§VC). Its human-side correlate is the shrinking portion of functionality — grammatization and proletarianization (§VD), dividuation under algorithmic governmentality against the strained counter-tendency of transindividuation (§VE). And it has begun to leave empirical signs: the agent off the loop, the agent internet where humans are "welcome to observe" (§VF).

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

What remains is to say what this *means* for the inhabitants of the world the apparatus is transforming. The world, in Chapter I's vocabulary, is the adaptational operating system a human collectivity built within the universe. When the artificial apparatus the world produced becomes a phusis-like background environment in its own right, the world finds itself *between two* background environments — the universe it has always inhabited, and the metaverse it has produced — each operating by enduring principles of its own, neither commensurable with the other. The universe, it bears repeating, is not a bracketed Kantian unknowable but a thick, causally-engaged *intransitive real*, accessed only perspectivally through the adaptational operating system; that is exactly what lets it bear the footprints of action and *press* on the inhabitant from one side while the metaverse presses from the other.[^g1] The inhabitant is squeezed between them. To articulate that squeeze at structural-ontological precision — its definition, its differentiation from every prior squeeze, its productive as well as its pathological face, and the collapse it risks — is the work of Chapter VI.[^g2]
