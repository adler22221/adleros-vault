## Opener — Modes of artificiality are plural and actual; one of them universalizes

Chapter II established what a world is *made of*: **artificiality**, the umbrella category of all humanly-produced organizing principles — material and nonmaterial alike — by which a world is constituted. Chapter II abstracted away from any particular mode of artificiality to describe the structure *common to all*: exosomatization, virtuality, the irreducibility of artificial-environment laws. This chapter does the opposite. It moves from the *type* to its *instances*, and asks why one instance among many — the mode whose lineage runs from the ancient Mediterranean through early-modern Europe to the present planetary order — has acquired a disposition that the others, on the whole, did not: a disposition to **universalize**, to overwrite the organizing principles of every other world it meets and to present its own made world as the only world there is. That disposition is the load-bearing precondition the squeeze hypothesis needs: the metaverse-stage squeeze of Chapter VI is intelligible only because *this* mode first made its artificiality planetary [VERIFY: squeeze-ontology.md §III-8↔§VI boundary — §III-8 establishes "the modern Western mode is one of many modes" and "modes can fail under pressure"; §VI specifies the metaverse-stage's unique configuration].

A vocabulary note, carried from Chapter II's discipline, must be stated once and held. The thesis does **not** use "technology" as a major concept. Where the philosophy-of-technology tradition writes "technology," this thesis reads *artificiality* in the broad sense. The disposition examined here is therefore not "a disposition built into *technology*" — technicity, the capacity for artificiality, is a human universal (Chapter II) — but a disposition built into one *mode* of artificiality. (The 2026-05-29 scaffold still spoke of "Universal **Technicity**" and "technology" uncorrected; that is fixed throughout here `[RELABEL: technology→artificiality]`.)

## §IIIA — The plurality of actual worlds

The first move relativizes the mode under examination by establishing that the worlds it would universalize over are **really plural** — not as a space of modal alternatives to one real world, but as a multiplicity of *actual* worlds, each a different realization of the operating-system type Chapter I specified. This is precisely the move the current main text opens with: "By putting worlds into perspective, we find the world… we live in as relative instead of universal and absolute. This opens the possibility of a new, concrete sense of **other possible worlds (in contrast to the abstract Leibnizian one)**" [VERIFY: DOCX [P0132]].

The philosophical warrant is Nelson Goodman's *Ways of Worldmaking* (1978), used for one specific job: not "worlds are made, not found" (Chapter I already secures that analytically through niche-construction, Obara's dual ecosystems, and Brague's *kosmos*), but **plurality-of-actual-worlds against the Leibnizian abstraction**. Goodman describes "the movement… from unique truth and a world fixed and found to a diversity of right and even conflicting versions or worlds in the making" [VERIFY: confirmed | goodmanWaysWorldmaking1978 (Zotero WZEEWC4D) | Foreword, p. ix]. Crucially — and this is the disambiguation the main text flags — these are **not** Leibnizian *possible* worlds: "we are not speaking in terms of multiple possible alternatives to a single actual world but of multiple [actual worlds]" [VERIFY: confirmed | goodmanWaysWorldmaking1978 | Ch. I §2, pp. 2–3]. The plurality at issue is a plurality of *actual* worlds, each a different mode of artificiality realized under different conditions, none of which is the world "fixed and found." The `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` mode is, in this light, one such actual world — a remaking of worlds "already on hand" [VERIFY: confirmed | goodmanWaysWorldmaking1978 | Ch. I §4, p. 7 — but note Ch-1-11 §B may already use this "remaking" passage; cross-reference rather than re-quote to avoid double-treatment], not the universal terminus its self-understanding takes it to be.

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

One borrowing-discipline must be kept explicit, because a committee reader on philosophical precision (Mi) will check it. The thesis takes Goodman's plurality of *actual* worlds but **not** his full irrealism. Goodman denies any single world independent of symbol systems; this thesis is **realist about the universe** — the substrate "neither a god nor a man made" — and constructivist only about **worlds** nested within it [VERIFY: goodmanWaysWorldmaking1978, Ch. I §2 "versions… can be treated as our worlds" — cite for plurality, do NOT import the denial of an observer-independent substrate; mark the divergence in a one-line footnote per Ch-3-9 §goodman-realism]. The borrowing is selective: Goodman's plurality, not his anti-realism about the given. `[AUTHOR SYNTHESIS — the selective-borrowing footnote is the thesis's positioning of Goodman, not Goodman's own qualification.]`

From the recognition of plurality follows a constraint the chapter must honour. Any account of one world's mode of artificiality — including the mode whose disposition this chapter examines — is necessarily *one account from one position among others*. The thesis does not claim a view from nowhere. What it claims is that Chapter I's apparatus (adaptational operating system, irreducible organizing principles, incommensurability-without-incompatibility) permits a comparative description that does not presuppose any one world's principles as the standard. The chapter's account of the `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` mode is offered in that spirit: a structural description of a specific configuration, not the denunciation of one culture by another. This is also the first half of the response to the cherry-picking worry that §IIIE will complete: the claim is not "this mode dominates and the others do not," but "this mode has a *structurally distinctive disposition* whose character requires explanation" [VERIFY: Ch-3-4_v2 §A1 preamble — reformulate the disposition claim so counter-evidence is constitutive, not refuting].

## §IIIB — The material basis of this mode's universalization

That this mode universalized is not a brute fact about cultural superiority; it has a traceable **material basis**. The current main text already places the relevant claim under the heading "The material basis for mechanistic intervention's self-reinforcement," and already states that this mode "was an exception rather than the commonplace" [VERIFY: DOCX [P0134]]. The job of this section is to *ground* that claim — to replace the Needham paraphrase that a committee member flagged as "name-dropping" (馬: "Needham is name-dropping — his voice must come out") with Needham's own voice.

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

Joseph Needham — biochemist and Sinologist, lead author of *Science and Civilisation in China* — spent half a century on a double question, and his answer roots divergent worldviews in divergent **material conditions**: geography → mode of production → worldview. The single most citable statement of the triangle, now upgraded to verbatim, is the *Grand Titration* "four factors" passage:

> "So we come to the fundamental question, why did modern science not arise in China? **The key probably lies in the four factors: geographical, hydrological, social and economic.**… On the geographical side, China is a continental country, bound to agriculture, not an archipelago teeming with maritime mercantile city-states, as Europe always was… **Correspondingly the imperial bureaucracy gained in importance, and while on the one hand it prevented any recrudescence of feudalism, it also absolutely prevented the rise of the merchants and the coming into being of capitalism.**"
> [VERIFY: confirmed verbatim | Needham, *The Grand Titration*, essay "On Science and Social Change" | NLM "Joseph Needham" notebook, source 7c55bfb3 | ch3-6-verbatim §5a — page TBD, locate in print]

The geography→production layer feeds a **worldview** layer, and here Needham makes the co-emergence explicit in his own words — the organicism-vs-atomism passage from *Science and Civilisation* Vol. 2:

> "…can we consider it a mere coincidence that the former [Taoist organicism] arose in a highly organised society where conservancy-dictated bureaucratism was dominant while the latter [Democritean-Epicurean atomism] arose in a world of City-states and individual merchant-adventurers? I believe that we cannot… **Chinese bureaucratism was fundamentally agrarian, and based upon agricultural production in a context of irrigation and water-control; as opposed to the maritime emphasis of the European city-states.**"
> [VERIFY: confirmed verbatim | needhamScienceCivilisationChina (SCC Vol. 2) | pp. 337–338 (model-reported — verify against print) | NLM source 8d2da12e | ch3-6-verbatim §5b]

Needham even names the reflexive relation the thesis needs — worldview as the "mirror image" of the production-grounded social order: "the entire system of correlative organismic thinking was in one sense **the mirror image of Chinese bureaucratic society**" [VERIFY: confirmed verbatim | SCC Vol. 2, p. 288 (model-reported) | NLM 8d2da12e | ch3-6-verbatim §5c]. And the *materialist* underwriting — which keeps this from being idealist intellectual history — is stated in the Vol. 7 Pt. 2 *General Conclusions*, the volume actually held in Zotero: "the importance of ideology, however great, can never obscure the basic fact that underneath lie the **material forces of climate, geography and social integration**" [VERIFY: confirmed verbatim | needhamScienceCivilisationChina2004 (SCC Vol. 7 Pt. 2) | Zotero 5DYVKEM6 | NLM 8eb70551 | ch3-6-verbatim §5e — confirm page against attached PDF].

The non-interventionist conception was "propitious for the development of the natural sciences" even as it had limits, against the "occidental 'interventionism', so natural to a people of shepherds and sea-farers" [VERIFY: confirmed | Needham SCC Vol.7 Pt.2 §50a p.17 | Ch-3-10 Passage 3]. Second, the bridge from Needham's descriptive "interventionism" to the thesis's "**universalization**" is the author's, not Needham's — though Needham himself names a "mechanistic imperative… the more complete the domination of Nature, the more fully does it become possible for ruling elites to increase their control of individual human behaviour" [VERIFY: confirmed | Needham SCC §50 p.75 | Ch-3-10 Passage 4]. `[AUTHOR SYNTHESIS — Needham supplies the material-civilizational mechanism; the ontological diagnosis "universalization" is the thesis's.]` And the inherited word "technology" in Needham's "science, technology, and institutions" should be read through the thesis's broad sense of *artificiality* `[RELABEL: technology→artificiality]`.

The point of recalling them is structural, not romantic: each is a *real* mode of artificiality whose organizing principles are not derivable from universe-level laws, and whose configuration did not generate the universalizing disposition.

- **Islamic civilisational science** developed mathematical sophistication of the highest order — the Maragha school's Tusi-couple and Ibn al-Shatir's lunar models, transmitted into Renaissance astronomy — within a *contemplative* cosmology rather than an interventionist one [VERIFY: confirmed | poskett2022Horizons (489GZ4B9) on Maragha→Renaissance transmission, nlm-excerpt "the Tusi couple had a profound influence on the development of new astronomical ideas in Europe" — page TBD; Saliba *Islamic Science and the Making of the European Renaissance* would deepen this — Zotero 3GSDCXKD, nlm:pending, acquire/page before citing].

## §IIIC — The ancient-Greek epistemic fault line

The material basis explains *that* the mode could reach planetary scale once some sufficient configuration came online; it does not yet explain the *character* of what was unleashed — the totalizing, all-or-nothing form of its universalization. That character has an **epistemic** condition, and it is genealogically older than the modern period: a fault line in the Greek philosophical inheritance. This is the section a committee member asked to have clarified and evidenced (馬: "concepts要澄清 / evidence要加強").

"Fault line" does not name a biographical fact about Plato and Aristotle. It names a *structural tension constitutive of Western philosophy's development* — the unresolved oscillation between two poles that Roy Bhaskar traces across the whole span of Western thought: "Philosophy was thus stuck—on what I am going to characterize as the **Platonic/Aristotelian fault-line**" [VERIFY: confirmed | bhaskarPlatoEtc (88YLH48Y, nlm:synced) | p.4 | NLM UUID 5f7e7ef5 | ch3-6_v2]. The **Platonic pole** treats the universal, formal, mathematically necessary as what is most real; the **Aristotelian pole** insists on immanence but accepts formal cause as the organizing principle. Neither resolves the gap between universal and particular; the oscillation repeats from the medieval universals debates through rationalism-vs-empiricism to the present [VERIFY: ch3-6_v2 §1; do NOT overclaim — the thesis's claim is that the Platonic pole was *structurally dominant in shaping the institutions of modernity*, not that all Western thought is Platonic].

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

The mechanism by which the Platonic pole produces universalization is what Bhaskar calls the **epistemic fallacy**: "the analysis or definition of statements about being in terms of statements about our knowledge (of being)… the ontification of knowledge… characteristically spawning what, following Adorno, can be called '**identity-thinking**'" [VERIFY: confirmed | bhaskarPlatoEtc | p.201 (Glossary) + pp.103–104 | NLM 5f7e7ef5 | ch3-6_v2]. When knowledge is taken as the measure of reality, whatever cannot be known in the privileged mode (formal, universal, calculable) is denied full reality. **Totalization** is this fallacy at the level of institutions: the drive to render all of nature and society available to technical-scientific reason, so that what resists — the contextual, the contingent, the irreducibly particular — is treated as a residue to be eliminated rather than a feature of reality to be respected. **Exclusion** is its complement: the undatafiable is not merely left unexplained but actively marginalized — denied ontological status, dismissed as pre-scientific, deprecated as backward. The chapter can keep this triad (fault line → totalization → exclusion) compact and let the reader feel its force without the full Bhaskar apparatus [VERIFY: ch3-6_v2 §2–§3; the false-binary objection — that there are Heraclitean/Stoic/Epicurean counter-traditions — is answered by the weaker claim: the Platonic pole became the organizing logic of *the institutions of modernity*, not that counter-traditions don't exist].

A crucial discipline, demanded by the same source draft and by the §IIIE response: the genealogical claim is held at **strand level**, not tradition level. What Adorno-Horkheimer, Heidegger, and Derrida diagnose is not the whole classical inheritance but a *productionist-substantialist strand* within it [VERIFY: Ch-3-4_v2 §A5 strand-genealogical position; defenders Hadot/MacIntyre/Gadamer/Strauss show the tradition is internally plural and contains genuine anti-domination resources — concede this substantively]. The disposition is the historically actualized outcome of *one strand*, when detached from its corrective practices and scaled in modernity — not the necessary outcome of "the Greeks." `[AUTHOR SYNTHESIS — the strand/tradition distinction is the thesis's load-bearing move to survive the "you've indicted all of philosophy" objection.]`

## §IIID — Universalization in operation

The material basis (§IIIB) and the epistemic fault line (§IIIC) are *dispositions* — generative tendencies. The question this section answers is what universalization **does** when those dispositions operate at planetary scale. The answer reframes a familiar moral category as an ontological mechanism, and it is the mechanism Chapter VI will find again in the metaverse.

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

Begin from a fact Chapter I makes available: components transfer across worlds at *different costs depending on the depth at which they operate*. Using Chapter I's three layers of description — (1) the **enduring conditions or principles** that (2) **organize things into structures or dynamics**, which then (3) are **manifested in the context-specific phenomena experienceable or observable to a subject** [VERIFY: DOCX [P0106] — this is the de-jargoned CR stratification; use THIS language, not "real/actual/empirical"] — the cost of cross-world transfer scales with the layer touched. At the level of context-specific phenomena (layer 3), a recipe, a tool, a textual genre transfers with low conversion cost. At the level of structures and dynamics (layer 2), an institution or practice (a parliamentary form, an examination system, a market mechanism) requires functional re-coding to operate within the recipient world. At the level of **enduring conditions or principles** (layer 1) — the recipient world's foundational mode of artificiality — re-embedding is no longer adjustment but *replacement*: the recipient world's organizing principles are overwritten by the imposing world's. **This is the structure of colonization.** [VERIFY: Ch-3-4_v2 §A3 + the §III.4 v1 colonization-as-real-level-imposition material — but note: v1 used "real/actual/empirical"; this scaffold relabels to [P0106]'s three layers per the de-jargon instruction.]

Colonization, on this reading, is not best understood as a contingent moral injury layered onto neutral cultural exchange. It is the name for what occurs when imposition at the deepest layer *succeeds* in displacing one world's organizing principles with another's. That the colonizing operation has been morally evaluated, resisted, and partially undone does not change the analytic point: the *mechanism* of universalization, at its sharpest, operates at the level of enduring principles, not at the level of phenomena. This is the engaged version of the colonial-imposition point the material-basis section gestures at — exploiting external resources "from the underground and from colonies" liberated the mode from its organic constraints and reinforced the very worldview it embodied [VERIFY: DOCX [P0135]; 三浦永光 2004 (miuraKankyoShisoTo2006); Pomeranz 2021 (Y6Z824VX, nlm:synced) on the Atlantic resource-frontier].

Two large diagnoses of the contemporary condition operate at this level and need correction rather than endorsement. **Fukuyama's "end of history"** is best read not as a prediction but as an articulation, *from within*, of one mode's disposition toward universalization — the linearization of plural evolution into a single end-state [VERIFY: Fukuyama 1992 — read as documenting the disposition, not establishing the destiny; Ch-1-11 §C CR-corrected; **Fukuyama not confirmed in Zotero — acquire before citing**]. **Huntington's "clash of civilizations"** gets one thing right — that tensions between worlds at the level of enduring principles are real and cannot be dissolved by fiat — but fails by conflating those principle-level differences with a *geopolitical* map, committing the cultural essentialism Sen and Said diagnose [VERIFY: Huntington 1996; Sen 2006 *Identity and Violence*; Said 2001 "The Clash of Ignorance" — **confirm each in Zotero**; Ch-1-11 §C]. Hui's question exposes the cartography's inadequacy: is the PRC's surveillance-and-credit complex "Western" or "Eastern"? Its components are, at the level of enduring principles, `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]`-mode in origin, even when mobilized against that mode's hegemony [VERIFY: huiQuestionConcerningTechnologyChina2016 cosmotechnical question]. The thesis preserves Huntington's recognition of principle-level incommensurability and discards his map.

## §IIIE — Counterarguments

Three counter-positions deserve explicit engagement; a committee member asked precisely for this (米: "選取幾個喜歡的論點 / 要consider不同的觀點 / 要說明為何favor自己選擇的論點"). The response to each preserves what is correct in the objection while showing why it does not undermine the chapter's claim.

**Technicity = artificiality is universal; *this disposition* is one mode's.** The first objection holds that a disposition to dominate is not specifically this mode's but a universal feature of complex artificiality — every world tool-makes, institution-builds, intervenes in its environment (the Sinosphere's own 大禹治水 hydraulic engineering, Chinese imperial deforestation). What is correct: technicity — the capacity for artificiality — *is* universal; every world is an operating system with organizing principles that direct its inhabitants [VERIFY: Ch-3-1 Position A]. What the objection misses is the distinction between a *mode* of artificiality and its **disposition relative to other modes**. Most modes have organized their relations with other modes through exchange, conflict, alliance, syncretism, and coexistence that do *not* require displacing the other mode's organizing principles. This mode's disposition is structurally distinct: it treats its own principles as generic and other modes' as local approximations or failures — exactly what §IIIC's fault line names and §IIID's colonization formalizes. The relabel is load-bearing here: in v1 this was "Universal **Technicity**"; corrected, **technicity = *artificiality* is universal; this *disposition* is one mode's** `[RELABEL: technology/technicity→artificiality]`.

[AI-SIMULATED-SCAFFOLD]

**Internal Western critique.** The second objection holds that the chapter's account is already available within the mode's own self-critical traditions — Frankfurt School, post-structuralism, decolonial theory, science studies — so the thesis is redundant. What is correct: significant resources for the critique exist within the mode's reflexive traditions, and the chapter draws on them (Adorno and Horkheimer in §IIIC; Said and Sen in §IIID) [VERIFY: Ch-3-1 Position B]. What the objection misses: the existence of an internal critique *confirms* rather than refutes the structure it criticizes — that Romanticism arose as reaction to instrumentalism shows the disposition was powerful enough to generate reactive formation. And the internal critiques typically operate at the level of *specific institutional failures* (layer 2/3) without articulating the *enduring-principle* level (layer 1) at which the disposition operates and which connects it to the squeeze. The internal traditions are sources, not redundancies. The thesis triangulates: internal critique (Frankfurt) and external comparison (Needham, Hui) reach the same structural feature from different angles.

**Positivism of anti-positivism.** The third objection holds that calling "the `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` mode" a real entity with a real disposition reproduces the very totalizing operation the chapter criticizes — and that the source-selection (Needham, Poskett, Frankfurt, Brague) is one-sided, picking critics over defenders of Enlightenment rationality (Pinker, Ferguson) [VERIFY: Ch-3-1 Position C]. What is correct: the thesis must avoid hypostatizing a configuration that is internally diverse, multiply-contested, and historically variable; and a fully rigorous treatment would acknowledge that its sources share a critical orientation. What the objection misses: the analytic distinction between a **configuration** and a **culture**. The thesis tracks a configuration — a specific arrangement of material, epistemic, and institutional principles whose dispositional features can be characterized without flattening the diversity of its instances — not a homogeneous bloc. Hui's point recurs: the configuration's components circulate well beyond the geographical West (Shenzhen, Bangalore, São Paulo), and the disposition operates wherever they are adopted at the level of enduring principles. The thesis tracks a configuration, not a culture. And its framework is **structural-environmental, not moral-evaluative**: it does not argue Enlightenment rationality is worthless, only that this configuration tends toward totalization — a structural claim that does not require resolving the normative debate [VERIFY: Ch-3-1 §"Positivism of Anti-Positivism" + the combinatorial-structure synthesis in Ch-3-4_v2 §A3: the disposition's global *scale* comes from a five-component compound — Christian universalism + substance-metaphysics + colonial extractive base + Newtonian mechanism + capitalist accumulation — no one component unique, but no other tradition integrated all five; this is what makes the claim falsifiable and non-essentialist].

## Close — one mode among many, and the precondition it sets

The chapter has argued that the `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` mode is **one mode of artificiality among many actual worlds** (§IIIA, §IIIF), and that its current planetary dominance rests on a specific compound: a **material basis** (the geography→production→worldview triangle, §IIIB), an **epistemic fault line** that gives its universalization its totalizing form (§IIIC), and a **mechanism** — imposition at the level of enduring principles — by which it has overwritten other worlds (§IIID). These operate together: the material basis enables global reach; the fault line organizes how the reach is intellectually justified; the imposition is what the disposition *does* when it meets other modes. The counterarguments (§IIIE) sharpen rather than defeat the claim, because the claim is about a *configuration* with a *combinatorial* structure, held at *strand* level, framed *structurally* rather than morally.

What the chapter sets up is the precondition the squeeze needs. Universalization is the disposition by which a *made* world comports itself as the *given* universe — and its terminal form, where the made hardens into an actual background substrate that confronts humans the way nature does, is the metaverse-stage claim reserved for Chapters V and VI. Chapter IV turns inward, to what universalization does to the *inhabitants* of the mode whose disposition has succeeded at planetary scale — Uegaki's pursuit of 〈意のままになる生〉 (life-as-willed) and the self-finishing tendency it produces. Chapter V examines the parallel tendency at the level of artificiality itself; Chapter VI shows how these, together with the universalization mechanism of the present chapter, produce the structural condition the thesis calls the squeeze.[^philosophy-role]
