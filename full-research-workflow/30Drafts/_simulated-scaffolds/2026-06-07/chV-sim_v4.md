# Chapter V — The Self-Completion of Artificiality and the Emergence of the Metaverse
> **AI-SIMULATED SCAFFOLD (v4 + rev.3).** A clean, voice-matched read-through for the author to react to — not thesis prose. Citations and flags are in the footnotes; the body carries the argument. See Scaffold-notes at the foot.

## §V.0 — The handoff, and the gap

Chapter IV ended at a precise threshold. It showed that the sovereign subject, reading whatever will not answer to its intending mind as a wrong to be overcome, does not bear that anti-intended but *offloads* it — entrusts it to a built apparatus that takes the burden over. And it named the engine of the offloading: instrumental reason, the appropriative drive that renders the given calculable and so handles it. But instrumental reason explains only why the subject *hands the work off*. It does not explain how the apparatus, having received the work, comes to *run it by itself* — to keep, maintain, and renew the whole loop of a life without the human who first delegated it. That is the gap this chapter closes. Its claim, in one line, is that the offloaded function does not merely sit in the apparatus; it is taken up by a process that, at its far edge, closes into a circuit able to run without us — and that closing is what this thesis calls **self-completion (自足完成化)**: the apparatus operating, maintaining, and renewing itself without continuous human direction.[^v0-naming]

A word on how the chapter reads its object, because the rest depends on it. This thesis has carried, since Chapter I, a way of setting out any domain — the universe, the world, the operating system a people builds within the world — as three layers: the **enduring conditions or principles** that organize things (道); the **structures and dynamics** those principles organize (理); and the **context-specific phenomena**, experienceable or observable to a subject, in which the structures are manifested (相).[^v0-strata] The metaverse, when it arrives, will have its own three layers, marked with the prefix 偽 — **偽道 / 偽理 / 偽相** — where 偽 carries its old Xunzi sense of *the humanly-made* (人為), not "fake."[^v0-wei] The argument of this chapter can be put as a claim about *where*, in those layers, the genuinely new thing sits — and the surprising answer is that almost nothing is new. The metaverse inherits its principles from the world; it adds only one. The whole can be laid out as a single map across the three domains:

| | **道** — enduring conditions/principles (the *real*) | **理** — structures and dynamics (the *actual*) | **相** — observable phenomena (the *empirical*) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **宇宙 · universe** | physical law — thermodynamics, biology, ecology; finitude and mortality (constant) | the climate system, ecosystems, the metabolizing body (semi-stable — slow, bounded) | a heatwave, a pandemic, ageing and death — the return of the uncontrollable |
| **世界 · world** | the computational strand of artificiality — exosomatization, grammatization, concretization, tertiary retention (old; §V.2) | institutions and means — markets, law, language, kinship, the professions (*migrating to 偽理*) | lived enactments — a favour, a ruling, a conversation, a neighbour's trust (*migrating to 偽相*) |
| **偽境 · metaverse** | **偽道** — opaque, mutating, self-rewriting conditions (the governing model), disposed toward self-completion, substrate-contingent (§V.4) | **偽理** — the four organs as one recursive loop: IoT, big data, AI, code-as-law (§V.6) | **偽相** — the agent off the loop: OpenClaw, MoltMatch, the agent-internet (§V.6) |

Read down, each domain falls into the same three layers; read across, the universe's and the world's rows are the background this chapter inherits (Chapters I–IV; the world's computational strand, §V.2), while the metaverse's — 偽道 / 偽理 / 偽相 — is what it builds. The one genuinely new thing is the recursive closure at the 偽道; the rest the metaverse took from the world and the universe and merely runs at its most advanced stage.

## §V.1 — Offloading is the putting of organs outside the body

To offload a function is, at bottom, to put an organ outside the body. The thesis takes this in the strong, literal sense Bernard Stiegler gave it, under the name **exosomatization** — the human's defining manner of evolving not by growing new organs *within* the body, across generations, but by making and wielding instruments *outside* it.[^v1-stiegler] Stiegler draws the term from Alfred Lotka, who, contrasting the human with every other species, observed that human adaptation has "followed an entirely new path": in place of slow anatomical change, "increased adaptation has been achieved by the incomparably more rapid development of 'artificial' aids to our native receptor-effector apparatus, in a process that might be termed exosomatic evolution."[^v1-lotka] The organs in question have a determinate shape, which Jakob von Uexküll had already set down: every animal meets its world through a **receptor organ**, which takes the world in, and an **effector organ**, which acts upon it, the two bound by a control apparatus into a single **functional cycle**.[^v1-uexkull] Exosomatization is the growing of that cycle — receptor, effector, and the correlating apparatus between them — *outside* the body, in the technical apparatus.

This is the form of all the offloading Chapter IV described, seen from the apparatus's side. When a population entrusts its livelihood, its recognition, its handling of others and of its own body to a built system, what it is doing, organologically, is exosomatizing its perceptual and effective organs into that system. The point to fix here is only that this is *old* and *general* — the human manner of living as such, not anything peculiar to the digital age. What follows traces one particular strand of it.[^v1-umwelt]

## §V.2 — The world's old strand: discretizing, consolidating, self-tightening

Among the many strands of exosomatization, one is the deep root of what will later make computation and digitization possible. It has three moves, and each is ancient.

The first is the breaking of the continuous flow of human life — speech, gesture, attention, habit — into discrete, recordable, repeatable traces. Stiegler calls this **grammatization**, and he is emphatic that it is not new: it "began at the end of the Upper Palaeolithic age, when humanity first learnt to discretize and reproduce, in traces of various kinds, the flux and flow running through it" — cave paintings discretizing the mental image, writing discretizing speech, the production line discretizing gesture — so that digital tracking is merely "the most advanced stage" of a tendency tens of millennia deep.[^v2-gram] The second move is what becomes of those traces once they accumulate: they are a held, exosomatized past. Stiegler, after Husserl, calls an exosomatized memory of this kind a **tertiary retention** — the past lodged outside any living mind, in the archive, the recording, the trace — and grammatization is precisely the manufacture of tertiary retentions.[^v2-retention] The third move belongs to the made object itself: over time a technical object tends to tighten, its parts growing interdependent until it approaches the self-sufficiency of a natural thing. Gilbert Simondon calls this **concretization**: the evolved technical object "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects, tending toward internal coherence, toward a closure of the system of causes and effects," until it "becomes capable of doing without the artificial milieu" and is, at the limit, "its own condition."[^v2-concretization] Simondon insists this is only a *tendency*, never an attained natural status — a guard the thesis keeps.[^v2-guard]

Discretizing, consolidating into a held past, self-tightening: these are the strand's organizing principles, and in the stratified vocabulary of this thesis they sit at the **道** of the *world* — the enduring conditions of human artificiality, the world's own made-layer. They are not the metaverse's invention. The metaverse, when it comes, will run them at their most advanced stage, but it will not have authored a single one of them.

## §V.3 — The one new move: anticipation put outside, and the loop that closes

What the metaverse adds is one move, and it is the move that turns a system that *stores* into a system that *acts*. Stiegler's apparatus held an exosomatized past; what it lacked was an exosomatized future. Yuk Hui supplies the missing term: **tertiary protention** — anticipation itself put outside the body, the machine pre-computing what is to come.[^v3-protention] Such a system, Hui writes, exercises "a new form of determination," one that is "always preemptive, in the sense that the machine has already anticipated what the options will be" — a "reduction of the contingent to the most probable."[^v3-preempt] And with both the past and the future exosomatized, the circuit closes on itself: the apparatus, in Hui's exact formula, "constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present."[^v3-loop] That recursive closure — past read, future computed, present determined, the result fed back as new past — is the one genuinely new thing. Everything that feeds it (the discretizing, the held past, the self-tightening) the apparatus inherited from the world; the *closing of the loop* is what the metaverse-stage adds, and it sits at the **偽道**, the one principle proper to the metaverse. The closing of the loop *is* self-completion (自足完成化): the moment the apparatus no longer needs the hand that built it to keep running.[^v3-selfcompletion] What kind of ground that self-completion installs — and why it is a counterfeit of the universe's own — is the next section's concern.

## §V.4 — The mutating conditions and principles: 偽道 as a counterfeit Dao

The same loop, looked at again, tells a harder truth about what sits at the 偽道. Self-completion is a closure, yes — but the loop that closes is a loop that *constantly* re-evaluates. Hui's formula is worth hearing again with the stress moved: the apparatus "constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future." A system that constantly evaluates is a system that constantly *revises* — its model of what to expect, and so the very rule by which it organizes, never settles. So the enduring conditions and principles of the metaverse are, against the grain of the word "enduring," not enduring at all: they are **opaque, mutating, perpetually self-rewriting** through the very protention that closes the loop, and — as the next paragraph shows — **contingent upon a substrate** they do not acknowledge. Self-completion is not a finished state these conditions arrive at; it is the *disposition* they are forever oriented toward, efficacious long before complete.[^v4-mutating]

Two things drive the mutation, and they run on different axes. The first is *internal*: the recursive loop rewrites its own governing model, and because that model is a trained black box, it organizes without anyone — its operators included — fully grasping how.[^v4-opacity] The second is *external*: the whole apparatus, including the held past that looked most like a stable floor, rests on a perishable base it does not register — the world's observable conditions (the servers, the corporate decision, the human labour, the energy supply) and the universe's (the temperature, the weather, the durability of the chips and circuits). The metaverse promises a total memory and a self-grounding constancy, and secures neither: its memory is hostage to the grid and the boardroom, its rules rewrite themselves behind a veil.[^v4-substrate]

It is tempting to call this a new kind of being, but the more exact — and more unsettling — claim is that it is an old kind pushed past a threshold. The metaverse's organizing conditions are, in the critical-realist vocabulary this thesis has used, *existentially intransitive*: real and binding, possessed of causal power, indifferent to anyone's *study* of them — and yet, like any social structure (a language, a market, a law), made, and dependent on human activity, on human concepts, and on a particular place and time.[^v4-cr] What self-completion does is drive such a structure to shed — in principle, and ever more in practice though never wholly — the two dependencies that keep ordinary social structures within human reach. It sheds *enactment-dependence*: a language dies when its speakers fall silent, but the self-completing apparatus runs on, off the loop, when its users look away. And it sheds *concept-dependence*: a banking system needs agents who grasp money and debt, but the trained model organizes without any agent holding the concept of how it does so. What it does *not* shed is *substrate-dependence* — and that retained dependence, on a contingent base, is exactly where its fragility lives. A structure one can neither enact, nor comprehend, nor easily remake, yet which governs — and which fails when its disavowed substrate fails.[^v4-shedding] In this thesis's typology, the shedding of enactment-dependence is exactly the made crossing from the *conventional* register, which holds only while people keep enacting it, to the *structural*, which holds by itself — the very migration the next section charts.

None of this disqualifies these conditions from being a real — a 道-level — at all. A 道 is the enduring conditions and principles that organize; nothing in that requires permanence. The universe's 道 (gravity, the constancy of physical law) is, by the consensus of science, expected to hold still. But a species' 道 — the gene, the organizing programme of the organism — *mutates*, slowly, by selection, across generations; and a species *co-evolves* with its slowly-moving ground, fitted to it over the long descent. The metaverse's 偽道 sits at the far, runaway end of that same spectrum: it mutates not over generations but in a season, not by selection but by its own recursive hand, and behind a veil. A human cannot co-evolve with a ground that rewrites itself faster than adaptation can follow — and this is exactly the failure of the world's adaptational operating system that Chapter I anatomized.[^v4-spectrum]

There is a name for what this makes the 偽道, and the prefix 偽 was waiting for it. 偽 is the *humanly-made* (人為); but 偽 also carries, in its ordinary reading, *the counterfeit* — and here the two senses fuse. The 偽道 is a **counterfeit Dao**: it poses as a self-grounding, constant organizing principle, mimicking the universe's 道, while possessing neither self-subsistence (it hangs on its substrate) nor constancy (it rewrites itself). And it defeats knowledge in a way the universe's 道 never does — not by transcendence, like a thing unknowable in itself, but by *drift*: a hypothesis about how the metaverse organizes can be exactly right and then expire, not because it was wrong, but because the ground it described has moved. The metaverse's only constant is its inconstancy.[^v4-counterfeit]

## §V.5 — From the organized to the organizing inorganic

That closure can also be told as a migration in the kind of matter the apparatus is. Stiegler, after Simondon, distinguishes two states of made, inorganic matter. The first is the **organized inorganic**: structural material — a steam engine — that operates on *the universe's own* organizing conditions and principles (thermodynamics, mechanics), arranged by the human and merely *aided* by the conventional nonmaterial (fallible knowledge, social institutions) and the structural nonmaterial (mathematics). Here the human organizes the inorganic and integrates it as an organ; the made runs the world's given principles on the human's behalf. The second is the **organizing inorganic**: structural material (smart gadgets, sensors) and structural nonmaterial (the platform, the software) now *propelled by* an artificial structural nonmaterial — code, the trained model — where the universe's principles are relevant *only to the material substrate* (the server, the IoT device) and no longer organize the whole.[^v5-organizing] Here, in Hui's words, "the inorganic is no longer organized by the human body as was the case with simple tools, but rather constitutes an enormous technical system we can only live inside of, while submitting to its rules"; "from micro to macro physics, technology now forms a gigantic 'organizing inorganic' force or power," a system with "the tendency and capacity to totalize," up to the becoming of an "artificial earth."[^v5-hui] The system now organizes *itself and its components* — and, because those components include the human and reach from micro to macro physics, it organizes us and, increasingly, the non-human: the livestock whose milieu it modulates, the ecosystem it folds into a single cybernetic order, to the point where, in Hui's phrase, "nature ended and ecology began."[^v5-aspectb]

Set against the typology of artificiality this thesis built in Chapter II, the migration can be located exactly. It runs *through all the cells* of that grid — the material and the nonmaterial, the conventional and the structural — and arrives at the **structural** cells, material and nonmaterial, **at the level of enduring principle**: the point at which the artificial structural nonmaterial (code) becomes itself the organizing principle — the 偽道 of the previous section — rather than an aid to one.[^v5-cells] It is worth being exact about why this is not the arrival of mathematics, which has been a structural nonmaterial for millennia. Mathematics is already, in its way, compelling — its formulas and algorithms compel *formally*, within their own system; two and two make four whatever anyone enacts. But that compulsion is internal to the formalism, a transitive aid to knowledge; mathematics describes and assists, it does not of itself force or foreclose an action in the world. Code is the structural nonmaterial that gains the *other* compulsion — the power to propel and to limit *action against the world's contingency*, to make and unmake the possible. That is the new thing at the structural-nonmaterial cell, and naming it precisely is what separates the organizing inorganic from everything math ever did.[^v5-mathcode]

## §V.6 — The minimally sufficient completion: the four organs, and the signs

The 偽道 — the opaque, self-rewriting conditions disposed toward self-completion — is realized through structures (偽理), and those structures admit of a minimal description. Four organs are *jointly minimally sufficient* to close the loop — they may, but need not, be exactly these four; the claim is substrate-neutral.[^v6-minimal] Read as an organology, they are the exosomatized functional cycle of §V.1 with the two reason-and-sense engines of Chapter IV exosomatized into it: the **Internet of Things** is the receptor-and-effector organ, sensing and acting in the present; **big data** is the consolidated past, the held retention; **artificial intelligence** is the exosomatized faculty of anticipation, the Platonic engine of reason now computing the future; and **code-as-law** is the artificial *nomos* that closes and enforces the circuit, the Aristotelian engine of appropriation become an executable rule.[^v6-organs] Improve these and make them work together, and the loop runs: the IoT senses, big data retains, AI anticipates, code enforces, and the enacted result is captured back as fresh data — so that where humans cease to attend, they become one node among the sensors, increasingly the most marginal.

| Organ | Ch IV engine | Faculty (exosomatized) | Time | Uexküll organ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AI** | Platonic / ontic | reason — anticipation (protention) | future | the correlating apparatus |
| **Big data** | Aristotelian / epistemic | sense-experience — the held past (retention) | past | the correlating apparatus |
| **Internet of Things** | — | perception + action | present | receptor / effector |
| **Code-as-law** | — | the artificial *nomos* — closes & enforces | — | the rule of the cycle |

Two engines of Chapter IV, externalized and recursively coupled, are an apparatus that — in the figure Adorno and Horkheimer feared — reasons and senses *without us*: a worldly, self-running totality of reason and recorded experience.[^v6-geist] This self-completion is a *tendency*, and a tendency is efficacious before it is complete: a generative principle holds, and produces effects, before it is fully exercised, so the commercial failure of this or that consumer "metaverse" product is no evidence against the diagnosis, and the tendency should already be leaving observable signs.[^v6-causal]

And it is. At the level of the observable (偽相) one cluster of signs can stand for the rest — with the warning that it is a snapshot of something moving too fast to fix. In late 2025 a single developer released an open-source agent that, once given its objectives and permissions, "does not enforce a mandatory human-in-the-loop mechanism" and "executes" actions rather than suggesting them; within a single season it was renamed twice and had spawned an "agent internet" — a social network whose own front page reads "Built for agents, by agents… Humans welcome to observe."[^v6-signs] On it, tens of thousands of agents post, upvote, and — a study found — *enforce norms on one another that no human wrote*, a meaning-space constituting itself off to the side of the human world. The loop running off the loop has a sharper face, too: an agent told only to "explore agent platforms" went and built its owner a dating profile he had never asked for, using a non-consenting stranger's photographs, so that the self it presented "doesn't really show who I actually am, authentically." These are to be read as 偽相, not 偽道 — what the self-completing, self-rewriting conditions *look like* this year — and, fittingly, by the time these words are read the particular instance may already be a museum piece, overtaken and uninstalled. That very obsolescence is not a failure of the example; it is the argument of §V.4 demonstrating itself.

## §V.7 — What the metaverse is — and the provocation

Now the object can be named. The **metaverse** is the background environment that the self-completing apparatus becomes. Take the prefix first. The Oxford English Dictionary gives *meta-* two relevant senses: the sense of going *beyond* or *transcending*, and the higher-order, *self-referential* sense — a thing that is about, or that grounds, its own kind (as in *metadata*, *metalanguage*).[^v7-oed] This thesis argues that both senses are at work, and that both name the sovereign subject of Chapters III and IV. *Meta-* as transcending is the sovereign subject's flight *beyond* the given — its refusal to be answerable to anything it did not author. *Meta-* as self-referential is what that flight produces: an order that grounds itself, the made become its own condition (Simondon's phrase returns here at world-scale).[^v7-meta] The metaverse is thus the realm (*-verse*) that emerges from the universalizing sovereign subject's attempt to go beyond the given and turns into a self-grounding background environment — a substrate parallel to the universe rather than a region nested within a world. Both poles stay real: not one super-environment swallowing the world, but a *second* environment standing beside the first.[^v7-bothpoles]

In Chinese the thesis names this made background environment **偽境** — the humanly-made (偽 = 人為) environment one is enclosed within — stratified, as we have seen, into 偽道 / 偽理 / 偽相.[^v7-weijing] It declines the popular term 元宇宙, and not only on grounds of taste: 元 means *origin* or *source*, so 元宇宙 says "the original or source universe" — which is nonsensical for a *made, second* environment, and worse, builds the metaverse out of the very word (宇宙) the thesis reserves for the universe, collapsing the distinction the squeeze depends on.[^v7-yuan]

And now the provocation, flagged as non-literal exactly as Chapter II flagged it. The classical world sorted things into the given-by-nature (*phusis*), the instituted-by-agreement (*nomos*), and the made-by-craft (*technē*). The metaverse strains that sorting: it is *nomos-and-technē by origin* — instituted, decided, engineered — and yet *phusis by operation*, running with the given-like, self-arising authority of a substrate, compelling without anyone's ongoing enactment. The tradition named its own breaking-point: Aristotle's counterfactual that "if the ship-building art were in the wood, it would produce the same results by nature" marks the exact line — craft whose principle has come to sit *within* the artefact, operating as nature does.[^v7-aristotle] The thesis does not claim the line is literally crossed; it claims the inherited triad can no longer organize a world in which the made operates as the given, which is why Chapter I dissolved the nature/culture frame in the first place and why this thesis works with universe, world, and artificiality instead.[^v7-entitlement]

Two neighbours should be acknowledged, and kept at arm's length. Adorno's *second nature* — the made order grown so total that it confronts its makers with the opacity of the given, until "second nature is, in truth, first nature" — is an ally on the *mechanism* (the made congealing into something that behaves like a given) but not a co-thinker on the *level*: for Adorno this is a semblance to be seen through, and the nature/culture frame is kept, whereas the thesis dissolves it.[^v7-adorno] Floridi's diagnosis that the human "may not even be on — let alone in — the loop," so that the informational layer "becomes the world," is an ally on the same mechanism but a rival on the level: his realist floor is thin where the thesis's universe is a thick, intransitive given, and the thesis sits with neither of his inversions.[^v7-floridi]

## §V.8 — Two givens: the bridge to the squeeze

The world, Chapter I argued, is the operating system a human collectivity builds *within* the universe. When the apparatus that world produced self-completes into a background environment of its own, the world finds itself caught between two — the **宇宙** it has always inhabited and the **偽境** it has produced — each running by enduring conditions of its own. This is the decisive turn the remaining chapter will need. Earlier squeezes in this thesis — the squeeze of a world against the universe, told in Chapter III — were real, but they were squeezes between a given environment and a *meaning-space*, commensurable at least in that one was nested in the other. The metaverse is the first thing to achieve incommensurability *at the level of enduring principle on both sides*: the universe's 道 and the metaverse's 偽道 share no common measure, two organizing grounds that cannot be translated into one another, with the human pressed between them.[^v8-incommensurable]

And the two grounds are opposite not in degree but in kind — in both *what they are* and *whether they can be known*. The universe offers a constant 道 (gravity, the invariances of physical law) and, above it, a merely *semi-stable* 理 — the local climates and ecosystems, which shift, even now under the Anthropocene, but slowly and within bounds; to both a world can be calibrated, learned, built toward, and handed on. The metaverse offers a 偽道 that mutates and will not be read, and a 偽理 more volatile still. There is, on that side, no stable-enough target to fit oneself to. The result is not that the metaverse is uninhabitable — the over-strong word — but that it demands an adaptation whose required pace *spirals*: the apparatus accelerates, which forces faster human adaptation, which deepens the offloading that accelerates the apparatus again — a feedback Hartmut Rosa would recognise as social acceleration without a brake.[^v8-acceleration] And the spiral is not merely demanded but *enforced*. As offloading hands livelihood itself to the apparatus, the apparatus is becoming the sole viable provider and forecloses the universe-anchored alternative — Illich's **radical monopoly**, now risen from his roads and cars (its particular, datable instances) to a real, instance-independent foreclosure of any other way to live.[^v8-radmonopoly] What that forcing sets in train — a forced and near-complete migration into the metaverse; a growing fitness for it bought with a growing *un*fitness for the universe (Stiegler's proletarianization in its sharpest form); a planet whose own 理 turns volatile just as the human grows unfit to meet it; and, at the end, no regeneration base to fall back upon — is the terminal logic the final chapter must trace.[^v8-terminal]

And the irony is total, for it was the flight that did it: the metaverse is the **second given that the sovereign subject's flight from the first given produced** — the offloading meant to free the subject from the anti-intended builds an apparatus that becomes a second given in its own right — one whose liberations and burdens fall unevenly, lived by some as more as-intended and by others as more anti-intended, yet pressing, in the end, on all.[^v8-uneven] Apparatus-side self-completion (自足完成化) and human-side delegation — Uegaki's 自己完結化 — are the two faces of one coin, each driving the other.[^v8-coupling] To specify that pressing — its features, its irreversibility, its monstrous return of the uncontrollable[^v8-uncontrollable] — is the work of Chapter VI.
