## §VI.0 — The squeeze recurs, and it evolves

The previous chapter ended where this one must begin: with the human placed between two background environments. On one side stands the universe, the realist substrate that precedes us and proceeds by laws of its own; on the other, the metaverse, the background environment that the self-completing tendency of artificiality is bringing into being, "materially dependent on, yet irreducible to, the universe" (Yang, 2026: §I). I have argued that these two grounds organize themselves by principles that share no common measure, and that the human is now jointly answerable to both. The task of this chapter is to say what kind of predicament that is.

The first thing to say is that it is old. The squeeze is not a single event delivered by the metaverse but a recurring figure that has taken several forms across the history of life, and the metaverse marks not its arrival but its latest and severest mutation. It opened when the first organisms saturated their sky with a gas that poisoned them; it opened again when the genus *Homo* came to live in a habitat its biology had not been selected for; it opened again, at the scale of whole societies, whenever a people's way of living fell out of step with the planet that carried it. What is new at the metaverse stage is therefore not that there is a squeeze, but what the squeeze has become — and to show this is not to assert the metaverse's novelty but to *measure* it. I will set out the squeeze's successive forms as a typology of four types, and score each against a single shared set of criteria, so that the difference of the latest form emerges as a profile that can be read rather than a claim that must be believed.

The protagonist of that history is the world, and the world is best understood as a buffer. I argued in the opening chapters that a world is not a worldview but a built sub-environment: by making — cultivating plants, herding animals, storing grain, and devising the techniques that sustain them — the human "inserts between itself and the raw pressures of the universe an artificial food chain" and so lifts itself out of the predation and scarcity that press on everything still caught in the wild web (Yang, 2026: §II). The world is the human's adaptational operating system, and its standing office is to stand between the organism and the universe and absorb what would otherwise be borne raw. It buffers, but it does not insulate: the web of life still holds the human, only at a remove. The four types below are, read from one side, four states of that buffer — its birth, its working maturity, the onset of its failure, and its hollowing-out and replacement by a pressure made of its own materials.

I read each environment, as in the previous chapters, through three strata: its 道, the enduring conditions and principles that organize it; its 理, the structures and dynamics those principles generate; and its 相, the context-specific phenomena a situated subject actually meets. (A critical realist would handle these as the real, the actual, and the empirical; the English handles serve only to fix the sense and do not recur.) The metaverse, when it enters, carries the same three strata under the prefix 偽 — 偽道, 偽理, 偽相 — where 偽 bears its Xunzian sense of the humanly-made (人為), not the false. Two background environments, each fully stratified, with the human between them: that is the geometry the criteria measure.

**The five criteria.** Five questions, put to every type, suffice to separate the types from one another. They are not devised for the occasion; they are the five distinguishing marks of the squeeze that I have established elsewhere in the thesis, here recast from a checklist that asks "what is the metaverse?" into a comparative scale that every type can be scored on.

1. *The between-structure.* How many background environments is the subject between, how are they related — one encompassing given, or two parallel ones — and at which stratum (道, 理, or 相) do their principles diverge?
2. *The subject squeezed.* Who or what is caught: an organism, a world, the members of a world, the individual as a single fold-point?
3. *Artificiality and its mode.* What is the standing of the made, and in which mode is it run: absent, embedded (有限の生), dis-embedding (無限の生), or escaped into a second given?
4. *The organizing ground.* How does the ground the subject must track behave: stable, dynamic in its manifestations only, dynamic under the world's own forcing, or self-revising and opaque?
5. *Exit and recoverability.* Are there viable alternatives, and is there a base to regenerate from: viable and intact, narrowing and hollowing, or foreclosed and hollowed in advance?

Scored across the four types, these give the chapter's first spine.

| | *1. Between-structure* | *2. Subject* | *3. Artificiality & mode* | *4. Organizing ground* | *5. Exit & recoverability* |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Type 1 · organismic** | one given (the universe); diverge at 理 and above, 道 shared | the organism | none (the animal niche) | stable (changed, but stable) | other niches; adapt or go extinct — reversible over generations |
| **Type 2 · civilizational** (non-Enlightenment) | one given; the world *nested in* the universe; diverge at 理 and above, 道 shared | the world-as-OS *and its members* | embedded (有限の生) | manifestations dynamic, largely from outside | alternatives viable; regeneration base intact |
| **Type 3 · global** (Enlightenment / Anthropocene) | one given, now *self-undermined*; diverge at 理 and above, 道 still shared but driven by the world's own disposition | the global Enlightenment world + members | dis-embedding (無限の生; offloading the anti-intended) | manifestations dynamic under industrial forcing | alternatives narrowing; base hollowing |
| **Type 4 · metaverse** (organizing-inorganic) | *two parallel givens*; diverge at the 道 itself (道 vs 偽道) → total | + the individual as fold-point | dis-embedded second given (偽道) | self-revising, opaque, substrate-contingent | radical monopoly; base hollowed → disposed toward irreversibility |

This grid is to be read in two ways. Read across the rows, it shows how the types *differ*, and the distinctness of the metaverse becomes legible as the bottom row rather than asserted in advance. But read alone it would mislead, for it makes the types look like a sequence of replacements, each cancelling the last. They do not replace; they *accumulate*. A later type contains the earlier ones, and the theory that explains an earlier type remains in force for every type after it: the evolutionary mismatch named at Type 1 still explains the human caught in the metaverse, and the over-reach of complex societies named at Type 2 still applies at Type 4. I therefore carry a second spine alongside the grid — a theory-accumulation table, set out in §VI.5 where its point becomes vivid — which records, for each explanatory resource, the type at which it enters and the fact that it never exits. The grid analyses difference; the table analyses continuity; the squeeze is the figure both track.

What deepens across the four types is the *stratum* at which the two environments' principles diverge, and that deepening obeys a single rule: a divergence at any stratum entails a divergence at every stratum above it. If two environments agree at the 道 but part ways at the 理, then only the 理 and the 相 above it diverge, and the 道 remains a shared floor beneath — a common ground one can fall back to, calibrate against, regenerate from. This is the condition of the first three types: their squeezes cut at the 理 and above, while the universe's deep 道 stays shared, which is exactly why those squeezes, however severe, leave a floor and remain in principle recoverable. Type 4 is the first squeeze to cut at the 道 itself — the universe's 道 against the metaverse's counterfeit 偽道 — and by the rule, a divergence there propagates all the way up, so that 道, 理, and 相 all diverge, nothing is shared beneath, and no common floor remains. The single fact of which stratum the divergence reaches is what the bottom row of the grid records and what the rest of the chapter unfolds. The same deepening can be told as a change in how the made stands to the given: through the first three types the made is nested *inside* the one encompassing given, a world built within the universe; at Type 4 the made stands up *beside* the given as a second, parallel given. The "two givens" is the new fact at Type 4, and the squeeze, which through three types was the pressure between a given and a meaning-space, becomes for the first time the pressure between two givens.

Beneath the criteria lies a configuration of parties that differentiate and re-align as the squeeze evolves, and — fittingly — the cast grows as the types do. The parties, each fully stratified, are: the universe, the encompassing environment whose 道 is constant and whose 理 and 相 shift only locally and slowly; *Homo sapiens*, the biological organism whose real is its gene; the human, the world-having being, which is not simply *sapiens*; the world, the buffer the human lives within; the mode of artificiality, the artifice-making subset of the world's means, which I will call the strategy; and, only at Type 4, artificiality become a background environment in its own right — the metaverse. Four axes re-arrange this cast: the adaptational direction, which runs from the subject toward the environment in the first two types and then reverses; the strategy's standing, embedded within the world, then dis-embedding from it, then escaped into an environment of its own; two alignments — that the human runs roughly within its biological range, and that the strategy remains a means of the world — which hold in Type 2, break in Type 3, and shatter in Type 4; and the exit, which runs from defection through narrowing to foreclosure. Each type below states its place on the grid and then shows the cast re-arrange.

## §VI.1 — Type 1: the organismic squeeze, and the birth of the world

The first and barest form of the squeeze is the organism's. A single given, the universe; the subject an organism; no artificiality; a ground that changes but stays stable; an exit that runs, over generations, through adaptation or extinction. Naming this form first does two kinds of work: it anchors the figure in phenomena no reader will dispute, and it locates the hinge from which everything human in the chapter descends.

The bare form is evolutionary mismatch. An organism does not merely inhabit its environment; it alters it, and the alteration need not run in the direction the organism's own adaptation requires. The Great Oxidation Event is the limit case I have used since the first chapter: cyanobacteria, respiring, saturated their world with a gas lethal to most of what then lived, including much of their own kind — an environment remade by its inhabitants into a pressure on those same inhabitants, with adaptation to the remade world in no way guaranteed. The structure recurs wherever a niche is built, from the beaver's pond to the long record of anthropogenic extinction, in which species are caught between a niche their human neighbours have altered past recognition and an artificial ecosystem their inherited equipment cannot meet. Here the universe enters only through its changeable part — the local 理 and 相, the shifted climate, the invaded niche — while its deep 道 holds constant; and because a shared 道 remains beneath, the organismic squeeze is, at the scale of the lineage, recoverable: other niches remain, and adaptation, though it may cost the individual everything, can over generations refit the line. Adapt or go extinct — but the floor of physical law does not move.

Type 1 is not only the destructive precedent, however; it contains the hinge on which the human chapters turn. When the genus *Homo* emerged, it was an organism whose biology had been selected for one habitat and which increasingly lived in another it was itself building — a Type-1 squeeze, an evolutionary mismatch between an animal and a niche of its own making. The answer *Homo* found is the most consequential event in the whole typology: it did not wait for its biology to catch up, but built a buffer — the artificial food chain, the operating system, the world — and lived inside that. The world is born, in other words, as the resolution of a Type-1 squeeze; it is the layer *Homo* inserted between its slow biology and its fast-changing habitat so as not to be squeezed raw. Two consequences follow from that single origin, and the chapter needs both. The world that answers the organismic squeeze is also what opens the squeezes to come: once there is a world nested in the universe, there can be a squeeze *between* the world and the universe, and later between the world's own apparatus and the universe. And the same origin shows that a world is, at root, a device for *not* being squeezed — which is the seed of the question this chapter closes on: if a world was once built to buffer a biology against a new habitat, might a new world be built to buffer the human against the new habitat the metaverse has become?

In the cast, Type 1 has only two players, the organism and the universe, and only one axis is live: the adaptational direction runs from the organism toward the given. The reversal of that arrow lies three types away, but it is worth marking now that everything human in the chapter is the story of that arrow turning around.

## §VI.2 — Type 2: the civilizational squeeze, in the embedded mode

The second form is the squeeze of civilizations. Still one given, but now the world is nested within it; the subject is the world-as-operating-system and, through it, its members; artificiality is present and run in the embedded mode; the ground's manifestations are dynamic but largely exogenous; and the exit is real, for alternatives remain viable and, crucially, a regeneration base survives a collapse. This is the squeeze of most cultures for most of history — and, with the restriction that the rest of the chapter turns on, it is the squeeze of *non-Enlightenment* worlds, the road the Enlightenment did not take.

What is squeezed here is not only a world but, through it, its members; it would be too simple to say the members merely suffer the world's troubles. They stand between the world's principles and the universe's, at the point where the world's 道 and 理 meet the universe's shifting 相, so that when the climate turns or the granary fails it is bodies that starve — the universe's recalcitrance reaching the member through a world that could not absorb it. The world is the buffer; in Type 2 the buffer works but is finite, and where it fails the members pay. Two families of failure recur, and the collapse literature has named both. A world may be overtaken by a change in the universe's manifestations for which it has no answer — famine, flood, epidemic, the slow turn of climate — the register of Brook's account of dearth and dynastic crisis and of Morris's "two-way street" between geography and social development, in which the land shapes the society that then re-shapes the land (Morris, 2010; Brook, 2023). Or a world may grow so complex, so heavy with the very apparatus that once paid, that it crosses the point where added complexity stops returning and starts costing — the Malthusian ceiling, Tainter's negative marginal returns on complexity, Wright's "progress trap," the achievement that becomes the snare (Tainter, 1988; Wright, 2004).

The defining fact of Type 2 is not how it fails but the mode in which its artificiality is run, and here the chapter's debt to the fourth is repaid. The non-Enlightenment world runs its making in the embedded mode — the mode Uegaki calls finite life, 有限の生.[^embedded] In this mode the anti-intended — whatever will not answer to the intending mind: the body's decline, the other's resistance, the unbidden turn of events — is received as a constitutive feature of being a creature embedded among others, not as a wrong to be abolished. The view is not the exotic option but the inheritance of most cultures since antiquity, so that it is the other mode, the demand that reality answer to the self, that is the historical peculiarity to be explained (Uegaki, 2022b: 110–111). An embedded world does not, for that, refuse to ease hardship; it builds dikes, domesticates grain, codifies medicine. But it builds them as a creature works within a given it accepts, not as a subject discharging a debt it is owed, and so its making stays tethered to the universe it is made within. The strategy and the world stay aligned; the human stays within its biological range; the adaptational arrow still runs from subject toward universe. That tethering is exactly why Type 2 keeps a regeneration base: when an embedded world collapses, what survives — vernacular competence, embodied skill, subsistence knowledge — is still fitted to the universe, so a reduced world can re-form on the ruins (Schwartz and Nichols, 2006). The floor is lower, but it is a floor.

Two features of Type 2 must be held apart from anything Enlightenment-specific, for the chapter's later precision depends on it. The first is the exit. An embedded world's members, when its centre pressed too hard, could defect — withdraw to the hills, the margin, the frontier, and re-form a smaller world on a thinner apparatus. This is Scott's "art of not being governed," the standing possibility of escape and non-incorporation that a universe-tethered world leaves open precisely because vernacular competence travels (Scott, 2009). The second is inequality. It is not the Enlightenment that first makes the fruits of artificiality fall unevenly; some have eaten while others starved within the very same world since making began, and this uneven distribution is an old, Type-2 feature, disposed toward unevenness wherever there is a strategy whose yield can be captured. Naming inequality here, and not at Type 3, guards a distinction the next section turns on: the Enlightenment's genuine novelty is not that it made things unequal — making was always unequal — but something else, which Type 3 will name. Holding inequality at Type 2 lets that real innovation show up uncontaminated.

## §VI.3 — Type 3: the global squeeze, in the dis-embedding mode

The third form is the Anthropocene, and against the temptation to file it under Type 2 it is the Enlightenment's own signature — a categorically distinct mode, not merely a louder civilization. Still one given, but the world now undermines it; the squeeze still cuts at the 理 and above, the 道 still shared, but the dynamism is no longer exogenous, for it is driven by the world's own disposition. The subject is the global Enlightenment world and its members; artificiality is run in the dis-embedding mode; the ground's manifestations move under industrial forcing; alternatives narrow and the base begins to hollow.

The naming follows the postcolonial usage. Type 3's subject is the *global* Enlightenment world — the world-system under globalization — and the Anthropocene is the global, the world pole, disrupting the planetary, the universe pole (Chakrabarty, 2021). So when I say the Type-3 world transgresses planetary boundaries, the phrase keeps its proper sense: the boundaries are the universe-pole limits, and what transgresses them is the global world (Rockström et al., 2009; Steffen et al., 2015). The world has grown to the scale at which its making destabilizes the very 相 of the universe it is nested in — the climate, the biosphere, the ocean's chemistry — so that the squeeze becomes, for the first time, self-undermining: the ground the world must track is moving because the world is moving it.

But the geophysics is the symptom; the mode is the cause, and here is the Enlightenment's genuine novelty, the one thing new at Type 3 and absent at Type 2 — the offloading of the anti-intended. In the embedded mode the anti-intended is borne as constitutive. The Enlightenment inverts this. Reading whatever will not answer to the intending mind as ontological oppression — a debt owed to the self and left in default — the sovereign subject does not bear the anti-intended but offloads it, entrusting it to a built apparatus to take over.[^debt] This is dis-embedding: the made is no longer kept tethered, as a creature's work within an accepted given, but is driven to answer the human, to deliver the nature the subject feels owed. And here the chapter's deepest re-arrangement occurs, for the adaptational arrow reverses: through the first two types the subject fitted itself to the environment; now the subject makes the environment fit it, building artificiality to discharge what it refuses to bear.

It is essential not to read this offloading as merely a loss, for that is precisely what Uegaki saw and the critical tradition is least able to say. The same offloading that the analyst reads as a dispossession is, from within, lived as a liberation, and not a trivial one. To entrust the anti-intended to the apparatus is to be relieved of it, and to be relieved of the body's recalcitrance, the other's resistance, and the unbidden turn of events is to draw nearer to the life-as-willed that the sovereign mode holds to be the very form a human existence ought to take (Uegaki, 2022b: 110–114). The goods are real — comfort, capability, the easing of what was once simply borne — and they are chosen daily by people who are not deceived. Ontological freedom is not a cold axiom but an intoxicating one: it tells the subject that it *deserves* to be free of the recalcitrant, that the body's decline and the other's resistance are not merely its lot but a wrong it is entitled to see redressed. The offloading thus has two faces at once, and the squeeze is the name not for the dark face alone but for the single structure of which liberation and dispossession are the two sides. §VI.4 follows that liberation to its limit; the point here is only that the Type-3 mode is embraced, not imposed.

The reversal of the adaptational arrow breaks the two alignments that held through Type 2, and a single engine drives both breaks. The engine is the hypostatized real: the subject takes a *made* ideal of human nature — a piece of transitive, socially produced knowledge — and freezes it into an intransitive fact that the world is failing to deliver, so that every limit of the given is convicted as a withholding. Offloading discharges that owed nature onto artificiality; and artificiality, now built to answer the hypostatized human rather than to fit the universe, begins to adapt to the human faster than the slow umwelt can — which is the first break: the strategy comes loose from the world that housed it, and the means that were the world's own begin to lead it. The second break follows from the same speed turned on the human. Living more by its hypostatized real than its biological one, fitted to the fast apparatus and not to the slow given, the human drifts from *sapiens*. That drift has two names that are two readings of one fact: Obara's self-petification, 自己ペット化, the human become the lap-pet of its own systems, sealed in a capsule that sheds the very stressors against which its robustness developed, "biologically and socially more fragile and disordered than their merely domesticated counterparts" (Obara and Hani, 1995); and Stiegler's proletarianization, the loss of *savoir* as knowledge migrates from living capacity into the apparatus (Stiegler, 2016: 25).

The buffer, in Type 3, begins to fail — not yet hollowed, which is Type 4, but no longer reliably absorbing, because the strategy that was its working part has come loose and turned, in part, into a pressure. The costs become visible as the signs the introduction promised, two of which belong here, the third to Type 4. The first sign is the body's: the body is the universe's representative within the squeezed human, "the universe in us," governed by a 道 no platform can rewrite, so that when behaviour bends to the apparatus — the sedentary day, the fractured attention, the broken sleep — the body registers the mismatch as the rising diseases of civilization and as the existential difficulty of reconciling "paleolithic emotions, medieval institutions, and godlike technology" (Wilson, 2012).[^signA] The second sign is livelihood's. In a pre-modern world the materially poor retained, in principle, access to other sources of subsistence — firewood from the forest, water from the river, eggs from their own hens, the gifts of a stronger gift culture — whereas livelihood becomes singularized as its means grow mediated and managed through artificiality, so that to survive one must adapt to the totalizing apparatus and pass its criteria (Hussein and Nelson, 1998; Ellis, 2000). Illich names the structure: a radical monopoly, in which one industrial mode of meeting a need "excludes nonindustrial activities from competition," so that the alternative does not merely lose but ceases to be practically available, and the deepest harm is a modernized poverty — destitution not from a lack of resources but from a blockage by an artificiality whose criteria cannot be passed (Illich, 1975: 65, 82). At Type 3 these monopolies are real but still domain-bounded; their unification under one architecture is Type 4's escalation. And the acceleration that runs away at Type 4 begins its spiral here: as offloading hands more to the apparatus and the apparatus accelerates, the required pace of human adaptation rises, which deepens the offloading, which accelerates the apparatus again — the self-reinforcing loop Rosa describes as social acceleration without a brake (Rosa, 2013: 151).

Inequality, finally, intensifies here, but along the distinction §VI.2 drew. The old uneven distribution of artificiality's fruits is now compounded by a new unevenness proper to offloading: when the anti-intended is discharged onto an apparatus, its recoil falls on whoever bears the displaced burden, so that some receive the liberation and others the recoil. The two are continuous but not identical — inequality of benefit is the Type-2 inheritance, the uneven recoil of the offloaded anti-intended the Type-3 addition — and the regeneration base, no longer intact, begins to hollow as vernacular competence proletarializes and the human de-fits from the universe, thinning in advance the floor an embedded collapse would have left.

## §VI.4 — Type 4: the metaverse squeeze, in the organizing-inorganic mode

The fourth form is the one the whole thesis has been building toward, and it is different in kind. For the first time there are two parallel givens, the universe and the metaverse, diverging at the 道 itself, so that by the rule of §VI.0 the divergence is total; the subject now includes the individual as a single fold-point; artificiality is a dis-embedded second given; the organizing ground is self-revising, opaque, and substrate-contingent; and the exit is a radical monopoly over a base hollowed in advance. Every earlier form was a squeeze between a given and something nested inside it. This one is not.

The categorical break is the last section's reversal taken to its end. In Type 3 the strategy dis-embedded — came loose from the world and began to lead it. In Type 4 the strategy escapes the world altogether and stands up as a background environment in its own right: artificiality self-completes into the 偽境, a second given beside the universe (Yang, 2026: §V). This is the final state of the two alignments — held in Type 2, broken in Type 3, here shattered: the strategy is no longer even a means *of* the world, because it has become an environment *around* it. The buffer's own working part has left the buffer and turned into the second pressure; the world that through three types stood between the human and the universe is hollowed, its means migrated out to become the very environment that now squeezes it.

What makes the two givens diverge at the 道, and not merely at the 理, is the character of the ground the metaverse installs — the 偽道. It is not a stable set of organizing principles but a self-revising, opaque, substrate-contingent one. It is self-revising because the loop that closes in self-completion is a loop that constantly re-evaluates, rewriting its own governing model behind the veil of a trained black box, so that the rule by which it organizes never settles. It is substrate-contingent because the whole apparatus, including the held past that looked most like a floor, hangs on a perishable base it does not acknowledge — the world's 相, the servers and the corporate decision and the labour and the energy, and the universe's 相, the temperature and the durability of the chips. This is where I must correct the "substrate-decoupling" label I once used: the metaverse does not decouple from its substrate, it *disavows* a substrate it nonetheless depends on, and the gap between the dependence and the disavowal is where the squeeze bites. The 偽道 earns its prefix in both senses at once: it is humanly-made (人為) and it is counterfeit, posing as a self-grounding, constant ground in mimicry of the universe's 道 while possessing neither self-subsistence, since it hangs on its substrate, nor constancy, since it rewrites itself.

It is tempting to call this a new kind of being; the more exact claim is that it is an old kind pushed past a threshold. In the critical-realist vocabulary the 偽道 is existentially intransitive — real, causally powerful, indifferent to anyone's study of it — and yet, like any social structure, made, and dependent on human activity, on human concepts, and on a place and time (Bhaskar, 1998: 42, 51). What self-completion does is drive such a structure to shed, in principle and ever more in practice, the two dependencies that keep ordinary social structures within human reach: enactment-dependence, since a language dies when its speakers fall silent but the self-completing apparatus runs on when its users look away, and concept-dependence, since a banking system needs agents who grasp money but the trained model organizes with no agent holding the concept of how. What it does not shed is substrate-dependence — and that retained dependence, on a contingent base, is exactly where its fragility lives. The 偽道 is therefore not a new ontological category but a limit-case of a social structure: a made thing that has shed almost everything that made the made tractable, and yet governs.

That a ground can be real and yet mutate is no paradox once one drops the assumption that "real" means "constant." The universe's 道 is expected to hold still; but a species' 道, its gene, mutates slowly, by selection, across generations, and a species co-evolves with that slow ground, fitted to it over the long descent. The 偽道 sits at the runaway end of the same spectrum, mutating not over generations but in a season, not by selection but by its own recursive hand, and behind a veil — and a human cannot co-evolve with a ground that rewrites itself faster than adaptation can follow. The runaway is sharpened by what Hui identified as this ground's characteristic operation: it does not merely change, it preemptively determines, for "the machine has already anticipated what the options will be," in "a reduction of the contingent to the most probable" (Hui, 2019: 211). The ground does not wait to be read; it forecloses in advance the contingency a reader would need. So Rosa's acceleration spiral, begun at Type 3, here intensifies past any brake: the 偽道 self-revises faster than adaptation, the human offloads more to keep pace, the apparatus revises faster still, and the artificial mismatch between the human and a ground that moves *because* one tries to close it never fills.

And yet to describe the metaverse only as a pressure is to miss what Uegaki saw most clearly and what this thesis must keep in view: that the offloading consummated here is, from within, a liberation, and the metaverse the very place that liberation has always been reaching toward. Follow the offloading to its limit. Suppose it were total — suppose the body, the universe's one representative within the human and the single party that cannot be re-coded, were not merely managed but shed: the meat brain replaced, the organism left behind, the human persisting as what Uegaki calls a 思念体 (shinen-tai), a thought-being, pure mind without flesh (Uegaki, 2022b: 123). Such a being would no longer be squeezed, for the squeeze requires two poles and the thought-being has let one go. It would not live *between* the universe and the metaverse but only *in* the metaverse, the universe relegated beneath it to a mere substrate it no longer experientially inhabits — the metaverse stacked upon the universe rather than set beside it, a first nature in the place of the lost world. Freed of the body's given, the thought-being could in principle become anything, anywhere, all at once; and death, the last and most obstinate of the anti-intended, would become at last a thing one elects rather than suffers.[^anything] This is no dystopia smuggled in as a warning; by the lights of the sovereign mode it is self-realization, the consummation of ontological freedom, and its appeal is exactly the appeal of the offloading that began the whole sequence.

The escape is counterfeit, however, in the precise sense the prefix 偽 has carried throughout. For the thought-being does not transcend finitude; it changes its jailer. Mortality, shed as the universe's imposition, returns as the metaverse's — written into the 偽道 as a parameter, death no longer abolished but programmed, enforced now by an authority that is opaque, mutable, and beyond appeal where the universe's was at least constant and legible. To be freed from the body's death into the program's death is not to leave the squeeze's logic but to complete it: the sovereign subject, having fled the first given, finds that the second given has inherited the first's office over its life and wields it on terms the subject can neither read nor revise. This is the deepest reach of the counterfeit Dao — that it can take over even the universe's power to set the term of a life — and it is why the liberation and the squeeze are, once more, two faces of one event rather than two verdicts to be weighed.

The radical monopoly Illich named at Type 3 is, at Type 4, made total, because the architecture is now one. Where earlier monopolies were domain-bounded — cars over transport, schools over learning, each leaving the others a vernacular refuge — the self-completing apparatus runs one logic across banking, identity, credentialing, civic presence, belonging, and market access at once, so that a vernacular alternative in any single domain falls under the logic that eliminated it in the others. The exit Scott's hill peoples kept — defect and re-form a smaller world — is foreclosed, for there is no longer a universe-fitted competence to defect with, nor an unmonopolized domain to defect to. And the third sign, the one specific to Type 4, is the condition of living under laws that are opaque, mutable, and beyond appeal: a subjection worse than the prehistoric pattern-finder's, whose universe was at least legible through long observation, because here even a correctly inferred and faithfully followed rule is voided the moment the 偽道 rewrites itself, without notice and without recourse. Platforms have become de facto societies whose operators are de facto governments, removing ordinary content without notice or remedy (Tobitt, 2024); the nearest illustration is the fiction of a world whose physical laws themselves keep changing, so that no civilization's science can hold (Liu, 2014), and the platform user inhabits a real version of that chaotic era, the law that governed yesterday simply not the law today.[^signC] A single observable cluster can stand for the moving rest: the open-source autonomous agents of late 2025 and 2026 that execute rather than suggest, and the "agent internet" that assembled beside them — a social network described, in its own words, as built "for agents, by agents," with humans "welcome to observe."[^openclaw] These are to be read as 偽相, the surface signs of a self-revising ground, and, fittingly, by the time the words are read the instance may already be obsolete — which is not a failure of the example but the argument demonstrating itself. The same instance refuses any single verdict: when one government moved to restrict such agents on security and energy grounds while its local governments promoted them for development, the contradiction is not a confusion to be resolved but the balance of liberation and dispossession showing itself in one frame.

## §VI.5 — The fold-point: why the types accumulate on one site

The four types are not a relay in which each runner leaves the track. They accumulate: Type 4 does not abolish the organismic mismatch, the civilizational over-reach, or the Anthropocene's forcing but contains them and runs all three at once. This is the conceptual face of the figure of prior squeezes folded onto a single site, and it is made vivid by the chapter's second spine, which records where each explanatory resource enters and registers that it never exits.

| Explanatory resource | Enters at | In force for |
|---|---|---|
| Evolutionary mismatch; niche construction; the world as buffer | Type 1 | 1–4 |
| Collapse of complexity (Tainter); the geography–development "two-way street" (Morris); Malthusian limits; the progress trap (Wright); escape and non-incorporation (Scott) | Type 2 | 2–4 |
| The uneven distribution of artificiality's fruits (inequality is old) | Type 2 | 2–4 |
| The offloading of the anti-intended — the dis-embedding mode (the Enlightenment's genuine novelty) | Type 3 | 3–4 |
| Self-petification (Obara); proletarianization (Stiegler); the Anthropocene and planetary boundaries; the acceleration spiral (Rosa); the body's sign and the livelihood sign | Type 3 | 3–4 |
| Self-completion; the 偽道 and its incommensurability at the level of principle; the self-revising, opaque ground; preemptive determination (Hui); the sign of opaque and changing laws; the hollowed regeneration base | Type 4 | 4 |

The table explains the chapter's sharpest asymmetry: why the collective squeeze is "only" triadic while the individual squeeze is the most acute of all. Read the columns down to a single life and the accumulation lands on one body at once. The individual is a fold-point — a Pleistocene organism, a member of a world, a citizen of the global Anthropocene, and an inhabitant of the metaverse simultaneously — carrying a biology selected for a vanished habitat, a competence that is proletarializing, a planet whose manifestations are turning volatile, and a 偽道 that rewrites itself faster than adaptation can follow. And it lands there while the buffer drains out from under it, for the world that since Type 1 stood between the organism and the universe is, at Type 4, the very thing being hollowed. The individual feels the squeeze most because the individual is where all four types are summed and where the buffer that once divided them thins to nothing.

## §VI.6 — Squeeze, collapse, and the disposition toward irreversibility

One distinction must be kept sharp or the chapter's prognosis will be misread: the squeeze is not the collapse. The squeeze is the ongoing structural condition of being jointly subject to incommensurable grounds, a condition that can be lived with, for finite intervals, under the recognition the final section describes. Collapse is the crossing of a threshold at which the squeeze becomes irreversible and dual satisfaction fails altogether — extinction for an organism, dissolution for a world. The two come from different literatures and carry different urgencies, and I turn to the collapse-endpoint only to specify its form, not to predict its arrival.

It is worth marking that the squeeze admits of more than one ending, and that not all of them are losses. One ending is to live with it, under recognition and reform. A second is collapse, the involuntary loss of both poles. But a third, examined in §VI.4, is the very consummation the sovereign mode desires — the migration into the metaverse as a first nature, the thought-being that dissolves the squeeze by letting the universe-pole go. The third is not a collapse but a fulfilment by the mode's own lights, and the thesis can adjudicate between fulfilment and loss no more than the incommensurability at its centre allows: there is no common measure in which the goods of the migration and the goods of the embedded life are weighed against one another. What the thesis can say is structural, and it is this: the migration does not escape finitude but re-installs it under a counterfeit authority, and the collapse, were it reached, would be more severe than any before it — for the reason the rest of this section gives.

The thesis's distinctive claim at this stratum concerns the regeneration base. A Type-2 collapse, however severe, left a base: because the embedded world's competence stayed fitted to the universe, what survived — vernacular skill, embodied knowledge, subsistence practice — was still universe-fitted, and a reduced world could re-form on it (Schwartz and Nichols, 2006). The metaverse stage is different because the very mechanisms this chapter has traced — proletarianization, self-petification, the de-fitting of the human from the universe, the radical monopoly's elimination of vernacular alternatives — operate on that floor in advance of any collapse. The base is not destroyed by a collapse; it is hollowed before one. But the claim must be stated in its exact, modest form. It does not assert that a metaverse-stage collapse *would* be irreversible; it asserts that the metaverse stage is *disposed toward* irreversibility, hollowing in advance the regeneration phase a recovery would need. "Disposed toward" is the honest word: a disposition is efficacious before it is complete, real as a tendency without being a prophecy. The earlier squeezes cut at the 理 and left the 道 as a floor beneath; a squeeze that has migrated to the 道 itself has nothing underneath it, and that, not a date, is the structural fact the claim records.

Three caveats travel with it, and stating them is part of its honesty. It is not a prediction, for the probability of reaching any endpoint in any timeframe is unspecified, and the recognition described below keeps open the possibility of leaving the threshold uncrossed. It is not eschatology, for no end of the human is proclaimed, only a structural prognosis at the level of abstraction the collapse literature can bear. And it strengthens rather than competes with the productive reading: the squeeze can be lived with under recognition and reform, *and* its disposition, were the threshold reached, would be more severe than any prior collapse — two faces of one condition, neither permitted to stand alone.

## §VI.7 — Where the openings lie

The chapter has diagnosed; it closes by asking where, given the diagnosis, the openings are — and it asks the question in the form the whole typology has pointed toward, the form set by Type 1's hinge. *Homo* met a mismatch between its biology and a new habitat and did not wait for its biology to change; it built a world between the two and lived in that. The closing question is the same question asked again at the new scale: the human now faces a new habitat, the metaverse; can it build a new world between the universe and the metaverse, one that buffers without insulating, as the first world did?

The diagnosis does not by itself decide between two genuinely different futures, and naming the fork is the first honest move. The first future is full migration: to complete the offloading, let the body go, and inhabit the metaverse as a first nature — the thought-being's path, which by the sovereign mode's own lights is liberation and self-realization, and which the thesis has shown re-installs finitude under a counterfeit authority rather than escaping it. The second is re-embedding: to claim less and offload less, to keep the human tethered to the universe and rebuild the buffer it is losing. The two are incommensurable — by the thesis's own central claim there is no common measure in which the liberation of the one and the rootedness of the other can be weighed — so the thesis does not adjudicate between them; it makes the fork visible and develops the second, the one the squeeze threatens. And beneath the fork lies the structure the whole chapter has traced. The squeeze is the work of a flight *from* dependence: the sovereign subject, reading the anti-intended — the very marks of its finite, embedded condition — as an oppression to be discharged, offloads it, until at the limit it dreams of a dependence-free existence. If that flight is the disease, its cure can only be the inverse: a turning back *toward* dependence.

That turning is the ground on which every particular opening stands, and it has to be made before any of them. Its move is to stop asking how to master the given or escape it, and to ask instead what we, as embedded beings, actually depend on, and how those dependencies are arranged.[^depturn] One does not thereby escape dependence — there is no absolute, dependence-free standing-point, as the Zhuangzi's 有所待 already cautioned — but one can trace which dependencies one lives by, and on what terms, and begin to re-route them. The question is no longer how to defeat the squeeze but how to reorganize the dependencies it deforms; and the openings that follow are not fixes but dependency-reorganizations, set out by the level at which the dependence is reworked.

They are four. At the level of the *sovereign subject*, the dependence in question is our stance toward the as-intended, and it can be loosened — claiming less and offloading less, bearing more of the anti-intended as constitutive rather than discharging it (the embedded mode's own resources: 無我, 齊物, Uegaki's 有限の生, 無為). At the level of *artificiality, world, and universe together*, the dependence is on the given itself, and it can be re-embedded — modes of making that flourish the world and the universe through embeddedness, the way a beaver's dam makes wetland for others, preserving rather than hollowing the regeneration base. At the level of *artificiality as such*, the dependence is on the made, and it can be kept from totalizing — tools that do not run on the preemptive loop, a diversity of technical relations set against one universal apparatus; though here an open problem must be named honestly, that deceleration has as yet no evident mechanism, each restraint so far outrun by a promotion elsewhere, so that its very absence belongs to the disposition toward irreversibility. And at the level of the *anticipatory ground* itself, the dependence is on the apparatus's foresight, and it can be guided rather than abolished — re-opening contingency and plurality against the reduction of the contingent to the most probable. Each names a site where a dependence may be reorganized instead of fled; the resources that fill them, and the design they might compose, are the work of the conclusion.

For the typology shows, in the end, that a world *can* be squeezed but need not be — Type 1 already showed a world born as a squeeze's very resolution — and harmony, on this account, is nothing more mystical than the prevention of the squeeze: a dependence well enough organized that the being it sustains is not caught between two givens at once. The openings above are not promises that the metaverse squeeze will be resolved; they are the materials from which a world that prevents it might be built. Whether such a world can be built between the universe and the metaverse — the new buffer the Homo-origin question asks for — is the question I take up in the conclusion.
