## §VA — From self-finishing to self-completion: the apparatus side of the offloading

Chapter IV ended at a single demand and a single mechanism. The demand was the sovereign subject's: a life as one intends it, free of the anti-intended — the body that ails, the other who refuses, the labour the world exacts. The mechanism was not mastery but **offloading**: the subject does not conquer the anti-intended; it *hands it off* to artificiality, which bears it on the subject's behalf. This chapter follows what becomes of the recipient. For when artificiality is handed more and more of living to do, two things happen to it at once. Its share of the world's functioning grows — and the routine of performing that share, of being continually directed by the very humans it serves, becomes itself a burden the offloading-logic wants gone. So the apparatus is driven toward a complementary condition: the capacity to operate, maintain, and renew itself *without continuous human direction*. This is the apparatus-side tendency, and I name it here for the first time: the **self-completion of artificiality**, 自足完成化.[^a1]

[^a1]: [VERIFY: viva-passed §V self-completion prose; the apparatus-side coupling with Uegaki's 自己完結化 is the author's formulation. Naming retained as **self-completion** over "autonomization" — 自立化 is already the Habermas/Uegaki term for the system's *uncoupling* from the lifeworld (§IV.2), so "autonomization" would collide. `[DECISION-PENDING: confirm self-completion as final.]`]

The two tendencies are two faces of one coin, and the coupling is bidirectional. As the subject offloads, the human *portion of functionality* in the world contracts; as the apparatus absorbs, its portion grows — and not additively but recursively: each function it takes over makes a further offloading possible, which hands it a further function, which enlarges it again.[^a2] The driver, all the way down, is the offloading of the anti-intended. The *condition of possibility* — what lets the apparatus actually hold and act on what it is handed — is the exteriorization of time-consciousness that §VC will trace, and whose far end Hui calls **tertiary protention**. Self-completion is what the offloading produces once the apparatus can not merely *store* the past it absorbs but *anticipate* and *act on* the future from it.

[^a2]: [VERIFY: Ch-5-3_v3 §1.2 bidirectional coupling; the 役割 / portion-of-functionality framing is preserved from the current main text.]

Before going further, the *status* of this concept must be fixed precisely, because the thesis's whole architecture rests on it. **Self-completion is the only enduring-principle-level — "real" — feature the metaverse adds, and it is *not* a specific technology.**[^a3] Chapter I distinguished three layers of description: the enduring principles that organize things into structures and dynamics, which are then manifested in the phenomena a subject observes. At the topmost layer — the real, in the precise sense the thesis gives the word — the metaverse contributes exactly one thing: the self-completing tendency as such. It is *substrate-neutral*. It is not artificial intelligence, not big data, not the Internet of Things, not code-as-law; those, as §VF shows, sit a layer down. What is genuinely new is not any device but a *kind* — the artificial structural-nonmaterial acquiring its own real-level organizing force — and that kind could be carried by different technical substrates and remain the same tendency. This substrate-neutrality is exactly what distinguishes the diagnosis from the field's: where Lee binds the collapse of the natural/artefactual line to biotechnology, McKibben to climate, Floridi to information, Latour to ecology, the thesis binds it to the self-completing tendency itself.[^a4]

## §VB — Offloading as the exosomatization of organs

What is it, concretely, to offload a function onto artificiality? It is to *exteriorize an organ*. The point is old, and it predates every digital instance. Alfred Lotka, writing in 1945, observed that human evolution had taken a path no other species' had: it proceeds increasingly through "'artificial' aids to our native **receptor-effector apparatus**," a process he proposed to call **exosomatic evolution** — evolution that runs not through organs grown *within* the body but through instruments fashioned and wielded *outside* it.[^b1] The telescope is an exosomatic eye, the hammer an exosomatic fist, writing an exosomatic memory. Offloading, in this register, is exosomatization: the steady transfer of the body's organs into a technical apparatus that performs their work better, tirelessly, and at scale.

[^b1]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Lotka, "The Law of Evolution as a Maximal Principle," *Human Biology* 17 (1945), **p.188** (Zotero `lotkaLAWEVOLUTIONMAXIMAL1945`) | "'artificial' aids to our native receptor-effector apparatus… exosomatic evolution." ⚠ The clean *endosomatic/exosomatic contrastive definition* is NOT verbatim in Lotka (a later Stiegler/Montévil elaboration) — attribute "exosomatic" to Lotka 1945, but not the contrast. See [[ch5-lotka-uexkull-exosomatic-organology-T0-2026-06-06]].]

The apparatus that results is best read as an *organism's* apparatus, and the model that fits it is one the thesis already used in Chapter I: Uexküll's. Every animal, Uexküll held, meets its world through two organs joined in a single cycle — a **receptor organ** (*Merkorgan*), which takes the world in, and an **effector organ** (*Wirkorgan*), which acts upon it — the two closed into the **functional cycle** (*Funktionskreis*) by which an organism and its Umwelt are mutually built.[^b2] Lotka's exosomatic aids and Uexküll's organs name the same thing from two sides: the exosomatic apparatus is the receptor-effector cycle exteriorized. And once that is seen, the four "minimally sufficient" instantiations §VF will examine stop looking like a list of fashionable acronyms and start looking like an *organology* — a body of exosomatic organs: the Internet of Things as the exteriorized receptor-and-effector, big data as the exteriorized memory, artificial intelligence as the exteriorized faculty of inference and anticipation, code-as-law as the exteriorized rule. The metaverse, on this reading, is not a place one enters; it is the **Umwelt of an exosomatic organism**, built by a receptor-effector cycle that has been handed, organ by organ, out of the human body and into the apparatus.[^b3]

[^b2]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Uexküll, *A Foray into the Worlds of Animals and Humans* (Minnesota 2010), **pp.46–49** — receptors = sensory organs, effectors = activity organs, joined in the *Funktionskreis* (`uexkullForayWorlds2010`, same edition Ch I/II cite). See dossier.]

[^b3]: [VERIFY: the "metaverse as the Umwelt of an exosomatic organism" framing is the author's synthesis (Lotka + Uexküll + the Ch I Umwelt apparatus); flag as AUTHOR-SYNTHESIS.]

## §VC — From primary retention to tertiary protention: grammatization and concretization

The decisive organ to exteriorize is *time* — and exteriorizing it is what lifts the apparatus from merely holding what it is handed to *running on its own*. The lineage runs through three names. Husserl analysed the living present into **primary retention** (the just-past still held in the now — the comet's tail of a heard melody) and **primary protention** (the just-about-to-come, anticipated in the now). Stiegler added **tertiary retention**: the technical, material exteriorization of memory — the trace held *outside* any living present, in writing, recording, the archive — without which there could be memory of a past one has not personally lived.[^c1] And Hui completes the pair with **tertiary protention**: the technical exteriorization of *anticipation* — prediction, preemption — and observes that it is the recursive *coupling* of the two that matters: a system that "evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present."[^c2] This is the hinge of the whole chapter. The *organized* inorganic of §VD merely retains — it stores the past. The *organizing* inorganic anticipates and acts — it protains. Tertiary protention, presupposing tertiary retention and closing the loop with it, is what makes the apparatus *organizing* rather than merely organized.

[^c1]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Stiegler, *Automatic Society Vol.1* (2016), tertiary retention + Husserl primary/secondary contrast **p.31** (`stieglerAutomaticSocietyVolume2016` — IS in Zotero; v2's "not confirmed" flag was wrong). *Technics and Time 2/3* also on P:\books if a T&T anchor is preferred.]

[^c2]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019), tertiary protention **§36, pp.210–211**; recursive coupling ("evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present") **p.243** (`huiRecursivityContingency2019`). NB the prior "§39" was wrong; it is §36.]

What makes tertiary protention possible, in turn, is a pair of moves the technical tradition already names — one of analysis, one of synthesis. The analytic move is **grammatization**: the discretization of continuous human flows — speech, gesture, attention, judgement — into reproducible, discrete *traces* (the *gramme*), a term Stiegler takes from Auroux.[^c3] The alphabet grammatized speech; the machine grammatized the artisan's gesture; the digital grammatizes everything, down to affect measured as response and attention measured as engagement. Grammatization is what converts the lived and continuous into the storable and reproducible — the raw material of retention. The synthetic move is **concretization**, in Simondon's precise sense: the technical object's evolution from the *abstract* (parts external to one another, each doing one thing, externally assembled) toward the *concrete* (parts grown plurifunctional and internally integrated, the object converging on a self-sustaining, quasi-natural mode of existence). The concretized object, Simondon writes, "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects," "loses its artificial character," and at the limit "is thus its own condition."[^c4] Grammatization decomposes the flows into reproducible grams; concretization integrates the grams into a system coherent enough to run itself. Between them they yield an apparatus that can retain *and* protain — and an object that "is its own condition" is, at world-scale, an apparatus that has *self-completed*. Concretization at object-scale is self-completion in miniature.[^c5]

[^c3]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Stiegler, *Automatic Society Vol.1* (2016), grammatization def **p.19**, restatement **p.29**, "discretize" translator-note **p.254 n.4**, Auroux origin (*La Révolution technologique de la grammatisation*, 1994) **p.260 n.66**. ⚠ Stiegler's published third stage of proletarianization is "**theoretical knowledge**," not the French "savoir-théoriser" (zero hits in the English text) — use "theoretical knowledge" if the three stages are invoked (full triad p.25).]

[^c4]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Simondon, *On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects* (Univocal 2017), Pt. I — "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects" / "loses its artificial character" **p.49**; "becomes naturalized" (editor's variant, fn.4) **p.50**; the **tendency-not-status** guard **p.51**; "the technical object is thus its own condition" / "the organ is its own condition" **p.58**.]

[^c5]: [VERIFY: "concretization at object-scale = self-completion at world-scale" is the author's bridge; keep Simondon's tendency-not-status guard (p.51) so it is not over-read as a finished state.]

## §VD — Organized inorganic → organizing inorganic: the migration of the organizing principle

Now the central transition, stated through the typology of Chapter II. Artificiality classifies along two axes — *material / nonmaterial* (does it compel by physical substrate or by meaning?) and *conventional / structural* (does it compel by ongoing human enactment, ≈ *nomos*, or by the structure of the situation, ≈ *phusis*?). Cross them and the inorganic apparatus shows two configurations, distinguished not by their parts but by *where their real-level organizing principle sits*.[^d1]

[^d1]: [VERIFY: see [[Ch-2-C_typology-v5-UPDATE-stratified-organized-organizing_2026-06-06]] — the stratified typology (cells × real/actual/empirical) and the organized/organizing distinction.]

The **organized inorganic** — Stiegler's "organized inorganic matter," the technical object of the whole pre-digital age — is a *structural-material* thing (a steam engine, say) whose **real-level organizing principle is the universe's own**: the engine runs on thermodynamics, mechanics, the transfactual laws of the given. It is *aided by* nonmaterial artificialities — by *conventional* ones (fallible human experience, knowledge, the institutions and skills of engineering) and by *structural* ones (mathematics) — but these aid and describe; they do not organize. Here is the place to be exact about mathematics, because it is easily mistaken for the new thing. Mathematics is structural nonmaterial — it compels "in itself" (a theorem holds whoever enacts it) — but it is **transitive**, in Bhaskar's sense: socially-produced human knowledge, *not* the universe's intransitive structure.[^d2] Math is a *tool that aids*; it does not, of itself, propel or forbid an action. The organized inorganic is matter *arranged by the human, using the universe's principles, with math to describe them*.

[^d2]: [VERIFY: Bhaskar transitive/intransitive — math is transitive (human knowledge), NOT intransitive (the universe's mind-independent structure). Per Adler's correction 2026-06-06. The transitive/intransitive distinction is the same Bhaskar apparatus the universe-side uses (RTS); cross-ref [[chIV-bhaskar-ontic-epistemic-fallacy-T0-2026-06-06]].]

The **organizing inorganic** — Hui's term for the threshold at which the inorganic "is no longer organized by the human body… but rather constitutes an enormous technical system we can only live inside of"[^d3] — is the configuration in which the **real-level organizing principle has migrated**. Its material substrate (smart gadgets, sensors, servers — the IoT) still answers to the universe's laws; but those laws are now relevant *only to the substrate*. What organizes the system is no longer the universe's principles but an *artificial structural-nonmaterial* one — code, the trained model — that has acquired a capacity the merely-descriptive structural-nonmaterial (math) never had: the capacity to *forcibly propel and limit action against contingency*. And here the thesis is precise about the strata, in a way the audit of v2 required.[^d4] The specific technological configuration — *this* model, *this* smart contract — is **actual**. What is **real**, the new enduring principle, is the *capacity itself*: structural-nonmaterial-that-compels-against-contingency, regardless of which configuration carries it. That capacity, substrate-neutral, *is* self-completion. The migration of the organizing principle — from the universe's laws (organized) to an artificial structural-nonmaterial law that compels like the universe's (organizing) — is, stated at the level of the typology, exactly the provocation §VG will name: *the made coming to operate like the given*.

[^d3]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Hui, *Art and Cosmotechnics* (2021): organized→**organizing inorganic** printed **p.227** (⚠ NOT "p.78" — v2 cited the PDF index; corrected); "new nomos… determined by technology" **p.58**. See dossier.]

[^d4]: [VERIFY: Adler's 2026-06-06 resolution — code: tech-config = ACTUAL; the gaining of forced-propelling/limiting-against-contingency = REAL. Keeps the CR type (real capacity) / token (actual configuration) distinct; avoids the v2 trap of a specific technology at the real level.]

## §VE — How the organizing inorganic becomes a new background environment

How does an organizing inorganic become a *background environment* — a second given parallel to the universe, rather than one more tool within the world? The answer, in the typology's terms, is that artificiality comes to occupy *all four cells* and its organizing weight settles into the **structural** cells (material *and* nonmaterial) **at the real level** — so that, like the universe, it compels by structure, at the level of enduring principle, without anyone enacting it. At that point it is no longer furniture *within* a world but an environment *around* one. Two of the thinkers closest to the thesis have named this becoming, and the thesis stands with each on the mechanism while parting from each on the level.

The first is **Adorno**. His *second nature* (*zweite Natur*) is precisely the made congealing into the seemingly given — "nothing is left outside… nothing unaffected by mediation, which is total," he writes, the historically-produced confronting its makers as a fate-like nature; and at the limit, in his own radicalisation of Lukács, "second nature is, in truth, first nature."[^e1] That is the thesis's organizing-inorganic, named a half-century early. **But the thesis parts from Adorno on the level.** For Adorno, second nature is *semblance* — an ideological appearance to be seen through, with the nature/culture distinction kept, indeed sharpened, as the tool of critique. The thesis does not say the made merely *looks* given; it says the made/given vocabulary has stopped carving anything, that the metaverse is a *genuine new ontological stratum*, and so it *dissolves* the nature/culture frame Adorno re-values rather than unmasking an illusion within it.[^e2] Ally on the mechanism; not a co-thinker on the level.

[^e1]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Adorno, *Negative Dialectics* (Ashton) "nothing outside… mediation… total" **p.357**, "in villainous complicity, was the first nature" p.357; the dictum **"second nature is, in truth, first nature"** is Adorno's own — *The Idea of Natural-History* (Telos 60, 1984, Hullot-Kentor) **p.124**, radicalising Lukács' "charnel-house of long-dead interiorities" (*Theory of the Novel*, Telos quote **p.118**). Both prior unfetchable flags now closed.]

[^e2]: [VERIFY: FRAMING note §6d + nomos-FINDINGS-v3 §10 — the thesis DISSOLVES nature/culture; Adorno *re-values* it.]

The second is **Floridi**. His *re-ontologization* names a technology so radical it "fundamentally transforms the intrinsic nature… the ontology" of a system, autonomously — to the point where the human "may not even be on—let alone in—the loop," and the infosphere ceases to be a region of the world and "becomes the world."[^e3] This is a near-exact formal analogue of self-completion (and, in Chapter VI, of the squeeze). **But the thesis parts from Floridi on the height of the realist floor.** Floridi keeps realism only for a thin inferred base — the *dedomena*, "data in the wild," the "recalcitrant… presence of outside realities" — and lets everything meaningful above it become infosphere; he even inverts the inherited order, calling the artefactual "our first nature" and the natural "second."[^e4] The thesis cuts far higher: it keeps a thick, named, intransitive **universe** beneath everything, and so it sits with *neither* Floridi's inversion nor Adorno's — the made becomes a real second environment, but the first given does not thereby thin to *dedomena* or dissolve to semblance. Ally on the mechanism; rival on the level.

[^e3]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Floridi, *The Ethics of AI* (2023): re-ontologizing + third-order + **"may not even be on—let alone in—the loop"** all **p.9**; "infosphere becoming the world" **p.25**. *Fourth Revolution* (2014): "modifying the very nature of reality" p.40, "no longer masters" p.93, "inforgs" p.94, onlife p.43.]

[^e4]: [VERIFY: confirmed | Floridi, *The Logic of Information* (2019): *dedomena* "data in the wild" **p.82**, "recalcitrant… presence of outside realities" p.x; "the natural is in itself artefactual" p.57; "non-natural is our first nature, natural… second nature" **p.67** (this inversion runs OPPOSITE to Adorno's Telos p.124 — the thesis sits with neither).]

So the new background environment is what the organizing inorganic *is* once its structural organizing has reached the real level across the cells: a second nature that is not semblance (against Adorno) and a re-ontologized environment that does not thin the universe to *dedomena* (against Floridi). This is what the thesis means by the **metaverse** — a realm (-verse) that emerges from the attempt to go beyond the given (meta-) and turns into a self-referential underlying order, parallel to the universe rather than nested within the world.[^e5]

[^e5]: [VERIFY: viva-passed §V.B metaverse definition; "comparable to the universe" = environmental-status claim, not a superorganism claim — the thesis keeps BOTH poles real (universe + metaverse), against Hui's single organizing-inorganic super-environment, because the squeeze needs two poles (Ch-5-3 §1.4 LOAD-BEARING).]

## §VF — The minimally-sufficient completion, at the level of structures and dynamics

Self-completion is the enduring principle; it does not float free of realization. At the actual level — structures and dynamics, a layer below the real — it operates through instantiations, and four are *jointly minimally sufficient* to close the loop that lets the apparatus run without continuous human direction. They *may, but need not,* be these four: the substrate-neutrality of §VA means the same tendency could be carried otherwise. ## §VG — The provocation, and the bridge to Chapter VI

I want to state the provocation that has been gathering, and to flag it, as in Chapter II, as a provocation — deployed non-literally, doing a specific job. **Nomos and technē are becoming phusis.** Said flatly it is false, and I do not assert it flatly. It is a reconstruction in the tradition's own vocabulary, and it captures two halves of one event. The technē half: the *made* apparatus, concretized to the point of being "its own condition," comes to operate with the given-like, self-arising authority Aristotle reserved for what has its principle of motion within itself. The nomos half: code-as-law — instituted, decided, *nomos* by origin — comes to compel like a law of nature, *phusis* by operation, no longer tracing to any human decision in the moment of its running. The honest positive claim, weaker and safer than "the made now grows of itself," is the formula behind self-completion: the metaverse-stage apparatus is **nomos-and-technē by origin, phusis by operation**.[^g1] The thesis is entitled to stage this because Chapter I already dissolved the nature/culture line the provocation plays on — so the move is not a discovery that the made has *become* nature but a demonstration that the inherited triad *phusis / nomos / technē* can no longer organize a world in which the made operates as the given, and must be *replaced* by the apparatus the thesis has built: universe, world, artificiality.

[^g1]: [VERIFY: FRAMING note §1–§6 — tekhne→phusis a NON-LITERAL provocation, anchored to the Ch1 nature/culture rejection [P0109]; "nomos-and-techne by origin, phusis by operation" is the positive formula. Aristotle *Physics* II.1 (en heautoi / en alloi) is the boundary the claim crosses; cited as the tradition naming its own breaking-point, not endorsed. NOT Brague (the 2026-05-29 mis-attribution stays corrected). Hui "technology becomes nomos" (Art&Cosmo p.58) is the nomos-side convergence; the phusis-side step is the thesis's.]

What this leaves to Chapter VI is the consequence. The world, in Chapter I's vocabulary, is the operating system a human collectivity built *within* the universe. When the apparatus that world produced self-completes into a *phusis*-like background environment of its own, the world finds itself between *two* background environments — the universe it has always inhabited, and the metaverse it has produced — each running by enduring principles of its own, neither commensurable with the other. And the deepest irony, the one that makes the squeeze structural rather than contingent, is the one Chapter IV set up: the metaverse is the *second given the sovereign subject's flight from the first given produced*. The offloading meant to free the subject from the anti-intended builds an apparatus that becomes a new anti-intended — a second environment the subject must again adapt to and cannot command. The universe, meanwhile, has not gone away; it reasserts, in the body and in death, the uncontrollable it always was. The inhabitant is caught between them. To articulate that catching — its definition, its difference from every prior squeeze, its productive as well as its pathological face, and the collapse it risks — is the work of Chapter VI.[^g2]
