## ★ NEW LOCK (2026-06-06): 意のままになる・ならない

**Author proposed and ratified** ("How about Life as-intended and anti-intended life, with Gemüt as its anchor" → "Ok go").

| Japanese | Locked English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 意のままになる生 | **the as-intended life** | the standing term; replaces all prior "as-willed life / life-as-one-wills-it / controllable life" |
| 意のままにならない (生・身体・他者) | **the anti-intended (life / body / other)** | replaces "not-as-willed / not-as-one-wills-it / uncontrollable" |
| 意のままになる他者 / 身体 | **the as-intended other / body** | parallel positive forms |

**Mandatory first-use gloss (every scaffold, at first occurrence of "the as-intended life"):**
> *the as-intended life (意のままになる生) — where "intended" carries the full reach of the Sinic 意 (the mind-heart inclusive of cognition, affect, and conation: will, wish, reason, emotion, motive, attention, intention, meaning), anchored in Kant's **Gemüt** and in the directedness Brentano named **intentionality** — not the narrower English "will."*

**Load-bearing rationale (do not drop any of the four):**
1. **Gemüt anchor is mandatory.** The author's correction: reducing 意 to "will" is an oversimplification; 意 spans the whole trilogy of mind. Gemüt (Kant's "mind/heart" as the seat of all faculties) is the inclusive Western referent; Brentano's *intentionality* (the directedness common to every mental act — cognitive, affective, conative) is the one gloss that keeps "intended" from collapsing back to "will." Cite Brentano **once**, at first use, as the warrant. ✅ **Confirmed 2026-06-06** ([[ch4-brentano-intentionality-gemut-T0-2026-06-06]]): the intentional-inexistence passage = *Psychology from an Empirical Standpoint* (Routledge 1973) **p.88** (English + German verbatim secured); Brentano's three-class division of mental phenomena (presentations / judgements / the love–hate class = "emotions, feelings, desires and acts of will") is the warrant that intentionality spans **cognition + affect + conation** — exactly the breadth of 意. Kant's *Gemüt* + three faculties (cognition / feeling of pleasure & displeasure / desire): Cambridge ed. (Guyer & Matthews), master table **Ak. 5:198**, Intro §IX **Ak. 5:197**, First Intro **Ak. 20:206**.
2. **なる, not する.** "as-intended" preserves the *intransitive* なる (reality *coming to conform*), not the transitive する (the act of *imposing*). This is why **"control / controllability is wrong for this term** — the author: "'control' is way more stronger… it would be 意のままに「する」." Keep "control" out of the *rendering* of 意のままになる. **Rosa-confirmed (2026-06-06):** this is a *two-level* distinction, not a clash with the mode-name — 意のまま**にする** (する) = Rosa's *verfügbar* / **controllability** drive (the mode/apparatus level), while 意のまま**になる** (なる) = the **as-intended** lived fruit (the user level). The apparatus does the する so the user experiences the なる (the entrustment hinge). See the resolved tension-note below.
3. **"anti-intended," not "unintended."** "unintended" is a false friend (= accidental). "anti-" is apt because the mode reads the non-conforming as a *thwarting* — 存在論的抑圧 — something set *against* the intending self. The anti-intended = Rosa's **Unverfügbarkeit**. ✅ **Confirmed 2026-06-06** ([[ch4-rosa-verfugbarkeit-T0-2026-06-06]]): authorized English = **"uncontrollability"** (Rosa, *The Uncontrollability of the World*, trans. Wagner, Polity 2020, Author's Note **p.ix** — translator *explicitly rejected* "unavailability"). The squeeze is in Rosa almost verbatim: the controllability drive "produces, behind our backs, a world that in the end is utterly uncontrollable" ("The Monstrous Return of the Uncontrollable," ~p.115). So **anti-intended = Unverfügbarkeit = the uncontrollable the squeeze reasserts.**
4. **Antonym pair.** as-intended ↔ anti-intended; the **limitless life (〈無限の生〉)** is the worldview-and-view-of-the-human that figures the anti-intended as a *wrong to be overcome* (glossary 16-02: 無限 = the limitless *actualisation* of an imagined ideal upon reality, NOT eternal life).

> **✅ RESOLVED (2026-06-06) — head-word settled + the "tension" was a level-confusion.** Two findings close it:
> 1. **Head-word = CONTROLLABILITY** (not availability). Rosa's authorized translation renders *Unverfügbarkeit* → "uncontrollability" and *verfügbar* → "controllability" as the running term; the translator **explicitly rejected "unavailability"** (Author's Note p.ix). "Availability / accessibility / disposability" are only sub-facets of Rosa's four dimensions of controllability (visible/knowable · reachable/accessible · manageable · useful). So naming the mode by "controllability" is underwritten by the canonical Rosa vocabulary and keeps the term-pair in lockstep: **universalizing controllability ↔ the squeeze = the monstrous return of the uncontrollable.**
> 2. **The "tension" was a two-level confusion, now dissolved** (see rationale #2): "controllability" (mode-name) = the apparatus-side する / *Verfügbarkeit* drive; "as-intended" (lived term) = the user-side なる fruit. Same root, two levels, bridged by the offloading/entrustment. No contradiction.
>
> **Still open — a *presentation* choice only (not a head-word choice):** whether "Enlightenment" is the **head** of the first-mention name (Option A: *"the Enlightenment mode of artificiality, characterized by its universalizing of controllability"*) or an **origin-marker** inside the characterization (Option B: head = "the controllability mode," *"the mode the Enlightenment brought to maturity"*). Awaiting the author's pick; **"controllability" stays the running handle either way.**

## ★ EVALUATION (2026-06-07): 無限の生 / 有限の生 — author candidate "sovereign life / embedded life"

**Author proposed** (2026-06-07): 無限の生 → **"sovereign life"**, 有限の生 → **"embedded life"** — motivated by reader economy ("the thesis already spans so many disciplines… keep them as straightforward as possible… I don't want to overwhelm the readers"). Asked for a coherence appraisal vs. Uegaki's meaning + alternatives.

**Status: PROPOSED — agent-recommended ADOPT (with 3 fixes); NOT author-locked.** The prior PROPOSED pair ("the limitless / finite life") stays the fallback until the author ratifies. Agent never promotes PROPOSED → LOCKED.

### Verdict
- **無限の生 → "sovereign life" — STRONG, endorse.** Uegaki's 無限の生 rests on 時空間的自立性 + 約束された本来性 (2022b: 110–112) = the sovereignty of the subject, which the chapter already names as the mode's root (live Ch IV P0634/P0651). "Sovereign life" collapses Uegaki's term into the spine the reader already tracks → real economy; it names the **root**, not the symptom.
  - **Cost 1 (interpretive):** Uegaki's 無限 = the *limitless* actualisation of an imagined ideal, NOT eternal life (16-02, 2022b: 110). "Sovereign" names the ground, drops the literal "limitless." The 無間地獄 argument runs on that limitlessness (16-10, 2022b: 118) → **fix: keep "limitless" as an adjective for the *demand*** in the no-respite-hell passage.
  - **Cost 2 (valence):** "sovereign" is a prestige word; can read as aspirational self-determination, softening the critique. Mitigated by the chapter's established *critical* use of "sovereignty of the subject."
- **有限の生 → "embedded life" — WORKS, one caveat.** Captures 〈存在の連なり〉/相互依存/担い手としての生 (2022b: 130–135, 111) and ties straight to **Ch I embeddedness**; "sovereign ↔ embedded" reads as an over/within polarity (= the squeeze setup); valence right (Uegaki *affirms* 有限の生; "embedded" is neutral-positive, unlike "dependent").
  - **Cost (the one real loss):** 有限 means *finitude* first — the **first principle 生物存在** (the body that ages, sickens, dies; "we can never escape the cruelty of being a biological existence," 2022b: 130). "Embedded" foregrounds relation, backgrounds mortality → **fix: first-use gloss restores it** ("the embedded life (〈有限の生〉): woven into a body, a nexus of others, and a death it does not author").

### Pair-level loss
sovereign/embedded is not the literal antonym 無限↔有限 (limitless↔finite); loses the 無限→無間地獄 wordplay. Recoverable in prose, but the trade is **literal polarity + finitude** for **content + thesis-spine economy**.

### Candidate pairs (for the author to lock)
| Pair (無限 / 有限) | Fidelity | Economy / thesis-tie | Main loss |
|---|---|---|---|
| **sovereign / embedded** (author's) | interpretive both | highest (both tie to spines) | finitude underplayed; not literal antonym |
| sovereign / finite | sovereign interpretive; finite literal | high; keeps "finite" (already in draft) | mixed axes |
| sovereign / creaturely | strong content + real polarity (commander↔creature) | high | "creaturely" = faint Christian tint; awkward vs the Christianity-as-source-of-無限 genealogy |
| limitless / finite (current PROPOSED) | most literal | low; needs "not immortality" gloss | abstract; misleads toward eternal life |
| life without limits / life within limits | literal polarity, no immortality misread | low (no spine tie) | longer; loses sovereignty tie |

### Agent recommendation
**Adopt sovereign / embedded**, with three anchoring moves:
1. First-use **literal-then-rendering** for both, 〈無限の生〉/〈有限の生〉 in parentheses.
2. **Foreground mortality** at first use of "embedded" (the body, the death it doesn't author).
3. Keep **"limitless"** as an adjective for the demand in the 無間地獄 passage.
Conservative fallback if the author wants only one interpretive term: **sovereign / finite** (keep literal "finite life").

> **Thesis-wide scope:** 無限/有限 likely recur in the Ch V/VI sims — locking this pair is a thesis-wide rendering choice; propagate consistently once ratified.
