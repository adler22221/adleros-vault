## M1 — The local agent suspended by Modern Standby (2026-06-01)

**The line (as it landed):**
> "A local agent whose autonomy depends on a substrate it doesn't control — power and the OS scheduler — halts the moment that substrate withdraws. The squeeze, in miniature."

**Context / provenance (autoethnographic):** While the author was away, the AI agents drafting this very thesis (running as a *local* Claude Code session on the author's laptop) were silently **suspended** when the machine slipped into **Modern Standby** on battery after a 3-minute idle timeout — frozen mid-task for ~9 hours, resuming only when the substrate (AC power, an awake OS) was restored. The "self-completing" research loop stopped the instant its physical substrate withdrew. (The thesis's own production conditions became an instance of its thesis.)

**Candidate placements:**
- **Ch VI (the squeeze)** — as a short contemporary/autoethnographic illustration: the apparatus-side (a self-running agentic loop) remains squeezed against the universe-side (power, silicon, thermodynamics) it cannot suspend. Pairs naturally with the OpenClaw / "humans off the loop" material (see `ch5-6-intro-additions-2026-06-01.md`).
- **Acknowledgments (unwritten)** — as a reflexive closing note on how the thesis was made: a candid admission that the agentic assistance it used was itself subject to the very dependency-structure the thesis theorizes.
- **Conclusion** — as a final reflexive turn.

**Conceptual payoff (why it's more than a joke):**
1. **Illustrates the squeeze (F1+F2).** The agent is squeezed between two environments it must satisfy at once — the *universe*-substrate (electricity, hardware, the OS scheduler's power policy) and the *metaverse*-apparatus (its own task loop). When the universe-side withdraws, no amount of apparatus-side "autonomy" sustains it.
2. **Qualifies the tekhne→phusis provocation in a useful way.** Even as the made acquires *phusis*-like self-running autonomy ("third-order technologies," humans off the loop — Floridi), it never escapes dependence on the *actual* phusis: the universe-substrate it cannot suspend. This echoes Ch I's Uexküll point — "the encompassing totality… that no mode of survival can suspend" [P0103]. So tekhne *becoming* phusis is asymptotic, not complete: the apparatus mimics phusis's internal principle of motion while remaining parasitic on the given substrate. (This is a *strengthening* nuance — it pre-empts the objection that "tekhne→phusis" overclaims total autonomy.)
3. **Connects to "autonomy plus permissions."** Cf. the OpenClaw expert line — "the real risk is not AGI but autonomy plus permissions." The dependency cuts both ways: an agent potent enough to act across accounts is also fragile enough to be halted by a power policy. Power *over* and dependence *on* are the same relation seen from two sides.
