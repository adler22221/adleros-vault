# The Soteriological Reading of Uegaki's 約束された本来性

> **What it is:** 約束された本来性 ("the human's true *endowed* nature") is not merely a premise of the 自由の人間学 — it is a **secularized Christian soteriology**: salvation-history transposed, term for term, into an immanent doctrine of the promised, not-yet-realised true human. This reading is *grounded in Uegaki's own genealogy* and *deepened* by Löwith's secularization thesis.

## 1. The transposition (the core)

| Christian salvation-history     | →   | Uegaki's 約束された本来性                                                                                      |
| ------------------------------- | --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| *imago Dei* / prelapsarian Adam | →   | the true human essence — 本来の人間 = 完全な人間                                                                 |
| the Fall                        | →   | alienation: society corrupts (Rousseau's 鉄鎖), estrangement (Marx), self-incurred immaturity (Kant)     |
| the covenant / **promise**      | →   | **約束**: the true nature is *owed*, the human's destiny/vocation — present in advance, not yet realised |
| redemption                      | →   | emancipation / enlightenment / revolution / self-realisation (具現化 on earth)                            |

The structure is: a true human nature **(a) given/promised in advance, (b) not yet realised, (c) to be realised in the future through freedom/effort** — and **the given present is condemned by the light of the promised future** ("そこから照射して、現実の方が克服されるべきものとして捉えられている", glossary 16-05).

## 2. Grounded in Uegaki's own words (not imported)

Uegaki *himself* names the theological root — the soteriological reading only makes its structure explicit:

- **Glossary 16-05 / 16-07:** 「この矛盾を解く手がかりとなるのは、おそらく**ルネッサンス期のキリスト教**であり、そこにあった、**神の似姿を与えられた**特殊な被造物である人間に、**神が与えた特別な計画や目的**がないはずはないという信念である。」
- **2022b: 116:** the *imago Dei* loftily celebrated; **理性こそが、その神性を体現する**; to know 「本来の人間」＝「完全な人間」 one must stand at the **「神の視点」**; the human's 「偉大な使命」 is to grasp the secret of the complete human and **「この地上に具現化させる」**.

So Uegaki gives the **creation + providence + mission-to-realise** structure. **Löwith** (*Meaning in History*) supplies what Uegaki leaves implicit: *why it persists after God drops out* — modern progress is **secularised eschatology**, the promise of the Kingdom transposed into history as the promise of realised humanity (Kant → Hegel → **Marx's *Gattungswesen***: species-being alienated, to be recovered).

## 3. Scholarly frame + the period-native lineage

- **Löwith, *Meaning in History* (1949)** — the secularization thesis (progress = secularised salvation-history). PRIMARY anchor.
- **The "vocation of man" / *Bestimmung des Menschen*** genre — Spalding (1748), Fichte (1800), Mendelssohn — the 18th-c. teleological-anthropology that *is* this structure. Period-native name for the lineage.
- **Two sub-strands:** *rational-perfectionist* (Kant, Condorcet — the true human is the rational/autonomous human) and *expressivist-authentic* (Rousseau, Herder → **Taylor's authenticity**; cf. Trilling, sincerity→authenticity). NB: this is why 本来の人間 ≠ "the authentic human" as a *default* rendering — see translation dossier.
- **⚠ Contested — the Löwith–Blumenberg debate.** Blumenberg (*The Legitimacy of the Modern Age*) argues modern self-assertion is *genuinely new*, not warmed-over theology. So present 約束された本来性 as **structurally** soteriological (Löwith), flagged against Blumenberg — do not assert the derivation as settled.

## 4. Placement in the architecture (load-bearing)

- **It is the structure of the IDEALIST engine only.** 約束された本来性 is the *ideal-pole* — the *toward-what* (remake the given to match the promised true human). It is complementary to ontological freedom's *will-pole* (the *against-what*: the given as not-mine). Together the given is doubly rejectable: *not-mine* (freedom) **and** *not-the-true-human* (約束された本来性).
- **The empiricist engine is NOT soteriological.** Self-preservation / instrumental-appropriative reason (Hobbes/Locke/Hume, A&H) has no promised essence. → **De-essentialising irony:** the *empiricists* are the *more thoroughly secular* (only nature, no promised telos); the strand the thesis targets is the one that **secularised but RETAINED** Christianity's redemption-structure. The mode is not reason-against-religion — it is **religion's soteriology carried forward in immanent, then technological, form.**

## 5. Why it secures the Western-exclusivity (the bridge to §III)

This is the link between Uegaki and the comparative argument. The Indian traditions have the *independent self* (時空間的自立性: Sāṃkhya *puruṣa*/kaivalya, Jain *jīva*, Advaita *ātman*) but turn it **out of** the world (mokṣa/kaivalya = release *from* saṃsāra). What turns the independent self **onto** the world — toward *mastering and perfecting* it rather than escaping it — is the **Judeo-Christian frame the soteriological reading names**: a creator who orders creation, the dominion mandate, **good creation to be *perfected* not escaped**, and **linear redemptive history with its goal *in and through* the world** (Kingdom → secularised progress). **約束された本来性 IS that world-perfecting vector in anthropological form.** Hence: soteriology = the engine that makes the Western independent-self *world-mastering* (vs Indian *world-renouncing*). (Self-hedge: the West's own *withdrawal*-version existed — Gnostics, monasticism, Neoplatonic ascent — and was *subordinated*; dominant-not-essence, matching Uegaki's 「唯一…具現化」.)

## 6. The terminus: a soteriological arc closing in the metaverse

The secularised promise demands *delivery*. The **metaverse / 〈生の自己完結化〉 / 〈生の脱身体化〉** is the **technological delivery of the secularised promise** — the apparatus as the secular saviour that finally realises the promised true human by liberating it from the given (body, finitude, the other). And the terminus is **structurally Gnostic**: the **思念体 / 脳人間** — the true self is mind/spirit, the body is the alien prison, "salvation" = liberation *from* the body (cf. Hans Jonas, *Gnostic Religion*). So the arc closes: **約束された本来性 → 脱身体化 → 思念体 → the metaverse**, a redemption narrative consummated in code. (Uegaki himself: 17-07 脳人間 in the メタバース; the 思念体 as 「行きつく未来」.)

## 7. Rendering note

約束された本来性 = **"the human's true endowed nature"** (locked). But "endowed" backgrounds the **約束 = covenant/promise** force — a destiny *owed and not-yet-realised*, which is exactly what licenses the Löwith reading. Keep the *promissory/eschatological* sense in view conceptually even though "endowed" stays in the prose.
