# Ch1 criterion-5 addition — the background environment is accessed only through the AOS (2026-06-01)

## To add to Ch1, criterion 5
The background environment — the **universe**, Uexküll's *Umgebung* — is **always only accessible through the AOS**; the members of an AOS **never perceive the background environment *as such*.** What a population inhabits and perceives is its *Umwelt* (the world its AOS constructs), never the *Umgebung* directly — "the encompassing totality that no population's Umwelt can ever fully absorb" [DOCX P0103]. The universe is real and operative "whether any AOS is present or if any AOS's members can perceive them" [P0105], yet it is given to members only *through* the mediation/filter of their AOS.

## Why it is load-bearing
- It makes the AOS a **perceptual-adaptive filter, not a window** — every world is a partial, *positioned* take on a universe that exceeds it. (Strengthens the §IIIA "no view from nowhere" discipline and the plurality-of-worlds claim.)
- It grounds the universe's **realism-without-direct-access**: realist (mind-independent, causally efficacious) yet only perspectivally accessible (through the AOS).
- It is the precise hinge for the **universe-vs-Kant-noumena** comparison (`universe-vs-kant-noumena-2026-06-01.md`, in progress): like the noumenon, the universe is never given "as such"; **unlike** the noumenon, it is causally engaged and knowable *in its effects/transformations* — the inaccessibility is **ecological / positional** (Uexküll), not **transcendental** (Kant). The AOS is an *Umwelt* filter, not Kant's a priori forms of intuition.
- It also separates the thesis's **universe** from Floridi's **dedomena**: a thick, nameable substrate accessed only through the AOS, not a bare sub-perceptual noumenal floor.
