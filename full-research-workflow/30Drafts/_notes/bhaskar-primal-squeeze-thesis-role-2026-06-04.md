# Bhaskar's primal squeeze — its role in the thesis (Q1 + Q2)

> Companion to [[bhaskar-PA-faultline-NLM-2026-06-04]] + [[bhaskar-PA-faultline-PLAIN-2026-06-04]]. Backlink [[master-thesis]] · [[squeeze-ontology]].

## Q1 — Why Bhaskar is *more explanatory* of universalization than Derrida / Adorno-Horkheimer / Habermas / etc.
The neighbours each light up a **facet**; Bhaskar is the **spine** because of what the thesis needs (realist, ontological, nature-relation). Distinctive advantages:
1. **He names the *positively real* thing excluded.** Derrida dissolves the referent; Adorno resists positive ontology (negative dialectics); Habermas brackets ontology. Only Bhaskar specifies the squeezed-out middle as **a real, stratified, mind-independent world / natural necessity** (the intransitive). The thesis is *realist about the universe*, so it needs a realist account of what's flattened — Bhaskar alone supplies it.
2. **Genealogy + mechanism, not pathos or periodization.** Bhaskar locates a datable founding fault-line (Platonic/Aristotelian failure to grasp ontological depth) + a precise mechanism (epistemic fallacy + actualism → two-jaw squeeze). A-H = sweeping myth (Odysseus→fascism); Habermas = modern system/lifeworld differentiation; Derrida = structural but not genealogically precise. Bhaskar explains *how/why it recurs* (the "unholy trinity").
3. **He explains the *totalizing drive itself*, structurally.** A knowledge-stance built on the demand for universal-and-necessary *certainty* MUST treat its criterion as the measure of all being and project itself as universal — "splits reality into two: that which conforms to its criterion and that which does not" (anthropic fallacy). Universalization is thus *built into the epistemics*, not contingent (Habermas) or merely linguistic (Derrida).
4. **Realist + emergentist → he can ground the *plural real* that's excluded.** Because CR restores a stratified, open, emergent real, the "other ways of knowing / countless aspects of reality" are excluded *as real* — the Enlightenment mode doesn't just marginalize other worlds, it *gets reality wrong*. The anti-realist/ontology-shy neighbours can't say this.
5. **He integrates epistemics + ontology + the nature-relation in one figure** — matching the thesis's own triple structure (epistemic squeeze ↔ ontological squeeze ↔ artificiality's domination of nature). A-H gives nature-domination not ontology; Habermas the social not nature; Derrida language not either.

**Honest caveat:** not "better simpliciter" — better-*fitted* to a realist, ontological, nature-relation thesis. Use the neighbours as **corroborators/facets** (A-H = the pathos & self-destruction; Habermas = the social encroachment; Derrida = the binary form), Bhaskar as the spine.

## Q2 — Is the "enlightenment-morphism / enlightenment-centrism" extension consistent with Bhaskar?
**Verdict: consistent — and partly *anticipated* by Bhaskar.**
- Bhaskar's **anthropic fallacy** = "the exegesis of being in terms of human being" (D:205), with two faces: **anthropocentrism** (man as measure) + **anthropomorphism** (cosmos in the image of man).
- **Textual warrant for the extension:** Bhaskar (via Hartwig, *Dict. of CR*) treats **"any regional geo-historical variant of anthropocentrism (e.g., Eurocentrism)"** as an ethnocentrism, and says these take **"an extreme form … in post/modernity."** So enlightenment-centrism = a geo-historical *variant/extreme form* of the anthropic fallacy — Bhaskar opens this door himself. `[VERIFY: pin Bhaskar's PRIMARY statement of the Eurocentrism-as-anthropocentrism-variant; the line above is Hartwig's synthesis citing Bhaskar.]`
- **Mapping:** "projecting the (made) **world** as (given) **universe**" = Bhaskar's **epistemic fallacy** (reducing being to our construct/knowledge of it) *fused with* the anthropic fallacy, expressed in the thesis's world/universe vocabulary.

**The author's contribution (the original move beyond Bhaskar):**
1. **Historicizing/particularizing** Bhaskar's *transhistorical* anthropic fallacy into one *mode* (the Enlightenment) and its **planetary universalization** — Bhaskar gives the genus, the thesis names the species.
2. **Coining "enlightenment-morphism / -centrism"** as that mode-specific variant.
3. **Recasting it ontologically** as "world-projected-as-universe" (ties Bhaskar's epistemics to the squeeze-ontology).
4. **Yoking the nonmaterial to the material** (Bhaskar ⟷ Needham–Pomeranz) — Bhaskar is purely philosophical.

**Tensions to flag (4):**
1. **Transhistorical vs historical-specific.** Bhaskar's anthropic fallacy is perennial across *all* irrealist philosophy, not unique to the Enlightenment. → Flag the Enlightenment mode as its *extreme, universalizing form* (warranted by "extreme form in post/modernity"), NOT as the sole locus; non-Western thought can commit the anthropic fallacy too — the thesis's point is that the Enlightenment mode *universalizes* it planetarily (cf. Needham–Pomeranz + Hui's monotechnology).
2. **Faculty vs mode.** Bhaskar's anthropomorphism projects *reason* (a faculty); the thesis projects a whole *mode of artificiality* (a sociohistorical formation). → Flag this as an extension from faculty-level to mode-level (defensible: the mode embeds a particular reason).
3. **Stay realist.** "World projected as universe" must mean the mode *mistakes its made-world for the given-universe* (epistemic fallacy) — NOT that there is no real universe. → Guardrail footnote: the universe is real; the error is the projection. (Keeps the thesis's realism intact.)
4. **Epistemic plurality ≠ relativism.** "Other ways of knowing" must use Bhaskar's **epistemic relativism within ontological realism + judgmental rationality** (many fallible knowledges of *one* real, rationally comparable) — NOT flat relativism, which Bhaskar rejects and which would clash with the thesis's own realism. → Flag explicitly.

**How to flag in-text (one sentence + footnote):** "I extend Bhaskar's [transhistorical] anthropic fallacy to name the Enlightenment mode as its extreme, universalizing form — *enlightenment-centrism/-morphism*, the projection of the made *world* as the given *universe* — warranted by Bhaskar's own treatment of Eurocentrism as a geo-historical variant of anthropocentrism that takes 'an extreme form in post/modernity'; the historicization, the world/universe recasting, and the yoking to the material drivers are my contribution." + footnote carrying tensions 3 & 4 (realism + epistemic-relativism-not-relativism).
