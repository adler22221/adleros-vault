## 1. The paired umbrella: 宇宙 ↔ 偽境

The thesis's load-bearing structure is a **squeeze between two background environments** — the **universe** (the realist, given natural substrate) and the **metaverse** (the new neo-artificial environment), with the **world** (the idealist, human-made meaning-space) pressed between them. The viva-passed definition is explicit that, past a threshold, artificiality

> "can no longer be considered merely a critical component of our world … Rather, it has become **another background environment comparable to the universe** – we reside in it, are not in charge of its workings, and must adapt to survive." — `docx-extract` **[P0164]**[^env]

So the two poles are **environments one inhabits**, on the same ontological footing. The Chinese naming should make that parity *visible on the surface* — two parallel names, each a two-character *environment/cosmos* word, distinguished by a single artificial-marking morpheme.

### 1.1 Why 宇宙 ↔ 偽境 is a smoother surface pairing than 道 ↔ 偽道

The translation note ([[metaverse-chinese-translation-evaluation-2026-06-02]]) made the strongest *depth-metaphysical* case for **道 / 偽道** as the squeeze pair (universe ≈ 道, the generative ground; metaverse = an artificial 道). That case is correct *at the principle layer* and is retained below as **L1**. But as the **umbrella / surface** pairing — the names that head the two poles in running prose, in the title-level framing, in the reader's first encounter — **宇宙 ↔ 偽境** is smoother, for four reasons:

1. **Parallel morphology — two 2-character environment-words.** 宇宙 (yǔzhòu) and 偽境 (wěijìng) are both **two-character compounds naming a spatial/cosmic environment**. 道 / 偽道 is a *one-character root* and its prefixed form — aesthetically a root-and-modifier pair, not two parallel environments. 宇宙 ↔ 偽境 reads as *two cosmoi side by side*; 道 ↔ 偽道 reads as *a thing and its negation*. For the umbrella level — which must announce **two environments of equal ontological weight** — the parallel 2+2 surface is the right music.

2. **宇宙 is already an *environment-word*, and 境 matches it.** 宇宙 is literally *space-time-as-container* (宇 = the four directions + up-down, spatial extent; 宙 = past-present-future, temporal extent — the 《淮南子·齊俗訓》 gloss 「往古來今謂之宙，四方上下謂之宇」). It names *the bounded all-encompassing environment one is inside*. **境 = 疆 (a bounded territory/environment one inhabits)** is the *same kind of word* — a region one is *within* (see §1.2). So 宇宙 ↔ 偽境 pairs **two environment-words**; 道 is a *path/principle*, not an environment, so 偽道 names the metaverse's *ground*, not the *environment* the human is squeezed inside.

3. **道 carries too much weight to be the umbrella.** Because 道 is the single most freighted word in the Chinese philosophical lexicon (the Way, the generative ground), making 偽道 the *umbrella* over-commits the surface to a metaphysical claim before the argument is made, and it strands 道 — which the thesis wants to *keep* as the universe's **L1 principle**, not spend as the universe's whole name. Reserving 道/偽道 for **L1** (where the depth-claim belongs) and using 宇宙/偽境 as the **umbrella** lets each term do one job.

4. **偽 marks the artificial pole cleanly at the surface.** 偽 = 人為 (the humanly-made; see §3 and [[Ch-2-0_why-artificiality-not-technology_2026-06-02]]) is a single morpheme that *flags the artificial pole* without naming a negation. 偽境 = "the made/instituted environment" reads as *a second environment that is humanly-originated* — exactly the neo-artificial background the thesis means — paired against 宇宙, the given one.

**One-line statement of the pairing:** the squeeze runs between **宇宙** (the given environment-cosmos) and **偽境** (the made environment-cosmos) — two parallel two-character environments, 偽 marking the artificial pole — with the human **世界** (world) pressed between.

> Note this does **not** discard 道/偽道: it *relocates* it. 道/偽道 is the **L1 (principle/real)** layer *inside each umbrella* (§3), exactly where the translation note's depth-pair belongs. The umbrella is the environment; 道 is the environment's enduring principle.

### 1.2 Why ancient 境 = 疆 grounds 偽境-as-umbrella

The decisive philological fact: the **ancient 境 is a bounded ENVIRONMENT one inhabits**, not the modern phenomenological "realm/state."

> 「**境，疆也。从土竟聲。經典通用竟。**」 — 《說文解字》土部.[^jing]

- **土 radical** → a region *of the earth*; **疆 gloss** → a frontier / marked-off territory. The ancient 境 is **spatial-territorial**: a *bounded region one is within and moves about in*. (The classics, the 《說文》 notes, ordinarily wrote it 竟 — the *end/boundary-marker* of something — which only reinforces the bounded-extent sense.)
- This is **exactly the umbrella meaning the thesis needs**: the metaverse is *what one is squeezed within* — a bounded background environment one resides in, does not control, and must adapt to ([P0164]). 偽境 = **the artificial bounded environment one inhabits**, paired against 宇宙, the given bounded environment one inhabits. The territory-word grounds the umbrella.

**Ancient vs. modern (境):**
- **Ancient (先秦 + 《說文》): 境 = 疆 = a bounded TERRITORY / environment.** A region of the earth one is inside. *This is the umbrella sense.*
- **Modern / later (drift): 境界 = a phenomenological-aesthetic "realm / spiritual state / level of attainment."** This enters as the Chinese rendering of the Buddhist *viṣaya* (the sphere/object of a sense or of cognition) and is then made the central *aesthetic* category by 王國維 (1877–1927) in 《人間詞話》 (c.1908–10; 有我之境 / 無我之境).[^jingjie] That phenomenological 境界 is **late**, and is **NOT** what the umbrella 偽境 means.

The thesis must **signal it means ancient 境=疆/territory-environment**, not modern 境界/Wang-Guowei aesthetic-state. Read ancient, 偽境 is the *bounded artificial environment* one is squeezed within — the umbrella over the whole metaverse-side stratification below.

## 2. Each umbrella stratifies into the same three layers

The thesis sets out the relationship among **universe / world / operating-system** as three layers of description ([P0106], verbatim):

> "The relationships among universe, world, and operating system can be set out as **three layers of description** — the 1) **enduring conditions or principles** that 2) **organize things into structures or dynamics**, which then 3) **generate or are manifested in the context-specific phenomena experienceable or observable to a subject**."[^l0]

These three layers map onto **critical-realist real / actual / empirical**: L1 enduring principle = *real*; L2 structures-dynamics = *actual*; L3 contingent observable phenomena = *empirical*. The design is: **both umbrellas stratify into these same three layers.** The universe-side runs **道 / 理 / 相**; the metaverse-side, as the **artificial (偽-) counterpart**, runs **偽道 / 偽理 / 偽相**. (L3 = **相/偽相** is a **tentative decision (2026-06-02)**, reached via the deep-dive §3 and grounded substantively in the CR-parallel subsection below; final call author's. The candidate table §3 is retained as the deliberation record.)

That discipline governs every 偽- term below.

### L1 — 道 / 偽道 (the enduring principle / real) ✅ settled

**Ancient sense — 《說文》: a road one walks.**

> 「道，所行道也。从辵从𩠐。一達謂之道。」 — 《說文解字》辵部.[^d1]

The graph is concretely spatial: a *road actually travelled*, "a single through-passage" (一達). This is the literal floor under the cosmic sense.

**Ancient sense — 先秦: the Way that is prior to and mother of all things.** The *Laozi* lifts the road-word into the cosmic ground without losing the path-metaphor:

> 「有物混成，先天地生…獨立而不改，周行而不殆，可以為天下母。吾不知其名，字之曰道。」 — 《老子》(王弼本) ch.25.[^d2]
> 「道生一，一生二，二生三，三生萬物。」 — 《老子》ch.42.[^d2]

And the *Yijing* fixes 道 as the **above-form** (metaphysical) pole of the form/principle distinction:

> 「形而上者謂之道，形而下者謂之器。」 — 《繫辭》上 I.12.[^d3]

**Ancient vs. modern (道):** ancient 道 = *path → the generative Way-ground / source-of-laws*. Modern 道 collapses toward "**morality / doctrine / a saying**" (道德 morality, 道理 a reason, 傳道 preach, 一道菜 a *course* of food). The thesis's 道 is the **ancient Way-ground**, NOT modern "morality."

**偽道** = the artificial counterpart at the same depth: the metaverse's own enduring principle — the self-completing tendency (自足完成化) as an *engineered ground* that has acquired given-like authority (the translation note's "nomos-by-origin, phusis-by-operation"). The pre-existing pejorative "false Dao" (伪道之路, distorted misuse of 天道; see [[metaverse-chinese-translation-evaluation-2026-06-02]] §6.1) is **owned, not avoided**: 偽=人為 neutralises it doctrinally, and the residual "counterfeit Dao" overtone performs the made→false drift the thesis thematises. First-occurrence gloss: 偽道（此處『偽』取荀子「人為」之義，非「詐偽」之貶）.

### L2 — 理 (文理) / 偽理 (the structures-dynamics / actual) ✅ lead candidate

**Ancient sense — 《韓非子·解老》: the pattern that completes a thing, derived from 道.** This is the single strongest L2 verbatim, because it states the 道→理 relation in *exactly* the thesis's L1→L2 terms:

> 「**道者，萬物之所然也，萬理之所稽也。理者，成物之文也；道者，萬物之所以成也。**」 — 《韓非子·解老》.[^li1]

Read against [P0106]/CR: **道** = "that whereby the myriad things are so / the converging-point of the myriad 理" = **L1**, the enduring principle (*real*). **理** = "the **文** (pattern/configuration) that **completes a thing**" = **L2**, the structure that organises things (*actual*). The *Hanfeizi* makes 理 concrete and structural:

> 「凡理者，方圓、短長、麤靡、堅脆之分也，故理定而後可得道也。」 — 《韓非子·解老》 (理 = the divisions of square/round, short/long, coarse/fine, hard/brittle).[^li1]

That "divisions/structurings of things" is *precisely* "organize things into structures."

**The 偽-decisive verbatim — 《荀子·禮論》 ties 偽 directly to 文理.** This is the clincher for the *artificial* side and the single best L2 verbatim for the metaverse column:

> 「**性者，本始材朴也；偽者，文理隆盛也。**」 — 《荀子·禮論篇》 (the inborn nature is raw material; **偽 is the flourishing of 文理** — pattern/structure).[^li3]

Here **偽 IS the flourishing of 文理**: in Xunzi's own definition, the *artificial* (偽=人為) *just is* the elaboration of structure/pattern. So **偽理 / 偽文理** is not an external graft — its artificial form is *defined into* the thesis's own 偽=人為 anchor by the very text grounding the 偽 prefix. This is the most *internally warranted* of all the metaverse-side middle terms.

**Ancient vs. modern (理):** ancient 理 = *jade-veins → the structural grain/pattern along which things divide and dynamics run* (《說文》 理，治玉也 — to work jade along its veins; 《莊子·養生主》 「依乎天理」 the butcher follows the natural grain). Modern 理 spreads to "**reason / logic / manage / science**" (道理 a reason, 物理 physics, 管理 manage, 理性 rationality). The risk: 偽理 may read as "false reasoning / sophistry." This drift is **milder and more controllable** than the L3 candidates' risks — the structural sense (紋理 grain, 條理 orderliness, 肌理 texture) is still ordinary, and 文理 (two characters) disambiguates toward "pattern/structure." Lead with **文理** on first definitional use, then settle to **理**.

**"Or equivalent" — alternatives to 理 at L2 (briefly):**
- **文 (wén)** — pattern/configuration/markings (the 文 in 文理; 《易》 「物相雜故曰文」). The root of 文理; too bare alone, but the source of the structural sense.
- **序 (xù)** — order/sequence/arrangement. Captures *ordering* well; weaker on *structure-as-grain*; 偽序 reads as "false sequence/precedence."
- **紀 (jì)** — ordering-thread / regulating principle (綱紀; 《禮記》 「四時之大經，五行之大紀」). Names *the regulating structure*; archaic-leaning; 偽紀 unfamiliar.
- **象-as-configuration** — the *Yijing*'s manifest configuration (見乃謂之象) *can* read as L2 (the structuring pattern between 道 and the formed thing), but its simulacrum-pull (偽象 → fake image) is the disqualifier that pushes it to the L3 candidate table instead (§3); listed there.

**Lead with 理/文理.** Its case: (a) matches [P0106] L2 wording most exactly via the 《解老》 道→理 ordering; (b) is the only candidate whose *artificial* form is **defined into** the thesis's own 偽=人為 anchor by Xunzi (偽者，文理隆盛也); (c) preserves the 道 L1 hierarchy (道 prior to and source of 理) rather than competing with it.

### L3 deep-dive — 象 vs 相

> **Scope.** A focused head-to-head of the two strongest "observable phenomenon" candidates: **象** (xiàng, image/figure/representation) vs **相** (xiàng, the observed aspect/look). The L3 slot names "context-specific phenomena experienceable or observable to a subject" (*empirical*). The decisive external constraint is the thesis's **non-simulationist AND causally-REAL virtuality**: the metaverse's nonmaterial things are *causally efficacious*, **not** illusions or mere images ([[Ch-2-0_why-artificiality-not-technology_2026-06-02]]; memory `thesis_virtuality_terminology`). So the 偽- reading of the L3 term must **not** smuggle in "unreal / mere appearance / fake image." This subsection evaluates; **the final call is the author's** (per the "evaluate, don't decide for me" stance). 象 already sits in the §3 candidate table; 相 is added here as its chief rival.

#### Per-term verbatim

**象 (xiàng) — image / figure / representation.**

- 《說文》: 「**象，長鼻牙，南越大獸，三秊一乳，象耳牙四足之形。凡象之屬皆从象。**」 — the graph is literally *the elephant* (a pictograph of the beast).[^dd-xiang-swjz]
- 《韓非子·解老》 — **the representation etymology** (the single best 象 verbatim): 「**人希見生象也，而得死象之骨，案其圖以想其生也，故諸人之所以意想者皆謂之象也。**」 People seldom see a *living* elephant; getting the bones of a dead one, they reconstruct its living form *from a diagram* — and so *whatever people form in conception (意想)* is called 象.[^dd-xiang-hf] This is the decisive datum: in the locus classicus, **象 = a mental reconstruction / conceived image of an absent thing.** Representation is built into the word's own folk-etymology.
- 《周易·繫辭》: 「**見乃謂之象，形乃謂之器**」「**在天成象，在地成形**」; and the self-gloss 「**易者，象也；象也者，像也**」 (象 *is* likeness/像) — 繫辭上 I.8 / I.1, 繫辭下 II.3.[^c-xiang] 象 is the *manifest figure*, prior to the formed 器; but it is figure-as-likeness, not the thing.
- **Net:** 象 = image / figure / symbol / representation. Its etymology and its 易-internal self-definition both pull toward **likeness/simulacrum**.

**相 (xiàng / xiāng) — the observed aspect / look.**

- 《說文》 目部: 「**相，省視也。从目从木。《易》曰：地可觀者，莫可觀於木。《詩》曰：相鼠有皮。**」 — **eye-root** (从目): 相 is *to inspect, to look-over* (省視); the nominal "appearance/aspect" sense (the *look a thing presents to an inspecting eye*) grows from this act of observation.[^dd-xiang2-swjz] This is the decisive datum on the 相 side: the word is *built on the eye* — it names what is **seen/observed**, i.e. phenomena *as presented to a subject*, which is exactly the L3 definiens.
- The "phase / contingent observable state" sense — **月相** (lunar phase), **相位** (phase/position) — names a *specific observable state a thing is in at a juncture*: the most exact lexical match to "context-specific phenomenon."
- 真相 / 假相 — *true* appearance / *false* appearance: 相 takes a truth-value modifier natively, i.e. an appearance can be *veridical*. (Contrast: there is no idiomatic 真象/假象 pairing carrying "veridical observed aspect"; 假象 exists but means "illusion/false impression," tellingly *without* its 真象 partner in ordinary use.)
- Buddhist usage — **lakṣaṇa = mark / characteristic / appearance**: 《金剛經》(鳩摩羅什) 「**凡所有相，皆是虛妄；若見諸相非相，則見如來**」(Wikisource prints 「即見如來」; 則/即 variant — [VERIFY]); the four 相 「**若菩薩有我相、人相、眾生相、壽者相，即非菩薩**」; 「**是實相者，則是非相，是故如來說名實相**」; and 無相, 色相.[^dd-jingang] Here 相 = the *characteristic/appearance* a thing presents — and the sūtra's whole move is to call those appearances 虛妄 (empty/illusory). **This is the principal hazard for 偽相** (see Criterion 2).
- The **體‑相‑用** three-tier — 《大乘起信論》(真諦譯): 「**一者體大，謂一切法真如平等不增減故；二者相大，謂如來藏具足無量性功德故；三者用大，能生一切世間出世間善因果故。**」[^dd-tixiangyong] 相 is the canonical **appearance/aspect layer** of a real Chinese three-tier ontology; cf. 性相 / 法相 (法相宗 / 唯識 the "characteristics-of-dharmas" school).
- **Polysemy to flag:** 相 also = 丞相/宰相 (minister, falling tone xiàng) and, read xiāng, = "mutual / each-other" (相互). Heteronym + homograph load.
- **Net:** 相 = observed aspect / look / characteristic / phase — *phenomenon as presented to an inspecting subject*.

#### The 5-criteria comparison

**1 — Semantic fit for "context-specific phenomena experienceable or observable to a subject."** **相 wins, decisively.** 相 is *etymologically the eye* (省視, 从目): it names the thing *as observed*, and its 月相/相位 sense is literally "the observable phase/state a thing is in here-and-now" — a near-exact lexical image of the L3 definiens (*context-specific*, *observable*, *to a subject*). 象, by its 《解老》 etymology, names the *conceived image / representation* — what the mind *reconstructs of an absent thing* (意想), one remove from the observed phenomenon. L3 wants the *observed*, not the *re-presented*: that is 相's home and 象's blind spot.

**2 — The 偽- reading vs the thesis's NON-simulationist + causally-REAL virtuality.** This is the crux, and it cuts **both** ways — but unevenly.
- **偽象 → simulacrum / "made image" (人為之象 = a manufactured likeness).** This collides *head-on* with non-simulationism: it says the metaverse's phenomena *are images/representations* — precisely the simulation reading the thesis rejects. And 「象也者，像也」 doubles down (象 *is* likeness). The clash is **structural** (built into the word's meaning), hence hard to gloss away.
- **偽相 → illusory / false appearance (假相 / 虛相).** This collides with the *causally-real* commitment: via 「凡所有相皆是虛妄」 it can mis-suggest the metaverse's phenomena are *unreal / empty*, which the thesis denies. The clash is **connotational/allusive** (it rides on a *famous text*, not on the word's core sense — 相's core sense, 省視 "observed aspect," is neutral as to reality).
- **Which unwanted connotation is more containable by a gloss, given virtuality is causally real?** **偽相's is more containable.** Because 相's *core* meaning (observed aspect) is **silent on reality-status**, a one-line gloss can fix the intended reading: *偽相 = the artificial (人為) observed aspect — a real, causally-efficacious appearance that is humanly-instituted, NOT 虛妄/illusory; the 金剛經 「相皆虛妄」 reading is explicitly NOT imported.* The 偽= 人為 anchor already does half this work (it says "made," not "fake"). By contrast 偽象's problem is in the **denotation** — 象 *means* image/likeness — so a gloss must fight the word's own sense, not just a textual echo. **A gloss can quarantine a famous-passage connotation (偽相) far more easily than it can overwrite a core denotation (偽象).** Advantage 相.

**3 — Phenomenal-LAYER pedigree (體‑相‑用): net-help or net-baggage?** **Net-help, with one boundary to police — but the help is real and the baggage is *separable*.** The help: 相 arrives **pre-installed as the "appearance" stratum of an actual Chinese three-tier ontology** (體‑相‑用), and in 性相 / 法相. That is a genuine asset for an L3 slot in a three-layer scheme — it is the *native* Chinese name for "the appearance layer," with millennia of use. The baggage to police: 體相用 is a 真如/如來藏 framework whose **體 ≠ the thesis's 道** (體 = 真如 suchness/unconditioned ground; 道 = generative Way) and whose **用 = function ≠ the thesis's L3-phenomena** (so one must not let readers slot the thesis's L1/L2/L3 onto 體/相/用 wholesale — the mapping is *only* 相↔L3, not 體↔L1 / 用↔L2). **But this baggage is detachable**: the thesis borrows 相 as the *appearance-layer name*, exactly as it borrows 偽 from Xunzi *without* the 性/偽 dualism (the established selective-borrowing discipline — [[Ch-2-0_why-artificiality-not-technology_2026-06-02]], §2 of this note). One first-occurrence gloss ("相 in its 省視/觀-aspect sense, NOT the full 體相用 如來藏 schema") fences it. So: a ready-made three-tier pedigree is a **net help** *provided* the 體≠道 / 用≠L3 boundary is stated once. 象 has no comparable three-tier pedigree (it is 易-internal figure-talk), so it offers neither the help nor the baggage — it is simply *thinner* at the layer-architecture level.

**4 — Polysemy / ambiguity.** **象 wins this one (cleaner monosemy).** 偽象 has essentially one reading direction (made image). 偽相 must survive two distractors: (a) 相 = 丞相/宰相 → "偽相" could misread as a *false/pretender minister* (a real collocation in historical prose); (b) 相 (xiāng) = "mutual" → a momentary mis-parse. Both are **dampened** by context (an ontology-layer term list is not a court-politics text, and 偽X compounds across the scheme — 偽道/偽理/偽相 — force the "artificial-X-layer" reading by parallelism), but they are *real* and 象 has nothing comparable. This is 相's one clear deficit; it is a **familiarity/first-parse** cost, not a *semantic-fit* cost, so it weighs less than Criterion 1 but is not zero.

**5 — Euphony / coherence with 道/偽道 + 理/偽理 and the 宇宙/偽境 umbrellas.** **Roughly even; a slight edge to 相 on register, a slight edge to 象 on tonal variety.**
- Both are single graphs yielding clean 偽X monosyllable-compounds: 偽道 / 偽理 / 偽象 or 偽相 — all 2-character, all parallel. Morphological fit is *identical*.
- *Register/lineage coherence:* 道, 理, and 相 are all **heavyweight philosophical-ontology terms** with their own classical "layer" pedigrees (道家/儒, 理學 the 理-tradition, 體相用/法相 the 相-tradition). 相 sits in the *same register-tier* as 道/理 — three load-bearing ontological nouns. 象 is more specialized (易學 figure / divination-image), a slightly *narrower* register. Edge: 相.
- *Tone music:* 道(去) 理(上) 象(去) 相(去, in the appearance sense). 道/理/象 = 去/上/去 (some contour variation); 道/理/相 = 去/上/去 as well — essentially the same. No real euphony separation. (The 宇宙 yǔ-zhòu / 偽境 wěi-jìng umbrellas are unaffected either way.)
- Net: 相's **register/lineage parity with 道 and 理** (three peer ontological terms, each a recognized "layer" word) is a mild coherence advantage; 象's narrower 易-register is a mild demerit. Slight edge 相.

#### Tentative lean + the single deciding reason

**Tentative lean: 相.** Four of five criteria favor it (1, 2, 3, 5), and the one demerit (4, polysemy) is a *first-parse familiarity* cost that context and the parallel 偽道/偽理/偽相 series substantially dampen — not a *semantic-fit* defect.

**The single deciding reason** (if only one may be cited): **Criterion 2 read together with Criterion 1 — 偽象's unwanted connotation lives in the word's *denotation* (象 *means* image/likeness → simulacrum), which a gloss cannot overwrite, whereas 偽相's unwanted connotation lives only in a *famous-text allusion* (金剛經 「相皆虛妄」), which a gloss can quarantine — and 相's *core* sense (省視, the observed aspect) is both reality-neutral and the exact L3 definiens.** Put plainly: for a virtuality that is causally REAL and explicitly NON-simulationist, the term whose problem is "you must not read it as *image/simulacrum*" (偽象) is the worse fit than the term whose problem is "you must not import the *one Buddhist passage* that calls appearances illusory" (偽相) — because the first problem is the word's *meaning* and the second is merely one of its *associations*.

**Caveat the author should weigh against the lean:** if avoiding *any* heteronym/polysemy at first encounter is prized above layer-fit (Criterion 4), 偽象 is the cleaner-on-the-page token — and the 體相用 baggage, though detachable, is a real gloss-cost 象 does not carry. The lean is *evaluative*, not a decision.

### L3 = 相 and the East Asian reading of critical realism

> **What this subsection adds.** The deep-dive above settled the *terminological* contest (相 over 象). This subsection states the **author's substantive contribution**: the reason 相 is *right* — not merely *cleaner* — is that the Buddhist **相 literature is an East Asian articulation of critical realism**. The 相 corpus performs, in classical Chinese and a millennium before Bhaskar, the very stratifying move that defines critical realism: it places *contingent appearance* (相 = the empirical) **beneath** *the real*, and refuses to let the real be exhausted by what appears. That is structurally identical to Bhaskar's **anti–epistemic-fallacy** stratification of *real / actual / empirical*. So choosing 相 for L3 does not merely borrow a convenient Chinese word for "observable phenomenon" — it grounds the thesis's critical-realist ontology in an *indigenous East Asian source*, which is a genuine cross-cultural warrant for CR, not a decoration. **The borrow is SELECTIVE** (exactly as the thesis borrows Goodman's *plurality* without his irrealism, and Xunzi's 偽=人為 without the 性/偽 dualism): the thesis takes the **相→真如 epistemology** — *the empirical is contingent; see through it to the real* — **without** Buddhist 唯識 idealism (萬法唯識 / consciousness-only) or 空/Madhyamaka anti-realism, both of which would strip the real (道/理/宇宙) of its mind-independent standing. The boundary is stated explicitly below.

#### The structural parallel (1–3), with verbatim

**The critical-realist target it rhymes with.** Bhaskar's foundational claim — the one the thesis's three-layer ontology ([P0106] = real/actual/empirical) presupposes — is that the empirical does **not** exhaust the real, and that collapsing being into our knowledge of being is a *fallacy*:

> "Emprirical realism is underpinned by a metaphysical dogma, which I call the **epistemic fallacy**, that statements about being can always be transposed into statements about our knowledge of being." — Bhaskar, *A Realist Theory of Science* (2008), **p.5**.[^cr-ef]

> "what I will call the **domains of the real, the actual and the empirical are distinct**." — *ibid.*, **p.2** (Table 0.1).[^cr-rae]

> "real structures exist independently of and are often **out of phase** with the actual patterns of events … an **intransitive dimension**, in which the object is the real structure or mechanism that exists and acts quite independently of men and the conditions which allow men access to it." — *ibid.*, **pp.2, 6**.[^cr-intrans]

So the CR move is: there is a *real* (mechanisms, structures) that is **stratified beneath** the *empirical* (experiences), the two are routinely *out of phase*, and to reduce the first to the second is the cardinal error. The 相 literature makes precisely this move — three times over.

**1 — 「凡所有相，皆是虛妄」 = the anti–epistemic-fallacy claim (the empirical does not exhaust the real).**

> 「**凡所有相，皆是虛妄；若見諸相非相，則見如來。**」 — 《金剛般若波羅蜜經》(鳩摩羅什譯, T0235).[^cr-jingang]

All 相 — all *appearances*, i.e. **the empirical** — are 虛妄: contingent, not ultimate, not self-standing. This is structurally the **anti–epistemic-fallacy** claim: *what shows up to a subject (相 / the empirical) is not the real, and must not be mistaken for it.* Bhaskar says the empirical is "out of phase" with the real and warns against transposing being into our knowledge of being; the 金剛經 says the 相 are 虛妄 and warns against taking them for the 如來 (suchness, the real). **Same stratifying refusal, opposite ends of Eurasia.** (Note the *discipline* §below polices: 虛妄 here means *contingent / not-ultimate*, NOT "ontologically unreal" in the thesis's causal sense.)

**2 — 「若見諸相非相，則見如來」 = "see beyond the empirical, into the real."**

The very next clause is the *epistemology of penetration*: do not stop at the 相 (appearance / empirical); **see *through* them** (諸相非相) to the 如來 / 真如 (suchness, the real). This is exactly Bhaskar's two-criteria demand that an adequate account "sustain the idea of the independent existence and activity of the objects of scientific thought" — i.e. reach *past* the experiences to the intransitive real. 相 → 真如 *is* empirical → real. The contingent surface is penetrated, not abolished; the goal is contact with what *stands independently behind* the appearance.

**3 — The 性/相 pair, 體‑相‑用, and 三性: indigenous *appearance-beneath-the-real* stratifications.**

- **性 / 相 (法性 vs 法相; 法相宗 / 唯識).** The 性/相 distinction is the native Chinese-Buddhist name for *the real nature of a dharma* (性, 法性 ≈ the real) versus *its presented characteristic/appearance* (相, 法相 ≈ the empirical). A whole school — 法相宗 — is named for the *appearance* side. This is the lexicalised form of the real/empirical stratification. `[VERIFY: 法相宗/唯識 association of 相 stated from standard doxography; not a primary verbatim this session.]`
- **體‑相‑用 (《大乘起信論》).** 相 is the **canonical middle "appearance/aspect" stratum** of a real three-tier Chinese ontology:
  > 「**所言義者，則有三種。云何為三？一者體大，謂一切法真如平等不增減故；二者相大，謂如來藏具足無量性功德故；三者用大，能生一切世間出世間善因果故。**」 — 《大乘起信論》(真諦譯, T1666).[^cr-tixiangyong]
  > and its 一心二門 frame: 「**依一心法有二種門。云何為二？一者心真如門，二者心生滅門。是二種門皆各總攝一切法。**」[^cr-yixin]

  相 here = the appearance-layer of suchness. **Boundary to police (carried from the deep-dive):** 體 = 真如/如來藏 ≠ the thesis's 道; 用 = function ≠ the thesis's L3-phenomena. The borrow is *only* 相 = "the appearance layer," not the wholesale 體/相/用 → L1/L2/L3 mapping.
- **三性 (遍計所執 / 依他起 / 圓成實) — and the load-bearing 依他起 parallel.** The Yogācāra *three natures* stratify three modes of a thing's standing:
  > 「**由彼彼遍計，遍計種種物，此遍計所執，自性無所有。依他起自性，分別緣所生。圓成實於彼，常遠離前性。**」 — 《唯識三十頌》(世親造，玄奘譯).[^cr-sanxing]
  - **遍計所執性** = the *imputed-illusory* (自性無所有, "has no own-being") — the merely-constructed misprojection;
  - **依他起性** = the *dependent-arising / conditioned* (**分別緣所生**, "arises from conditioned discrimination / dependent conditions") — **and this is the decisive parallel: 依他起 ≈ the contingent actual/empirical.** It is what arises *dependently, conditionally, relationally* — not self-standing (so not the 遍計 illusion), not yet the perfected real (圓成實), but the **conditioned middle** that *actually shows up because conditions converge*. That is, almost exactly, Bhaskar's **actual/empirical**: the patterns of events-and-experiences that are *real-but-conditioned*, dependent on and "out of phase" with the underlying mechanisms. 依他起 = "dependent-on-other-arising" is the Buddhist name for *the contingent conditioned phenomenon* — the L3/empirical's own ontological signature.
  - **圓成實性** = the *perfected-suchness* (圓成實於彼，常遠離前性) — the real as it stands when the imputation is removed.
  > So the three-natures schema *itself* is a stratification "appearance/conditioned beneath the perfected-real" — the same architecture as 道/理/相 = real/actual/empirical, with 依他起 sitting where the thesis's contingent empirical 相 sits. **[VERIFY:** 成唯識論 T1585 prose gloss — 「謂心心所及所變現眾緣生故，如幻事等非有似有誑惑愚夫，一切皆名依他起性 … 此空所顯識等真性名圓成實」 — fetched via search this session; lock against CBETA T1585 卷8 line-marked text before quoting the prose.**]**

#### The selective-borrowing discipline — where Buddhist metaphysics DIVERGES and the thesis does NOT follow

The parallel is **structural** (a stratification of contingent-appearance beneath the real), and that structure is all the thesis takes. Two Buddhist commitments travel *with* the 相 corpus that the thesis **explicitly refuses**, because the thesis is **REALIST** — the universe is **Bhaskar's intransitive real**, and **virtuality is causally REAL** (non-simulationist; memory `thesis_virtuality_terminology`; [[Ch-2-0_why-artificiality-not-technology_2026-06-02]]):

1. **唯識 idealism (萬法唯識 / consciousness-only) — REFUSED.** Yogācāra grounds the three natures *in mind*: 成唯識論 says the three自性 「**皆不遠離心心所法**」 (all three are *inseparable from mind and mental factors*), and 依他起 arises as 「**所變現**」 (what consciousness *transforms-and-manifests*). The thesis takes the *stratification* (依他起 = contingent-conditioned beneath the perfected-real) but **rejects its idealist grounding**: for the thesis the real (道/理/宇宙) is **mind-independent** — it "interacts with all things and carries the total effect of all interactions … indifferent and unaffectable by anything" (universe def. P0104–P0105; see [[universe-vs-kant-noumena-2026-06-01]]). The conditioned phenomena (相) are conditioned by *real mechanisms in a mind-independent world*, **not** by consciousness-only. This is the exact point at which 相-as-CR and 相-as-唯識 part: the thesis keeps the *anti–epistemic-fallacy* skeleton and discards the *consciousness-only* flesh.
2. **空 / Madhyamaka anti-realism (emptiness-of-svabhāva → no mind-independent real) — REFUSED.** If 「凡所有相皆是虛妄」 is read in the strong 空 register (all appearances are empty *because nothing has own-being, including any putative real substrate*), it would deny the universe its mind-independent standing — which the thesis cannot accept. The thesis therefore reads 虛妄 in its *weak/stratifying* sense only: **相 are contingent and non-ultimate** (do not mistake the empirical for the real), **NOT** *there is no real*. Bhaskar's intransitive real is precisely a *svabhāva*-bearing, mind-independent mechanism-stratum — the opposite of universal emptiness. So the thesis borrows the 「諸相非相」 *penetration* (see through the empirical) but stops short of the 空 *dissolution* (there is nothing real to reach). **The arrow 相 → 真如 is kept; the claim 真如 itself = 空/no-svabhāva is dropped.**

#### What 偽相 therefore means (the payoff)

On this reading, **偽相 = the *contingent empirical surface* of 偽道 / 偽理** — the artificial real's observable appearances, to be **seen *through*** to the engineered real beneath, **NOT** a claim that the metaverse's phenomena are "unreal." The 金剛經 hazard (偽相 → 「相皆虛妄」 → *illusory*) is not merely *glossed away* (as the deep-dive argued on terminological grounds); it is **inverted into the thesis's own engine**: just as 相 are 虛妄 *qua contingent-not-ultimate* yet are the very things one penetrates to reach 真如, 偽相 are the contingent artificial appearances one penetrates to reach 偽道/偽理 — the artificial real.
