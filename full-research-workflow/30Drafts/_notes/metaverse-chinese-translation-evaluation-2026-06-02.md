## 1. What "the metaverse" IS in this thesis — the constraint a translation must satisfy

The thesis's "metaverse" is **not** the popular/academic object (XR, a 3-D virtual world of avatars). It is a **background environment** — a *substrate* on the same ontological footing as the universe — and the whole burden of a Chinese term is to carry *that*, not the phenomenal-realm sense.

Four marks, from the thesis's own text and locked apparatus:

**(a) A substrate "comparable to the universe," not a realm within the world.** The viva-passed definition is explicit that, past a threshold, artificiality
> "can no longer be considered merely a critical component of our world … Rather, it has become another background environment comparable to the universe – we reside in it, are not in charge of its workings, and must adapt to survive." — `docx-extract` **[P0164]**

and the revised definition:
> "A realm (-verse) that emerges from the human attempt to go beyond the given (meta-) and turns into (-verse) a self-referential (meta-) underlying order (meta-)." — `docx-extract` **[P0165]**

The squeeze is between **two incommensurable background environments** (universe vs. metaverse), each "operating through" its own real-level generative structures (`squeeze-ontology.md` §1, F2). A translation that names only a *phenomenal/experiential* realm under-translates.

**(b) The metaverse = the self-completing tendency (自足完成化) of artificiality become substrate.** At the enduring-principle ("real") layer the metaverse contributes *exactly one* thing — the self-completing tendency as such, **substrate-neutral**, *not* any specific technology (`chV-sim_v2` §VA; FRAMING §6b). The four technologies (AI / big data / IoT / code-as-law) sit a layer *down*. So the Chinese term ought to name a *principle-level ground*, not a gadget or a medium.

**(c) "tekhne becoming phusis" — the made acquiring the self-arising, ground-like character of the given.** This is a **non-literal provocation** (FRAMING §1): the apparatus is *nomos-by-origin, phusis-by-operation* — humanly instituted yet running with given-like, law-like authority that no longer traces to any human decision (`chV-sim_v2` §VA). A good term should let the metaverse read as *an artificial counterpart to a natural ground* — an engineered phusis.

**(d) The universe ≈ 道 (Dao): a realist, intransitive, generative ground.** The thesis's universe is "the fundamental substrate of all … the foundational ontological level … the source of laws, energies, or matters that are indifferent and unaffectable by anything" (`docx-extract` [P0104]–[P0105]); a **Bhaskarian intransitive real**, "knowable in its effects," NOT a Kantian bracketed noumenon (`universe-vs-kant-noumena`). That profile maps onto 道 as the classical Chinese name for *the ultimate generative ground that spontaneously gives rise to all things* (verbatim, §5 below). If **universe ≈ 道**, then the cleanest Chinese name for its artificial twin is *an artificial 道* — which is the case for **偽道** developed in §4 and §7.

**The translation problem, precisely stated:** find a Chinese term that names a *substrate / enduring-principle-level ground* (not a phenomenal realm), in the *humanly-made / 人為* register (neutral and appropriable, not merely pejorative), consistent with **universe ≈ 道** and the squeeze (**道 vs. its artificial counterpart**), grounded in the thesis's **偽 = 人為** equation, readable, and ideally able to *mirror the thesis's own stratification* (enduring-principle vs. observable-phenomenon).

## 2. Why 元宇宙 must be ruled out for the thesis's TECHNICAL term

The thesis itself, in the **Introduction**, already rejects 元宇宙 — and the reason is even stronger once the universe/metaverse ontology is in place.

The Intro's stated motive for the whole project:
> "the word metaverse was translated into Chinese as '元宇宙 yuan yu zhou' … This translation is problematic, as the meaning of 'meta-' is misrepresented by '元' – meaning 'origin,' 'source,' or 'previous' in Chinese, and leads to an absurd translation that literally means 'original universe.' … A thorough understanding of the metaverse is required to develop a suitable translation and reclaim futures from the capitalists." — `docx-extract` **[P0012]**

Two compounding problems for *this* thesis specifically:

1. **元 mistranslates meta-** (the thesis's own point [P0012]): 元 = origin/source/previous, so 元宇宙 reads "original/source universe."
2. **宇宙 IS the thesis's *universe*** — the natural substrate, the first pole of the squeeze, the term the thesis reads as ≈ 道. So 元宇宙 = "meta-*宇宙*" literally *names the metaverse with the universe's own word*, collapsing the very universe/metaverse distinction the entire thesis is built on. The standard academic gloss confirms the conflation: Park & Kim define metaverse as "a compound word of transcendence meta and universe" (`docx-extract` [P0017]) — i.e. 宇宙 sits *inside* the popular term. For a thesis whose load-bearing move is *universe ≠ metaverse*, adopting 元宇宙 as the technical term would be self-defeating.

**Verdict: 元宇宙 is ruled OUT as the thesis's technical term.** It may be *named once* as the popular/PRC-standard term the thesis displaces (it is unavoidable as the word the reader arrives with), but it must not carry the thesis's concept.

Key eliminations at a glance: **元宇宙** and **偽宇宙** both reuse 宇宙 (= the universe) and so erase the universe/metaverse distinction; **偽界** collides with 世界 (the thesis's *world*, a third distinct term); **第二自然** re-imports a frame the thesis explicitly dissolves; **偽天/人工天** smuggles in 天 as an unlicensed third cosmological term.

## 4. The layered solution (incorporating, then exceeding, Perplexity)

**[PPX] Perplexity's recommended strategy** (per the author's discussion): a **layered** naming — **偽境** as the surface/phenomenal name, **道 / 偽道** as the deep metaphysical pair, and a technical substrate form **偽道本體 / 偽道基體** for maximum precision; and it flagged that 境界論 is one of *four axes* (宇宙論 / 本體論 / 工夫論 / 境界論) of Chinese metaphysics, which is why 境 reads phenomenally.

This note **endorses the layered move** but re-grounds it in the thesis's own three-layer ontology rather than in the four-axis taxonomy alone:

- **偽道 = the enduring-principle / substrate pole** (the "real" in the thesis's sense): the self-completing artificial *ground*, paired against 道 (= universe). This is the technical term.
- **偽境 = the observable-phenomenon pole**: the *experienced* artificial milieu — the source-manuscript's existing term, which the thesis can keep precisely *as* the surface name, with its phenomenality now a feature, not a bug.

The pairing is not a mere convenience: it **mirrors the thesis's Ch1 stratification** (enduring principles → … → observable phenomena, `docx-extract` [P0106]). 道/偽道 names the *ground*; 境/偽境 names *how that ground shows up to a subject*. See §6.4.

On the heavier technical forms **偽道本體 / 偽道基體** **[PPX]**: useful as a one-time *gloss* at first occurrence ("偽道（偽道本體）"), but too heavy for running prose; the thesis's own "background environment 背景環境" already does the substrate work in apposition. Recommend: introduce **偽道** with a parenthetical substrate gloss once, then run 偽道.

## 5. Verbatim grounding — 道 as generative ground (the case for the 道/偽道 pair)

**《道德經》 (Wang Bi 王弼本 base text, verbatim — `zh.wikisource.org/wiki/道德經_(王弼本)`, fetched 2026-06-02; ctext.org was CAPTCHA-blocked this session):**

> **第一章:** 道可道，非常道，名可名，非常名；無名，天地之始，有名，萬物之母。…此兩者，同出而異名，同謂之玄。玄之又玄，眾妙之門。

> **第二十五章:** 有物混成，先天地生。寂兮寥兮，獨立而不改，周行而不殆，可以為天下母。吾不知其名，字之曰道，強為之名曰大。…故道大，天大，地大，王亦大。…人法地，地法天，天法道，道法自然。

> **第四十二章:** 道生一，一生二，二生三，三生萬物。萬物負陰而抱陽，沖氣以為和。

These fix 道 as **the generative ground prior to heaven-and-earth** (先天地生), **the mother of all under heaven** (天下母 / 萬物之母), and **the source from which the myriad things issue** (道生一…三生萬物) — exactly the "ultimate generative ground / intransitive substrate" profile the thesis assigns the *universe*.

**SEP, "Laozi" (plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/, OA, fetched 2026-06-02) — verbatim:**
> "Dao is said to be the 'beginning' of all things (chs. 1, 25)."
> "Dao gives birth to One; One gives birth to Two; Two gives birth to Three; Three gives birth to the ten thousand things' (ch. 42)."
> "Suggestive of its creativity and nurturance, Dao is likened to a mother (e.g., chs. 1, 25)."

**SEP, "Daoism" (plato.stanford.edu/entries/daoism/, OA, fetched 2026-06-02) — verbatim:**
> "Dào gives rise to, sustains (guides), and eventually eliminates things."
> "Chinese Daoism is holistic or monist in the sense of the whole being prior to its parts."

**Britannica, "dao" (britannica.com/topic/dao, fetched 2026-06-02) — verbatim:** the Cosmic Dao is
> "not any particular thing in the universe but rather the totality of all things as well as the matrix within which they come into and pass out of being"

and "incessantly generates the ten thousand things," "the source of the universe," "immanent, always present and always emerging."

**Upshot:** the universe≈道 reading is well-grounded. The squeeze, in Chinese, becomes **道 (natural ground) vs. 偽道 (artificial ground)** — a maximally clean two-term pair.

## 6. Five thesis-specific arguments (developing beyond Perplexity)

### 6.1 偽=人為 RESCUES 偽道/偽境 from the pejorative — and the thesis OWNS the "false-Dao" reading

**[PPX] Perplexity correctly found** that 偽道/伪道 already circulates pejoratively as "false / heretical Dao." Verbatim, confirmed this session:

> **PhilArchive YANTSA-2** — Charles X. Yang, 《儒学"七伪"：从孔孟到张载的伪道之路》 / *The Seven Falsehoods of Confucianism: The Pseudo-Daoist Path from Confucius to Zhang Zai*. The paper characterizes 伪道之路 as a path that "on the surface cloaked in '天理'（Heavenly Principle）, '仁義'（benevolence and righteousness）… but in essence executes human rule in the name of '天命'（Mandate of Heaven）" — a distortion/misuse of 天道. (philarchive.org/rec/YANTSA-2 + /archive/YANTSA-2, fetched 2026-06-02.)
> *(Perplexity also cited 張定 / Zhang Ding 儒教辨謬 for 「伪道之路」 as distorted misuse of 天道 — not independently re-fetched this session; carry as [PPX-cited, unverified].)*

Perplexity reads this as a *liability* (偽道 "alone doesn't evoke environment/substrate" and drags a pejorative). **The thesis inverts this into a feature.** Within the thesis's apparatus, **偽 = 人為** — the neutral, classical name for *the humanly-made* — established verbatim from Xunzi (reused from [[Ch-2-0_why-artificiality-not-technology_2026-06-02]]):

> *Xunzi* 性惡篇: 「人之性惡，其善者偽也」;「可學而能、可事而成之在人者謂之偽。」 (zh.wikisource, verbatim)
> 楊倞 (Tang) 荀子注: 「故『偽』字『人』傍『為』，亦會意字也」 — 偽 *just is* 人為, the human-done.
> *Shuowen* 說文解字 人部: 「偽，詐也。」 — the *general-usage* pejorative drift (偽 → deceive).

The thesis's Ch II move is **exactly** to recover the neutral 偽=人為 *against* the 偽→詐 ("false/deceive") drift — and it pairs this with the parallel Western drift *artificial → fake* (etymonline: "fictitious, not genuine" 1640s) as "the prejudice the thesis resists." So:

> **"偽道 = false Dao" is not a bug to avoid; it is the thesis's own thesis.** The metaverse *is* a humanly-made / counterfeit 道 — an artificial ground that has acquired given-like authority. The pejorative "false Dao" and the neutral "human-made Dao" are the *same fact* read with and without the prejudice — and the thesis explicitly thematizes that prejudice (偽→詐, artificial→fake). 偽道 thus performs, in its very reception-history, the cross-linguistic drift the thesis names. This is a *strength* unique to 偽道, available to no neutral coinage.

The pejorative is therefore not neutralized by hiding it but **claimed**: a first-occurrence gloss does the work — "偽道（此處『偽』取荀子『人為』之義，非『詐偽』之貶）" — turning the reader's "false Dao" reflex into the thesis's demonstration-in-miniature.

### 6.2 元宇宙 is actively MISLEADING for THIS thesis — recommend AGAINST as technical term

Developed in §2. The decisive thesis-specific point (beyond the Intro's "元 mistranslates meta-"): **元宇宙 = meta-*宇宙*, and 宇宙 is the thesis's *universe***. The popular term builds the metaverse *out of the universe's own name*, erasing the universe/metaverse distinction the squeeze requires. **Acknowledge 元宇宙 only as the popular term the thesis displaces; never as the concept's carrier.**

### 6.3 第二自然 (second nature) — poor fit; explain

第二自然 fails on two thesis-specific grounds:
1. **It re-imports the nature/culture frame the thesis dissolves.** Ch1 rejects the nature/culture divide outright ("no split is possible"; "artificiality … replaces culture", [P0109]). "Second *nature*" presupposes a first nature/culture contrast the thesis has discarded.
2. **Adorno's *second nature* is engaged as a *predecessor the thesis goes beyond*, not a synonym.** Per FRAMING §6d and `chV-sim_v2`/`chVI-sim_v2`: Adorno *re-values* the nature/culture distinction (the made *appears* given — "second nature is, in truth, first nature"), whereas the thesis *dissolves* it and replaces it with universe/world/artificiality. Adopting 第二自然 as the metaverse's name would re-collapse the thesis onto the predecessor it differentiates itself from. Use it (if at all) only when *discussing Adorno*, not as the term.

### 6.4 Layered 偽道 / 偽境 MAPS the thesis's stratification — not a mere convenience

The thesis's Ch1 three layers (`docx-extract` [P0106]): **enduring conditions/principles → structures/dynamics → context-specific observable phenomena.** The two Chinese terms align to the *top* and *bottom*:

| Thesis layer | Chinese term | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Enduring principle / "real" / substrate** | **偽道** | 道 is the classical name for an *enduring generative ground* (§5); 偽道 = the artificial counterpart at the same depth. This is where 自足完成化 lives — the only "real" feature. |
| **Observable phenomenon / the milieu as experienced** | **偽境** | **[PPX]** 境/境界論 is the *phenomenal-aesthetic-experiential* axis (one of 宇宙論/本體論/工夫論/境界論; corroborated this session via Mou Zongsan / Lao Siguang scholarship on 工夫論與境界論). 偽境 = the artificial ground *as it appears to and is inhabited by* a subject. |

So the author's worry — "**境 is phenomenological**, sits *beside* 本體論 not beneath it, but the metaverse is *beyond* phenomenological" — is **resolved by the layering itself**: 境 *should* be phenomenal, because 偽境 is the *observable-phenomenon* name; the substrate/ontological work is carried by **偽道**, which sits at the 道/本體 depth. The two are not rivals; they are the *same metaverse described at two strata*, exactly as the thesis describes everything at multiple strata. This upgrades Perplexity's layered strategy from "use a phenomenal name on the surface and a metaphysical pair underneath" to "**the two-term naming is a reflection of the thesis's own ontology.**"

### 6.5 道 / 偽道 is the thesis's strongest Chinese framing of the squeeze

If **universe ≈ 道** (§1d, §5) and **metaverse = an artificial, self-completing 道**, then the squeeze in Chinese is **道 與 偽道 之間的壓搾** — the human squeezed between the natural generative ground (道) and its artificial, self-completing counterpart (偽道). This is:
- **maximally economical** — one character (偽/人為) does all the differentiating work, on a term (道) the thesis already leans on for the universe;
- **structurally faithful** — two *grounds* of the same depth, incommensurable (F2), neither reducible to the other (`squeeze-ontology` F2; [P0167] "laws of its own");
- **resonant with the thesis's tekhne→phusis provocation** — 偽道 literally says *an artificial 道*, i.e. a *made* thing that has taken on the ground-like, self-arising character of the *given* (道法自然) — the Chinese face of "nomos-by-origin, phusis-by-operation."

> This is assessed as **the thesis's strongest Chinese framing.** Its one cost — the inherited "false Dao" pejorative — is precisely what §6.1 converts into an asset. Note the source-manuscript's own Chinese squeeze term is **二重圧搾 / 二重壓搾** ("double squeeze," per memory `session_2026_05_10`); 道/偽道 slots into that as the *named poles* of the squeeze the manuscript already articulates.

## 7. Highlighted strong candidates + recommendation

### ★ Candidate A — 偽道 (the technical / substrate term) — RECOMMENDED PRIMARY

**The case:** It is the only candidate that names a **substrate-level ground** (criterion 1) in the **人為 register** (criteria 2, 4) while forming the **cleanest possible squeeze-pair with the universe** (道 / 偽道, criterion 3) and sitting at the thesis's **enduring-principle stratum** (criterion 6). Its sole weakness — the pre-existing pejorative "false Dao" (**[PPX]**, verbatim §6.1) — is, uniquely, a weakness the thesis *owns*: 偽=人為 neutralizes it doctrinally, and the residual "false/counterfeit Dao" overtone *performs* the thesis's own argument about the 偽→詐 / artificial→fake prejudice (§6.1). Introduce with a one-time gloss disambiguating 偽=人為 from 偽=詐.

### ★ Candidate B — 偽境 (the surface / observable-realm term) — RECOMMENDED COMPANION

**The case:** It is the **source-manuscript's existing term** (人為之境/伪境, submitted to 総合人間学会), already neutral via 偽=人為, and maximally readable. The author's worry that **境 is phenomenological** is *correct* — and is exactly why it belongs at the **observable-phenomenon layer** (criterion 6), as the metaverse-*as-experienced*, with 偽道 carrying the substrate weight. Retaining 偽境 preserves continuity with the published Sogo paper while fixing its only limitation (phenomenality) by *assigning* it the phenomenal role rather than letting it over-reach into the substrate role.

### Recommendation

**Adopt a layered pair, mirroring the thesis's own stratification:**
- **偽道** — the **primary technical term** for the metaverse-as-substrate (the self-completing artificial ground; paired against 道 = universe). First occurrence: **偽道（背景環境；此『偽』取荀子「人為」之義）**.
- **偽境** — the **companion surface term** for the metaverse-as-experienced milieu (continuity with the Sogo source-manuscript).

Decline **元宇宙** for technical use (name once as the displaced popular/PRC term); decline **偽宇宙 / 偽界 / 第二自然 / 偽天 / 人工天** for the reasons in §3/§6. The heavy forms **偽道本體 / 偽道基體** (**[PPX]**) are kept as an optional first-occurrence gloss only.
