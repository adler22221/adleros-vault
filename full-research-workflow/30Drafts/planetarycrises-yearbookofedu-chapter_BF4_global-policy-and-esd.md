# BF4 — Global Policy Architectures and ESD: A Critical Literature Brief

## 1. The Architecture (Brief Map)

- **SDG 4.7 (UN 2015)**: the foundational normative anchor — commits states to ensure by 2030 that all learners "acquire the knowledge and skills needed to promote sustainable development, including, among others, through education for sustainable development and sustainable lifestyles, human rights, gender equality, promotion of a culture of peace and non-violence, global citizenship and appreciation of cultural diversity." Operationalised by UNESCO as the **ESD for 2030 Roadmap** (2020–2030).
- **Berlin Declaration on ESD (UNESCO World Conference, 17–19 May 2021)**: 2,800 delegates from 160 countries; sets **16 commitments** for ESD to permeate all levels of education by 2025 and become a core element of teacher training; reframes ESD as "key instrument for the achievement of the SDGs" ([UNESDOC 381228](https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000381228)).
- **Transforming Education Summit (TES, New York, Sept 2022)**: launches the **Greening Education Partnership (GEP)** as one of five action tracks. GEP frames itself as a "whole-of-system" approach with **four pillars**: (i) greener schools, (ii) greener curricula, (iii) greener teacher capacity, (iv) greener communities. By Nov 2025, 98 Member States and 2,000+ organisations have joined ([UNESCO GEP](https://www.unesco.org/en/sustainable-development/education/greening-future)).
- **Greening Curriculum Indicator (UIS, Feb 2025)** operationalises GEP pillar (ii) into a measurable SDG-aligned metric ([UIS metadata 473](https://www.uis.unesco.org/sites/default/files/medias/fichiers/2025/08/Metadata_473_Greening_Curriculum_Indicator_0.pdf)).
- **OECD's positioning**: PISA 2025 introduces, for the first time, a Science Framework organised around **"Agency in the Anthropocene"** — three core competencies (Explain, Make Informed Decisions, Demonstrate Respect for Diverse Perspectives) ([PISA 2025 framework](https://pisa-framework.oecd.org/science-2025/)); paralleled by the **Environmental Sustainability Competence Toolbox** (OECD 2024) and the *Rethinking education in the context of climate change* report (OECD Feb 2024).
- **GEM Report 2024/5**: MECCE-led monitoring shows climate is **the least integrated SDG-4.7 domain** — absent from grade-3 syllabi in 78 countries; only **39%** of states have a national climate-education law/policy; **63%** of teacher-training plans include climate ([GEM Report 2024 — Education and climate change](https://www.unesco.org/gem-report/en/2024ccec)).

## 2. Core Critiques

### 2.1 Depoliticization & the "Technical Fix" Frame
- Adding "sustainable" to "development" does not alter international development's tendency to **misrepresent political problems as technical ones**; SDG indicators reinforce "one-size-fits-all" depoliticizing narratives (Skinner et al. in *Compare*, [Tandfonline 2022](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03057925.2022.2129957)).
- UNESCO's Mahatma Gandhi Institute (MGIEP) emphasis on **neuroscience + positive psychology + "individual empowerment"** is read as deflecting critique away from structural drivers, and as articulating with authoritarian-nationalist governance contexts (esp. India) ([Compare 2024 — "Still the conscience of humanity?"](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03057925.2024.2371259)).
- **Anchor authors**: Karen Mundy; Aaron Benavot; Will Brehm; Vanessa Andreotti (development-education recalibration, [DERC Issue 33](https://www.developmenteducationreview.com/issue/issue-33/proposal-recalibrating-sdg-47)).

### 2.2 Technocratic Measurement / Competency-Frame Reductionism
- Sustainability is **disaggregated and parcelled to expert silos** (social/economic/environmental); the resulting competency lists are managerial, behaviourist, and "not primarily concerned with supporting inner development" ([Frontiers in Education 2025 — Rethinking sustainability in engineering education](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2025.1587430/full)).
- Critique of OECD's PISA "Agency in the Anthropocene": measuring agency as an individual cognitive competence risks naturalising the **liberal-individualist subject** that drove the crisis in the first place ([Cambridge — Engaging Environmental Education through PISA](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/australian-journal-of-environmental-education/article/engaging-environmental-education-through-pisa-leveraging-curriculum-as-a-political-process/1FF5A745BF7F4A5BDA31D3D283491856); [J. Environmental Education 2023, Education to build agency in the Anthropocene](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00958964.2023.2277209)).
- **Anchor authors**: Arjen Wals; Bob Jickling; Lucy Bell.

### 2.3 The "More Education = Better Climate Outcomes" Fallacy
- Silova, Rappleye & Komatsu (2019, *ECNU Review*) use PISA-2015 + per-capita CO₂ data to show countries with "better" education tend to have **higher** environmental impact; the SDG-4 expansionist logic is empirically anti-ecological ([Measuring What Really Matters](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2096531119878897)).
- Follow-up: Silova, Rappleye & Komatsu (2025) "**What If More Education Is Not the Answer? Beyond the Business-as-Usual Approach to SDGs**" ([Sage 2025](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/09734082251355100)) — the strongest direct attack on SDG-4's productivist core.
- NORRAG: "Facing the Climate Change Catastrophe: Education as Solution or Cause?" ([NORRAG blog](https://www.norrageducation.org/facing-the-climate-change-catastrophe-education-as-solution-or-cause-by-iveta-silova-hikaru-komatsu-and-jeremy-rappleye/)).

### 2.4 Coloniality / Exclusion of Indigenous & Non-Western Traditions
- UNESCO ESD reproduces a **hegemonic Euro-Western "saviour" discourse** that silences Indigenous ways of knowing ([ResearchGate — Tracing the Lines of Power, Coloniality, and Neoliberalism in UNESCO's ESD Policy, 2023](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/372598521)).
- A UNESCO review of 46 countries found Indigenous-knowledge integration "insufficient and inadequately linked to SD priorities."
- Recent decolonial work: *Environmental Education Research* (2025) on Tanzania ([Tandfonline](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13504622.2025.2484324)); *PROSPECTS* (2025) "Embracing the decolonial, critical, and ethical shift in GCED" ([Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11125-025-09735-x)); MDPI *Sustainability* (2024) on Africa ([MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/16/9/3744)).
- **Anchor authors**: Vanessa Andreotti; Sharon Stein; Cash Ahenakew; Dalene Swanson.

### 2.5 North–South Power Asymmetries & Policy Borrowing
- **Steiner-Khamsi**'s borrowing/lending framework: international agencies act as facilitators and *initiators* of transnational policy transfer; ESD/SDG-4.7 indicators travel as if culturally neutral but are authored in OECD/UNESCO/World-Bank epistemic centres ([Teachers College Press](https://www.tcpress.com/the-global-politics-of-educational-borrowing-and-lending-9780807744932); [FreshEd #359](https://freshedpodcast.com/359-steiner-khamsi/)).
- The 2012 *World Yearbook* (Steiner-Khamsi & Waldow) is the foundational anchor for this argument in *this very book series* — useful precedent to cite for the editors.

### 2.6 Ed-Tech / Corporate Capture & the "Greening = Digitalisation" Slippage
- GEM Report 2023 (and the new "Ed-Tech Tragedy?" UNESCO report, 2026): ed-tech transfers authority from teachers/communities to **for-profit actors**, characterised by "data extraction, advertising, top-down control, surveillance" ([Diane Ravitch summary, 2026](https://dianeravitch.net/2026/05/05/unesco-report-on-the-dangers-of-education-tech/)).
- The slippage: "green skills" partnerships at TES 2022 (WorldSkills, GBC-Education) blend climate-education objectives with **workforce-readiness for the green economy**, creating space for corporate co-authorship of GEP's pillar (iv) ([WorldSkills/TES](https://worldskills.org/media/news/un-transforming-education-summit-partnership-close-green-skills-gap/)).

### 2.7 Rhetoric / Resourcing Gap
- One year post-TES: only **a third** of countries at highest child-climate-risk reported concrete government action on greening education ([World Education Blog, Feb 2024](https://world-education-blog.org/2024/02/15/more-than-one-year-after-the-transforming-education-summit-what-progress-have-countries-made/)).
- TES omitted **resilience / crisis-preparedness** despite framing the crises rhetorically (Save the Children critique, [2024](https://www.savethechildren.org.uk/blogs/2024/what-progress-has-been-made-since-the-2022-transforming-educatio)).
- Earth Day audit: 70% of teachers self-report **unprepared** to teach climate; 23% feel able to address climate action ([Earth Day Lowdown](https://www.earthday.org/the-lowdown-on-climate-education/)).

## 3. Empirical / Comparative Case Material

- **Europe (UK, Germany, Italy)** — Frontiers in Education (2023) comparative geography-curriculum study: Italy is an outlier, climate marginal; UK and German curricula assign significant weight but diverge on natural vs socio-economic framing ([Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2023.1216780/full)). Only 60% of Italian principals report climate in their curriculum vs OECD-average 88% ([WEF 2022](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2022/08/climate-change-schools-education/)).
- **Brazil** — Lula government's 2024 inclusion of climate change in the National Environmental Education Policy (PNEA), staged for COP30 Belém 2025; a politically charged counter-example to OECD-centric framing.
- **Five-country implementation study** (J. Research in Science Teaching, 2024) — voices of policymakers, PD providers, teachers: persistent decoupling between national policy text and classroom enactment ([Tandfonline](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09500693.2024.2314572)).
- **UNESCO 2021 country audit**: only ~half of national curricula reference climate change ([UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/only-half-national-curricula-world-have-reference-climate-change-unesco-warns)).
- **MECCE Data Platform** (McKenzie et al.) — UNFCCC national submissions cross-country comparison (Bonn 2023 update); the dominant empirical infrastructure for monitoring policy uptake ([MECCE](https://mecce.ca/)).

## 4. Politically Inflected Analyses — Who Benefits, Who Is Silenced

- **Beneficiaries**: OECD measurement industry; large EdTech and "green-skills" certification consortia; multilateral monitoring infrastructure (UIS, MECCE) that produces policy-legitimating data; UNESCO itself as convener.
- **Silenced**: Indigenous and community knowledge-holders; teacher unions (largely absent from GEP governance); youth climate movements (instrumentalised as "voice" without authorship — see [NORRAG: When Education Is the Problem](https://www.norrageducation.org/when-education-is-the-problem-lessons-from-youth-climate-activism)); Global-South curriculum scholars.
- **What gets measured vs what matters**: Greening Curriculum Indicator counts *keyword presence in syllabi*; what is NOT measured — political economy of fossil-fuel curricula sponsorship, the carbon footprint of mass schooling itself (Silova et al.'s point), pedagogical relationality, intergenerational justice.

## 5. Tensions the Chapter Could Exploit

1. **Liberal-internationalist promise vs decolonial critique**: GEP/SDG-4.7 universalism collides with the empirical finding that Westernised education *correlates with higher* ecological impact — a direct contradiction at the heart of the architecture.
2. **Measurement vs transformation**: PISA 2025 "Agency in the Anthropocene" attempts to measure agency, but the very act of comparable measurement entrenches the technocratic individualism that critical scholarship identifies as the problem.
3. **Whole-of-system rhetoric vs four-pillar fragmentation**: GEP's "whole-of-system" claim is operationalised as four siloed pillars + a single Greening Curriculum Indicator — performing holism while reproducing reductionism.
4. **Crisis framing vs crisis omission**: TES invoked climate emergency rhetorically while omitting resilience/preparedness; the architecture treats climate as a *curriculum content* problem rather than a *systemic condition* of education.
5. **Author-editor leverage**: McKenzie co-leads MECCE (insider-monitoring) while co-editing this *World Yearbook* — the chapter can productively position itself in the **reflexive seam** between policy-instrumental scholarship and critical scholarship the same volume is convening.
