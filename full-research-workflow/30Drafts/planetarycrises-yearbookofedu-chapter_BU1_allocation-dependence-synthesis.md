## Core Definition (User's Own Framing — Preserve Exactly)

**Allocation Dependence** describes how people and institutions become reliant on **externally allocated resources** — degrees, income, funding, titles, permissions, social validation — and must conform to **allocation measures** (tests, rubrics, bureaucratic criteria, algorithms) to gain or retain access. Agents adapt through:

- **Assimilation**: adopting traits externally rewarded and measurable
- **Trimming**: discarding personal qualities that don't fit the expected mold, even if meaningful or regenerative
- **Instrumentalizing** identity and labor for performative recognition

These adaptations shift the logic of action from **value regeneration** (creating meaning, care, coherence) to **value redemption** (extracting validation from metric systems or gatekeepers). This is the user's master conceptual axis — not a description of exploitation but a structural inversion of what learning is for.

---

## Empirical Foundation

**Natural experiment** (Ethnographic, Taiwan): An alternative high school whose explicit mission was to free students from credential pressure. Students nonetheless voluntarily maintained credentialist behaviors — trimming passions, aligning to "what counts" — even in a high-agency, unpressured environment. Key finding: Allocation Dependence is **not** driven by structural scarcity or cultural legacy (e.g., Confucianism) alone, but by a deeper logic of survival dependence on allocated opportunity.

This case grounds the mechanism and anchors the empirical claim in Asian educational context — directly relevant to the CFP's comparative framing.

## Mechanism: Fractal and Transfactual

Allocation Dependence is described as **fractal** (appears at all scales) and **transfactual** (causally operative across different domains regardless of surface differences). Manifestations include:

| Domain | How Allocation Dependence Operates |
|--------|-----------------------------------|
| Education | Students trim uniqueness to pass credentialist gates |
| Workplace | KPIs steer toward optimization over meaningful contribution |
| Global South | Conformity to Global North standards to receive aid or access platforms |
| Digital ecology | Algorithmic attention allocation → users reshape content and self-presentation to fit invisible metrics |
| Animal domestication | Survival through human allocation erodes wild traits and ecological resilience |
| Academia | Precarity despite meeting criteria; unfit for alternatives after a decade of assimilation |

The **transfactual** property is what makes allocation dependence a *generative mechanism* (not a surface description), and what positions it as a lens on planetary crises more broadly.

## Consequences the User Flags

1. **Control and internalized fetishism**: metrics gradually dictate self-worth; individual becomes proxy for the allocation system
2. **Perpetuation of inequality through the unequalization of uniqueness**: marginalized not by lack but by epistemic misfit with dominant criteria
3. **Alienation and deterioration**: incompatible molds → excess and deficiency in development; means subjugate ends
4. **Waste and pollution**: human potentials misallocated, discarded, or deployed where they degrade — *mirroring ecological pollution*
5. **Resource exhaustion**: value is redeemed not regenerated → systemic depletion of emotional, ecological, cultural resources
6. **Fragility and cascading risk**: every step of life depends on successful passage through narrow contingencies

**Note**: consequences 4, 5, and 6 are the direct bridge to planetary crises framing. The ecological-pollution analogy is user-generated and should be preserved.

## Theoretical Architecture

The concept sits within the user's **Modes of Dependencies** framework (5 modes):

| Mode | Chinese | Core Question |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Cosmic-Terrestrial | 天地依存 | Can I sustain life from this place and its rhythms? |
| **Allocation** | **配分依存** | Do I qualify under the criteria this apparatus has set? |
| Money | 貨幣依存 | Can I purchase what I need? |
| Artificiality | 人工性依存 | What human-built supports exist and can I access them? |
| Controllability | 制御性依存 | How much of the unpredictable can be brought under designed control? |

The **inverted pyramid** insight: Cosmic-terrestrial dependence is materially largest (it conditions all life) but experientially smallest (noticed only in catastrophe); Controllability dependence is materially most fragile but experientially most present (platforms, connectivity feel like existence infrastructure). This inversion is the structural driver of both the ecological crisis and the failure of education to respond to it.

**Dialogue with Karatani**: Allocation dependence maps onto Karatani's Mode B (redistribution/plunder) but adds the self-organizing, voluntary internalization dimension Karatani misses.

**Dialogue with Polanyi**: Re-embedding markets is necessary but insufficient if social relations are themselves severed from ecological conditions. Dependency mode theory reconnects the circulation layer to the metabolic condition Polanyi backgrounds.

## Intervention Typology (User's Own)

Many reforms address effects without recognizing the underlying mechanism:
- Circular economy: helps with resource exhaustion but not fragility
- Holistic admissions: diversifies filters but reproduces the gatekeeping logic

Allocation dependence offers a **diagnostic lens** distinguishing compensatory interventions (treat symptoms) from transformative ones (change the base rule of agent behavior). The counter-rule is **Learning by Caring**: grow not by competing for credentials but by caring for real-world issues, people, or ecosystems.

## Connection to Planetary Crises (for the Chapter)

1. **The 1.5% problem** (BF4/BF5 cross-reference): Education receives <1.5% of global climate finance. The allocation dependence lens explains *why* — education systems are structurally locked into allocation patterns that cannot be redirected by marginal finance injections
2. **Waste and pollution analogy**: Allocation Dependence produces psychosocial waste (suppressed human potential) that mirrors ecological pollution — a frame the editors would recognize
3. **Self-domestication**: The mechanism by which education under planetary crisis continues reproducing maladaptive behaviors despite knowing better
4. **Fragility**: Allocation-dependent education systems are fragile precisely when resilience is needed most (under climate stress, refugee displacement, etc.)
