## 1. Why Phenomenology Matters Here

The thesis's universe / world / metaverse architecture risks a charge of *philosophical thinness*: even granting the critical-realist stratification (ANNOT-L3), the Bhaskar/Archer authority for treating culture-as-real (ANNOT-L3 §3), the Obara evolutionary-ecological warrant (ANNOT-Obara), and the Hui cosmotechnics specification (ANNOT-Hui), the architecture has not yet articulated *how the world is lived*. The world has been characterised as a real-level cultural-symbolic stratum with emergent causal powers; it has not yet been characterised as the *lived-experiential field* in which any actually existing human encounters and reproduces it.

This is the gap a phenomenological supplement fills. Two candidates are available: Watsuji Tetsurō's *fūdo* (climate-habitat) analysis and Edmund Husserl's *Lebenswelt* (lifeworld) analysis. Both diagnose a structurally similar phenomenon — the erasure of the lived-experiential ground by formal-universalist abstraction. Both were produced in or near 1927–1937, when European philosophy was beginning to register what subsequent decades would call the technospheric crisis. But they take radically different routes to that diagnosis: Watsuji from a *climatic-spatial* situatedness deeply marked by his Asian standpoint; Husserl from a *transcendental-temporal* analysis in the European Cartesian-Kantian tradition.

This document defends the choice — already implicit in `Ch-3-4_v2_suppl_Hui-Watsuji.md` — to deploy Watsuji as the **primary phenomenological supplement** and Husserl as the **Option-β contrast** signalled in a single linking footnote. The choice is not arbitrary; it is determined by what the thesis's argument needs at this particular juncture and by which thinker's analysis better tracks the empirical variation the squeeze hypothesis must explain.

## 2. Watsuji's *Fūdo* — The Primary Phenomenological Concept

### 2.1 The basic concept

Watsuji's *fūdo* (風土, climate or climate-habitat) is, on first inspection, a familiar concept: it names the climatic-geographical conditions of a region. This first-inspection reading is wrong, and Watsuji's own opening pages of *A Climate* are devoted to refusing it. *Fūdo* is **not** a naturalistic concept. It is **not** the climate as objective geographical given. It is, in Watsuji's own (T0-confirmed) phrasing:

> [VERIFY: confirmed | watsujiClimatePhilosophicalStudy1961 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"it is essential to my position that the phenomena of climate are treated as expressions of subjective human existence and not of natural environment" — NLM source UUID `5c9f4410` (A-Sources `c68ca15e`) | confirmed 2026-05-05 T0 pass]

*Fūdo* is the climate-habitat *as a field of human self-objectivation*. It is climate-as-lived, climate-as-the-medium-through-which-the-self-becomes-legible-to-itself. The decisive Watsuji formulation:

> [VERIFY: confirmed | watsujiClimatePhilosophicalStudy1961 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"Climate, then, is the agent by which human life is objectivised, and it is here that man comprehends himself; there is self-discovery in climate." — NLM source UUID `5c9f4410` (A-Sources `c68ca15e`) | confirmed 2026-05-05 T0 pass | additional confirmed passages from same source: "we ourselves are already in the coldness of the outside air"; "history is climatic history and climate is historical climate"]

Three points deserve unpacking:

**(a) "The agent."** Climate is not a setting in which life happens; climate is an *agent*. It does something. It objectivises human life — that is, it gives human life its objective expression, its legible form, its situated character. Without the climate-agent, the human life would not appear as the determinate kind of life it is.

**(b) "By which."** The relation is instrumental-mediating: climate is the *means* by which human life takes objective form. Not a condition that constrains an independently-existing human life; not a backdrop against which life unfolds; but the *medium* through which life acquires its determinate character.

**(c) "Objectivised."** Watsuji's term is ontologically loaded. To be "objectivised" is to acquire objective being — to come into determinate existence as a particular kind. Human life *as such* is not yet determinate; it becomes determinate-in-this-or-that-way through the climatic mediation.

This is a phenomenological concept in the strict sense: it characterises the structure of lived experience. But it is not a Husserlian-transcendental phenomenology — it is climatic-existential. Climate is not analysed as it appears to a transcendental ego; it is analysed as it constitutes the human as a being-in-its-climate.

### 2.2 The three climate types

Watsuji's *A Climate* develops the framework through three specific climate-types, each producing a different mode of human self-understanding:

- **Monsoon** (Asia, including Watsuji's Japan): the human is *receptive*, *enduring*, oriented toward submission-and-resignation in the face of overwhelming natural force (typhoons, flooding, the seasonal violence of the rains)
- **Desert** (Arabia, North Africa, parts of West Asia): the human is *combative*, oriented toward unity-against-environment, producing the strongly tribal-confederal social form
- **Meadow** (Europe, the temperate zones): the human is *rational-productive*, oriented toward systematic management of an environment that is neither overwhelming nor scarce

Two cautions are essential before the thesis deploys this typology:

**Caution 1: Watsuji's typology is not ethnological essentialism.** It is *not* a claim that all monsoon-region humans are receptive, or that all desert-region humans are combative. It is a claim about the *constitutive medium* — what climate-habitat does to the structure of human self-understanding when it is lived as the dominant medium. Within any actually existing climate, multiple historical formations and individual variations exist.

**Caution 2: The 1943 revision risk.** The *A Climate* text was originally drafted from Watsuji's 1927 Berlin lecture-notes and published in book form in 1935; a revised edition was published in 1943 under wartime pressure. The 1943 revision introduced nationalist inflections that compromise specific passages. The Janz commentary (T0-confirmed UUID `fce95d0b`, A-Sources) establishes which passages belong to the original 1935 stratum and which to the 1943 revision. **The *fūdo* concept itself precedes and survives the revision; cite with care, using Janz as companion.** This is a load-bearing methodological caution for any thesis use of Watsuji.

### 2.3 The constitutive role for the world-stratum

For the thesis, *fūdo* is the **phenomenological complement to the structural-stratification analysis**. The critical-realist stratification (ANNOT-L3) tells us that the world is `dr`-level real with emergent causal powers. The Hui cosmotechnics analysis (ANNOT-Hui) tells us that the world is a particular human-emergent compound of cosmological orientation and technical embodiment. The Watsuji *fūdo* analysis tells us that the world is *always already climatically situated*: the world is not an abstract symbolic system hovering above material life; it is condensed in particular climates, constituted by them as the medium of human self-objectivation, and irreducibly local in its texture.

This three-fold characterisation — structural (Bhaskar/Archer), cosmotechnical (Hui), phenomenological (Watsuji) — together constitutes the thesis's full account of *what a world is*. The squeeze hypothesis can then state precisely what is being squeezed: a real-level stratum that is at once cosmotechnically articulated and phenomenologically lived through climatic situatedness.

## 3. Watsuji's *Aidagara* — The World as In-Betweenness

Watsuji's later *Ethics* (*Rinrigaku*, 1937–1949) extends the *fūdo* analysis with the concept of **間柄 (aidagara)** — in-betweenness, the relational space between persons within which ethics arises. This concept is central to Watsuji's broader philosophical project and supplements the *fūdo* concept by characterising the *social-relational* dimension of the same lived-world that *fūdo* characterises in its *climatic* dimension. [VERIFY: pending-T0-retrieval | NLM-auth-restored-2026-05-05 | check Watsuji *Ethics (Rinrigaku)* in A-Sources (UUID lookup needed); if present, retrieve *aidagara* definition, *ningen* (人間) etymology, and trust-as-grounding passages.]

The fundamental Watsujian claim is that the human (人間, *ningen*) is intrinsically *inter-personal*: the very kanji compound 人間 — literally "person-between" — names the human's constitutive between-ness, the inability to be a human without being-among-others. The Western philosophical tradition's Cartesian solitary ego is, on Watsuji's reading, an artefact of a particular philosophical methodology rather than a feature of human reality. The actual human is always already in *aidagara*.

For the thesis, *aidagara* contributes the **trust-and-betweenness dimension of the world-stratum**. The world is not only climatically situated; it is also relationally textured. What a world *is* includes its specific configuration of *aidagara* — the specific patterns through which persons are between persons in this place.

The squeeze hypothesis can then specify a further dimension: the metaverse-stratum's threat is not only to *fūdo* (climatic-particular world-constitution) but also to *aidagara* (relational between-ness). Algorithmic mediation tends to disaggregate *aidagara* into platform-mediated, dyadic, individual-to-platform encounters that erase the between-many character of communal relational life. Hartmut Rosa's *resonance* concept and his social-acceleration analysis develop a closely parallel diagnosis from a different (Frankfurt-school) angle [VERIFY: pending-T0-retrieval | NLM-auth-restored-2026-05-05 | Rosa resonance/acceleration analysis in separate notebook UUID `a345bbc4`; cross-reference if §VI develops the aidagara-dissolution argument].

## 4. The 1927 Berlin Convergence

A specific historical fact deserves the thesis's attention. In 1927:

- **Heidegger** publishes *Sein und Zeit* in February
- **Watsuji** is studying philosophy in Berlin from May 1927 to summer 1928 on a sabbatical from Kyoto Imperial University; he reads *Sein und Zeit* almost immediately upon publication
- **Husserl** is preparing the lectures that will become *The Crisis of European Sciences* (the published 1936 *Crisis* derives from Husserl's 1935 Vienna and Prague lectures, but the underlying analytical line is being developed throughout the late 1920s and early 1930s)

Watsuji's response to *Sein und Zeit* — already developed during his Berlin sojourn and worked into book form as *Fūdo* — is that Heidegger's analysis of Dasein subordinates *spatiality* to *temporality* in a way that misses the constitutive role of climatic-spatial situatedness. The *fūdo* analysis is partly a German-language-philosophy intervention, conducted by a Japanese philosopher writing back into the European phenomenological tradition.

Husserl's *Crisis* analysis, developed in the same intellectual milieu, takes a different route to a structurally analogous diagnosis: the modern crisis is the forgetting of the *Lebenswelt* (lifeworld) — the pre-theoretical, pre-scientific, intersubjective world of immediate experience that scientific abstraction (the Galilean idealisation) presupposes and progressively replaces.

Both Watsuji and Husserl, in 1927–1937, are diagnosing the same fundamental phenomenon — the erasure of lived-experiential ground by formal-universalist abstraction — from different starting points and with different vocabularies. This convergence is the strategic citation-link the thesis can deploy: the diagnosis is not parochially Asian (Watsuji alone) and not parochially European (Husserl alone) but is registered by attentive philosophical observation from *both* sides of the European-Asian divide in the same decade.

This is what makes Husserl available as Option-β: not as a primary interlocutor in his own right within this thesis, but as a philosophically-informed-reader's recognition-link that strengthens the Watsuji argument by showing the diagnosis is robust across radically different philosophical methodologies.

## 5. Mapping Watsuji to Universe / World / Metaverse / Squeeze

| Thesis term | Watsuji correspondent | Operational specification |
|-------------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Universe** | The climatic-geographical-physical substrate that *fūdo*-analysis takes as its starting point | Not denied or replaced; presupposed. The climate as physical fact (Watsuji never denies that monsoons rain and deserts are dry) is the universe-stratum to which *fūdo* adds the phenomenological dimension. |
| **World** | *Fūdo* + *aidagara* — climate-as-self-objectivation-medium + in-betweenness as relational texture | The world-stratum's full lived character: climatically situated, relationally textured, irreducibly local. |
| **Metaverse** | The formal-abstract overlay that erases *fūdo*-particularity | Not directly named by Watsuji (he writes before the digital era), but his *fūdo*/non-*fūdo* contrast prefigures the diagnosis. The metaverse as algorithmic system is the most extreme form of formal-abstract universalism that erases climatic-relational particularity. |
| **Squeeze** | The displacement of climatically-situated experience by universalist mediation | The squeeze, in Watsujian terms, is the progressive erasure of *fūdo* as the medium of human self-objectivation: the human's self-understanding is no longer formed *through* climatic situatedness but *despite* it, with the metaverse-stratum supplying the new mediating field. |

The mapping has two important features. **First**, the universe-stratum is preserved as physical-climatic substrate; *fūdo* does not replace this stratum but adds the phenomenological dimension to it. **Second**, the squeeze is articulated as a *phenomenological-experiential* harm, not only as a structural-causal one. This articulation gives the thesis the empirical-experiential vocabulary for §IV's psychosomatic-symptom claims: what is being lost when humans report attentional fragmentation, resonance-loss, ambient anxiety is precisely the *fūdo*-grounded self-objectivation that the metaverse-stratum is replacing with its own mediating field.

## 6. Husserl's *Lebenswelt* and *Crisis* — The Contrast

Husserl's *Crisis* analysis can be summarised in five points relevant to this annotation [LOOKUP: Husserl 1970 *Crisis*, Northwestern UP — knowledge-base summary; verbatim Husserl quotations require Husserl in A-Sources]:

**(a) The pre-theoretical lifeworld is always already there.** Before any scientific or philosophical theorising, humans inhabit a world of immediate experience: a world of perceived objects, embodied actions, intersubjective relations, practical orientations. This lifeworld (*Lebenswelt*) is not "subjective" in any private-mental sense; it is the public, shared field of pre-theoretical experience that any actual human inhabits.

**(b) Scientific abstraction abstracts from the lifeworld.** Modern science, beginning with Galileo's mathematisation of nature, abstracts from the lifeworld: it replaces immediate-perceptual qualities (colour, warmth, sound) with mathematical idealisations (wavelength, temperature, frequency), and then treats the mathematical idealisations as more real than the lifeworld qualities they idealise.

**(c) The Galilean idealisation founds modernity.** This abstractive replacement is not a regional methodological move within science; it is the *founding gesture of modernity as a philosophical-cultural epoch*. The mathematisation of nature spreads from physics to all subsequent sciences and from the sciences to general culture as a privileged mode of access to reality.

**(d) The crisis is the forgetting of the lifeworld ground.** The "crisis" Husserl diagnoses is not that science has bad consequences (which it may also have); it is that science has *forgotten* the lifeworld ground that it presupposes. The lifeworld is always already the soil from which scientific abstraction grows; but science treats its abstractions as standing-on-their-own, as if the lifeworld were a primitive that science has surpassed.

**(e) Phenomenology is the return to the lifeworld.** Husserl's late phenomenology proposes itself as the disciplined return to the lifeworld — a methodologically rigorous recovery of the pre-theoretical ground that scientific modernity has occluded.

The diagnostic structure is precisely homologous with Watsuji's *fūdo* analysis:
- Watsuji: human self-objectivation has its medium in climatic-situated *fūdo*; modern universalism erases this medium.
- Husserl: human cognitive life has its ground in the pre-theoretical *Lebenswelt*; Galilean idealisation occludes this ground.

Both diagnose the erasure of a lived-experiential ground by formal-universalist abstraction. The convergence is the strategic citation-link.

## 7. Why Watsuji Is Primary, Husserl Is Option-β

The choice to deploy Watsuji as primary supplement and Husserl as one-sentence-footnote contrast is principled, not merely pragmatic. Five reasons:

**(a) Geo-cultural plurality is constitutive of Watsuji, gestural in Husserl.** Watsuji's *fūdo* analysis is *intrinsically pluralist*: the three climate-types are not exceptions to a single norm but the basic differentiation through which any *fūdo*-analysis must operate. Husserl's *Lebenswelt* is, in his own writings, treated as a single (though intersubjective) field; the pluralisation of lifeworlds becomes prominent only in subsequent work (Schutz, Berger & Luckmann, etc.). The thesis's argument requires plural-worlds-as-constitutive, which Watsuji supplies natively and Husserl does not.

**(b) Empirical-variation grounding.** The thesis must explain *why* the squeeze's empirical signatures vary across geographies (the hikikomori configuration is not the gig-precarity configuration is not the Social-Credit configuration — see ANNOT-L3 §5). Watsuji's three climate-types and the broader *fūdo*-pluralism gives the thesis a *direct* explanatory resource for empirical variation. Husserl's *Lebenswelt* analysis, taken alone, does not.

**(c) Asian standpoint as anti-essentialism guard.** Watsuji writes from a Japanese-philosophical position deploying the Greek-German phenomenological tradition critically. This is exactly the kind of cross-traditional methodological move the thesis's anti-essentialism requirement (D2, Ma) calls for. Husserl, while a great philosopher, writes from an unambiguously European standpoint and does not provide the same cross-traditional warrant.

**(d) Convergence with Hui's cosmotechnics-pluralism.** Watsuji's *fūdo*-pluralism and Hui's cosmotechnics-pluralism are structurally homologous; deploying both gives the thesis a *coherent pluralist phenomenology + philosophy-of-technology* couple. Husserl's universalist-transcendental framing does not couple as cleanly with Hui's pluralism.

**(e) Less philosophical-historical baggage.** Husserl's later *Crisis* work entered late-20th-century philosophy through specific reception channels (Merleau-Ponty, Schutz, the Frankfurt school, deconstruction) that have already accumulated thick interpretive layers. Watsuji is, for most committee readers, less encumbered by reception history; the thesis can deploy Watsuji directly without first navigating decades of secondary literature.

For these five reasons, the deployment is: **Watsuji as primary phenomenological supplement, developed in one paragraph in §III-4 §A3 (per existing supplement file `Ch-3-4_v2_suppl_Hui-Watsuji.md`); Husserl as Option-β footnote signalling the 1927-Berlin convergence, one sentence in a footnote, no separate development needed**.

## 8. Differentiator: Climatic-Spatial vs Transcendental-Temporal

A precise philosophical differentiation between Watsuji and Husserl, useful for any committee member who presses on the choice:

- **Watsuji**: situatedness is **climatic-spatial**. The medium of self-objectivation is the spatially extended, climatically articulated, geographically particular *fūdo*. Time enters the analysis through the seasonal cycles, the historical accumulation of climatic-cultural form, the intergenerational handing-on of *fūdo*-knowledge — but these are derivative, not foundational. Space is foundational; time articulates space.

- **Husserl**: situatedness is **transcendental-temporal**. The medium of cognitive life is the internal time-consciousness, the protentional-retentional structure of pre-thematic experience. The lifeworld is *temporally* constituted, with intersubjectivity as a constitutive feature. Spatial features enter the analysis as objects-of-time-consciousness — but space is derivative, not foundational. Time is foundational; space is a thematic articulation of time.

This contrast is decisive for the thesis. The squeeze hypothesis is a hypothesis about *real-level mechanism* operating across multiple *strata*. Strata are spatially differentiated (the universe-stratum is not the world-stratum is not the metaverse-stratum). The phenomenological supplement that fits this stratified-spatial structure is therefore the spatially-articulated one — Watsuji's *fūdo* — rather than the temporally-articulated one — Husserl's internal time-consciousness. The thesis architecture is structurally phenomenologically Watsujian, and Husserl is contributing a complementary analysis from a different (temporal) starting point.

## 10. The 1943 Revision Risk and Janz Commentary

The Watsuji deployment requires explicit methodological care because of the 1943 revision risk. The 1935 *A Climate* and the 1943 revised edition are not the same text; the 1943 revisions introduced nationalist-imperial inflections that are philosophically and morally compromising and which the thesis must not inherit.

The Janz commentary [T0-confirmed UUID `fce95d0b`, A-Sources] is the methodological resource for navigating this. Brian Janz's commentary establishes which passages belong to the original 1935 stratum, which belong to the 1943 revision, and which can therefore be cited safely from each. [VERIFY: pending-T0-retrieval | NLM-auth-restored-2026-05-05 | UUID fce95d0b (A-Sources) confirmed in prior sessions; targeted follow-up query needed to retrieve specific Janz passages identifying which *A Climate* sections belong to the 1935 stratum vs the 1943 revision — page-level precision needed before thesis deployment.]

For the thesis's purposes, the *fūdo* concept itself precedes the revision and survives it. The conceptual distinction *fūdo* / non-*fūdo*, the three climate-types analysis, the methodological move of climatic-philosophical anthropology — these are 1935-stratum and citable. What the thesis must not do:

- Cite Watsuji passages from chapters where Janz identifies specifically wartime-imperial revisions (typically the application chapters where the climatic typology is connected to specific civilisational evaluations)
- Treat the climatic typology as a hierarchy ranking (any reading of "monsoon receptivity" as inferior to "meadow rationality" or any reading of "Japan-as-special-fūdo" as nationally exceptional reproduces the 1943 distortion)
- Cite Watsuji's later wartime essays (which lie outside *A Climate* but contributed to a related body of nationalist work)

The methodological discipline is straightforward: **cite only the *fūdo* concept-formation passages; cite always alongside Janz's commentary; cite always within the typological-comparative framework, never as the special elevation of any one *fūdo*-type**.

This is the third anti-essentialism guard the thesis carries (the others being: the strand-genealogical reading of the Platonic-Aristotelian fault line in Ch-3-4 §A5; the strategic-primitivism methodological position of Hui-VdC in ANNOT-Hui §6).

## 12. Connecting Note: The Three-Stratum Phenomenological Picture

When Watsuji's *fūdo* analysis is integrated with Hui's cosmotechnics analysis (ANNOT-Hui) and Obara's 人工生態系 / 風土 analysis (ANNOT-Obara), the thesis arrives at a *three-stratum phenomenological picture* of the world that is unusually coherent:

| Stratum | Watsuji | Hui | Obara |
|---------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Lived character** | *Fūdo*-grounded self-objectivation | Cosmotechnical embeddedness | 人工生態系-adequacy to 内なる自然 |
| **Plurality** | Three climate-types and beyond | Cosmotechnical multiplicity | Multiple historical *fūdo* configurations (Obara uses the same term) |
