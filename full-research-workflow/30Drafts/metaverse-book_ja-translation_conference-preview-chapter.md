## Introduction + A

*EN:* Introduction

**JA:** 序論

*EN:* A. Isn’t the “Metaverse” already over?

**JA:** A. 「メタバース」はもう終わったのか？

*EN:* By the time these words are read, the word that names this thesis’s subject may sound like a closed case. In October 2021, the company, then called Facebook, renamed itself Meta and staked its future on the metaverse; for a season, the term was everywhere. Then, as most fads in history do, it gradually became quiet; and – ultimately – gone. At the time this thesis is being substantially expanded in 2026, Meta has already lost tens of billions (CNBC, 2026) in their “Metaverse,” and announced a shift in their focus into AI – publicly read as a resignation from their costly corporate misadventure. *EN:* My argumentative engine is retroduction — the inference, akin to but not identical with what the philosophy of science calls inference to the best explanation, by which one posits the generative structures that must exist for a phenomenon to be possible at all, rather than merely selecting the most plausible among already-given explanations.[fn4] I reason from the observable signs of our predicament — and in particular from the seemingly contradictory symptoms and diagnoses that the cataloged signs of human malaise present across Chapters III–V, where the evidence pulls in opposite directions at once — toward the underlying disposition that, were it real, would account for them. *EN:* My inquiry is necessarily transdisciplinary, for the squeeze falls within the borders of no established discipline. It draws, first, on a pair of Continental lineages. ## E

*EN:* E. Beneath governance, ownership, and liberty

**JA:** E. 統治、所有、そして自由の底流に

*EN:* The central claim can now be stated. As artificiality self-completes into a second background environment, the human comes to be situated between two background environments rather than within one — between the universe, the given it has always inhabited, and the metaverse, the second given its own making has produced — each running by organizing principles of its own, and each exacting a cost from those who fail to meet it. This is the latest emerging form of what I call a squeeze, a structural condition in which an organism bears survival pressure from at least two environments, with earlier forms squeezing diachronically and later forms synchronically. It is produced by two coupled tendencies that Chapters IV and V develop: a self-finishing tendency of human life, by which the modern subject entrusts ever more of living to artificiality in pursuit of a life that answers to its intention; and a self-completing tendency of artificiality, by which the apparatus so entrusted comes to operate, maintain, and renew itself without us. The two are the faces of one coin — the more thoroughly we delegate, the more the apparatus completes itself; the more it completes itself, the smaller our own part in the world becomes.

**JA:** ここで本論文の中心的な主張を述べる。人工性が自足完成化し、第二の背景環境となるにつれて、人間は単一の背景環境の中にいるのではなく、二つの背景環境の間に位置することになる。すなわち、常に居住してきた与件である宇宙と、自らの創造が産み出した第二の与件である偽境（メタバース）との間である。それぞれが独自の組織原理によって作動し、それに適応できない者には代償を求めるのである。これが、私が趨圧と呼ぶものの最新の出現形態であり、少なくとも二つの環境から生存圧力を受ける構造的状況を指す。その初期形態は通時的に、後期形態は共時的に趨圧を発生させてきた。それは、第IV章および第V章で展開する二つの結合した傾向によって生み出される。一つは、人間生活の自己完結化傾向であり、現代の主体がその意図に応える生を追求するために、生活のますます多くの部分を人工性に委ねるというものである。もう一つは、人工性の自足完成化傾向であり、その委ねられた装置が我々なしに自らを操作し、維持し、更新するようになるというものである。この二つは表裏一体である。すなわち、我々が委任すればするほど、装置は自足完成化し、装置が自足完成化すればするほど、世界における我々の役割は小さくなるのである。

*EN:* Put this way, the thesis locates artificiality’s threat at a level beneath the registers in which it is usually diagnosed. The predicament is read, by one influential tradition descending from Montesquieu, as a problem of governance — of an apparatus whose power outruns the institutions meant to check it; by a second, descending from Marx, as a problem of ownership — of an apparatus concentrated in private hands that extract from the rest; by a third, descending from Mill and Berlin, as a problem of liberty — of a freedom to be defended against encroachment; and, most prominently in our own moment, by the program of AI safety and existential risk — the alignment problem that runs from Norbert Wiener (1950) to Stuart Russell (2019), and the reckoning of catastrophic risk in Nick Bostrom (2014) and Toby Ord (2020) — as a problem of control, of an artificial agent whose purposes must be brought into line with ours before its powers grow decisive. Each names and addresses something actual; none, I will argue, reaches the level at which the squeeze obtains. The first three presuppose the subject whose governance, property, or freedom is in question, and ask how to secure it; the fourth tends to lodge the decisive threat in a future actualization[fn9] — often, though not always, imagined as the act of an artificial agent, and asks how to control it — lodging the threat thereby in a coming event rather than a present condition. But the squeeze is a condition of what the subject depends on — a rearrangement, beneath governance and ownership and liberty and control alike, of the very environments a human being relies on to live. This is the level at which the turn to dependence opens, and the level at which this thesis works.

**JA:** このように述べると、本論文は人工性の脅威を、通常それが診断される認識水準よりも深いレベルに位置づけるものである。この窮状は、モンテスキューに連なる有力な思想的伝統によれば、統治の問題、すなわちその権力が抑制機関を凌駕する装置の問題として解釈される。第二に、マルクスに連なる伝統によれば、所有の問題、すなわち残りの人々から収奪する私的な手に集中した装置の問題として。第三に、ミルやベルリンに連なる伝統によれば、自由の問題、すなわち侵犯から防衛されるべき自由の問題として。そして、我々の現代において最も顕著なのは、AI安全性と実存的リスクのプログラム — ノルベルト・ウィーナー（1950）からスチュアート・ラッセル（2019）に至るアラインメント問題、そしてニック・ボストロム（2014）とトビー・オード（2020）による破局的リスクの計算 — による、コントロールの問題としてである。すなわち、人工エージェントの目的がその力が決定的になる前に我々の目的と一致させられなければならないという問題としてである。それぞれが実際に存在する何かを特定し、対処しているが、趨圧が生じるレベルには到達していない、と私は主張する。最初の三つは、統治、財産、あるいは自由が問題となっている主体を前提とし、それをいかにして確保するかを問うものである。第四のものは、決定的な脅威を未来の現実化[fn9]に置く傾向がある — しばしば、常にではないが、人工エージェントの行為として想像され、それをいかにコントロールするかを問う — その結果、脅威を現在の状況ではなく、来たるべき出来事に置いているのである。しかし、趨圧とは、主体が依存するものの条件であり、統治、所有、自由、そしてコントロールといったもの全ての下層で、人間が生きていくために依拠するまさにその環境の再配置なのである。これが依存への転換が開かれるレベルであり、本論文が取り組むレベルである。

*EN:* The cases of liberty and alignment/control are the most telling, because they show why the threat is so easily mislaid — and because they prove, on inspection, to be two sides of one coin. One expects a threat to freedom to take the form of constraint — an authority forbidding what the subject would otherwise do. But the metaverse-stage subject is, in the formal sense, freer than ever: unconstrained in its choices, and equipped by the apparatus to realize them. Consider the recommendation feed: entrusting to it what to read, watch, and want, the subject is unconstrained — every choice met almost before it is formed — yet the feed aligns itself to a model of the subject and, in feeding that model, shapes the very wants it appears only to serve. Each selection is freely made; what quietly slips away is the capacity to want otherwise. What it loses is not freedom but the ground of freedom — the embodied competence, the lived relations, the capacity to meet what does not answer to its intention — and it loses these not against its will but in the very pursuit of a life that answers to its intention. The offloading by which it draws nearer to such a life is experienced, genuinely and not falsely, as liberation; the diminishment is the same act seen from its other side. Alignment is the same structure read from the apparatus’s side rather than the subject’s. The worry there is that the artificial agent’s purposes might diverge from ours; but the offloading that hands the agent ever more of our living is, again, willed and welcomed, and the danger does not only lie in the apparatus turning against the subject’s intention (which I acknowledge and do not argue against) but also in the extent the apparatus serves the intention so completely as to absorb the very capacities by which a subject could stand apart from it. Liberty and alignment thus name one rearrangement twice: the subject freely entrusting itself to an apparatus it asks to align with its will, and losing, in the entrusting, the standing-apart that both freedom and alignment presuppose. Freedom, then, is not the axis on which this threat is measured, and neither is control. And this is also why the threat requires none of the apparatus of science fiction — no artificial intelligence awakening to outwit or destroy us. That the apparatus is becoming a second environment we must adapt to, and that we are losing the means to adapt to the first, is a threat enough, and is underway already; the more dramatic scenario, and why even its strongest form does not hold, I reserve for the Conclusion.

## F

*EN:* F. The course of the argument

**JA:** F. 本稿の論旨

*EN:* The argument proceeds in six chapters. Chapter I develops the conception of a world as an adaptational operating system nested in the universe, grounding it in the niche-construction record across the tree of life and in the emergence of Homo — the animal that, meeting a mismatch between its biology and its habitat, built a world between the two and lived in that. That a world is, at root, something built to keep its makers from being squeezed will return at the very end as a question: whether a new world might be built now. Chapter II analyzes what worlds are made of — artificiality in its three aspects: the exosomatized organ — the exteriorization of human organs (Lotka, 1945) and memory (Stiegler, 1998); virtuality as causally efficacious nonmaterial artificiality; and the irreducibility of the laws of artificial environments to those of the universe. Chapter III asks why, of the many modes of artificiality, the Enlightenment mode came to predominate, tracing its disposition to universalize to both a material basis (Needham et al., 2004; Pomeranz, 2021) and an epistemic one (Bhaskar, 2008). Chapter IV turns to the human-side engine: through the work of the environmental philosopher and synthetic anthropologist Uegaki Takahide (上柿崇英, 2022a; 2022b), it examines the modern pursuit of a life-as-intended and the self-finishing of human life that follows from reading whatever does not answer to one’s intention as a wrong to be overcome. Chapter V turns to the apparatus-side engine: the self-completion of artificiality, made possible by tertiary protention – the ability for artificiality to anticipate and organize itself accordingly (Hui, 2019), minimally actualizable in the cybernetic coupling of artificial intelligence, big data, the Internet of Things, and code-as-law (Lessig, 2006), and the emergence from it of the metaverse as a second background environment. Chapter VI draws the two together as the evolution of the squeeze, distinguishing the metaverse-stage squeeze from its precursors and reading its present signs. The Conclusion returns to the threat, sets the thesis’s account apart from the governance, ownership, and liberty traditions and from the science-fiction scenario, and asks where, given the diagnosis, the openings lie — whether, in the end, a new world can be built between the universe and the metaverse.

**JA:** 議論は六つの章で展開される。第I章では、宇宙のなかにネストされた適応的オペレーティングシステムとしての世界の概念を展開する。これは、生命の樹全体にわたるニッチ構築の記録と、ホモ属の出現に根拠を置く。ホモ属とは、その生物学と生息地の間のミスマッチに遭遇し、その両者の間に世界を構築して生きた動物である。世界が、根本的には、その作り手たちが趨圧されるのを防ぐために構築されたものであるという事実は、本稿の最後に、「新たな世界が今構築されうるのか」という問いとして立ち戻る。第II章では、世界が何から構成されるのか、すなわち人工性を三つの側面から分析する。第一に、外体化された器官――人間の器官（Lotka, 1945）と記憶（Stiegler, 1998）の外在化。第二に、因果的に効果的な非物質的人工性としての仮想性。そして第三に、人工環境の法則が宇宙の法則に還元できないこと、である。第III章では、数ある人工性の依存様式のうち、なぜ啓蒙主義の様式が優勢になったのかを問い、その普遍化への性向を、物質的基盤（Needham et al., 2004; Pomeranz, 2021）と認識論的基盤（Bhaskar, 2008）の両方に遡って分析する。第IV章では、人間側の駆動機構に焦点を当てる。環境哲学者で総合人間学会会員の上柿崇英（2022a; 2022b）の研究を通じて、意図通りの生を追求する近代の試みと、意図に合わないものを乗り越えるべき誤謬と見なすことから生じる人間の生の自己完結（化）を考察する。第V章では、装置側の駆動機構に焦点を当てる。それは、第三次先行保持――人工性が自らを予測し、それに従って組織する能力（Hui, 2019）――によって可能となる人工性の自足完成（化）であり、人工知能、ビッグデータ、モノのインターネット、そしてコード＝法（code-as-law）（Lessig, 2006）のサイバネティックな結合において最小限に現実化され、それから第二の背景環境としての偽境（メタバース）が出現する。第VI章では、この二つを趨圧の進化として統合し、偽境（メタバース）段階の趨圧をその前駆体と区別し、その現在の兆候を読み解く。結論では、脅威に立ち戻り、本論の記述をガバナンス、所有、自由の諸伝統およびSFシナリオから区別し、この診断に基づいて、どこに突破口があるのか――最終的に、宇宙と偽境（メタバース）のあいだに新たな世界を構築できるのか――を問う。

*EN:* The contribution I claim is this: that beneath the familiar readings of our technological predicament lies a structural condition they do not name — the squeeze — and that seeing it requires a turn from asking how to “free” ourselves from the “chains we are born into” to asking how to attune ourselves to what we depend on. The chapters that follow make that turn.

**[5]** My own diagnosis of the Enlightenment condition is Uegakian — the universalizing elevation of the sovereign subject — not Heideggerian (enframing/Gestell). Stiegler (exosomization/organology) and Hui (cosmotechnics) have effectively deconstructed Heidegger’s universalizing account of technics that would otherwise diffuse the focus of this thesis.
> この第二の大陸系の系譜は、技術哲学とは区別されるものの交差する、近代の**批判的理論**である。ヴェーバー（1946）の脱魔術化（Entzauberung）と合理化、ホルクハイマーとアドルノの啓蒙の弁証法と道具的理性、ハーバーマスのシステムからの生活**世界**の植民地化、そしてローザの加速と**世界**の可制御性（Verfügbarkeit）への近代的な衝動などが挙げられる。本論文は、この伝統における近代に特徴的な性向についての診断を援用する一方で、そのいずれかの枠組みを支配的な存在論として採用することは、やはり差し控えている。

**[6]** This second Continental lineage is the critical theory of modernity, distinct from the philosophy of technology though intersecting it: Weber (1946) on disenchantment (Entzauberung) and rationalization; Horkheimer and Adorno on the dialectic of enlightenment and instrumental reason; Habermas on the colonization of the lifeworld by system imperatives; and Rosa on acceleration and the modern drive toward controllability (Verfügbarkeit) of the world. The thesis draws on this tradition’s diagnosis of modernity’s characteristic disposition while, again, declining to take any one of its frameworks as the governing ontology.
> 二つの橋は、それぞれ異なる働きをするため、分析的に区別される。ポストコロニアル批判（ミニョーロの脱植民地性および植民地権力マトリックス；デ・ソウザ・サントスの南の認識論および「多元宇宙（pluriverse）」；チャクラバルティのヨーロッパの地方化および近年の惑星化）は、近代批判を外部へと、つまり他の**世界**のあり方へと拡張する。これは、単一の啓蒙の**世界**が凌駕しようとする、私が「適応的オペレーティングシステム」と呼ぶものの多様性を指す。人類学における脱人間中心主義的な「存在論的転回」（デスコラ；ヴィヴェイロス・デ・カストロ）は、異なる動きをする。それは、自然／文化の区分を解消し、主権的な人間を特権的な項としての地位から引きずり下ろす。私はこの第二の橋を、本論文がすでに扱っている人類学、すなわち現代の自然／文化の区分を数ある宇宙論の一つに還元するデスコラの四つの存在論（第III章）、そして一つの自然と多くの文化という現代の定住様式を逆転させるヴィヴェイロス・デ・カストロの透視主義と多自然主義（第II章）から引き出しており、別個のポストヒューマニストの装置からではない。デスコラとヴィヴェイロス・デ・カストロは、哲学流派としての「ポストヒューマニズム」（ブラッドッティ、ウォルフ、ハラウェイ）ではないが、存在論的転回は本論文の証拠基盤内に留まりつつ、同様の脱中心化の働きをする。第二の橋を第一の橋に統合してしまうと、それぞれの貢献が失われる。すなわち、自然／文化の区分を解消することなく**世界**の多元性を脱地方化することもでき、また植民地主義的差異に対処することなくその区分を解消することも可能である。本論文には両方が、別々に必要とされるのである。

**[7]** The two bridges are kept analytically distinct because they do different work. The postcolonial critique (Mignolo’s decoloniality and the colonial matrix of power; de Sousa Santos’s epistemologies of the South and the “pluriverse”; Chakrabarty’s provincializing of Europe and, latterly, the planetary) carries the critique of modernity outward into the standing of other worlds — the multiplicity of what I call “adaptational operating systems” that a single Enlightenment world would override. The post-anthropocentric “ontological turn” in anthropology (Descola; Viveiros de Castro) makes a different move: it dissolves the nature/culture partition and unseats the sovereign human as the privileged term. I draw this second bridge from the anthropology the thesis already engages — Descola’s four ontologies, which reduce the modern nature/culture partition to one cosmology among several (ch. III), and Viveiros de Castro’s perspectivism and multinaturalism, which invert the modern settlement of one nature and many cultures (ch. II) — rather than from a separate posthumanist apparatus. Descola and Viveiros de Castro are not “posthumanism” in the philosophical-school sense (Braidotti, Wolfe, Haraway), but the ontological turn does the same decentering work while staying inside the thesis’s evidence base. Collapsing the second bridge into the first would lose what each contributes: one can de-provincialize the world’s plurality without dissolving the nature/culture partition, and one can dissolve that partition without addressing colonial difference. The thesis needs both, separately.

**[8]** Synthetic anthropology (総合人間学, sōgō ningengaku) is the program of the Japan Association of Synthetic Anthropology (総合人間学会, Sōgō Ningen Gakkai, founded 2006), which pursues the single question “what does it mean to be human?” against the fragmentation of the specialist sciences, holding that the contradictions of contemporary civilization “have their roots in the self-alienation of the human being.” (総合人間学会, 2006)
