## §1 — Real-level meta-law: 自足完成化 (Self-Completion of artificiality)

### §1.1 — The named real-level meta-law

[SCAFFOLD] State the disposition. Honor the [!REVISION] annotation on Ch-5-3 v2: at the real level, only one disposition. The five operational signatures (opacity, mutability, extraction, optimization, temporal acceleration) move to actual/empirical levels (§4 below).

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The real-level meta-law of the metaverse is the structural disposition of artificiality to become progressively self-sufficient — capable of operating, maintaining, and extending itself without continuous human direction. This disposition is what I name **Self-Completion 自足完成化**. It is the only real-level claim of this section; the various operational features that Ch-5-3 v1/v2 sometimes treated as parallel real-level claims (architectural opacity, designed mutability, extraction, optimization, temporal acceleration) are not themselves dispositional. They are *signatures* — actual-level patterns and empirical-level instances — through which the real-level disposition becomes observable.
>
> This stratification follows the critical-realist methodology developed in §I-6 and §III-6 (after Bhaskar): real-level claims specify causal powers that hold transfactually, whether or not they are exercised in any specific event; actual-level claims describe the events through which real-level powers operate; empirical-level claims describe the observed instances of those events. The single real-level claim of §V is 自足完成化; the rest of this section unpacks the architectural, actual, and empirical strata through which it operates.

### §1.2 — Bidirectional coupling with Uegaki's 自己完結化

[PROSE-TENTATIVE — preserve from viva-passed §V lines 599–609 verbatim where possible; expand on the bidirectional mechanism]
> 自足完成化 is bidirectionally coupled with Uegaki's "self-finishing 自己完結化" of human life (§IV). The two tendencies are two sides of one coin. As humans pursue life-as-willed by delegating their tasks and responsibilities to artificiality, the routine operation and maintenance of artificiality become structurally undesirable to keep performing — they hinder the actualization of the as-willed life — and the human-side tendency therefore drives artificiality toward becoming self-complete, capable of operating and maintaining itself on its own. On the flip side of the same coin, as humans increasingly entrust their tasks to artificiality, the "portion of functionality 役割" humans play in their world contracts — exacerbated, as I argued in §IV, by the dilution of relationality and the desomatization of life. This shrinking of the human portion structurally reinforces the growth of artificiality's portion. The coupling is bidirectional and self-reinforcing: each tendency, in actualizing, deepens the structural conditions for the other.
>
> The mechanism through which this bidirectional coupling becomes operational is specified at the architectural-mechanism stratum (§2 below): the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention is the technical-philosophical structure by which the human delegation of memory and anticipation enables the technical system to close its operational loop without continuous human noetic involvement.

### §1.3 — Convergent diagnosis from other lineages

[SCAFFOLD] Cite Hui's "organizing inorganic" as the closest existing analogue from a different lineage. Use this as a *convergence* claim that supports the real-level disposition's robustness, while marking the ontological divergence (§1.4 below). Reference the staging file `self-completion-vs-organizing-inorganic.md` for the full comparison.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The disposition I name 自足完成化 has been described, under different vocabularies and from different angles, by four converging traditions of analysis. **Obara (1995, 2000)** describes the bio-evolutionary self-development of the human-artificial niche, where the niche-builder becomes structurally absorbed into the niche it has built (§I); **Uegaki (2017, 2022)** describes the existential demand for life-as-willedness that drives delegation to the social apparatus (§IV); **Adorno, Horkheimer, and Marcuse** describe the politico-economic reification of instrumental rationality into a self-perpetuating administrative apparatus (§III); **Stiegler (1998, 2015)** describes the techno-historical grammatization through which the technical system progressively absorbs human noetic functions until it "tends to close in upon itself, so that it becomes a closed system" (Stiegler, *Automatic Society*) [VERIFY: Automatic Society p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"Fully automated informational fixed capital, moreover, tends to close in upon itself..."]. These four accounts are not in opposition; they are partial-perspective generative accounts of the same real-level disposition.
>
> In contemporary technology-philosophy, the closest structurally homologous diagnosis is **Yuk Hui's "organizing inorganic"** (*Recursivity and Contingency*, 2019). Hui distinguishes the "organized inorganic" of Stiegler's earlier work (technical objects that depend on human organization) from the "**organizing inorganic**" of the contemporary cybernetic age, where machines "are no longer simply tools or instruments but rather gigantic organisms in which we live" [VERIFY: Hui Recursivity and Contingency Introduction | nlm-excerpt:"What we are witnessing today is a shift from the organized inorganic to the organizing inorganic..."]. The convergence between Hui's diagnosis and the present thesis's 自足完成化 supports the real-level disposition's robustness: two distinct philosophical lineages — Hui via Stiegler-Simondon-Bergson; the present thesis via Obara-Uegaki-Bhaskar — arrive independently at the same threshold-claim.
>
> The thesis's contribution at the real-level is not to discover the disposition (it has been described before) but to (i) name it formally as 自足完成化 in coordinate parallel with Uegaki's 自己完結化, (ii) specify its bidirectional coupling with the human-side tendency, (iii) ground it in a stratified-real architecture that allows the disposition to be analyzed alongside its architectural mechanisms and actual-level instantiations, and (iv) specify (§3 below) the minimally sufficient technologies through which it becomes empirically actualizable.

### §1.4 — Ontological commitment: environmentalist-stratified, not organicist-fused

[SCAFFOLD] LOAD-BEARING. Per `self-completion-vs-organizing-inorganic.md` §5 — Hui's organizing-inorganic is a *superorganism that is also an environment* (fused). The thesis's framing is *background environment with laws of its own* (stratified). This distinction is what makes the squeeze hypothesis articulable.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Hui's framework and the present thesis differ at the ontological level despite the convergent threshold-claim. For Hui, the organizing inorganic is a *superorganism that is also an environment* — the contemporary cybernetic age is precisely when, in his words, "the apparatus and environment are becoming organismic" [VERIFY: Hui Recursivity and Contingency | nlm-excerpt:"The mode of participation of technology is fundamentally environmental while at the same time transforming the environment"]. Hui explicitly reassigns the older Schellingian/Lovelockian "general organism" term, formerly applied to nature, to the technological system; for Hui, the universe ceases to be the relevant gigantic organism in favor of the cybernetic system that has supplanted it [VERIFY: Hui R&C Introduction p.28 | nlm-excerpt:"In the time of Schelling..."].
>
> The present thesis declines this reassignment. The metaverse is analyzed as a **background environment with its own laws** — parallel to and incommensurable with the universe-as-environment — rather than as a Gaia-style superorganism. The stratified-real architecture (after Bhaskar) requires an environmentalist framing: the metaverse is a milieu humans adapt to, not an organism humans are inside of. The structural diagnosis of self-completion is shared with Hui; the ontological commitment about *what kind of thing* the self-completing thing is, is different.
>
> This ontological difference is not merely terminological. It is what makes the squeeze hypothesis (§VI) articulable. Hui's organicist-fused framework cannot generate the squeeze because the universe-pole has been reassigned to technics — leaving a single totalizing superorganism that humans live inside of. The thesis's environmentalist-stratified framework keeps both poles real: the universe remains a real-level background environment with its own transfactual laws, indifferent to data-generativity; the metaverse becomes a *new* real-level background environment with its own laws (its self-completing tendency). Humans are caught not inside a single superorganism but between two incommensurable real-level environments.
>
> The thesis's framing is therefore structurally stronger than Hui's for the specific argument the thesis advances. Hui's framework is more powerful for analyzing the technical system's internal logic (cosmotechnics, recursivity, the digital milieu); the present thesis's is more powerful for analyzing the human condition of being squeezed between two non-anthropocentric real-level domains.

### §1.5 — Methodological bracketing of the AI-consciousness question

[SCAFFOLD] Brief paragraph here; full treatment in §I-6 and Conclusion. Reference `consciousness-bracketing-prebrief.md`.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> One methodological clarification before proceeding. The 自足完成化 claim does not depend on, and does not commit to, any view of whether artificial intelligence — or the metaverse as a whole — is *phenomenally conscious*. Whether the metaverse "cares" about anything (including human flourishing) in any phenomenological sense is an open question in philosophy of mind that this thesis brackets, parallel to the way physics, ecology, and environmental philosophy bracket panpsychist or related claims about whether the universe is in some sense conscious. The bracket is methodological, not substantive: I do not deny that the consciousness question is meaningful, only that the thesis's structural-environmental claims do not depend on its resolution. The metaverse is treated as a background environment with structural laws of its own; those laws and their operational signatures are analyzable at this level of abstraction without committing to any view of the metaverse's phenomenal status. The bracket is justified by three converging routes: phenomenological epoché (Husserl), Bhaskar's transitive/intransitive distinction (already this thesis's methodology), and Floridi's level of abstraction. The full elaboration is at §I-6; the design corollary — how to make the metaverse-environment behave *as if* aligned with human flourishing regardless of its phenomenal status — appears in the Conclusion. ## §2 — Architectural-mechanism stratum: how the meta-law operates

[SCAFFOLD] This is the new section — not in viva-passed §V. It implements the stratified-real architecture from `transfactuality-test-lessig-hui-stratified-real.md`. The architectural-mechanism stratum holds REAL-level claims that are medium-independent (would hold under any technically-mediated environment, including future quantum or biocomputing) but specify HOW the meta-law operates structurally. Three architectural-mechanism claims belong here: (i) Lessig's architecture-as-regulation; (ii) Hui's productive-ontologies-via-associated-milieus; (iii) the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention. All three are subordinate to 自足完成化 at the meta-law stratum.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE — opening of §2]
> The real-level disposition of self-completion does not operate in a vacuum. It operates through structural mechanisms that hold for any technically-mediated environment, regardless of its specific medium. These mechanisms are themselves medium-independent — they would hold under quantum computing, biocomputing, or yet-undeveloped substrates — and so they belong, by Bhaskar's transfactuality test, at the real level. But they are subordinate to 自足完成化: they specify *how* the meta-law operates, not *whether* it operates. I treat them as constituting an architectural-mechanism stratum within the real level, and I describe three of them here.

### §2.1 — Architecture as a fourth modality of regulation (Lessig)

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The first architectural-mechanism claim is Lessig's: that **architecture itself is a regulator**, alongside law, social norms, and the market, and that architectural regulation operates by physical/structural constraint rather than by command. Lessig writes: "Thus, four constraints regulate this pathetic dot — the law, social norms, the market, and architecture — and the 'regulation' of this dot is the sum of these four constraints" [VERIFY: Lessig *Code 2.0* p.122 | citekey RCYT4KFH]. The constraint of architecture is medium-independent: it includes locked doors, town planning, the speed of light, gravity, and — at the digital scale — code. Lessig's foundational claim is that "regulation in cyberspace can help us see something important about how all regulation works" [VERIFY: Code 2.0 p.27]; the cyberspace analysis is meant to illuminate a general structural property of any architecturally-mediated environment.
>
> Crucially for the present thesis, architectural regulation operates whether or not the regulated subject is aware of it: "Architectural constraints, then, work whether or not the subject knows they are working" [VERIFY: Code 2.0 p.344]. This is a structural-real property of architectural mediation: the regulator does not need to be perceived to be effective. Once architecture is a sufficiently rich medium, regulation is built into the very conditions of action.
>
> When this structural-real claim is applied to the metaverse, it provides one of the architectural conditions of possibility for self-completion. If the metaverse is built on increasingly rich architectural affordances, then governance is increasingly an *architectural* affair rather than a propositional one — and the regulated humans need not understand, or even perceive, the rules that govern them.

### §2.2 — Productive ontologies via associated milieus (Hui)

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The second architectural-mechanism claim is Hui's: that the technical system does not merely regulate pre-existing beings; it **constitutes** new beings through productive ontologies operating in associated milieus. Hui writes: "Ontologies are what make digital objects objects rather than merely data. **Ontologies are productive, like Kant's categories**" [VERIFY: Hui *On the Existence of Digital Objects* p.34 | locate citekey]. And: "We can see that databases, algorithms, and network protocols become the associated milieux of digital objects" [VERIFY: Hui DO Ch.1].
>
> Hui's claim runs deeper than Lessig's. Where Lessig describes architecture *regulating* a pre-existing agent's behavior, Hui describes architecture *constituting* the digital being itself. The agent does not exist prior to the architectural milieu and then encounter regulatory affordances; rather, the agent emerges through the milieu's productive ontologies. Hui makes this medium-independent by explicit anti-silicon-reductionism: "Searching downward we may end up with the mediation of silicon and metal. And finally we could go into particles and fields. But this kind of reductionism doesn't tell us much about the world. … A new theory must therefore move away from the question of substance, and that for me is a theory of relations" [VERIFY: Hui DO p.10 / Metaphilosophy 2012].
>
> For the present thesis, Hui's claim specifies a deeper actual-level grammar than Lessig's. Where Lessig describes the regulatory affordances of architecture, Hui describes the constitutive structure of digital being itself. Both grammars hold across mediums; both are conditions of possibility for self-completion. When the metaverse becomes a sufficiently rich associated milieu — when its productive ontologies stabilize a sufficient number of relations — its inhabitants are no longer just regulated agents but constituted beings whose mode of existence depends on the milieu.

### §2.3 — The recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The third architectural-mechanism claim is the recursive coupling of **tertiary retention** (Stiegler) and **tertiary protention** (Hui's coordination of Stiegler) — and it is, I will argue (§3 below), the precise techno-philosophical mechanism by which the disposition of self-completion is actualized.
>
> Stiegler's intervention against Husserl's phenomenology is to add a third category to the analysis of memory. Where Husserl had distinguished primary retention (the just-passed within the now-perceived) and secondary retention (recollection), Stiegler argues that human consciousness is constituted by a third layer: **artificial, externalized, technically-recorded memory** — what he names tertiary retention. "Tertiary retention is in the most general sense the prosthesis of consciousness without which there could be no mind, no recall, no memory of a past that one has not personally lived, no culture" [VERIFY: Stiegler *Cinematic Time* / *Technics and Time, 2*]. Tertiary retention is, Stiegler argues, the same thing as "organized inorganic matter" — technical objects considered from the perspective of their mnemic function. Without artificial memory supports, there is no human temporality at all: "There is no already-there, and therefore no relation to time, without artificial memory supports" [VERIFY: Stiegler *Technics and Time, 1* p.159].
>
> Hui's intervention against Stiegler is to argue that Stiegler's framework, while extensively developing tertiary retention, leaves a structural lacuna at the side of *anticipation*. Husserl had distinguished primary protention (immediate anticipation) and secondary protention (longer-term internal anticipation), but Stiegler did not formalize a third category for **artificial, externalized, technically-stabilized anticipation**. Stiegler in *Automatic Society* uses the phrase "tertiary protention" but, as Hui correctly observes, treats it as a derivative effect of tertiary retention rather than as a distinct concept. Hui writes: "The lack of discussion of tertiary protention in Stiegler's work seems very paradoxical… this silence on tertiary protention seems to suggest that tertiary protention is reducible to tertiary retention, which would be contradictory" [VERIFY: Hui *Recursivity and Contingency* §36]. Hui's positive contribution is to develop tertiary protention as a distinct philosophical category, parallel to and irreducible to tertiary retention: the technically-stabilized anticipation that contemporary algorithmic systems instantiate.
>
> The decisive structural claim, for the present thesis, is Hui's: "**This missing element — tertiary protention — is crucial to understanding the evolution of technical systems, meaning their becoming organic**" [VERIFY: Hui R&C §36]. Without tertiary protention, the inorganic merely stores; with the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention, the inorganic begins to organize. Hui describes this coupling as the basis of the cybernetic circuit: "Smart objects take part in the milieu by playing the role of organs of motor-perception (converting approximate sense perceptions to signals), organs of analysis (based on calculation and existing data), and **organs of synthesis (constitution of the tertiary protention)**" [VERIFY: Hui R&C §37]. And: "The preemption of the tertiary protention is possible only because of the computational hermeneutics, which is essentially recursive: It constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present. **Human beings are reintegrated into the temporality of machines**" [VERIFY: Hui R&C §39].
>
> The recursive retention/protention coupling is medium-independent: it would hold under any technical substrate where past-capture and future-anticipation can be exteriorized and recursively coupled. It is therefore at the architectural-mechanism real stratum, alongside Lessig's architectural-regulation and Hui's productive-ontologies. But unlike those two, it is not a static property of any architectural medium — it is a *dynamic* mechanism. It specifies the structural process by which the technical system becomes self-organizing.

### §2.4 — How the three architectural-mechanism claims work together

[SCAFFOLD] Brief synthesis paragraph — the three claims are not in competition; they describe different aspects of the architectural-mechanism stratum.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The three architectural-mechanism claims describe complementary aspects of the same stratum. **Lessig** describes how architectural mediation regulates pre-existing agent behavior. **Hui's productive-ontologies** describes how architectural mediation constitutes new digital beings. **The recursive retention/protention coupling** describes how architectural mediation, when sufficiently rich, becomes self-organizing through the coupling of past-capture and future-anticipation. The first two are static structural properties of architecturally-mediated environments; the third is the dynamic process through which such environments become self-completing. Together, they specify the medium-independent conditions of possibility for the disposition of 自足完成化 to actualize.

## §3 — Actual level: the four minimally sufficient technologies

[SCAFFOLD] This is the viva-passed §V.A material, deepened. The four technologies (AI / big data / IoT / code-is-law) get their philosophical roles specified via the retention/protention mapping. Reference `tertiary-retention-protention-deep-dive.md` §5 for the detailed mapping.

### §3.1 — The four technologies

[PROSE-TENTATIVE — preserve viva-passed §V.A four-technology specification verbatim where possible]
> Despite artificiality being yet to be fully self-complete, the minimally sufficient technologies for such self-completion are already emerging. These technologies at least include:
>
> a) **artificial intelligence (AI)** — an artificially created system capable of making decisions on its own;
>
> b) **big data** — the aggregate of exponentially growing information collected from almost every aspect of every individual's day-to-day life;
>
> c) **internet of things (IoT)** — a network of everyday objects that enables data collection, communication, and operation by and throughout the network; and
>
> d) **code is law** (Lessig, 2006) — codes that function as laws without the need for third-party jurisdictions, unstoppable once executed by pre-programmed algorithms.

### §3.2 — Why these four are jointly sufficient: the retention/protention mapping

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The choice of these four technologies is not arbitrary. Each maps onto a distinct organ within the recursive retention/protention coupling specified at the architectural-mechanism stratum (§2.3 above). Following Hui's organology of smart objects:
>
> - **IoT** instantiates the *organs of motor-perception*: sensors capture sensory flow into technical traces. This is the input side of tertiary retention.
> - **Big data** instantiates the *organs of analysis*: the captured past is held as a computational substrate and organized for retrieval. This is the storage side of tertiary retention.
> - **AI** instantiates the *organs of synthesis*: the computational substrate is operated upon to anticipate, predict, recommend. This is the constitution of tertiary protention proper.
> - **Code-as-law** instantiates the *crystallization of protention into pre-decision*: the anticipated future is locked into algorithmic execution. This is what Hui (after Rouvroy) calls **preemption**.
>
> The four technologies are jointly sufficient because they jointly close the recursive loop. IoT and big data exteriorize the past (retention); AI and code-as-law exteriorize the future (protention); the loop closes when AI's predictions become the IoT's next-cycle input via code-as-law's enforcement, the resulting actions are captured back into the retention substrate, and the prediction updates. The technical system can then run its cycle without continuous human noetic involvement: the human is captured by IoT/big data, anticipated by AI, regulated by code, and the cycle closes.
>
> This is the techno-philosophical mechanism that the viva-passed §V did not yet specify. The viva-passed listed the four technologies and described the cybernetic system that synthesizes them; the present version specifies *why* this set is minimally sufficient — because they jointly instantiate the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention at the actual level.

### §3.3 — Bhaskar's autonomized philosophy / autonomized sense-experience synthesis

[SCAFFOLD] Preserve viva-passed §V's Bhaskar passage — the synthesis of the historically antagonistic Platonic-Aristotelian fault line as actualized in the cybernetic system. Cross-reference §III where this is established.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE — preserve from viva-passed §V lines 621–633]
> In Bhaskar's language (§III), big data is becoming the new autonomized sense-experience that feeds AI as the new autonomized philosophy. This synthesis of the historically antagonistic yet dialectically interdependent Platonic-Aristotelian fault line emerges as a reified version of the Hegelian Spirit or the Nietzschean Will — an all-encompassing whole that subsumes all experiences and dynamically directs collective progress. For such dynamic directing to occur, this directing whole needs the means of control over materials and social behaviors. When connected to autonomized big data and AI, the IoT can provide the material infrastructure for the former to control anything connected to it. On the other hand, code-as-law can serve as the virtual shaping and propelling force under the command of the autonomized dual.

### §3.4 — Why the contemporary moment is structurally distinctive

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The contemporary moment is structurally distinctive because it is the first moment when *all four* technologies have reached operational sufficiency simultaneously. Earlier epochs had retention substrates without protentional synthesis (writing without prediction-engines); or had local protention without retention scale (small-group anticipation without industrial data); or had analog retention with statistical protention (twentieth-century media + actuarial calculation) without the recursive feedback that would close the loop. With AI's protentional sufficiency at planetary scale, coupled to IoT-mediated retention and code-as-law preemption, the loop can now close in ways it could not before. This is the threshold the thesis identifies: **self-completion becomes empirically actualizable when AI achieves protentional sufficiency at scale alongside the other three technologies**.

### §4.2 — Hui-side signatures: ontological constitution in operation

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Two of the operational signatures are best understood as instantiations of the Hui-side ontological-constitution grammar:
>
> **(d) Extraction.** The actual-level pattern of behavioral data extraction is not just regulatory — it is constitutive. Each captured behavioral trace becomes part of the productive ontology that constitutes the digital being whose action it was. The user is not first a self that then has data extracted from it; the user is constituted as a "user" precisely through the extraction process that produces the dividuated data-profile.
