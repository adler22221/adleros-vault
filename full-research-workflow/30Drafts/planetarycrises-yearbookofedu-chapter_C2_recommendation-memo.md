## §1. Recommended Angle (revised)

**Recommended angle: "From Allocation Dependence to Learning by Caring: A Modes-of-Dependencies Framework for Education as Sustainable Development under Planetary Crisis."**

The chapter advances the user's **five-mode dependency framework** (天地依存 → 配分依存 → 貨幣依存 → 人工性依存 → 制御性依存) as a structural-causal account of why education systems remain locked in productivist trajectories even when actors recognize planetary stakes. Within this frame, *allocation dependence* (配分依存) is the dominant educational pathology — the mechanism by which institutions and learners trim themselves to fit externally imposed metrics of validation. *Learning by Caring* (德中學) is then introduced as the agent-based counter-pathway, grounded in 德 — an ancient East Asian concept whose pre-moralized sense (inner power, generative virtue) provides a *cross-traditional common ground* spanning Confucian, Daoist, and Buddhist thought, used **as theory**, not as case study. *Education AS Sustainable Development* names the institutional horizon — academia that lives as the system it seeks to create.

**Section fit: CFP §3 (Education policy pathways for planetary futures)**

The CFP's §3 language maps directly: "forward-looking approaches… more just, participatory, and **regenerative** education systems… **policy imaginaries** emerging from Indigenous leadership, youth participation, and community-based movements… beyond incremental reform… deeper **structural and systemic change**." The user's "value regeneration vs. value redemption" axis is verbatim in the section description; "modes of dependencies" provides the structural-and-systemic register; Learn-by-Caring + Awakening Cooperative Lab supply the community-based policy imaginary.

**Why this beats §1**: §1 is a critique of *existing* policy architectures (UNESCO GEP, OECD, SDGs). The chapter has diagnostic content but is fundamentally a **counter-pathway** — the prescriptive weight is on Learn by Caring + EaSD. §3 honors that center of gravity. The 1.5%-of-climate-finance hook still appears in §3 as the framing that motivates the pathway proposal — diagnosis can serve a pathways chapter without belonging to a diagnostic section.

**Editor hits**:
- **Silova**: anti-SDG-4 alignment (Silova, Rappleye & Komatsu 2025); East Asian theoretical voice as theory (the standout field gap); modes-of-dependencies provides the *postsocialist* + *decolonial* + *more-than-human* triangulation she champions (cosmic-terrestrial mode delivers the more-than-human; allocation/money modes deliver the postsocialist; the East Asian grounding delivers the decolonial)
- **McKenzie**: governance/allocation assemblage frame (not curriculum); 1.5% climate-finance hook as policy-architecture evidence; Nxumalo co-cite

**Risks and mitigation**:
- *Risk 1: Five-mode framework is dense for 400w.* Mitigation: name the framework in one sentence, then immediately specialize to allocation dependence as the educational pathology; the other modes are alluded to as the structural context, not enumerated.
- *Risk 2: East Asian framing read as decorative.* Mitigation: anchor the dependency-modes diagnostic in the Daoist 有所待 (Zhuangzi *Qiwulun* — boundlessness of dependency chains) and Buddhist 十二因縁 (twelve links of dependent origination), both of which appear as theoretical bridges in the user's March 2026 dependency-modes paper. Anchor LbC in the pre-moralized sense of 德 — a Confucian/Daoist/Buddhist common-ground concept that grounds relational pedagogy across traditions without collapsing into any single school.
- *Risk 3: Multiple concepts compressed into 400w.* Mitigation: structure as diagnosis → counter-pathway → horizon, with one concept per paragraph; treat the five-mode framework as the lens and allocation dependence + learn by caring + EaSD as the three named contributions.

## §2. One-Sentence Thesis (revised)

Planetary crises persist in and through education because modern educational systems are constituted by a misalignment of dependency modes — chiefly **allocation dependence**, which forecloses regenerative pedagogical capacities by binding learners and institutions to externally imposed metrics of validation — and the structural counter-pathway is an agent-based educational practice the author calls **Learning by Caring** (德中學), grounded in the pre-moralized sense of 德 as a cross-traditional East Asian relational ontology, enacted through **Education AS Sustainable Development**: institutions that live as the regenerative systems they seek to cultivate.

## §3. 400-Word Abstract Outline (revised)

Five paragraphs, ~80w each.

**¶1 — Hook + framing (≈75w)**
Open with the 1.5%-of-global-climate-finance figure and COP28 Loss & Damage absence. Frame: not a funding oversight but a structural symptom. Cite Silova, Rappleye & Komatsu (2025) to align with editors' anti-productivist diagnosis. Position the chapter in §3: a pathway proposal that begins from a diagnostic that the field has not yet named structurally. Suggested opening phrase: *"Education receives less than 1.5% of global climate finance and was absent from COP28's Loss and Damage Fund — a misallocation that this chapter argues is symptomatic of a deeper structural condition."*

**¶2 — Diagnostic framework: modes of dependencies + allocation dependence (≈85w)**
Introduce the five-mode dependency framework — articulated through dialogue with Daoist 有所待 (Zhuangzi *Qiwulun*) and Buddhist 十二因縁 (dependent origination) as the philosophical antecedents of what the author terms the "dependency turn" — as the macro-architecture. Specialize to **allocation dependence** (Yang 2021, 2023) as the dominant educational pathology: the *transfactual, fractal* mechanism by which learners and institutions trim themselves to fit externally imposed metrics, foreclosing value regeneration in favor of value redemption. Cite Pierson (2000) on path dependence and Steiner-Khamsi (2012, WYoE) on policy borrowing as governance-architecture warrants; bridge to climate-coloniality (Sultana) and Andreotti, Stein & Ahenakew (2022).

**¶3 — Counter-pathway: Learning by Caring (≈90w)**
Introduce **Learning by Caring** (德中學) as an agent-based generative mechanism — a regenerative relational cycle in which the learner's caring act becomes the organizing principle of pedagogy, resisting both AI capture and neoliberal capture because the caring act itself cannot be fully metricized or automated. Ground the framework in the pre-moralized sense of 德 (de) — an ancient East Asian concept whose meaning as inner power and generative virtue (distinct from later moralized 道德) provides a *cross-traditional common ground* spanning Confucian, Daoist, and Buddhist thought, making LbC compatible with all three rather than reducible to any one. Bridge to Tronto (2013) for the care → political-economy linkage and to Common Worlds Collective (Nxumalo & McKenzie 2023) for the relational-pedagogy register the editors recognize.

**¶4 — Methodology + empirical anchor (≈80w)**
Name the method: **retroductive critical-realist case analysis with autoethnographic and practitioner-inquiry components**. Two empirical anchors: (a) a seven-year critical autoethnography of an alternative high school in Taiwan grounding the allocation dependence mechanism (Yang 2023, IACR); (b) practitioner-action-research at the Awakening Cooperative Lab tracking the emergence of Learning by Caring across 16 youth, 7 from disadvantaged backgrounds. The methodology does not test a prior intervention but retroduces the generative mechanisms operative in lived educational practice.

**¶5 — Institutional horizon + contribution (≈70w)**
Position **Education AS Sustainable Development** (Yang 2022/23, KAS) as the institutional horizon that Learning by Caring requires — academia not merely teaching about sustainability but living as the regenerative system it cultivates. Close with the contribution: a structural-causal account of educational climate inaction that gives the field's anti-productivist critique a transfactual mechanism, a regenerative counter-pathway, and a non-Western theoretical voice currently absent from the planetary-education conversation.

## §10. Methodology Note (for the full chapter, not for the abstract)

For the chapter's methodology section (post-acceptance), the recommended framing is:

> **"Retroductive critical-realist case analysis with critical-autoethnographic and practitioner-inquiry components."**

The logic: Bhaskar's Critical Realism explicitly authorizes **retroduction** — the abductive movement from observed phenomena back to the generative mechanisms that could have produced them. Learning by Caring was not a designed intervention you tested; it is a generative mechanism you *identified retroductively* from emergent practice. This is the standard CR move and requires no prior experimental design. Two empirical bases:

- **(a) Critical autoethnography (Yang 2023 IACR)**: a seven-year ethnographic study of your own alternative-school cohort under Taiwan's standardized high school exam pressure, grounding the abductive identification of allocation dependence.
- **(b) Practitioner action research (Awakening Cooperative Lab, n=16)**: documentation of how Learning by Caring emerged and was iteratively refined in lived mentoring practice over multiple years.

Citable methodological anchors:
- **Bhaskar (1975, 2008)** — retroduction as legitimate scientific inference
- **Sayer (2000) *Realism and Social Science*** — CR case study methodology
- **Danermark, Ekström, Jakobsen & Karlsson (2002) *Explaining Society***— CR methodology textbook
- **Cochran-Smith & Lytle (2009) *Inquiry as Stance*** — practitioner inquiry as rigorous methodology without pre-design
- **Adams, Holman Jones & Ellis (2015) *Autoethnography*** (Oxford) — autoethnography as method
- **Reason & Bradbury (eds., 2008) *SAGE Handbook of Action Research*** — emergent action research

For the **abstract** (400w): naming the method in 4-6 words is sufficient. Suggested: *"retroductive critical-realist case analysis with autoethnographic and practitioner-inquiry components."*

## §13. The PhD Question and the Wild-Card Strategy

**Direct answer to your question**: No, don't ask. Here's the reasoning.

**Why asking is a weak move:**
1. The CFP nowhere mentions a PhD requirement. WYoE has historically included policy practitioners and non-traditional contributors. Asking "is a PhD required?" invites the editors to consider whether they should impose one — when they probably had no intention of doing so.
2. Asking frames you as outside the gates needing permission to enter. The editors will read this as either anxious or as a soft pitch. Either way, they will form an impression of your status *before* they read your abstract.
3. The CFP's "Author role(s)/expertise relative to the Yearbook focus" field is the designed slot for non-traditional credentials. Use that slot, don't pre-empt it with a question.

**Why the wild-card play is a strong move:**
1. **The CFP's own §3 invites you specifically**: "policy imaginaries emerging from… youth participation, and community-based movements"; "Indigenous, youth and community contributors." McKenzie and Silova built this section expecting exactly your kind of contributor.
2. **Your credentials are genuinely strong**: IACR Council Member (the field's flagship CR association), JASA Board Member (peer-reviewer, journal-listing decision-maker), TIAL Inaugural Fellow alongside contributors from UNDP, Demos Helsinki, Berggruen Institute. These are scarce, externally-verifiable positions.
3. **Your trajectory IS the thesis**: the chapter argues that allocation dependence forecloses regenerative capacities and that Learning by Caring enables a different pathway. Your own pathway — unschooled, autodidactic, prosocial-action-driven, with peer-reviewed publication and policy impact emerging from caring engagement — is the *living empirical case* for the argument. This is rhetorically powerful when foregrounded.
4. **The documentary is rare cultural capital**: *If There is a Reason to Study* with 10+ international festival recognitions and direct contribution to BCT discontinuation is unusual in the WYoE contributor pool and signals practical impact.

**How to deploy the wild card** — in three places, with calibration:

- **Email signature block (§12)**: roles only. Lets credentials speak.
- **Author role/expertise statement (§8, 60-90w)**: foreground the embodied-thesis framing. This is where the wild-card play happens explicitly.
- **150w bio**: standard scholarly bio voice; lead with the Lab/IRAEEH, then the governance roles, then publications. Don't explain the pathway in the bio — the expertise statement does that work. Bio just establishes you're a working scholar with publications and institutional standing.

**What NOT to do**: don't apologize for or explain the lack of PhD anywhere in the submission. If the editors notice, your credentials and the embodied-thesis framing already answer the question. If they don't notice (likely, given how the submission is structured), no need to draw attention.

**One caveat**: if you accept and proceed to the full chapter, the methodology section should be rigorous on the retroductive-CR framing (see §10). The full chapter is where the methodology stands or falls — the proposal stage is purely about whether your concept and positioning convince the editors.
