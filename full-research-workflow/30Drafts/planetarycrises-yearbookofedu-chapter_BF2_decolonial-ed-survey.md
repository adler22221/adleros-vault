# Decolonial, Indigenous, and Global South Educational Philosophy and Planetary Crises — Literature Brief

Survey of scholarship most likely to resonate with the *World Yearbook of Education* Vol 29 co-editors Marcia McKenzie (climate-education policy; affect, place, geographies of education) and Iveta Silova (postsocialist/postcolonial/decolonial comparative education; more-than-human and "beyond the human" pedagogy). Focus is on lineages, anchor texts, transferable concepts, and live tensions — not a complete bibliography.

## 1. Decolonial Latin American — Modernity/Coloniality and Buen Vivir

Tracks the Mignolo/Quijano/Escobar critique of *coloniality of knowledge* through Andean *Sumak Kawsay* / *Buen Vivir* into pedagogy and ecological ethics.

- **Anchor authors / works:**
  - Walter Mignolo, *The Darker Side of Western Modernity* (2011); Aníbal Quijano, "Coloniality of Power, Eurocentrism, and Latin America" (2000) — backbone of the modernity/coloniality matrix.
  - Catherine Walsh, *Interculturalidad, Estado, Sociedad* (2009); the Walsh strand explicitly carries decolonial thought into pedagogy via *interculturalidad crítica*.
  - Eduardo Gudynas on Buen Vivir as post-developmental ethic; recent applications include UNAE (Ecuador) collaborative-learning pedagogy and the *Sustainability* (MDPI 2025) and *Environmental Communication* (2024) treatments of Buen Vivir as ecomedia and care pedagogy.
- **Travelling concepts:** coloniality of knowledge/being; *interculturalidad crítica*; *Sumak Kawsay* as relational well-being beyond GDP; rights of nature (Ecuador 2008 constitution).
- **Method signature:** essayistic-philosophical; manifesto-adjacent; often co-authored with Indigenous intellectuals (e.g., Walsh–Mignolo–Linera).

## 2. Indigenous Anglo-Settler-Colonial Scholarship (Australia, Aotearoa, Canada, US)

The lineage most directly oriented to *land-based* and *kin-based* pedagogies — and the one Silova's "beyond the human" agenda draws from most heavily.

- **Anchor authors / works:**
  - Tyson Yunkaporta (Apalech, Deakin IKS Lab), *Sand Talk: How Indigenous Thinking Can Save the World* (2019) — yarning, sand-drawing, pattern thinking as pedagogy.
  - Linda Tuhiwai Smith, *Decolonizing Methodologies* (1999/2021 3rd ed.) — still the methodological hinge.
  - Glen Sean Coulthard, *Red Skin, White Masks* (2014); Leanne Betasamosake Simpson, *As We Have Always Done* (2017) — *grounded normativity*, resurgence-as-pedagogy.
  - Bawaka Country et al. (Yolŋu and non-Indigenous co-authors incl. Sarah Wright, Kate Lloyd), "Co-becoming Bawaka" (*Progress in Human Geography* 2016) — *Country* as co-author; widely cited in childhood/more-than-human work.
  - Robin Wall Kimmerer, *Braiding Sweetgrass* (2013) — entry text bridging Indigenous science and Western education audiences.
- **Travelling concepts:** *Country* as agent and teacher; grounded normativity; kincentric ecology; *yarning* and storywork (Jo-ann Archibald); land as first teacher.
- **Method signature:** storywork; co-authorship with Country/non-human entities; refusal of extractive citation; place-based and ceremonial.

## 3. Common Worlds / More-than-Human Childhood Studies (Anglo academy with strong Indigenous interlocutors)

Bridge lineage — academically Anglo/posthumanist but constitutively in dialogue with #2. Likely closest to Silova's own positioning.

- **Anchor authors / works:**
  - Affrica Taylor, *Reconfiguring the Natures of Childhood* (2013); Taylor & Pacini-Ketchabaw, *The Common Worlds of Children and Animals* (2019).
  - Karen Malone, *Children in the Anthropocene* (2018); editor of the *Research Handbook on Childhoodnature* (2020, with Cutter-Mackenzie-Knowles & Barratt Hacking).
  - Fikile Nxumalo, *Decolonizing Place in Early Childhood Education* (2019) — explicit Black/Indigenous decolonial framing inside the common-worlds family; **co-author with McKenzie on "Climate change and educational research: Mapping resistances and futurities" (*Policy Futures in Education*, 2023)** — direct McKenzie cross-reference.
  - Taylor & Pacini-Ketchabaw, "Beyond stewardship: common world pedagogies for the Anthropocene" (*Environmental Education Research*, 2015).
- **Travelling concepts:** *common worlds*; multispecies / intra-active pedagogy; *unsettling* settler-colonial early childhood; child–place–species entanglement; *staying with the trouble* (Haraway lineage).
- **Method signature:** ethnographic-affective; walking, noticing, multispecies field notes; deliberately small-scale and slow.

## 4. African Philosophical Traditions — Ubuntu, and the Wynter Hinge

Two distinct sub-lineages often run in parallel rather than together.

- **Ubuntu / Afro-communitarian ecology:**
  - Mogobe Ramose, *African Philosophy through Ubuntu* (1999/2002).
  - Kyei-Nuamah & Peng, "Ubuntu philosophy for ecological education and environmental policy formulation" (*Journal of Philosophy of Education*, 2024) — recent flagship for the ecological turn.
  - *Ubuntugogy* literature in nursing/planetary-health education (2025 *Nurse Education in Practice* piece) shows the concept being operationalised for professional curricula.
- **Sylvia Wynter (Caribbean / Black radical):**
  - "Unsettling the Coloniality of Being/Power/Truth/Freedom" (*CR: New Centennial Review*, 2003) — foundational for current decolonial educational theory.
  - Andersson, "Sylvia Wynter's Decolonial Philosophy" (*Educational Theory*, 2024) — recent uptake explicitly for curriculum.
- **Travelling concepts:** *I am because we are*; Afro-eco-communitarianism; Wynter's *genres of the human* / overrepresented Man; reparative origin stories for curriculum.
- **Method signature:** Ubuntu strand: communitarian-ethical, policy-facing. Wynter strand: dense theoretical-historical synthesis, baroque prose.

## 5. Decolonial-Futures Pedagogy and Post-Activism (boundary lineage)

Hybrid stream that is *citationally* central in current sustainability/climate-education debates and explicitly addresses "the end of the world as we know it" — which maps almost verbatim onto the *Planetary Crises* yearbook framing.

- **Anchor authors / works:**
  - Vanessa Andreotti, Sharon Stein, Cash Ahenakew, Rene Suša, Tereza Čajková, "From 'education for sustainable development' to 'education for the end of the world as we know it'" (*Educational Philosophy and Theory*, 2022).
  - Andreotti, *Hospicing Modernity* (2021); the Gesturing Towards Decolonial Futures (GTDF) collective's social cartographies (*HEADS UP*, *bus*, *house modernity built*).
  - Báyò Akómoláfé, *These Wilds Beyond Our Fences* (2017); post-activism essays via The Emergence Network.
- **Travelling concepts:** *hospicing modernity*; preparing to be good ancestors; "the times are urgent, let us slow down"; *post-activism*; depth-education / stamina-building rather than solutionism.
- **Method signature:** mixed-genre — diagrammatic social cartographies, parable, autobiographical essay, trickster mode (Akómoláfé). Deliberately anti-prescriptive.

## Concepts That Travel Well into "Education and Planetary Crises"

- **Kincentric / relational ontology** (Indigenous Anglo + Latin American + Ubuntu) — provides a non-stewardship grammar that escapes "environment-as-object."
- **Country / land as teacher and co-author** (Bawaka, Yunkaporta, Simpson) — operationalisable in curriculum design without collapsing into metaphor.
- **Slow / depth / hospicing pedagogy** (Andreotti, Akómoláfé, common-worlds) — directly responds to the climate-solutionism critique McKenzie's MECCE work documents.
- **Climate coloniality** (Sumida Huaman & Stein 2025; Sultana) — names the asymmetry between who produces climate knowledge/policy and who bears climate harm; bridges policy (McKenzie) and decolonial theory (Silova).
- **More-than-human / common-worlds pedagogy** — Silova's "beyond the human" vocabulary; well-developed in early-childhood literature, less so in secondary/HE.
- **Genres of the human / reparative origin stories** (Wynter) — provides the meta-curricular argument: which "human" is climate education even for?

## Debates and Tensions

- **Decolonial-as-academic-fashion.** Tuck & Yang's "Decolonization is not a metaphor" (*Decolonization*, 2012) is the standing rebuke; the more recent worry (Stein, Ahenakew) is that "decolonising the curriculum" has become an inclusion device that leaves modernity/coloniality's material structures untouched.
- **Indigenous critique of non-Indigenous uptake.** Especially of common-worlds and posthumanist appropriation of Indigenous relational ontology without ceremonial, land, or accountability obligations (Watts on *Place-Thought*; Todd on "ontological turn as another Western appropriation"; Yunkaporta on extractive citation).
- **Latin-American decolonial vs Indigenous-Anglo-settler-colonial.** The Mignolo/Walsh strand is sometimes read by North American Indigenous scholars as overly abstract / under-grounded in specific Indigenous nations and treaties (cf. critiques in *Settler Colonial Studies*).
- **Posthumanism vs decoloniality.** Recent comparative-education work (Silova, Zhao, Carney "Beyond the human" FreshEd; 2025 critical-Black-studies intervention by Carney and colleagues) flags that posthumanism can flatten the human just when Black and Indigenous critique is fighting *for* differently-conceived humans (Wynter, Weheliye).
- **Ubuntu universalised.** Risk of detaching Ubuntu from specific Bantu-language contexts into a generic "African values" pedagogy — paralleling the Buen Vivir critique that state-led versions (Ecuador, Bolivia) have hollowed the concept.
- **Asian/Sinosphere underrepresentation.** Daoist, Confucian-ecological, and Kyoto-school resources are visible mostly via Weili Zhao's comparative-education work and isolated philosophy-of-education pieces; this is a *gap* the proposal could deliberately address.

## Particularly Relevant for McKenzie / Silova

- **McKenzie** co-authors with Fikile Nxumalo (decolonial childhood-nature) and Joseph Henderson (climate education); her MECCE work is policy-mapping but her 2023 *Policy Futures* piece explicitly invokes Nxumalo's decolonial framing — so the *common-worlds + climate-coloniality* axis is the live bridge.
- **Silova** repeatedly cites Andreotti/GTDF, Bawaka Country, Affrica Taylor, and Weili Zhao; her "Wonderland of comparative education" (2019) and "Beyond the human" (FreshEd 2021) signal that *postsocialist + more-than-human + decolonial* triangulation is her signature move. A proposal that explicitly triangulates a *non-Western-non-Indigenous-Anglo* tradition (e.g., Sinosphere, post-Soviet, post-Yugoslav) with the common-worlds vocabulary is likely to land well.
- **Cross-reference list of authors both editors are likely to recognise as common ground:** Andreotti, Ahenakew, Stein, Nxumalo, Taylor, Malone, Bawaka Country, Yunkaporta, Wynter, Akómoláfé, Sumida Huaman.
