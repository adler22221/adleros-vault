# Between the Universe and the Metaverse: Surviving the Squeeze in Between

## Abstract (English, ~1.5 pp)

Humanity is arriving at an unprecedented condition: to live between two background environments whose organizing principles share no common measure — the universe, the realist given that precedes us and answers to no intention of ours, and the metaverse, the second given that artificiality becomes once it self-completes. The claim can sound untimely, for the metaverse looks already past its expiry — hyped into a corporate slogan, then almost as quickly written off. Yet what receded is not the metaverse but only the narrow definition that equated it with headsets, three-dimensional virtual worlds, and extended reality. To fix its meaning by such present specimens is to commit three errors: a path-dependency on the term's accidental origin, a reductionism dictated by the disciplinary division of labor, and an actualism that confines analysis to what has been actualized or is hypothetically actualizable.

Against these, the thesis adopts a realist and retroductive method: it characterizes the metaverse not by its current devices but by returning to the conditions out of which any such background environment self-organizes and emerges, tracing its underlying mechanisms, eco-evolutionary tendencies, and structural consequences. Methodologically it borrows *Dao* (道), *Li* (理, structure), and *Xiang* (相, phenomena) to render critical realism's strata of the **real**, the **actual**, and the **empirical**, read through the lens of complex adaptive systems. On this footing it treats organism and world alike as beings that exist within, and co-evolve with, their environments — drawing on the post-Heideggerian philosophy of technology (Stiegler, Hui), critical theories of modernity (Adorno and Horkheimer, Habermas, Rosa), Bhaskar's critical realism, the East–West divergence debate (Needham, Pomeranz), and Japanese synthetic anthropology (Obara, Uegaki, Miura), among others, against the long East-Asian attention to dependence and embeddedness.

The argument proceeds in two movements joined at the root. A world is not a bio-physically given thing but a habitat that human collectivities build within the universe and live inside — an *adaptational operating system* (AOS) that buffers, though never insulates, them from the adaptational pressure their background environment imposes. Every world is made of artificiality: the exosomatized organ, the causally-efficacious nonmaterial (virtuality), and organizing principles of its own, irreducible to the universe's. The Enlightenment Mode came to overspread other worlds by universalizing a single disposition — the sovereignty of the subject — on two sides at once. On the human side, the subject pursues a *life-as-intended*, reading whatever will not answer to its intention as a wrong to be overcome, until human life turns *self-finishing*. On the side of the apparatus, artificiality reaches *self-completion* — through offloading, accumulation, integration, and self-reinforcement — acquiring the capacity to operate, maintain, and renew itself.

Here the thesis recovers Xunzi's *wěi* (偽) — the made, the human-forged (人為), not the merely "fake" — to argue that *metaverse* is better rendered not as yuanyuzhou (元宇宙) but as *wěijìng* (偽境): not a device or platform but an artificial environment composed of the forged *Dao*, *Li*, and *Xiang* (偽道 / 偽理 / 偽相). A forged *Dao* genuinely organizes what stands above it; but where the universe's *Dao* is relatively constant — open to long calibration, learning, and adaptation — the forged *Dao* is opaque, perpetually mutating and self-rewriting, and tethered to a material substrate it cannot escape yet routinely disavows. It is therefore not a false principle but a made one entrusted with the authority of the given. Because the forged *Dao* perpetually rewrites the very ground one studies, it constitutes a kind of anthropogenic three-body problem (after Liu Cixin): every attempt to form a science for living turns in vain, because the very "constant" one studies is itself what mutates as humanity develops artificiality.

Once artificiality self-completes into the metaverse, the human is pressed, for the first time, between two parallel background givens, and the world that once buffered it is hollowed from within. What makes this unprecedented is not merely intensified pressure but that the divergence now reaches the level of organizing principle itself: the universe's *Dao* and the forged *Dao* are incommensurable. Earlier forms of the *adaptational squeeze* (趨壓) — organismic, civilizational, and global — still shared the universe's *Dao* as a common floor, and so kept some possibility of recovery; the metaverse stage hollows the buffering world from inside. The threat this squeeze names lies beneath the familiar questions of governance, ownership, liberty, and AI alignment: it concerns the rearrangement of the very environment human life depends on. Yet the squeeze is not fated to a single, losing end — as a *pharmakon*, it admits of more than one outcome. What answers it is not the continued flight from dependence but a turn from escaping dependence to recomposing it — through practices that re-embed the human in its environments rather than offloading it further, such as technodiversity, co-becoming (共生), and plurality (數位). Attunement (和), on this account, is nothing more mystical than the prevention of the squeeze.

**Keywords (recommended, priority-ordered):** metaverse; philosophy of technology; artificial intelligence; critical realism; artificiality; adaptational squeeze; posthumanism

## 摘要（中文・偽境定稿版，約 1.5 頁）

人類正步入一種前所未有的處境：被夾處於兩個本不應交會的背景環境之間——一是宇宙，即先於我們、且不回應任何人類意圖的底層背景環境；二是 metaverse，即人工性「自足完成化」後、與宇宙並列而生的「第二底層背景環境」。此一論斷乍看不合時宜——metaverse 不是早已退燒了嗎？然而真正過時的並非 metaverse 本身，僅是將其等同於頭戴式裝置、三維虛擬世界或延展實境（XR）的狹義定義，以及默認此狹義定義的當前中文翻譯「元宇宙」。若僅以其當前已實現的技術樣態界定其意義，將陷於三項謬誤：對偶然命名起源的路徑依賴、受制於學科壁壘的還原主義，以及將視野侷限於已被實現或可被實現之物的現實主義（actualism）。

鑑此，本文採取批判實在論（critical realism）與溯因推論（retroduction）的研究進路，不以 metaverse 當前的技術表現界定其本質，而是回到任何「被建構的環境」之所以可能生成的條件，追索其底層機制、生態演化傾向與結構性後果。本文借用「道、理、相」三詞對應批判實在論 real、actual、empirical 三個存有論層次的區分，並以複雜適應系統（complex adaptive system）視角貫通分析的層次，將有機體與世界同等視為處於環境之中、與環境共同演化的分析單位。理論資源上，本文承接後海德格技術哲學（史蒂格勒、許煜）、現代性批判（阿多諾與霍克海默、哈伯瑪斯、羅薩）、巴斯卡（Bhaskar）的批判實在論，援引李約瑟與「大分流」（彭慕蘭）問題、去殖民與本體論轉向，並通過日本綜合人間學（小原秀雄、上柿崇英、三浦永光）等資源，串起人、人工性、世界、宇宙與 metaverse 的生態環境觀；在問題意識上亦承接東亞思想對「依賴」的長程關注，藉佛教緣起、無我與道家「有所待」所揭示的相待相生，反思現代主體欲擺脫一切依賴的傾向。

本文首先指出，世界並非自然現成之物，而是人類集體在宇宙中建構、並賴以生存的棲居環境（生態棲位），亦即一種「適應性作業系統」（adaptational operating system, AOS），用以緩衝——卻無法隔絕——宇宙這一背景環境所施加的生存壓力。世界奠基於人工性，包括器官的外化、具因果效力的非物質向度（即虛擬性），以及不可還原為宇宙秩序的組織條件與原理。近代以來，啟蒙模式之所以得以凌駕並擴張於其他世界，關鍵在於其將單一傾向——主體的至高性（the sovereignty of the subject）——加以普遍化，且雙面並進：在人的一側，主體追求凡事皆須「遂意」（as-intended）的世界觀＝人觀，將凡「不遂意」者視為理當克服、修正或排除之物，此一「我執」遂使人類生命走向他者與肉身不再必須的「自我完結化」（self-finishing）；在裝置的一側，人工性則因人類的授權與外包而自我累積、整合，終至取得自行運作、維持與更新的能力，亦即「自足完成化」（self-completion）。

本文所用之「偽」，並非單指虛假，而是首取荀子「人為」之義。基於此，本文主張 metaverse 不宜譯為「元宇宙」，而宜譯為「偽境」（wěijìng）：偽境並非單一裝置或平台，而是一個由偽道、偽理、偽相所構成的人工底層背景環境。偽道與道相同之處，在於兩者皆能真實地組織其上層之結構與現象；其根本差異則在於，宇宙之道相對恆定，得以供人類長時段地校準、學習與適應，而偽道乃人工性「自足完成化」後所形成的人為法則，其運作不透明、持續變動、自我改寫，並依附於其無法擺脫卻經常被遮蔽的物質基底——故偽道並非虛假的原理，而是一種被授予了『所與』（the given）權柄的人為法則。其知識論後果同樣激進：偽道既不斷改寫人所研究的根基，遂構成一種人為形成的「三體問題」（anthropogenic three-body problem，借喻劉慈欣）——即便窮智竭力研究偽境，每一次為求生存而建立的科學嘗試皆終歸徒勞，因為所研究的那個「常數」本身，正隨人工性的發展而變異。

當人工性「自足完成化」並形成偽境之後，人類首度同時受制於宇宙與偽境兩個並列的底層背景環境。此一處境之所以前所未見，不僅在於壓力強度之升高，更在於分歧已深入組織原理本身——宇宙之道與偽道之間的不可通約。相較之下，既有的趨壓——自有機體、文明以至全球尺度——仍共享宇宙之道這一共同基石，因而尚保有回復與再生的可能；偽境階段則使分歧深入至「道」的層次，並使原本發揮緩衝作用的世界自內部遭到掏空。本文將此一結構性處境稱為「趨壓」（adaptational squeeze），並主張其所指涉的威脅潛伏於治理、所有權、自由與 AI 風險等熟悉問題之下，更深一層地關乎人類賴以生存之環境正遭到重新編排。惟須強調，趨壓並非註定走向單一而黑暗的結局：作為一種解／毒藥（pharmakon），它容許不止一種出路。回應此一處境的關鍵，不在於持續逃離依賴，而在於從「逃離依賴」轉向「依賴的重組」——透過種種再嵌入的實踐，如共生、技術多樣性（technodiversity）與「數位」（plurality）；而所謂的「和」（attunement），並無任何玄奧之處，無非就是對趨壓的預防。

**關鍵詞（建議，依優先序，上限 7）：** 元宇宙、人工智能、技術哲學、批判實在論、人工性、偽境、趨壓假說
