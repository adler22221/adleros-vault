## 1. The Three Registers

### Biological register: self-domestication (Wrangham, Hare et al.)

The self-domestication hypothesis, in its strongest contemporary form, holds that *Homo sapiens* underwent selection against reactive aggression across the Pleistocene, producing a constellation of morphological and behavioural changes — neoteny, craniofacial feminization, reduced sexual dimorphism, prolonged developmental plasticity, heightened tolerance, and enhanced social cognition — that parallels the syndromes documented in domesticated animals. Hare, Wobber and Wrangham (2012, `hareSelfdomesticationHypothesisEvolution2012`) developed the hypothesis for bonobos as a model, and Wrangham (2019, `wranghamGoodnessParadox2019`) extended it to humans through the "capital punishment" mechanism: collective execution by language-coordinated coalitions selectively removed persistently aggressive individuals, generating a docility syndrome at the species level. The register is biological-evolutionary; the timescale is hominin-Pleistocene; the agent of selection is collective human behaviour acting on the human gene pool.

### Cultural-historical register: self-petification (Obara Hideo 小原秀雄)

Obara Hideo's 自己家畜化論 — usually rendered "self-domestication theory" but more precisely *self-petification* in the sense developed across his work [RESOLVED 2026-05-05: `obaraJikoKachikuKa1995` → Zotero 6L278LXZ | nlm:synced ✅; `obaraHumanizationSelfDomestication2000` → Zotero 2Q8KYMHC | nlm:synced ✅] — names a *cultural-historical* process by which humans modify themselves through the sedimentation of artificial environments and routinized practice. The decisive shift relative to the biological register is that the modifying pressure is *itself* human-made: built environments, fixed dietary regimes, urban density, schooling, mass entertainment, and clinical-pharmaceutical management produce a humanity progressively detached from the unbuilt cosmos and progressively *patterned* — "stone-like" or *petrified* — in its appetites, postures, and rhythms. Uegaki Takahide's 2014 critical reconstruction of Obara (`uegakiJikoKachikuKa2014`) frames Obara's contribution as the bridge from biology to social theory: self-petification is what biological self-domestication *becomes* once the selective environment is exhaustively curated by humans for humans.

### Ontological / social register: self-finishing 自己完結 (Uegaki)

Uegaki Takahide's *Jiko-kanketsu shakai* (『自己完結社会』, 2022a/b — `uegakiJikoKanketsuShakai2022a`, `uegakiJikoKanketsuShakai2022b`; foreshadowed in J3YDIF8D 2020) names the contemporary actualization: individuals organize their entire relational, productive, and consumptive lives within self-selected, algorithmically managed environments — curated feeds, niche communities, gig-mediated provisioning, parasocial relations — such that the unchosen world ceases to function as a *given* horizon of encounter. Where Obara's self-petification operates on the body and the disposition, Uegaki's self-finishing operates on the *relational structure of life as a whole*: the human becomes ontologically self-completing in the sense that the conditions of intelligibility, recognition, and provision are all routed through systems the individual has (apparently) chosen. The register is ontological-social; the timescale is the past three decades; the agent is the individual interacting with infrastructural mediation.

## 2. Why the Spectrum Coheres (the Real-Level Mechanism)

What licenses treating these three registers as a *single* spectrum rather than three loosely related observations is a real-level mechanism shared across all three: **progressive substitution of a curated artificial mediation layer for the unchosen cosmic substrate as the proximate environment of human formation**. In Bhaskar's terms (ANNOT-L3), this is a `dr`-level tendency — a generative mechanism of self-enclosure — that is *actualized* differently at each register because the available mediating materials differ:

- at the biological register the mediating material is *behavioural coalition* operating on the gene pool (Wrangham);
- at the cultural-historical register the mediating material is *built environment + routinized practice* operating on the body and disposition (Obara);
- at the ontological-social register the mediating material is *infrastructural-algorithmic curation* operating on the relational structure of life (Uegaki).

The structurally analogous effect, across all three, is the *thickening of the artificial layer between the human and the universe-substrate* and the *progressive loss of unchosen encounter*. Read through the universe / world / metaverse triad, the spectrum tracks the historical deepening of the world-layer's enclosure of the human and culminates in the metaverse-layer's threat to detach the world itself from the universe (the "squeeze" of the thesis title). Crucially, this is a *tendency* under specific historical-material conditions (settled agriculture, industrial urbanism, networked computation), not a teleology: each step is contingent and reversible in principle, even if asymmetrically so (the retreat-cost is high — see ANNOT-L2 §3 "asymmetric retreat-cost").

The spectrum is therefore not a chronological succession in which one register *replaces* another; the registers *layer*. Contemporary humans are *simultaneously* self-domesticated bodies (biological inheritance), self-petrified subjects (cultural-historical formation), and increasingly self-finished individuals (ontological-social mediation). The deepening enclosure is the *co-active* operation of all three at once — a laminated phenomenon in Bhaskar's sense (ANNOT-L3 §2).

## 3. Standalone Passage (Drop-In for Ch-4-5, Ch-4-6, §VI)

> *Insert with minor contextual editing into Ch-4-5 A3 (spectrum elaboration), Ch-4-6 (transhumanism background), or §VI revision-draft. Length ≈ 470 words. Citekeys are pre-resolved against Zotero; pages flagged `[VERIFY: page]` pending PDF check or NLM excerpt retrieval post-relogin.*

The trajectory by which the human becomes increasingly enclosed within a self-curated artificial layer can be read as a single spectrum running from the biological register through the cultural-historical to the ontological-social. At its biological end stands the self-domestication hypothesis: that *Homo sapiens* underwent Pleistocene selection against reactive aggression — through, on Wrangham's reconstruction, language-coordinated collective sanction against persistently aggressive individuals — producing a docility syndrome of neoteny, reduced craniofacial robusticity, and heightened social cognition (Wrangham 2019, `wranghamGoodnessParadox2019` [VERIFY: confirmed | wranghamGoodnessParadox2019 | Ch.7 (no page nos. in digital text) | nlm-excerpt:"Language-based conspiracy was the key, because it gave whispering beta males the power to join forces to kill alpha-male bullies...Genetic selection against the propensity for reactive aggression was an unforeseen result of eliminating the would-be despots." — NLM UUID c774be6d (A-Sources c68ca15e) ⚠️ UUID verify]; Hare, Wobber & Wrangham 2012, `hareSelfdomesticationHypothesisEvolution2012` [VERIFY: confirmed | hareSelfdomesticationHypothesisEvolution2012 | p.578 | nlm-excerpt:"Together with reduced aggression and increased tolerance these constitute the domestication syndrome. Traits in the domestication syndrome occur with varying degrees of consistency and fall into four major categories." — NLM UUID 27610fcb (A-Sources c68ca15e)]). The mechanism at this register is collective human behaviour acting on the human gene pool over evolutionary time.

Obara Hideo extended this into a cultural-historical register through his theory of human self-petification (自己家畜化論), in which routinized practice and built environment progressively pattern the body and disposition into stone-like fixity — appetites, postures, and rhythms cease to be elastic responses to an unchosen cosmos and become precise correlates of a curated milieu (Obara 1995 [RESOLVED: Zotero 6L278LXZ | nlm:synced ✅], 2000 [RESOLVED: Zotero 2Q8KYMHC | nlm:synced ✅]; reconstructed critically in Uegaki 2014, `uegakiJikoKachikuKa2014` [VERIFY: page]). Where biological self-domestication selects on the gene pool, self-petification operates on the body and habitus through the sedimentation of an artificial environment that humans themselves curate for themselves.

Uegaki Takahide (2020, J3YDIF8D; 2022a/b, `uegakiJikoKanketsuShakai2022a`, `uegakiJikoKanketsuShakai2022b`) names the present actualization: the *self-completing society* (自己完結社会), in which individuals arrange the totality of their relational, productive, and consumptive life within self-selected, algorithmically managed environments. Recognition, provision, and meaning are routed through systems the individual has apparently chosen, with the consequence that the unchosen world progressively ceases to function as a given horizon of encounter. The register is ontological-social; its mechanism is infrastructural-algorithmic mediation; its timescale is the past three decades.

The three registers should not be read as a chronological succession in which each replaces its predecessor. They *layer*. Contemporary humans inhabit a self-domesticated body, a self-petrified disposition, and an increasingly self-finished relational structure simultaneously. What licenses treating the three as a single trajectory is a shared real-level tendency — the progressive substitution of a curated artificial mediation layer for the unchosen cosmic substrate as the proximate environment of human formation. Each step thickens the world-layer between the human and the universe; the metaverse register threatens to detach the world from the universe altogether. The spectrum is thus not a teleology but a tendency that has been actualized under specific historical-material conditions (settled agriculture, industrial urbanism, networked computation) and remains, in principle, contestable — though the cost of contestation rises asymmetrically at each step.

## 4. Counter-Positions Acknowledged

The spectrum invites three credible objections that the thesis must absorb rather than dismiss.

First, the biological end is empirically contested: paleoanthropological evidence for the Wrangham-style capital-punishment mechanism is indirect, and craniofacial-feminization datasets have been read both for and against the hypothesis (see e.g. Cieri et al. 2014 [RESOLVED: DOI 10.1086/677209 confirmed via CrossRef 2026-05-05; add `cieriCraniofacialFeminization2014` to Zotero thesis-sources]; specific opposing readings within Pleistocene paleoanthropology). The thesis does not need the strong biological claim to do its work; the weaker, register-relative claim — that selection against reactive aggression is *one* contributing mechanism of human self-modification — is sufficient.

Second, communitarian, virtue-ethical, and care-ethical traditions will read the enclosure framing as overweighting individual self-curation and underweighting ongoing communal practices (kinship obligations, religious assemblies, mutual aid networks) that continue to expose the individual to unchosen encounter. The spectrum should be presented as a *dominant tendency* under conditions of late-modern infrastructural mediation, not as an exhaustive description of contemporary lifeworlds.

Third, the trajectory is contingent, not teleological. The spectrum *describes* a deepening that has occurred under specific historical-material conditions (Pleistocene coalition behaviour, agrarian-urban sedimentation, networked computation); it does *not* claim the deepening was inevitable or that it must continue. The asymmetric retreat-cost (ANNOT-L2 §3) explains why retreat is hard, not why it is impossible.
