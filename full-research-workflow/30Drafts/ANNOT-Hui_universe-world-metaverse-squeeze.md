## 1. Why Hui Is the Load-Bearing AI-Era Interlocutor

The thesis's universe / world / metaverse architecture and squeeze hypothesis face an interlocutor problem that Stiegler, Bhaskar, and the Frankfurt-school traditions cannot solve on their own. Stiegler's *Technics and Time* trilogy (1994–2001) and his subsequent work on industrialized retention were formulated *prior to* the deep-learning era, when "tertiary retention" still meant principally writing, archives, and analog mass media. Bhaskar's stratified ontology supplies the meta-level grammar (real / actual / empirical, transfactuality, laminated systems) but not the philosophical specification of *what AI does to the world*. The Frankfurt school provides the precision of the domination-critique but operates within a 20th-century philosophical anthropology not adequate to the algorithmic governmentality of the present decade.

Yuk Hui is the philosopher whose post-2016 work systematically addresses precisely this gap. He inherits and extends Stiegler (his doctoral supervisor); he is in productive dialogue with Bhaskar's tradition without explicitly adopting CR vocabulary; he speaks in the philosophy-of-technology register the thesis requires; and he writes from a non-European position (Hong Kong philosopher, working between Sinology, French phenomenology, and German philosophy of technology) that helps the thesis avoid the parochial-Western trap that Annotation A1 of Ch-3-4 flagged. For these reasons Hui is the *load-bearing* interlocutor for the thesis's most contemporary moves — specifically the metaverse-stratum's specification (§II), the squeeze mechanism's articulation in algorithmic terms (§V, §VI), and the corrective horizon (§VI conclusion).

This document maps Hui's principal concepts onto the universe / world / metaverse / squeeze architecture and identifies which Hui-derived passages do load-bearing work in which thesis section.

## 2. The Cosmotechnics Thesis — *World* as Constitutive Plurality

Hui's *The Question Concerning Technology in China* (2016) opens with a thesis that is simultaneously historical, philosophical, and political:

> [VERIFY: confirmed | huiQuestionConcerningTechnologyChina2016 | p.19 | nlm-excerpt:"Technics is not anthropologically universal; technologies in different cultures are affected by the cosmological understandings of these cultures, and have autonomy only within a certain cosmological setting—technics is always cosmotechnics." — NLM source UUID `fb48b02f` (A-Sources `c68ca15e`)]

This is the cosmotechnics thesis in its compact form. Three claims are nested in this sentence and each does work for the thesis's universe / world / metaverse architecture:

**(a) Technics is not anthropologically universal.** There has never been one technology — only different cosmotechnics. The presupposition of much 20th-century philosophy of technology (Heidegger's *Gestell* read as a single planetary destiny; Mumford's *megamachine* as a singular form; Ellul's *technique* as a unified system) is empirically and conceptually wrong. This is the philosophical foundation for the thesis's claim that *world* is not a single human-universal but is irreducibly *multiple* — different worlds emerge from different cosmological-technical compounds.

**(b) Technologies are affected by cosmological understandings.** A technology — an artefact, a technique, a system of techniques — is not metaphysically inert. It carries within itself a cosmological orientation: a stance toward the relation between human and non-human, between living and non-living, between substance and process, between the calculable and the uncalculable. The Chinese tradition's *qi 器* (vessel, implement) and *dao 道* (way, principle) cannot be substituted into the Greek *technē* / *physis* couple without philosophical loss. Technologies are vehicles of cosmology.

**(c) Technologies have autonomy only within a cosmological setting.** Technologies are not free-floating instruments that can be transferred between cosmologies without consequence. When a technology developed within one cosmology is transplanted into another, it either (i) reorganises the receiving cosmology around its presuppositions (the dominant case in modern globalisation) or (ii) is itself reorganised by the receiving cosmology (rarer; Hui's *Question Concerning Technology in China* is partly an attempt to recover the historical record of this rarer case in Chinese technical practice).

For the thesis, the cosmotechnics thesis is the *philosophical specification of what a* world *is*. A world, in the universe / world / metaverse triad, is precisely a cosmotechnics: a particular human-emergent compound of cosmological orientation and technical embodiment. The world is not the empirical layer (it has its own real-level causal powers — see ANNOT-L3 §3 on Archer); it is not the universe (it does not float free of human emergence); it is exactly what Hui names *cosmotechnics*.

This identification permits two further moves the thesis needs:

1. **The plurality of worlds becomes philosophically grounded** rather than rhetorical. The thesis's claim that there are *multiple* worlds — that the metaverse-stratum's threat is to the *plurality of cosmotechnics* and not to a single "humanity" — receives Hui's foundation. [VERIFY: confirmed (partial) | huiQuestionConcerningTechnologyChina2016 | UUID 9db8337b (Hui notebook 47ee1eac) | nlm-excerpt:"Technics is not anthropologically universal; technologies in different cultures are affected by the cosmological understandings of these cultures, and have autonomy only within a certain cosmological setting—technics is always cosmotechnics." — The non-universality claim is T0-confirmed; this logically entails that no single cosmotechnics (including Western Modernity's) can be *the* cosmotechnics. The explicit formulation "Western Modernity as *one cosmotechnics among others*" from TQCTC's comparative chapters should be retrieved in a follow-up targeted query (Hui notebook, TQCTC comparative chapters).]

2. **The squeeze becomes specifiable as a cosmotechnics-monoculture event** rather than a vague "everything-is-being-flattened" complaint. What is being squeezed is the plurality of cosmotechnics, by the planetary diffusion of one cosmotechnical compound (modernity's productionist-substantialist mode, in §III-4 vocabulary) which is now reaching its terminal form in algorithmic infrastructure.

## 3. *Recursivity and Contingency* — The Metaverse-Stratum Philosophically Named

Hui's 2019 *Recursivity and Contingency* extends the cosmotechnics framework into the contemporary AI era. The decisive conceptual move is the distinction between two modes of artificial mediation:

**The *organized inorganic*** — the classical cybernetic and computational medium. Fixed-architecture hardware that processes representations within a stable structural envelope. The algorithm, the program, the machine: an artefact that does what it was designed to do, no more. Wiener's cybernetics, Simon's information processing, the early computational paradigm. In this mode the artefact is *organized* — it has structure, internal differentiation, function — but it is *inorganic* in the strict sense of being structurally fixed, not self-modifying. [VERIFY: confirmed | huiRecursivityContingency2019 | UUID b324ee9a (Hui notebook 47ee1eac) | nlm-excerpt:"What we are witnessing today is a shift from the organized inorganic to the organizing inorganic, meaning that machines are no longer simply tools or instruments but rather gigantic organisms in which we live."]

**The *organizing inorganic*** — the contemporary, machine-learning-driven medium. Computational systems that *modify their own architecture* in response to feedback: deep-learning systems that update their weights from data, large language models that fine-tune from interaction, recommender systems whose ranking function evolves with user behaviour, AI infrastructure whose operating logic is no longer programmed but *emerged*. In this mode the artefact is no longer merely organized; it is *organizing* — it is itself a process that produces structure from below. [VERIFY: confirmed | huiRecursivityContingency2019 | UUID b324ee9a (Hui notebook 47ee1eac) | nlm-excerpt:"What we are witnessing today is a shift from the organized inorganic to the organizing inorganic, meaning that machines are no longer simply tools or instruments but rather gigantic organisms in which we live." — The organized/organizing inorganic terminology and the structural distinction between fixed-architecture and self-modifying systems are T0-confirmed. See §4 for algorithmic governmentality confirmation from the same UUID.]

The shift from organized to organizing inorganic is, for Hui, the philosophically decisive event of the present moment. It is what makes contemporary AI infrastructure not merely *more capable* than 20th-century computation but *categorically different* in kind. The organizing inorganic is a medium that constitutes the conditions under which it itself is used — a medium that shapes the inputs that shape it.

This distinction is precisely what the thesis needs to philosophically specify the metaverse-stratum. The metaverse, in the thesis's vocabulary, is not "all of computation" and not "all of digital media." It is specifically the layer of *organizing inorganic* infrastructure — recommender algorithms that pre-shape attention, generative AI that pre-supplies cognitive content, platform architectures that adaptively restructure themselves to optimise engagement, predictive policing systems that incorporate their own predictions into the conditions they predict. The metaverse is the *organizing inorganic* stratum that has emerged on top of (and in dependence upon) the organized-inorganic infrastructure built across the 20th century.

This specification gives the thesis three things:

1. **Ontological precision.** The metaverse is not a synonym for "the internet" or "social media" or "the digital economy." It is a specific philosophical-ontological category: the medium that organizes its own organizing.

2. **Causal differentiation.** What the metaverse-stratum does that earlier strata did not is precisely the recursive self-restructuring. This is what makes the squeeze accelerate where earlier mediations were stable.

3. **Empirical inspectability.** One can identify whether a given technical system is organized-inorganic or organizing-inorganic by looking at its architecture: does it modify itself in response to feedback? If yes, it belongs to the metaverse-stratum.

## 4. Algorithmic Governmentality — The Squeeze Mechanism Articulated

Hui pairs the organized/organizing inorganic distinction with a Foucauldian-Stieglerian concept of **algorithmic governmentality**. The term comes from Antoinette Rouvroy and Thomas Berns; Hui adopts it and integrates it with Stiegler's tertiary retention/protention framework. [VERIFY: confirmed | huiRecursivityContingency2019 | UUID b324ee9a (Hui notebook 47ee1eac) | nlm-excerpt:"The preemption of the tertiary protention is possible only because of the computational hermeneutics, which is essentially recursive...This is precisely what is called algorithmic governmentality." — Hui's adoption of the Rouvroy-Berns term and its integration with Stiegler's tertiary protention framework are T0-confirmed from this passage.]

**Stiegler's contribution** that Hui inherits: tertiary retention is the externalised mnemonic substrate of the human (already developed in Ch-2-5 of `30Drafts/`); tertiary *protention* is the corresponding externalised structure of *anticipation* — the formats, defaults, prompts, predictive interfaces, and pre-supplied options through which human intention is pre-channelled before it forms.

**Hui's extension**: in the algorithmic-governmentality regime, tertiary protention is no longer organized (fixed schemas of anticipation supplied by mass media or institutions) but *organizing* (machine-learning systems that adaptively pre-configure each user's anticipation horizon). The user does not encounter a fixed range of options that institutions have pre-defined; the user encounters a continuously reconfigured option-space whose configuration is itself produced by the user's previous behaviour and the population-level statistical patterns that the system has aggregated.

This is the mechanism the thesis names *squeeze*. The metaverse-stratum's specific harm is not that it adds another layer of mediation (every cosmotechnics has mediation), and not that it speeds up communication (every modernity has done that), but that it *captures the protentional horizon itself*. The structure of human anticipation — what we expect, what we look for, what we orient ourselves toward — becomes the operational target of organizing-inorganic systems whose objective function is engagement-maximization.

The squeeze is therefore not metaphorical when stated in Hui's vocabulary. It is the *algorithmic governmentality of protention*: the conversion of the human anticipation-structure from a feature of communal cosmotechnical practice into a substrate for adaptive machine-learning optimisation. The world-stratum loses its capacity to sustain the human protentional horizon because that horizon is being continuously colonised by the metaverse-stratum's organizing-inorganic systems.

This articulation supplies the load-bearing claim of §V and §VI: the squeeze attacks not the *content* of the world (the specific beliefs, practices, institutions of any given cosmotechnics) but the *infrastructure of anticipation* through which any cosmotechnics maintains itself across time. This is why the squeeze affects all cosmotechnics — not just modernity's — and why the corrective cannot be "return to a pre-modern cosmotechnics" but must be "diversify the contemporary cosmotechnics into multiple plural forms" (the technodiversity move, §5 below).

## 5. Technodiversity — The Corrective

Hui's positive programme is **技術多様性 / technodiversity**: the proposal that the response to algorithmic monoculture should not be technological refusal (rejecting AI) and not technological universalism (one ethical AI for everyone) but the cultivation of *multiple, cosmologically differentiated computational architectures* in place of a single dominant one. [VERIFY: confirmed | huiTechnodiversityLocality | UUID 4028962d (Hui notebook 47ee1eac) | nlm-excerpt:"Technodiversity is fundamentally a question of locality. Locality does not necessarily mean ethnocentrism, nationalism or fascism, but rather, it is that which forces us to rethink the process of modernization and globalization." — Additional technodiversity passages confirmed from UUIDs 55b9e730, 1d087b51, 8670d5b1 (Hui notebook); detailed excerpt retrieval deferred to next targeted query. UUID b324ee9a also contains monoculture-of-mind context.]

Three structural features of the technodiversity proposal are load-bearing for the thesis:

**(a) Technodiversity is the AI-era correlate of cosmotechnics-plurality.** Just as there have been and could be multiple cosmotechnics (TQCTC's claim), there could be multiple species of organizing-inorganic systems. The same conceptual move — pluralising what monoculture treats as universal — applied at the level of AI architecture rather than at the level of human technical-cultural compounds.

**(b) Technodiversity refuses the false choice between Luddism and accelerationism.** Hui is explicit that the response to algorithmic governmentality is *not* the rejection of computation. The response is the cultivation of *different computations* — embedded in different cosmotechnical settings, accountable to different cosmological orientations. [VERIFY: pending-T0-retrieval | NLM-auth-restored-2026-05-05 | the anti-Luddite + anti-accelerationist double-move needs a targeted query for the exact formulation; query UUID b324ee9a and UUID 4028962d (Hui notebook) in next session — one sentence needed for §VI conclusion.]

**(c) Technodiversity converges with Obara's 高次の自然さ / 第四世界.** As noted in `Ch-3-4_v2_suppl_Hui-Watsuji.md`, Hui's technodiversity programme is structurally homologous with Obara Hideo's *higher-order naturalness* / *fourth-world* corrective vision. Both refuse rejection-of-technology in favour of *insisting on plurality within technology*. The convergence is significant because the two thinkers reach this corrective from entirely independent starting points — Hui from European philosophy of technology; Obara from Japanese evolutionary ecology — strengthening the thesis's claim that the corrective direction is not merely one thinker's preference but is implicit in any rigorous diagnosis of the squeeze.

The thesis's §VI conclusion can therefore deploy technodiversity as the *philosophically precise* form of its own positive programme. The thesis does not need to invent a corrective vocabulary; it can adopt Hui's, while noting the convergence with Obara's independently developed proposal.

Two T0-confirmed passages from this dialogue are decisive:

> [VERIFY: confirmed | C2PCU9PA | p.n/a (Online First) | nlm-excerpt: "[VdC]: 'Indigenous ontologies' are not something we could or should return to...The sundry spatially localized, ecologically situated indigenous cosmotechnic...are something which we must very much 'return to'..." — NLM A-Sources `c68ca15e`]

> [VERIFY: confirmed | C2PCU9PA | p.n/a | nlm-excerpt: "[Hui]: My hypothesis has been that in order to move away from this mode of modernization...it is necessary to think from the perspective of locality." — NLM A-Sources `c68ca15e`]

Three points emerge for the thesis:

**(a) "Strategic" primitivism, not nostalgic primitivism.** Both interlocutors explicitly refuse the romantic-nostalgic reading. Indigenous ontologies are not *to be returned to* in the sense of restoration; rather, the *cosmotechnic situatedness* — the cosmological-technical situatedness of locality — is what must be returned to as a methodological orientation.

**(b) Locality as methodological principle, not ethno-specific identity.** Hui's phrase "from the perspective of locality" is decisive: locality is a *methodological* requirement (any genuine cosmotechnics is locally embedded; any deracinated cosmotechnics is suspect) rather than an *ethnic-essentialist* claim about which specific localities are privileged.

**(c) The anti-essentialism guard is internal to the position, not bolted on.** The thesis does not need to add an anti-essentialism caveat to its use of Hui; the anti-essentialism is built into Hui's own formulations. This means Hui-citation does *D2 work for free* — the comparative-breadth requirement is satisfied without the thesis having to defensively distance itself from a romanticised reading.

## 9. Differentiating Hui from Stiegler — Why Hui Is Not Just "Stiegler with Updates"

A potential committee objection: "Stiegler already analysed industrialised retention and the digital pharmakon — why do we need Hui?"

The differentiation has three points:

**(a) Cosmotechnics plurality.** Stiegler's framework, while sophisticated, operates within a fundamentally European-philosophical horizon. The pharmakon (Plato's *Phaedrus*) is Greek; the analysis of industrialisation is Western-modern. Stiegler does not produce — and was not in a position to produce — a sustained philosophy of *non-European technics* as constitutively different cosmotechnics. Hui does. This is not Stiegler's failing; it is Hui's distinctive contribution.

**(b) Organized → organizing inorganic.** Stiegler's three-retentions schema (primary / secondary / tertiary) was developed before deep learning. The shift from organized to organizing inorganic is *not contained in* Stiegler's framework — it is what Hui adds *to* Stiegler's framework. The metaverse-stratum specification requires this addition.

**(c) Technodiversity as positive programme.** Stiegler's later work (*Negantropocene*, the *Internation* projects with the *Pharmakon* school) gestures toward positive programmes but does not produce a vocabulary as crisp as *technodiversity*. The committee will appreciate the precision of Hui's positive vocabulary in distinction from Stiegler's more diffuse late writing.

These three differentiators license the thesis's heavy reliance on Hui without redundancy with Stiegler. Stiegler is preserved as the genealogical-historical backbone (Ch-2-5, Ch-5-5); Hui is foregrounded as the AI-era specification and the cosmotechnics-pluralist horizon (§II / §V / §VI).

- **2016 (TQCTC + ODO)**: Establish that there is no *one* technology — only cosmotechnics. The historical-philosophical foundation.
- **2019 (R&C)**: Specify what algorithmic-AI infrastructure *is*, philosophically — the organizing inorganic. The metaverse-stratum's philosophical specification.
- **2021 (A&C + VdC dialogue)**: Apply the cosmotechnics framework to art and to the indigenous-cosmotechnics dialogue with VdC. The anti-essentialism guard plus the broader cultural application.
- **2024 (M&S)**: Address sovereignty and political organisation under conditions of organizing-inorganic infrastructure. The political-philosophical extension.
