# Ch-3-5 — Hypotheticals Audit: §III–V

## Committee concern

米 (Mi): "too many hypotheticals" — throughout the thesis, conditional formulations weaken the argument's logical grip. This is specifically a concern about deductive/inductive rigour (Mi's primary focus).

## Audit: Located hypotheticals and recommended replacements

---

### HYPOTHETICAL 1

**Location:** §III, "Worlds as modes of artificiality for survival" (DOCX para 180)

**Current text (approximately):** "The reoccurring apocalyptic collapse of Santi civilization portrayed in the fiction *The Three-Body Problem* (Liu, 2014) *may seem* extreme, but it illustrates..."

**Problem:** The hedging "may seem extreme" invites the reader to dismiss the analogy before it is made.

**Replacement (drop the hedge; assert the structural function):**

> The fictional collapse of the Santi civilization in *The Three-Body Problem* (Liu, 2014) is not introduced as a realistic scenario but as a structural limit-case: it isolates the relationship between a mode of artificiality and survival conditions by removing all historical noise. The theoretical function of the example is analogical, not predictive.

**Why this works for Mi:** It converts the conditional "may seem" into a clear methodological statement about what kind of evidence the fiction provides — answering Mi's "why use this?" implicitly.

### HYPOTHETICAL 2

**Location:** §V, "Minimally sufficient technologies / emergence of the Metaverse" (DOCX paras 206–213)

**Current text (approximately):** "If we improve these technologies and make them work together seamlessly, we can *reasonably expect* the following cybernetic system to emerge..."

**Problem:** The "if we improve" framing makes the metaverse a future conditional, weakening the "already causally efficacious" argument.

**Replacement A (keep conditional but anchor in present):**

> These technologies do not need to reach completion to exert structural effects. The cybernetic loop — IoT collection → big data aggregation → AI decision → code-as-law execution → behavioral redirection — is already operative at partial instantiation. The metaverse, as defined in this thesis, names this loop's tendency toward self-closure, not its fully realized state. The following describes the direction of that tendency, extrapolated from its present trajectory [VERIFY: cite current IoT/AI market integration reports if available].

**Replacement B (briefer, if Liu is concerned about scope):**

> Even at partial instantiation, this cybernetic loop exhibits the structural tendency this thesis analyses. The following describes that tendency at its logical limit — not as a prediction but as a structural extrapolation from already-documented tendencies.

### HYPOTHETICAL 3

**Location:** §V, continuation (DOCX paras 212–213)

**Current text (approximately):** "If humans *cease to* — whether voluntarily or involuntarily — participate in the operation and decision-making process in a cybernetic system... *If this becomes the case*, such artificiality can no longer be considered merely a critical component of our world..."

**Problem:** Two stacked conditionals ("if humans cease... if this becomes the case") make the argument feel remote.

**Replacement:**

> Whether voluntarily (through preference for algorithmic curation) or involuntarily (through opaque code-as-law that precludes effective appeal), there are already documented instances of human decision-making being structurally excluded from platform governance. When this exclusion becomes the governing condition rather than the exception — not merely a technical malfunction but the architectural norm — the resulting environment can no longer be understood as a component of a human-made world. It has become the background against which human decisions are registered, not their medium [VERIFY: cross-reference Ch-6-10 algorithmic governance evidence].

### HYPOTHETICAL 4

**Location:** §VI, squeeze opening (DOCX para 217)

**Current text (approximately):** "*If* this new mode of artificiality eventually gained *complete independence* from humans, the world we live in becomes squeezed between the universe and the metaverse."

**Problem:** The "if... eventually... complete independence" framing delays the squeeze into the future, undermining the argument that the squeeze is already observable (paras 218–225 then immediately assert "existing signs").

**Replacement:**

> The metaverse's independence from human governance is not a threshold to be crossed at some future point but a gradient already underway. The squeeze names the structural condition that emerges as this gradient advances — the progressive reduction of habitable space between two environments whose laws are mutually incommensurable. This condition is not projected: it is already documented, as the following three classes of evidence show.

## Editorial note

Mi's "too many hypotheticals" is primarily a concern about **logical structure**, not vocabulary. The fix is not to hunt down every "if" clause but to reposition the conditional claims so that:

1. **Documented present evidence precedes structural extrapolation** (not vice versa)
2. **Limit-case scenarios are explicitly marked as structural analysis**, not prediction
3. **The gradient language** (already operative / tendency toward) replaces binary on/off conditionals

The replacements above can be inserted in full or adapted; the key move in each case is the same: ground the conditional in what is already verifiable before projecting forward.
