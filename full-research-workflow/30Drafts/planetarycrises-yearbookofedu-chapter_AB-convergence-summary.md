## Two Candidate Angles Now in Play

### Angle A — "Allocation Dependence as a Lens on Education under Planetary Crisis"
- **Section fit**: §1 (Global policy architectures) — or §3 (pathways) if reframed prospectively
- **One-sentence thesis**: Education systems' chronic failure to respond meaningfully to planetary crisis is not a flow problem (need more climate finance) but a structural lock-in problem; "allocation dependence" names the path-dependent commitments that make alternative responses impossible.
- **Empirical hook**: the 1.5% problem; school infrastructure under climate stress; education in protracted refugee contexts
- **Theoretical anchors**: Pierson (2000), Verger et al. (2017), Tronto (2013) for the political-economy of care
- **Distinctive move**: "allocation dependence" is genuinely unclaimed terminology in education scholarship (BF5 confirmed)
- **Editor fit**: matches McKenzie's policy-mobility commitments + Silova's "what if more education is not the answer" line directly
- **Risk**: needs to feel theoretically generative, not just analytical; needs at least one decolonial pillar to land with these editors

### Angle B — "Learn by Caring: An East-Asian Relational Ontology of Planetary Education"
- **Section fit**: §3 (pathways) — or §2 if framed against algorithmic/colonial constraints
- **One-sentence thesis**: Current care-pedagogy literature is bounded by Western (Noddings, Tronto, Bozalek/Zembylas) and Indigenous-Anglo-settler frames; an East-Asian relational ontology (Daoist/Confucian/Taiwanese-Indigenous) offers a different theoretical engine for "learn by caring" as a curriculum architecture for planetary crisis.
- **Empirical hook**: can be illustrated by a Taiwan-based case (educational practice or policy)
- **Theoretical anchors**: Tronto for the political-care bridge; Common Worlds Collective for the more-than-human; East-Asian philosophical sources (you supply)
- **Distinctive move**: brings non-Western ontology *as theory*, not as comparative case study
- **Editor fit**: Silova's signature triangulation move; McKenzie's seriousness-of-decolonial filter
- **Risk**: care-pedagogy is a crowded space; the East-Asian-as-theory move must be substantive, not invocational

### Hybrid Angle C — "Allocation Dependence and the Care Deficit"
- Bridges A and B: allocation patterns systematically defund the relational/care infrastructures that planetary-crisis education requires; "education as sustainable development" reframes the question from *how do we educate for sustainability* to *what makes an education itself sustainable*
- Pulls all three of your topics together; may be too ambitious for 400w; flag as option
