## 3. Where the Five-Period Schema Lives — Best Candidates

The five-period schema referenced in Ch-4-4 (Period 1 confrontation → Period 2 collective high-growth → Period 3 consumerist hollowing → Period 4 recognition anxiety / 漂流人 → Period 5 自己完結社会 / 非干渉の倫理) is most likely developed in:

### Primary candidate: J3YDIF8D (2020)
**「生活世界」の構造転換と「自己完結社会」の未来**
The title itself invokes the *structural transformation of the lifeworld* leading to the *future of the self-completing society* — exactly the periodized arc Ch-4-4 reconstructs. This is the most likely single article-length presentation. **Recommend this be the primary citation for the Ch-4-4 historical schema**, with the 2022 books cited as the comprehensive elaboration.

### Comprehensive treatment: CYAAA3IK + VESTLJGF (2022, 2 vols)
『自己完結社会』の成立 — the systematic 2-volume monograph. Vol. 1 (上) is the historical/genealogical exposition; Vol. 2 (下) is likely the theoretical/normative articulation. The five periods, the 漂流人 type, 承認不安, and 非干渉の倫理 should all be developed across these volumes.

### Methodological grounding: DJQP54CM (2017)
総合人間学と「中間理論」 — Uegaki's methodology paper. Provides the "middle theory" framework that licenses the five-period schema as an *empirical-anthropological* claim grounded in *theoretical anthropology*. Useful for the Ch-3-7 PROSE cycle (philosophy role) and for a footnote justifying why a sociological periodization belongs in a philosophical thesis.

### Self-domestication background: YCFPXYI8 (2014)
「自己家畜化論」から「総合人間学的本性論・実在論」へ — Uegaki's critical engagement with Obara's self-domestication theory. This feeds **ANNOT-G1** (the self-domestication → self-petification → self-finishing spectrum, Cycle 3) directly. Uegaki here establishes his position vis-à-vis Obara, which is the lineage the spectrum-articulation will need to render coherent.

### Synthetic refresher: WM49NNFX (2022 bookSection)
ポストヒューマン時代 における人間存在の諸問題 — short-form synthesis post-2022 books. Useful as a single-piece citation if a compact gloss is preferred over the 2-vol monograph.
