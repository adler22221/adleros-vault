### A1: Source-manuscripts material for paradox-to-squeeze

The manuscript's §2.4 develops a directly analogous double-squeeze argument that the thesis can draw on as scaffolding (NOT lift verbatim):

- **The pivot from "tool" to "background environment":** When humanity offloads ever more uncertainty-reduction onto control systems, the human role in operating the world shrinks, and the control apparatus auto-completes itself. Once it functions self-sufficiently without human participation, what was originally an adaptive instrument *for* the cosmos becomes a *new* background environment that humans must, in turn, adapt *to*. (Manuscript names this 偽境 — DO NOT use the term; recast as "neo-artificial background" or "the metaverse layer" using thesis vocabulary.)
- **The simultaneity condition:** The squeeze becomes a genuine double-pressure only when (a) the cosmos itself has already been disturbed by human intervention and is "running away," AND (b) the artificial layer has gained operational independence. These two conditions converge to strip humans of their collective adaptive purchase on the cosmos while simultaneously forcing them to adapt to the artificial layer. This is the squeeze as a structural trap, not as a metaphor.
- **The gradient framing:** §3 frames the squeeze as a *progressive* phenomenon — the deeper the offloading goes, the harder it is to retreat to a more fundamental adaptive substrate. Each successive "shell" makes the next one more inevitable. This matches the thesis's gradient-pressure framing in Ch-4-2 and supports recasting "squeeze" as an ongoing differential, not a binary state.
- **The "cosmos-side" of the squeeze:** The manuscript is explicit that the cosmic side of the pressure is not just "ecological collapse" in the standard sense — it is the loss of *direct metabolic interface* with the cosmos. Even if ecosystems were healthy, the offloading would still produce squeeze, because the channel of contact has been severed. This is a sharper articulation than the thesis currently carries and is **safely importable** if reframed in universe/world/metaverse vocabulary.
