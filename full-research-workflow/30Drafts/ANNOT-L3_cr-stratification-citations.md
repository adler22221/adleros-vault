## 1. Bhaskar — Real / Actual / Empirical

### Primary source
- **`bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008`** — Bhaskar, Roy. *A Realist Theory of Science*. London/New York: Routledge (Classical texts in critical realism), 2008. [Originally 1975; the 2008 Routledge reprint is the edition in Zotero. Source-manuscript also references the edition as "1975/2013" — same work, different reprint years.]
  - This is the locus classicus for the three-domain stratification.
- Secondary/accessible restatement: **`bhaskarEnlightenedCommonSense2016`** (Bhaskar & Hartwig, *Enlightened Common Sense: The Philosophy of Critical Realism*, Routledge, 2016) — useful for short, citation-clean restatements of the schema; intended as accessible exposition.

### The three-domain schema (paraphrase, with verification flags on quoted passages)

Bhaskar distinguishes three nested domains of reality, ordered by inclusion (each subsumes the next):

- **Real (`dr`)** — the domain of underlying *generative mechanisms*, *structures*, *powers*, and *tendencies* that exist independently of whether they are activated and independently of whether they are observed. Mechanisms are *transfactual*: they continue to exist and operate as tendencies even when their effects are countervailed or unobserved.
  - [VERIFY: confirmed | bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008 | p.2 (Table 0.1, Introduction) + p.46 (Table 1.1, §1.6) | nlm-excerpt: "Mechanisms, events and experiences thus constitute three overlapping domains of reality, viz. the domains of the real, the actual and the empirical." — NLM UUID cb8db854] *(page confirmed 2026-05-05 via T0 retrieval — earlier estimate of p.~13 was incorrect)*

- **Actual (`da`)** — the domain of *events* and *states of affairs* that actually occur, whether or not anyone observes them. Events are the manifestations of mechanisms when those mechanisms are triggered, but events do not exhaust the mechanisms that produce them (most mechanisms are not actualized at any given moment, and most actualizations are co-determined by multiple mechanisms in open systems).
  - [VERIFY: confirmed | bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008 | p.~13 | nlm-excerpt:"Mechanisms, events and experiences thus constitute three overlapping domains of reality, viz. the domains of the real, the actual and the empirical" — NLM source UUID 15591783 + cb8db854; p.46 (Table 1.1) + p.2 (Table 0.1) — page confirmed 2026-05-05]

- **Empirical (`de`)** — the domain of *experiences*: events that are actually observed/perceived/recorded by some subject. The empirical is the smallest domain, a proper subset of the actual, which is itself a proper subset of the real.
  - [VERIFY: confirmed | bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008 | p.~13 | nlm-excerpt:"Mechanisms, events and experiences thus constitute three overlapping domains of reality, viz. the domains of the real, the actual and the empirical" — NLM source UUID 15591783 + cb8db854; p.46 (Table 1.1) + p.2 (Table 0.1) — page confirmed 2026-05-05]

The relation is one of *strict inclusion*: `de ⊆ da ⊆ dr`. Bhaskar's foundational critique of empiricism (and of positivism's "epistemic fallacy") is that it collapses `dr` into `de` — treating reality as exhausted by what can be observed. Critical realism insists on the irreducibility of mechanisms (`dr`) to events (`da`) and of events to experiences (`de`).

### Application to thesis (universe / world / metaverse)
- **Universe** (in the thesis's vocabulary) maps onto Bhaskar's `dr` — generative mechanisms (physical, biological, cosmic) that exist mind-independently and operate transfactually.
- **World** (human-constructed meaning-space) is *not* the empirical layer. It is itself a real-level cultural-symbolic stratum (see §3, Archer below) — emergent from but irreducible to the universe-stratum, with its own causal powers.
- **Metaverse** is a new real-level stratum: a neo-artificial layer of generative mechanisms (computational, infrastructural, algorithmic) whose powers operate transfactually on humans whether or not any given user observes or "experiences" them.
- The **squeeze hypothesis** is therefore a *real-level causal claim* about the interaction of three real-level strata, not a generalization over empirical observations. Geographic and cultural variation belong to the empirical/actual layer (different actualizations); the squeeze itself is the underlying mechanism.

## 2. Bhaskar — Laminated Ontology

### Primary source
- Concept developed across Bhaskar's later work, especially **`bhaskarEnlightenedCommonSense2016`** (Bhaskar & Hartwig, 2016) and **`bhaskarPossibilityNaturalismPhilosophical1998`** (Bhaskar, *The Possibility of Naturalism*, 3rd ed., Routledge, 1998 [orig. 1979]).
- Source-manuscript `20260313交換様式の地平を掘り下げる` §1 already invokes Bhaskar's laminated ontology as the warrant for the universe/world nesting (see ANNOT-L2 line 91).

### Concept (paraphrase)
A **laminated system** is a real-world object or process whose adequate explanation requires invoking generative mechanisms operating *simultaneously at multiple ontological strata* — physical, biological, psychological, social-relational, cultural, normative — none of which can be reduced to the others. Lamination is Bhaskar's later move beyond simple stratification: the strata are not merely "stacked" but *co-active and mutually conditioning* in any concrete event.

For the thesis: the human predicament under metaverse expansion is paradigmatically a laminated phenomenon — physical embodiment, biological need, social relation, cultural meaning, and infrastructural mediation are all simultaneously implicated. No single stratum (technology, economy, culture, biology) explains the squeeze on its own.

## 3. Archer — Culture as Real-Level

### Primary sources
- **`margarets.archerRealistSocialTheory1995`** — Archer, Margaret S. *Realist Social Theory: The Morphogenetic Approach*. Cambridge: CUP, 1995. (The systematic statement of analytical dualism applied to structure/agency.)
- **`archerCultureAgencyPlace1996`** — Archer, Margaret S. *Culture and Agency: The Place of Culture in Social Theory*. Cambridge: CUP, 1996 [revised ed.; orig. 1988]. (The systematic application to culture specifically — most directly relevant to Ch-3-7 A1.)

### Analytical dualism
Archer's *analytical dualism* (against Giddens's "duality" / structuration) holds that **structure and agency** — and, separately, **culture and agency** — are *analytically* distinguishable real strata even though they are *empirically* always co-implicated. The distinction is not a matter of substance dualism but of explanatory irreducibility: structural (and cultural) properties have causal powers (`dr`-level mechanisms in Bhaskar's sense) that are emergent from past human activity but, once emergent, operate independently of and conditioning upon present agents.

[VERIFY: confirmed | margarets.archerRealistSocialTheory1995 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"This conception is 'analytical dualism' and it is based on two premises. Firstly, it depends upon an ontological view of the social world as stratified, such that the emergent properties of structures and agents are irreducible to one another, meaning that in principle they are analytically separable. Secondly, it asserts that given structures and agents are also temporally distinguishable..." — NLM source UUID e82d5224-f57a-49d2-aed1-76fdee8ead33 (Archer notebook 8f37548c); locate page in Zotero PDF. Additional excerpt: "Structures (as emergent entities) are not only irreducible to people, they pre-exist them, and people are not puppets of structures because they have their own emergent properties which mean they either reproduce or transform social structure, rather than creating it." ⚠️ Note: citekey `margarets.archerRealistSocialTheory1995` has a dot-prefix anomaly — may need cleaning before final bibliography export.]

### Why culture is "real" (not merely empirical or actual)
Archer's *Culture and Agency* (1996, esp. Ch. 5–6) argues against:
- (a) the "Myth of Cultural Integration" (Parsons, classical anthropology) — which treats culture as a seamless whole and so cannot account for cultural change; and
- (b) downward conflationism (cultural reduction to the social) and upward conflationism (treating culture as merely epiphenomenal of agents' beliefs).

Her positive thesis: the **Cultural System** (CS) — the set of intelligible items (propositions, theories, doctrines, beliefs) standing in *logical relations of contradiction or complementarity* with one another — exists as a real-level emergent property, irreducible to and analytically separable from **Socio-Cultural** (S-C) interaction (the empirical layer of who-believes-what-when-with-whom). The CS has causal powers: its internal contradictions and complementarities *condition* (without determining) the situations agents face and the strategies available to them.

This is exactly what the thesis needs: **"world" (in the universe/world/metaverse triad) is not a merely empirical or interpretive layer; it is a real-level cultural-symbolic stratum with its own emergent causal powers**, irreducible to either the universe-stratum (physical mechanisms) or to individual minds (psychological states). The squeeze is therefore not a perceptual artifact but a causal interaction across real strata.

## 4. Counter- / Qualification Sources (light touch — full development in Cycle 4 V1)

- **`sayerMethodSocialScience2010`** — Sayer, Andrew. *Method in Social Science: A Realist Approach*. Routledge, 2010 [revised; orig. 1984/1992]. Sayer accepts Bhaskar's stratified ontology but qualifies/operationalizes it for empirical social science: emphasizes the distinction between *necessary* and *contingent* relations among objects; insists that mechanisms are always activated in *concrete*, *open-system* conjunctures, so empirical predictions are not entailed by the existence of mechanisms. Useful in V1 to forestall the objection that CR over-claims about real-level causation.
  - Also relevant: **`sayerNormativityNaturalismIf2019`** — Sayer's normative-realist extension; not central to ANNOT-L3 but flag for Ch-3-7 if normative grounding becomes a counterpoint.
- **Pragmatist objection** (Rorty, Putnam, Brandom) to depth-ontology: argues CR's "real mechanisms" hypostasize what is methodologically a useful posit. Not in current Zotero — see §6 [LOOKUP] flags.
- **Latour / actor-network** flat-ontology objection: rejects stratification altogether in favor of symmetry between human and nonhuman actants. Likely already represented in thesis-sources (Latour items not searched here); flag for V1 if needed.
