# §IID — The Double-Edgedness of Artificiality

## §IID.1 — The convergent structural claim

The typology of §IIC establishes *what kinds of artificiality there are*: four cells under two axes, one cell newly populated at scale. This subsection takes up the diagnostic question the typology motivates: *given that cell 4 has been newly populated at scale, what follows for the artificiality field as a whole?* The answer draws on five canonical traditions developed in pre-metaverse settings — late-modern French philosophy of technics, mid-century anti-institutional sociology, Weimar-exilic German critical theory, contemporary Japanese ethological anthropology, and classical Chinese ethics — each of which independently diagnosed the same structural property of artificiality-at-scale.

The structural claim, in the most compressed form: **artificialities at sufficient scale or depth of social embedding tend to generate the conditions for the harm they were instituted to relieve, absent active counter-work.** Each tradition names a different *mechanism of inversion*, a different *counter-work* required to prevent the inversion, and a different *failure-state* when the counter-work is absent or inadequate.

| Tradition | Mechanism of inversion | Required counter-work | Failure-state |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pharmakon (Stiegler ← Plato) | Every artificiality is simultaneously remedy and poison; the disposition is settled by the regime of care, not by the artificiality itself | *Care* (le soin); transindividuation; deliberate cultivation of therapeutic relation | Toxic pharmakon: drift toward poison-dominance under exploitative time-economies |
| Two watersheds (Illich) | Tools cross thresholds; past the second, they invert their intended function (counterproductivity); radical monopoly eliminates non-platform pathways | Threshold-vigilance; conviviality; scale-limitation | Radical monopoly: structural elimination of alternatives |
| Dialectic of Enlightenment (Adorno/Horkheimer) | Reason that emancipates from mythic domination becomes instrumental reason, which produces administered domination as its consummation | Dialectical critique; non-identity thinking; aesthetic refusal | The administered world: total instrumentalization |
| Self-domestication / self-petification (Obara) | Humans constructing 人為的生態系 (artificial ecosystems) become subject to *auto-artificial selection* within their own constructions; biological capacities atrophy under capsulization | Preserve biological-ecological exposure; maintain capacity-exercise that artificiality cannot register | Self-petification: humans become "pets" of their own systems; biological hardiness, autonomic regulation, and somatic competence rot in sealed environments |
| 君子不器 (*Analects* 2.12) | Artificiality at institutional scale tends to reduce humans to 器 (vessels / instruments) within its selection criteria | 修身 (self-cultivation); deliberate development of capacities not reducible to instrumental function | Generalized 器化: human-being-ness collapses into the dimensions the artificiality can register |

The convergence across these five traditions is not coincidence. It is independent diagnosis of a structural property of artificiality-at-scale — a property the thesis treats as load-bearing for its later argument that the metaverse-stage squeeze is not an avoidable misuse of cell-4 artificiality but a structural-typological consequence of cell-4 artificiality becoming dominant.

The single structural claim is stronger than the familiar "tools have unintended consequences" thesis: *the inversion is internal to the artificiality's own logic; it does not require external misuse.* The pre-metaverse squeeze defined by `20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` §1.3 (universe-originated ecosystems vs. human-created artificial ecosystems, applying to both human and animal cases) is the squeeze condition these five traditions were diagnosing in their respective settings; the metaverse-stage radicalizes their diagnoses in five distinct but convergent modes specified in §IID.3.

## §IID.2 — Five diagnoses

### Pharmakon (Stiegler reading Plato)

**Pre-metaverse diagnosis.** In *Phaedrus*, Plato has Socrates report the Egyptian myth of Theuth and Thamus: writing is offered as a remedy (*pharmakon*) for memory, and Thamus warns that the same artifice is a poison to memory, replacing the living recollection with the dead inscription. The artificiality is pharmacologically undetermined at the moment of inscription. Stiegler radicalizes the Platonic insight: *every* artificiality, once exteriorized from the human body, gains independence from its user and so can return as toxic. In *States of Shock*, he gives the operative definition: "I have introduced the concept of tertiary retention in order to designate artificial retentions – mnemotechnics in general" — the material exteriorization of mnesic content that constitutes culture itself. The pre-metaverse case Stiegler analyzes most extensively is print-stage grammatization (a cell-3 case under §IIC's typology — nonmaterial-causally-efficacious artificiality borne by inscribed material substrate). Print extended cognitive capacity and standardized memory; it also progressively displaced oral memory practices and made the cognitive habit non-recoverable for many readers. The pharmakon is undetermined until a regime of care intervenes to keep it therapeutic.

**Counter-work.** Stiegler's *otium* — leisure-time devoted to care, transindividuation, deliberate development of therapeutic relations to artificiality — against *negotium* (exploitative time-economies). The pharmacological disposition is determined by the regime, not by the artifact. As he writes in *Technics and Time, 3*: "Tertiary retention is in the most general sense the prosthesis of consciousness without which there could be no mind, no recall, no memory of a past that one has not personally lived, no culture." The same prosthesis can sustain or dissolve consciousness — the regime of care determines which.

**Metaverse-stage radicalization — *rate-compression*.** The pharmakon now mutates at platform-development speed (months); the care-regime that determines its disposition operates at biographical and intergenerational speed (decades). The mismatch was contingent in the print case (literacy could in principle be paced to allow care). It becomes structural in the cell-4 case because the artificiality's own optimization-loops outrun any deliberative practice that could constitute care. Algorithmic grammatization of attention and behavior is the case where the regime of care is *most* absent — not because users do not care, but because the rate of mutation forecloses care-time. **Segue.** §V-5 deploys pharmakon as the analytical frame for metaverse-stage grammatization. §VI documents the rate-mismatch case-by-case (attention economy, recommender-system behavioral selection).

### Illich's two watersheds

**Pre-metaverse diagnosis.** *Tools for Conviviality* and *Medical Nemesis* name two thresholds for every tool or institution: the first watershed marks the point at which the artificiality begins to fulfill its intended purpose effectively; the second marks the point at which its effectiveness inverts, producing the opposite of what it was instituted for. Past the second watershed, the artificiality enacts a *radical monopoly* — it does not merely dominate the market for its function but eliminates the conditions under which alternatives could exist. Illich defines the radical monopoly precisely: a branch of industry "imposes a radical monopoly… by virtue of its acquired ability to create and shape the need which it alone can satisfy" (*Beyond Economics and Ecology*). Past the threshold, "a radical monopoly deprives the environment of those features that people need in a specific area to subsist outside the market economy… A radical monopoly paralyzes autonomous action in favour of professional deliveries" (*Tools for Conviviality*). Illich's pre-metaverse cases are paradigmatically cell-1 / cell-2 institutional artificialities: cars (which past the second watershed restructure cities so that walking ceases to be a viable means of transportation), schooling (which past the second watershed disables self-directed learning), medicine (which past the second watershed produces iatrogenic disease at rates that overtake its therapeutic benefit), mass media. This cumulative second-watershed crossing across cell-1/2 domains *is* the pre-metaverse squeeze condition in the post-2026-05-12 lock's terms — the human-created artificial ecosystem becoming progressively incommensurable with universe-originated conditions.

**Counter-work.** Threshold-vigilance, scale-limitation, conviviality. Illich treats these as institutional design problems: tools and institutions must be sized and structured so that the second watershed remains diagnosable from within the dominant configuration.

**Metaverse-stage radicalization — *three convergent modes*.** (i) *Typology-cell migration*: second-watershed dynamics extended from cell-1/2 to cell-4 artificialities. (ii) *Rate compression*: Illich's cases took roughly 100–150 years to cross the second watershed; cell-4 cases compress this to ~10–15 years (the social-media radicalization arc; the algorithmic-recommendation behavioral-selection arc). (iii) *Radical-monopoly extension*: pre-metaverse radical monopoly captured specific functions (mobility, learning, healing); metaverse-stage radical monopoly captures generalized participation, recognition, and livelihood — the platform mediates the conditions for any further function. **Segue.** §III-8 deploys Illich for modal mode-failure analysis. §VI-8 develops the car-restructured city as the worked pre-metaverse case (the analytical material previously housed in Ch-5-4 / skyscraper has been absorbed here per the 2026-05-14 Ch-5-4 decision; see `30Drafts/V-4_skyscraper-disposition-domination.md` superseded note); the metaverse case extends the same analytical machinery to cell-4.

### Dialectic of Enlightenment (Adorno / Horkheimer)

**Pre-metaverse diagnosis.** Adorno and Horkheimer's claim is sharper than "Enlightenment had a dark side": *the form of rationality* that produces emancipation is dialectically identical to the form that produces administered domination. "Myth is already enlightenment, and enlightenment reverts to mythology." Their pre-metaverse cases are the cultural-industrial artificialities of mid-20th-century capitalism — the culture industry, Fordist production, bureaucratically administered welfare and warfare, the standardized mass-media subject. Instrumental reason consummated as the administered subset of social life. In the §IIC typology, these are cell-2 and cell-3 artificialities (material-with-conventional-norms; nonmaterial-causally-efficacious) operating under the regulatory ideal of cell-4 logic — even though cell 4 had no sustained population in their period.

**Counter-work.** Dialectical critique, non-identity thinking, micrological attention, aesthetic refusal. Late Adorno acknowledges the difficulty: critique that uses instrumental rationality reproduces it; critique that refuses instrumental rationality risks irrelevance.

**Metaverse-stage radicalization — *scope totalization*.** Instrumental rationality reaches its operational consummation as algorithmic optimization: means/ends calculation operationalized at scale, with the "ends" specified by external value-functions (engagement, profit, prediction-accuracy) that the rationality itself cannot interrogate. The "administered world" Adorno feared was the *partial* domination of social life by instrumental reason; the cell-4-dominant world is the *total* domination — the "outside" Adorno hoped for (aesthetic refusal, non-identity thinking) is operationally captured (aesthetic recommendation, non-identity as data-point). On the Frankfurt diagnosis, this is not the corruption of cell-4 artificialities but their consummation: the engineered cell-4 artifacts are precisely the form instrumental rationality takes when it has access to operational power adequate to its conceptual ambition. **Segue.** §V-6 deploys the Frankfurt diagnosis for algorithmic governmentality as administered-world-specified. §VI-10 documents algorithmic governance evidence; the convergence with Foucauldian governmentality is the analytical bridge.

### Self-domestication and self-petification (Obara)

**Pre-metaverse diagnosis.** Obara Hideo's ethological-anthropological framework treats the human case at the biological-evolutionary register. Humans, unlike other species, have constructed sufficient artificial ecosystems (人為的生態系) that the species now undergoes *auto-artificial selection* (自己人為淘汰): the selection pressures shaping biological adaptation come from human-constructed environments rather than from natural selection. The dominant trajectory across the past 10,000 years Obara names *self-domestication* (自己家畜化) — humans selected for prosociality, reduced aggression, increased tolerance for crowding, and adaptive flexibility within constructed habitats, parallel to the domestication of wolves into dogs. The dynamic Obara identifies in modern industrial conditions is more severe: *self-petification* (自己ペット化) — total *capsulization* (カプセル化) of life within sealed artificial environments where biological capacities atrophy because they are no longer exercised. In *ペット化する現代人* (1995), Obara writes: "テレビや玩具、それにかもし出される人間どうしの関係、置かれているすべてのもの、ペット等々、こうしたすべての具体的な世界こそが社会であり環境でもあり、人間を「自己家畜化」する「道具」「モノ」なのである" (Televisions, toys, the human relationships they engender, all the objects placed around us, pets — this entire concrete world is society and environment, and these are the *tools* and 「モノ」 that self-domesticate humans). His coral-reef analogy is exact: humans secrete artificial ecosystems and then become embedded within them, like coral polyps inside their own reef, increasingly dependent on the very structure they built.

**Counter-work.** Obara's framework is more diagnostic than programmatic, but the implicit counter-work is the preservation of *biological-ecological exposure* — the maintenance of conditions under which biological capacities (somatic resilience, autonomic regulation, embodied attention, multi-species ecological relations) continue to be exercised rather than atrophied. The 庭イヌ vs. 座敷イヌ (yard-dog vs. lapdog) contrast captures the structural distinction: self-domestication preserving some exercise of biological capacity is sustainable; self-petification, in which capsulization is total, is not.

**Metaverse-stage radicalization — *capsulization completion*.** Pre-metaverse capsulization was partial: even the most domesticated modern human lived within an artificial ecosystem that still required *some* unmediated biological-ecological engagement (walking outdoors, embodied work, weather exposure, multi-species contact). Cell-4 metaverse artificialities enable the structural completion of capsulization — algorithmic mediation of attention, platform-mediated social relation, screen-mediated work, indoor-controlled climate, food-system mediation removing the seasonal-ecological cycle, biometric self-tracking replacing somatic self-sense. The metaverse-stage doesn't introduce capsulization (Obara diagnosed it pre-metaverse) but *completes* it at a register the biological organism cannot register as artificial: the capsule becomes seamless. Self-petification becomes generalized rather than confined to specific populations. The biological-capacity foreclosure Obara identified as a tendency under industrial conditions becomes an operational logic under cell-4 conditions: what cannot be platform-mediated is structurally defunded; biological capacities not registered by the artificial-ecosystem's selection logic are systematically lost. **Segue.** §VI-1, Ch-6-3, and lock §3 deploy Obara's self-petification trajectory as the human-side trajectory feeding the bidirectional coupling of 自己完結化 ↔ 自足完成化. §VI-2 (animals already squeezed) extends the auto-artificial-selection framework to non-human species under anthropogenic ecosystem reorganization.

### 君子不器 (*Analects* 2.12)

**Pre-metaverse diagnosis.** The dictum 君子不器 — "the cultivated person is not a vessel / utensil" — is among the most compressed statements of an anthropological diagnosis. The negation is positive: it names what human-being-ness is for (the cultivated development of moral-practical capacity not exhausted by specialized function) by naming what it is against (reduction to 器, the instrumentalized role within an institutional artificiality). The Confucian tradition contains long-standing analytical resources for diagnosing how institutional artificiality at scale tends to reduce human-being-ness to specialized function — Zhu Xi's 朱熹 elaboration of 君子 cultivation, Wang Yangming's 王陽明 critique of formalist bureaucratization, and the late-imperial scholar-critics (Huang Zongxi 黃宗羲, Gu Yanwu 顧炎武) who diagnosed examinatorial culture as an instance of 器化. Late-imperial Chinese history (the bureaucratic-mode crossing toward enforced 器化 under examinatorial conditions, with detailed historical treatment deferred to §III modal pluralism) provides a non-Western pre-modern case of the diagnosis operationalized. The pre-metaverse squeeze in the Confucian frame is the perennial 器化-risk under all artificiality at scale, with cyclically varying institutional defenses.

**Counter-work.** 修身 (self-cultivation); the 修齐治平 chain from self-cultivation to family ordering to state governance to world-ordering. The institutional supports for 修身 were not universal but had developed historical forms — Confucian education, ritual practice, the scholar-official's reading community, the 君子 ideal as regulatory.

**Metaverse-stage radicalization — *structural enforcement*.** 器化 ceases to be a tendency-under-conditions and becomes the operational logic of platform-legibility. What cannot be measured, optimized, or monetized is structurally defunded; the dimensions of human being that remain non-instrumentalizable — moral imagination, contemplative attention, non-platform-legible presence — receive no infrastructural support. 修身 loses its institutional anchors. The Confucian counter-work, which depended on slow temporal architectures (extended reading, ritual repetition, mentored self-examination), faces the same rate-mismatch the pharmakon does, but with the additional load of having no contemporary institutional home of comparable scale. **Segue.** §III modal pluralism deploys the Confucian mode as counter-example to the Frankfurt diagnosis's universal-instrumentalism claim — the Confucian mode of artificiality was structured to resist instrumental inversion, with different failure-modes (rigidification, examinatorial 器化) but not the Frankfurt one. §IV's anthropological stakes are made explicit via 不器. The conclusion's positive program is anchored here.

## §IID.3 — The five modes of radicalization

The five diagnoses converge on a single structural fact viewed from five analytical positions: **the metaverse-stage is the limit case of artificiality's tendency-toward-inversion, achieved by acquiring its own laws-stratum at cell 4 of the §IIC typology.** Each tradition names a different mode by which the metaverse-stage radicalizes the pre-metaverse inversion-mechanism it diagnosed:

- **Rate-compression** (pharmakon): the inversion-cycle outruns the counter-work-cycle. Care-time operates at biographical and intergenerational pace; pharmakon mutates at platform-development pace; the mismatch becomes structural rather than contingent.

- **Typology-cell migration + rate-compression + radical-monopoly extension** (Illich): the second-watershed mechanism migrates into a new typological cell (cell 4), accelerates by an order of magnitude or more (~10–15 years vs. ~100–150 years), and extends its scope from specific functions to generalized participation.

- **Scope totalization** (Frankfurt): the *partial* administered subset of social life becomes the *all-encompassing* world. Instrumental rationality reaches operational consummation; the "outside" Adorno hoped for is captured into the optimization function.

- **Capsulization completion** (Obara): pre-metaverse capsulization was partial — even highly domesticated modern humans retained some unmediated biological-ecological engagement. Cell-4 cell artificialities enable structural completion: algorithmic mediation of attention, platform-mediated social relation, screen-mediated work, indoor-controlled climate, food-system mediation, biometric self-tracking. The capsule becomes seamless; self-petification generalizes from population-specific to species-wide. The biological-capacity foreclosure Obara identified as tendency under industrial conditions becomes operational logic at cell-4 scale.

- **Structural enforcement** (君子不器): what was a tendency-under-conditions becomes the operational logic; the counter-work loses its institutional home, and 器化 becomes the default rather than the deviation.

These are not four separate phenomena. They are four analytical readings of the same structural transition: the artificial side of the universe-world-metaverse triad acquiring, at cell 4, its own real-level laws-stratum (per the squeeze-ontology lock's Aspect A) and operating on universe-law manifestations (Aspect B). §V specifies this transition's operational signatures; §VI documents its squeeze-effects.

The §IID convergence claim is the analytical warrant for treating the metaverse-stage as *the* limit case rather than as one more instance of artificiality-at-scale. Each tradition independently diagnosed a structural property that, at cell 4, reaches its consummation. The thesis claims novelty for the metaverse-stage without claiming discontinuity: the squeeze pre-existed (per `20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` §1.3); what is new is the four-fold convergent radicalization that cell-4 artificiality enables.

- **§VI (squeeze cases)** receives the four modes as the structural form of the metaverse-stage squeeze. §VI-8 deploys Illich's radical monopoly directly (with the car-restructured city as the worked pre-metaverse case, per the 2026-05-14 Ch-5-4 absorption decision). §VI-9 (Taleb fragility) reads as the operationalization of pharmacological toxicity at scale. §VI-10 (algorithmic governance) = administered world at the metaverse stage.
