## Problem claims (where conclusion exceeds structure)

### Problem 1: The generalized warning about self-completing systems

**Current formulation (approximately):** "Whenever we entrust something to a self-completing system, [consequence]."

**Why it exceeds:** The thesis analyzes one class of self-completing systems (the metaverse as Western Modernity's culmination). Generalizing to "self-completing systems" as a category is a new claim not derived from the body.

**Fix:** Replace the generalization with a specification. Instead of "whenever we entrust X to a self-completing system," say:

> What the analysis of the metaverse shows is this: once a mode of artificiality achieves the functional independence described in Section V — where its laws operate as background conditions rather than tools — the options available for responding to it are structurally constrained in advance. This is not a general law about technology but a structural consequence of the metaverse's specific architecture: incommensurability between universe-laws and metaverse-laws means no optimization within either domain resolves the squeeze.

---

### Problem 2: The "meta-verse as self-reflection" prescription

**Current formulation (approximately):** "The relativity of Western Modernity offers us a space to *meta-verse* (turn to take a step back, to self-reflect) the dominant mode of artificiality."

**Why it exceeds:** The body sections (§III) establish the *historical contingency* of Western Modernity's disposition. But this does not itself *derive* a normative recommendation to "self-reflect." The thesis has argued that WM is contingent, not that contingency implies the ability to step outside it.

**Fix A (conservative — as footnote if Liu is concerned about scope):**

> The historical analysis in Section III establishes only that the disposition to domination is not logically necessary — it emerged under specific conditions and has faced alternatives. Whether this contingency translates into practical alternatives under present conditions remains a question for subsequent inquiry. This thesis has aimed at an adequate diagnosis; the prescription is properly the subject of a separate project.

**Fix B (bolder — keep the reflection claim but derive it more carefully):**

> The contingency of Western Modernity's disposition to domination, established in Section III, has one practical implication that falls within the scope of this thesis: it forecloses fatalism. The squeeze is not the inevitable endpoint of human history but the outcome of a historically specific trajectory. This analytical finding — that things could have been otherwise, and in other traditions partly were — is the precondition for asking what structural alternatives exist. This thesis does not answer that question, but it establishes that it is a genuine one.

**Recommendation:** Use Fix B as main text; use Fix A as footnote alternative if Liu flags scope.

## Revised Conclusion (full version)

The following provides a complete revised conclusion that tightens derivation throughout, drawing explicitly on the body sections before making each claim.

---

### Revised Para 1 (summary of what has been argued):

The preceding argument has traced a single structural tendency across five domains. Section I established that worlds — human-made habitats of meaning and action — are nested within the universe and formed by the cumulative logic of artificiality. Section II showed that artificiality is not supplementary to human life but constitutive of it, while also being pharmacological: capable of poisoning what it enables. Section III located the disposition to domination as a historically specific mode of artificiality — an exception in the global record that has achieved exceptional global reach. Section IV identified the self-finishing tendency of human life under this mode: the drive to realize life-as-willed leads to a structural paradox. Section V showed this paradox at civilizational scale as the metaverse's self-completing tendency. Section VI argued that these two trajectories — universe and metaverse — have created a squeeze: a progressive structural incommensurability that is already observable in psychosomatic malfunctions, singularized livelihoods, and lives governed by opaque algorithmic law.

---

### Revised Para 2 (what follows — within the structure):

This analysis yields two conclusions that remain strictly within what has been argued.

First, the metaverse thesis is not a prediction but a structural description. The squeeze is not what *will happen* if the metaverse fully actualizes; it is what *is already happening* as the metaverse partially instantiates. Causal efficacy precedes completion (Section Ch-4-1). The gradient model — where squeeze severity scales with degree of self-completion — means that the analysis is already empirically verifiable at present levels of instantiation, not only at hypothetical future levels.

Second, the squeeze offers a more diagnostically adequate account of the human techno-social predicament than XR-centric or political-economy framings. It is more adequate not because it identifies more problems, but because it identifies the structural double-bind that makes the problems irreducible: both engagement and withdrawal from the metaverse are shaped by metaverse-laws. This is a different kind of problem from a problem that can be solved by better regulation, more competition, or platform exits — and the difference matters for how responses are designed.

---

### Revised Para 3 (what does NOT follow — tightening against excess):

What this analysis does not yield — and does not claim to yield — is a specification of practical alternatives. The diagnosis of structural incommensurability does not generate a solution. The historical contingency of Western Modernity's disposition, established in Section III, forecloses fatalism: the squeeze is not the necessary endpoint of human history. But contingency does not directly translate into availability of alternatives under present conditions. That question — what structural responses are possible given the squeeze — belongs to a subsequent inquiry that this thesis prepares rather than completes.

---

### Revised Para 4 (what the thesis contributes):

The contribution of this thesis is therefore diagnostic. By reconceptualizing the metaverse as the self-completing tendency of a historically specific mode of artificiality, rather than as a specific set of technologies, it makes the question of the metaverse's threat to humanity tractable in a way that XR-centric and disciplinary-reductive approaches cannot. The squeeze hypothesis names a structural condition; the three classes of evidence in Section VI document its current observable effects; the gradient model in Section IV specifies the relationship between present and projected effects. Whether this diagnosis is correct is an empirical question; how to respond to it, if correct, is a practical one. This thesis has attempted to make both questions askable.
