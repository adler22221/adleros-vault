# OS-Spectrum Elaboration: Searle's Taxonomy + Posthumanist Critique + Castoriadis Evaluation

## Purpose

This document elaborates the **OS-spectrum** (ontological-status spectrum) nodes with greater
precision by applying John R. Searle's taxonomy of social-ontological facts — brute facts,
institutional facts, status functions, and deontic powers — as an analytical layer. It then
evaluates where Searle's framework applies, where its anthropocentric premises require
posthumanist qualification, and whether Cornelius Castoriadis's imaginary institution remains
necessary after the Searle + posthumanist extension.

The OS-spectrum as currently defined runs from:
- **Node 0**: Simple organisms (cyanobacteria, slime moulds) — structural coupling, no intentionality
- **Node 1**: Social organisms — proto-semiotic Umwelt, eusocial signalling, primate hierarchies
- **Node 2**: Tool-making organisms — extended phenotype, niche construction, stigmergy, proto-artefacts
- **Node 3**: Human — self-domesticated, cumulative culture, language, full institutional reality
- **[Metaverse layer]**: Neo-artificiality constituted through the Node 3 technosphere

## Part I: Searle's Taxonomy — Analytical Apparatus

### 1.1 Brute facts

Brute facts are mind-independent features of physical reality. The mountain exists at height H
regardless of any human or animal attitudes, representations, or practices. Water has molecular
composition H₂O regardless of whether any organism knows this. Brute facts constitute the
**universe stratum** in the thesis's architecture — the real-level (`dr`) generative substrate
whose existence and causal powers are ontologically prior to any world-constitution.

[VERIFY: confirmed | searleSpeechActs1969 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"Such facts as are recorded in my above group of statements I propose to call institutional facts. They are indeed facts; but their existence, unlike the existence of brute facts, presupposes the existence of certain human institutions." — NLM source UUID a341de17-7f83-4279-9239-191da5604981 (Searle notebook 8186ca58); cit.3 in searle-institutional-facts-tier0-retrieval.md; locate page in Zotero PDF. ⚠️ Verify Zotero citekey: `searleSpeechActs1969` may need confirming against actual Zotero entry.]

### 1.2 Institutional facts

Institutional facts are observer-relative: they exist only relative to collective attitudes,
practices, and assignments. Money, property rights, marriages, corporations, and governments are
institutional facts. Their existence depends on collective intentionality — a form of "we intend"
that cannot be reduced to the sum of individual "I intend" states.

The logical form of institutional fact creation is the **constitutive rule**:
> **X counts as Y in context C**

A piece of paper (X) counts as twenty dollars (Y) in the context of the US monetary system (C).
The counting-as is the assignment of a **status function** — a function that the object cannot
perform merely in virtue of its physical features. The paper can perform the function of money only
because the relevant community collectively accepts that it does.

[VERIFY: confirmed | searleMakingSocialWorld2010 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"Characteristically, regulative rules have the form 'Do X,' constitutive rules have the form 'X counts as Y in context C.' Thus, for example, such and such counts as a legal knight move in a game of chess, such and such a position counts as checkmate. Applied to institutions, because Barack Obama satisfies certain conditions X, he counts as the president Y, of the United States C." — NLM source UUID 78e4b3dc-e285-4fb8-8ee3-ff91e07b70d6 (Searle notebook 8186ca58); cit.4 in searle-institutional-facts-tier0-retrieval.md; locate page in Zotero PDF. ⚠️ Verify Zotero citekey: `searleMakingSocialWorld2010` may need confirming against actual Zotero entry.]

### 1.3 Deontic powers

The most important consequence of status-function assignment is the creation of **deontic powers**:
rights, duties, obligations, permissions, and authorizations that attach to the role. A president
has the right to sign bills into law (deontic power), and citizens have the obligation to follow
those laws (reciprocal deontic power). These normative consequences are what give institutional
facts their practical grip on behavior — they cannot be reduced to regularities of habit or
conditional predictions.

[VERIFY: confirmed | searleMakingSocialWorld2010 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"we accept the status functions and in so accepting, we accept a series of obligations, rights, responsibilities, duties, entitlements, authorizations, permissions, requirements, and so on. As a shorthand, I call these deontic powers. […] These deontic structures make possible desire-independent reasons for action." — NLM source UUID 276308cc-dc01-4a90-8898-611cb8831c3d (Searle notebook 8186ca58); cit.1 in searle-collective-intentionality-tier0-retrieval.md; locate page in Zotero PDF. ⚠️ Verify Zotero citekey: `searleMakingSocialWorld2010` may need confirming against actual Zotero entry.]

### 1.4 Collective intentionality

Searle's account requires **collective intentionality** — a primitive, irreducible form of
"we intend" — as the ground of institutional reality. Crucially, Searle holds that this
requires **language**: "the capacity for language is necessary for the creation of institutional
reality" because status functions can only be assigned through explicit or implicit linguistic
expression.

[VERIFY: confirmed | searleMakingSocialWorld2010 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"Institutional reality can only exist insofar as it is represented as existing. Something can be money, a football game, a piece of private property, a marriage, or a government only insofar as it is represented as such… But the representation of these institutional facts always requires a language." — NLM source UUID 276308cc-dc01-4a90-8898-611cb8831c3d (Searle notebook 8186ca58); cit.18 in searle-collective-intentionality-tier0-retrieval.md; locate page in Zotero PDF. ⚠️ Verify Zotero citekey against actual entry.]

### 1.5 The Background

Underlying all intentionality (individual and collective) is the **Background**: a set of
pre-intentional capacities, skills, dispositions, and know-how that enable intentional states
to function but are not themselves intentional (not beliefs, desires, or representations). The
Background is the evolved biological substratum of social cognition — it is what makes the
status functions feel natural and self-evident rather than arbitrary conventions.

[VERIFY: confirmed | searleIntentionality1983 | p.142+ | nlm-excerpt:"The Background is a set of nonrepresentational mental capacities that enable all representing to take place. Intentional states only have the conditions of satisfaction that they do, and thus only are the states that they are, against a Background of abilities that are not themselves Intentional states." — NLM source UUID 01c10701-c901-4fff-b4e2-becee2835514 (Searle notebook 8186ca58); cit.11 in searle-collective-intentionality-tier0-retrieval.md; text appears at p.142+ (Background chapter). ⚠️ Verify Zotero citekey: `searleIntentionality1983` may need confirming against actual Zotero entry.]

## Part II: Searle Applied to OS-Spectrum Nodes

### Node 0 — Simple organisms (cyanobacteria, slime moulds)

**Searlean analysis:**

Only brute facts apply at Node 0. Cyanobacteria respond to chemical gradients through
structural coupling (Maturana-Varela autopoiesis) but possess no intentionality — no
representation, no purposive action in the philosophically loaded sense. They have no Umwelt
in Uexküll's sense (no subjective relation to a meaningful environment); they have only
biophysical transactions with a physical environment.

No status functions, no institutional facts, no deontic powers. The organism-environment
relation is exhausted by brute fact interaction.

**Posthumanist addition:** None required. Even the most expansive posthumanist accounts do not
attribute proto-institutional structures to prokaryotes or slime moulds. Niche construction
theory (Odling-Smee et al. 2003 [VERIFY: confirmed | odmogSmeeCDH-NicheConstructionNeglected2003 | p.1-2 | nlm-excerpt:"Organisms play two roles in evolution. The first consists of carrying genes...organisms also interact with environments...modify at least some of the natural selection pressures present in their own, and in each other's, local environments. This second role for phenotypes in evolution...We call it 'niche construction'...niche construction should be regarded, after natural selection, as a second major participant in evolution." — NLM A-Sources c68ca15e; ⚠️ no source UUID returned in query]) applies technically at this level (organisms modify selective
environments), but this is a brute-causal process, not an institutional one.

**OS-spectrum status:** Brute-fact substrate only. Pure universe-stratum.

### Node 1 — Social organisms (proto-semiotic / Umwelt / eusocial)

**Searlean analysis:**

Node 1 introduces meaningful environments: Uexküll's Umwelt (the organism-specific sign-sphere
in which features of the environment bear significance for the organism). Alarm calls in vervet
monkeys, pheromone trails in ants, waggle dances in bees — these involve functional
differentiation in which signals carry semiotic weight, altering conspecific behavior.

Standard Searle: these are **not** institutional facts, because (a) they lack language, and
(b) they lack the explicit X-counts-as-Y structure of constitutive rules. A vervet alarm call
for eagles is not the assignment of a status function; it is a signal that has evolved to
trigger escape behavior. There is no collective recognition that "this sound counts as a
warning" — there is only an evolved neural response to an evolved sound pattern.

**Posthumanist challenge — primate hierarchy evidence:**

The evidence from primate social structure pushes harder against Searle's boundary than the
eusocial insect case:

1. **Flack et al. 2006** (Pigtail macaque policing): A small coalition of "policing" males
   suppressed conflict among non-related individuals in a way that conferred functional
   authority on them — the group's behavior systematically deferred to the policing coalition
   in conflict contexts. When the policing males were experimentally removed, the social
   structure fragmented. This is functionally analogous to a status-function assignment:
   the policing males occupy a role whose deontic consequences (deference, restraint, legitimate
   intervention) are collectively maintained by behavioral recognition — without language.
   [RESOLVED 2026-05-05: Zotero 7SRYNNJ5 "Policing stabilizes construction of social niches in primates" (Flack, de Waal et al.) | nlm:[] — in Zotero, not yet nlm:synced; add nlm:pending for T0 retrieval]

2. **Sapolsky 2005** (baboon dominance culture): Following the selective death of aggressive
   high-ranking males in a baboon troop, a more affiliative social culture persisted for over
   a decade, transmitted through social learning. The social role expectations (lower aggression,
   more grooming) functioned as collectively maintained norms — proto-deontic expectations
   about how one occupies a social position. [RESOLVED 2026-05-05: Zotero IXFAMNSW "A Pacific Culture among Wild Baboons" (Sapolsky & Share 2004) | nlm:[] — in Zotero, not yet nlm:synced]

**Assessment:** At Node 1, proto-status functions emerge through **behavioral recognition
without language** — collective behavioral patterns that confer roles, expectations, and
functional authority on specific individuals. Searle's language-requirement is too strong here.
A revision is needed: status function assignment can occur through **collective behavioral
recognition** (not only through linguistic expression), with the intensity and stability of
the proto-institutional structure scaling with social complexity.

**OS-spectrum status:** Brute-fact substrate + proto-semiotic Umwelt + pre-linguistic
proto-status functions (behavioral recognition). No full deontic powers (no explicit
obligations), but functional role-deference as proto-deontic structure.

### Node 2 — Tool-making organisms (extended phenotype / niche construction / proto-artefacts)

**Searlean analysis:**

Node 2 introduces technical artefacts as extensions of the organism's causal powers (Dawkins'
extended phenotype) and niche construction as intentional-ish modification of the selective
environment. Crucially, Node 2 includes instances where **artefacts themselves acquire status
functions** — where an object's role is assigned rather than merely its physical function used.

Standard Searle: tool use alone is insufficient for institutional facts (a chimpanzee using a
stick to extract termites assigns no status function to the stick — it exploits a physical
function). The stick is used because of its physical properties, not because it "counts as"
anything beyond what it is.

**Posthumanist challenge — proto-currency and proto-ritual evidence:**

1. **Addessi et al. 2007** (capuchin token exchange): Capuchin monkeys could be trained to
   use tokens to "purchase" food items from an experimenter. The tokens function as a medium
   of exchange — X (grape-shaped token) counts as Y (food reward) in context C (exchange with
   experimenter). The capuchins generalised this to novel tokens and showed preferences for
   higher-value tokens. This is the clearest non-human analogue to a Searlean institutional
   fact: an artefact assigned a function it cannot perform by virtue of its physical properties.

obligations), but they exceed mere physical tool use. The X-counts-as-Y structure is present in
proto-form.

**OS-spectrum status:** Brute-fact substrate + extended phenotype / niche construction +
proto-status functions on artefacts + proto-deontic expectations. Not yet full institutional
reality, but genuine proto-institutional structures.

### Node 3 — Human (full institutional reality)

**Searlean analysis:**

At Node 3, full Searlean institutional reality appears:

1. **Language**: The recursive, compositional structure of human language allows status
   functions to be assigned explicitly, at a distance, to absent referents, and to categories
   rather than particular objects. "Anything that the Federal Reserve designates as legal tender
   counts as money" — this can only be stated and understood linguistically. Language enables
   the **declarative** speech act that changes reality by representing it as changed.

2. **Cumulative institutional hierarchy**: Institutions build on institutions recursively.
   Property rights presuppose a legal system, which presupposes a government, which presupposes
   elections, which presuppose citizenship, which presupposes a state. This recursive nesting
   is unique to the human capacity for decontextualised, language-dependent collective
   intentionality.

3. **Collective intentionality at scale**: Human collective intentionality extends to millions
   of non-acquainted individuals through shared linguistic representations (laws, contracts,
   narratives, brands). No primate colony can sustain institutional facts among strangers;
   human institutional reality is precisely the extension of the "we intend" beyond the scope
   of direct social recognition.

4. **Self-domestication as biological Background**: The reduced reactive aggression,
   increased prosociality, and enhanced perspective-taking produced by the self-domestication
   process (Wrangham 2019 [VERIFY: confirmed | wranghamGoodnessParadox2019 | Ch.7-9 (no page nos. in digital text) | nlm-excerpt:"compared with other primates, we practice exceptionally low levels of violence in our day-to-day lives, yet we achieve exceptionally high rates of death from violence in our wars. That discrepancy is the goodness paradox." — NLM source UUID c774be6d (A-Sources c68ca15e) ⚠️ UUID may conflict with Derrida OG attribution from earlier session — verify in A-Sources source list]; Hare, Wobber & Wrangham 2012 [VERIFY: confirmed | hareSelfdomesticationHypothesisEvolution2012 | p.578 | nlm-excerpt:"Together with reduced aggression and increased tolerance these constitute the domestication syndrome. Traits in the domestication syndrome occur with varying degrees of consistency and fall into four major categories." — NLM source UUID 27610fcb (A-Sources c68ca15e)]) constitute the evolved Background of human collective intentionality —
   the biological substrate that makes the "we" of institutional reality psychologically
   tractable for Homo sapiens specifically.

**OS-spectrum status:** Full Searlean institutional reality. Language-dependent status functions,
recursive deontic powers, institutional hierarchies, collective intentionality at unlimited scale.
This is the human "world" in the thesis's technical sense — the human-made meaning-space nested
within the universe.

## Part III: Searle's Limits — A Précis

The posthumanist evidence establishes three specific limits on Searle's standard account:

| Limit | Searle's claim | Posthumanist revision |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Language necessity** | Language is necessary for institutional facts | Proto-status functions exist through behavioral recognition without language (Node 1–2) |
| **Human uniqueness** | Only humans have genuine collective intentionality | Primates have proto-collective intentionality for role-assignment and proto-deontic expectation |
| **Anthropocentric Background** | The Background is human biology + human culture | The Background has deep evolutionary roots visible in non-human social cognition |

These are genuine revisions, not mere marginal extensions. They establish that the OS-spectrum's
gradations (Node 0 → 1 → 2 → 3) mark genuine ontological transitions in the complexity of
institutional-ontological structure — not a binary human/non-human divide — and that the thesis's
claim about the OS-spectrum as a continuum is defensible against a standard Searlean objection.

The revisions do **not** collapse the Node 2/3 boundary. Full Searlean institutional reality —
with its recursive nesting, linguistic decontextualisation, and deontic powers extending across
millions of strangers — remains a qualitative threshold, not merely a quantitative escalation.
The posthumanist evidence shows **proto-institutional structures** below the threshold, not full
institutional reality above it.

## Part IV: Does Castoriadis Remain Necessary?

### What the Searle + posthumanist extension achieves

After the extension, Searle gives us:
- An analytical account of **how individual institutional facts are constituted** at each node:
  the X-counts-as-Y schema, deontic powers, collective intentionality
- A spectrum from brute fact (Node 0) through proto-institutional (Nodes 1–2) to full
  institutional reality (Node 3)
- Posthumanist grounding that resists anthropocentric essentialism

### What Searle cannot give us

**Searle's analysis is compositional — it explains the building blocks.** It does not explain
**what selects which status functions get assigned**, **which world gets built**, or **which
meanings come to feel self-evident rather than arbitrary**.

The constitutive rule X-counts-as-Y can only operate within a prior meaning-horizon in which
Y makes sense as a category worth instantiating. Money (Y) only functions as a status function
within a social imaginary in which **value**, **exchange**, **property**, and **accumulation**
are already instituted as meaningful modes of social relation. The deontic power of the president
(Y = legitimate authority) only operates within a social imaginary in which **sovereignty**,
**representation**, and **political order** are already meaningful categories.

In other words: Searle's analysis is **synchronic** (how a status function is constituted and
maintained at a given moment) and **compositional** (how individual facts add up). What it
lacks is a **whole-system ontological account** of how an entire world — with its specific
evaluative horizon, its sense of what is real, what matters, what is worth doing — is first
instituted and maintained.

**This is Castoriadis's contribution.**

### The Castoriadis complement

Castoriadis's **social imaginary significations** (significations imaginaires sociales) are the
pre-reflexive meaning-schemas that constitute a society's world: not individual facts but the
entire horizon within which individual facts acquire their significance. They cannot be derived
from functional necessity (why *this* set of significations rather than another?) or from
individual intentionality (who "decided" that value and exchange are the primary categories?).
They are, on Castoriadis's account, the product of **radical imagination** — the society's
creative self-institution, which is neither determined by external necessity nor reducible to
individual choices.

[VERIFY: confirmed | castoriadisPhilosophyPoliticsAutonomy | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"institution is creation. It cannot be reduced to what was there before, nor to 'real' or 'rational' factors external to that society. It is the work of the radical social-historical imaginary, just as an original work of art is the work of the radical imagination of an individual." — NLM source UUID c4532a98-4edb-47f1-9171-c24d0fd82b52 (Castoriadis notebook 306bff73); locate page in Zotero PDF (Zotero item 846M7AVG). ⚠️ Verify identification: page evidence (p.52 "Itinerary", p.57 "Imaginary Significations", p.113 "On the Possibility of Creating a New Form of Society") suggests Philosophy, Politics, Autonomy (Oxford UP, 1991) — confirm against PDF before final citation.]

The relation between Castoriadis and Searle is therefore not one of competition but of
complementarity at different levels of analysis:

| Dimension | Searle | Castoriadis |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Unit of analysis** | Individual institutional fact (X-counts-as-Y) | Whole system of social imaginary significations |
| **Mechanism** | Collective acceptance of status function | Radical imagination as creative self-institution |
| **Mode** | Compositional bottom-up (facts build on facts) | Holistic (the imaginary is the ground of all facts) |
| **Ontological claim** | Observer-relative; requires collective intentionality | Creatively constituted; cannot be derived from function |
| **Temporal structure** | Synchronic (how facts are maintained now) | Diachronic + genetic (how worlds get instituted and transformed) |
| **Language** | Necessary for full institutional reality | Not the primary site; the imaginary precedes any particular linguistic form |

**A concrete example:** In a modern capitalist society, the Searlean analysis can explain how
a stock share is an institutional fact (X-counts-as-Y: this certificate counts as ownership of
1/N of corporate assets in context C), how the corporation is an institutional fact built on
the share, how the share exchange is an institutional fact built on both, etc. But it cannot
explain why **value**, **investment**, **growth**, and **ownership** are the categories through
which social life is organized — why these particular imaginary significations (rather than
gift-economy, mutual obligation, or subsistence sufficiency) constitute the background horizon.
That is Castoriadis's question, and it requires Castoriadis's answer.

### Verdict: Both remain necessary

**Searle + posthumanist extension** handles:
- The analytical structure of individual institutional facts across OS-spectrum nodes
- The scaling from proto-institutional (Nodes 1–2) to full institutional reality (Node 3)
- Resistance to the anthropocentric objection through biological evidence

**Castoriadis** handles:
- The whole-system ontological dimension: what makes a world (not just individual facts)
- The question of why certain imaginary significations (value, sovereignty, nature, God) come
  to constitute the background against which Searlean facts operate
- The genesis and transformation of institutional worlds over time (instituant vs. instituted)
- The closure and contingency of any given world — why it could have been otherwise

**In terms of the thesis's universe / world / metaverse architecture:**
- Searle explains the **micro-ontology** of world-constitution: how particular items within
  the human world acquire institutional status
- Castoriadis explains the **macro-ontology** of world-institution: how the human world as
  a whole is constituted as *this* world (with its evaluative horizon, its imaginary of nature,
  its conception of the person) rather than another

The thesis needs both because the squeeze argument operates at both levels. The metaverse is
a new layer of neo-artificiality that (a) creates new institutional facts on a massive scale
(Searlean level: new status functions for digital identities, virtual property, algorithmic
authority) and (b) threatens to institute a new social imaginary in which the pre-given world
progressively ceases to function as the background reality against which institutional facts make
sense (Castoriadian level: the imaginary of the self-completing society).

## Part V: Integration Into OS-Spectrum Definition — Proposed Additions

The following additions are proposed for the OS-spectrum definition to incorporate the Searle
and Castoriadis layers:

**For each node, add an "ontological-status dimension":**

| Node | Brute-fact status | Institutional-status | Deontic-status | World-imaginary |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| 0 | Physical substrate only | None | None | None |
| 1 | Physical + semiotic | Proto-status functions (behavioral recognition, pre-linguistic) | Proto-deontic expectations (role-deference, fairness) | None (no self-instituting community) |
| 2 | Physical + semiotic + artefactual | Proto-institutional facts (token exchange, proto-currency, proto-ritual) | Proto-deontic obligations (inequity rejection) | None stable |
| 3 | Physical + semiotic + artefactual + linguistic | Full institutional facts (recursive, decontextualised) | Full deontic powers (rights, obligations, legal standing) | Social imaginary significations (Castoriadis): the self-instituting world |
| Metaverse | Technically: brute (hardware); functionally: neo-artefactual | Full institutional facts + new status functions (digital identity, virtual property, algorithmic governance) | Deontic powers re-routed through platform governance rather than political sovereignty | Risk of new social imaginary (self-completion) that dissolves the world-stratum |

**Precision formulation for the thesis:**

> The OS-spectrum marks a series of real-level transitions in the ontological constitution of
> the organism-environment relation: from brute-fact structural coupling (Node 0), through
> proto-semiotic Umwelt and pre-linguistic proto-institutional structures maintained by
> collective behavioral recognition (Nodes 1–2), to full linguistic-institutional reality with
> recursively nested deontic powers and a self-instituting social imaginary (Node 3). The
> Searlean X-counts-as-Y schema identifies the micro-ontology of status-function assignment
> operative from Node 1 upward, qualified by posthumanist evidence that language is not
> necessary for proto-institutional structures. The Castoriadian social imaginary identifies
> the macro-ontology of world-institution that grounds the coherence of Searlean status
> functions within an evaluative horizon — an ontological dimension absent below Node 3 and
> threatened by the metaverse's self-completing tendency.
