## Key Passages — Relevant Content Found

### Passage 1 — Phantomization and temporal simultaneity (pp. 164–166)
> "At the same time, media sources (especially television) create a virtual contemporaneity with the whole world, i.e. a virtually shared world-moment. Originally, a constitutive element of imagery was the temporal differentiation between the image and that which it portrayed; it was always a recreation of its object. A simulation, however, goes hand in hand with simultaneousness of image and reality."

— Fuchs, Thomas. "The Virtual Other: Empathy in the Age of Virtuality." *Journal of Consciousness Studies* 21(5–6), 2014. p. 165.

**Relevance:** Virtual media impose a false "contemporaneity" — collapsing the temporal distance between image and reality, past and present, here and elsewhere. This is a temporal distortion: the lived body's time (grounded in the rhythm of presence, absence, and memory) is replaced by the frictionless "now" of virtual simultaneity. This is the conceptual building block for the body-time/virtual-time distinction.

---

### Passage 2 — Disembodied communication (pp. 166–168)
> "Phantomization... (b) disembodied or virtual communication... phantomization, i.e. the dissolution of differences between image, illusion, and reality... disembodied communication which shifts the modes of empathy towards the fictional pole at the risk of merely projecting one's own feelings onto the other."

— Fuchs, *The Virtual Other*, pp. 164–166.

**Relevance:** Virtual communication is *disembodied* — severed from the intercorporeal resonance that grounds primary empathy. The body's temporal rhythm (breath, gaze, co-presence, response latency) is stripped away in virtual exchange. What remains is projection without grounding — fictional empathy masquerading as real contact.

---

### Passage 3 — Intercorporeality as primary temporal ground (pp. 153–156)
> "Primary, intercorporeal empathy... the lived body as the primary medium of empathy... bodily resonance..."

The article opens by establishing that primary empathy is *intercorporeal* — grounded in the lived body's responsiveness to another body. This is the baseline from which virtual time departs: the body's time is the time of mutual presence, response, and co-affection.
