# §IIC — A Typology of Artificiality

## §IIC.1 — Recap and the question §IIC asks

§II opens with the claim that artificiality has *three intertwined aspects* (per revision DOCX intro, line 75): **technology**, **virtuality**, and the **laws of artificial environments**.

- **§IIA — Technology as exosomatization.** Following Lotka (1945) and Stiegler (1998, 2018), technology is the *exteriorization of organs and memory* to compensate for human biological weakness. Writing extends memory; weapons extend force; clothing extends thermoregulation. Per Stiegler, exosomatization "locally trans-forms the cosmic order in unpredictable ways" — forms *techno-geographical milieus* parallel to Obara's artificial ecosystems.
- **§IIB — Virtuality as causally efficacious nonmaterial artificiality.** Against Pierce's "as if" simulationism, against Chalmers' "computer-based" definition, and against Baudrillard's pure-simulation reading, the thesis defines virtuality as the *causally efficacious nonmaterial* dimension of artificiality, anchored on Giddens (social structures exist virtually) and Bhaskar (the virtual as a "sui generis real emergent stratum of reality with its own irreducible causal powers"). Examples across history: gods, money, laws, narrative, customary structures. Per Durante: "humans have always dealt with the virtual ever since humans lived by the stories they collectively invented." Per the author's #40 elaboration: virtuality's character is *substrate-independent* — shells, coins, digits, or biological circuitry are all merely temporal media for the underlying causally efficacious nonmaterial principle.
- **§IIC — Laws of artificial environments.** Artificial environments depend on and are constrained by the universe but are *not reducible* to it. The screwdriver/scissors example (verbatim from §IIC): a screwdriver simulated in a digital environment is *use-less* because the conditions for its functioning (gravity, physical fasteners, soreness of legs that desires a chair) are absent. The "physically fittest" example: in a world, charisma or wit may matter more than physical strength because the local organizing principles are not those of universe-laws.

Three committee questions converge on §IIC:

- **米 / II-2**: "rule比law更合適" — is "rule" not more appropriate than "law" when speaking of artificial environments?
- **米 / II-4**: "laws為何reducible?" — argue, do not assert, that the laws of artificial environments are not reducible to universe-laws.
- **Author / II-7**: "Rules? Laws? / Argumentation needed."

Per the author's answer to the examiner's "if world is in universe, where is virtuality?" (recorded in `claude-snippets.md` line 634): the nested structure is `universe [world [material artificiality + virtual artificiality]]`. World is composed of *both* material artificiality (technology) *and* virtual artificiality (virtuality). Both dimensions together constitute world; neither is reducible to universe-laws.

§IIC asks: **how are technology, virtuality, and the laws of artificial environments structurally related to one another, and what makes the contemporary moment structurally distinctive?** The answer is a typology that cross-cuts two axes — substrate and register — and identifies one cell that was effectively empty across most of human history but has been newly populated at scale by the metaverse-stage.

## §IIC.2 — Technology and virtuality: distinct dimensions, dialectically interdependent

Before the typology proper, the technology/virtuality distinction must be sharpened, because the two are easily conflated.

**Technology** (§IIA) is a *process* — the exteriorization of organs and memory. Its primary definition is *functional*: technology compensates for biological weakness. Technology can be paradigmatically material (a hammer, a wall, infrastructure) but can also produce or carry nonmaterial elements (writing-as-technology produces nonmaterial narratives; printing-as-technology produces nonmaterial discourses; code-as-technology produces nonmaterial algorithmic rules).

**Virtuality** (§IIB) is an *ontological state* — the causally efficacious nonmaterial dimension of artificiality. Its primary definition is *ontological*: virtuality is what is real-and-causal-but-not-material. Virtuality is paradigmatically nonmaterial (gods, money-as-concept, laws-as-concept, narrative, social structures); its material instantiations (shells, coins, digits, sound waves, biological circuitry) are *temporal media* of the underlying virtuality, not its essence.

**They are dialectically interdependent** (per §IIB): "the nonmaterial forms of artificiality are indispensable for the development of material counterparts and dialectically lead to each other." Without virtuality, material artificiality would be "just aggregates without being integrated into functioning systems." Without technology, virtuality would lack the temporal media through which it operates and persists across generations.

**They overlap but are not the same**:

- *Technology that produces virtuality*: writing → narrative virtuality; printing → discursive virtuality; coining → monetary virtuality; code → algorithmic virtuality. The technology is material; what the technology *produces* or *carries* is the nonmaterial virtuality.
- *Virtuality that uses technology as substrate*: contemporary monetary virtuality lives on digital ledgers; legal virtuality lives in printed statutes and digital filings; narrative virtuality lives in books and platforms. The technology is the *temporal medium*; the virtuality is the *underlying causal-efficacious-nonmaterial principle*.
- *Technology without specific virtuality*: a raw hammer, a brick wall, a steam engine considered purely as a material apparatus — these are technology without producing or carrying specific virtuality. (Stiegler would argue all technology is virtuality-implicated through tertiary retention; the thesis allows for paradigmatically-technology cases where the nonmaterial dimension is minimal or absent.)
- *Virtuality without specific technology-mediation*: oral myths, customary norms, body-enacted ritual — Obara's animal-ritual cases (gorilla chest-beating, crane courtship) — these are virtuality without dedicated technical mediation, though the body itself serves as the temporal medium. (Again, Stiegler would argue all virtuality is technology-mediated through tertiary retention; the thesis allows for cases where the technology-side is the body / voice / gesture rather than an external artifact.)

In what follows, the typology classifies *artificial organizing principles* — not specifically "technology" or specifically "virtuality" — by two structural axes. Technology and virtuality both map onto the typology, occupying different parts of it. Their dialectical interdependence is what gives the typology its analytical traction: the four cells are not isolated boxes but structurally-related positions in the artificiality landscape.

## §IIC.3 — Two axes for classifying artificial organizing principles

### Axis 1 — Substrate: material / nonmaterial

Does the organizing principle operate *as a material thing* (its compulsion is given by its physical instantiation) or *as a nonmaterial thing* (its compulsion is given by its meaning, with the physical instantiation merely the temporal medium)?

- **Material**: the organizing principle's compelling power is given by its physical-material properties operating under universe-laws. A wall stops you because of mass and structural integrity. A door opens because of hinge geometry. A locked door compels by the physical properties of lock and frame. The substrate is constitutive of the principle.

- **Nonmaterial**: the organizing principle's compelling power is given by its *meaning* — the rule, the value, the institutional fact — and the physical-material substrate is merely the temporal medium of operation. Money compels by what it means, not by the metal or paper or digits it inhabits. A god compels by faith and community, not by any specific material relic. An algorithm compels by what it computes, not by the specific server farm running it. The substrate is incidental; the principle is *substrate-independent* (per §IIB's argument; per the #40 DNA-programming elaboration).

**This is the same axis as substrate-coupling** (embedded in universe-substrate vs. decoupled from universe-substrate) but named in §IIB's vocabulary. Material = embedded; nonmaterial = decoupled. The axis carries the **technology / virtuality distinction in its substrate dimension**: technology is paradigmatically material (cell 1 and cell 2); virtuality is paradigmatically nonmaterial (cell 3 and cell 4); their dialectical interdependence operates across the substrate boundary.

### Axis 2 — Register: conventional / causally-binding `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]`

Does the organizing principle compel by ongoing human enactment (conventional register) or by its own structural operation (causally-binding register)?

- **Conventional register**: the principle compels because and to the extent that humans continue to enact it. A statute is enforced because police, prosecutors, judges, and prisons exist and act. A norm holds because the community sanctions violators. A market clears because participants honor contracts. A religious commandment compels because the faithful believe and the community enforces. **When practice ceases, the principle ceases to operate.** The Lessig modalities of *law*, *norms*, and *market* (`Code 2.0`) are paradigmatic.

- **Causally-binding register**: the principle compels by its own structural operation, regardless of ongoing human enactment. A locked door does not require an enforcer to remain locked. An algorithm executes regardless of which user encounters it or whether the user believes in it. A blockchain smart contract performs its conditional regardless of subsequent regret. Lessig's fourth modality — *architecture* — is paradigmatic. **Once instantiated, the principle operates transfactually**; revision requires re-engineering, not re-persuading.

The register-pair accommodates **both deterministic and probabilistic mechanisms**. A lock is deterministic; a recommendation system is probabilistic. Both are causally-binding in this technical sense: within the system, the user cannot override the principle's output by simply asking it to be otherwise. Probability does not make ML conventional; it makes the causally-binding output statistical.

The register is a **dominant** claim, not absolute. Pre-metaverse conventional artificiality has *causally-binding margins* (legal coercion enforced by state monopoly on violence; infrastructural lock-in; market compulsion that approaches structural inescapability). Metaverse-stage causally-binding artificiality has *conventional margins* (platform policies are revisable; community guidelines are contested). What §1.3 of the squeeze ontology names is the *progressive shrinking* of the conventional margin through 自足完成化 — initial decisions become harder to revise once the system reaches operational maturity; algorithmic systems become entrenched; auditability decreases.

## §IIC.4 — The four cells

The two axes cross to yield four cells of artificial organizing principles.

| | **Material (embedded in universe-substrate)** | **Nonmaterial (substrate-independent)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Causally-binding** `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` | **Cell 1** — Material technology whose compelling power is given by universe-laws: walls, locks, infrastructure, the built environment as Lessig-style architectural constraint | **Cell 4** — Nonmaterial virtuality with structural compulsion: code-as-law, algorithmic content moderation, recommendation systems, ML decision systems, blockchain smart contracts |
| **Conventional** | **Cell 2** — Material technology with conventional use-norms: tools whose use is governed by norms (a fork in dining etiquette; a ceremonial object); physical infrastructure subject to political revision (zoning, traffic conventions) | **Cell 3** — Nonmaterial virtuality with conventional enforcement: gods, money-as-institutional-fact, customary law, narrative, social structures, religious commandments, scientific paradigms, etiquette |

### Cell 1 — Material + Causally-binding: paradigmatic material technology

A locked door compels by the physical properties of lock, frame, and material. A wall stops you because of mass and structural integrity. Infrastructure shapes movement because of physical geography. These are *substrate-embedded* (the compelling power is given by universe-laws acting on the material substrate) and *causally-binding* (the compelling power does not require ongoing human enactment; the lock stays locked when nobody is watching).

Cell 1 includes both naturally-given features (gravity, terrain, weather) and humanly-engineered features (walls, doors, plumbing, electrical grids). What matters for the classification is that the compelling power is given by universe-laws operating on the substrate, not by ongoing human enactment.

### Cell 2 — Material + Conventional: material technology with conventional use

A fork is a physical object, but its proper use is governed by dining-etiquette norms. A ceremonial mask is a physical object, but its meaning is enforced by community convention. A road exists physically, but its "right-of-way" rules require ongoing enactment. A border is materially-instantiated (border posts, fences) but its function as a border is conventionally enforced (passports, border guards, treaties — all of which can be revised, ignored, or violated, with conventional sanctions).

Cell 2 is the cell of *technology subject to norm*. The artifact is material; the *rule* governing its use, ownership, or meaning is conventional. This cell is heavily populated by historical artifacts of culture: tools-with-use-norms, ceremonial objects, vehicles-with-traffic-rules, weapons-with-warrior-codes.

### Cell 3 — Nonmaterial + Conventional: pre-metaverse virtuality (the vast historical field)

This is the cell of §IIB's virtuality in its dominant historical form. **The vast majority of human civilizational virtuality, across all of recorded history until very recently, has occupied this cell.**

Examples per §IIB and per Harari's cognitive-revolution argument (claude-snippets.md line 1318):

- **Gods and religious systems**: causally efficacious (faith elicits emotions, rituals shape behavior, religious law constrains action) but nonmaterial; enforced by community sanction and the believer's ongoing belief
- **Money-as-institutional-fact** (the underlying virtuality of which shells, coins, paper, and digits are merely temporal media)
- **Laws-as-institutional-fact** (statutes are written on parchment / paper / digital files, but the *law itself* is the nonmaterial rule; enforced by ongoing apparatus)
- **Narratives and stories** (per Durante: "humans have always dealt with the virtual ever since humans lived by the stories they collectively invented"; orally communicated gods and embodied ceremonies)
- **Customary law and social structures** (per Giddens: social structures exist virtually); etiquette, kinship rules, status hierarchies
- **Scientific paradigms** (Kuhn): the paradigm itself is nonmaterial-causally-efficacious; enforced by ongoing scientific practice and community
- **Cognitive-revolution shared fictions** (per Harari): gods, nations, laws, money — "the nonmaterial scaffolding of the first human worlds" that "broke through Dunbar's number" by enabling cooperation among strangers
- **Animal ritual** (per Obara, per §IIB): gorilla chest-beating, crane courtship — proto-virtual systems, showing virtuality is not uniquely human and predates technological mediation

Cell 3 has been the operative virtuality-cell for all of human civilization since the cognitive revolution and possibly before (in animal proto-virtuality). It is *vast*; it is the *default* form of virtuality across history.

### Cell 4 — Nonmaterial + Causally-binding: metaverse-stage virtuality

This is the cell of virtuality that compels by its own structural operation rather than by ongoing human enactment.

Examples per the metaverse-laws specification of §V (V-3 v3 §1–§5):

- **Code-as-law** (Lessig): an algorithm's output is the rule, and the rule executes regardless of belief or consent
- **Algorithmic content moderation**: a post is removed by automated decision; the user's appeal is itself processed algorithmically
- **Recommendation systems**: the user cannot ask to see different content; the algorithm's probabilistic distribution is what determines what is seen
- **ML inference systems**: the model's decision is the decision; the user has no recourse within the system
- **Blockchain smart contracts**: once instantiated, the contract executes its conditional regardless of subsequent regret or social agreement
- **Platform affordances**: what the user *can* do is structurally circumscribed by the platform's design; what is not afforded is not negotiable within the system

Cell 4 has the same *structural-causal compulsion* as cell 1 (in cell 1, the wall stops you because of physics; in cell 4, the algorithm decides because of code) but operates in the nonmaterial register that virtuality has always occupied (in cell 4, the rule is engineered-nonmaterial — code's meaning, an algorithm's logic — not given by universe-laws).

## §IIC.5 — The structural-novelty claim: cell 4 was empty

Cells 1, 2, and 3 have all been occupied for most of human history.

- **Cell 1** has been occupied for as long as humans have built walls, roads, and tools — from the late Pleistocene niche-construction transitions onward; naturally-given Cell-1 features (gravity, terrain) precede humanity entirely.
- **Cell 2** has been occupied since the emergence of material culture with associated use-norms — i.e., as soon as humans produced artifacts and developed conventions about them, perhaps from the Paleolithic.
- **Cell 3** has been occupied since the cognitive revolution (per Harari, ~70,000 years ago), and animal proto-virtuality (per Obara) extends even further back. **The cell-3 population — gods, money, narrative, customary law, social structures — is the entire vast field of human civilizational virtuality across all of recorded history.**

**Cell 4 was effectively empty until very recently.** Across most of human history, *causally efficacious nonmaterial artificiality* compelled by *conventional enforcement* (cell 3), not by structural operation. A statute became operational because police and courts existed; a religious commandment became operational because the faithful enforced it; money became operational because all participants accepted it. The principle was nonmaterial and causally efficacious, but its compulsion was conventional — it required ongoing human enactment.

The metaverse-stage's structural distinctness is that **cell 4 has been newly populated at scale**. Code-as-law is virtuality (nonmaterial: the rule is the program's logic, not the silicon) that compels structurally (the program executes regardless of consent or belief). This is *new in human history* — not as a quantitative expansion of cell 3 (more virtuality), but as the *qualitative populating of a previously-empty cell* (virtuality acquiring causal-binding compulsion).

This is the structural fact that the squeeze ontology's §1.3 names as the "stratified two-environment configuration": the metaverse is a new real-level background environment because cell 4 is now occupied at scale, and cell-4-occupied virtuality has the structural-causal character that makes it function as a *real-level background environment* (parallel to cell 1's universe-laws) rather than as a derivative cultural-institutional layer (cell 3 occupies this position).

**Putting it precisely**: pre-metaverse virtuality was nonmaterial but conventional (cell 3); metaverse-stage virtuality is nonmaterial AND causally-binding (cell 4). This is what V-3 v3 §1.4 specifies as *Yang's 自足完成化*: artificiality acquiring the disposition to operate self-sufficiently, without continuous human direction. The cell-4 entry is the structural-cell-name for this disposition.

## §IIC.6 — Answering the committee questions via the typology

### On rule vs. law (II-2, II-7)

The terminological question "rule vs. law" admits a structural answer: the two terms have been doing double-duty for *different cells* of the typology.

- **"Law" in cell 1's context** names the physical-architectural laws governing universe-substrate (gravity, thermodynamics, biology). These are *universe-laws* operating on substrate-embedded artifacts. They are causally-binding by nature.
- **"Rule" in cell 2's context** names use-norms attached to material technology — etiquette, traffic conventions, ceremonial protocols. These are conventional.
- **"Rule" or "norm" in cell 3's context** names the vast conventional virtuality field — religious commandments, customary law, narrative structures, social roles, paradigms. These are virtuality-with-conventional-enforcement.
- **"Law" in cell 4's context** names engineered-causally-binding organizing principles — code-as-law (Lessig), algorithmic decisions, smart contracts. These are virtuality-with-causal-binding.

A defensible terminological convention:
- Use **"law"** for *causally-binding* principles whose violation is structurally prevented rather than externally punished (cells 1 and 4).
- Use **"rule"** or **"norm"** for *conventional* principles whose violation is externally punished but not structurally prevented (cells 2 and 3).

Under this convention, the metaverse genuinely has *laws of its own* — not a rhetorical extension of "law" but a structural homology with cell 1's universe-laws. The difference between cell 1's laws and cell 4's laws is that cell-1 laws are *naturally given* (operating by universe-substrate) and cell-4 laws are *humanly engineered* (operating by code-as-substrate). The structural-causal character — they compel without enforcer-intervention — is shared between the two cells.

Conventional and causally-binding are *registers*, not absolute classifications, so the convention has edge cases. Juridical statutes (cell 3 in dominant register, but with cell-1-like structural-coercion margins via the state's monopoly on violence) sit awkwardly between cells; this is a real fact about how juridical law has always tried to acquire structural-compelling power despite its essentially conventional nature. The metaverse's cell-4 character is therefore the *successful structural-compelling power* that conventional law has long aspired to and never fully achieved.

### On irreducibility (II-4, II-7)

Cell 4's laws are not reducible to cell 1's laws for the same structural reason that *a piece of software's semantics are not reducible to the physics of the transistors implementing it*: the substrate-embedded layer (the physical hardware) is necessary (the code must run on physical hardware; the algorithm needs servers and electricity) but *not semantically determinative* (the code's meaning is given by the program, not by the transistor physics). This is the **multiple-realizability** argument from philosophy of mind, formalized for artificial environments.

The viva-passed §IIC's screwdriver/scissors example precisely illustrates this irreducibility. A screwdriver in cell 1 works because of:
- physical fastener (a screw exists materially with helical threading)
- physical rotational mechanics (torque applied through grip → screwdriver tip → screw)
- gravity (objects to be tightened stay in place)
- soreness of legs (which makes chairs needed for prolonged use)

A screwdriver *simulated* in a digital environment fails to work *not* because the simulation is functionally limited but because the *conditions for screwdriver functionality are absent*: there are no physical fasteners, no gravity, no soreness. The digital environment has its own mechanisms for "tightening up" — algorithms, references, dependencies — none of which a simulated screwdriver could operate on. The screwdriver is *use-less* in the digital environment, not for technical reasons but for structural ones: cell 4's organizing principles are different from cell 1's.

The "physically fittest" example from §IIC pushes the same point. In a *purely biological* environment (cell 1), the physically fittest person has the best survival odds. In a *world* — an artificial environment with cell-3 or cell-4 organizing principles — the physically fit person may have a *higher* mortality rate (designated to hunting, war, heavy labor) while a physically unfit person uses charisma or wit to concentrate power and resources. The fitness function operative is different: cell 1 selects for embodied resilience; cells 3 and 4 select for capacities aligned with the artificial environment's organizing principles (rhetoric, social-network position, algorithmic-platform legibility).

The irreducibility is *structural*, not merely epistemological. Universe-laws (cell 1) constrain cells 2, 3, and 4 only at the substrate-implementation boundary (servers need power; bodies need food; institutions need humans) — not at the semantic-operation level (algorithms decide; institutions confer status; narratives organize meaning — none of these are derivable from universe-laws even though they are universe-laws-implemented).

This irreducibility is what the squeeze ontology's §1.3 names as the "two real-level background environments" configuration: universe (cell 1's laws + cell 1's manifestations) and metaverse (cell 4's laws + cell-4-mediated manifestations) each have their own real-level generative structures, neither reducible to the other. Cells 2 and 3 are *world-internal* organizing principles (still part of the *world* per the author's nested structure: `universe [world [material artificiality + virtual artificiality]]`); cell 4 is what makes the *metaverse* operationally separable from the world as a parallel background environment.

## §IIC.7 — Integration with the squeeze ontology (post-2026-05-12 lock revision)

### Cells, Level 1, and Level 2

The typology classifies *artificial organizing principles* (by substrate and register). The squeeze ontology's two-level squeeze (`20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` §1.3) classifies *subjects of the squeeze* (organisms at Level 1; worlds-as-systems-of-means at Level 2). The two classifications are orthogonal and combine cleanly:

- A Level-1 organism (human or other creature) navigates organizing principles from multiple cells: cell-1 universe-laws (gravity, climate, biology), cell-2 material-technology with use-norms (the tools of one's daily life), cell-3 conventional virtuality (the social structures, narratives, religious or paradigmatic frames one inhabits), cell-4 metaverse-stage causally-binding virtuality (algorithmic recommendations, platform-mediated access, ML decisions encountered).

- A Level-2 world-as-OS is constituted by a configuration of means drawn from cells 1–3 (pre-metaverse) and increasingly from cell 4 (metaverse-stage), with the cell-4 means progressively gaining apparatus-side independence from the world that produced them (the 自己完結化 ↔ 自足完成化 bidirectional coupling per §V §1.2; the Level-2 squeeze names the world's diminishment as its means migrate from internal-to-the-world cells 2 and 3 into background-environment cell 4).

### Aspect A and cell 4

Cell 4's structural-character is exactly what `squeeze-ontology.md` §1.3 Aspect A specifies: **conventional in origin** (the code, the algorithm, the smart contract is humanly engineered — engineered nonmaterial artifacts, designed and decided) **AND causally-binding in operation** (once instantiated, it compels without ongoing human enactment), **with conventional space progressively shrinking** through self-completion. The cell-4 occupancy is what makes Aspect A operationally significant; the typology gives Aspect A its cell-position-name.

### Aspect B and cells 1–3

Cell 4's organizing principles act on cells 1, 2, and 3 — the action of metaverse-laws on the universe-side manifestation field per §1.3 Aspect B (operation-terms `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]`: mediation, overlay, hybridization).

- **On cell 1**: smart-city infrastructure reorganizes physical-architectural constraints (traffic flows mediated by algorithms; sensor networks overlaying urban geography); algorithmic agriculture reorganizes biological-substrate management of livestock and crops; environmental monitoring reorganizes wildlife ecology.
- **On cell 2**: platform-mediated tools (e.g., gig-economy interfaces) reorganize how material technology is wielded; the proper-use norms of physical objects increasingly come pre-installed in algorithmic platforms rather than as conventional social transmission.
- **On cell 3**: cell-4 affordances progressively absorb or displace cell-3 conventional virtuality; algorithmic decision systems substitute for human judgment in lending, hiring, sentencing, content moderation; community-level moderation is augmented or replaced by algorithmic enforcement.

The typology gives Aspect B a cell-action signature: cell 4 → cells 1, 2, 3 is the structural form of Aspect B; whichever terms eventually replace mediation / overlay / hybridization will name specific kinds of cell-4-to-other-cells actions.

### Environmentalist-stratified commitment

The typology preserves the squeeze ontology's environmentalist-stratified commitment (§1.3 / §V §1.4): cell 4 is a new *real-level background environment* (because virtuality has acquired causal-binding structural-compulsion at scale), NOT a Hui-style organizing-inorganic *superorganism* that fuses apparatus and environment. The cells are *kinds* of organizing principles, not stages of a single super-environment. The squeeze hypothesis requires that cells 1 and 4 both remain real and structurally distinct; the typology codifies this.

### Anthropocene continuation

The Anthropocene literature documents that human worlds (operating from cells 1, 2, 3) have reshaped universe-law manifestations (cell-1 effects: climate, biosphere, ocean chemistry, geological-stratigraphic effects). ## §IIC.8 — Modal pluralism as cell-distributions (cross-reference to §III)

§III's modal pluralism — that the modern Western mode of artificiality is one mode among many — admits a typological articulation. Different historical and cultural modes of artificiality occupy different distributions across the four cells.

- A **forager mode** might be characterized by low cell-1 occupancy (limited built architecture), substantial cell-2 occupancy (tools with use-norms), high cell-3 occupancy (rich cosmologies, kinship structures, narrative traditions, ritual), and zero cell-4 occupancy.
- A **classical agrarian mode** would have substantial cell-1 (settlements, fields, irrigation infrastructure), substantial cell-2 (tools, vehicles, ceremonial objects with use-norms), heavy cell-3 (statecraft, religion, customary law, scholarly traditions, art), and zero cell-4.
- A **modern industrial mode** would have heavy cell-1 (factories, infrastructure, transportation grids), heavy cell-2 (industrial tools, household appliances, vehicles with regulatory rules), substantial cell-3 (bureaucratic law, market institutions, mass-media narratives, scientific paradigms), and emerging cell-4 in late 20th C (early code-as-law in finance, early algorithmic systems).
- The **contemporary metaverse-stage mode** has all four cells heavily occupied, with cell-4 becoming increasingly dominant and acting on cells 1, 2, 3 per Aspect B.

This is a *cross-sectional* typology of modes (cell-distribution at a time-slice); the *developmental trajectory* (why the modern Western mode shifted from heavy cell-3 reliance toward cell-4 dominance) is §IV's genetic claim about life-as-willed and self-finishing. The cross-sectional typology does not entail the trajectory; modal pluralism per §III remains preserved.
