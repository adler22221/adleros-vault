## Non-Digital Virtuality — The Continuum Argument

### The Core Claim

The thesis's argument that virtuality is not a product of digital technology requires evidence that representational systems — systems in which a sign layer mediates between information and material substrate — can be instantiated in non-silicon, non-digital media. This serves the historical continuum argument: virtuality is a feature of information-processing systems in general, and the digital metaverse is its current expression, not its origin.

### DNA as Paradigm Case

DNA is the original non-digital virtuality:

- **The software/hardware distinction in biology**: DNA encodes *instructions* (the sequence of base pairs) that are *read* and *executed* by the cellular machinery (ribosomes, transcription factors, enzymes) to produce proteins. The relationship between the DNA sequence and the protein produced is virtual in the precise structural sense: the sequence is a *representation* of the protein structure, not the protein structure itself. The cellular machinery reads the representation and produces the represented object — exactly the structure of all representational systems.

- **DNA computing** (Adleman 1994 and subsequent work) [VERIFY: Adleman 1994 *Science* — "Molecular computation of solutions to combinatorial problems" — check Zotero]: the demonstration that DNA molecules can be used to compute — to store, process, and retrieve information — by exploiting the hybridization properties of complementary base sequences. Information is encoded in molecular form; computation proceeds through molecular recognition rather than electronic switching. This is virtuality instantiated in biological chemistry rather than silicon.

- **DNA data storage**: DNA has been demonstrated as a storage medium for arbitrary digital information (synthetic oligonucleotides encoding binary data), with extraordinary density (~1 exabyte per cubic millimeter) [VERIFY: Organick et al. 2018 *Nature Biotechnology* — DNA storage — check Zotero]. The DNA molecule does not "know" it is storing text or image data; the information is virtual relative to the molecular substrate.

### Other Non-Silicon Virtualities

- **Molecular computation**: chemical reaction networks can be engineered to perform logic operations; the chemical system's behavior represents the computation's results without the computation being implemented in silicon
- **Quantum computation**: quantum superposition allows information to be processed in states that have no classical physical analog — the qubit represents a superposition of 0 and 1 states that collapses only on measurement; the virtual state is the computation, not the physical outcome
- **Wetware / biological circuits**: engineered gene circuits in living cells (synthetic biology) implement logical and computational functions through gene expression dynamics — the cell's gene regulatory network is a biological computer

### The Continuum Argument — Completed

The biological chain is now complete:
1. **DNA** (molecular biology): the original information-representation system in living matter — the virtual layer that specifies protein structures without being those structures
2. **Animal behavior** (ethology, S1-19): proto-virtual systems in which behavioral displays represent states rather than directly enacting them (gorilla chest-beat, crane courtship)
3. **Human world** (the thesis's central argument): the full-scale virtual layer constituted by language, institution, meaning, and collectively maintained meaning-space
4. **Digital metaverse**: the current technical extension of world-making virtuality into algorithmically managed computational substrates
5. **DNA computing / synthetic biology**: virtuality extending back *below* silicon into biological chemistry — demonstrating that the computational-representational capacity underlying the metaverse is not unique to silicon but is a property of information-processing matter at multiple scales

The argument: virtuality is substrate-independent. It can be instantiated in DNA, in neural circuits, in silicon, in social practices. The digital metaverse is not the invention of virtuality; it is the current technical apex of a representational capacity that evolution discovered billions of years ago.
