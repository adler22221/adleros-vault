# §IIC — A Typology of Technology and Virtuality

## §IIC.1 — The framing problem and its committee questions

§IIA established that human worlds depend on technical exteriorization (after Stiegler's tertiary retention) and §IIB established that virtuality is not uniquely digital (after Obara's animal-ritual cases and the broader continuum from gorilla chest-beating to DNA programming). What §IIC must now establish is the **logical structure of the organizing principles** through which artificial environments operate.

Three committee questions converge here:

- **米 / II-2**: "rule比law更合適" — is "rule" not more appropriate than "law" when speaking of artificial environments?
- **米 / II-4**: "laws為何reducible?" — why are the laws of artificial environments not reducible to universe-laws?
- **Author / II-7**: "Rules? Laws? / Argumentation needed" on the reducibility claim itself.

These three questions can be answered structurally rather than terminologically. The answer is a typology that distinguishes *kinds* of organizing principles by two axes, identifies which cells of the typology have been historically occupied, and shows that the metaverse-stage represents the first occupancy of a previously-empty cell. This typology is the analytical framework on which §III's modal pluralism, §V's metaverse-laws specification, and §VI's squeeze argument all rest.

## §IIC.2 — Two axes

The typology cross-cuts two structural axes.

### Axis 1 — Substrate-coupling

Does the organizing principle operate *within* the universe-substrate (subject to universe-laws of physics, chemistry, biology) or *decoupled from* it (operating in a domain whose semantics are not given by universe-laws)?

- **Embedded in universe-substrate**: the artifact's organizing principle operates by universe-laws. A wall stops you because gravity and material rigidity. A door opens because hinge geometry and metallurgy. Even when the artifact is humanly engineered, the principle that makes it operative is given by universe-laws.

- **Decoupled from universe-substrate**: the artifact's organizing principle operates in a domain whose semantics are constituted by the artifact itself, not by universe-laws. A line of code does not stop you because of gravity; it stops you because the runtime executes a conditional. A platform's content-moderation rule does not exclude you by the laws of biology; it excludes you by the platform's internal logic. The semantics of decoupled artifacts are *humanly produced* and *humanly altered*; their operation is not derivable from universe-laws (though they require universe-substrate to run, the universe-substrate is incidental to *what they mean*).

This is F4 from `20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` §2 (substrate-decoupling), now formalized as an analytical axis rather than as a property of the metaverse specifically. Substrate-decoupling is a *general structural possibility* of which the metaverse is a particular instance.

### Axis 2 — Organizing-principle register: conventional vs. causally-binding `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]`

Does the organizing principle compel by ongoing human enactment (conventional register) or by its own structural operation (causally-binding register)?

- **Conventional register**: the principle compels because and to the extent that humans continue to enact it. A statute is enforced because police, prosecutors, judges, and prisons exist and act. A norm holds because people sanction violators. A market clears because participants honor contracts. When practice ceases, the principle ceases to operate. The Lessig modalities of *law*, *norms*, and *market* (`Code 2.0`) are paradigmatic.

- **Causally-binding register**: the principle compels by its own structural operation, regardless of ongoing human enactment. A locked door does not require an enforcer to remain locked. An algorithm executes regardless of which user encounters it. A blockchain smart contract performs its conditional regardless of whether any party wishes it otherwise. Lessig's fourth modality — *architecture* — is paradigmatic. Once instantiated, the principle operates transfactually; revision requires re-engineering, not re-persuading.

**The register-pair accommodates both deterministic and probabilistic mechanisms.** A lock is deterministic; a recommendation system is probabilistic. Both are causally-binding in this technical sense: within the system, the user cannot override the principle's output by simply asking it to be otherwise. Probability does not make ML conventional; it makes the causally-binding output statistical. (`[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` flag: "causally-binding" is a working signpost; the term-choice will be revisited before final draft. Candidate alternatives include "architectural," "compelling," and "hard-constraint." See `20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` header note item 5 for the full discussion.)

Conventional and causally-binding are *dominant* registers, not absolute classifications. Pre-metaverse human-created artificial ecosystems have causally-binding margins (legal coercion enforced by state monopoly on violence; infrastructural lock-in; market compulsion) but the dominant register is conventional (institutions, norms, customs require ongoing practice to operate). Metaverse-stage organizing principles have conventional margins (platform policies are revisable; community guidelines are contested) but the dominant register is causally-binding (the code executes regardless of whether the user agrees), and **the conventional margin progressively shrinks as 自足完成化 advances** (see §V).

## §IIC.3 — The four cells

The two axes cross to yield four cells. The structural argument of this section is that all four cells are now occupied, but cell 4 was empty until very recently in human history. This is what makes the metaverse-stage structurally novel.

| | **Embedded in universe-substrate** | **Decoupled from universe-substrate** |
|---|---|---|
| **Causally-binding** `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]` | **Cell 1: Physical architecture** — walls, gravity-shaped roads, plumbing, locked doors, the built environment as Lessig-style architectural constraint operating by physics | **Cell 4: Metaverse-stage artificiality** — code-as-law, algorithmic content moderation, recommendation systems, ML decision systems, blockchain smart contracts, platform affordances |
| **Conventional** | **Cell 2: Juridical law and social norms** — statutes, custom, etiquette, market institutions, the Lessig modalities of law / norms / market operating by ongoing human enactment | **Cell 3: Online conventional norms** — community guidelines, forum etiquette, soft moderation, social-media norms whose enforcement remains conventional (human moderators, social pressure) even though the substrate is decoupled |

### Cell-by-cell examples and structural notes

**Cell 1 (Embedded + Causally-binding) — Physical architecture.** A locked door compels by the physical properties of lock, frame, and material. The wall of a prison compels by mass and structural integrity. The geography of a city — its grid, its rivers, its mountains — compels by the laws of motion and material. Lessig's "architecture" applied to the universe-substrate. Examples include both naturally-given features (gravity, terrain, weather, biological constraint on the body) and humanly-engineered features (walls, doors, roads, plumbing, electrical grids) — what matters for the cell-classification is that the compelling-power is given by universe-laws operating on the substrate, not by ongoing human enactment.

**Cell 2 (Embedded + Conventional) — Juridical law and social norms.** A statute compels because the legal apparatus exists and acts. A custom compels because the community sanctions violators. A market institution compels because participants honor contracts. The compelling-power is given by ongoing human enactment; remove the enactment, the principle ceases to operate. Substrate-embedded because these principles operate on bodies and goods *in* the universe — they constrain physical action without altering the laws of physics.

**Cell 3 (Decoupled + Conventional) — Online conventional norms.** Forum guidelines, community standards, online etiquette, soft-moderation cultures. The substrate is decoupled (the principles operate in a virtual domain whose semantics are not given by universe-laws), but the enforcement remains conventional (human moderators, social pressure, voluntary compliance). When a community rule is broken, the response is a human-mediated sanction (mute, ban, callout) rather than an automatic structural prevention. Many of the early-internet governance modes occupied this cell; some still do (Wikipedia's editorial culture; smaller forum communities; non-algorithmic peer norms).

**Cell 4 (Decoupled + Causally-binding) — Metaverse-stage artificiality.** Code-as-law (Lessig), algorithmic content moderation, recommendation systems, ML inference systems, blockchain smart contracts, platform affordances. The substrate is decoupled (the principles operate in a virtual domain whose semantics are humanly produced) AND the operation is causally-binding (the principle compels by its own structural operation; the user cannot override the algorithm's output by asking; the smart contract executes regardless of subsequent regret). This is the cell that Yang's 自足完成化 specifies as the apparatus-side disposition of artificiality (§V §1.1); it is the cell that V-3 v3 §1–§5 develops in detail; and it is the cell whose structural-architectural mechanisms (Lessig's architecture-as-regulation; Hui's productive-ontologies-via-associated-milieus; the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention) operate (V-3 v3 §2).

## §IIC.4 — The structural-novelty claim: cell 4 was empty

Cells 1, 2, and 3 have all been occupied for millennia.

- Cell 1 has been occupied for as long as humans have built walls, roads, and tools — i.e., from the late Pleistocene niche-construction transitions onward, and naturally-given Cell-1 features (gravity, terrain) precede humanity entirely.
- Cell 2 has been occupied since the emergence of customary and statutory law — for any complex human society.
- Cell 3 has been occupied since at least the emergence of literate culture (early correspondence networks, scholarly republics) and was extensively occupied during the early internet (Usenet, early Web, BBS communities, mailing-list cultures, the pre-platform internet generally).

**Cell 4 is the structurally novel position.** It became operationally significant with the conjunction of (i) sufficient computational substrate to enable real-time algorithmic enforcement, (ii) platform business models that monetize friction-elimination, and (iii) AI's recent achievement of protentional sufficiency at scale (V-3 v3 §3.4). This is a recent and ongoing transition; cell 4 was effectively empty as recently as the 1990s and is now the structurally dominant cell of human meaningful action.

**This is not a quantitative claim about cell-4's expansion** (more code, more platforms, more algorithmic governance); it is a *qualitative* claim about cell-4's *being occupied at all* in a structurally significant way. The squeeze ontology's §1.3 stratified two-environment configuration — the metaverse as a second real-level background environment parallel to the universe — depends on cell-4 being occupied: only when an artificial-side domain is *both* decoupled from universe-substrate AND causally-binding does it constitute a genuine real-level environment with its own laws rather than a derivative cultural-institutional layer atop universe-laws.

## §IIC.5 — Answering the committee questions

### On rule vs. law (II-2, II-7)

The typology dissolves the rule/law dichotomy by showing that both terms have been doing double-duty for *different cells*.

- "Law" in cell 1's context names the **physical-architectural laws** governing universe-substrate (gravity, thermodynamics, biology). These are universe-laws operating on substrate-embedded artifacts.
- "Law" in cell 2's context names **juridical law** — statutes operating through conventional enforcement. These are nomos in the classical sense.
- "Rule" in cell 3's context names **virtual conventions** — community guidelines, forum etiquette, soft moderation. These are nomos applied to decoupled substrate.
- "Law" in cell 4's context names **engineered-causally-binding organizing principles** — code-as-law in Lessig's sense, algorithmic affordances. These are humanly produced (nomos-origin) but operate transfactually (with the compelling power that universe-laws have in cell 1) — what §V calls the conventional-origin + causally-binding-operation register-transgression.

A defensible terminological convention:
- Use **"law"** for transfactually-operating principles whose violation is structurally prevented rather than externally punished (cells 1 and 4).
- Use **"rule"** or **"norm"** for practice-enacted principles whose violation is externally punished but not structurally prevented (cells 2 and 3).

Under this convention, the metaverse genuinely has *laws of its own* — not a rhetorical extension of "law" but a structural homology with cell 1's universe-laws (with the difference that cell 4's laws are humanly engineered rather than naturally given). The committee question "rule vs. law" is not a verbal dispute but a request for this terminological clarification, which the typology supplies.

### On irreducibility (II-4, II-7)

Cell 4's laws are not reducible to cell 1's laws for the same structural reason that a piece of software's semantics are not reducible to the physics of the transistors implementing it: the substrate-embedded layer is *necessary* (the code must run on physical hardware) but *not semantically determinative* (the code's meaning is given by the program, not by the transistor physics). This is the **multiple-realizability** insight from philosophy of mind, formalized for artificial environments.

A concrete instance: a content-moderation algorithm decides whether to remove a post by computing a probability based on training data. The decision is *implemented on* universe-substrate (servers running CPU operations under thermodynamic constraints), but the *content* of the decision (this post stays; that post goes) is not derivable from the thermodynamics. It is derivable only from the training data, the model architecture, and the prompt — all of which are decoupled-substrate phenomena. The causal-binding force of the decision (when the algorithm decides to remove, the post is removed) operates regardless of whether any human enacts the removal. So cell 4: decoupled + causally-binding.

The irreducibility is structural, not merely epistemological. Universe-laws (cell 1) cannot determine cell-4 laws because cell-4 laws operate at a stratum decoupled from universe-substrate semantics. Universe-laws constrain cell-4 laws only at the substrate-implementation boundary (the servers need power; the chips have a temperature ceiling; physical sabotage of the data center destroys the system) — not at the semantic-operation level (the algorithm's decisions are not given by physics even though they are physics-implemented).

This irreducibility is what the squeeze ontology's §1.3 names as the "two real-level background environments" configuration: universe and metaverse each have their own real-level generative structures, neither reducible to the other.

## §IIC.6 — Integration with the squeeze ontology (post-2026-05-12 revision)

### Cells, Level 1, and Level 2

The typology classifies *organizing principles* (kinds of laws and rules). The squeeze ontology's two-level squeeze (`20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` §1.3) classifies *subjects of the squeeze* (organisms at Level 1; worlds-as-systems-of-means at Level 2). The two classifications are orthogonal and combine cleanly:

- A Level-1 organism (human or other creature) navigates organizing principles from multiple cells: cell-1 universe-laws (gravity, climate, biology), cell-2 social norms (culture, law), cell-3 virtual conventions (online community norms), cell-4 metaverse-stage causally-binding affordances (algorithmic recommendations, platform-mediated access).
- A Level-2 world-as-OS is constituted by a configuration of means drawn from cells 1–3 (pre-metaverse) and increasingly from cell 4 (metaverse-stage), with the cell-4 means progressively gaining apparatus-side independence from the world that produced them (the 自己完結化 ↔ 自足完成化 bidirectional coupling per §V §1.2).

### Aspect A and cell 4

Cell 4's structural-character is exactly what `squeeze-ontology.md` §1.3 Aspect A specifies: **conventional in origin AND causally-binding in operation, with conventional space progressively shrinking through self-completion.** The cell-4 entry-into-occupation is what makes Aspect A operationally significant; the typology gives Aspect A its cell-position-name.

### Aspect B and cells 1–3

Cell 4's organizing principles act on cells 1, 2, and 3:

- **On cell 1**: smart cities reorganize physical-architectural constraints (traffic flows mediated by algorithms; sensor networks overlaying urban geography); algorithmic agriculture reorganizes biological-substrate management of livestock and crops; environmental monitoring reorganizes wildlife ecology.
- **On cell 2**: platform community guidelines progressively absorb (or displace) juridical and social-normative governance; algorithmic decision systems are increasingly substituted for human judgment in lending, hiring, sentencing, content moderation.
- **On cell 3**: cell-4 affordances structurally reshape what cell-3 conventional norms can do; community-level moderation is increasingly augmented (or replaced) by algorithmic enforcement.

These cell-4 → cells-1/2/3 actions are what §1.3 Aspect B names (operation-terms `[TERMINOLOGY-PENDING]`: mediation, overlay, hybridization). The typology gives Aspect B a cell-action signature.

### Environmentalist-stratified commitment

The typology preserves the squeeze ontology's environmentalist-stratified commitment (§1.3 / §V §1.4): cell 4 is a new real-level background environment, NOT a Hui-style organizing-inorganic superorganism that fuses apparatus and environment. The cells are *kinds* of environments / organizing principles, not stages of a single super-environment. The squeeze hypothesis requires that cells 1 and 4 both remain real; the typology codifies this.

### Anthropocene continuation (cells 1 and 4 interaction)

The Anthropocene literature documents that human worlds (operating from cells 1, 2, 3) have reshaped universe-law manifestations (cell-1 effects: climate, biosphere, ocean chemistry, geological-stratigraphic effects). At the metaverse-stage, cell 4 extends this agency through new mechanisms: cell-4 algorithmic systems acting on cell-1 universe-side conditions (algorithmic agriculture, IoT environmental monitoring, AI-mediated conservation, smart cities, satellite tracking). The typology positions the Anthropocene-continuation precisely: pre-metaverse human worlds operated in cells 1–3 to reshape cell-1; metaverse-stage human worlds operate increasingly through cell 4 to reshape cell 1.

## §IIC.7 — Modal pluralism and cell-occupancy (cross-reference to §III)

§III's modal pluralism — that the modern Western mode of artificiality is one mode among many — admits a typological articulation. Different historical and cultural modes of artificiality occupy different distributions across the four cells.

- A **forager mode** might be characterized by low cell-1 occupancy (limited built architecture), high cell-2 occupancy (custom and ritual), and effectively zero cell-3 / cell-4 occupancy.
- A **classical agrarian mode** would have substantial cell-1 (settlements, fields, irrigation), substantial cell-2 (custom + early statutory law), and zero cell-3 / cell-4.
- A **modern industrial mode** would have heavy cell-1 (factories, infrastructure) and cell-2 (legal-administrative state), some cell-3 (early networked communication culture), and zero cell-4 until the late 20th century.
- The **contemporary metaverse-stage mode** has all four cells occupied, with cell-4 increasingly dominant and acting on cells 1, 2, 3 per Aspect B.

This is a *cross-sectional* typology of modes (cell-distribution at a time-slice); it does not claim a developmental trajectory. (§IV's account of Western modernity's specific trajectory toward cell-4 dominance is the *genetic* claim, not entailed by the cross-sectional typology.) Other contemporary modes (Indigenous, non-Western, off-grid, post-platform, fediverse-style) may have different cell-distributions even now; modal pluralism per §III remains preserved.

§VI.5 will deploy this typology for the differentiation argument that distinguishes the metaverse-stage squeeze from prior squeezes. Specifically:

- **F3** (stratified two-environment dynamism with shrinking conventional space, `squeeze-ontology.md` §2 / §1.3): the cell-4 register's progressive closing of conventional revision-space is the F3 mechanism.
- **F4** (substrate-decoupling, `squeeze-ontology.md` §2): now formally Axis 1 of this typology; F4's "substrate-independence" claim is rephrased as cell-4 (and cell-3) occupying the decoupled column.
- **F5** (radical monopoly over livelihood, `squeeze-ontology.md` §2): the cell-4 register's progressive expansion into livelihood-relevant domains (banking, identity, civic participation, professional credentialing) is the F5 mechanism. The fact that cell-4 acts on cells 1–3 (Aspect B) is what makes radical monopoly structurally possible at cell-4-stage maturity.

§VI.5's differentiation argument can then be stated economically: prior F1+F2 squeezes (animal cases since late Pleistocene; human cases since self-domestication) operated against artificial-side organizing principles in cells 2 and 3 (and increasingly cell 1 as the built environment grew); the metaverse-stage F1+F2 squeeze is the first in which the artificial-side organizing principles occupy cell 4. This is the *structural distinctness* of the metaverse-stage squeeze — not a different F1+F2 condition, but the same condition with the artificial side in a previously-empty cell of the typology.
