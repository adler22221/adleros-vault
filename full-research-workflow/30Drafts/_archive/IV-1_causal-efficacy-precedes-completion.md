## The Argument: Causal Efficacy Precedes Full Actualization

### 1. The Bhaskar Distinction: Real vs. Actual

Roy Bhaskar's stratified ontology (*A Realist Theory of Science*, 1975; *The Possibility of Naturalism*, 1979) distinguishes three domains:

- **The Real**: structures, mechanisms, and powers that exist whether or not they are activated or observed
- **The Actual**: events that occur when mechanisms are triggered
- **The Empirical**: what is observed or experienced

A mechanism can be **real** and **causally operative** without being **fully actualized**. A geological fault is real and exerts causal pressure on the surrounding rock even before the earthquake occurs. The earthquake (full actualization) is the event; but the fault's causal efficacy has been shaping the landscape for millennia before it.

**Application to the metaverse**: The thesis's claim is not that the squeeze is complete or that the metaverse has reached full self-completion. The claim is that the *tendency* toward self-completion is real and already causally efficacious. The squeeze is being felt — in psychosomatic symptoms, in the singularization of livelihood through platform dependency, in the replacement of vernacular world-making by algorithmic governance — even while the metaverse is still an incomplete project.

### 2. The Gradient Model: Squeeze as Spectrum

Mi's question implies a binary model: either the metaverse is "fully complete" (squeeze obtains) or it is not (squeeze does not obtain). The thesis should reject this binary and argue for a **gradient model**:

**Adaptational pressure scales with the degree of environmental transformation.** When a new environment begins to emerge, early adaptors gain advantages; those adapted to the old environment begin to lose capacity. This pressure exists and intensifies *throughout* the transition — not only at the terminus.

**Analogy**: The Great Oxidation Event (the thesis's own opening example) did not produce mass extinction only when the last anaerobic niche disappeared. Oxygen-sensitive organisms began dying, adapting, or migrating to refugia as the atmospheric oxygen concentration crossed local thresholds — a gradient process that lasted hundreds of millions of years. The squeeze was real and causally efficacious throughout.

Similarly, the metaverse does not need to be complete for the squeeze to be operative. Every increment of metaverse self-completion is an increment of displacement pressure on the vernacular world. The thesis's argument holds for any *sufficient* degree of self-completion — a threshold that the empirical evidence suggests has already been crossed.

### 4. Answering Mi's Sub-Questions Directly

**"Fully actualized 還沒有self-complete?"**

The metaverse has not reached full self-completion — but full actualization is a theoretical endpoint, not a precondition for causal efficacy. The claim is not "the metaverse is complete" but "the tendency toward self-completion is real and already operative at a degree sufficient to generate the squeeze."

**"Will it ever complete?"**

This is an empirical question, not a philosophical one. The thesis does not need to commit to a determinate endpoint. The squeeze argument holds regardless: *as long as the tendency toward self-completion is operating*, its adaptational pressure is real. Whether the metaverse achieves final completion is orthogonal to whether it is currently squeezing.

**"怎麼樣的程度才算complete?"**

The thesis can define a *functional threshold* rather than a completion state: the metaverse is "sufficiently complete" when its institutional logic restructures the conditions of livelihood, identity, and world-access to such a degree that adaptation to it becomes a precondition for effective social participation. By this criterion, the metaverse has crossed the threshold in several domains (labor, communication, public discourse, governance) while remaining incomplete in others.

**"還沒之前是不是沒有那麼嚴重?"**

Yes — severity scales with degree of self-completion. The Illich two-watersheds model applies: before the first watershed, the institution is genuinely helpful and its squeeze effects are minimal; after the second watershed, the institution's self-perpetuating logic produces the pathologies the thesis describes. The severity of the squeeze is a function of how far the metaverse's self-completing tendency has progressed. Currently, we are somewhere between the two watersheds — far enough for the squeeze to be measurably operative, not yet at the terminal stage.
