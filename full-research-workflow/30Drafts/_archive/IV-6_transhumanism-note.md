## The Transhumanist Proposal and Why It Fails as a Resolution

### The Surface Logic

Transhumanism — in its various forms, from neural interfaces (BCI technology: Neuralink, neural lace) through genetic enhancement, pharmacological cognitive augmentation, and cybernetic body modification — presents itself as the solution to the body-as-obstacle problem (IV-5): if the biological organism is inadequately fitted for the metaverse's demands, the solution is to change the organism rather than the environment.

This is superficially appealing as a response to the squeeze: if the problem is the incommensurability of the organism's universe-calibrated biology with the metaverse's demands, then engineering the organism toward better metaverse-fitness would appear to dissolve the incommensurability. The transhumanist program promises to close the adaptive gap by bringing the organism itself into alignment with its new environment.

The author's note identifies the structural flaw in this proposal with precision: this is "return to endosomatic evolution via exosomatic means" — not a genuine resolution but a technical mimicry of the evolutionary process that produced the organism in the first place. And it is mimicry that disrupts rather than restores the resonance/synchrony it claims to achieve.

### The Endosomatic/Exosomatic Distinction

The distinction between endosomatic and exosomatic evolution — internal (within the body) and external (outside the body, in technical objects) — is foundational to the tradition that runs from Lotka through Stiegler. Stiegler's *Technics and Time* follows Leroi-Gourhan and Simondon in treating the human as the being whose evolution has been predominantly exosomatic: the human lineage has developed not by changing the organism biologically but by extending its capacities through technical objects (tools, language, institutions) that evolve at the speed of culture rather than the speed of genetics [VERIFY: Stiegler T&T1 (YPJ94B8W) — pp.152–153 on prosthetic constitution of the human; also Lotka's endosomatic/exosomatic distinction — check Zotero].

The transhumanist proposal inverts this trajectory: it proposes endosomatic change — direct biological modification of the organism — to serve exosomatic objectives (better performance within technically constituted environments). This inversion is the precise structure of the author's note: "return to endosomatic evolution via exosomatic means."

The inversion is not merely historical reversal; it is a structural change in the relationship between organism and technical object. In the exosomatic evolutionary trajectory, the technical object adapts to the organism's biological configuration (hand-tools are shaped for human hands; interfaces are designed for human sensory capacities). In the transhumanist trajectory, the organism is modified to better fit the technical configuration — the human is reshaped to fit the machine rather than the machine being shaped to fit the human. This is what makes the transhumanist response to the squeeze a deepening rather than a resolution.

### Why This Disrupts Resonance/Synchrony with the Universe

The author's concept of "intrinsic resonance/synchrony with the universe" names the biological attunement that evolution has built into the human organism: the circadian rhythms calibrated to the earth's rotation, the seasonal physiological changes calibrated to astronomical cycles, the social bonding capacities calibrated to the scale and tempo of small human communities, the sensory and motor systems calibrated to the physical properties of the ancestral environment.

These attunements are not arbitrary features of human biology; they are the product of millions of years of selection pressure by precisely those environmental features that constitute "the universe" in the thesis's sense — the realist background that exists independently of human construction. The organism that evolution produced is in resonance with the universe's patterns because it was shaped by them.

Transhumanist modification disrupts this resonance in two ways:

**First, it re-tunes the organism to the metaverse's patterns rather than the universe's.** A neural interface designed to optimize human performance in digital environments is by design shifting the organism's calibration toward metaverse-fitness. This is not neutral augmentation; it is a reorientation of the organism's attunement from the universe to the metaverse — precisely the move that the squeeze thesis identifies as the central dynamic, now accomplished through direct biological modification rather than environmental conditioning.

**Second, it removes the biological signals that make the squeeze perceptible.** The psychosomatic malfunctions that §VI documents — the sleep disorders, the attention fragmentation, the social anxiety, the chronic fatigue — are the biological organism's signals that its universe-calibration is being violated by metaverse adaptation pressures. These signals are the universe speaking through the body: they are the medium through which the organism communicates that its resonance with the universe is being disrupted. Transhumanist modification that eliminates these signals (pharmacological dampening of stress responses, neural modification of attention systems, genetic removal of sleep requirements) does not resolve the underlying incommensurability; it eliminates the organism's capacity to register and communicate that incommensurability.

This is the paradox of the transhumanist response to the squeeze: it promises to resolve the squeeze by making the organism better adapted to the metaverse, but it achieves this by destroying the organism's capacity to experience the squeeze — which is also the organism's capacity to signal that the universe's demands are not being met. The transhumanist organism is an organism that no longer knows it is being squeezed, not because the squeeze has been resolved but because its nervous system has been modified to not register it.

### The Squeeze Deepened, Not Resolved

Transhumanism thus represents not a resolution of the universe-metaverse squeeze but its deepening in a specific sense: it makes the squeeze irreversible at the biological level. Where the squeeze currently operates between environments that an unmodified organism inhabits (and can, in principle, disengage from or navigate differently), transhumanist modification inscribes the metaverse's demands into the organism's biology itself — making the organism structurally dependent on the metaverse's conditions not just economically or socially but biologically.

This is the squeeze's terminal form: not the double-bind of an organism caught between two environments, but the elimination of the organism's universe-fitness through the biological inscription of metaverse-fitness. The universe does not disappear; the organism simply loses its attunement to it. What remains is an organism that is maximally fit for the metaverse and progressively incapable of sustaining itself in the universe — the self-petification (Obara [VERIFY]) completed at the biological level.
