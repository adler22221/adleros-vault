## Audit: Hypotheticals Identified in the DOCX

### Category A: Documented replacements possible — replace with evidence

**A1. §I OS Spectrum (DOCX line 158):**
> "is the PRC's social credit system with surveillance technology western or eastern cosmotechnics? – yuk hui would probably disagree"

This is a rhetorical question used speculatively. It weakens the argument about Western vs. non-Western modes of artificiality.

**Replacement:** Convert to a declarative with evidence.
> "The case of China's Social Credit System is instructive here: while its logic of behavioral optimization through algorithmic scoring appears to instantiate Bhaskar's Platonic pole — reducing subjects to legible, quantifiable profiles — its institutional framing draws on both Western technocratic rationality and distinctly Sinospheric traditions of moral governance (禮). As Yuk Hui has argued, the question of whether a given technology embodies Western or alternative cosmotechnics cannot be resolved at the level of the technology alone; it requires analysis of the normative framework within which the technology is embedded (Hui, 2016). The Social Credit System exemplifies the assimilation of Western technoscience for non-Western governance ends — 師夷所長以制夷 — without resolving the underlying cosmotechnical tension."

**A2. §I OS Spectrum (DOCX line 159):**
> "only that they assimilate the dominating modern western cosmotechnics (師夷所長以制夷) to fight against the west (?)"

The "(?)" flags authorial uncertainty — this should either be argued or removed.

**Replacement:** Either commit to the claim or restructure as a documented observation:
> "The reoccurring pattern in the twentieth and twenty-first centuries — from Meiji-era Japan's technological modernization to post-1978 China's 'reform and opening' — demonstrates that resistance to Western domination has characteristically operated by appropriating Western technoscience as a competitive tool rather than developing genuinely alternative cosmotechnical frameworks. This is Needham's Mediterranean interventionist logic replicated in its former opponents: the disposition to domination is inherited along with the technological forms that express it (Needham et al., 2004, p.17; Poskett, 2022)."

**A3. §III — implicit hypothetical in the Needham argument (DOCX line 218):**

The Needham passage currently moves from historical description to a conditional projection about how Western modes developed. If the DOCX contains wording like "if a different mode of production had prevailed..." or "had Western Modernity not developed mechanistic intervention..." — these should be replaced.

**Replacement strategy for any such conditionals:**
Use Needham's own historical evidence as the proof — the non-Western case is not hypothetical but historical:
> "That alternative modes of production generate non-interventionist worldviews is not a hypothesis but a historical fact: the Sinospheric agricultural mode, sustained over millennia, produced precisely the non-interventionist, harmony-seeking scientific and institutional culture Needham documents. The interventionist trajectory is not a necessity of modernity in general but a contingent feature of the Mediterranean production complex — animal husbandry, seafaring, slavery — that gave Western Modernity its initial form (Needham et al., 2004, pp.1–2, 17). The non-interventionist alternative existed; it was superseded, not refuted."

### Category B: Hedging required — retain but hedge explicitly

**B1. §V Metaverse emergence (DOCX lines 241–248):**
> "If we improve these technologies and make them work together seamlessly, we can reasonably expect the following cybernetic system to become a possibility..."
> "If humans cease to — whether voluntarily or involuntarily — participate in the operation and decision-making process in a cybernetic system like this..."

These are the clearest hypotheticals in the thesis. Mi's concern is that the argument depends on conditions that may not obtain. The solution is not removal but proper hedging and the addition of current empirical evidence.

**Revised passage for §V (replace the "if" chain with hedged projection anchored to current evidence):**

> The technologies constituting this cybernetic system are not speculative: they are operational. Artificial intelligence systems already make consequential decisions — credit approvals, content moderation, insurance underwriting, diagnostic recommendations — at scales and speeds that preclude case-by-case human oversight. Big data aggregation already produces what Shoshana Zuboff has called "behavioral surplus" — data collected beyond what is needed for a service, repurposed for prediction and modification [VERIFY: Zuboff *Surveillance Capitalism* — check Zotero]. IoT already embeds decision-making infrastructure in physical environments. Code already functions as law in the sense Lessig identified (2006): it does not prohibit but makes impossible, and it does not regulate but constitutes the conditions of interaction.

> What remains incomplete is the *integration* of these systems into a seamless cybernetic loop. But this is a quantitative threshold, not a qualitative transformation: the dispositional tendency — artificiality acquiring its own operational logic, indifferent to individual human choices — is already operative. The squeeze is a gradient, not a binary switch (see §VI). What follows from this is not a conditional projection but a structural diagnosis of the present.

---

**B2. §V "If this becomes the case" (DOCX line 248):**
> "If this becomes the case, such artificiality can no longer be considered merely a critical component of our world..."

**Revised:** Replace the conditional with a threshold claim grounded in the §VI evidence:
> "As the evidence in §VI demonstrates, this condition is already partially operative: portions of livelihood, social participation, and civic recognition are already mediated by systems whose operational logic is not governed by human decisions but by algorithmic optimization. The threshold is not a future possibility but an emerging reality whose full actualization remains in process. The metaverse is not what will happen when technology improves; it is what is happening as the self-completing tendency of Western artificiality continues to close its feedback loop."

The research directions that follow from this diagnosis — appropriate technology, tools for conviviality (Illich, 1973), cosmotechnics of co-becoming (Hui, 2016) — represent promising frameworks for the normative response, but their development requires the kind of sustained engagement with non-Western philosophical and technological traditions that lies beyond what a single thesis can offer."
