## Passage A — End of §IV / Opening of §V (~220 words)

> The individual's self-finishing tendency — the drive toward self-designed existence in which conditions of life are brought increasingly under the will's control — does not arise or operate in isolation. §IV traced its genealogy through Uegaki's five periods of postwar social development and identified in the Drifter the psychological type most fully constituted by this tendency. But the self-finishing individual does not produce the metaverse, nor does the metaverse emerge independently of the individuals whose tendency it addresses. The relationship is one of mutual constitution: the individual's drive toward controlled existence generates the cultural demand that the metaverse's environments are designed to supply; the metaverse's environments intensify the self-finishing tendency by delivering more reliable control than any preceding social arrangement. Each iteration deepens the other.
>
> §V examines the other side of this constitution: not the individual tendency but the civilizational and technical system through which the metaverse's own self-completing tendency has developed. The individual's self-finishing takes place inside an environment that is itself self-completing — that operates according to its own organizing logic independently of any individual's intention. If §IV shows why individuals are drawn into metaverse environments, §V shows what the metaverse *is*: not merely a new technology or platform but a second environment that has emerged from the universe's substrate as something not reducible to it [T1: source-ms-kaikan §2.4; squeeze-ontology.md F4 — emergent irreducibility]. Its organizing principles do not derive from thermodynamics, biology, or geography even though they depend on them.

## Passage B — End of §V / Opening of §VI (~230 words)

> §V has established what the metaverse is in structural terms: a second environment whose organizing logic — code-as-architecture, data-extractive governance, algorithmic dividual management, network-effect coercion — constitutes a distinct set of selection criteria, adaptation demands, and conditions for success. These criteria are not merely different from the universe's conditions; they are **incommensurable** with them [squeeze-ontology.md F2]: what succeeds in one does not automatically succeed in the other; competencies developed for one are not fully transferable; the value-currencies of each cannot be exchanged at a stable rate. This is not a problem of transition or adjustment — it is the structural condition of any organism that must simultaneously satisfy both environments' demands.
>
> §VI traces what this condition — the between-environment structural squeeze — produces at the level of observable life. It does not introduce a new argument; the squeeze is not an additional claim beyond §III–§V. It is the name for the situation the preceding analysis has already established: that a being exists *between* two environments [squeeze-ontology.md F1] whose laws are not mutually translatable, with no optimization that satisfies both simultaneously. §VI asks: what does this look like for the organism that lives it? The answer covers three domains — the biological signal (psychosomatic malfunctions), the economic expression (singularization of livelihood), and the governance form (opaque algorithmic disruption of life without recourse).

## Passage C — End of §VI / Opening of Conclusion (~280 words)

> The three domains documented in §VI are not isolated pathologies. They are manifestations of a structural condition whose architecture §III–§V established: an organism that must satisfy the selection criteria of two environments — universe and metaverse — whose organizing laws are not mutually translatable [squeeze-ontology.md F1+F2]. The squeeze is this structural condition; §VI's cases are its observable surface.
>
> Four features of this structure constrain the range of adequate responses:
>
> **First, the squeeze is not experienced uniformly.** Its intensity scales with the degree of metaverse completion in a given domain and with the extent to which the organism's universe-competencies have been expropriated through the self-finishing and self-completing dynamic. The gradient model (§IV-1) predicts that those most thoroughly adapted to the metaverse's logic face the heaviest universe-incompatibility costs, while those least integrated face the heaviest metaverse exclusion costs. No position on the gradient is cost-free.
>
> **Second, the squeeze is asymmetric in its risk distribution.** Those who design and govern the metaverse's infrastructure extract upside while users bear downside; algorithmic optimization treats individual ruin as an acceptable system cost rather than a cost to be avoided (§VI-9, Taleb risk-dumping and naive interventionism).
>
> **Third, the squeeze accumulates hidden fragility.** Platform governance suppresses visible volatility while building structural conditions for catastrophic future disruption. The stability that the metaverse's self-completing tendency projects is real at the empirical surface but fragile at the structural level (§VI-9).
>
> **Fourth, and most consequentially, the squeeze is designed to be irresolvable through individual adaptation.** The mechanism of metaverse governance — dividual optimization, algorithmic management of data profiles rather than deliberating subjects (§V-6) — operates at a level that circumvents individual deliberation. The adaptive repertoires the metaverse most demands (cognitive flexibility, platform literacy, attention-economy participation) are being expropriated at the same rate as the demands on them increase (§V-5, proletarianization of *savoir-théoriser*).
>
> These four features converge on a single structural implication: personal adaptation strategies address the symptom without addressing the condition. The 〈依存転回〉 — the shift in framing from victim of external forces to participant in self-organized dependency structures [T1: source-ms-kaikan §6] — is the precondition of any response adequate to the structural level. What this requires is not a personal decision but a reorganization of the dependency modes through which life is sustained: the question of which metabolic circuits can be re-opened, reinforced, or newly constructed.
>
> The conclusion derives from this what the structure of an adequate response would require — and what it cannot require. It cannot require simply more individual adaptation, because the fourth feature above shows that individual adaptation is precisely what the squeeze's design renders insufficient. It can require something at the level of the architecture: a rethinking of how the dependency modes are organized and what role deliberate, trans/antidisciplinary understanding plays in making that reorganization possible.
