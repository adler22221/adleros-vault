## How the Three Topics Fit Together

The three research threads form a **single causal argument** when assembled correctly:

```
DIAGNOSIS:           Allocation Dependence
                     ↓
                     explains why education systems structurally fail
                     to respond to planetary crises (the 1.5% problem
                     is a symptom; AD is the mechanism)
                     
COUNTER-RULE:        Learn by Caring
                     ↓
                     the agent-level rule that can reorganize educational
                     systems from within — not top-down reform but a
                     shift in the simple rules of agent behavior
                     
NORMATIVE HORIZON:   Education AS Sustainable Development
                     ↓
                     the institutional vision where education embodies
                     rather than merely teaches sustainability;
                     where the EaSD inversion is fully realized
```

The **Modes of Dependencies** framework (天地依存→配分依存→貨幣依存→人工性依存→制御性依存) provides the overarching ontological architecture that situates all three within a theory of how humans organize access to survival resources — and how the current configuration produces planetary crises.

**The master throughline**: planetary crises are not failures of knowledge or intention but consequences of misaligned dependencies. Education fails to respond because it is itself allocation-dependent — its resource flows, accreditation, and survival logic are governed by the same mechanisms it would need to transcend. Learning by Caring is the agent-level counter-move; Education as Sustainable Development is the institutional horizon.
