### The punchier (stakes-first) abstract opening

This replaces the current first paragraph; **¶2 ("The argument proceeds in two movements…") and ¶3 (the convergence) stay exactly as they are.** It opens on the predicament rather than on the word, and hands off to the method:

> Humanity is arriving at a unprecedented condition, which is to live between two background environments that were never meant to collide. On one side is the universe — the realist given that precedes us and answers to no intention of ours; on the other, the metaverse — not a class of devices but the second given that artificiality becomes when it self-completes, running by laws of its own that are, unprecedentedly, opaque and perpetually self-rewriting. Between them is the world: the human-built habitat that once buffered us from what we did not author, now being hollowed from within. This thesis names that predicament the **squeeze** — not a coming catastrophe but a present structural condition, already obtaining in graded form, in which the human is pressed between two givens at once. Seeing it requires a prior question. Where the dominant framings of artificiality's threat — governance, ownership, liberty, and, most prominently, AI alignment — ask how to secure a subject already standing over against the apparatus, this thesis asks what an embedded being depends on to be a subject at all. The metaverse matters here not first as a technology to be governed but as a transformation in the structure of our dependence; to characterize it I adopt a realist, retroductive method, after critical realism — reading not its present specimens but the dispositions of the conditions from which any such environment must arise, and holding a post-Heideggerian philosophy of technology and a critical theory of modernity together with the East Asian synthetic anthropology of Obara, Uegaki, and Miura.

**What it trades:** it drops the "the word _metaverse_ is misnamed → three errors" hook, which mirrors the thesis's own first move. That hook is excellent in the Introduction body; the question is whether the _abstract_ should lead with the diagnosis (more arresting for an award reader) or with the misnaming (more faithful to your argument's order). I'd lean stakes-first for the award. Say the word and I'll swap it into the copy as an alternative tracked change so you can see both in place.


> The word "metaverse" names more than its current devices can show. Fixed to today's headsets and rendered virtual worlds, it invites three errors: a path-dependency on an accidental coinage, a disciplinary reductionism, and an actualism that pins analysis to whatever has so far been built. Against these I adopt a realist, retroductive method, after critical realism: I characterize the metaverse not by its present specimens but by the dispositions of the conditions from which any such environment must arise, holding a post-Heideggerian philosophy of technology and a critical theory of modernity together with the East Asian synthetic anthropology of Obara, Uegaki, and Miura. With this method comes the question I claim as the contribution. The dominant framings of artificiality's threat — governance, ownership, liberty, and, most prominently, AI alignment — each presuppose a subject standing over against the apparatus and ask how to secure its position; I ask the prior question of what an embedded being depends on to be a subject at all. The metaverse matters here not first as a technology to be governed but as a transformation in the structure of our dependence.
>
> The argument proceeds in two movements joined at their root. A world, I hold, is not a worldview but a built habitat — an adaptational operating system a human collectivity raises within the universe and lives inside of, made of artificiality: the exosomatized organ, the causally efficacious nonmaterial, and organizing principles irreducible to the universe's. Beneath the mode that came to overspread other worlds lies a single disposition: the elevation of the sovereign subject, a self raised above the given. On the human side, that subject pursues a life-as-intended, reading whatever does not answer to its intention as a wrong to be discharged, and offloads ever more living onto the apparatus, until human life turns self-finishing. On the side of the apparatus, the artificiality so entrusted self-completes — acquiring the capacity to operate, maintain, and renew itself — until it stands up as a background environment around the world: the metaverse, a second given beside the universe, running by laws of its own that are, unprecedentedly, opaque and perpetually self-rewriting rather than enduring. The more we delegate, the more it completes; the more it completes, the smaller our part in the world becomes.
>
> The two movements converge in the latest squeeze: not a coming catastrophe but a present structural condition, obtaining already in graded form, in which the human is pressed between the universe and the metaverse as the world that once buffered it is hollowed from within. This is the level beneath governance, ownership, liberty, and alignment alike, which secure the subject where the squeeze concerns what the subject depends on. The danger is not the one science fiction names — not that the apparatus will awaken and replace us, but that we, in fleeing what we depend on, will hollow ourselves toward the condition of an apparatus and call the hollowing freedom. What answers it can only be its inverse: a turn from the flight from dependence toward its reorganization, recovering the older recognition that to be is to arise in dependence on conditions. Attunement, on this account, is nothing more mystical than the prevention of the squeeze. Whether a new world can be built between the universe and the metaverse I do not answer; the diagnosis is what this thesis owes, and it is the diagnosis that makes the question askable.

**Keywords:** the squeeze; universe / world / metaverse; artificiality; the sovereign subject; self-finishing and self-completion; offloading; the dependence turn; critical realism and retroduction
