# Ch-5-5: Grammatization, Proletarianization, and the Shrinking Human

## §0 — Framing

[SCAFFOLD] Ch-5-3 v3 establishes the apparatus-side of the bidirectional coupling: 自足完成化, the recursive retention/protention loop, the four minimally sufficient technologies. Ch-5-5 develops the human-side: what is *lost* as the loop closes, and how this loss is described — in convergent but not identical vocabularies — by Stiegler (proletarianization), Obara (self-petification), Uegaki (dilution of relationality + desomatization), Frankfurt critical theory (reification + one-dimensionality), Illich (radical monopoly + modernized poverty), and Hui (dividuation). The section then identifies where the thesis integrates these lineages and what potential contributions remain to develop.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> §V's argument so far has specified the apparatus-side disposition (自足完成化) and its actualization mechanism (the recursive retention/protention coupling instantiated by AI + big data + IoT + code-as-law). The remaining question, before §VI takes up the squeeze, is: what happens to the human as this loop closes? The literature offers six convergent diagnoses of human dispossession by technical-institutional mediation — Stiegler's *proletarianization* via *grammatization*; Obara's *self-petification* under *capsulization*; Uegaki's *dilution of relationality* and *desomatization of life*; the Frankfurt School's *reification* and *one-dimensionality*; Illich's *radical monopoly*, *disabling professions*, and *modernized poverty*; and Hui's *dividuation* under *algorithmic governmentality*. These are not in opposition; they describe the same phenomenon from six different angles. This section traces the convergence, names the divergences, and locates the thesis's contributions.

## §1 — Stiegler's grammatization and proletarianization

### §1.1 — Grammatization: the discretization of human flows

> "Digital tracking technologies are the most advanced stage of a process of grammatization that began at the end of the Upper Palaeolithic age, when humanity first learnt to discretize and reproduce, in traces of various kinds, **the flux and flow running through it and that it generates: mental images (cave paintings), speech (writing), gestures (the automation of production), frequencies of sound and light (analogue recording technology) and now individual behaviour, social relations and transindividuation processes (algorithms of reticular writing)**." [VERIFY: Stiegler *Automatic Society* p.TBD]

Grammatization is the technical-historical process by which previously continuous human flows are *discretized* — broken into reproducible units, traces, "grammes" — and externalized into technical supports. Each epoch of grammatization captures more of the human flow:

1. Upper Paleolithic — mental images (cave paintings)
2. Writing — speech / language
3. Industrial revolution — gestures / labor-knowledge
4. Analog recording — perceptual / attentional flow
5. Digital reticulation — behavior, social relations, transindividuation itself

### §1.2 — Proletarianization: the loss of knowledges

> "**The proletarianization of minds and spirits, that is, of the noetic faculties enabling theorization and deliberation… are short-circuited by the current operator of proletarianization, which is digital tertiary retention** – just as analogue tertiary retention was in the twentieth century the operator of the proletarianization of life-knowledge, and just as mechanical tertiary retention was in the nineteenth century the operator of the proletarianization of work-knowledge." [VERIFY: Stiegler *Automatic Society* p.TBD]

Stiegler develops a three-stage proletarianization:

| Stage | Lost knowledge | Operator | Era |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Savoir-faire** (work-knowledge / how to make) | Mechanical tertiary retention (factory machines) | 19th century |
| 2 | **Savoir-vivre** (life-knowledge / how to live) | Analog tertiary retention (mass media, advertising) | 20th century |
| 3 | **Savoir-théoriser** (theoretical knowledge / how to think) | Digital tertiary retention (algorithms, AI) | 21st century |

Each stage discretizes a different domain: hand → emotion-attention → noesis itself.

### §1.3 — The threshold and its consequences

> "These systems produce an automatic performativity that channels, diverts and short-circuits individual and collective protentions **by outstripping and overtaking the noetic capacities of individuals precisely insofar as they are protentional capacities**." [VERIFY: Stiegler *Automatic Society* p.TBD]

> "Fully computational capitalism is as such the completion of nihilism." [Stiegler]

The threshold-claim: when grammatization reaches digital reticulation, the technical system can short-circuit deliberation faster than human reason can catch up. The result is *generalized proletarianization* — the loss not just of specific skills but of the noetic capacity that would let humans resist or reconstitute the loss.

## §2 — The five convergent diagnoses

### §2.1 — Obara: self-petification under capsulization

Obara's vocabulary is bio-ethological. Where Stiegler describes loss of knowledges, Obara describes loss of *biological capacity*:

> "現代の子どもの状況に何かしらの不気味さを感じているのは、筆者だけではあるまい… **庭イヌというのは、座敷イヌが出現するまでは、区分されていなかったイヌのありかたである… 現代の人間は座敷イヌ、ひと昔前の人間は庭イヌであろうか**" (Obara). The contemporary human is to the previous human as the lap-dog (座敷イヌ) is to the yard-dog (庭イヌ): better-cared-for, less robust, structurally fragile.

> "**自然の脅威を除くことで、エアコンに見られるような人為的な淘汰適応条件をつくり出している**… すべての動物は、動物園で飼育されている野生でない野生動物に見られるように、生理と行動によって、適応や順応をなしとげている." (Obara)

The mechanism is *capsulization* (カプセル化): the human inhabits a sealed, climate-controlled, conflict-managed environment that progressively sheds the natural stressors against which biological capacities developed. The lost capacities include autonomic regulation, metabolic flexibility, conflict-mediation skills, and — at the social level — relational substrate ("子どもどうしのごたごた" that develops attack-and-restraint mechanisms).

**Convergence with Stiegler**: capsulization is to biological capacity what grammatization is to noetic capacity. Both describe the externalization of a human capacity into the artificial environment, with the consequence that the capacity withers in the human.

**Divergence**: Obara's analysis is *evolutionary-ecological* (the artificial niche creates new selection pressures); Stiegler's is *techno-historical* (the technical system grammatizes what was previously continuous). Obara stops at biological/social fragility; Stiegler extends to noetic short-circuiting.

### §2.2 — Uegaki: dilution of relationality and desomatization

Uegaki names two parallel losses, both downstream of the human pursuit of life-as-willedness via the social apparatus:

> "**Without sufficient relational experience and skills, people become, paradoxically, more sensitive to the not-as-willedness of others' existence**… This leads to 'relational pathologies 関係性の病理' and reinforces the dependence on the social apparatus to arbitrate relational struggles and entanglements." [Yang's viva-passed §IV gloss on Uegaki]

> "**This leads to a 'desomatization of life 生の脱身体化,' as what it means to live becomes increasingly detached from the biological body** – this also leads to a 'confusion of life 生の混乱,' as the less one is biologically her/himself, paradoxically, the more ontologically realized s/he may become." [Yang's viva-passed §IV]

The two Uegaki losses:

| Uegaki concept | What is lost |
|---|---|
| 関係性の希薄化 (dilution of relationality) | Capacity for unmediated human relationship; "意味のある〈関係性〉" (relations of meaning) |
| 生の脱身体化 (desomatization) | Capacity to live as an embodied being whose body has its own meaning |

**Convergence with Stiegler**: dilution of relationality is to relational savoir-vivre what proletarianization is to work-knowledge — both describe a domain of human capacity progressively absorbed into artificial mediation.

**Divergence**: Uegaki's mechanism is *existential-anthropological* (driven by 〈無限の生〉 / life-as-willed) rather than techno-historical. The social apparatus is not just a technical structure that grammatizes; it is a structural correlate of the human existential demand for as-willedness.

### §2.3 — Frankfurt critical theory: reification, one-dimensionality, the absorbed administrator

Adorno and Horkheimer's *Dialectic of Enlightenment* and Marcuse's *One-Dimensional Man* describe a different mechanism converging on similar consequences:

> "An organization is forced into independence by self-preservation; at the same time **this establishment of independence leads to alienation from its purposes and from the people of whom it is composed**… the world tends to become the stuff of total administration, which absorbs even the administrators." [Adorno]

> "**The technical apparatus of production and distribution… functions, not as the sum-total of mere instruments which can be isolated from their social and political effects, but rather as a system which determines a priori the product of the apparatus as well as the operations of servicing and extending it**." [Marcuse]

> "Operationalism, in theory and practice, becomes the theory and practice of containment. Underneath its obvious dynamics, this society is a thoroughly static system of life: **self-propelling in its oppressive productivity and in its beneficial coordination**." [Marcuse]

The Frankfurt mechanism: the commodity form, exchange-equivalence, and instrumental rationality become reified into administrative apparatus. The apparatus then *absorbs even the administrators* — including, eventually, those who would resist it.

**Convergence with Stiegler**: Frankfurt's "absorbs even the administrators" is structurally analogous to Stiegler's "short-circuits the noetic capacities of individuals." Both name a threshold beyond which the system operates without requiring (or permitting) genuine human noetic involvement.

**Divergence**: Frankfurt's generative mechanism is *politico-economic* (commodity, exchange-equivalence). Stiegler's is *techno-historical* (grammatization). Per the Ch-5-3 v3 §1.3 framing, both are partial-perspective accounts of the same real-level disposition under different vocabularies.

### §2.4 — Illich: radical monopoly, disabling professions, modernized poverty

Illich's analysis sits at a different stratum: not technical (Stiegler) or politico-economic (Frankfurt) but *institutional-professional*. He describes the same human dispossession as the result of a different mechanism:

#### §2.4.1 — Radical monopoly

> "**I speak about radical monopoly when one industrial production process exercises an exclusive control over the satisfaction of a pressing need, and excludes nonindustrial activities from competition**." [Illich, *Tools for Conviviality*]

> "A radical monopoly goes further [than commercial monopoly]: **it deprives the environment of those features that people need in a specific area to subsist outside the market economy… A radical monopoly paralyzes autonomous action in favour of professional deliveries**." [Illich, *Energy and Equity*]

> "**The establishment of radical monopoly happens when people give up their native ability to do what they can do for themselves and for each other, in exchange for something 'better' that can be done for them only by a major tool**." [Illich]

Examples: cars monopolize traffic (eliminating walkability); schools monopolize learning (delegitimating non-school knowledge); medicine monopolizes care (criminalizing self-care). In each case, the monopoly destroys the *environment* in which the alternative would be viable.

#### §2.4.2 — Disabling professions

> "**Today, doctors and social workers — as formerly only priests and jurists — gain legal power to create the need that, by law, they alone will be allowed to satisfy**… the new dominant professions claim control over human needs, tout court." [Illich, *Disabling Professions*]

> "**Professionals assert secret knowledge about human nature, knowledge which only they have the right to dispense.** They claim a monopoly over the definition of deviance and the remedies needed… debilitating and effectively disabling the individual." [Illich]

#### §2.4.3 — Modernized poverty

> "**In the new dispensation, the poor are no longer those who survive by their vernacular activities because they have only marginal or no access to the market. No, the modernized poor are those whose vernacular domain, in speech and in action, is most restricted** – those who get least satisfaction out of the few vernacular activities in which they can still engage." [Illich, *Shadow Work*]

> "Modernized poverty combines the lack of power over circumstances with **a loss of personal potency**." [Illich]

#### §2.4.4 — Counterproductivity and the two watersheds

> "**At a second point, the progress demonstrated in a previous achievement is used as a rationale for the exploitation of society as a whole in the service of a value which is determined and constantly revised by an element of society, by one of its self-certifying professional elites**." [Illich]

> "**It is sufficient to recognize the existence of these two watersheds**… In one decade several major institutions have moved jointly over their second watershed. Schools are losing their claim to be effective tools to provide education; cars have ceased to be effective tools for mass transportation; the assembly line has ceased to be an acceptable mode of production." [Illich, *Tools for Conviviality*]

#### §2.4.5 — Convergence and divergence with Stiegler

**Convergence**: Illich's "people give up their native ability to do what they can do for themselves and for each other, in exchange for something 'better' that can be done for them only by a major tool" is structurally identical to Stiegler's proletarianization — a human capacity is externalized into industrial-institutional mediation and lost as a living capacity. Illich's "modernized poverty" (the loss of vernacular satisfaction-of-needs) is the institutional counterpart to Stiegler's loss of savoir-faire/savoir-vivre/savoir-théoriser. Some commentators have noted this convergence directly — that "Stiegler's critique of the power imbalances brought about by exteriorization dovetails well with Illich's call for a convivial society." [VERIFY: Conviviality, the Internet, and AI — locate exact source].

**Divergence**: Illich's mediation-substrate is *institutional-professional* (schools, hospitals, transport infrastructure, administrative bureaucracies); Stiegler's is *technical* (writing, recording, algorithms). Illich's mechanism operates through *legal monopolization of need-definition* and *disabling certification*; Stiegler's operates through *discretization of flows into reproducible traces*. These can be seen as the same phenomenon viewed at different strata — Illich at the institutional-actual level, Stiegler at the technical-architectural level.

### §2.5 — Hui: dividuation via algorithmic governmentality

Hui's vocabulary is the most contemporary. His mechanism builds on Stiegler but adds the protentional dimension Stiegler underdeveloped:

> "**The preemption of the tertiary protention is possible only because of the computational hermeneutics, which is essentially recursive: It constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present.** Human beings are reintegrated into the temporality of machines, not only as individuals but also as collectives and communities. **This is precisely what is called algorithmic governmentality**." [Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency* §39]

> "Rouvroy observes that this form of governmentality does not address the subject, but rather the **infra-individual** (which are numerised relations) and the **supra-individual** (meaning the profile automatically constructed by machines through pattern analysis)." [Hui citing Rouvroy]

> "In such a modulative process, however, **each individual is finally divided into what Deleuze calls the 'dividual'**." [Hui]

**Convergence**: Hui's *dividuation* is the structural form of what Stiegler's generalized proletarianization produces. The dividual is the entity that emerges when noetic capacity has been short-circuited and the pattern-extraction algorithm has substituted for the integrating subject.

**Divergence**: Hui specifies the *protentional* mechanism (preemption + computational hermeneutics) where Stiegler had stayed primarily at retention. Per Ch-5-3 v3 §2.3, this is what makes the cybernetic loop close: not just the past being captured but the future being anticipated faster than human deliberation.

## §4 — How the thesis integrates the six lineages

The thesis's contribution is not to choose one of these vocabularies but to *integrate them as partial-perspective accounts of the same real-level disposition*. The integration runs through the stratified architecture established in Ch-5-3 v3:

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The six lineages are not in competition. Each describes the same threshold — where artificiality crosses into self-completion and humans cross into shrinking portion-of-functionality — from a different stratum of analysis. Stiegler describes the technical-architectural mechanism (grammatization → tertiary retention/protention); Obara the bio-ecological substrate (self-domestication → self-petification); Uegaki the existential-anthropological driver (life-as-willedness → delegation to social apparatus); Frankfurt the politico-economic substrate (commodity-form → administered apparatus); Illich the institutional-professional mediation (radical monopoly → modernized poverty); Hui the recursive-cybernetic mechanism (dividuation via algorithmic governmentality). These are partial-perspective accounts of the same real-level disposition: the bidirectional coupling of human self-finishing (自己完結化) and apparatus self-completion (自足完成化), actualized through the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention.
>
> The thesis's framework is structurally capable of holding all six because the stratified-real architecture (Ch-5-3 v3 §2) admits multiple architectural-mechanism real-level claims subordinate to the meta-law. Lessig's architecture-as-regulation, Hui's productive-ontologies, and the recursive retention/protention coupling all operate at the architectural-mechanism stratum. The institutional-professional grammar (Illich), the politico-economic grammar (Frankfurt), the bio-ecological grammar (Obara), and the existential-anthropological grammar (Uegaki) operate at strata that Bhaskar's laminated ontology accommodates as further mechanism-strata through which the meta-law operates. Each lineage names a real mechanism; each mechanism operates at a different stratum; all are subordinate to the meta-law of self-completion at the topmost real-level stratum.

### §4.1 — Re-framing proletarianization as the human-side of self-completion's bidirectional coupling

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The thesis's reframing of proletarianization is structurally distinctive. Where Stiegler describes proletarianization as a one-way loss (the human is dispossessed of knowledge by industrial and digital tertiary retention), the thesis reframes it as the *human-side of a bidirectional coupling*. As humans delegate retention to IoT/big data and protention to AI/code-as-law, the apparatus side completes its loop and the human side correspondingly contracts — what the viva-passed §V calls "the shrinking of humans' portion of functionality 役割." The shrinkage is not just the loss of knowledges (Stiegler) or skills (Illich) or relationality (Uegaki) or biological capacity (Obara); it is the structural correlate of the apparatus side's self-completion. Each delegation is at the same time a constriction: each thing the apparatus learns to do is something the human stops doing, and stops being able to do.
>
> This reframing matters because it answers a question the convergent literature does not directly address: *why* does the dispossession happen? The Frankfurt answer is "capital"; the Stiegler answer is "technics"; the Illich answer is "professional self-certification"; the Uegaki answer is "the existential demand for as-willedness." The thesis answers: dispossession happens because both human-side delegation AND apparatus-side absorption are structurally required for either side's tendency to actualize. Each side enables the other; each side completes the other. The dispossession is not a contingent side-effect of capitalism or technics or professionalism — it is the structural correlate of the meta-law of self-completion.

### §4.2 — The shrinking portion of functionality as more than proletarianization

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> "Shrinking portion of functionality" is more general than proletarianization. Proletarianization presupposes a labor-economic frame in which knowledge or skill has economic value; the loss is then framed as economic dispossession. "Shrinking portion of functionality" is environmental-ecological: it describes the human's reduced *role-share* in an environment, regardless of whether that environment is structured by labor markets. As humans delegate functions to artificiality, the human's structural place in the world contracts even when no labor relation is at stake. This frame accommodates Stiegler's labor-economic proletarianization, Illich's institutional disabling, Obara's ecological-biological fragility, and Uegaki's existential dispossession as instances of the same structural shrinkage.

## §5 — Potential contributions not yet elaborated

Beyond what is already in the viva-passed thesis and Ch-5-3 v3, the comparison surfaces five potential contributions the thesis could develop:

### §5.1 — Illich's two-watersheds applied to AI's protentional sufficiency

Illich's threshold-of-counterproductivity could be applied directly to the thesis's "minimally sufficient technologies" framework. Illich claims that institutions, beyond a *second watershed*, become counterproductive to their stated purposes — schools make people stupider, medicine sicker, transportation slower (in expected value). The thesis's claim that AI achieves "protentional sufficiency at scale" in the contemporary moment can be sharpened by adding: this is the moment when the AI/big-data/IoT/code-as-law cybernetic system crosses Illich's *second watershed* — beyond which it becomes counterproductive to the human capacity it was meant to serve.

This would tie the threshold-claim in Ch-5-3 v3 §3.4 ("why now") to a falsifiable historical-sociological framework. The current revision draft already cites Illich's radical monopoly for §VI.B (singularization of livelihood); extending Illich's two-watersheds to AI's threshold would unify §V and §VI under a single Illichian frame.

[VERIFY: Illich *Tools for Conviviality* p.TBD on the two watersheds]

### §5.2 — Modernized poverty extended to "metaverse poverty"

Illich's *modernized poverty* — "the modernized poor are those whose vernacular domain, in speech and in action, is most restricted" — directly anticipates the thesis's §VI.B argument about *singularization of livelihood and artificial destitution amid abundance*. The viva-passed §VI.B already names "artificial destitution" as a phenomenon distinct from traditional poverty; Illich's *modernized poverty* gives this a precise philosophical grounding.

Potential extension: the thesis can name a new phase — *metaverse poverty* — in which the vernacular domain is restricted not just by industrial-professional mediation (Illich) but by *algorithmic-protentional preemption* (Hui via Yang). The metaverse-poor are those whose protentional capacity is most subjugated to algorithmic preemption — those who experience the future as already-decided by the system. This tightens §VI.B's analysis and connects it precisely to Ch-5-5's grammatization/proletarianization story.

### §5.3 — A convergent design horizon: vernacular + convivial + cosmotechnical + pharmacological

Each resistance tradition proposes an alternative:

- Illich: convivial tools / vernacular practices
- Stiegler: pharmacological care / contributive economy
- Hui: cosmotechnics / technodiversity
- Uegaki: 〈有限の生〉 — accepting limits
- Obara: re-engagement with biological "naturalness"
- Frankfurt: negative dialectics

These converge on a structural prescription: **design that preserves human capacity for non-mediated activity within technically-mediated environments**. The convergence point — *vernacular-convivial-cosmotechnical-pharmacological-finite-naturalist* — is the design horizon for the thesis's Conclusion. Currently the 2026-04-26 author comment names "appropriate technology, tools for conviviality, kyosei, non-Western cosmotechnics" as the conclusion's design discussion; this Ch-5-5 comparison gives that design discussion its precise philosophical pedigree.

The thesis could articulate this convergence as: **the resistance tradition specifies the structural conditions under which human portion-of-functionality can be preserved against the meta-law of self-completion** — namely, conditions of vernacular access, convivial tooling, cosmotechnical plurality, pharmacological care, finite-life acceptance, and biological-ecological reconnection.

### §5.4 — Stratified mapping of resistance possibilities

Just as the diagnoses are stratified, the resistance possibilities are stratified. A potential thesis contribution is to map them:

| Stratum | Disease (mechanism of dispossession) | Cure (structural condition for capacity-preservation) |
|---|---|---|
| Real-level meta-law | 自足完成化 | Bracketing the meta-law via design-for-finitude (the metaverse never fully completes) |
| Architectural-mechanism | Lessig's architectural-regulation; Hui's productive-ontologies; recursive retention/protention | Design for affordance-plurality; theory-of-relations open-ended; non-recursive feedback |
| Politico-economic | Commodity-form / instrumental rationality | Negative dialectics, contributive economy |
| Institutional-professional | Radical monopoly, disabling professions | Convivial tools, vernacular re-engagement |
| Technical-historical | Grammatization, proletarianization | Pharmacological care, transindividuation |
| Bio-ecological | Self-petification, capsulization | Substrate-respecting design (re-engagement with 天地依存 — though noting Uegaki's caveat against romanticizing) |
| Existential-anthropological | 〈無限の生〉 / life-as-willedness | 〈有限の生〉 / acceptance of as-not-willedness |

This stratification clarifies that no single resistance tradition addresses all strata. The thesis's design horizon must be *multi-strata* — addressing the real-level meta-law, the architectural-mechanism stratum, and the actual-level mechanisms simultaneously.

### §5.5 — The bidirectional-coupling framing reveals what proletarianization-alone misses

Stiegler's account, even in its most sophisticated form, can sound like a one-way technological determinism (the technical system grammatizes; the human is dispossessed). Adding the bidirectional coupling (Ch-5-3 v3 §1.2) gives a more accurate picture: humans *demand* delegation (Uegaki's life-as-willed); the apparatus *responds* by self-completing; humans then have less reason to resist further delegation.

This has a sharp implication for resistance: if the bidirectional coupling is the mechanism, then resistance cannot be addressed only at the technical-architectural side (designing better tools, regulating algorithms). It must also be addressed at the existential-anthropological side (rejecting 〈無限の生〉, accepting 〈有限の生〉). Uegaki's contribution to the convergence is therefore irreducibly important: without it, every other resistance prescription (Illich's conviviality, Stiegler's care, Hui's cosmotechnics) risks being co-opted back into the apparatus by humans who still pursue life-as-willedness through the very tools meant to resist apparatus-completion.

The thesis's *contribution* here is not to invent the resistance prescription (each tradition has one) but to specify the *structural condition* under which any resistance prescription can succeed: only if the existential demand that drives delegation is itself reframed.

A possible short opening for Ch-5-5 in thesis prose:

> [PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The apparatus-side disposition of self-completion, analyzed in §V.A–B above, is structurally coupled with a human-side dispossession that the literature on technologically-mediated society has described under several converging vocabularies. Stiegler names it *proletarianization* — the loss of savoir-faire, savoir-vivre, and ultimately savoir-théoriser through grammatization's discretization of human flows into technical traces. Obara names it *self-petification* — the loss of biological capacity through capsulization. Uegaki names *dilution of relationality* and *desomatization of life* — the loss of relational and embodied modes of being through delegation to the social apparatus. The Frankfurt School names *reification* and the *administered world that absorbs even its administrators* — the loss of substantive reason through commodification and instrumental-rational reification. Illich names *radical monopoly* and *modernized poverty* — the loss of vernacular capacity through institutional-professional monopolization. Hui names *dividuation* and *algorithmic governmentality* — the fragmentation of the integrated subject into infra- and supra-individual data-traces.
>
> These six diagnoses are not in opposition. They describe the same threshold — where the human's portion of functionality contracts as the apparatus's portion expands — from six different strata of analysis. The contribution of this thesis is to show that all six are partial-perspective accounts of the same real-level disposition, the bidirectional coupling of self-finishing (自己完結化) and self-completion (自足完成化), actualized through the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention. The strata at which they operate (technical-architectural; bio-ecological; existential-anthropological; politico-economic; institutional-professional; recursive-cybernetic) are not competing alternatives; they are complementary mechanism-strata through which the meta-law of self-completion operates. The shrinking of human portion of functionality is therefore overdetermined: it is mechanism-supported at every stratum the literature has examined. This overdetermination is what makes the dispossession structurally robust — and what makes any resistance prescription that addresses only one stratum, however brilliant, structurally insufficient.
