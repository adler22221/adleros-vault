## 1. The Body as the Universe's First Claim on the Self

The thesis's argument about *life-as-willed* requires a concrete account of what the will encounters as resistant. The body is that first resistance. The body is not an instrument the will picks up; it is the condition under which there is any will at all — and a condition that imposes its own temporality, materiality, and finitude in ways that the self-finishing tendency (§IV) is structurally bound to register as obstacle, failure, or inadequacy.

The body-as-obstacle is not a modern discovery. It is the perennial human experience of the gap between the *willed self* (the self as the will conceives and projects it) and the *given self* (the self as the universe-stratum has materially deposited it). What is historically specific to the contemporary moment is the emergence of technical environments — culminating in the metaverse — that promise, for the first time at scale, to close this gap: to give the will more complete control over the conditions of its own expression by relocating existence into environments where the body's material limitations are suspended, overridden, or rendered cosmetic.

Read through the universe / world / metaverse triad, the body occupies a determinate ontological position. The body is the only stratum at which the universe (as `dr`-level generative substrate, in Bhaskar's vocabulary; see ANNOT-L3) and the self (as world-located, meaning-organized agent) are *not separable*. Every other access the self has to the universe-substrate is mediated — by perception, by tool, by language, by symbol. Only at the body is the substrate itself the self. This is what makes the body the universe's *first* claim on the self: not first chronologically, but first in the order of necessity. To be a self at all is to be a body that the universe has and continues to produce.

This fact is the structural reason the self-finishing tendency must register the body as obstacle. Self-finishing — the disposition, articulated by Uegaki Takahide, by which the individual organizes the totality of relational, productive, and consumptive life within self-selected, algorithmically managed environments [VERIFY: confirmed | uegakiJikokanketsuShakai2017jo | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"〈自己完結社会〉 ーそれは情報技術、ロボット/人工知能技術…〈社会的装置〉に人々が深く依存し、生身の他者と関わっていく必然性、生身の身体とともに生きる必然性が失われていく社会のことである" — see selfcompleting-totalism-tier0-retrieval.md cit.1; maps to 2022a/b — locate page in Zotero CYAAA3IK/VESTLJGF PDF] — is the project of constructing a life whose every constitutive element passes through the filter of *apparent choice*. The body is the one element this project cannot incorporate. The body is given. The body is what the self has not chosen and could not have chosen, because the body is the condition of choosing. To the self-finishing logic, the body therefore appears not as the self's substrate but as the self's *limit case* — the residual unchosenness that the curated layer cannot absorb.

This is the somatic correlate of self-finishing: the body becomes obstacle not because it has changed but because the relational structure surrounding it has changed. The body's recalcitrance — its rhythms, its aging, its illness, its appearance — is the same recalcitrance the body has always had. What is new is that the self that registers this recalcitrance now inhabits a relational structure in which everything *else* is curated. Against a wholly given world, the body's givenness is unremarkable; against a wholly curated world, the body's givenness becomes conspicuous, embarrassing, intolerable.

## 2. Concrete Examples of the Body's Recalcitrance

### 2.1 The body's basic rhythms as interruption

Sleep, hunger, fatigue, and pain are the body's most basic assertions of its own agenda against the will's projects. The will wants to continue working; the body demands sleep. The will wants to maintain focus; the body imposes hunger. The will wants to perform; the body breaks down.

The contemporary repertoire of responses to these assertions is systematically antagonistic in form: stimulants to override fatigue; appetite suppressants and GLP-1 agonists to manage hunger; continuous-glucose monitors to optimize metabolic performance; sleep-tracking devices and chronopharmacology to rationalize sleep. Each technology constitutes an attempt to manage the body's rhythms according to the will's priorities rather than the universe-time to which the body is evolutionarily calibrated. The body's assertion of its universe-time against the self's willed-time is then experienced not as a *given* but as a *malfunction*. The phenomenology is diagnostic: where the body is felt as bug rather than baseline, the self-finishing horizon has already supplied the standard against which "baseline" is measured.

### 2.2 Aging — the widening gap between willed and biological self

Aging makes the gap between the *experienced* self (the self the will inhabits) and the biological body increasingly visible and increasingly resistant to closure. The self that the will maintains — its sense of competence, its energy, its social identity — does not age at the rate at which the biological body ages. The result is a form of daily dispossession: the discovery, in the mirror, in the physiotherapist's office, in the social situation that privileges youth, that the body is not following the will's project.

The cosmetic industries, the longevity research complex (Calico Life Sciences, Altos Labs, the broader anti-aging biotech sector), the supplement market, and the broader gerontological anxiety of contemporary culture are all responses to this dispossession — all attempts to manage the body's aging according to the will's preference for non-aging. The metaverse offers a more radical solution: in the avatar-mediated environment, the represented self need not age at all; the body's temporal claims on the self are suspended at the level of presentation. Uegaki's *漂流人* (drifter) figure of late-modern recognition anxiety [VERIFY: confirmed | uegakiJikokanketsuShakai2017jo | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"〈漂流人〉は、生まれながらにして〈存在の連なり〉から浮遊し、「〈共同〉のための意味」も「〈共同〉のための技能」も受け継がれることなく成長していく" — see drifter-ethics-tier0-retrieval.md cit.1; locate page in Zotero PDF] inhabits, in the metaverse's controlled environments, a self-image unconstrained by biological time — at the price that the unrepresented body continues to age regardless.

### 2.3 Illness and chronic conditions

Chronic illness and chronic pain impose the body's agenda with particular force. They are not temporary interruptions but structural features of lived experience that make the gap between the willed self and the biological self permanent and unresolvable through individual effort. The person with chronic fatigue syndrome, fibromyalgia, ME/CFS, or a progressive degenerative condition encounters the body as the universe's constant claim — a claim that does not yield to the will's projects, planning, or optimization.

Chronic illness is for that reason one of the clearest cases in which the body functions as the universe's assertion against the self-finishing tendency. It is also the case in which the self-finishing apparatus does its most ambivalent work. The digital environments that allow individuals with mobility limitations or chronic conditions to maintain social, educational, and economic participation — which is genuinely valuable, and the value is not to be discounted — simultaneously instantiate the universe-metaverse squeeze at the body level: what makes the metaverse *livable* for these individuals is precisely its suspension of the universe-time constraints (mobility, physical presence, embodied co-location) that the body imposes. The mediation is enabling and enclosing in the same gesture. The point of analysis is not to evaluate the trade-off but to see the structure: where the body cannot be brought into alignment with the will's project by intervention on the body, the self-finishing apparatus offers alignment by intervention on the surrounding mediation instead.

### 2.4 Cosmetic intervention and the body-modification economy

Cosmetic surgery, dermatological intervention, body-contouring procedures, and the broader aesthetic-medicine economy have grown consistently across every major economy over the past three decades [LOOKUP: ISAPS Global Survey 2022 / ASPS national plastic surgery statistics — exact figures]. This growth is the clearest quantitative indicator of the body-as-obstacle dynamic operating at the surface register of appearance.

It is essential to frame this case carefully. The argument is *not* that patients pursuing cosmetic intervention are failing, deceived, victims of false consciousness, or inadequately reflective about their choices. They are not. The autonomy-respecting feminist scholarship on cosmetic surgery — Kathy Davis's *Reshaping the Female Body* and her subsequent essays [LOOKUP: `davisReshapingFemaleBody1995`; `davisDubiousEqualitiesEmbodied2003`], and Iris Marion Young's phenomenological work on lived embodiment [LOOKUP: `youngThrowingLikeGirl1980`; `youngOnFemaleBodyExperience2005`] — has long since established that to read women's (or men's) cosmetic-surgical decisions as merely the internalization of patriarchal or commercial pressure is itself a failure of analytical respect: it denies agency to the very subjects whose conditions of choice the critique purports to defend.

What the autonomy-respecting frame *does* license is a structural critique of the *conditions* under which the choice is made. Patients pursuing cosmetic intervention are responding rationally to a structural condition: their *mediated self-image* — the face on the video-call grid, in the social-media feed, in the dating-app preview — has become a primary self-reference, and the technically available means of bringing the materially given face into closer alignment with that primary self-reference are surgical, dermatological, and pharmacological. Within that condition, intervention on the medium-amenable domain (the body's appearance) is a *reasonable* allocation of effort; it is what a competent agent does when the medium reshapes the substrate. The point of structural critique is not to fault the choice but to make the conditions of the choice analytically visible — and to ask whether the conditions themselves are ones the broader collective should accept as background.

The "Zoom dysmorphia" reported by clinical dermatologists during the COVID-19 pandemic [LOOKUP: Rajanala, Maymone & Vashi 2018, "Selfies — Living in the Era of Filtered Photographs," *JAMA Facial Plastic Surgery* — citekey not in current Zotero `thesis-sources`; also LOOKUP: clinical reports on Zoom dysmorphia 2020–2021, e.g., Kourosh & Goldenberg in *International Journal of Women's Dermatology*, 2021] — the marked increase in patients seeking procedures specifically to correct what they had observed in their video-call self-images — is a direct measurement of the medium-substrate dynamic. The video-mediated face is not the face the patient ever sees in the mirror; it is laterally inverted, lit by ring-light, foreshortened by webcam optics, and rendered at compressed bit-depth on a screen the patient stares at for hours daily. The "self" the patient has become acquainted with through this medium is, in a non-trivial sense, a different self than the mirror-self. The request for intervention is the request to bring the substrate into agreement with what the medium has, by overwhelming exposure, made the *primary* image of the self.

This is the appearance-squeeze documented in §VI-2 operating through the body. It is because the biological face is increasingly experienced as the inadequate material substrate of the algorithmically-shaped mediated self that aesthetic intervention accelerates. The squeeze does its work not through false consciousness but through the structure of mediation itself: patients respond accurately to a real change in the relative authority of mirror and medium, and their response is the rational response within the conditions given.

The critique the thesis makes is therefore directed at the conditions, not at the response. To pathologize the patient is both unjust to her and analytically misdirected. To name the condition — that under saturated image-mediation the medium-self acquires primary self-reference, and that the body becomes legible as the obstacle to alignment with that primary self — is the structural diagnosis the thesis is in a position to make.

## 3. From Body-as-Obstacle to the Self-Finishing Spectrum

The body-as-obstacle phenomenon is the somatic surface of a deeper structural transformation that runs across multiple registers. To make the deeper structure visible — and to license the claim that body-as-obstacle is not an isolated phenomenon but the somatic articulation of a single trajectory — the following passage maps the trajectory at three registers.

[NOTE: from ANNOT-G1; coheres at real-level per ANNOT-L3]

> The trajectory by which the human becomes increasingly enclosed within a self-curated artificial layer can be read as a single spectrum running from the biological register through the cultural-historical to the ontological-social. At its biological end stands the self-domestication hypothesis: that *Homo sapiens* underwent Pleistocene selection against reactive aggression — through, on Wrangham's reconstruction, language-coordinated collective sanction against persistently aggressive individuals — producing a docility syndrome of neoteny, reduced craniofacial robusticity, and heightened social cognition (Wrangham 2019, `wranghamGoodnessParadox2019` [VERIFY: page]; Hare, Wobber & Wrangham 2012, `hareSelfdomesticationHypothesisEvolution2012` [VERIFY: page]). The mechanism at this register is collective human behaviour acting on the human gene pool over evolutionary time. *The body the contemporary self inherits as obstacle is, at this register, already a self-modified body — the somatic deposit of two million years of intra-specific selection.*
>
> Obara Hideo extended this into a cultural-historical register through his theory of human self-petification (自己家畜化論), in which routinized practice and built environment progressively pattern the body and disposition into stone-like fixity — appetites, postures, and rhythms cease to be elastic responses to an unchosen cosmos and become precise correlates of a curated milieu (Obara 1995 [LOOKUP]; Obara 2000 [LOOKUP]; reconstructed critically in Uegaki 2014, `uegakiJikoKachikuKa2014` [VERIFY: confirmed | uegakiJikoKachikuKa2014 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"に相応しい存在様式，すなわち人間存在の「自然さ」というものを問題とすることで，「自然さ」との整合性を失いつつある現代文明のライフスタイルを批判する「自己ペット化」の概念" — see drifter-ethics-tier0-retrieval.md cit.9 (UUID 14b62364); locate page in Zotero YCFPXYI8 PDF]). Where biological self-domestication selects on the gene pool, self-petification operates on the body and habitus through the sedimentation of an artificial environment that humans themselves curate for themselves. *At this register the body becomes obstacle in a more determinate way: the petrified body — habituated to climate-controlled interiors, calorie-dense diets, sedentary postures, and machine-paced cognition — registers any encounter with unmediated substrate (a long walk, a missed meal, an overheated room) as discomfort exceeding the discomfort an un-petrified body would register.*
>
> Uegaki Takahide (2020, `J3YDIF8D`; 2022a/b, `uegakiJikoKanketsuShakai2022a`, `uegakiJikoKanketsuShakai2022b`) names the present actualization: the *self-completing society* (自己完結社会), in which individuals arrange the totality of their relational, productive, and consumptive life within self-selected, algorithmically managed environments. Recognition, provision, and meaning are routed through systems the individual has apparently chosen, with the consequence that the unchosen world progressively ceases to function as a given horizon of encounter. The register is ontological-social; its mechanism is infrastructural-algorithmic mediation; its timescale is the past three decades. *At this register the body becomes obstacle in the most acute and conspicuous form: the self that has organized its relations, its work, its provisioning, and its leisure within curated environments encounters the body as the one stratum of life that resists incorporation into the self-finishing logic. Aging, illness, appearance, hunger, and fatigue are the residual unchosenness that the curated layer cannot absorb — and the self-finishing apparatus accordingly extends to the body itself, through pharmacological, surgical, and (prospectively) bioengineering means.*
>
> The three registers should not be read as a chronological succession in which each replaces its predecessor. They *layer*. Contemporary humans inhabit a self-domesticated body, a self-petrified disposition, and an increasingly self-finished relational structure simultaneously. What licenses treating the three as a single trajectory is a shared real-level tendency — the progressive substitution of a curated artificial mediation layer for the unchosen cosmic substrate as the proximate environment of human formation. Each step thickens the world-layer between the human and the universe; the metaverse register threatens to detach the world from the universe altogether. The spectrum is thus not a teleology but a tendency that has been actualized under specific historical-material conditions (settled agriculture, industrial urbanism, networked computation) and remains, in principle, contestable — though the cost of contestation rises asymmetrically at each step.

The body-as-obstacle phenomenon documented in §2 above is, on this reading, the somatic display surface of the spectrum's full operation. Sleep deprivation, metabolic optimization, and chronopharmacology address the body at the self-petrification register (the body that has been habituated out of its evolved rhythms by a curated milieu). Aging anxiety, longevity biotech, and cosmetic intervention address the body at the self-finishing register (the body that, in a relational structure of universal apparent choice, is the one stratum of givenness left). The three registers are co-active in any concrete person, and the body bears the load of all three at once.

## 4. The Body as the Somatic Correlate of 自己完結社会

Returning now from the spectrum to the specifically Uegaki-anchored argument: the body-as-obstacle is the somatic correlate of the *self-completing society*'s relational enclosure. To say this precisely:

Uegaki characterizes 自己完結社会 as a society in which individuals organize the conditions of their recognition, provision, and meaning within self-selected, algorithmically managed environments [VERIFY: confirmed | uegakiJikokanketsuShakai2017jo | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt:"〈自己完結社会〉 ーそれは情報技術、ロボット/人工知能技術…に人々が深く依存し、生身の他者と関わっていく必然性…が失われていく社会" — see selfcompleting-totalism-tier0-retrieval.md cit.1; also see cit.3 for 自立した個人 / non-intervention articulation; maps to 2022a/b — locate pages in Zotero CYAAA3IK/VESTLJGF PDFs]. The constitutive feature of such a society is that the *unchosen world* — the horizon of encounter that the individual has not curated — progressively ceases to function as a structural element of life. Encounters with otherness, contingency, and recalcitrance are routed through systems whose terms the individual has (apparently) accepted in advance, and whose recalcitrance is therefore experienced not as the world's resistance but as the system's malfunction.

The body is the one site at which this routing fails. The body does not run on terms the individual has accepted in advance; the body's terms predate any individual self and condition the existence of any individual self. The body is the residual *unchosen world inside the self*. As the rest of the relational structure becomes self-completing, the body's unchosen-ness becomes correspondingly conspicuous. What in a previous configuration was simply *the body* — a continuous given of which the self was the lived expression — becomes, in the configuration of self-completing society, *the body as obstacle to self-completion*: the residual recalcitrance that the curated layer cannot absorb.

This is what licenses the formulation that closes the section: the body-as-obstacle is the *somatic correlate of relational enclosure*. It is not the case that the body has changed. It is the case that the relational structure within which the body is registered has changed, such that the body's unchanged facticity now appears under a new aspect — the aspect of obstacle. The phenomenon is Uegaki's at one stratum (relational structure) and is the same phenomenon at another (somatic experience). To analyze the body-as-obstacle without the relational frame is to mistake the symptom for the condition. To analyze 自己完結社会 without the somatic frame is to miss the stratum at which the relational logic is most acutely felt — and the stratum at which it must, for any actually embodied human, founder.

It also follows that the metaverse, as the maximally curated relational environment, is the configuration in which body-as-obstacle becomes most acute. Uegaki's *Drifter* figure inhabits the metaverse not because the metaverse is more pleasant than the universe-substrate (it is not, in metabolic terms — the body still requires the universe's metabolic inputs while the avatar inhabits the curated environment) but because the metaverse, alone among environments, allows the *self-image* to operate without being constantly reproved by the body's recalcitrance. The relief is real; the relief is also temporary, because the body is still there, still aging, still hungry, still tired, still ill — only now operating largely outside the field of the self's curated attention. Self-finishing does not resolve body-as-obstacle. It relocates the body from foreground to *unattended background*, where the body's claims continue to accumulate without the self's adaptive response.

This is the real-level structural reason that the metaverse cannot be the resolution of the squeeze on the world-stratum. The metaverse, however far it extends, leaves the body in the universe — and the body, as the universe's first claim on the self, will continue to call the self back to a stratum the metaverse cannot incorporate. The squeeze on the world is the *form* under which the impossibility of full self-completion appears; the body's persistent recalcitrance is the *fact* in which the impossibility resides.
