## Core Definition (User's Own Framing — Preserve Exactly)

**Learning by Caring** is an educational model developed as a direct response to Allocation Dependence. It reframes learning as a **regenerative process rooted in prosocial action, reciprocity, and intrinsic motivation**.

Rather than reshaping individuals to fit external criteria, it begins with what learners genuinely care about — a social issue, a personal experience, the wellbeing of someone or something in their lives. The caring act becomes the organizing principle of the learning process.

**The relational cycle** (user's own model):
1. **Learner as Carer** — growth through caring itself
2. **Cared & Their Community** — engaging real-world people and issues
3. **Reciprocity** — mutual benefit generates feedback and new opportunities
4. **Caring as Self-Actualization** — development becomes inseparable from contribution

The key inversion: "instead of desperately fitting into allocation measures to redeem resources, resources naturally flow to them as others reciprocate to their caring endeavors."

## The Teacher-as-Habitat Critique (User's Distinctive Move)

From user task file (2026-05-22, in-preparation — do not cite directly):

> "At a micro level, the teachers themselves can become a ceiling or the 'habitat' that students must adapt to in order to thrive in the classroom. Because the student is dependent on the teacher for learning, education, and resources, the scope of this habitat becomes a limiting factor."

This is the micro-level complement to the macro-level critique of marketization/neoliberalism. Both are manifestations of Allocation Dependence at different scales:
- **Macro**: the market dictates the direction of student development based on resource availability
- **Micro**: the teacher becomes the habitat whose expectations, motivations, and aspirations determine what the student can become

**Under Learning by Caring**: educators and parents, instead of setting boundaries of the habitat, become **supporting grounds** for students. The locus of organization shifts from teacher-as-boundary to caring-as-self-organizing-principle.

## Contrasts with Existing Models (User's Positioning)

| Model | Problem Identified by User |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Dewey's "Learn by Doing"** | Fails to address resource dependency. Education remains directed by resource-holders. Without addressing this dependency through the learning process itself, the framework is captured by the same allocation logic it purports to escape. |
| **Problem-based learning** | Still "cookbook-like" — problems are predetermined or assigned by the teacher, not arising from authentic care of the learner |
| **Learning by Reasoning** | Does not generate value creation; remains within the knowledge-reproduction logic |
| **Ultralearning** | Is 術 (technique/method), not 道 (Way/principle); needed only for artificial or non-natural goals. Most people only need the 道 — axiologically good and effective |
| **Susan Wolf's "Fitting Fulfillment"** | Requires an external "objective arbiter" to adjudicate what is objectively attractive. LbC resolves this: feedback from situated caring action provides external appraisals that are beyond the actor's subjectivity without requiring a neutral arbiter |

## Key Properties of Learning by Caring (User's Framework)

**Inherently transdisciplinary / anti-disciplinary**: Because the organizing principle is the caring aspiration, not a disciplinary framework. "Everything that is required to achieve aspirations of care needs to be learned on the go and become interconnected." There is little point in following existing disciplinary structures.

**Agent-based and adaptive**: "The act of care can happen in any circumstances by utilizing whatever is at hand." No predetermined requirements for sufficient educational resources. Enables learning without being dragged behind by resource availability — because anything available can be utilized toward the act of care.

**Dignity equality**: The measure is the learner's own sense of self-actualization or transcendence, not a standardized common measure. "A very rich student can be in the very beginning of his or her learning-by-caring journey... whereas a materially marginalized student can achieve greatly to the extent that he or she feels satisfied with his or her act of care."

**AI-proof by design**: Learning is not appraised by truth/falsity (where AI can substitute) but by contribution to value creation. The caring act necessitates:
1. Human action directed toward and received by another human or agent of concern
2. Adaptation, empathy, understanding, and reflection in the caring act itself
3. A learner recalibrating from the real response received (AI cannot do this feedback loop alone)

**Intersubjective**: Caring is not single-directional. "The act of care must not be the fulfillment of predetermined measures, but a constantly evolving, always adapting means of the other. It requires the other to be recognized, to be understood, to be empathized with, and to be treated with dignity."

**Social cohesion driver**: Caring as intersubjective act requires recognizing otherness → counters competition-toward-predetermined-measures → addresses social polarization. Possible bridge to civic solidarity and collective action impulse.

## Capital Formation Without Financial Determinism

User explicitly notes (task file): Learning by Caring "builds some forms of capital (social, cultural, and perhaps even symbolic capital in their respective fields or communities of concern)" but "doesn't necessarily bring financial capital."

This is the user's honest acknowledgment of a structural limitation — and it maps directly to the care-worker literature (BF3 cross-reference: Tronto, Noddings on the undervaluation of care labor). The user frames this not as a failure of the model but as a systemic condition requiring political-economic attention — bridging to the allocation dependence angle.

## Empirical Base (Awakening Cooperative Lab)

Mentoring 16 youth under 18 (7 from disadvantaged backgrounds) through real-world collaborations:
- 5 gained top-tier university admission (4 from disadvantaged backgrounds)
- 8 pursued alternative paths with stable, passion-driven careers
- Notable outcomes included award winners and social impact leaders

User's methodological stance: the emphasis is "not on how brilliant my intervention was, but on the causal power of learning by caring itself. Once the gist of learning by caring is comprehended by the learner, it nearly becomes automatic and doesn't require my further intervention."

Proposed chapter method: educational, philosophical, and social theoretical approach + autoethnography of personal journey + action research on existing practice.

## Connection to Field Literature (BF3 Cross-Reference)

The care-pedagogy literature (Noddings, Tronto, Bozalek/Zembylas) provides Western anchors. User's "Learning by Caring" differs in its:
1. **Structural critique of dependency**: grounded in a causal mechanism (allocation dependence) rather than values/ethics alone
2. **Agent-based self-organization**: not a teacher-led caring curriculum but a learner-led caring practice
3. **East Asian relational ontology**: the Daoist 道 (Way) vs 術 (technique) distinction positions care not as a supplement to learning but as its foundational ontological mode (see BU3 for East Asian angle)

Closest existing neighbor: **Common Worlds Collective** (Taylor, Pacini-Ketchabaw, Nxumalo) — relational more-than-human pedagogies. But LbC is explicitly human-social-first before scaling to ecological, and is grounded in a political-economic critique (allocation dependence) that the Common Worlds literature lacks.
