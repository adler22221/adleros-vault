## B. Terms and Definitions (with page)

### Core triadic terms

**〈人間〉 (Human)** — pp. 106–108  
Distinguished from the biological body "ヒト" (Hito = the anatomical organism). 〈Human〉 is the *subject* who is constituted *through* 〈Society〉. The "Robinson Crusoe raised from birth on a desert island" thought-experiment illustrates that a 'ヒト' is not automatically a '人間': subjectivity requires social mediation. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 107–108, lines 4021–4040 approx.]

**〈社会〉 (Society)** — pp. 106–108  
The "artificially constructed ecosystem" (人為的生態系) layered on top of the natural ecosystem. Comprises three strata: (i) 社会的構造物 (social structures/artefacts), (ii) 社会的制度 (social institutions), (iii) 意味体系＝世界像 (meaning-systems = world-pictures). This triad is already established in Ch. 3 and is presupposed throughout Ch. 4.

**〈自然〉 (Nature)** — pp. 106–108  
The natural ecosystem underlying 〈Society〉. Used both as bio-ecological substrate and as the horizon of constraint against which 〈Society〉 must maintain "consistency" (整合性).

**三項関係 (triadic relation)** — p. 106  
The analytic frame: the three terms are mutually constitutive but differentiable. Each 特異点 is defined as a *structural transformation* (構造転換) of this triadic relation — a shift in how the three poles are articulated. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 106, lines 3934–3944]

**特異点 (singularity)** — p. 105  
Qualitative inflection-points in human history where the three-term relation undergoes large-scale structural transformation. Not gradual evolution but discontinuous threshold-crossings. Uegaki identifies two confirmed and one emergent singularity in this chapter.

**整合性 (consistency / coherence with Nature)** — p. 120–121  
Pre-modern 〈Society〉 had to remain "consistent" with the natural ecosystem; the second singularity dissolves this constraint. The third singularity dissolves the analogous consistency with 〈Human〉.

**〈社会的装置〉 (social apparatus)** — p. 122  
The term Uegaki uses to designate the structural supports of 〈Society〉 that become autonomous in the modern mode: state bureaucracy + market economy as the twin pillars. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 122, line 4542 approx.]

**〈自己完結社会〉 (self-finishing society)** — pp. 122–125  
The name for a society that has passed through the third singularity. A society in which 〈Society〉 expands purely on its own logic, having severed its consistency both with 〈Nature〉 (singularity 2) and now with 〈Human〉 (singularity 3). See §C below for full definition.

## C. The Three Singularities — Full Reconstruction

### 第一の特異点 — "The Establishment of Agriculture" and "Indirection between 〈Nature〉 and 〈Human〉" (p. 109)

**Historical anchor:** ~10,000 years ago; agriculture arises independently in multiple regions (Southwest Asia ~11,000 BP, China ~9,000 BP, Americas ~4,000–5,000 BP). [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 111–112, lines 4108–4114]

**Pre-singularity baseline:** In the hunter-gatherer mode, 〈Society〉's influence is minimal. The dominant relationship is direct: 〈Human〉 ↔ 〈Nature〉, governed by natural selection. The "band" unit (25–50 people) has very limited accumulation of the social. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 111, lines 4076–4094]

**The transformation:** Agriculture does not merely change subsistence technique; it initiates the explosive growth of 〈Society〉 as a self-accumulating system. Key effects: (a) settled society; (b) large-scale social units with stratification and division of labour; (c) political hierarchy and territorial states; (d) accelerated knowledge and technological accumulation; (e) cascading agriculturalisation of surrounding groups. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 112–113, lines 4147–4170]

**Structural transformation in the triadic relation:** Before this singularity, 〈Human〉 faced 〈Nature〉 directly; the dominant force was natural selection. After it, every relationship between 〈Human〉 and 〈Nature〉 is *mediated* through 〈Society〉. Uegaki calls this 「〈人間〉と〈自然〉の間接化」 — "indirection between 〈Human〉 and 〈Nature〉." The biological body (ヒト) is effectively shielded from natural selection from this point onwards. All subsequent human transformation becomes a transformation of 〈Society〉, not of the organism. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 113, lines 4188–4207]

**Key formulation:** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 113, lines 4188–4196] — 〈Human〉's relationship with 〈Nature〉 becomes "indirect" (間接的) because 〈Society〉, now a thick accumulating layer, permanently intervenes. The logic driving change is no longer natural selection but "the logic of 〈Society〉 itself" (〈社会〉の論理).

### 第二の特異点 — "The Establishment of the Modern Social Mode" and "Severance between 〈Society〉 and 〈Nature〉" (p. 114)

**Historical anchor:** Roughly the last several hundred years; the three constitutive elements — nation-state, market economy, fossil fuels — coalesce and spread from Europe, reaching a kind of completion in "the 20th century." [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 117–120, lines 4211–4402]

**Pre-singularity baseline (post-singularity 1):** The agro-civilizational world is a patchwork of "traditional communities" (伝統的共同体) and "urban communities" (都市共同体). Both remain embedded in natural ecology and non-monetary mutual aid; 〈Society〉 must maintain "consistency" with 〈Nature〉. Ancient civilisations that failed to do so collapsed. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 115–116, 120–121, lines 4228–4461]

**The three constitutive elements:**

1. **国民国家 (nation-state):** Dissolves all intermediate social units (communities, guilds, kin-groups) and reconstitutes individual human beings as the direct atoms of a legally created sovereign entity. Produces a massive bureaucratic apparatus to integrate spatially dispersed individuals. Theoretical root: social contract theory. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 116–118, lines 4272–4326]

2. **市場経済 (market economy):** Polanyi's "disembedding": the market was formerly embedded within social/non-monetary relations; modernity dis-embeds it and makes all production and consumption pass through commodity exchange. Everything becomes a "good" with property rights, and every act becomes a "service." The informal non-monetary welfare net of traditional community is replaced by market-mediated equivalents. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 118–119, lines 4327–4375; Polanyi reference at line 4343]

3. **化石燃料 (fossil fuels):** Shifts the entire energetic base of 〈Society〉 away from wood/animal/water/wind power. Coal was a reluctant substitute for depleted wood; the steam engine emerged as a pump for flooded mines before being repurposed. This shift made 〈Society〉's material expansion effectively unlimited *within geological timescales*. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 119–120, lines 4376–4402; Ponting reference at line 4391]

**Structural transformation in the triadic relation:** Before this singularity, 〈Society〉 was constrained — it had to maintain *coherence* (整合性) with the natural ecosystem. The combination of bureaucratic state, market economy, and fossil energy removes that constraint. 〈Society〉 can now expand indefinitely on "pure social logic" alone — growing the more it can extract, ignoring natural-systemic feedback. Uegaki calls this 「〈社会〉と〈自然〉の切断」 — "severance between 〈Society〉 and 〈Nature〉." [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 120–121, lines 4446–4470]

**Key formulation:** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 121, lines 4462–4470] — Under the modern social mode, natural-ecological influence is placed unilaterally under scientific-technological prediction and control. The bureaucratic apparatus and market economy, fuelled by fossil energy, sustain the continuous expansion of 〈Society〉 without reference to natural limits. The world-picture that legitimates this is scientific rationality. Uegaki concludes: this singularity is symbolically named "severance between 〈Society〉 and 〈Nature〉."

### 第三の特異点 — 〈自己完結社会〉 = "Severance between 〈Society〉 and 〈Human〉" (pp. 122–125)

**Status:** Uegaki frames this as the singularity we are *currently passing through*. It is not retrospectively confirmed (unlike singularities 1 and 2) but is diagnosed as an emergent threshold. The chapter can only sketch its contours; fuller elaboration is deferred to later chapters.

**Continuity with singularity 2:** 〈自己完結社会〉 is explicitly continuous with the second singularity — the 〈社会的装置〉 (market economy + bureaucracy) that constitutes it was established at that stage. The self-finishing society is therefore "the endpoint of the second singularity's trajectory." [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 122–123, lines 4540–4548]

**Non-continuity / the new element:** Yet it is not *merely* a continuation. Something qualitatively new has appeared: computers, the internet, digital technology create an "information world" (情報世界); information, machines, and pharmaceuticals automate experience without conscious awareness; even the finite body is placed under prediction-and-control in the name of individual "self-realisation." These cannot be reduced to the logic of 〈Society〉/〈Nature〉 severance alone. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 123–124, lines 4555–4566]

**The third structural transformation:** Just as singularity 1 placed 〈Society〉 between 〈Human〉 and 〈Nature〉 (indirection), and singularity 2 severed 〈Society〉 from 〈Nature〉 (first severance), singularity 3 introduces a *second severance* — now between 〈Society〉 and 〈Human〉. The explosive 〈Society〉 that has already cut itself from 〈Nature〉 is now cutting itself from 〈Human〉 as well. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 124, lines 4570–4580]

**Key formulation:** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 124, lines 4570–4580] — "We are at this very moment passing through the third singularity, which can be called '〈Society〉 and 〈Human〉 severance' (〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断)." For the first time in 7 million years of human history, *the living presence of other embodied persons* is no longer necessary for life, and *the finite body itself* is losing its meaning.

**The biological body as evidence:** The ヒト body has not changed genetically since singularity 1. We carry a hunter-gatherer body into hyper-artificial urban space. Kobara Hideo's (小原秀雄) concept of "petification" of modern humans is invoked: modern social conditions have moved beyond what the ヒト body presupposes as "natural" (ナチュラルさ), generating social pathologies. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 125, lines 4597–4605]

**Definition of 〈自己完結社会〉:** The society that has passed through the third singularity — a society in which 〈Society〉 (as 〈社会的装置〉: bureaucracy + market economy) has become so overgrown and autonomous that it has lost its "coherence" (整合性) first with 〈Nature〉, then with 〈Human〉. At its macroscopic level: the social apparatus overrides the lifeworld; 〈生の自己完結化〉 (self-enclosure of life) and 〈生の脱身体化〉 (de-embodiment of life) are its symptomatic expressions. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp. 122–125, lines 4505–4606; fuller definition flagged at lines 6470 and 6563 in later chapters]

**The chapter closes with a deferral:** Uegaki explicitly states that the third singularity cannot yet be fully theorised because the conceptual apparatus is still incomplete. The chapter will return to it after introducing new theoretical tools. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p. 125, lines 4607–4611]

## F. Relevance to Thesis Ch. IV §C: Typology / Cell-3→Cell-4 Migration / Three Singularities

**1. The three singularities as the historical spine for Cell progression**  
Ch. IV §C of the thesis builds a typology of 〈社会的装置〉 modes. Uegaki's three singularities map directly onto this: singularity 1 (agriculture) inaugurates the cell-trajectory; singularity 2 (modern social mode) marks the passage to a fully autonomous 〈Society〉 — what the thesis might call a Cell-3 configuration; singularity 3 (〈自己完結社会〉) marks the Cell-4 / terminal configuration in which 〈Society〉 has also severed itself from 〈Human〉.

**2. The 三項関係 as shared vocabulary**  
The thesis already uses 自然/社会/人間 as a three-term structure. Uegaki's Ch. 4 provides its historical operationalisation: the three terms are not static — each singularity *restructures their articulation*. This allows the thesis to claim that the self-finishing society is not a mere quantitative expansion but a genuinely qualitative threshold.

**3. The 〈社会的装置〉 concept**  
Uegaki's definition — bureaucratic apparatus + market economy as the autonomous pillars — is the source for the thesis's use of this term. It is explicitly tied to singularity 2 (its origin) and singularity 3 (its hypertrophic endpoint). The thesis should cite this when characterising the apparatus dimension of the metaverse.

**4. 整合性 as diagnostic concept**  
The progressive loss of "coherence" with 〈Nature〉 then with 〈Human〉 provides the thesis with a *criterion* for what makes the self-finishing society a singularity rather than merely a continuation. This aligns with the thesis's squeeze hypothesis: the universe-metaverse squeeze is the singularity 3 configuration in which 〈Human〉 is being displaced between 〈Universe〉 (= 〈Nature〉 in Uegaki's terms) and 〈Metaverse〉 (= the self-finishing social apparatus).

**5. The ヒト/人間 distinction**  
Uegaki's argument that the biological body has not changed since singularity 1, while the social environment has become maximally artificial, is strong evidential backing for the thesis's "body-as-obstacle" motif (see `Ch-4-5_v2_body-as-obstacle.md`) and for the squeeze's existential stakes.

**6. Kobara (小原秀雄) reference**  
The "petification" concept (〈ペット化〉) appears here and elsewhere in Uegaki (Ch. 3). It is already used in the thesis; this chapter contextualises it *within* the third singularity argument, which strengthens the causal chain: singularity 3 → loss of ナチュラルさ → petification / de-embodiment.

## G. Final Report — 8 Bullets

- **Chapter function:** Ch. 4 is Uegaki's historical argument for why the 〈自己完結社会〉 is not a contingent cultural development but a *structural terminus* of a long triadic transformation — making it the legitimating backbone for the thesis's claim that the self-finishing society represents a qualitatively new predicament.

- **第一の特異点 (pp. 109–113):** Agriculture (~10,000 BP). Structural effect: 〈Society〉 becomes the permanent *medium* between 〈Human〉 and 〈Nature〉. The direct human-nature interface is replaced by an indirect one. Named: 「〈人間〉と〈自然〉の間接化」. Consequence: all subsequent human transformation is social, not biological; the ヒト body stabilises from this point.

- **第二の特異点 (pp. 114–121):** Modern social mode (~18th–20th c.). Three constitutive elements: nation-state (disembeds individuals from communities), market economy (disembeds economy from social relations — Polanyi), fossil fuels (provides unlimited energetic substrate). Structural effect: 〈Society〉 is *severed* from 〈Nature〉's constraining coherence — it now operates purely by internal logic. Named: 「〈社会〉と〈自然〉の切断」.

- **第三の特異点(pp. 122–125):** 〈自己完結社会〉 — emergent/current. The 〈Society〉 that has already severed itself from 〈Nature〉 now severs itself from 〈Human〉. Structural effect: for the first time in human history, bodily co-presence and embodied finitude become superfluous to life. Named: 「〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断」. The 自己完結社会 is the *society that has passed this threshold*.

- **Triadic logic across all three:** Each singularity adds a new "cut" — first the direct Human/Nature interface is mediated (indirection); then the Society/Nature bond is severed (first severance); finally the Society/Human bond is severed (second severance). The progression is strictly cumulative.

- **〈社会的装置〉:** Uegaki's paired concept — bureaucracy + market economy — is the specific institutional form through which singularity 2 produces and singularity 3 completes the self-finishing society. It is the "spine" of 〈自己完結社会〉 and the thesis's Ch. IV typological endpoint.

- **Figure 3 is critical but unreadable from OCR.** Needs to be read from the physical book at pp. 122–123. It apparently diagrammes the triadic reconfiguration across singularities 1 and 2 (singularity 3 is by implication the rightmost column).

- **Deferral note:** Uegaki explicitly defers full theorisation of the third singularity to later chapters (Ch. 6 in the 下巻 appears from line searches to contain the elaboration, including the macro-definition of 〈自己完結社会〉 at line 6470 as: 〈社会的装置〉 overwhelming 〈生活世界〉). The thesis may need to draw on those later chapters for the full definition. [VERIFY-PAGE: lines 6470, 6476 — exact book page TBD in 下巻 or later 上巻 chapters]
