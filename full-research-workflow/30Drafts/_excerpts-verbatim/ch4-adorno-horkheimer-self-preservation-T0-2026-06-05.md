## Q1 — Self-Preservation as the Principle of (Instrumental) Reason

### 1a. Reason reduced to mere aid; self-preservation drives logical formalism (p.23)

> "…reason itself has become merely an aid to the all-encompassing economic apparatus. Reason serves as a universal tool for the fabrication of all other tools, rigidly purpose-directed and as calamitous as the precisely calculated operations of material production, the results of which for human beings escape all calculation. Reason's old ambition to be purely an instrument of purposes has finally been fulfilled. The exclusivity of logical laws stems from this obdurate adherence to function and ultimately from the compulsive character of self-preservation."

*(Jephcott p.23; PDFpage 43. Running header: "The Concept of Enlightenment 23".)*

**Evidence note:** Supports Q1 directly. Self-preservation is presented as the ultimate root of logical formalism — not merely of survival instinct but of the structure of reason itself. "Compulsive character of self-preservation" magnified to "choice between survival and doom" is the foundational motor.

---

### 1b. Spinoza proposition: self-preservation as the maxim of Western civilization (p.22)

> "Enlightenment's mythic terror springs from a horror of myth. It detects myth not only in semantically unclarified concepts and words, as linguistic criticism imagines, but in any human utterance which has no place in the functional context of self-preservation. Spinoza's proposition: 'the endeavor of preserving oneself is the first and only basis of virtue,' contains the true maxim of all Western civilization, in which the religious and philosophical differences of the bourgeoisie are laid to rest."

*(Jephcott p.22; PDFpage 42. Running header: "The Concept of Enlightenment" chapter.)*

**Evidence note:** A&H here identify Spinoza's *conatus* as the explicit philosophical codification of what enlightenment has always implicitly assumed. Anything outside "the functional context of self-preservation" is designated as myth — i.e., self-preservation is the criterion of rationality itself. This is the strongest single statement of the empiricist-drive-as-engine-of-reason thesis.

---

### 1c. Reason as organ of calculation; the system's principles are those of self-preservation (p.65 / Excursus II p.65)

> "Reason is the organ of calculation, of planning; it is neutral with regard to ends; its element is coordination."

*(Jephcott p.69; PDFpage 89. Running header: "Juliette or Enlightenment and Morality 69".)*

> "The system which enlightenment aims for is the form of knowledge which most ably deals with the facts, most effectively assists the subject in mastering nature. The system's principles are those of self-preservation. Immaturity amounts to the inability to survive. The bourgeois in the successive forms of the slave-owner, the free entrepreneur, and the administrator is the logical subject of enlightenment."

*(Jephcott p.65; PDFpage 85. Running header: "Juliette or Enlightenment and Morality 65".)*

**Evidence note:** Two closely related formulations from Excursus II (Juliette). Confirms Q1: the "system" (=enlightenment as a totalizing epistemic formation) has self-preservation as its organising principle; reason is literally reduced to a planning and coordination instrument.

---

### 1d. Self-preservation as the constitutive principle of science / soul of the categories (p.68)

> "Self-preservation is the constitutive principle of science, the soul of the table of categories, even if, as in Kant, it has to be deduced idealistically. Even the ego, the synthetic unity of apperception, the agency which Kant calls the highest point, from which the whole of logic must be suspended, is really both the product and the condition of material existence. Individuals, in having to fend for themselves, develop the ego as the agency of reflective foresight and overview…"

*(Jephcott p.68; PDFpage 88. Running header: "68 Excursus II".)*

**Evidence note:** The strongest theoretical statement of Q1. Self-preservation is not merely a motivation but the *constitutive principle* structuring the Kantian categories — the transcendental apparatus itself is rooted in survival. This collapses the distinction between empirical drive and transcendental reason: the Hobbesian driver and the Kantian formal structure are one.

## Q2 — Self-Preservation → Domination of Inner and Outer Nature (Renunciation)

### 2a. "The history of civilization is the history of the introversion of sacrifice" (p.43, Excursus I)

> "The human being's mastery of itself, on which the self is founded, practically always involves the annihilation of the subject in whose service that mastery is maintained, because the substance which is mastered, suppressed, and disintegrated by self-preservation is nothing other than the living entity, of which the achievements of self-preservation can only be defined as functions — in other words, self-preservation destroys the very thing which is to be preserved. The antireason of totalitarian capitalism, whose technique of satisfying needs, in their objectified form determined by domination, makes the satisfaction of needs impossible and tends toward the extermination of humanity — this antireason appears prototypically in the hero who escapes the sacrifice by sacrificing himself. The history of civilization is the history of the introversion of sacrifice — in other words, the history of renunciation. All who renounce give away more of their life than is given back to them, more than the life they preserve."

*(Jephcott p.43; PDFpage 63. Running header: "Odysseus or Myth and Enlightenment 43".)*

**Evidence note:** Core passage for both Q2 and Q3 simultaneously. The introversion of sacrifice = renunciation of inner nature in service of self-preservation. But the passage immediately pivots to Q3: "self-preservation destroys the very thing which is to be preserved." The Odysseus figure is the prototype of the bourgeois self: self-renouncing in order to self-preserve, but this logic is internally self-defeating.

---

### 2b. The Sirens: workers/labourers vs. Odysseus the bourgeois — renunciation for self-preservation (pp.26–27)

> "Humanity had to inflict terrible injuries on itself before the self — the identical, purpose-directed, masculine character of human beings — was created, and something of this process is repeated in every childhood. The effort to hold itself together attends the ego at all its stages, and the temptation to be rid of the ego has always gone hand-in-hand with the blind determination to preserve it. Narcotic intoxication, in which the euphoric suspension of the self is expiated by deathlike sleep, is one of the oldest social transactions mediating between self-preservation and self-annihilation, an attempt by the self to survive itself."

*(Jephcott p.26; PDFpage 46. Running header: "26 The Concept of Enlightenment".)*

> "He plugs their ears with wax and orders them to row with all their might. Anyone who wishes to survive must not listen to the temptation of the irrecoverable, and is unable to listen only if he is unable to hear. Society has always made sure that this was the case. Workers must look ahead with alert concentration and ignore anything which lies to one side. The urge toward distraction must be grimly sublimated in redoubled exertions. Thus the workers are made practical. The other possibility Odysseus chooses for himself, the landowner, who has others to work for him. He listens, but does so while bound helplessly to the mast, and the stronger the allurement grows the more tightly he has himself bound. Just as later the bourgeois denied themselves happiness the closer it drew to them with the increase in their own power."

*(Jephcott pp.26–27; PDFpage 46–47. Running headers: "26 The Concept of Enlightenment" / "The Concept of Enlightenment 27".)*

**Evidence note:** The Sirens episode explicitly stages the class-differentiated structure of renunciation: workers have no access to pleasure (ears plugged, literally incapable of hearing); Odysseus-as-bourgeois hears but denies himself the consequence. Both modes operate in service of self-preservation. The "grimly sublimated" exertions of workers and the self-binding of the entrepreneur are structurally parallel — domination of inner nature to secure survival.

---

### 2c. Control of internal and external nature made the absolute purpose of life (p.24)

> "At the turning points of Western civilization, whenever new peoples and classes have more heavily repressed myth, from the beginnings of the Olympian religion to the Renaissance, the Reformation, and bourgeois atheism, the fear of unsubdued, threatening nature — a fear resulting from nature's very materialization and objectification — has been belittled as animist superstition, and the control of internal and external nature has been made the absolute purpose of life. Now that self-preservation has been finally automated, reason is dismissed…"

*(Jephcott p.24; PDFpage 44. Running header: "The Concept of Enlightenment 23/24" — OCR artifact, page confirmed by position in chapter.)*

**Evidence note:** Confirms Q4 anchor (see also §Q4a below). The formulation "absolute purpose of life" indexes self-preservation's totalising character: not one purpose among others but the encompassing telos that has displaced all others. "Finally automated" signals the transition to Q3: once automated, self-preservation no longer needs the subject — and the subject becomes dispensable.

## Q3 — The Dialectical Reversal: Self-Preservation That Destroys the Self

### 3a. "Self-preservation destroys the very thing which is to be preserved" (p.43, Excursus I)

[See full passage at Q2a above — the Q2 and Q3 passages are identical in location; the dialectical reversal is embedded within the introversion-of-sacrifice paragraph.]

The operative sentence isolated:

> "…in other words, self-preservation destroys the very thing which is to be preserved."

*(Jephcott p.43; PDFpage 63.)*

---

### 3b. Self-preservation become indistinguishable from self-destruction (p.71)

> "With the development of the economic system in which the control of the economic apparatus by private groups creates a division between human beings, self-preservation, although treated by reason as identical, had become the reified drive of each individual citizen and proved to be a destructive natural force no longer distinguishable from self-destruction. The two principles combined in a murky fusion. Pure reason became unreason, a procedure as immune to errors as it was devoid of content."

*(Jephcott p.71; PDFpage 91. Running header: "Juliette or Enlightenment and Morality 71".)*

**Evidence note:** Q3 stated explicitly in propositional form. Self-preservation and self-destruction are declared indistinguishable — the dialectical collapse is here made structural (tied to the form of the economic apparatus), not merely a contingent outcome. "Murky fusion" is the key phrase; the two drives are not opposed but have become the same.

---

### 3c. "Nature as true self-preservation… unleashed" — the reversal at the level of society (p.23)

> "But nature as true self-preservation is thereby unleashed, in the individual as in the collective fate of crisis and war, by the process which promised to extirpate it."

*(Jephcott p.23; PDFpage 43. Running header: "The Concept of Enlightenment 23".)*

**Evidence note:** Complicating passage. A&H distinguish between formal/systematic self-preservation (the drive that organises enlightenment) and "nature as true self-preservation" — the latter is unleashed *by* the former's suppression. The enlightenment project, in trying to master nature in the name of self-preservation, releases an untamed natural force. This is the inversion: the domestication of instinct produces its explosive return.

---

### 3d. Self-preservation as "isolated schema" that threatens to dominate culture (p.161)

> "So calamitous is the mind's tendency to false projection that, as the isolated schema of self-preservation, such projection threatens to dominate everything which goes beyond self-preservation: culture. False projection is the usurper in the realm of freedom as of culture; paranoia is the symptom of the half-educated."

*(Jephcott p.161; PDFpage 181. Running header: "Limits of Enlightenment 149" — NOTE: PDFpage 181 offset = p.161; confirm: "Elements of Anti-Semitism" chapter.)*

**[Caution: PDFpage 181 header reads "Limits of Enlightenment 149" — offset inconsistency detected. Book page = PDFp - 20 = 161, but chapter header says 149. This section is in "Elements of Anti-Semitism" which has a different section header. VERIFY the exact book page independently before citing this passage as p.161.]**

**Evidence note:** Flags the danger of self-preservation becoming a "schema" (=isolated, totalised logic) that colonises domains beyond survival — including culture. This prefigures the annihilation argument: when self-preservation is totalized, it destroys precisely what it claimed to protect (culture, subjectivity, freedom).

### A2. Renunciation as surplus extraction — "All who renounce give away more of their life than is given back" (p.43)

[See Q2a: "All who renounce give away more of their life than is given back to them, more than the life they preserve."]

**Evidence note bearing on the affect-tension flag:** A&H's self-preservation produces an **ascetic** subject (renunciation, self-suppression, deferral of pleasure). ## Three-Line Synthesis

**Does A&H's self-preservation thread make the empiricist driver the engine of enlightenment (thesis: poles united at root)?**
Yes, strongly. A&H present self-preservation (the Hobbesian/Spinozist empiricist drive) as the *constitutive principle* of science, the root of logical formalism, and the organising telos of Western civilisation: there is no separate "rationalist" pole that is not already shaped by survival logic. Reason is not merely *used* for self-preservation; it is *constituted* by it (p.68: "the constitutive principle of science, the soul of the table of categories").

**Does A&H's self-preservation → self-destruction prefigure the self-undoing terminus (Uegaki's 〈無限の生〉 necessarily loses)?**
Structurally yes, but with a critical difference in mechanism. For A&H, the self-defeat is immanent and systemic: the same process that suppresses inner nature in order to preserve the self also hollows out the self ("self-preservation destroys the very thing which is to be preserved," p.43); the reversal is internal to the logic, not caused by external resistance. For Uegaki, 〈無限の生〉 loses through a cosmological/ontological limit (the universe as realist constraint). A&H's reversal is social-structural (capitalism, domination); Uegaki's is ontological (the resistance of being itself). The prefiguration is real but the mechanism diverges — worth marking in synthesis.

**Affect-tension: renunciation (A&H) vs. anti-renunciation (late modernity)?**
A&H's self-preservation produces a fundamentally *ascetic* subject — the Odyssean self is defined by self-binding, denial of pleasure, sublimation. The late-modern as-willed subject (the 〈無限の生〉 paradigm, the metaverse as fulfillment of desires) appears structurally *anti-ascetic*: it refuses the renunciation that A&H describe as constitutive of the Western self. Yet A&H's dialectic may already anticipate this: "Now that self-preservation has been finally automated, reason is dismissed" (p.24) — once survival is secured by apparatus, the ascetic drive is no longer functionally necessary, and what is released is precisely the unbound pleasure-drive that civilisation had suppressed. The as-willed subject may therefore be *the post-ascetic terminus of the same self-preservation logic*, not its opposite.
