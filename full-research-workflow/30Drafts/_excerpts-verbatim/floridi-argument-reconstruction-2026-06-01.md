## PART 1 — THE RECONSTRUCTED ARGUMENT

### Premise 1 — Metaphysics: Informational Structural Realism (ISR). Reality's ultimate nature is informational/structural, not substantial.

The whole argument rests on a metaphysics in which the ultimate furniture of the world is **structure/relation**, not impenetrable material substance. Floridi's system is —

> "rooted in an ontology which sees reality as ultimately informational, a form of realism termed Informational Structural Realism (ISR). Data is defined as any difference or lack of uniformity, and information as well-formed, meaningful, truthful data" — **IIS** (Bawden/Robinson/Floridi 2022)

> "there are good philosophical, mathematical, and scientific reasons to commit ourselves to a structuralist/informationalist ontology according to which mathematical as well as physical objects do not play the role of ultimate realia. Like icons on a computer screen, they are user-friendly, but they should not be confused with what lies behind them and constitutes their nature. … **Structures are not second-class citizens of our ontology, with first-class citizens represented by material things**, the usual chairs and horses, kicked stones and white snow … They are the *ipokeimena*, what lies below the surface of the world as we experience it. … we are always dealing with dynamic structures in flux, whether empirically or mathematically reified as objects." — **LoI** (2019)

**Why this premise does the work:** if both the physical world and digital technologies are, at bottom, informational structure, the substance-boundary between them dissolves, and a technology that manipulates information manipulates *the same stuff reality is made of*. Floridi makes this bridge explicit:

> "The intrinsic nature of the tools (software, algorithms, databases, communication channels and protocols, etc.) is now the same as, and therefore fully compatible with, the intrinsic nature of their resources, the raw data being manipulated. … from a physical perspective, it would be impossible to distinguish between data and programs in the hard disk of your computer: they are all digits anyway." — **FR** (2014)

> "In the infosphere, populated by entities and agents all equally informational, where there is no physical difference between processors and processed, interactions become equally informational." — **FR** (2014)

### Premise 1a — The realist floor (LOAD-BEARING for the thesis): a mind-independent substrate that is NOT a construction.

Crucially, ISR is a **realism**. Beneath the constructed semantic world Floridi posits *dedomena* — mind-independent "data in the wild" that are never themselves experienced or constructed, only inferred as the external anchor of all information. This is the realist limit the slogans hide:

> "There is no specific name for such 'data in the wild'. One may refer to them as *dedomena*, that is, 'data' in Greek. … **Dedomena are not to be confused with environmental information. They are pure data, that is, data before they are interpreted or subject to cognitive processing. They are not experienced directly, but their presence is empirically inferred from, and required by, experience, since they are what has to be there in the world for our information about the world to be possible at all.**" — **LoI** (2019)

> "As 'fractures in the fabric of Being', they can only be posited as an external anchor of our information … They can be reconstructed as ontological requirements, like Kant's noumena or Locke's substance: they are not epistemically experienced, but their presence is empirically inferred from, and required by, experience." — **RHPI** (2016), Bynum on Floridi

> Bynum's gloss: the structural objects Floridi presupposes "are what he calls *dedomena*: 'mind-independent points of lack of uniformity in the fabric of Being' … These cannot be directly perceived, and they cannot be detected by any kind of scientific instrument. Instead, Floridi infers their existence by a transcendental argument according to which dedomena must exist to make it possible for any structured entities at all to exist." — **RHPI** (2016)

> "I never seriously question for a moment the recalcitrant and often grating presence of outside realities." — **LoI** (2019), Preface

> "As cognitive amphibians, we are embodied and embedded physical agents, and we live in and interact with the world in itself, Kant's noumena. We eat and drink, handle and build, avoid and crash into noumena. But as informational organisms, we experience and inhabit the world as a semantic reality both afforded and constrained by the world in itself." — **LoI** (2019)

> [!important] The decisive distinction for positioning against the thesis
> Floridi's "the natural is in itself artefactual" does **NOT** say the *noumenal substrate* is constructed. It says **the rich, conceptualized "natural"** (the natural *as we perceive and conceive it*) is a semantic construction *over* an unaffectable noumenal floor. The floor — *dedomena*, "constraining affordances", "the recalcitrant and often grating presence of outside realities" — is mind-independent and not an artefact. See §Divergence: this floor is structurally the thesis's **universe** ("neither a god nor a man made", unaffectable).

### Premise 2 — The infosphere: the totality of informational entities; minimally a region, maximally coextensive with reality.

> "Minimally, infosphere denotes the whole informational environment constituted by all informational entities, their properties, interactions, processes, and mutual relations. It is an environment comparable to, but different from, cyberspace, which is only one of its sub-regions … since the infosphere also includes offline and analogue spaces of information. **Maximally, infosphere is a concept that can also be used as synonymous with reality, once we interpret the latter informationally. In this case, the suggestion is that what is real is informational and what is informational is real.**" — **FR** (2014)

### Step (a) — ICTs do not merely use or re-engineer the world; they RE-ONTOLOGIZE it (transform its intrinsic nature).

The pivotal move. "Re-ontologizing" is defined as a re-engineering so radical it changes a thing's *essence/ontology*, not just its arrangement. (Origin: Floridi 2010b = **VSI**; restated and sharpened in **EAI**.)

> "There is no term for this profound form of transformation. In the past (Floridi 2010b), I used the expression re-ontologizing to refer to a very radical kind of re-engineering. **This sort of re-engineering not only designs, constructs, or structures a system (e.g. a company, a machine, or some artefact) anew but fundamentally transforms the intrinsic nature of the system itself — that is, its ontology.** In this sense, nanotechnologies and biotechnologies are not merely re-engineering but re-ontologizing our world." — **EAI** (2023) `[VERIFY: VSI exact 2010 wording]`

What licenses the leap from "re-engineer" to "re-ontologize" is the digital's autonomy as a **third-order technology** plus its **cleaving power**:

> "the digital is a third-order technology. It is not just a technology between us and nature, like an axe (first order), or a technology between us and another technology, like an engine (second order). It is rather a technology between one technology and another technology … Due to the autonomous processing power of the digital, we may not even be on — let alone in — the loop. … the digital is not merely enhancing or augmenting reality. It radically transforms reality because it creates new environments that we then inhabit, and new forms of agency with which we then interact." — **EAI** (2023)

> "**The digital 'cuts and pastes' our realities both ontologically and epistemologically.** By this I mean that it couples, decouples, or recouples features of the world (ontology) and therefore our corresponding assumptions about them (epistemology) that we thought were unchangeable. It splits apart and fuses the 'atoms' of our 'modern' experience and culture … It reshapes the bed of the river, to use a Wittgensteinian metaphor." — **EAI** (2023)
> (Worked examples: location/presence *decoupled*; producer/consumer *recoupled* into "prosumer/produmer". — **EAI** 2023)

### Step (b) — The Fourth Revolution: the human is DECENTERED — an "inforg" among informational agents.

The re-ontologization is accompanied by a self-understanding shift: the human loses its central, unique position, joining a continuum of natural and artificial informational agents.

> "today we acknowledge that we are not immobile, at the centre of the universe (Copernican revolution), that we are not unnaturally separate and diverse from the rest of the animal kingdom (Darwinian revolution), and that we are far from being Cartesian minds entirely transparent to ourselves (Freudian or neuroscientific revolution). … Turing displaced us from our privileged and unique position in the realm of logical reasoning, information processing, and smart behaviour. **We are no longer the undisputed masters of the infosphere.**" — **FR** (2014)

> "we are not at the centre of the universe (Copernicus), of the biosphere (Darwin), of the mental space (Freud), and now of the infosphere (Turing)." — **EAI** (2023)

> "We are slowly accepting the post-Turing idea that we are not Newtonian, stand-alone, and unique agents, some Robinson Crusoe on an island. **Rather, we are informational organisms (inforgs), mutually connected and embedded in an informational environment (the infosphere), which we share with other informational agents, both natural and artificial, that also process information logically and autonomously.**" — **FR** (2014)

### Step (c) — Onlife: the online/offline and natural/artefactual distinctions blur, then disappear; the infosphere BECOMES the lived environment.

> "With interfaces becoming progressively less visible, the threshold between here (analogue, carbon-based, offline) and there (digital, silicon-based, online) is fast becoming blurred … **The digital-online world is spilling over into the analogue-offline world and merging with it.** … I prefer to refer to it as the onlife experience. … We are increasingly living onlife." — **FR** (2014)

> "In the near future, the distinction between online and offline will become ever more blurred and then disappear." — **FR** (2014)

> "In any mature information society today … we no longer live online or offline. Instead, we live onlife … that special space, or infosphere, that is seamlessly analogue and digital, offline and online." (the "mangrove societies" / brackish-estuary analogy) — **EAI** (2023)

> "The digital transition acts as a huge accelerator of the blurring of these once effective distinctions. The multiplication of sensors and prostheses, the progress of cognitive sciences and biological engineering blur the distinction between humans and artefacts." — **OM** (2015)

And the environment-level conclusion that ties (a)–(c) together:

> "We have seen how the digital is re-ontologizing the very nature of (and hence what we mean by) our environments as **the infosphere is progressively becoming the world in which we live.** While we were unsuccessfully pursuing the inscription of productive AI into the world, we were actually modifying (re-ontologizing) the world to fit reproductive, engineering AI. The world is becoming an infosphere increasingly well adapted to AI's bounded capacities." — **EAI** (2023) *(note the "enveloping" mechanism: we reshape the world to fit the machines, not vice versa)*

> "The infosphere will not be a virtual environment supported by a genuinely 'material' world. Rather, **it will be the world itself that will be increasingly understood informationally, as an expression of the infosphere.** … the infosphere will have moved from being a way to refer to the space of information to being synonymous with reality itself." — **FR** (2014)

### Step (d) — "The natural is in itself artefactual": the EPISTEMOLOGICAL engine (semantic constructionism / non-naturalism).

This is the deepest premise and the one that earns the slogan. The argument is **not** "technology has covered nature over." It is a constructionist epistemology: we never access reality-in-itself; we build models of it through human-designed levels of abstraction; "the natural", as a rich concept, is one of those constructs — hence *made*, hence artefactual.

> "we do not have access to the world in itself … We always access any reality through specific interfaces, what I shall define … as levels of abstraction … The world provides the data, to be understood as constraining affordances, and we transform them into information, like semantic engines. But such transformation … is not equivalent to portraying, or picturing, or photographing … It is more like cooking: the dish does not represent the ingredients, it uses them to make something else out of them, yet the reality of the dish and its properties hugely depend on the reality and the properties of the ingredients." — **LoI** (2019)

> "humans are natural-born data hackers" — repurposing perceptual data for new, non-natural meanings (the flag/country/foe example). — **LoI** (2019)

The conclusion of the chapter "The Artefactual Nature of the Natural":

> "It is the non-natural that enables us to create categories such as 'natural', 'naturalized', and 'naturalization'. **There is nothing anti-realist or relativistic in such an acknowledgement.** … Therefore, either the natural is only a very minimalist and uncontroversial way to refer to data in themselves (noumenal) as constraining affordances, and this is uninteresting; or the natural — as we conceive and perceive it in a rich and multifarious way — is a construction, a model of the noumenal (reality in itself). **The naturalization of the non-natural turns out to be an expression of the artefactual nature of the natural.** … the non-natural is our first nature, and the natural is actually our second nature. And this means that what we need is **a genealogy of the natural from the non-natural, not vice versa.**" — **LoI** (2019)

> "I shall conclude that the natural is in itself artefactual (a semantic construction), and that the information revolution is disclosing a tension not between the natural and the non-natural, but a deeper one between a user's and a producer's interpretation of knowledge." — **LoI** (2019)

> "**To be human is to refuse to accept the natural as naturalistic, and to take full responsibility for such a refusal.**" — **LoI** (2019)

> [!note] Read the scope precisely (per Premise 1a)
> The "natural" that is artefactual is the *rich conceptualized* natural — "as we conceive and perceive it in a rich and multifarious way" — which is a *model of the noumenal*. The noumenal (data in themselves / constraining affordances) is **not** artefactual; it is the mind-independent floor. Floridi himself stresses "There is nothing anti-realist or relativistic in such an acknowledgement." So (d) is a thesis about the **concept** "nature", not about the existence of a mind-independent substrate.

### Step (e) — The "marriage of *physis* and *techne*": DESCRIPTIVE blurring vs NORMATIVE project. (Keep these apart.)

This is where the prior pass risked conflation. There are **two distinct registers**:

**(e-i) DESCRIPTIVE** — as a matter of fact, ICTs are *already* dissolving the natural/artificial boundary (this is the *onlife* / re-ontologization material above; cf. OM "blur the distinction between humans and artefacts").

**(e-ii) NORMATIVE** — the "marriage" / "alliance" of the Green (*physis*) and the Blue (*techne*) is a **project we ought to design and take responsibility for**. It is prescriptive, not merely diagnostic:

> "an approach that does not privilege the natural or untouched, but treats as authentic and genuine all forms of existence and behaviour, even those based on artificial, synthetic, hybrid, and engineered artefacts. … This sort of synthetic (both in the sense of holistic or inclusive, and in the sense of artificial) environmentalism will require … **how we might negotiate a new alliance between the natural and the artificial.**" — **FR** (2014)

> "**The marriage between nature (phusis) and technology (techne) is vital for the prosperity of the planet**, its inhabitants, and therefore every society. Today, the solutions found by good politics … must be both green (environmental and cultural economy and policy), and blue (digital economy and information policy). … they must coexist in symbiotic relationships of mutual benefit." — **G&B** (2022), thesis/aphorism 69

> "AI4SG is part of a new marriage between the Green of all our habitats (whether natural, synthetic, and artificial, from the biosphere to the infosphere …) and the Blue of our digital technologies … **It is our responsibility to design and manage both successfully.**" — **EAI** (2023)

> [!warning] Descriptive vs normative — the load-bearing qualification
> Floridi holds the *blurring* is **happening** (descriptive) AND that a *good* marriage of Green and Blue is something **we must deliberately engineer** (normative). The slogan "physis and techne are married" therefore straddles both: ICTs have made the divorce untenable (fact), so we are responsible for designing the union well (ought). The thesis must not cite the *normative* marriage as if it were Floridi's *descriptive* claim about what reality now is.

### CONCLUSION (Floridi) — with scope.

Because (P1) reality is ultimately informational structure over a mind-independent floor, (P2) the infosphere is the totality of informational entities and can be read as coextensive with reality, and because ICTs (a) re-ontologize rather than merely re-engineer, (b) decenter the human into an inforg, (c) erase the online/offline (and natural/artificial) boundary in onlife, (d) while our "natural" was always already a semantic construction over the noumenal — therefore:

> "**ICTs are modifying the very nature of, and hence what we mean by, reality, by transforming it into an infosphere.**" — **FR** (2014)

**Exact scope / qualifications (do not over-read):**
1. The claim is about **what we mean by reality** and about the **rich conceptualized** world — NOT about abolishing the noumenal substrate (Premise 1a). "There is nothing anti-realist or relativistic in such an acknowledgement."
2. The *marriage of physis/techne* is part **descriptive**, part **normative project** (Step e). Floridi assigns *responsibility* — it is something to be *designed*, not only observed.
3. It is **not** dystopian/Matrix-style: "The infosphere will not be a virtual environment supported by a genuinely 'material' world" — the point is reinterpretation, not simulation. (FR)
4. Decentering is **not** anti-exceptionalism flattening: in EAI he keeps a residual human exceptionalism ("a beautiful glitch", "successfully dysfunctional"). `[VERIFY: page]`

## PART 2 — POSITION vs THE THESIS (Between the Universe and the Metaverse)

The thesis already rejects the nature/culture divide (Ch.1, via Haila, Jones, Obara — see [[nomos-phusis-tekhne-CONSOLIDATED-2026-06-01]]). So there is genuine alignment with Floridi *at the level of dismantling the given/made binary*. But the thesis stays **realist about the universe** and builds a **constructive replacement** (universe / Adaptational Operating System / world / artificiality), where Floridi tends to let the *natural-as-concept* dissolve into the informational-artefactual. The map below is more precise than "agree / disagree" because Floridi's realist floor (Premise 1a) is the hinge.

### Sharpest CONVERGENCE
Both reject the nature/culture (given/made) dichotomy as a *naïve* starting point, and both do so **without lapsing into anti-realism**. Floridi: "the natural is in itself artefactual (a semantic construction) … There is nothing anti-realist or relativistic in such an acknowledgement" (**LoI**). The thesis likewise treats the *lived, meaning-laden* world as humanly constructed while preserving a real substrate. Floridi's **re-ontologization** mechanism (a technology that changes the intrinsic nature of its environment, autonomously, with the human out of the loop) is a near-exact formal analogue of the thesis's **squeeze**: a new neo-artificial layer (metaverse) reshapes the lived world rather than merely adding tools to it. And Floridi's third-order, autonomous, "out-of-the-loop" technology matches the thesis's interest in **autonomous, human-independent** technology (the technosphere acting with its own disposition).

### Sharpest DIVERGENCE
**The status of the substrate.** The thesis is realist about the **universe** as a substrate "neither a god nor a man made" — unaffectable, prior, the given natural ground that the world is nested within. The slogan reading of Floridi ("the natural is in itself artefactual"; the infosphere becomes "synonymous with reality itself") looks like a **collapse of the natural into the informational-artefactual** — exactly what the thesis refuses.

> **BUT — the crucial qualification (Premise 1a) softens this into a difference of *level*, not a flat opposition.** Floridi *also* posits *dedomena* / noumena — "mind-independent points of lack of uniformity in the fabric of Being", "the recalcitrant and often grating presence of outside realities", "constraining affordances" — which we "avoid and crash into" but never construct. **That floor is functionally the thesis's universe.** So the real divergence is narrower and sharper than the slogans suggest:
> - Floridi reserves "realism" for an *inaccessible, structurally-described, sub-perceptual* layer (dedomena), and lets **everything humanly meaningful** above it — including the rich concept "nature" — be construction/infosphere.
> - The thesis keeps a **thicker, nameable, ontologically dignified** realist layer (the universe as substrate with its own standing), and reserves construction for the **world** specifically, NOT for the universe.
>
> In short: **where Floridi puts the realism/construction cut very LOW (only the bare noumenal floor is real, the rest is infosphere), the thesis puts it HIGHER and gives the realist side a positive name and role (universe), so the substrate is not just an "external anchor" but a load-bearing ontological term.**

A second divergence of **method/stance**: Floridi is largely **diagnostic-plus-normative-design** (re-ontologization is happening; now design the Green/Blue marriage well). The thesis is **constructive-ontological** — it does not only deconstruct the binary; it erects a replacement architecture (universe / Adaptational Operating System / world / artificiality / neo-artificiality) in which the squeeze can be *located*. Floridi gives a mechanism and an ethics; the thesis gives a *map*.

A third, smaller divergence of **affect/direction**: Floridi's marriage of physis/techne is, normatively, *hopeful and symbiotic* ("symbiotic relationships of mutual benefit", flourishing). The thesis's "squeeze" is a *pressure/displacement* framing — the world is squeezed, displaced, transformed — closer to loss than to marriage. Same descriptive substrate (boundary dissolving), opposite valence.

### The Latour parallel — and a sharp caution
The author links Floridi and Latour (both diagnose the breakdown of the modern Constitution's *purification* of nature vs society; Latour: *We Have Never Been Modern* — the moderns never actually kept the poles apart, hybrids proliferate). The parallel is real **at the level of the conclusion** (the nature/society or nature/artifice purification is untenable; hybrids/onlife are the norm).

> [!warning] Floridi EXPLICITLY DISOWNS Latour — this is the author's bridge, not Floridi's.
> When Markus Gabriel flagged the "astonishing absence" of Latour, Floridi replied: "the absence of any reference to Bruno Latour … should be seen as meant. It is not an oversight. I know, of course, about actor-network theory. … **I meant to avoid it.** … It is precisely because I stay away from such »post-modernist« and »post-humanist« ways of thinking that I do not link my line of reasoning to them, Latour included." — **G&B** (2022), Reply to Markus Gabriel
>
> **Implication for the thesis:** present Floridi + Latour as **convergent diagnoses reached by independent (indeed mutually disavowed) routes** — Latour from STS/anthropology of the moderns; Floridi from analytic philosophy of information / ISR. Do **not** present Floridi as drawing on, or sympathetic to, Latour. The convergence is *the author's triangulation*, and is stronger for being arrived at independently. ## PART 3 — WHAT THE THESIS BORROWS vs MUST REJECT

**BORROW (mechanism + diagnosis):**
- **Re-ontologization** as a named mechanism: a technology that *transforms the intrinsic nature* of its environment (not mere re-engineering / tool-use). Direct fuel for the squeeze and for "metaverse as a new ontological layer, not a new gadget."
- **Third-order / autonomous / out-of-the-loop technology** — warrant for the thesis's *autonomous, human-independent* technology / technosphere with its own disposition.
- **The decentered human (inforg)** — supports the thesis's anti-anthropocentric framing of the squeeze (the human is not the master of the new environment).
- **Onlife / boundary-dissolution** — independent corroboration that natural/artificial and offline/online distinctions are empirically eroding (descriptive).
- **The realist-yet-constructionist combination** — that one can deny the naïve nature/culture binary *without* anti-realism. This licenses the thesis's own "reject the binary but keep a realist universe" structure. (Floridi's *dedomena* is a usable precedent for "a real substrate that is never itself a construction.")
- **Descriptive/normative discipline** — Floridi's clean split (it is happening / we ought to design it well) is a model for keeping the thesis's *diagnosis* of the squeeze separate from any *prescription*.

**REJECT (or recast):**
- **The dissolution of a thick realist substrate** into the informational-artefactual. The thesis must NOT follow the slogan-level "infosphere = reality itself" / "the natural is artefactual" all the way down. It keeps the **universe** as a thick, named, mind-independent substrate, where Floridi keeps only a thin inferred floor (*dedomena*) and lets the rest become infosphere. (Use Premise 1a to show Floridi is *closer* than he looks — but still cuts the realist/constructed line too low for the thesis.)
- **The hopeful "marriage" valence** as a description of the present. The thesis's "squeeze" is pressure/displacement, not symbiosis; borrow the *descriptive blurring*, not the *normative optimism*, and never cite the normative marriage as a descriptive ontological claim.
- **Floridi's informational monism as the thesis's own metaphysics.** The thesis needs universe / world / metaverse as distinct strata; collapsing all into "information" would erase exactly the distinctions the squeeze hypothesis runs on. Borrow the *mechanism* (re-ontologization), not the *monism* (everything is information).
- **Any framing that makes Floridi a Latourian / post-humanist.** He disowns it; cite as independent convergence only.
