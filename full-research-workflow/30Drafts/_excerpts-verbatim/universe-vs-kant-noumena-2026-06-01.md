## PART A — The thesis's "universe" (VERBATIM)

Source: `P:\_AI agents\full-research-workflow\.research\cache\docx-extract-20260530.txt`, paras as labelled. Chapter I, Section E ("World as AOS in the Universe").

**The definition (P0102–P0105):**

> [P0102] "A universe is,
> [P0103] the ultimate background environment that encompasses and conditions all AOSs, approximate to what Uexküll calls Umgebung: the encompassing totality that no population's Umwelt can ever fully absorb, and that no mode of survival can suspend;
> [P0104] the fundamental substrate of all, which interacts with all things and carries the total effect of all interactions, therefore transforms with all things – manifested in its spatial-temporal diversity (e.g., the diversity of things from blackholes, stars, to planets, and their respective internal or interrelating diversity in relation to time and space – from the configuration of matters, dynamics of energies, to the environmental impacts by animal and human footprints);
> [P0105] the foundational ontological level of all, which is the source of laws, energies, or matters that are indifferent and unaffectable by anything (e.g., the real referents behind what is referred as "gravitation" or "thermodynamics" operate whether any AOS is present or if any AOS's members can perceive them)."

**Not the nature/culture dualism — no split is possible (P0109):**

> [P0109] "the distinction between the world and the universe in this thesis is not a rebranding of the two discrete metaphysical categories in the culture/nature dualism … As I already showed with ecological evidence and my definitions, a world is nested in the universe – no split is possible between the two. … Artificiality – my analytical unit that replaces culture – is what makes human human, and a world world."

**Load-bearing support: criterion 5 + the Umwelt/Umgebung distinction (the "access-only" point).**

Criterion 5 of the AOS ideal type (P0082):

> [P0082] "5. From the vantage point of an AOS's member, the extensional AOS that includes its total relations is the member's totality; in other words, an extensional AOS is a population-dependent totality."

The phenomenological gloss (P0090):

> [P0090] "In this sense, a extensional AOS is also an Umwelt (modified by author from Uexkull, 1934/2010) of its members: It is the population-centric, bounded totality constructed from what its members can meaningfully perceive (Merkwelt, perceivable totality) and act upon (Wilkwelt, effectable totality). … This is different from what Uexkull calls Umgebung, the indifferent, population-agnostic totality encompassing all population-dependent totalities, one that can never be fully perceivable and acted upon by any of its members."

The mapping to "universe" (P0094) and the reflexive caution (P0097):

> [P0094] "Such a concept of universe is philosophically approximate to Uexkull's Umgebung, but with its connotating magnitude that the latter's English translation as 'surrounding' lacks."

> [P0097] "As Uexkull reminds us, while humans tend to think we investigate animal-specific Umwelts in relation to the total, objective Umgebung, what we consider as Umgebung 'is nothing else but our own, human Umwelt' (1934/2010: 53); in the same vein, universalization can be understood as an act of treating what is of a specific Umwelt as what is of Umgebung of all."

> **The access claim distilled:** members of an AOS access the universe ONLY through their Umwelt (the population-dependent totality); the universe (≈ Umgebung) "can never be fully perceivable and acted upon by any of its members." This is the criterion-5 "access-only" point.

## PART C — Structured compare / contrast

### 1. Similarities

| # | Shared feature | Universe (thesis) | Noumenon (Kant) |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | **Mind-independent** | "indifferent and unaffectable by anything"; operates "whether any AOS is present or if any AOS's members can perceive them" (P0105) | The thing-in-itself is what objects are "in themselves," independent of "our mode of intuiting" (A250/B307; A42/B59) |
| S2 | **Substrate / ground of what appears** | "the fundamental substrate of all"; "the foundational ontological level of all" (P0104–P0105) | The ground of appearances; appearances are appearances "of" things in themselves (SEP opening) |
| S3 | **Not humanly constructed** | A world is human-made; the universe is precisely what is NOT — "not defined by human presence" (P0094); "neither a god nor a man who has made it" (kosmos, P0096) | Things in themselves are not constituted by the mind; only appearances are mind-dependent |
| S4 | **Sets a limit to subject-access** | Umgebung "can never be fully perceivable and acted upon by any of its members" (P0090) | The noumenon is "a merely limiting concept … to curb the pretensions of sensibility" (A255/B311) |

> So at the level of *function in the architecture* — a mind-independent outer bound on a subject's accessible totality — the two concepts rhyme. This is why the resemblance is worth raising at all.

### 2. Differences (the crux)

| # | Axis | Universe (thesis) | Noumenon (Kant) |
|---|---|---|---|
| D1 | **Causal status** | **Causally efficacious & realistically engaged**: "interacts with all things and carries the total effect of all interactions, therefore transforms with all things" (P0104) | **Not a cause in the empirical sense.** Causality is a *category* whose "employment … can never extend further than to the objects of experience" (B309). The noumenon may be a "ground" but cannot be known *as* a cause. |
| D2 | **Knowability** | **Knowable in its effects/transformations** — leaves "spatial-temporal diversity," "environmental impacts by animal and human footprints" (P0104); has "real referents behind 'gravitation'/'thermodynamics'" (P0105) | **Strictly unknowable** — "we cannot have determinate cognition of … things in themselves"; thinkable but not cognizable (B5; A256/B312 "an unknown something") |
| D3 | **Spatiotemporality** | **Spatiotemporal and thick** — "manifested in its spatial-temporal diversity … blackholes, stars … planets" (P0104) | **Non-spatiotemporal** — "Space represents no property at all of any things in themselves" (A26/B42) |
| D4 | **Conceptual ambition** | **A thick, nameable, realist term** doing positive ontological work across the whole thesis (the realist pole of the squeeze) | **A limit-/boundary-concept (*Grenzbegriff*)** of "only … negative use" (A255/B311) — a brake on sensibility, not a populated ontological floor |
| D5 | **Source of inaccessibility** | **Ecological / positional** — inaccessibility flows from the Umwelt being a "population-dependent totality" (P0082); the filter is adaptive-perceptual (Merkwelt/Wirkwelt) | **Transcendental** — inaccessibility flows from the a priori forms of intuition (space/time) and the limits of the categories; the filter is the constitution of cognition as such |

> **The single sharpest line:** the thesis's universe *transforms with all things* and bears the footprints of what acts on it; Kant's noumenon, by contrast, cannot even be *said* to be a cause, because causality is a category that applies only inside experience. A substrate that "carries the total effect of all interactions" is the conceptual opposite of a substrate bracketed out of the causal order.

### 3. The epistemic point (load-bearing for Ch.1 criterion 5)

The universe is accessible ONLY through the AOS: a member's totality is its Umwelt (criterion 5, P0082), and the Umgebung "can never be fully perceivable and acted upon by any of its members" (P0090). No member perceives the background environment *as such* — exactly as Uexküll's Umgebung is never given to the Umwelt.

- **Resemblance to Kant:** like the thing-in-itself, the universe is never given "as such" to the experiencing subject. There is no view from outside the perceptual-adaptive frame onto the substrate-in-itself. The reflexive caution in P0097 — that what we take to be "Umgebung" is "nothing else but our own, human Umwelt" — is itself a quasi-Kantian move: it polices the boundary against mistaking our access-conditions for the unconditioned whole.
- **Yet the difference is decisive on two counts:**
  1. **The filter is different in kind.** The AOS is an *adaptive-perceptual filter* (an Umwelt built from Merkwelt + Wirkwelt — what a population can *meaningfully perceive and act upon*), **not** Kant's *a priori forms of intuition* (space, time) plus the categories. The thesis's filter is biological, semiotic, population-relative, and historically variable across AOSs (P0081, P0089 incommensurability); Kant's is universal, necessary, and constitutive of any finite discursive cognition.
  2. **The inaccessibility is ecological/positional, not transcendental.** The universe is inaccessible *as a total* because every vantage is a *position inside it* (a "population-dependent totality"), not because it lies *beyond all possible cognition*. Crucially, the universe **is** known — partially, perspectivally, in its effects and transformations — whereas Kant's noumenon is in principle never cognized at all. The thesis's substrate is epistemically *thin-from-any-one-position* but ontologically *thick and engaged*; Kant's is epistemically *void* and ontologically *bracketed*.

> Put compactly: Kant's gap is between *the knowable* (phenomena) and *the unknowable* (noumena). The thesis's gap is between *the totality* (universe/Umgebung) and *any positioned access to it* (a world/Umwelt) — a part/whole, perspectival gap, not a knowable/unknowable, transcendental gap.

### 4. Relation to Floridi's *dedomena* (the other noumenal floor)

Floridi himself analogizes *dedomena* to Kant: dedomena "can only be posited as an external anchor of our information … They can be reconstructed as ontological requirements, like **Kant's noumena** or Locke's substance: they are not epistemically experienced, but their presence is empirically inferred" (Bynum on Floridi, RHPI 2016 — verbatim in [[floridi-argument-reconstruction-2026-06-01]]). Bynum: dedomena "cannot be directly perceived, and they cannot be detected by any kind of scientific instrument … inferred … by a transcendental argument."

So both Kant's noumenon and Floridi's dedomena are **thin, inferred, sub-perceptual floors** — posited as external anchors, not characterized in positive content.

**The thesis's universe is a thicker, higher-cut realist substrate than either.** Naming the difference precisely:

- **vs. Kant's noumenon:** the universe is *causally engaged, spatiotemporal, knowable-in-effects, and positively named*; the noumenon is *causally bracketed, non-spatiotemporal, unknowable, and merely limiting*. (Section 2.)
- **vs. Floridi's dedomena:** dedomena are the *bare* floor — "mind-independent points of lack of uniformity in the fabric of Being," *inferred only*, never themselves experienced or causally narrated. The thesis "puts the realism/construction cut HIGHER and gives the realist side a positive name and role (universe), so the substrate is not just an 'external anchor' but a load-bearing ontological term" (from [[floridi-argument-reconstruction-2026-06-01]]). The universe is the floor **plus** everything physical and ecological that interacts and transforms — stars, planets, thermodynamic referents, footprints. It is the *whole engaged substrate*, not just its bare structural precondition.

> Ordering of substrate "thickness" (thin → thick): **Kant's noumenon** (unknowable limit-concept) → **Floridi's dedomena** (inferred bare floor, knowable only as posit) → **thesis's universe** (knowable-in-effects, causally engaged, spatiotemporally thick realist substrate). The thesis cuts the realism/construction line *highest* and gives the real the *most* positive content.

### 5. Verdict

**The thesis's universe is NOT a Kantian noumenon, and is more than Floridi's dedomena. It is best called a realist, causally-efficacious substrate accessed only perspectivally through the AOS — closer to a Uexküllian–Bhaskarian "intransitive real" than to a Kantian noumenon.**

Reasoning:
- It fails the noumenon test on three of Kant's own defining marks: it **is causal** (P0104 vs. B309), it **is spatiotemporal** (P0104 vs. A26/B42), and it **is knowable in its effects** (P0105 vs. A256/B312). A Kantian noumenon is none of these.
- Its inaccessibility is **ecological/positional** (every Umwelt is a part of the Umgebung) rather than **transcendental** (beyond all possible cognition). The resemblance to Kant is at the level of *function* (an outer limit on a subject's accessible totality), not *kind*.
- The structure — a mind-independent ("intransitive") real, knowable only through historically/biologically conditioned ("transitive") frames of access — matches **Bhaskar's critical-realist intransitive/transitive distinction** far better than Kant's phenomena/noumena. The universe is the intransitive object; each AOS/Umwelt is a transitive mode of access. `[VERIFY: Bhaskar is invoked here as the closest fit by structure; no Bhaskar text is cited in the thesis extract or in Zotero searched here. If the final draft leans on the "intransitive real" label, add a Bhaskar source (e.g., *A Realist Theory of Science*) and a citekey before use.]`
- It is therefore a **third thing**: a *perspectivally-accessed realist substrate*. Not Kant's bracketed unknowable; not Floridi's bare inferred anchor; a thick, engaged, positively-named real whose only "veil" is positional finitude, not the constitution of cognition.

## PART D — Implications for the thesis

1. **Pre-empts the "this is just Kant" objection (likely from 米/Mi on terminological precision; possibly 馬/Ma on comparative grounding).** The dossier gives a clean, citable answer: the universe shares the *limit-function* of the noumenon but inverts its three defining marks (causal / spatiotemporal / knowable-in-effects). Recommend a one-paragraph footnote at §1E making exactly this distinction, anchored on P0104–P0105 vs. B309 + A26/B42. This is the criterion-5 "access-only" point doing double duty: it explains *why* the universe is never given as such (positional finitude) **without** conceding that it is unknowable.

2. **Criterion-5 access-only point is the hinge — and it is NOT transcendental.** State explicitly that the inaccessibility of the universe-as-such is *ecological and positional* (an Umwelt is a part of, hence cannot exhaust, the Umgebung), so it does not import Kant's machinery (a priori forms of intuition, the unknowable thing-in-itself). This protects the squeeze hypothesis: the squeeze operates on a *real, causally-engaged* universe that bears the footprints of action (climate, technosphere), which a Kantian noumenon could never do. A genuinely Kantian universe would make the "ecological evidence" (P0109) of the squeeze incoherent.

3. **Floridi positioning strengthens the realist novelty claim.** The thesis can say: where Floridi (and, differently, Kant) put the realist cut at the bare, inferred floor, this thesis cuts higher and names the realist pole *universe* — a load-bearing ontological term, not a mere external anchor. This is a defensible originality claim and connects to the existing [[floridi-argument-reconstruction-2026-06-01]] dossier. Use the thin→thick ordering (Kant < Floridi < thesis) as the explicit comparative frame.

4. **Consider the Bhaskar bridge for terminological economy.** "Intransitive real accessed through transitive frames" is a one-line, pre-existing vocabulary that captures the universe/Umwelt relation and pre-distinguishes it from Kant. But it requires a citation the thesis does not yet have. `[VERIFY: add Bhaskar citekey or drop the explicit label and keep only the Uexküllian framing.]`
