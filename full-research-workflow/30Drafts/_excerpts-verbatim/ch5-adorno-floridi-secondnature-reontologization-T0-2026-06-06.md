## PART A — ADORNO / HORKHEIMER: SECOND NATURE (zweite Natur) / NATURGESCHICHTE

> The made/historical congealing into the seemingly natural, given-like — the made appearing and
> *operating* as given. For Ch. V: the metaverse as a "new background environment" that takes on the
> fate-like, self-running character of nature while remaining a historical product.

### A.0 — RESOLUTION: WHO says "second nature is first nature"? (the central question)

**Answer: ADORNO says it** — verbatim, as the culminating thesis of his 1932 lecture. It is **not** a Lukács
line. Adorno is *radicalising* Lukács: where Lukács keeps second nature as alienated/reified convention awaiting
a "metaphysical act of reawakening," Adorno collapses the gap and declares their identity.

**Source (NOW FETCHED — prior dossier flagged UNFETCHABLE):** Theodor W. Adorno, "The Idea of Natural-History"
(*Die Idee der Naturgeschichte*, 1932), trans. Robert Hullot-Kentor, *Telos* 60 (Summer 1984), pp.111–124.
Access tier: T4 OA — `dehiscence.noblogs.org/files/2022/06/Adorno-The-Idea-of-Natural-History.pdf` (14-pp PDF;
printed pagination 111–124 in running header). Citekey: `adorno_idea_of_natural_history_telos1984`.

> "Second nature is, in truth, first nature. The historical dialectic is not simply a renewed interest in
> reinterpreted historical materials, rather the historical materials transform themselves into the mythical
> and natural-historical."

[VERBATIM: Adorno, "Idea of Natural-History" (Telos 60, 1984) | p.124 | "Second nature is, in truth, first nature."]

**Context (same page, immediately preceding):** "…where semblance in its being-just-as-it-is [*Sosein*] proves
itself to be historically produced, or, in traditional philosophical terms, where semblance is the product of
the subject/object dialectic. Second nature is, in truth, first nature."

> [!important] Read the direction precisely
> The 1984 Telos line ("second nature **is**, in truth, first nature") is Adorno asserting the *identity* — the
> reified historical world (second nature) is no less "nature" than alienated first nature, because both are
> semblance/myth produced by the subject/object dialectic. This is the EARLY (1932) programmatic form.
> The LATER *Negative Dialectics* (1966) form runs the blade the OTHER way to attack Hegel: "what he glorified
> in it [the world-historic as second nature], **in villainous complicity, was the first nature**" (ND p.357,
> A.4 below) — i.e. Hegel illegitimately dignified second nature with first nature's authority. Same identity,
> opposite polemical use. **Ch. V should cite the 1932 Telos line for the positive thesis (second nature = a
> genuine nature/stratum) and the ND p.357 line for the critical edge (the danger of mistaking the made for the
> given).** Do not blur them.

### A.6 — THESIS CAVEAT vs Adorno/Horkheimer (keep in the prose)

> **Ally-on-mechanism, NOT co-thinker-on-level.**
> A&H and the thesis agree on the *mechanism*: the made congeals into a fate-like, self-running, "no-outside"
> given (the *Bann*/spell; total mediation; myth-regression; souls-into-things). The thesis borrows this engine
> for the metaverse-as-new-background-environment.
> **But the levels diverge sharply.** For A&H, second nature is **ideological *semblance*** — "the negation of any
> nature that might be conceived as the first" (ND p.357); a *Schein*/myth to be *seen through* and (in principle)
> dissolved by restoring its historical genesis. Their project is **critical-emancipatory: re-value, de-naturalise,
> wake from the spell.** For the THESIS, by contrast, the metaverse names a **genuine new ontological stratum** —
> not merely a false appearance of givenness but a real layer of neo-artificiality that actually reorganises the
> conditions of existence. The thesis **DISSOLVES the nature/culture distinction** (Ch.1, via Haila/Jones/Obara)
> rather than mobilising "true (first) nature" against ideological "(second) nature." So: take A&H's *description
> of how the made becomes self-running given*, but **reject** (a) the semblance/ideology ontology (second nature
> as mere *Schein*), and (b) the residual first/second-nature *normative* hierarchy A&H still trade on. Adorno's
> 1932 "second nature is, in truth, first nature" is the closest A&H come to the thesis's flattening — cite it as
> the bridge, while noting Adorno reaches it *within* a myth/semblance frame the thesis does not keep.

> [!important] Floridi's first/second-nature inversion vs Adorno's — a precise contrast for the thesis
> **Floridi (LoI p.67):** "the **non-natural** is our **first** nature, and the **natural** is actually our **second**
> nature" — i.e. our primary, constitutive mode is the *constructed/semantic* (the non-natural), and "nature" (the
> rich concept) is a *derived* construction layered over it. The cut is EPISTEMIC/SEMANTIC and leaves a thin realist
> floor (dedomena, B.8) untouched.
> **Adorno (Telos p.124):** "second nature **is**, in truth, **first** nature" — the *reified historical* (second
> nature) is no less nature than alienated first nature, because both are myth/semblance. The cut is
> CRITICAL/DIALECTICAL.
> Both invert the first/second-nature order, but for **opposite reasons** (Floridi: constructionist non-naturalism;
> Adorno: ideology-critique). The thesis sits with NEITHER fully: it keeps a **thick** first nature (the universe)
> that is neither semblance (contra Adorno) nor a merely thin inferred floor (contra Floridi's dedomena).

### B.10 — THESIS CAVEAT vs Floridi (keep in the prose)

> **Ally-on-mechanism, RIVAL-on-level.**
> Floridi's **re-ontologization** (B.1) + **autonomous, out-of-the-loop, third-order** technology (B.2) + the
> **enveloping** "infosphere becoming the world" (B.4) are a near-exact formal analogue of the thesis's **squeeze**:
> a new neo-artificial layer reshapes the lived world rather than merely adding tools. Borrow the mechanism.
> **But the level of the realist floor diverges.** Floridi puts the realism/construction cut **very LOW**: only the
> bare, sub-perceptual, structurally-described **dedomena** ("data in the wild," "the recalcitrant … presence of
> outside realities," B.8) are mind-independent; **everything humanly meaningful above it** — including the rich
> concept "nature" — becomes infosphere/construction (B.9). The THESIS keeps the cut **HIGHER** and gives the
> realist side a **thick, named, ontologically dignified** term — the **universe** ("neither a god nor a man made,"
> unaffectable, prior) — reserving *construction* for the **world** specifically, not the universe. Floridi's
> dedomena is *functionally* the thesis's universe but **far thinner**: an "external anchor" only, not a load-bearing
> stratum. So: **ally-on-mechanism (re-ontologization ≈ squeeze; out-of-the-loop autonomy ≈ technosphere's own
> disposition), rival-on-level (thin dedomena vs thick intransitive universe).**
> Two further guardrails (from the 2026-06-01 reconstruction): (i) do NOT cite Floridi's *normative* "marriage of
> physis & techne" (G&B §69; EAI Green/Blue) as a *descriptive* ontological claim — it is a prescriptive design
> project; (ii) do NOT make Floridi a Latourian — he **explicitly disowns** Latour (G&B, Reply to Gabriel); present
> any Floridi–Latour convergence as the author's independent triangulation only.
