# 1 — SIMONDON: concretization (abstract → concrete; naturalization; self-conditioning)

## 1a. The abstract (primitive) technical object — parts external, not a natural system
[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.49 | "The abstract technical object, in other words the primitive technical object, is far from constituting a natural system; it is the translation into matter of a set of notions and scientific principles that are deeply separate from one another, which are attached only through their consequences and converge for the purpose of the production of a desired effect. … it cannot be examined inductively like a natural object, precisely because it is artificial."]
— Pt. I (Ch. 1, "The Process of Concretization"), p.49.

## 1b. The concrete (evolved) object "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects" + "loses its artificial character" — ★ KEYSTONE
[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.49 | "On the contrary, the concrete technical object, which is to say the evolved technical object, comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects, tending toward internal coherence, toward a closure of the system of causes and effects that exert themselves in a circular fashion within its bounds, and it moreover incorporates a part of the natural world that intervenes as a condition of functioning, and is thus part of the system of causes and effects. As it evolves, this object loses its artificial character …"]
— Pt. I, p.49. *(The prior §E.1 quote stitched "comes closer…" to "loses its artificial character" with an ellipsis and marked the second clause "not retrieved"; both are on the same page, p.49, and are now joined verbatim with the intervening clause restored.)*

**Simondon's stipulation of "artificiality" (same page, load-bearing for the thesis's artificiality vs. neo-artificiality distinction):**
[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.49 | "the essential artificiality of an object resides in the fact that man must intervene to maintain the existence of this object by protecting it against the natural world, giving it a status of existence that stands apart. Artificiality is not a characteristic denoting the fabricated origin of the object in opposition to spontaneous production in nature: artificiality is that which is internal to man's artificializing action …"]
— Pt. I, p.49. *(Artificialization = "a process of abstraction within the artificialized object," p.49 — the inverse of concretization; the hot-house double flower is Simondon's example.)*

## 1c. "technical concretization makes the primitively artificial object increasingly similar to a natural object" + the editor's "becomes naturalized" variant
[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.50 | "Conversely, technical concretization makes the primitively artificial object increasingly similar to a natural object.⁴ This object needed a regulative external milieu in the beginning, the laboratory, workshop, or sometimes the factory; it gradually increases its concretization, it becomes capable of doing without the artificial milieu, because its internal coherence increases, its functional systematicity closes as it organizes itself. The concretized object is comparable to the spontaneously produced object … it associates itself with other objects, or suffices unto itself, whereas at first it was isolated and heteronomous."]
— Pt. I, p.50.
**Editor's footnote 4 (verbatim, p.50):** [VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.50 n.4 | "Variant: the object frees itself and becomes naturalized. — Ed."] *(This is the source of the "becomes naturalized" wording flagged PENDING in §E.1. It is an editor-reported manuscript **variant**, footnoted to the "increasingly similar to a natural object" sentence — cite as such, p.50 n.4, not as Simondon's main-text phrasing.)*

**The same-status claim (p.50):** [VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.50 | "By existing, they prove the viability and stability of a certain structure that has the same status as a natural structure, even if it might be schematically different from all natural structures."]

## 1d. The crucial guard — concretization is a TENDENCY, not attained natural status (p.51)
[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.51 | "the only thing we can say is that technical objects tend toward concretization, whereas natural objects, such as living beings, are concrete to begin with. One mustn't confuse the tendency toward concretization with the status of entirely concrete existence. To a certain extent, every technical object has residual aspects of abstraction … it is more concrete than the preceding ones, yet it is still artificial."]
— Pt. I, p.51. *(Load-bearing for the thesis: Simondon explicitly REFUSES the identification of the technical with the natural/living; the made only *tends* toward the natural mode of existence. The thesis's stronger "operates as given" claim must own this divergence — cf. §E.1 reading "made *operates* as given," not merely "tends toward.")*

## 1e. "The technical object is thus its own condition" + "self-conditioning" + "The organ is its own condition" — ★ the internal-archē analogue
[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.58 | "The technical object is thus its own condition, as a condition of existence of this mixed milieu which is simultaneously both technical and geographical. This phenomenon of self-conditioning defines the principle according to which the development of technical objects is made possible …"]
— Pt. I (Ch. 2, "Evolution of Technical Reality"; §I "Hypertely and self-conditioning"), p.58. *(The chapter section-title itself, PDFidx 53, names "self-conditioning"; the French margin marker at this passage is 68.)*

[VERBATIM: Simondon, *Mode of Existence* (Univocal 2017) | p.58 | "between man and nature a techno-geographical milieu arises that only becomes possible through man's intelligence: the self-conditioning of a schema as a result of its functioning necessarily requires the use of an inventive function of anticipation … The organ is its own condition. It is in a similar manner that the geographical world and the world of already existing [technical objects relate] …"]
— Pt. I, p.58 (French margin 69). *(Note the "inventive function of anticipation" here — the hinge that links Simondon's self-conditioning to Hui's tertiary protention in §2 below: anticipation is what the concretizing object/system must internalize.)*

> **Reading for [[chV-sim_v2]]:** concretization = the made acquiring **internal recurrent (circular) causality**, **self-conditioning**, and the capacity to "suffice unto itself" — the hallmarks of φύσις's internal *archē*. The thesis ≈ Simondon **scaled from the discrete technical object to the world-layer** (metaverse/technosphere). Guard (p.51): Simondon keeps it a *tendency* and bars the natural/technical identification; the thesis's projection forward to a self-completing total layer (自足完成化) is the author's extension, not Simondon's claim. Pages now firm: **pp.49–50** (concretization/naturalization), **p.58** (its-own-condition/self-conditioning).

# 2 — HUI: tertiary protention + preemption (the recursive coupling)
> **Source-section CORRECTION:** the brief located this "≈§39"; the tertiary-protention section in *Recursivity and Contingency* is **§36, "Tertiary Protention and Preemption"** (Ch.4 "Organizing Inorganic," printed p.210). §37 "Inorganic Organicity or Ecology" follows at p.215. The recursive-coupling sentence itself lands later, in Ch.5 ("The Inhuman That Remains"), **p.243**.

## 2a. Tertiary protention introduced as the missing element completing Stiegler's retention/protention circuit
[VERBATIM: Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019) | p.210 | "In *On the Existence of Digital Objects* I have noticed that, in the retentional and protentional circuit that Stiegler has proposed, there is a missing element. This missing element—tertiary protention—is crucial to understanding the evolution of technical systems, meaning their becoming organic."]
— §36 "Tertiary Protention and Preemption," Ch.4, p.210. *(Hui's fn.48: he first develops "tertiary protention and recursivity" in *On the Existence of Digital Objects* (2016), chap. 6.)*

## 2b. Tertiary protention = a new, PREEMPTIVE form of determination — "the machine has already anticipated what the options will be"
[VERBATIM: Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019) | p.211 | "The concept of tertiary protention seems to me crucial in order to investigate temporal structure, reconstituted by digital technology, which also presents itself as a new form of determination. Such a determination is always preemptive, in the sense that the machine has already anticipated what the options will be: In this case, freedom means choice. This precisely means that it is a reduction of the contingent to the most probable."]
— §36, Ch.4, p.211. *(This is the exteriorization/automation of ANTICIPATION the brief targets: the technical system pre-computes the field of the possible. Directly squeeze-relevant — the metaverse-stage layer narrows action-possibility in advance.)*

## 2c. The recursive coupling — past → future → present — ★ THE quoted sentence
[VERBATIM: Hui, *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019) | p.243 | "In chapter 4 we named the new faculty of the machine to anticipate tertiary protention. The preemption of the tertiary protention is possible only because of the computational hermeneutics, which is essentially recursive: It constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present. Human beings are reintegrated into the temporality of machines, not only as individuals but also as collectives and communities. This is precisely what is called algorithmic governmentality."]
— Ch.5 "The Inhuman That Remains," p.243. *(The exact recursive-loop formula in the brief — "evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present" — is here, p.243, and is explicitly tied to "algorithmic governmentality" (Rouvroy & Berns).)*

> **Reading for [[chV-sim_v2]]:** Hui completes Stiegler's tertiary-RETENTION (the exteriorization of memory — see sibling [[ch5-stiegler-grammatization-retention-T0-2026-06-06]]) with tertiary-PROTENTION (the exteriorization of anticipation/expectation). Under planetary computation the loop runs past→future→present recursively, reintegrating humans into machine temporality. This is the precise mechanism by which the metaverse-stage layer acquires Simondon's "inventive function of anticipation" (§1e) **at system scale** — i.e., the technosphere internalizes anticipation and thereby behaves with the self-conditioning Simondon reserved for the concrete object.
