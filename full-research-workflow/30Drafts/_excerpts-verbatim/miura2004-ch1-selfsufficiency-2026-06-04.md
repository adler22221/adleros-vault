## English gloss — the feedback mechanism Miura describes
1. **Industry presupposes raw materials.** Industrial development in the advanced industrial nations is possible only by securing raw materials; industry *is* the processing of raw materials, and without them industry cannot exist.
2. **Colonies/developing nations are fixed as supply-and-dump zones.** Since the industrial revolution, industry has manufactured its products mainly from resources/primary commodities extracted from the colonies (cotton, wheat, tea, coffee, sugar, metals, raw silk, wool, oil, timber, rubber, etc.). These natural resources are shipped to the advanced industrial nations as material or energy, processed into manufactures, and sold back on the world market. Colonies are thereby **"pushed down and held in place" (押しとどめられた)** in the dual position of *resource-supply zone* for the industrial nations and *market* for the industrial nations' products.
3. **The international division of labour outlives formal empire.** This industrial-nation/colony international division of labour **persists fundamentally** between the advanced industrial nations and the developing nations even after colonial rule formally disappeared following WWII — i.e., the asymmetry is structural, not merely political.
4. **Cheap-bought resources force ever-greater export = dependence.** Because natural resources are **bought cheap / beaten down in price (安く買い叩かれる)** on the international trade market, the developing nations **must export resources in massive volume to earn foreign currency (外貨獲得のために大量の資源を輸出しなければならない)**. This is the explicit dependence/subordination mechanism: the terms of trade compel resource export, which (5) accelerates resource depletion and environmental destruction in the developing nations.
5. **The loop closes on the West's wealth.** The same flow that enriches the industrial nations' firms and populations (subsection 4) is what subordinates the developing nations (subsection 5): undermining their economic autonomy converts them into a supply base whose forced exports feed back into Western growth, wealth, and dominance, while the ecological cost is displaced onto them.

> Caveat on exact lexis: the strongest *explicit* dependence wording Miura uses in this passage is the **structural-persistence** statement (「依然として…基本的に存続している」) plus the **compulsion** statement (「外貨獲得のために大量の資源を輸出しなければならない」). The terms 依存/従属 themselves are carried by the *argument* (supply-zone + market lock-in + forced export) rather than appearing as a single「従属」noun in these two pages. If a verbatim「依存」/「従属」token is required for citation, it should be sought in subsection-4/序章 framing or later in the chapter; on pp.15–16 the dependence is stated via 「押しとどめられた」/「存続している」/「輸出しなければならない」.
