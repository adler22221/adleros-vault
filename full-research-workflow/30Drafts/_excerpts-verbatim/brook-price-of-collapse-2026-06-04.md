## A. The book's thesis — climate, via prices
- **p.105 (pidx 120):** "global climate, not global trade, drove Ming grain prices to crippling levels." *(stated as the claim of the next two chapters)*
- **p.106 (pidx 121):** "at long last, we arrive at the core phenomenon around which I have written this book: the prices to which grain rose in times of dearth. Famine prices do not appear in Ming documents with any consistency before 1450, yet from that date forward, they begin to pile up over the following two centuries, making up **the longest series of prices in China before the eighteenth century**."

## B. The Little Ice Age (the universe-side morph)
- **preface p.ix (pidx 8):** "What happened in the early 1640s was a phase of the long global period of lower temperatures known as the Little Ice Age. … Late in the 1630s, the Little Ice Age began to plunge toward an even colder phase, inaugurating what is called the Maunder Minimum…" (onset now dated to the fourteenth century; the early 1640s saw "massive climate cooling, pandemic, and military invasion sent millions to their deaths").
- **preface p.x (pidx 9) — the multi-causal verdict:** "This downturn precipitated the collapse of the Ming Great State (1368–1644)… The Ming had survived with reasonable stability and durability for close to three centuries of the Little Ice Age. **It was not singlehandedly destroyed by climate, but its collapse cannot be explained in the absence of climate and human responses to climate.** The dynasty responded, but its responses were overwhelmed during the deep downturn in the 1630s and 1640s."

## C. Price as the index of the squeeze (the method)
- **ch.1, p.5 (pidx 20):** in the Chen Qide narrative, "Chen turns to the price of rice to quantify the disaster and track its course." (p.4/pidx 19: grain "soared and stayed high for months on end … Refugees filled the roads, and corpses sprawled in the streets.")
- **ch.1, ~p.8 (pidx 23):** "In this collapsed economy, prices put pigs above people. Not until the end of the summer of 1642 when the rice crop was harvested did the disaster start to ease."

## D. Squeeze-PREVENTION and its failure (the link to §1)
- **p.111 (pidx 126):** if the Ming "had an advantage over other peoples, it was in living under an administration that actively championed the public good and **maintained a system of grain storage explicitly to adjust the price of grain in times of dearth**. The dynastic founder was keenly aware of the vulnerability of the poor to famine grain prices."
  → The ever-normal-granary system is a concrete instance of the harmony/squeeze-prevention of §1 (a world organizing itself *not* to outstrip its universe). The Ming HAD it; a downturn of sufficient depth overwhelmed it — which is why §3 stresses that the *interventionist* Enlightenment mode, abandoning even the aspiration to such restraint, courts the squeeze rather than guarding against it.

## E. Price-regime collapse = political-regime collapse
- **p.131 (pidx 146):** "as he was witnessing the collapse of the Ming price regime, he was also witnessing the collapse of the Ming political regime. Beijing fell to peasant rebels in April 1644, then to an invading Manchu army. Nanjing held out for another year, but eventually it too had to surrender to the Qing armies that rolled south…"
- **p.119 (pidx 134):** the peak famine-price years were 1639–1642; 1644 = "the year in which Chongzhen committed suicide and the dynasty collapsed."

## Use in §2
- **Primary:** *Price of Collapse* — the quantitative squeeze (LIA → harvest failure → famine grain-price surge → price-regime ↔ political-regime collapse), with the granary apparatus as the squeeze-prevention that failed.
- **Complement:** *Troubled Empire* — the "nine sloughs" rhythm + the prose multi-causal verdict ("their combination was what brought down the house of the Ming," p.255) + Qing-as-new-dynasty (p.255) / "only the end of the Ming, not… the imperial system" (p.23).
- **Framing:** collapse = **mismatch, not unfitness** (coheres with §6). The Qing succeeding the Ming = a *different AOS/world* (evidence of plurality). The regeneration base (Tainter; Schwartz-Nichols) is what the Enlightenment mode's *planetary* universalization threatens to consume (the §2→§3 hinge).
