## CROSS-CUTTING FLAGS — Read These First

**(a) 環世界 conflation caution.** Uegaki uses 環世界 (Umwelt) for BOTH the natural Umwelt and the artificial Umwelt. He distinguishes: 自然的環世界 (the biophysical layer) vs. 人為的環世界 (the artificial layer = 〈社会〉). The thesis reserves 環世界 / "world" (世界) for the human meaning-space alone — corresponding to Uegaki's 人為的環世界 specifically. When deploying Uegaki in Ch IV, always invoke his **人為的環世界** (not 環世界 in general) to avoid conflating the two layers.

**(b) 自立化 ≠ 自律化.** Uegaki's term throughout Ch.6 is **自立化** (becoming self-standing / detaching from the lifeworld). The term 自律化 (self-governance in a normative sense) does not appear. Footnote 9 (pp.190–191) explicitly credits Habermas's 「システムの生活世界からの**自立化**」 as the antecedent, using 自立化 there too. Confirmed on OCR lines: pp.178 (lines 6265, 6274, 6284–6285), p.181 (line 6330), p.184 (line 6470), p.187 (line 6573).

**(c) Stage-set / apparatus = world / metaverse mapping.** The thesis's world corresponds to Uegaki's 〈生〉の舞台装置 (stage-set = social structures + institutions + meaning-system, inseparably integrated). The thesis's metaverse/neo-artificial apparatus corresponds to Uegaki's 〈社会的装置〉 (stage-set *minus* meaning-system). The apparatus is the shell that self-proliferates after the meaning-layer has been severed.

**(d) Scheler's *weltoffen* at Ch.3 p.91 n.19.** OCR lines 3407–3409: "人間とは、無制限に「世界開放的(weltoffen)」に行動しうるところのXである." (Scheler 2012:50 / Scheler 2016:38 — verify citekeys in Zotero.) This is the §A foil: Scheler's claim that humans alone have *Geist* enabling escape from Umwelt. Uegaki refutes it; the metaverse appears to realise the Schelerian fantasy — and Uegaki's refutation reveals why that appearance is illusory.

**(e) Uegaki's natural-ecosystem ≈ thesis-universe/Umgebung; Uegaki's 意味体系=世界像 ≈ thesis-world.** Alignment is robust but not identical. Uegaki's full 人為的生態系 covers material structures + institutions + meaning-systems; the thesis's "world" maps primarily onto the meaning-system layer. See Theme 1 below.

## Theme 1: The 〈環境〉 / Dual-Structure Ontology

**Thesis relevance: §A (squeeze hypothesis, universe–world–metaverse ontology)**

### 1.1 The ontological 〈環境〉 concept

Uegaki recovers the pre-ecological meaning of 環境 — 「めぐりとりまく外界」, the outer world *as it surrounds a subject* — stripping away the 1960s–70s environmentalist value-loading. The resulting concept is ontological: 〈環境〉 is *simultaneously* (a) defined by the particular subject and (b) the ground on which that subject's existence is constituted and fully actualised. Without the appropriate 〈環境〉, existence remains incomplete (Kohara's flying-squirrel ムササビ example: raised without height to glide from, the animal cannot express its nature). This is what distinguishes Uegaki from naive-realist environmentalism (「素朴実在論的環境概念」): environment is always "environment for something" — a universally shared environment is strictly inconceivable. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.86–90]

Uexküll's 環世界 (Umwelt) gives this precision: each organism inhabits a subjectively constituted world reconstructed through its perceptual capacities (tick: only butyric acid, warmth, impact). [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.88–89]

### 1.2 The dual structure (二重構造)

Human specificity lies not in escaping the Umwelt (contra Scheler — see Flag (d)) but in having a *dual* 〈環境〉:

1. **自然生態系** — the natural ecosystem in which the human body is embedded as a biological organism.
2. **人為的生態系** — the artificial ecosystem humans create on top of it; an accumulation of 「モノ」 (artefacts) in an ordered relational whole that constitutes its own 環世界, inaccessible to non-human animals.

The 二重構造 is not a nature/culture binary but a continuum of artificial-ecosystem *density* (濃淡) diffused throughout the entire domain of human existence (farmland, city, 里山, 奥山). [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.89–95]

### 1.3 Three components of the 人為的生態系

| Component | Type | Content | Theorists cited |
|-----------|------|---------|-----------------|
| **社会的構造物** | Material | Tools, farmland, architecture, infrastructure | — |
| **社会的制度** | Non-material (institutional) | Mechanisms coordinating individual actions into collective outcomes (market, bureaucracy, caste) | Durkheim, Weber |
| **意味体系=世界像** | Non-material (semantic) | The conceptual-semantic framework through which things are understood; the "world as one image" | Heidegger (世界内存在), Husserl/Schutz (生活世界) |

The three are mutually constitutive: material structures require meaning to be "orderly"; institutions and meaning-systems require material embodiment. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.93–95]

**Figure 2** (book p.95): 自然生態系 → 人為的生態系 (社会的構造物 / 社会的制度 / 意味体系=世界像), labelled 自然的環世界 and 人為的環世界. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.95 — verify diagram layout in print]

### 1.4 〈社会〉 as the master term

The 人為的生態系 / 人為的環世界 is relabelled 〈**社会**〉 — not in the everyday sense of "group" but in the sociological-tradition sense (Durkheim, Weber, Marx) of a quasi-independent entity with its own momentum, transmitted without interruption across generations, preceding each individual as a pre-given 〈環境〉 while remaining the target of that individual's modifications. Each generation's modifications become the next generation's constitutive ground.

Human definition (book p.100 — paraphrase): "the living being that creates 〈社会〉 as a 人為的生態系 on the surface of the natural ecosystem and lives within that dual 〈環境〉, and that — unlike other social animals — transmits this endlessly to successive generations." [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.100]

### 1.5 Thesis-alignment note (Flag (e) in detail)

| Uegaki | Thesis |
|--------|--------|
| 自然生態系 / 自然的環世界 | Universe (宇宙) — realist, independent, Umgebung |
| 意味体系=世界像 (within 人為的環世界) | World (世界) — idealist, human-made meaning-space, Umwelt |
| 〈社会〉 / 人為的生態系 as a whole | Covers both "world" and the proto-metaverse layer; thesis needs to specify: metaverse = 〈社会〉 that has undergone 自己完結化 |

**For Ch IV:** the thesis uses 環世界 to name the human meaning-world (= Uegaki's 人為的環世界, not his full dual structure). When citing Uegaki, always specify 人為的環世界 / 人為的生態系 to preserve this distinction.

## Theme 2: The Three 特異点 (Historical Spine)

**Thesis relevance: §B (historical typology), §C (third singularity as the squeeze event)**

### 2.1 The 三項関係 framework

Ch.4 introduces the analytic frame: 〈人間〉 / 〈社会〉 / 〈自然〉 as three mutually constitutive but differentiable terms. Each 特異点 is a *structural transformation* (構造転換) of how the three poles are articulated — not gradual evolution but discontinuous threshold-crossings. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.106]

Key distinction: **〈人間〉** (≠ anatomical ヒト) = the subject constituted *through* 〈社会〉. The "Robinson Crusoe raised from birth on a desert island" thought-experiment: a ヒト is not automatically a 人間; subjectivity requires social mediation. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.107–108]

**整合性 (coherence / consistency):** Pre-singularity-2, 〈社会〉 had to maintain coherence with the natural ecosystem; ancient civilisations that failed collapsed. The second singularity dissolves this constraint. The third dissolves the analogous coherence with 〈人間〉. Loss of 整合性 = the diagnostic criterion for a singularity.

### 2.2 第一の特異点 — Agriculture (~10,000 BP)

Named: **「〈人間〉と〈自然〉の間接化」** (indirection between 〈Human〉 and 〈Nature〉).

Pre-singularity: hunter-gatherer bands (25–50 people); 〈Social〉 accumulation is minimal; dominant relation is direct ↔ 〈Natural〉, governed by natural selection.

The transformation: agriculture triggers explosive self-accumulating growth of 〈社会〉 — settled society, stratification, division of labour, political hierarchy, accelerated knowledge accumulation. After this point, every 〈人間〉–〈自然〉 relation is *mediated* through 〈社会〉. The biological body (ヒト) is effectively shielded from natural selection. All subsequent human transformation is transformation of 〈社会〉, not of the organism. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.109–113]

[VERIFY-PAGE: book p.113] — Key formulation: "〈Human〉's relationship with 〈Nature〉 becomes indirect because 〈Society〉, now a thick accumulating layer, permanently intervenes; the logic driving change is no longer natural selection but 〈社会〉の論理."

### 2.3 第二の特異点 — Modern Social Mode (~18th–20th c.)

Named: **「〈社会〉と〈自然〉の切断」** (severance between 〈Society〉 and 〈Nature〉).

Three constitutive elements:
1. **国民国家 (nation-state):** Dissolves all intermediate social units; reconstitutes individuals as direct atoms of a legally created sovereign entity; produces a massive bureaucratic apparatus. Root: social contract theory. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.116–118]
2. **市場経済 (market economy):** Polanyi's "disembedding" — the market escapes social/non-monetary relations; all production and consumption pass through commodity exchange; informal mutual aid is replaced by market-mediated equivalents. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.118–119; Polanyi reference line 4343]
3. **化石燃料 (fossil fuels):** Shifts energetic base entirely away from wood/animal/water/wind; makes 〈社会〉's material expansion effectively unlimited within geological timescales. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.119–120; Ponting reference line 4391]

Structural transformation: 〈社会〉 can now expand indefinitely on "pure social logic" alone — ignoring natural-systemic feedback. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.120–121]

[VERIFY-PAGE: book p.121] — Key formulation: natural-ecological influence is placed unilaterally under scientific-technological prediction and control; the apparatus and market, fuelled by fossil energy, sustain continuous expansion without reference to natural limits.

### 2.4 第三の特異点 — 〈自己完結社会〉 (contemporary / emergent)

Named: **「〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断」** (severance between 〈Society〉 and 〈Human〉).

Status: framed as the singularity we are *currently passing through*. Not retrospectively confirmed but diagnosed as an emergent threshold.

**Continuity with singularity 2:** 〈自己完結社会〉 is continuous with it — the 〈社会的装置〉 (market + bureaucracy) constituting it was established at singularity 2. The self-finishing society is the *endpoint of singularity 2's trajectory*. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.122–123]

**New element:** Digital/information technology creates an 情報世界; information, machines, and pharmaceuticals automate experience below the threshold of awareness; even the finite body is placed under prediction-and-control in the name of "self-realisation." [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.123–124]

[VERIFY-PAGE: book p.124] — Key formulation: "We are at this very moment passing through the third singularity — '〈Society〉 and 〈Human〉 severance'. For the first time in 7 million years of human history, the living presence of other embodied persons is no longer necessary for life, and the finite body itself is losing its meaning."

**Triadic logic of accumulation:** Each singularity adds a new cut — the Human/Nature interface is mediated (indirection) → the Society/Nature bond is severed (first severance) → the Society/Human bond is severed (second severance). The progression is strictly cumulative.

**Definition of 〈自己完結社会〉** (Ch.4 version): the society that has passed through the third singularity — 〈社会〉 (as 〈社会的装置〉: bureaucracy + market) has become so overgrown and autonomous that it has lost 整合性 first with 〈自然〉, then with 〈人間〉. Symptomatic expressions: 〈生の自己完結化〉 and 〈生の脱身体化〉. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.122–125]

Two-level description (Ch.6 elaboration): Macroscopically = the 自立化 of 〈社会的装置〉 from the lifeworld. Microscopically = the completion of 'life as a user.' [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.184]

**Kobara's "petification" (ペット化):** the ヒト body has not changed genetically since singularity 1; we carry a hunter-gatherer body into hyper-artificial urban space; singularity 3 moves beyond what the ヒト body presupposes as "natural," generating social pathologies. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.125]

## Theme 3: 〈生〉の三契機 + 根源的葛藤

**Thesis relevance: §A (ontological grounding of "world" and the squeeze), §B (user–cocoon arc)**

### 3.1 Methodological frame: inside vs. outside

Part II examined human existence from the "outside" (environmental-philosophy perspective). Part III approaches from the "inside": the standpoint of the full-sized living person (等身大の〈生活者〉). This is the "geocentric analogy" (天動説の比喩): the heliocentric model is structurally correct, but for a human living inside the system, the sun rises and sets. The two perspectives are complementary, neither cancels the other. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.143]

### 3.2 The three moments of life (〈生〉の三契機)

Formal definitions — [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.146–148, Table 2]:

| Moment | Japanese | Orienting dimension | Formal content |
|--------|----------|--------------------|--------------  |
| **Survival** | 〈生存〉 | 生命性 (life-character) | Securing necessary goods, processing materials, making tools, accumulating knowledge *within* the lifeworld |
| **Existence-in-society** | 〈現実存在〉 | 社会性 (social character) | Forming collective selfhood; sharing information and building trust; collective decision-making and role-division — encompasses passive socialisation (becoming human through others) and active socialisation (solving collective problems) |
| **Succession** | 〈継承〉 | 歴史性 (historical character) | Receiving what the prior generation inherited, improving it, passing it on — introduces the temporal chain dimension |

The three moments are the anthropological definition of what it means for a human being to be alive in any era or culture.

### 3.3 Inner connection of the three moments

The three are not parallel but structurally entailed:

- **Why 〈現実存在〉 is required for 〈生存〉:** Human survival is irreducibly *collective* survival. Realising 〈生存〉 requires forming collective selfhood and solving collective problems → 〈現実存在〉 is entailed. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.159–160]
- **Why 〈継承〉 is required for 〈生存〉:** The lifeworld sustaining collective survival is humanly constructed (人為的); it can be destroyed. Its robustness required the accumulated effort of many generations. Succession = not merely biological reproduction but: receiving the 社会的なもの, improving it, handing it on. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.160]
- **〈継承〉 + 〈現実存在〉:** In possessing a transgenerational 〈社会〉, humans became "bearers of the times" (時の担い手). Biological death does not complete one's life because life is embedded in the "chain of being" (存在の連なり). [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.160–161]

Together the three moments allow the human being to *take hold of life as something felt as real*, and to root existence in the world. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.148–149]

### 3.4 根源的葛藤 (Fundamental Conflict)

Collective survival generates an inherent contradiction: the genetic unit in mammals is the individual, yet the strategy is collective. Social insects and colonial invertebrates escape this because they have minimal individuality. Humans cannot — they remain individual genetic units who must cooperate. This tension is irreducible. Named: **〈根源的葛藤〉**. Formulation: the irresolvable friction between "my survival" (点の〈生存〉, individual) and "our survival" (集団的〈生存〉). [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.158]

Society-as-stage-set (〈生〉の舞台装置) originally developed as an **auxiliary apparatus** (補助装置) for mitigating 根源的葛藤 and enabling collective survival to proceed smoothly. This is why the three components (structures + institutions + meaning-system) are *inseparable* in the traditional lifeworld — each act (rice-planting, roof-thatching, assembly) simultaneously realised all three moments and managed the fundamental conflict. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.158–159]

**Thesis extension:** Fundamental conflict is a candidate deep-anthropological ground for the urgency of the squeeze. The universe-metaverse squeeze can be read as an intensification of the original 根源的葛藤 — now the individual is squeezed between biological substrate (universe) and neo-artificial apparatus (metaverse), with the meaning-bearing world (stage-set) being hollowed out in between.

## Theme 4: The Apparatus — Stage-Set vs. 〈社会的装置〉; 自立化; User-Arc; 無限の生; Habermas

**Thesis relevance: §B (user → cocoon arc; apparatus as metaverse precursor), §C (third singularity as the squeeze event), §F (Habermas positioning)**

### 4.1 The stage-set / apparatus distinction (critical for Flag (c))

**〈生〉の舞台装置** (stage-set of life): society understood from *within* the life-analysis framework — the humanly constructed arena in which social structures, institutions, and meaning-system are **inseparably integrated** and embedded in the lifeworld. Every act (assembly, hoe-use, festival) is simultaneously material, institutional, and meaning-laden. Origin: mitigating 根源的葛藤 + enabling collective survival. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.158–159, 175–177]

**〈社会的装置〉** (social apparatus): the *same* social structures (社会的構造物) and institutions (社会的制度), but now **purified from** / operating independently of the meaning-system/world-image (意味体系=世界像). Formal definition [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.177]: 〈社会的装置〉 = the portion of the former stage-set consisting of 「社会的構造物」 and 「社会的制度」, which has *separated from* the originally-inseparable 「意味体系=世界像」 and **protruded abnormally** (肥大化). What matters to the apparatus is **function** (delivery of goods/services), not the *meaning* of actions involved.

Structural summary: **stage-set = structures + institutions + meaning-system (integrated)**; **apparatus = structures + institutions (meaning-system severed)**. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.177]

### 4.2 Three registers of self-regulation

The apparatus possesses a **自己調整機能** operating across three registers [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.172–175]:

1. **Classic coordination:** Market economy + bureaucracy coordinate aggregated individual actions into collective outcomes without anyone intending the aggregate. The information-world / net-space (情報世界) displays the same logic.
2. **Compelled connection:** Every person is compelled to live as 〈ユーザー〉; the option of *not* being a user does not exist. Users, driven by life-necessity, collectively drive the apparatus without awareness. The apparatus is driven by accumulated life-necessity.
3. **Fine-grained permeation:** Navigation, cashier systems, autonomous vehicles, personalised medical supply, AI for product recommendation / partner-matching — the apparatus spreads into every corner of daily life, regulating human action below the threshold of awareness.

### 4.3 自立化: the historical process (Flag (b))

Autonomisation was *not* immediate upon the establishment of market + bureaucracy. As long as the lifeworld retained a strong human foundation, the apparatus remained a **supplementary device** (補助装置) embedded within it. Autonomisation required two preconditions: (a) dissolution of local community (地域社会), and (b) hollowing-out (空洞化) of the lifeworld — which is to say, the *completion* of 'life as a user.' [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.178–179]

**Pattern (school and welfare as case studies):** (i) apparatus begins as auxiliary → (ii) delegation increases → (iii) lifeworld loses capacity, driving further delegation → (iv) apparatus alone remains, hypertrophied; lifeworld hollow. Initial choice; then threshold crossed; then obligatory chain reaction. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.179–181]

**Consequences for the meaning-system:** Three residual candidates are assessed and found insufficient [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.181–182]:
- System of scientific knowledge: explains facticity but cannot supply meaning for *how to live*.
- Ethics of economic activity: norms for exchange-context only; cannot substitute for a world-image.
- Nationalism-as-user: the nation-state provides the only framework for belonging, but without a substantive lifeworld it is easily idealised and excessively conceptual.

### 4.4 The 〈生活者〉 → 〈ユーザー〉 entrustment arc

**Entrustment (委託):** In modernity, all three moments of life are realised through delegation to the apparatus — a black box. Survival = economic activity; existence-in-society = self-realisation; succession = school education. Root of three moments is carried by the apparatus; what remains visible to the individual is only fragmented end-products. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.149–153]

**Life-as-a-user (〈ユーザー〉としての生):** Modern humans = users of a web service: create an account (nationality), acquire a key (money), log in (connect to the apparatus), receive all services — but cannot modify the system, cannot set its rules, remain at the periphery of a vast black box. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.151–155]

**Hollowing-out (空洞化):** Pre-modern: every act of livelihood (rice-planting, roof-thatching, community meeting) simultaneously realised all three moments as a unified whole — the question "am I really living?" would have been unintelligible. In modernity this fusion has dissolved: lifeworld becomes hollow. Transition: 〈生活者〉 (livelihood-person) → 〈ユーザー〉. Uegaki is explicit: the trajectory is irreversible; most modern people have already lost the capacities needed for the earlier mode. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.153–156]

**Rootlessness:** Life-as-a-user is structurally *floating* — detached from 存在の連なり (the spatio-temporal chain of others across past and future). The three moments become invisible; the originary meaning of 根源的葛藤 is rendered unfelt. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.162]

### 4.5 無限の生 (Infinite Life) — the pathology of severance

When total delegation renders the three moments invisible, people float detached from 存在の連なり. The result is **「無限の〈生〉」**: the self becomes boundless — not in the sense of continuity-through-others across time (= *permanence of life* grounded in 〈連なり〉), but in the sense that *only the static "now" of the isolated self extends infinitely*. [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.186]

Chain: 〈ユーザー〉としての生 = completion of this infinite life. 〈生の脱身体化〉 = the further extension as body-level prediction and control (biotech, robotics, AI) is added. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.186–187]

**Naked individual (宇城輝人):** Outside the apparatus's functional context (economic exchange), 〈ユーザー〉s face each other as "naked individuals" — stripped of social-meaning context. Within the apparatus's context, powerful social forms (producer/consumer) structure encounter. Outside it, 根源的葛藤 is exposed without the buffers the old stage-set provided. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.184–185; fn.19, p.193]

**〈存在の強度〉:** People formerly confirmed the *meaning* of life through direct embodied confrontation with human-scale life-tasks. This grounded 存在の強度 (intensity/density of existence). Total delegation removes this confirmation. [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.185–186]

### 4.6 Habermas connection + three departures (§F positioning)

Uegaki's 自立化 thesis is explicitly traced to Habermas's "colonisation of the lifeworld by the system" (*Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns*, 1981) — Footnote 9 uses 「システムの生活世界からの**自立化**」 [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.190–191, fn.9].

Three departures from Habermas:
1. Habermas over-valorises the 〈自立した個人〉 as the subject of communicative consensus.
2. Habermas's framework can only capture contemporary social pathology within 'alienation' and 'reification' categories.
3. Habermas's "system" concept covers only Register 1 of self-regulation (coordination); it does not account for the infrastructure dimension (Register 3) or the life-sustaining basis of 'user life' (Register 2).

## Headline Findings — 7 Items the Orchestrator Must Not Miss

1. **Dual structure + three components is the load-bearing ontological frame.** 〈環境〉 is always subject-constituted and existence-grounding. The human-specific 二重構造 (natural ecosystem + 人為的生態系) is not a binary but a density gradient. The 人為的生態系 / 〈社会〉 has exactly three components: 社会的構造物 / 社会的制度 / 意味体系=世界像. These align as: 社会的構造物+制度 → thesis apparatus/metaverse; 意味体系=世界像 → thesis world/meaning-space.

2. **The three singularities are a strictly cumulative sequence of "cuts."** Singularity 1 (agriculture): direct Human–Nature interface mediated by 〈社会〉 (間接化). Singularity 2 (modernity: nation-state + market + fossil fuels): 〈社会〉 severs coherence with 〈自然〉 (切断). Singularity 3 (current/emergent 〈自己完結社会〉): 〈社会〉 severs coherence with 〈人間〉 (second 切断). Each adds a layer; none cancels the previous. Singularity 3 = the thesis's squeeze event.

3. **The stage-set / apparatus distinction is the world / metaverse mapping.** Stage-set = structures + institutions + meaning-system, inseparably integrated, embedded in the lifeworld. Apparatus = stage-set *minus* meaning-system, self-standing, function-only. This is the most precise conceptual anchor for the thesis's claim that the neo-artificial layer lacks world-completeness.

4. **自立化 (not 自律化) confirmed throughout Ch.6.** Every instance of the concept is 自立化 — becoming self-standing / detaching from the lifeworld. Habermas's 「システムの生活世界からの自立化」 is the explicit antecedent (fn.9, pp.190–191). The apparatus *detaches*; it does not *self-legislate*. Any claim about the apparatus "autonomously governing itself" in a normative sense would mistranslate Uegaki.

5. **The user-arc mechanism.** Entrustment → invisibilisation of three moments → lifeworld hollows out → apparatus becomes self-standing → users are structurally rootless (浮遊) and cut from 存在の連なり → 無限の生 (static, isolated, bodyless). This is the social-ontological *mechanism* underlying the squeeze, not just a cultural diagnosis. Thesis §B user → cocoon arc maps directly onto this sequence.

6. **Three moments + fundamental conflict give the squeeze anthropological depth.** The irreducibility of 根源的葛藤 (individual-vs-collective tension in a maximally-collective mammal) explains *why* 〈社会〉 developed at all. The squeeze/third singularity is not a contingent pathology but the terminal trajectory of a fundamental anthropological tension — 〈社会〉 grew to manage 根源的葛藤 and has now overshot the human substrate it was managing.

7. **Habermas antecedent + Uegaki's three departures.** For §F: Uegaki explicitly acknowledges the Frankfurt School lineage but marks three advances — Register-2 and -3 self-regulation (user-compulsion and fine-grained bodily permeation) are beyond Habermas's framework; and Habermas's over-valorisation of the 自立した個人 is itself a symptom of the apparatus's ideological effect.
