# Findings & Strategic Implications: tekhne→phusis

**Compiled:** 2026-06-01 · **For:** [[master-thesis]] · Companion to [[nomos-phusis-tekhne-CONSOLIDATED-2026-06-01]].

> [!warning] The two syntheses are the AUTHOR'S
> "**Nomos has always been tekhne**" and "**tekhne is becoming phusis**" are the author's own claims — *evidenced-by*, not *stated-by*, the sources. Everything below is the strategic case for *how* to deploy the verbatim in support of those claims while keeping the attribution discipline intact. No source asserts either claim; Brague only *groups* tekhnē + nomos.

## 1. The porosity map — the boundary was never sealed, and exactly one barrier was never crossed

The decisive result of the exhaustive retrieval is that the phusis/technē boundary is **already porous in the ancient canon itself, and porous in both directions**. The thesis does not have to fight the entire tradition; it inherits a tradition that already half-draws the figure it wants to complete.

**Three ancient phusis→technē vectors** (the canon runs the relation this way three times):
1. **technē *completes* phusis.** Aristotle *Physics* II.8: "**art partly completes what nature cannot bring to a finish, and partly imitates her**" (199a15–18). The made is a *continuation* of the given, not sealed off from it.
2. **technē *imitates* phusis.** *Physics* II.2 194a21 ("art imitates nature"); and von Staden's documentation that the ancients *also* ran it the other way — "physis as technē," nature characterised as *technikē* (Erasistratus, Galen). The conceptual traffic is ancient.
3. **technē *conquers/overcomes* phusis.** Antiphon (via Hui): "**we conquer by techne things that defeat us by physis**"; Hadot's "Promethean" forcing of nature to reveal her secrets. This is the domination vector — the deep-historical pedigree of the [[squeeze-ontology]] "disposition to domination."

**One ancient technē→phusis inversion** (the converse, and the closest anticipation of the author's arrow):
- **technē/nous/soul is *prior to and grounds* phusis.** Plato *Laws* X 892b: "**the great and primitive works and actions will be works of art; they will be the first, and after them will come nature … under the government of art and mind**"; and 892c: "the soul may be said to exist by nature." Plato does not merely blur the line — he *reverses the priority* (made grounds given) and re-describes the made-principle (soul/nous) *as* phusis in the truest sense. ⚠ But Plato's "art" is divine cosmic nous, his "nature" is *demoted* not *displaced by a new artificial layer*, and there is no temporal/technological *becoming*. The *form* of the inversion is Platonic; the metaverse-conditioned content is the author's.

**The single un-crossed barrier — the internal *archē*.** Every one of the four vectors above leaves intact the one thing the canon never lets technē have: an **internal principle of motion** (*archē*). This is the wall, stated identically across the canon and the lexica:
- Aristotle *Physics* II.1: natural things have "within itself a principle of motion"; products of art "have no innate impulse to change."
- Aristotle *NE* VI.4: art's products have their "origin in the maker and not in the thing made."
- Heidegger: phusis = "the arising of something from out of itself" (*en heautōi*); the artifact's "origin of the making is 'outside' the thing made" (*en allōi*).
- Iwanami (Japanese, independent): Aristotle's physis = "自分自身のうちに運動の原理をもつもの" ("that which has the principle of motion within itself").

**This is the whole game.** The author's claim — that the metaverse-made is acquiring a *self-running, internally-principled, self-completing* character — is precisely the claim that this last barrier, the internal *archē*, is being crossed. The dossier shows the tradition crossed every *other* aspect of the boundary anciently; what the metaverse does that antiquity did not is supply the made with the one mark antiquity reserved for the given. That sharpens the thesis to a single, defensible point rather than a vague "the boundary is collapsing."

## 2. The Aristotle II.8 counterfactual is the KEYSTONE

The most valuable single sentence in the entire retrieval is Aristotle's own counterfactual at *Physics* II.8 (199b26–31):

> "Art does not deliberate. **If the ship-building art were in the wood, it would produce the same results by nature.** If, therefore, purpose is present in art, it is present also in nature."

This is the tradition's *own statement of the threshold the metaverse satisfies*. Aristotle says, in effect: the *only* thing that keeps ship-building from being a natural process is that the art is *not in the wood* — it is in the shipwright, external to the matter. Put the art *in the wood* — internalise the principle of motion — and you have phusis. The author's entire thesis can be stated as: **the metaverse is the ship-building art getting into the wood.** Self-completing artificiality (Yang's 自足完成化), third-order autonomous systems (Floridi), concretized self-conditioning objects (Simondon) — these are technē acquiring exactly the internal *archē* whose absence is, on Aristotle's own statement, the *sole* thing separating the made from the given.

The dialectical value is that this is **Aristotle arguing the reverse and handing the author the premise anyway.** Aristotle deploys the conditional to argue that *nature*, like art, acts for an end (teleology in both). He never says the barrier is crossed. So the author cites Aristotle's *counterfactual* as the precise specification of the threshold, then argues — as his own contribution — that the metaverse is the condition under which the counterfactual's antecedent becomes actual. This is bullet-proof against an over-reading objection: the author is not claiming Aristotle's authority for the conclusion, only for the *statement of the threshold*.

## 3. The etymological crossing is the most compact way to state how radical the claim is

For the committee members who want the claim's stakes stated in one stroke (米/Mi on philosophical precision; 馬/Ma on evidence-grounding), the etymology delivers it:

- **τέχνη ← \*tek- "produce" / \*te-tk- "build, timber"** (Beekes), the same root as **τέκτων, "the carpenter."** Technē is, at the root, *building*.
- **φύσις ← \*bʰeh₂u- "grow, arise, be"** (Beekes), the verb φύομαι "to grow / be born." Phusis is, at the root, *growing/being*.

These two roots are **maximally opposed**: *to build* vs *to grow/to be*. "Tekhne is becoming phusis" is therefore not a soft metaphor about technology feeling natural; it is the claim that **the built is acquiring the character of the grown** — a category-crossing at the deepest lexical stratum the language has. The Latin twins corroborate from a second family (natura ← *nascor* "be born"; ars ← "the fitting"; lex ← "a collection"), and the Japanese gives the made-pole its sharpest third-party label: nomos as **人為的につくられた** ("artificially made by human agency"). The author can compress the whole stakes-of-the-claim into the single contrast *build → grow*, with verbatim lexical backing in three language families.

## 4. The contemporary field map — ally, rival, frame-challenger

The author's arrow (the made acquiring the character of the given, **A→N**) is the *minority* direction in the contemporary literature; the dominant move is **N→A** (the given absorbed or abolished by the made: McKibben, Vogel, Lee, Preston). Positioning *within* this field — not against it — is what makes the claim respectable rather than eccentric.

**Closest ALLY — Gilbert Simondon.** Simondon's *concretization* is, almost exactly, technē-becoming-phusis: the evolved technical object "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects, tending toward … a closure of the system of causes and effects that exert themselves in a circular fashion within its bounds"; "**As it evolves, this object loses its artificial character**"; and the manuscript variant — "**the object frees itself and becomes naturalized**." The object becomes "its own condition" (self-conditioning, associated milieu). This is *the* precedent that makes the author's mechanism philosophically respectable rather than metaphorical: a serious philosopher of technology already argued that the made acquires the internal recurrent causality (φύσις's internal *archē*) through its own evolution. **The author's thesis = Simondon's concretization scaled from the discrete technical object to an emerging total layer (the metaverse/technosphere), and projected forward as not-yet-"fully-actualized."** Lead with Simondon to establish the move is legitimate; the author's value-add is the *scaling* and the *world-layer* application.

**Closest RIVAL — Luciano Floridi (engage head-on).** Floridi is the rival the author *must* meet, because he shares the *re-ontologizing mechanism* but runs the arrow the opposite way. Floridi's explicit directional statement is the *named inverse* of the author's: "**a genealogy of the natural from the non-natural, not vice versa**"; "the natural is in itself artefactual." For Floridi the *natural* was always already a semantic *construction* — he collapses phusis *into* technē at the level of knowledge. AND Floridi's "marriage of physis and techne" is a *symmetric* normative symbiosis (green ↔ blue co-flourishing), not a one-way conversion. So Floridi diverges twice: on *symmetry* (marriage vs absorption) and on *direction* (natural-from-non-natural vs made-becoming-given). Yet Floridi is also the author's strongest evidential *ally* on mechanism: "re-ontologizing" (the made transforms intrinsic ontology) and "third-order technologies … in charge of their own behaviours … human-independent" are the closest contemporary verbatim to Aristotle's internal principle of motion. **The winning framing: the author *completes / redirects* Floridi's arrow rather than echoes it.** Floridi documents the mechanism (re-ontologizing, autonomy) and the boundary's collapse, but mislabels the *outcome* (he calls the result "infosphere/reality," and where directional, "the natural from the non-natural"); the thesis names the *directional ontological outcome* Floridi's own mechanism implies but he declines to draw — the made hardening into a new given. This both forestalls the "you're just restating Floridi" objection and sharpens the contribution.

**Frame-challenger — Bruno Latour (answer, don't ignore).** Latour neither allies nor rivals on direction; he denies the poles: "nature does not exist (as a domain); it exists only as one half of a pair … one single concept." Latour's challenge is that "technē → phusis" *presupposes* the two purified terms the moderns invented. The author's defensible reply (already half-staged in the squeeze ontology's environmentalist-stratified commitment): the metaverse does not re-assert the *moderns' purified Nature*; it **re-instates, at a higher order, a *new given*** — a neo-artificiality that confronts humans the way nature does, a *re-naturalized made layer* rather than a return to the purified pole. The thesis keeps both poles real (universe = realist substrate; metaverse = new real-level background environment) precisely so the squeeze is articulable — which is itself the reply to Latour.

**The ontological TEST — Keekok Lee.** Lee, though a rival on valuation and default direction, supplies the *criterion* the author must satisfy: the natural is "what is *not* the material embodiment of deliberate human intention" plus an independent "trajectory." So the author's claim, to succeed, must show the metaverse-made has acquired *both* marks: intention-transcending autonomy + a self-running trajectory. Floridi's "human-independent … in charge of their own behaviours" and Simondon's "self-conditioning" are exactly the verbatim that meet Lee's test.

## 5. The author's distinctive signature (what is genuinely original)

Against every interlocutor in §4, the thesis is distinguished on three axes simultaneously — and no other author combines all three:
1. **Asymmetric.** One arrow (made → given-as-*real*-phusis), not Floridi's "marriage," not Latour's "dissolution," not a symmetric reconciliation.
2. **Emerging, not accomplished.** The metaverse is "yet-to-be-realized" / not "fully actualized." McKibben, Vogel, Floridi-*Logic* treat their collapse as *accomplished*; Simondon's is per-object. The author's is a forthcoming world-layer condition.
3. **Metaverse/technosphere-specific.** Driven by a *specific* technology, not biotech (Lee, Preston), not climate (McKibben), not information-in-general (Floridi), not ecology (Latour).

The author should also explicitly **distinguish from Adorno's "second nature."** Adorno/Lukács = the made *appears* as given (ideological *semblance*, "imprisonment"). The thesis is stronger: the metaverse does not merely *appear* natural but acquires the *operative* marks of phusis (autonomy, self-running principle). The slogan: not "the made *looks* given" (Adorno) but "the made *operates* as given" (Simondon-Floridi-flavoured). And it must absorb Bensaude-Vincent's caution: the polarity *evolves*, it does not simply terminate — so the author should frame the collapse as *emerging/directional*, never as a *completed* event.

## 6. How to argue tekhne→phusis in Ch V/VI — a concrete recipe (keep it a flagged PROVOCATION, not a discharge)

The claim is reserved for Chapters V/VI and must remain a **flagged provocation** — argued *for*, positioned, and engaged with rivals, but never presented as proven or as stated-by any source. The recipe:

1. **Lead with the warrant, not the conclusion.** Open with the *etymological + Aristotelian-counterfactual* warrant: the build/grow root-opposition (§3) states the stakes; Aristotle II.8's "if the ship-building art were *in the wood*…" (§2) states the precise threshold. Frame the chapter's claim as: *the metaverse is the condition under which Aristotle's counterfactual antecedent becomes actual.* This grounds the provocation in the tradition's own terms before any contemporary move.

2. **Position via Simondon (legitimation).** Introduce concretization as the precedent that makes "the made acquires an internal *archē*" a respectable philosophical move, not a metaphor. State the author's specific extension explicitly: *Simondon scaled from the technical object to the emerging total layer.* This is the chapter's central positive move.

3. **Engage Floridi as the rival (sharpening).** Lay Floridi's re-ontologizing + third-order autonomy alongside as the contemporary *mechanism* warrant — then mark the divergence head-on: Floridi's arrow runs natural-from-non-natural and his "marriage" is symmetric; the thesis *completes/redirects* the arrow. Doing this engagement *in the chapter* is what protects against the "restating Floridi" objection and demonstrates the contribution.

4. **Answer Latour (frame-defence).** State and answer the dissolution objection: the metaverse re-instates a *new higher-order given* (neo-artificiality as a new phusis), not the moderns' purified Nature. Tie to the environmentalist-stratified commitment of [[squeeze-ontology]] §1.3 (both poles kept real).

5. **Meet Lee's test (criterion).** Show the metaverse-made satisfies the ontological criterion (independence-of-intention + self-running trajectory) via the Floridi/Simondon autonomy verbatim. This is the falsifiability hook.

6. **Footnote where the argument is insufficient.** Be explicit, in a footnote, that: (a) no source asserts the claim — it is the author's synthesis (keep the attribution guard); (b) Floridi's own directional arrow runs the *opposite* way; (c) Plato *Laws* X inverts priority but demotes nature rather than displacing it with a new layer, and uses divine nous not human technē; (d) the collapse is *emerging*, not accomplished (Bensaude-Vincent caution); (e) the claim is a *provocation* offered for its diagnostic yield (it makes the squeeze intelligible), not a demonstrated ontological theorem. Flagging the insufficiency *is* the scope-discipline answer to 劉/Liu (what is argued vs merely asserted) and the logical-derivation answer to 米/Mi.

**Companion claim discipline — "nomos has always been tekhne" (Ch II).** Keep this the author's *grouping*-to-*identity* reading: cite Brague (pp. 14, 47) + LSJ/Preus/Antiphon/Iwanami for the *placement* of nomos on the made side; attribute the *identity* (convention simply *is* a species of making) to the author. The new etymology *strengthens* the placement (νόμος ← "distribute/allot" = humanly apportioned; Iwanami's 人為的につくられた) while keeping the identity the author's move. Keep the Brague guard explicit — reviewers will check whether Brague is over-read.
