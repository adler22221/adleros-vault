# Bhaskar's Primal Squeeze — in Plain Language

> Companion to the verbatim dossier [[bhaskar-PA-faultline-NLM-2026-06-04]]. Verbatim there is authoritative; this file is the de-jargoned reading. Bhaskar's own one-line definition (Dialectic glossary, p.83 area): *"Primal squeeze. The squeeze between the domains of metaphysics … and the a priori, and those of experience … and the a posteriori, ruling out empirically controlled scientific theory and natural necessity alike."*

## A. The squeeze on one page — what / by which two forces / how / why

**WHAT is being squeezed (the "middle term").** The real, knowable middle: the idea that **things in the world have real underlying structures and causal powers** — call it *natural necessity*; water boils because of *what water is* — and that **we come to know these through fallible, experiment-tested theory**, never with certainty but really. Bhaskar's phrase: "empirically controlled scientific theory and its intransitive object … natural necessity" (dossier 2.1, 2.5). Plainly: *the working scientist's commonsense — real mechanisms out there, grasped imperfectly by theory-plus-experiment — gets crushed.*

**BY WHICH TWO FORCES (the two jaws of the vice).** (dossier 2.5, 2.6)
- **Jaw 1 — pure Reason / metaphysics** (the *Platonic / rationalist / "speculative"* side). Thought alone, without experience, is held to reach necessary truth (Plato's Forms; Descartes' certainty; *nous*, "intellectual intuition"). → empirical science is made **redundant**: "Empirically controlled theory is rendered redundant by metaphysical a priorism and rationalism" (2.6). Necessity is kept — but as something known a priori, by mind.
- **Jaw 2 — pure Experience / empiricism** (the *Aristotelian-into-Humean / "positivistic"* side). All we have is sense-data and the regular patterns we notice ("constant conjunctions"). → real necessity **vanishes**: "natural necessity disappears in the empiricist problem of induction" (2.6). Experience is kept — but yields only regularities, never necessity (Hume).

**HOW the squeeze happens.** Both jaws secretly share one assumption: *the real is exhausted by the actual* (**actualism**) and *to be is to be knowable* (the **epistemic fallacy**). Grant that, and — as Bhaskar says — "rationalism and empiricism (or some combination of them) seem to exhaust the alternatives" (2.6): there is **no room** for the third thing, real structures that are neither self-evident to reason nor directly visible to the senses, knowable only indirectly and fallibly. So the middle is crushed from both sides at once — Reason: *we don't need it*; Experience: *there's no such thing*. Rationalism and empiricism look like sworn enemies but are "**necessary conditions of each other**" (5.2), two routes to one denial — which is why Western philosophy **swings between them endlessly** instead of resolving the problem. That endless swing *is* the "fault-line."

**WHY the squeeze happens (the motive).** The **demand for certainty**. The founding wish — Bhaskar calls it "fundamentalism" — is for knowledge with a doubt-proof foundation, "an unhypothetical starting-point" (5.4, 6.x), and he reads it as a **fear of change and error**: "The quest for certain foundations is expressive of fear of change, which 'manifests itself in the normative guise of the fear of error'" (6.6). But certainty can come only from pure reason *or* pure given fact — and both require throwing out the fallible real middle. **The squeeze is the price of the craving for certainty.**

**WHAT IT COSTS — and why it is the thesis's *nonmaterial* driver.** Once natural necessity is squeezed out, there is no longer a **real, structured world standing over against us**. The world collapses either into **mind** (idealism — "the world … reduced to an autonomised philosophy"; *anthropomorphism*, the cosmos "painting … in the image of man" — 3.3, 3.4) or into **our facts** (empiricism — "autonomized sense-experience," reading the world "like the time off a clock"; *anthropocentrism*, "taking man as the centre … of the cosmos" — 4.1, 4.3). Either way **the human becomes the measure of the real** (the "anthropic fallacy," 7.1) — and whatever fails the certainty-criterion is **excluded**: the quest for an indubitable foundation "inevitably splits reality into two (viz., that which conforms to its criterion and that which does not)" (6.4/6.6). *That split is the intellectual engine of the mode's disposition to totalize and exclude.*

## B. Plain-English glossary
- **intransitive** = the world as it really is, independent of us (structures, mechanisms). **transitive** = our fallible, changing knowledge of it.
- **epistemic fallacy** = defining what *exists* by what we can *know* (mistaking reality for our access to it). **ontic fallacy** = the reverse — knowledge as a passive imprint of the facts.
- **actualism** = assuming the real is only what *actually happens* — no unexercised powers, no mechanisms beneath the events.
- **ontological monovalence** = assuming reality is all positive presence, no real *absence* — so genuine change/coming-to-be becomes unthinkable.
- **natural necessity** = the real causal power of a thing (why, given its nature, it *must* act so), holding whether or not it shows up in an event ("transfactually"). *The squeezed-out middle.*
- **alethic truth** = the truth *of* things (their real ground), not merely true statements.
- **speculative illusion** = dissolving science + world *into* reason/philosophy (Platonic/idealist pole). **positivistic illusion** = dissolving theory *into* given sense-experience (Aristotelian/empiricist pole).
- **autonomisation** = treating as free-standing what isn't — reason cut from the world ("autonomised philosophy"), or perception cut from theory ("autonomised sense-experience"). **parthenogenetic** = "virgin birth": thought generating reality purely out of itself.
- **anthropomorphism** = projecting *mind/reason* onto the cosmos (world = reason). **anthropocentrism** = making *man the measure* (real = perceivable). **anthropic fallacy / anthroporealism** = the umbrella — defining *being* by *human being*.
- **unholy trinity** = ontological monovalence + epistemic fallacy + primal squeeze = "irrealism" (denial of a real, structured, independent world).
- **underlabouring** = (Locke's term, Bhaskar's self-description) humble philosophical ground-clearing *for* science — not system-building from on high.

## C. Per-point plain gloss (keyed to the verbatim dossier)
- **Pt 1 — the fault-line:** Plato grounds sure knowledge in a *separate realm* of Forms; Aristotle in essences-plus-intuition read off *this* world. Neither has the idea of *real hidden structures*, so neither can actually justify universal/necessary knowledge — philosophy gets "stuck" here, and has been ever since.
- **Pt 2 — primal squeeze:** see A.
- **Pt 3 — Platonic pole:** reality reduced to self-generating Reason; world made in the image of mind (anthropomorphism); rationalism.
- **Pt 4 — Aristotelian pole:** reality reduced to bare facts read straight off the world "like the time off a clock"; man the measure (anthropocentrism); empiricism.
- **Pt 5 — anti-essentializing (the defense):** Bhaskar is **not** attacking Plato or Aristotle the men — both had "correct intuitions"; he is diagnosing a shared *mistaken problematic* the whole tradition inherited ("not … a case of Plato or Aristotle").
- **Pt 6 — quest for certainty:** the whole mess is driven by the craving for an absolutely certain foundation that needs no further justification — which Bhaskar reads as fear of change/error.
- **Pt 7 — anthropic fallacy:** the deep bias of Western thought — explaining *being* in terms of *human* being; rejecting it "carries through the Copernican revolution … in philosophy."

## D. Two squeezes, one shape (the link to the thesis)
- **Bhaskar's primal squeeze is *epistemic*:** the real middle (natural necessity / the structured world) is squeezed *out of knowledge*, between pure reason and pure experience.
- **The thesis's squeeze is *ontological*:** the *world* (the made meaning-space) is squeezed *out of being*, between the universe and the metaverse.

Same figure — *the elimination of the middle* — driven by the same totalizing disposition (certainty-seeking reason; the self-completing artificiality-mode) that admits only what conforms to it and flattens the plural middle. **Bhaskar's primal squeeze is the nonmaterial / epistemic ancestor of the thesis's ontological squeeze:** the founding *intellectual* move that, once materially realized (Needham–Pomeranz: mechanistic interventionism + externalisation), becomes the planetary squeeze.
