### C1. Kant, "An Answer to the Question: What Is Enlightenment?" (1784)
- **Status:** ✅ VERIFIED from public-domain OA (essay is public domain; quoting in full is unproblematic).
- **Akademie pagination:** the opening definition + *Sapere aude!* motto sit at the very start of the essay = **Ak. 8:35** (Kants gesammelte Schriften, Preußische Akademie der Wissenschaften, Vol. VIII, p. 35). German original: "Aufklärung ist der Ausgang des Menschen aus seiner selbst verschuldeten *Unmündigkeit*. … *Sapere aude!* Habe Muth dich deines eigenen Verstandes zu bedienen!"
- **Verbatim (Ted Humphrey trans., Hackett 1992 — matches the wording flagged in the task):** source = NYPL public PDF; extracted locally via PyMuPDF.

> [VERBATIM] "Enlightenment is man's emergence from his self-imposed immaturity. Immaturity is the inability to use one's understanding without guidance from another. This immaturity is self-imposed when its cause lies not in lack of understanding, but in lack of resolve and courage to use it without guidance from another. *Sapere Aude!* 'Have courage to use your own understanding!'—that is the motto of enlightenment."
> — (Kant, 1784 / trans. Humphrey 1992: Ak. 8:35)

- **Translation-variant note (so the author can match register):** *Unmündigkeit* is rendered differently across the standard English editions — pin whichever the surrounding Ch3-v5 prose already uses:
  - **Humphrey (Hackett 1992):** "emergence from his self-imposed **immaturity**" / "Have courage to use your own understanding!" (above).
  - **Mary J. Gregor (Cambridge, *Practical Philosophy*, 1996):** "Enlightenment is the human being's emergence from his self-incurred **minority**. **Minority** is inability to make use of one's own understanding without direction from another. … *Sapere aude!* [dare to be wise] Have courage to make use of your *own* understanding!" — (Kant, 1784 / trans. Gregor 1996: Ak. 8:35).
  - **Lewis White Beck (*On History*, 1963):** "Enlightenment is man's release from his self-incurred **tutelage**. **Tutelage** is man's inability to make use of his understanding without direction from another." (the older standard; "Have courage to use your own reason!").
  - The task brief's phrasing ("emergence from his self-incurred immaturity (Unmündigkeit) … Sapere aude! Have courage to use your own understanding!") is a **hybrid** — "emergence/immaturity/your own understanding" = Humphrey, "self-incurred" = Gregor. Recommend citing **one** edition consistently; Humphrey or Gregor both carry the exact "your own understanding" gloss.

**Route for Ch3-v5:** Canonical anchor for "Enlightenment" as self-emancipation through reason. Cite with Ak. 8:35 + the chosen translator. > **(a) Opening / "disenchantment of the world" — chapter "The Concept of Enlightenment", p.1 (carries to p.2):**
> [VERBATIM] "Enlightenment, understood in the widest sense as the advance of thought, has always aimed at liberating human beings from fear and installing them as masters. Yet the wholly enlightened earth is radiant with triumphant calamity. Enlightenment's program was the disenchantment of the world. It wanted to dispel myths, to overthrow fantasy with knowledge."
> — (Horkheimer & Adorno, 2002: 1)

> **(b) Domination of nature → domination of human beings, p.2 (Bacon passage gloss):**
> [VERBATIM] "What human beings seek to learn from nature is how to use it to dominate wholly both it and human beings. Nothing else counts."
> — (Horkheimer & Adorno, 2002: 2)

> **(c) "Enlightenment is totalitarian", p.4:**
> [VERBATIM] "… by being used as arguments they are made to acknowledge the very principle of corrosive rationality of which enlightenment stands accused. Enlightenment is totalitarian."
> — (Horkheimer & Adorno, 2002: 4)

> **(c′) Restated, p.18:**
> [VERBATIM] "For enlightenment is totalitarian as only a system can be. Its untruth does not lie in the analytical method … but in its assumption that the trial is prejudged."

> **(d) Domination paid for by estrangement; "industrialism makes souls into things", p.21:**
> [VERBATIM] "Not only is domination paid for with the estrangement of human beings from the dominated objects, but the relationships of human beings, including the relationship of individuals to themselves, have themselves been bewitched by the objectification of mind. … Animism had endowed things with souls; industrialism makes souls into things."
> — (Horkheimer & Adorno, 2002: 21)

> **(e) Control of internal *and* external nature as the "absolute purpose of life", p.24 (the dialectic by which mastery of outer nature becomes repression of inner nature/self):**
> [VERBATIM] "At the turning points of Western civilization, whenever new peoples and classes have more heavily repressed myth, from the beginnings of the Olympian religion to the Renaissance, the Reformation, and bourgeois atheism, the fear of unsubdued, threatening nature — a fear resulting from nature's very materialization and objectification — has been belittled as animist superstition, and the control of internal and external nature has been made the absolute purpose of life."
> — (Horkheimer & Adorno, 2002: 24)

**Route for Ch3-v5:** These ground the §C `[VERIFY]` lines on the Enlightenment's disenchantment/mastery program and the dialectic whereby domination of nature recoils into domination of human beings and the self. Cite via `horkheimerDialecticEnlightenment2002` with the page numbers above.

**The "simultaneous" emperorship — printed pp.10–11:**
> [VERBATIM] "In the case of the Qing empire, this rulership was definitively an emperorship: a mechanism of governance over a domain in parts. The Qing emperorship was in its expression what I have called 'simultaneous' (in Chinese *hebi*, in Manchu *kamcime*). That is, its edicts, its diaries, and its monuments were deliberately designed as imperial utterances in more than one language (at a minimum Manchu and Chinese; very commonly Manchu, Chinese, and Mongolian; and after the middle eighteenth century frequently in Manchu, Chinese, Mongolian, Tibetan, and the Arabic script of many Central Asian Muslims that is often called 'Uigur'), as simultaneous exp[ressions] …"

**Multi-constituency rulership as an "ensemble of instruments" — printed pp.9–10:**

> [VERBATIM] "For some nations presently existing, those 'earlier times' were centuries of government by empires of conquest, whose rulerships had need of constructing categories of affiliation that would correspond to multiple, simultaneously expressed codes of legitimacy in the rulership. ### B2. Rawski, *The Last Emperors: A Social History of Qing Imperial Institutions* (1998, Univ. California Press, ISBN 9780520228375) — multi-ethnic empire; success ≠ sinicization
- **Status:** ⚠️ NOT held locally; libgen attempt in background. Below = publisher/MUSE-review summary (no verbatim page yet).

> [SECONDARY — publisher copy / Project MUSE review, NOT verbatim] Rawski's central argument: "the Qing empire was founded on multiethnic coalitions," and its rulers "sought to perpetuate these alliances by addressing each of the constituent peoples that came under Qing rule in their own cultural vocabularies." - **Route for §B:** Rawski is the locus classicus of the "New Qing History" anti-sinicization claim — the Qing as a consciously multi-ethnic empire whose universal-rulership integrated distinct constituencies. Pairs with Crossley for the "different AOS" point.

**(c) The self-reinforcing feedback loop — the "vicious cycle" (悪循環) by which technical mastery of nature generates new harms, p.156:**
> [VERBATIM-JP] "現代の科学技術文明がいかに無意識に、また安易に自然界から資源を収奪し、大量に消費することの上に成り立っていたか… もし新たに開発される技術が、たとえば原子力発電、「緑の革命」、遺伝子組換え作物などのように、新たな形態の環境負荷、資源の大量消費、生態系の攪乱を引き起こす危険をはらむものであるなら、それは問題の解決にはならず、むしろ、その特定の目的（たとえばエネルギー生産や食糧生産）を達成すると同時に新たな害悪と難問題をも作り出すという悪循環に陥ることになる。"
> — (三浦 [Miura], 2006: 156)
> **English gloss:** "[We were compelled to recognize] how unconsciously and how easily modern scientific-technical civilization had been built upon plundering (収奪) resources from nature and consuming them in vast quantities. … If the newly developed technology is one that — like nuclear power, the 'Green Revolution', or genetically-modified crops — carries the danger of provoking new forms of environmental burden, mass consumption of resources, and disruption of ecosystems, then it is no solution to the problem; rather, it falls into a *vicious cycle* (悪循環) in which, at the very moment it achieves its specific purpose (e.g. energy or food production), it simultaneously produces new evils and intractable problems."

**(d) Root cause = the power of capital and state; multinational firms + the state of advanced-industrial nations as the greatest driver of limitless resource-plunder, p.261:**
> [VERBATIM-JP] "三種の合理性による生産力の増大はとくに発展途上国において際限なき資源の収奪と自然破壊を加速させている一方、先進工業国では大量生産・大量消費の結果として大量の廃棄物を蓄積して環境負荷を増し… 発展途上国と先進工業国に共通する環境悪化の根本原因は三種の合理性を追求する権力、すなわち資本と国家にある。国内および発展途上国で環境悪化を拡大し加速させている最大の原因は先進工業国の多国籍企業と国家なのである。"
> — (三浦 [Miura], 2006: 261)
> **English gloss:** "While the increase of productive power driven by the *three rationalities* [economic, scientific-technical, political] accelerates limitless plunder (収奪) of resources and destruction of nature especially in the developing countries, in the advanced industrial nations the result of mass production and mass consumption is an accumulation of vast waste that increases the environmental burden… The fundamental cause of environmental degradation common to both developing and advanced-industrial countries lies in the *power that pursues the three rationalities — namely capital and the state*. The greatest cause expanding and accelerating environmental degradation, both domestically and in the developing countries, is the multinational corporations and the state of the advanced industrial nations."
