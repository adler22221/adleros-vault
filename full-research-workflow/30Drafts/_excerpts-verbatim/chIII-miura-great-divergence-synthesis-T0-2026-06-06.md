# Ch III — Does Miura have a "Great Divergence / why-the-West" synthesis? (T0 verification)

> **Compiled:** 2026-06-06. **Mandate:** verify whether Miura Nagamitsu has an *explicit* "Great Divergence / why-the-West" synthesis — an argument grounding the West's distinctive modern/developmental path (vs China / the non-West) in its **metaphysical relation to nature** (nature as object of domination/property). If it exists, quote it; if it does **not** exist as an explicit divergence-thesis, say so plainly. Honest negative findings are valuable.
> **Builds on (does NOT re-extract):** `[[ch4-translation-research-2026-06-04]]` §BONUS-b (Miura's Locke/Native-American dispossession verbatim, print pp.87/95–99) and `[[miura2004-ch1-selfsufficiency-2026-06-04]]` (the 2004 supply-zone/dependence argument). ## ★ VERDICT

**NO explicit "Great Divergence / why-the-West" synthesis exists in Miura.** — *Confidence: HIGH.*

In neither `環境思想と社会` (2006, the nature-domination book) nor `国際関係の中の環境問題` (2004, the supply-zone book) does Miura advance an argument that grounds the *West's distinctive developmental/modern path relative to China or the non-West* in the West's metaphysical relation to nature. He has no comparative West-vs-China chapter, no "why did modern science/capitalism arise in Europe and not China" question, and **no engagement with the Great-Divergence literature** (大分岐 = 0, なぜ西洋/なぜヨーロッパ = 0, ポメランツ/Pomeranz = 0, ニーダム/Needham = 0, ウォーラーステイン-in-body = 0, 近代化/modernization = 0; 中国/China = 4 hits, all about **contemporary military/nuclear spending**, unrelated). See the term-scan and TOC evidence below.

What Miura actually supplies are **two PARTS** out of which a divergence synthesis can be *constructed by the thesis* — but the synthesis would be the **thesis's own**, not Miura's:

1. **An internal-Western genealogy of nature-domination** (2006): a 思想史 of 自然観 tracing how *Western* thought came to treat nature as an object of mastery/property — Greek + Christian roots → **Bacon** (技術的自然支配) → **Descartes** (機械論的自然支配) → **Locke** (所有のための自然支配, incl. colonial dispossession) → **Adam Smith / Marx** (productivism). This is an account of a *Western* tendency; it is **not** posed against China and **not** offered to explain a divergence. Its question is "why did the modern *environmental crisis* arise," not "why did the *West* diverge from the *rest*."
2. **A structural North–South supply-zone/dependence argument** (2004): advanced industrial nations fix colonies/developing nations as resource-supply-and-dump zones; the asymmetry outlives formal empire. This is a **political-economy** dependence claim keyed to 産業革命以後 (after the industrial revolution), **not** a *metaphysics-of-nature* explanation of *why the West rather than China* industrialised first.

**Therefore:** the claim that the thesis's "Enlightenment mode" aligns with "Miura's synthesis of the great divergence" is **not supportable as stated**. The alignment must rest on the **thesis's own synthesis** — assembled from Needham (the why-the-West *science* question) + Pomeranz (the contingency answer) + Miura's *Locke/nature-domination parts* — with Miura cited only for those parts. (This exactly matches the pending flag the draft already carries: `[[chIII_enlightenment-mode-definition_2026-06-06]]` Integration-notes line "NOT used: Miura's 'Great Divergence *synthesis*' framing (still unverified)… The divergence-synthesis claim is the pending T0." **This T0 resolves it: do not attribute a divergence synthesis to Miura.**)

### 1b. The book's own stated project (はじめに, printed pp. iii–vi / PDF pp.4–7)
Miura states the book's question and method in the preface — and it is **not** the divergence question:

> 現代の環境問題もこれまで人類が抱いてきたさまざまな自然観とそれにもとづいて自然を利用してきた仕方によって生み出されたものである。…**現代の環境問題がなぜ生じたのか**を知るためには、過去の人類がどんな自然観を描いて自然とつきあってきたのかを知らなければならない。
>
> EN: "The contemporary environmental problem too was produced by the various **views of nature** humankind has held and the ways of using nature based on them. … To know **why the contemporary environmental problem arose**, we must know what views of nature past humankind painted and how they consorted with nature."
`[MIURA-VERBATIM: miuraKankyoShisoTo2006 | print p.iii (PDF p.4) | "現代の環境問題がなぜ生じたのか…どんな自然観を描いて自然とつきあってきたのか"]`

The driving "why" is **why the environmental crisis**, answered through a history of *views of nature* — **not** "why did the West diverge." The comparand is *past humanity vs. present*, not *West vs. China*.

### 1c. The full table of contents (目次, PDF pp.9–14) — no comparative/divergence chapter
The seven parts:
- **I** 古代における人間と自然の理解 (ancient: Greek — Hesiod/Plato/Aristotle — + Christianity)
- **II** 近代思想史における理性と自然 — **the nature-domination genealogy**: 「実用的技術による自然支配―フランシス・ベーコン」/「数量的・機械論的科学による自然支配―デカルト」/「所有の増大のための自然支配―ジョン・ロック」/ Adam Smith / Marx
- **III/IV** 進化と人間の倫理 (Darwin, Huxley, Kropotkin, Bookchin, Lorenz, Wilson)
- **V** 現代資本主義の経済合理性とその帰結 (incl.「資源供給地としての植民地・発展途上国」「先進工業国家の発展途上国に対する経済的支配」)
- **VI** 思想史における合理性と理性 (Aristotle, Locke, Whitehead, Horkheimer, Habermas, Picht, Kant)
- **VII** 永続可能性と公正 (sustainability & justice)

**There is no part, chapter, or subsection comparing the West with China / East Asia, and none asking why modernity/industry/science arose in Europe rather than elsewhere.** The nature-domination thread (Part II) is presented as a *Western* lineage of ideas leading to the *environmental* crisis, full stop.

### 1d. Picht passage — Miura's own placement of the nature-domination tradition as *internal-Western*
Where Miura does periodize the "domination of nature" stance, he traces it to **Bacon and Descartes** *within Western thought*, not to a West-vs-China fork:

> 後者はフランシス・ベーコンとデカルト以来の伝統で、自然探究を人間生活の便宜と安楽のために利用することを目的とする。…**自然支配**と人間の物質的福祉の向上を唯一の目的に据えた。
>
> EN: "The latter [tradition of science] is the tradition **since Francis Bacon and Descartes**, aiming to use the inquiry into nature for the convenience and ease of human life … setting the **domination of nature** and the advance of human material welfare as its sole purpose."
`[MIURA-VERBATIM: miuraKankyoShisoTo2006 | print p.275 (PDF p.290) | "フランシス・ベーコンとデカルト以来の伝統で…自然支配と人間の物質的福祉の向上を唯一の目的に据えた"]` *(Miura summarizing G. Picht.)*

## 2. What Miura DOES have (the PARTS, with the closest-to-divergence sentence quoted)

### 2a. The internal-Western nature-domination genealogy (2006, Part II)
Bacon → Descartes → Locke → Smith/Marx, each titled as a mode of 自然支配. Miura explicitly generalizes the Locke/Smith/Marx over-valuation-of-labour / under-valuation-of-nature as **a feature of *modern* thought** (近代思想の特徴) — note this is "modern," characterized internally, **not** "Western-as-against-Chinese":

> 労働の生産力への過大評価と自然の生産力への過小評価はロックだけでなく、…アダム・スミス…やマルクス…にも共通する**近代思想の特徴**といえよう。
>
> EN: "The over-valuation of labour's productive power and the under-valuation of nature's productive power may be called **a feature of modern thought** common not only to Locke but … to Adam Smith … and Marx."
`[MIURA-VERBATIM: miuraKankyoShisoTo2006 | print p.88 (PDF p.103) | "労働の生産力への過大評価と自然の生産力への過小評価は…近代思想の特徴"]`

The Locke/colonial-dispossession material (print pp.87, 95–99 — "nature, mediated by labour, is made an object of property, and the ownership of nature … is bound up with domination over … alien peoples") is **already transcribed verbatim** in `[[ch4-translation-research-2026-06-04]]` §BONUS-b1–b5; not re-quoted here. That is the part the thesis legitimately cites for the *mechanism* of one world overwriting another.

### 2b. THE single closest sentence to a "Western particularity" claim — and why it is NOT a divergence synthesis
The one place Miura calls something "particular to the early-modern West" is about **Locke's low valuation of land**, and he immediately frames it as a *historically contingent constraint on Locke* (colonial America's cheap land), explicitly **disavowing** any general/essential law — the opposite of a civilizational-essence divergence claim, and in fact congenial to *Pomeranz-style contingency*:

> したがって、ロックによる土地の低い評価は**近代初期の西欧が置かれた特殊な歴史的状況に制約されている**と思われる。しかしもしもそのような制約から離れて、一般的に考えるならば、ある国の土地の価格は人口の増加とともに上昇するであろう。
>
> EN: "Therefore Locke's low valuation of land seems **constrained by the particular historical situation in which the early-modern West was placed**. But if we think generally, apart from such a constraint, the price of a country's land will rise as its population increases."
`[MIURA-VERBATIM: miuraKankyoShisoTo2006 | print p.88 (PDF p.103) | "ロックによる土地の低い評価は近代初期の西欧が置かれた特殊な歴史的状況に制約されている"]`

This is a claim about **Locke's context**, used to *relativize* his nature-valuation — not a thesis that the West's metaphysics of nature *caused its developmental divergence from China*. It contains no comparand civilization and no developmental-path claim. It is the high-water mark, and it falls well short of a divergence synthesis.

### 2c. The 2004 supply-zone / dependence argument — political economy, not metaphysics-of-divergence
From `[[miura2004-ch1-selfsufficiency-2026-06-04]]` (image-recovered verbatim; the 2004 PDF text-layer is CID-mojibake — confirmed again this pass: a Kanji-term scan of `.research/cache/txt/miura2004_full.txt` returns 0 for *every* term incl. 技術, i.e. the layer is unusable, so the image-recovered note is authoritative). Miura (2004, print pp.15–16) argues colonies/developing nations are fixed as resource-supply-and-dump zones and that this asymmetry persists after formal empire. Crucially it is keyed to **産業革命以後** and stated as **structural political economy** (「押しとどめられた」/「依然として…基本的に存続している」/「外貨獲得のために大量の資源を輸出しなければならない」), **not** as a metaphysics-of-nature account of *why Europe and not China* industrialised. (Its Ch.1 endnotes cite Weber's *Protestant Ethic* and Mill — again, *citations in passing*, not a why-the-West synthesis.)

This is the part the thesis can cite for the *outward, world-fixing* face of the mode (the supply-zone lock-in), but it is a **North–South dependence** thesis, not a **Great-Divergence** thesis.

## 3. How the thesis should cite Miura on the divergence

**Do NOT write "Miura's synthesis of the great divergence" or attribute a why-the-West argument to Miura.** That synthesis does not exist in his texts; asserting it would be a fabricated characterization (precisely the `[VERIFY]`-class error this T0 was run to prevent).

**Recommended handling (keeps every claim grounded):**
1. **Keep the divergence *frame* as the thesis's own**, assembled from the literature the draft already names at "standard level": **Needham** (why systematic controlling science arose in Europe, not a technically-equal China) + **Pomeranz** (the *contingency* answer — coal, New-World land/labour — rather than civilizational essence). Cite these for the divergence *question and its materialist answer*. *(Both are summarized, not quoted, in `[[chIII_enlightenment-mode-definition_2026-06-06]]`; pin one Needham locus — e.g. *The Grand Titration* — and Pomeranz, *The Great Divergence* (2000), if leaned on.)*
2. **Cite Miura only for the PARTS he actually argues**, and label them as such:
   - the **Locke/nature-as-property → colonial-dispossession mechanism** — `(三浦 / Miura 2006: 95–99)` — for "a theory of property does the work of a justification of conquest; the ownership of nature is bound up with domination over alien peoples" (verbatim in `[[ch4-translation-research-2026-06-04]]`);
   - optionally the **internal-Western nature-domination genealogy** (Bacon→Descartes→Locke→Smith/Marx as 自然支配 / 近代思想の特徴), `(三浦 2006: e.g. 88, Part II)`, if the chapter wants Miura's lineage of the controllability-relation's *Western* pedigree;
   - optionally the **2004 supply-zone/dependence** point `(三浦編 2004: 15–16, ch.1 by 三浦)` for the world-fixing/overwriting face — flagged as **North–South political economy**, not divergence.
3. **Phrase the thesis's added move explicitly as the thesis's.** E.g.: *"To this materialist story [Needham/Pomeranz] the thesis adds a metaphysical layer — that what the West universalised was, before any technology, the controllability-relation itself — and reads Miura's Locke as the mechanism in miniature."* (This is exactly how `[[chIII_enlightenment-mode-definition_2026-06-06]]` line ~30 already phrases it. **That phrasing is correct and should be kept; the only fix is to ensure no sentence elsewhere credits Miura with the *divergence synthesis*.**)

> One-line citation rule of thumb: **Needham/Pomeranz → the divergence; Miura → the nature-domination *mechanism* (Locke) and, separately, the supply-zone *dependence*. The synthesis joining them is the thesis's.**
