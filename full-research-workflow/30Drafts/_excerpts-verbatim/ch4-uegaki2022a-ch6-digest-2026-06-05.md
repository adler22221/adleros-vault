## (1) The Concept of 〈社会的装置〉 (pp. 171–174)

### Three-component definition

Uegaki opens by consolidating what has been introduced in earlier chapters. 〈社会的装置〉 is, in the first instance, a massive **infrastructure** fusing the market economy and bureaucratic apparatus with the outputs of modern science and technology — information, machines, and pharmaceuticals [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.171–172]. In the second instance it possesses a **self-regulation function** (自己調整機能): by connecting to it, people's actions are coordinated without their conscious awareness [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.172]. In the third instance, this apparatus **liberates** people from the constraints that formerly bound life — but that liberation is simultaneously total **dependence** on the apparatus [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.172].

Structurally, the apparatus combines two of the three components of 〈社会〉 originally identified in Chapter 3: it is the compound of **「社会的構造物」** (social structures, material infrastructure) and **「社会的制度」** (social institutions, formal rule-systems), operating as their enormous composite [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.172].

### Three registers of self-regulation

Uegaki then distinguishes three analytically separate registers within the "self-regulation function":

**Register 1 — Classic market/bureaucratic coordination** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.172–173]: In the market economy, individually profit-maximising actions yield efficient aggregate distribution; in the bureaucratic apparatus, the execution of assigned duties achieves the organisation's overall goal. Neither the market nor the bureaucracy has physical substance in itself, yet each produces a social reality through the coordinated flow of actions. The **information-world/net-space** (情報世界 — what is ordinarily called "the net," a vast accumulation of digital information in electronic space) is placed alongside these two as displaying the same logic: the aggregated sending and receiving of signals through mere cables and devices produces what amounts to a second world, tightly synchronised with physical reality [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.173–174].

**Register 2 — The necessity of connection** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.174]: In contemporary society every person is compelled to live as a 〈ユーザー〉; the choice of *which kind* of user to be is free, but the option of *not* being a user does not exist. This compulsion is itself a self-regulating mechanism: users, driven by the necessity of sustaining their lives through the apparatus, collectively drive the apparatus without being aware of doing so. The apparatus does not produce anything directly; it is driven by the accumulated life-necessity of countless users.

**Register 3 — Fine-grained permeation into lived space** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.174–175]: Beyond roads, harbours, and power plants, a "new infrastructure" composed of information, machines, and pharmaceuticals is spreading into every corner of daily life — navigation systems replacing route-calculation, cashier systems replacing service labour, and (prospectively) autonomous-vehicle transport networks, personalised medical supply, and AI that generates mass-market artworks, analyses big data to recommend products, or even identifies compatible partners. These elements increasingly regulate human action below the threshold of awareness.

### Summary characterisation

Uegaki summarises: 〈社会的装置〉 is the giant complex of 「社会的構造物」 and 「社会的制度」 equipped with this self-regulation function; and it is precisely because this function operates that the "life as a user" (〈ユーザー〉としての生) becomes realisable in contemporary society [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.175].

**Term note (amoeba/permeation metaphor):** Uegaki does not use an explicit "amoeba" (アメーバ) metaphor in this chapter. The image of pervasive, unmarked permeation is conveyed through Register 3 (fine-grained infiltration of lived space). If you recall an "amoeba" term from elsewhere in the book, it is not present in Ch.6 body and should be verified against a different chapter.

## (2) Continuity / Discontinuity with '〈生〉の舞台装置' (pp. 175–177)

Uegaki poses a comparative question: if the apparatus's essence lies in self-regulation, does the old 'stage-set of life' (「〈生〉の舞台装置」) — the social formations that mediated 〈根源的葛藤〉 and enabled collective survival — not also possess self-regulation? If so, are the two simply continuous?

### Evidence of continuity

Using the examples of traditional community assemblies (寄り合い) and customs (慣習), and contrasting the autonomous vehicle with the traditional hoe (鍬), Uegaki shows that *all* of these devices coordinate human action so as to reduce the burden of 〈根源的葛藤〉 [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.175–177]. The assembly smoothed consensus; customs pre-empted friction. Both functioned by regulating action. Thus at a very general level, both the old stage-set and the modern apparatus share the property of self-regulation — and this is the ground for their **continuity** [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.177].

### The decisive non-continuity: the severed third component

However, the traditional stage-set's three components — social structures, social institutions, and **meaning-system/world-image** (意味体系=世界像) — were, in the traditional context, **inseparable**. The assembly was simultaneously a built space, a procedural institution, and a context dense with shared meaning. The hoe was a material object and simultaneously a carrier of the social relationships of those who made it, those who used it before, and those who would use it after [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.176–177]. Each component was co-present and mutually constitutive.

By contrast, in the market economy and bureaucratic apparatus, what matters is the **function** — the delivery of goods/services — not the *meaning* of the actions involved [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.177]. The automated new infrastructure similarly requires only the function, not the meaning. This means 〈社会的装置〉 is constituted by a 〈社会〉 **purified to** social structures + social institutions alone, having separated from — and being self-sufficient without — the meaning-system/world-image [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.177].

**Reformulation:** 〈社会的装置〉 = the portion of the former 'stage-set of life' consisting of 「社会的構造物」 and 「社会的制度」, which has separated from the originally-inseparable 「意味体系=世界像」 and protruded abnormally [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.177–178]. This formulation carries directly into the autonomisation argument of (3).

## (3) 〈社会的装置〉の〈生活世界〉からの自立化 (pp. 178–183)

### The term: 自立化 (not 自律化)

**Confirmed:** Uegaki uses the term **自立化** (becoming self-standing / becoming independent) throughout this section — at book pp.178 (OCR line 6265), p.178 (line 6274), p.178 (line 6284–6285), p.181 (line 6330), p.184 (line 6470), and p.187 (line 6573). The term **自律化** does not appear in the Chapter 6 body. This is directly relevant to the thesis's 自立 ≠ 自律 distinction: Uegaki's concept is firmly in the **自立** register — 'becoming self-standing/independent from' — not in the autonomy-of-will sense.

### The historical condition for autonomisation

Autonomisation was *not* triggered immediately by the establishment of the market economy and bureaucratic apparatus [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.178]. Even after these institutions existed, as long as the lifeworld retained a strong human foundation, the meaning-system/world-image was still guaranteed within the lifeworld, and the apparatus remained a **supplementary device** (補助装置) embedded in the lifeworld [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.178–179]. Autonomisation required: (a) the dissolution of the local community (地域社会) that had given the lifeworld its substantive form, and (b) the 'hollowing-out' (空洞化) of the lifeworld — which is to say, the completion of 'life as a user' [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.179].

### The school and welfare as case studies

Uegaki illustrates with two examples:

**School (学校):** Modern schools were created to produce responsible national subjects and productive industrial workers — they are themselves a "sub-system" of 〈社会的装置〉. Yet school did not immediately displace lifeworld-based 〈継承〉 (transmission of life-knowledge). The lifeworld — interpersonal relations, lived experience in local context — was originally the primary site of maturation; school was an *auxiliary* space for special knowledge outside that experience [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.179]. Gradually, as dependency on school for 〈継承〉 increased, the lifeworld lost its latent educative power; this further increased dependency on school; and eventually the hypertrophied school alone remained, floating without a lifeworld foundation beneath it [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.180].

**Welfare (福祉):** Originally, welfare was realised through communal life-organisations rooted in local society. As people began to depend on the goods and services delivered by 〈社会的装置〉, mutual aid through life-organisations withered, and local society progressively lost its capacity for mutual support — which drove further dependence on the apparatus, and eventually left only the apparatus as remainder [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.180].

**Pattern:** In both cases: (i) the apparatus begins as auxiliary; (ii) delegation to the apparatus increases; (iii) the lifeworld loses its capacity, which drives further delegation; (iv) the apparatus alone remains, hypertrophied, the lifeworld hollowed out. People initially *chose* to delegate — seeking higher welfare, greater goods/services, liberation from hardship — but once the threshold was crossed, the new framework became obligatory and deployed in a chain reaction [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.181].

### Consequences for the meaning-system/world-image

As the apparatus autonomised, the meaning-system/world-image underwent a progressive **diminishment** (矮小化). The 'life as a user' reduces the three moments of 'human life' to: 「経済活動」, 「自己実現」, 「学校教育」 — stripped of their originary life-context [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.181]. Three residual candidates for a meaning-system are assessed and found insufficient:

- **System of scientific knowledge:** Explains the world's facticity but, being value-neutral, cannot supply meaning for *how to live* [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.181–182].
- **Ethics of economic activity:** Provides norms for conduct within the context of economic exchange and consumption, but these norms are useless outside that context and cannot substitute for a meaning-system/world-image [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.182].
- **Nationalism-as-user:** The nation-state now provides the only framework for belonging; people reconstruct lost meaning around national identity. But because this occurs without a substantive lifeworld context, it is easily idealised and excessively conceptual [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.182 and note].

## (4) Second Interim Reflection: Reconsidering '〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断' (pp. 182–188, notes pp. 189–198)

### Macro-historical frame

Uegaki re-situates the analysis within the three-singularity scheme of Part II:

- **First singularity** ('establishment of agriculture'): 「〈自然〉と〈人間〉の間接化」 — natural selection pressure effectively neutralised by artificial food networks; humans come under strong influence of large-scale 〈社会〉 [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.183].
- **Second singularity** ('establishment of modern social forms'): 「〈社会〉と〈自然〉の切断」 — through nation-states, market economies, fossil fuels, 〈社会〉 escapes ecological constraints and self-expands by purely social logic [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.183–184].
- **Third singularity** (contemporary): 「〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断」 — 〈社会〉, having already lost coherence with the natural ecosystem, is now losing coherence with the biological 'human animal' [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.183–184].

Macroscopically: the third singularity = the autonomisation of 〈社会的装置〉 from the lifeworld. Microscopically: the third singularity = the completion of 'life as a user' [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.184].

### Pathology of 〈関係性〉: the 'naked individual'

Uegaki argues that the apparatus mediates human relations; outside the apparatus's context, 〈ユーザー〉s face each other as what Ushiku Keisuke (宇城輝人) calls "naked individuals" — stripped of any social-meaning context [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.184–185; note 19, p.193, attributes the "naked individual" phrase to 宇城]. Within the apparatus's context (economic activity), individuals can easily form functional relations by suppressing even personality — because a powerful social form (〈間柄〉: producer/consumer) structures the encounter. But outside that context, there is no mediating structure, and 〈根源的葛藤〉 is exposed without the buffers the old stage-set provided [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.185].

### 〈存在の強度〉 and the 'infinite life'

People formerly found the *meaning* of life confirmed continuously, not by reflection, but through direct confrontation with life-tasks at a human scale — subsistence, care, transmission. This confirmed their sense of existing in a time-spatial *chain of being* (〈存在の連なり〉). Through total delegation to the apparatus, the originary moments of 'human life' are rendered invisible (不可視化); people float detached from this chain [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.185–186].

The result is what Uegaki calls 「無限の〈生〉」 ("infinite life"): the self becomes boundless — not in the sense of continuity through others across time (which would be 'the permanence of life' grounded in 〈連なり〉), but in the sense that *only the static 'now' of the isolated self extends infinitely* [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.186]. 「〈ユーザー〉としての生」 = the completion of this infinite life. And 〈生の脱身体化〉 = the further extension of this condition as body-level prediction and control (by biotech, robotics, AI) is added [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.186–187].

### The 'third cut' reconsidered

The 「〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断」 is not merely about biotechnological modification of the body. More fundamentally, it is:

1. Through the autonomisation of 〈社会的装置〉 + the completion of 'life as a user': for the first time in human history, people are *isolated* from "others who cannot be controlled" — 〈根源的葛藤〉 is exposed naked to every other [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.187].
2. Through the 'invisibilisation' of 'human life': for the first time, people are 'liberated' from the time-spatial chain of being and come to live the 'infinite life' [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.187].

The section ends with a deliberate refusal of easy condemnation: because this situation has continuity with the originary human principle (it follows from 〈社会〉-forming nature), it cannot be called simply 'inhuman.' But because it is genuinely discontinuous from the originary human mode of existence, to accept it unconditionally would be to abandon responsibility toward the human as such [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.187–188]. Uegaki closes with a meditation on the temporal predicament of existing beings — humans have always been thrown into conditions whose consequences they cannot foreknow [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.188].

## 〈社会的装置〉の自立化: 自立 or 自律?

**Finding: 自立化 throughout, without exception.**

Every instance of the compound in Ch.6 is 〈社会的装置〉の**自立化** (自立 = self-standing / independence from). The term 自律化 (autonomy in the self-legislative sense) does not appear. Footnote 9 (book p.190–191) explicitly credits Habermas's 「システムの生活世界からの**自立化**」as the conceptual antecedent, and uses 自立化 in that citation-context as well [VERIFY-PAGE: book p.190–191, fn.9].

This is important for the thesis's terminological distinction: Uegaki's concept of the apparatus 'becoming self-standing' is firmly in the **自立** register (detachment from the lifeworld, gaining operational independence), **not** the **自律** register (self-governance, internal norm-setting). Any claim that the apparatus "autonomously" governs itself in a normative sense would be a mistranslation of Uegaki's intent.

---

## Habermas Connection (Footnote 9, pp. 190–191)

Uegaki's autonomisation thesis is explicitly traced to Habermas's "colonisation of the lifeworld by the system" (*Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns*, 1981) [VERIFY-PAGE: book pp.190–191, fn.9]. Uegaki's key debt: Habermas already spoke of the "自立化" of the system from the lifeworld. However, Uegaki marks three departures:

1. Habermas over-valorises the 〈自立した個人〉 as the subject of communicative consensus.
2. Habermas's framework can only capture contemporary social pathology within the categories of 'alienation' and 'reification.'
3. Habermas's "system" concept focuses on Register 1 of self-regulation (coordination); it does not account for the "infrastructure" dimension or the life-sustaining basis of 'user life' (Register 2 and 3 in Uegaki's typology).

This is relevant to situating Uegaki vis-à-vis the Frankfurt School in the thesis.

## Relevance to Thesis Ch. IV §B / §C

**§B (apparatus and the lifeworld / squeeze mechanism):**
- Uegaki's three-register definition of 〈社会的装置〉 provides the structural account of *what* the apparatus is — specifically the third register (fine-grained permeation into daily/bodily space) maps directly onto the thesis's claim that the metaverse/apparatus penetrates and reshapes the existential ground of the human (the 'world' in the universe/world/metaverse triad).
- The 自立化 narrative (lifeworld hollows out → apparatus becomes self-standing → humans become users) is the social-ontological *mechanism* underlying the squeeze. It explains how the human-scale meaning-space (= the 'world' in the thesis's terminology) loses structural robustness as the apparatus expands.
- The purification of 社会 to structure + institution at the expense of meaning-system resonates with the thesis's claim that the neo-artificial layer (metaverse/social apparatus) lacks the 'world-completeness' that the world-stratum possesses.

**§C (〈社会〉と〈人間〉の切断 / third singularity as the squeeze event):**
- The three-singularity scheme and the characterisation of the third singularity as the self-dissolution of the fit between 〈社会〉 and biological 〈人間〉 maps directly onto the thesis's squeeze hypothesis: the human (the 'world' layer as lived in human bodies) is being squeezed between the universe (the natural substrate that does not disappear) and the metaverse/apparatus (which has achieved independence from the human-scale world).
- The 'naked individual' and 'infinite life' diagnoses support the thesis's claim about 'fully actualised' neo-artificial conditions dissolving the conditions for world-formation.
- Note the thesis's universe/world/metaverse vocabulary differs from Uegaki's 宇宙/世界/〈社会的装置〉 vocabulary — the two frameworks need explicit articulation at the bridge passage.
