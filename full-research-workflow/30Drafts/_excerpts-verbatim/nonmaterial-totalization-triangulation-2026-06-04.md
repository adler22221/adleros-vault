## GROUP A — Corroborators (independent diagnoses of the same founding move)

> The founding move Bhaskar locates on the Platonic/Aristotelian fault-line — the substitution of an idealised, self-certifying construct for the open, stratified real, ruling out rival accounts — recurs under other names: Husserl's *mathematisation of nature*, Heidegger's *mathematical projection* / *world-as-picture*, Whitehead's *fallacy of misplaced concreteness*, the 17th-century *quest for certainty* (Dewey, Toulmin), and the Greek *demand for demonstration* (Lloyd).

**A1.3 — "world picture … does not mean a picture of the world but the world conceived and grasped as picture."** *QCT*, NLM pp.129–130 `[VERIFY-PAGE]`. **[VERBATIM-RAW]**

**A1.4 — The "mathematical" (*ta mathēmata*) = what is known in advance; the ground-plan/projection that decides what counts as a thing.** *QCT*, NLM pp.118–119 `[VERIFY-PAGE]`. **[VERBATIM-RAW]**
> "Ta mathemata means for the Greeks that which man knows in advance in his observation of whatever is and in his intercourse with things: the corporeality of bodies, the vegetable character of plants, the animality of animals, the humanness of man."
> — Heidegger, *QCT and Other Essays* · route: NLM

**A1.5 — The mathematical projection "stipulates in advance" what nature must be (the ground plan / Grundriss).** *QCT*, NLM p.119 `[VERIFY-PAGE]`. **[VERBATIM-RAW]** — this is the load-bearing "decides in advance" passage.
> "If physics takes shape explicitly, then, as something mathematical, this means that, in an especially pronounced way, through it and for it something is stipulated in advance as what is already-known. That stipulating has to do with nothing less than the plan or projection of that which must henceforth, for the knowing of nature that is sought after, be nature: the self-contained system of motion of units of mass related spatiotemporally. […] Every event must be seen so as to be fitted into this ground plan of nature. Only within the perspective of this ground plan does an event in nature become visible as such an event."
> — Heidegger, *QCT and Other Essays* · route: NLM

**A1.6 — The mathematical projection "skips over the things" (Glazebrook glossing *Modern Science, Metaphysics, and Mathematics*).** NLM p.58, quoting Heidegger MSMM 267–68 `[VERIFY-PAGE]`. **[VERBATIM-RAW]** — *secondary* (Glazebrook); use as gloss, attribute to Glazebrook quoting Heidegger.
> "First, it is 'a project of thingness which, as it were, skips over the things' (MSMM 267–68/FD 71). The mathematical projection of nature establishes the domain of physics as the realm of moving bodies. This determination of the thingness of things 'skips over' the things by approaching them on the basis of a prior understanding. There is no opportunity for things to speak for themselves, that is, to show themselves other than as bodies in motion."
> — Glazebrook, *Heidegger's Philosophy of Science* (quoting Heidegger, MSMM) · route: NLM

> **Corroboration value (A1):** Heidegger names the *same* founding gesture as Bhaskar's primal squeeze from the other side: a projection (Entwurf) that is *settled in advance* and into which every entity "must be fitted," so that things lose the "opportunity to speak for themselves." This is the nonmaterial, ontology-foreclosing move — a *Grundriss* that pre-decides the real. Strong.

### B1 — Adorno & Horkheimer: instrumental reason / identity-thinking / Enlightenment reverts to myth

Bhaskar's mechanism is *ontological*: the squeeze on natural necessity at the Platonic/Aristotelian fault-line, i.e., a defect in the *theory of being* that makes the foreclosure structurally necessary, not just a fateful tendency of the reasoning subject. For the thesis this is a precise *contrast*: Habermas explains the institutional-economic vector of foreclosure but treats reason as partly innocent; Bhaskar locates the defect *inside* the dominant theoretical reason itself (the epistemic fallacy / squeeze), so the foreclosure is not a regional encroachment but an ontological default of the whole post-Humean tradition.
