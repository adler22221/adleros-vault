# Findings & Strategic Implications: tekhne→phusis — v2 (REFRAMED)

**Compiled:** 2026-06-01 · **For:** [[master-thesis]] (Between the Universe and the Metaverse) · **Supersedes:** `nomos-FINDINGS-and-implications-2026-06-01.md` (v1). · **Applies:** [[tekhne-phusis-FRAMING-clarification-2026-06-01]]. · Verbatim substrate: [[nomos-phusis-tekhne-CONSOLIDATED-2026-06-01]] (v2).

> [!warning] What v2 changes, and why
> v1 read "tekhne is becoming phusis" as a (flagged but) **literal directional ontological claim** — the made *acquiring the internal* archē *of the grown*, with Aristotle II.8 as the "premise" and the metaverse as the condition under which its antecedent "becomes actual." The author's 2026-06-01 clarification **overrides** that reading: the claim is **NON-LITERAL — a critical/creative reconstruction and a provocation against the inherited triad** phusis / nomos / techne *as they have stood*, anchored in the thesis's **Chapter 1 rejection of the nature/culture divide** (where "artificiality" is the analytical unit that *replaces* culture). v2 keeps every strong structural finding but recasts each as serving the provocation, not a literal process. It also **corrects** the Floridi divergence (see §3) from "Floridi collapses the natural" to a *height-of-the-cut* distinction — both keep a realist floor.

> [!warning] The two syntheses remain the AUTHOR'S
> "**Nomos has always been tekhne**" and "**tekhne is becoming phusis**" are the author's own claims — *evidenced-by*, not *stated-by*, the sources. No source asserts either; Brague only *groups* tekhnē + nomos. Everything below is the strategic case for deploying the verbatim **as a reconstruction**, with attribution discipline intact.

## 0. The master reframe (read first — governs everything below)

The headline claim is **not** that the metaverse literally turns the built into the grown — that the made literally acquires φύσις's internal *archē*. That literal path the author "already knew would be in vain," because the thesis **already discarded the nature/culture divide in Chapter 1** (via Haila, Jones, Obara — "there is no pre-culture nature… the cultural is already what is natural for us"; **"artificiality — my analytical unit that replaces culture"**). The inherited **phusis / nomos / techne** map onto exactly the **nature / culture / technology** divides the thesis abandons. So "tekhne is becoming phusis" functions as a **provocation in the tradition's own vocabulary**: it wields the canon's terms to *expose that those terms were never stable*, and thereby to *motivate the thesis's constructive replacement* — **universe / world / Adaptational Operating System (AOS) / artificiality**.

The value of the dossier's two great empirical results — **ancient porosity** (the canon already runs the phusis/technē relation in every direction) and **etymology** (the made/given line is cross-linguistically deep yet internally fractured) — is therefore **not** to prove a directional ontological process. It is to **demonstrate that the categories were always unstable**, which is what *warrants the reconstruction*. The strong structural findings below are kept; their job is changed from *premises of a literal argument* to *warrants for a provocation*.

One-line statement (for the prose): *The metaverse does not literally turn the made into the given; it makes the inherited distinctions phusis/nomos/techne (nature/culture/technology) unworkable — which is why the thesis abandons them for universe / world / AOS / artificiality, keeping a thick, named realist universe where Floridi keeps only a bare noumenal floor (dedomena) and Latour dissolves the poles entirely.*

## 1. Finding — the categories were ALWAYS unstable; that instability warrants the reconstruction (not a literal crossing)

The decisive result of the exhaustive retrieval is that the phusis/technē boundary is **already porous in the ancient canon itself, and porous in both directions** — and that the lexica record the poles *recombining* repeatedly. v1 read this as "the thesis inherits a tradition that already half-draws the figure it wants to complete." v2 reads it as the **evidential backbone of the provocation**: if the tradition that *invented* the distinction could never keep it stable, the distinction was never the firm ground a literal phusis/technē ontology would need — so the right move is not to *complete* the figure but to *retire* it.

**Three ancient phusis→technē vectors** (the canon runs the relation this way three times):
1. **technē *completes* phusis.** Aristotle *Physics* II.8: "**art partly completes what nature cannot bring to a finish, and partly imitates her**" (199a15–18). Cassin's ART entry gives the Greek (ἐπιτελεῖ ἃ ἡ φύσις ἀδυνατεῖ ἀπεργάσασθαι) and reads it as technē being at once *dependent on* and *superior to* its model.
2. **technē *imitates* phusis.** *Physics* II.2 194a21; and von Staden's documentation that the ancients *also* ran it the other way — "physis as technē," nature characterised as *technikē* (Erasistratus, Galen).
3. **technē *conquers/overcomes* phusis.** Antiphon (via Hui): "**we conquer by techne things that defeat us by physis**"; Hadot's "Promethean" forcing of nature. (Cassin's ART entry independently flags technē's "Promethean excess.")

**One ancient technē→phusis inversion** — Plato *Laws* X 892b: "**the great and primitive works and actions will be works of art; they will be the first, and after them will come nature … under the government of art and mind**." Plato *reverses the priority* (made grounds given) and re-describes the made-principle (soul/nous) *as* phusis "in the truest sense."

**And the lexica record the poles RECOMBINING.** New in v2: HWPh's Physis/Nomos article (Deitz, Bd. 7) documents that after the Sophists sharpened the antithesis, **Aristotle deflates it** (admitting ἄγραφοι νόμοι / νόμοι τῆς φύσεως), the **Stoa fuses** the poles (Chrysippus: the Logos as ὅς ἐστι φύσει νόμος, "a law that is by nature"), and **Paul** makes the Gentiles "a law to themselves" (νόμος φύσεως, "law of nature"). The very terms the Sophists drove apart, the tradition repeatedly welds back together.

> [!important] How v2 uses this (vs v1)
> v1: porosity shows the tradition "already half-draws the figure," so the author only has to "complete" it. **v2: porosity (in BOTH directions) + the lexica's repeated re-welding of the poles shows the categories were never stable — so the reconstruction that retires them is warranted, not eccentric.** The porosity is a warrant for *abandoning* the triad, not a runway for a literal crossing.

## 2. Finding — the single un-crossed barrier (internal *archē*) is the tradition's own marker of where the distinction breaks down — used as the EDGE of the provocation, not a threshold literally crossed

Every one of the four ancient vectors leaves intact the one thing the canon never lets technē have: an **internal principle of motion** (*archē*). The wall is stated identically across canon and lexica:
- Aristotle *Physics* II.1: natural things have "within itself a principle of motion"; products of art "have no innate impulse to change."
- Heidegger: phusis = "the arising of something from out of itself" (*en heautōi*); the artifact's origin is "outside the thing made" (*en allōi*).
- Iwanami (Japanese, independent): Aristotle's physis = 自分自身のうちに運動の原理をもつもの ("that which has the principle of motion within itself").

**The keystone — Aristotle's own counterfactual, *Physics* II.8 (199b26–31):**
> "Art does not deliberate. **If the ship-building art were in the wood, it would produce the same results by nature.**"

v1 treated this as the thesis's *load-bearing premise*: the internal *archē* is the *sole* barrier, and the metaverse is "the ship-building art getting into the wood" — i.e. the antecedent literally *becomes actual*. **v2 corrects the modality.** The II.8 counterfactual is valuable not because the metaverse literally puts the art "in the wood," but because it is **the tradition's own statement of the exact point at which the made/given distinction would dissolve** — Aristotle himself locating the hinge on which the whole edifice turns. The thesis cites it as *the tradition naming its own breaking-point*, then makes the provocation: the metaverse is the condition under which *talking as if* the art were in the wood becomes unavoidable — at which point the distinction stops doing work and must be replaced (by universe / world / AOS / artificiality), not "crossed." The dialectical gain is the same robustness v1 prized — Aristotle is deploying the conditional to argue the *reverse* (that nature, like art, acts for an end), so the author claims his authority only for *the statement of the hinge*, never for the conclusion — but the conclusion is now **"the distinction is unworkable," not "the barrier is literally crossed."**

> [!note] Why this matters for over-reading objections (米/Mi, 劉/Liu)
> A literal "the made now has an internal *archē*" invites the rebuttal "no, the metaverse is still *en allōi* — its principle is in its designers/operators." The reframe sidesteps this entirely: the thesis is **not** asserting the barrier is literally crossed. It is showing that the barrier, as the tradition's *own* sole marker of the distinction, has become **impossible to police** under metaverse conditions — which is a claim about the *categories' workability*, not about the metaverse's metaphysics. This is both safer and truer to Ch1.

## 3. Finding — Floridi is ALLY-ON-MECHANISM, RIVAL-ON-LEVEL; the divergence is the HEIGHT OF THE REALIST/CONSTRUCTED CUT (NOT "Floridi collapses the natural") — corrected in v2

v1's central error: it read Floridi's "the natural is in itself artefactual" as *the named inverse arrow* — Floridi "collapses phusis into technē at the level of knowledge," runs the arrow "natural ← artefactual," and the author "completes/redirects" it. **The Floridi argument-reconstruction shows this misreads him.** Floridi keeps an explicit **realist floor** and says so:

- **The floor is real and never constructed:** *dedomena* = "mind-independent points of lack of uniformity in the fabric of Being" (RHPI/Bynum), "data before they are interpreted … not experienced directly, but their presence is empirically inferred from, and required by, experience" (LoI). "I never seriously question for a moment the recalcitrant and often grating presence of outside realities" (LoI). "We … live in and interact with the world in itself, Kant's noumena. We eat and drink, handle and build, avoid and crash into noumena" (LoI).
- **The "artefactual natural" is scoped to the *concept*, not the substrate:** "either the natural is only a very minimalist … way to refer to data in themselves (noumenal) … and this is uninteresting; **or the natural — as we conceive and perceive it … — is a construction, a model of the noumenal**." And explicitly: "**There is nothing anti-realist or relativistic in such an acknowledgement.**"

So Floridi does **not** collapse the natural; he relocates the realism *low* — to a bare, inaccessible, sub-perceptual noumenal floor (*dedomena*) — and lets *everything humanly meaningful above it*, including the rich concept "nature," be infosphere/construction. **The thesis cuts the same line HIGHER**: it keeps a **thick, named, ontologically dignified realist layer — the *universe* ("neither a god nor a man made", unaffectable)** — and reserves construction for the **world** specifically.

> [!important] The corrected divergence (the v2 headline on Floridi)
> **Not** "Floridi collapses the natural, the thesis keeps it." **But:** *both keep a realist floor; the disagreement is the **height of the cut**.* Floridi cuts **low** (only the bare noumenal *dedomena* is real; the rest is infosphere). The thesis cuts **high** (a thick, named *universe* is real; only the *world* is construction). This makes Floridi **closer** to the thesis than the slogans suggest — and the disagreement **sharper and more precise**: it is about *where* the realist/constructed line falls and *how much* of the real gets a positive ontological name, not about realism-vs-anti-realism.

**Ally on mechanism.** Floridi's **re-ontologization** — a technology that "fundamentally transforms the intrinsic nature of the system itself — that is, its ontology," autonomously, with the human "out of the loop" (third-order technology) — is a **near-exact formal analogue of the thesis's squeeze**: a new layer reshapes the lived world rather than merely adding tools to it. This is the strongest contemporary corroboration the thesis has *for its mechanism* — and, read through the reframe, it doubles as evidence that the *inherited natural/artefactual distinction is already unworkable* (onlife: "the distinction between online and offline will … blur and then disappear"; "blur the distinction between humans and artefacts"), which is precisely the provocation's point.

**Two disciplines to keep (both from the reconstruction):**
- **Floridi disowns Latour.** When Gabriel flagged Latour's "astonishing absence," Floridi: "It is not an oversight. … **I meant to avoid it.** … It is precisely because I stay away from such »post-modernist« and »post-humanist« ways of thinking …" So the Floridi+Latour pairing is the **author's independent-convergence triangulation**, *stronger* for being reached by mutually disavowed routes — never present Floridi as Latourian.
- **Descriptive vs normative split.** Floridi's "marriage of physis and techne" is part *descriptive* (the blurring is happening) and part *normative project* ("It is our responsibility to design and manage both"). **Cite only the descriptive side as an ontological claim**; never the normative marriage as a description of what reality now is.

## 4. Finding — Simondon is the closest ALLY on the directional move, recast as the precedent for a legitimate *reconstruction* (not a literal mechanism)

Simondon's **concretization** is the closest thing in the literature to "the made acquiring the character of the given": the evolved technical object "comes closer to the mode of existence of natural objects … a closure of the system of causes and effects that … within its bounds"; "**As it evolves, this object loses its artificial character**"; manuscript variant — "**the object frees itself and becomes naturalized**"; it becomes "its own condition" (self-conditioning).

v1 used Simondon to make the *literal mechanism* respectable ("a serious philosopher already argued the made acquires φύσις's internal *archē*"; "the author's thesis = Simondon scaled from object to world-layer"). **v2 keeps Simondon as the closest ally but recasts his role to fit the reframe:** Simondon is the precedent that makes it **philosophically legitimate to deliberately blur the made/given line in serious ontology** — i.e. the precedent for the *reconstruction-move itself*, not for a literal claim that the metaverse grows an *archē*. He shows a major philosopher of technology already found the inherited natural/artificial categories inadequate to technical reality and *re-described* the made in terms the tradition reserved for the given. The thesis's value-add is not "scaling a literal mechanism" but **generalizing that inadequacy to a world-layer and drawing the constructive conclusion Simondon did not** — abandoning the triad for universe / world / AOS / artificiality. (Keep Lee's criterion — natural = "not the material embodiment of deliberate human intention" + an independent "trajectory" — as the *test the categories impose*, the better to show why they buckle, rather than as a falsification target the metaverse must literally pass.)

## 5. Finding — the etymological crossing *tek-"build" → *bʰeh₂u-"grow" is the most compact statement of the provocation's force (not of a literal category-jump)

The etymology, now triply-backed by the new lexica, states the stakes in one stroke:
- **τέχνη ← \*tek- "build" / \*te-tk- "build, timber"** (Beekes), cf. **τέκτων "the carpenter"**; Cassin: technē root **\*teks- "construct, make,"** ars = technē; HWPh: **\*tek- "bauen, zimmern."**
- **φύσις ← \*bʰeh₂u- "grow, arise, be"** (Beekes); Cassin/Aristotle (*Metaph.* Δ.4): "the *genesis* of growing things"; HWPh: the *Werden/Wachstum* sense.

These roots are **maximally opposed** — *to build* vs *to grow/to be*. v1: this shows "the built is acquiring the character of the grown … a category-crossing at the deepest lexical stratum." **v2: this shows how radical the *provocation* is** — to *say* "tekhne is becoming phusis" is to deliberately collide the two most opposed roots the language has, precisely in order to make audible that the inherited build/grow opposition can no longer organize our world. The HWPh "Technik" entry sharpens this: it records the arts (τέχναι, artes) as, since antiquity, "**the expression of culture over against mere nature**" — i.e. the technē/physis axis *is* the culture/nature axis the thesis discarded in Ch1. The etymology thus does double duty: it proves the made/given line is **deep** (cross-linguistic) *and* shows the provocation is aimed at exactly the nature/culture/technology triad Ch1 retired.

## 6. Finding — the author's distinctive signature (triple-distinct), recast: what is original is the RECONSTRUCTION, not a novel literal direction

Against every interlocutor, the thesis is distinguished on three axes at once — and no other author combines all three. v2 keeps the signature but states what it is a signature *of*: a **constructive reconstruction**, not a novel literal ontological arrow.
1. **Asymmetric provocation, not a symmetric reconciliation.** Not Floridi's "marriage," not Latour's "dissolution" — a one-directional *pressure* (the squeeze) that the provocation makes legible.
2. **Emerging, not accomplished.** The metaverse is "yet-to-be-realized" / not "fully actualized"; the squeeze is a forthcoming world-layer condition, not (pace McKibben, Vogel, Floridi-*Logic*) an accomplished collapse. (Absorb Bensaude-Vincent's caution: the polarity *evolves*, it does not simply terminate.)
3. **Metaverse/technosphere-specific.** Driven by a *specific* emerging technology — not biotech (Lee), climate (McKibben), information-in-general (Floridi), or ecology (Latour).
**Plus** the constructive differentiator the reframe foregrounds: the thesis does not only *deconstruct* the binary (as Floridi/Latour do); it **erects a replacement architecture** (universe / world / AOS / artificiality / neo-artificiality) in which the squeeze can be *located*. Floridi gives a mechanism and an ethics; Latour dissolves the poles; **the thesis gives a map.** That map — not a literal tekhne→phusis process — is the original contribution.

Distinguish, finally, from **Adorno's "second nature"**: Adorno = the made *appears* given (ideological *semblance*, "imprisonment"). The thesis is not even making the *operative*-vs-*semblant* claim v1 leaned on; under the reframe it is making a **categorial** claim — the made/given vocabulary itself fails, so neither "looks given" nor "operates as given" is the load-bearing point; the point is that the distinction no longer carves anything.

## 7. How to argue tekhne→phusis in Ch V/VI — recipe, updated for the reframe (a FLAGGED PROVOCATION that motivates the apparatus)

The claim is reserved for Chapters V/VI and must remain a **flagged provocation** — argued *for*, positioned, and engaged with rivals, but **never** presented as a literal ontological process, a proven result, or a claim *stated-by* any source. The recipe:

1. **Open by cross-referencing Ch1.** State at the outset that the thesis has *already* rejected the nature/culture divide (Haila/Jones/Obara; "artificiality replaces culture"), so the inherited **phusis/nomos/techne** (= nature/culture/technology) triad is *already* under suspicion. The chapter does not *discover* the made becoming given; it *stages a provocation in the tradition's terms* to show why that triad must be retired.

2. **Deploy the warrants AS warrants-for-the-reconstruction, not premises of a literal proof.**
   - *Etymology (§5):* the maximally-opposed roots (build vs grow), triply-backed (Beekes/Cassin/HWPh), state how radical it is to even *say* the phrase — the rhetorical force that makes the categories' breakdown audible.
   - *Ancient porosity + the lexica's re-welding (§1):* the canon runs the relation in every direction and the lexica repeatedly fuse the poles (Aristotle's deflation; Chrysippus' φύσει νόμος; Paul's νόμος φύσεως) — proof the categories were never stable.
   - *Aristotle II.8 counterfactual (§2):* the tradition's *own* statement of the hinge on which the distinction turns — cited as "the tradition naming its breaking-point," not as a threshold the metaverse literally satisfies.
   - *Simondon (§4):* the precedent legitimating a serious re-description of the made in the given's terms — i.e. precedent for the reconstruction-move, not for a literal *archē*-acquisition.

3. **Engage Floridi as ally-on-mechanism / rival-on-level (§3).** Lay re-ontologization + third-order autonomy alongside the squeeze as the contemporary *mechanism* corroboration (and as evidence the natural/artefactual distinction is already unworkable). Then mark the divergence *precisely*: **height of the cut** — Floridi cuts low at the bare noumenal *dedomena*; the thesis cuts high with a thick, named *universe*. Do **not** say "Floridi collapses the natural." Keep the descriptive/normative split (cite only the descriptive blurring as ontological).

4. **Triangulate Floridi + Latour as independent convergence (§3).** Present them as kindred rejections of the naïve categories reached by *mutually disavowed* routes (Floridi explicitly avoids Latour) — the convergence is the author's, and stronger for being independent. The thesis reaches the same rejection via its **ecological/Obara** grounding, then *diverges* from both by **building** (universe/world/AOS/artificiality) where they only dissolve.

5. **Land on the constructive payoff.** The provocation's *purpose* is to motivate the apparatus: once the inherited triad is shown unworkable, the chapter introduces universe / world / AOS / artificiality as the replacement in which the **squeeze** is locatable (re-ontologization ≈ the squeeze; the world displaced/transformed under a new neo-artificial layer; the realist *universe* preserved as the thick floor).

6. **Footnote where the argument is thin (scope discipline — 劉/Liu; logical precision — 米/Mi; evidence — 馬/Ma).** Be explicit in a footnote that: (a) the claim is the author's *reconstruction/provocation*, asserted by no source (attribution guard; Brague only *groups*); (b) it is **non-literal** — the made does **not** literally acquire an internal *archē* (anchor to Ch1); (c) Floridi keeps a realist *dedomena* floor and his directional/genealogical and "marriage" registers are his own (the divergence is *level*, not realism); (d) Plato *Laws* X inverts priority but *demotes* nature and uses divine *nous*, not human technē; (e) the squeeze is *emerging*, not accomplished (Bensaude-Vincent). **Flagging the insufficiency IS the scope-discipline answer to Liu and the logical-derivation answer to Mi.**

**Companion claim — "nomos has always been tekhne" (Ch II).** Same reframe, lighter touch: keep it the author's *grouping-to-reading* move — cite Brague (pp. 14, 47) + LSJ/Preus/Antiphon/Iwanami (人為的につくられた) + the new lexica (Cassin: nomos = "division," law "established through procedure, does not preexist it"; HWPh: nomos = *Nahme*/"division of pasture," the *instituted*) for the *placement* of nomos on the made/instituted side; attribute the *identity* (convention simply *is* a species of the humanly-instituted-made) to the author. The new lexica **strengthen the placement** while leaving the identity the author's. Keep the Brague guard explicit — reviewers will check whether Brague is over-read.
