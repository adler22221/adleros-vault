# Ch V — Lotka + Uexküll T0 verbatim: the "exosomatic organs" framing

Links: [[chV-sim_v2]] · [[master-thesis]]

> **Purpose.** Verbatim grounding for Chapter V's claim that **AI, big-data, and the IoT are *exosomatic organs*** — the human's perceptual and effective organs exteriorized out of the body into the technical apparatus / artificiality. Two primary anchors:
> 1. **Lotka** supplies the *exosomatic / endosomatic* distinction (organs grown *within* the body vs. instruments made/used *outside* it).
> 2. **Uexküll** supplies the organism-model being exteriorized: the **receptor organ** (Merkorgan) + **effector organ** (Wirkorgan) joined in the **functional cycle** (Funktionskreis) that builds the Umwelt.
> The two combine to license Ch V's mapping: **IoT sensors = receptor organs of the apparatus; IoT actuators = effector organs; AI/big-data = the correlating apparatus between them — the whole loop now grown *outside* the human body.**

## 1. LOTKA — endosomatic vs. exosomatic

**Primary-confirmed.** Zotero `lotkaLAWEVOLUTIONMAXIMAL1945` (item key **GRKYNJKL**), PDF at `…\Zotero\storage\ECQZUFCV\LOTKA - 1945 - THE LAW OF EVOLUTION AS A MAXIMAL PRINCIPLE.pdf`.
Source: A. J. Lotka, "The Law of Evolution as a Maximal Principle," *Human Biology* 17, no. 3 (1945): 167–194.

### 1.1 The locus classicus — the coinage of "exosomatic" (p.188)

This is the sentence the entire Stiegler/Hui "exosomatization" lineage descends from. It defines the *exosomatic* by contrast with the body's *native* (i.e., endosomatic) apparatus:

[VERBATIM: Lotka 1945, *Human Biology* | p.188 | "The one outstanding exception is the human species. Here evolution, especially in more recent times, has followed an entirely new path. In place of slow adaptation of anatomical structure and physiological function in successive generations by selective survival, increased adaptation has been achieved by the incomparably more rapid development of 'artificial' aids to our native receptor-effector apparatus, in a process that might be termed exosomatic evolution."]

Continuation on the same page (the exosomatic apparatus as *amplifier* of the native organs):

[VERBATIM: Lotka 1945, *Human Biology* | p.188 | "Man, one of the latest, and in his own judgment the highest product of evolution, has hitherto signally conformed with the principle of increasing energy flux. By ingenious contrivances he has immensely refined and multiplied the operation of his receptor-effector apparatus."]

And later in the paper, naming the field of development "exosomatic" again:

[VERBATIM: Lotka 1945, *Human Biology* | p.191 | "…the exosomatic evolution of the human species is indisputably subject to orthogenesis. Knowledge breeds knowledge…"]
> [VERIFY-PAGE] — printed p.191; the phrase sits a few lines after the running head "THE LAW OF EVOLUTION 191" in the PDF text layer (PDF index 25/26). Page is near-certain but flagged because the OCR footnote numbering on adjacent pages is corrupt.

### 1.2 KEY PROVENANCE FINDING — "exosomatic" is a 1945 coinage, NOT in *Elements of Physical Biology* (1925)

The brief asked for the 1925 *Elements* verbatim. **It is not the source of the terminology.** I downloaded the full public-domain text of *Elements of Physical Biology* (Williams & Wilkins, 1925) from archive.org (`elementsofphysic017171mbp`, full djvu, 972 KB) and searched it exhaustively: **the strings "exosomatic" and "endosomatic" do not occur anywhere in the 1925 book.** What *Elements* contains is the conceptual seed — Lotka's "correlating apparatus" of **receptors / adjustors / effectors** and the "energy transformers" of the organism (the material later re-described, in 1945, with the endo/exo vocabulary). The 1925 reference that appears *inside the 1945 paper's footnote* (p.188) is to energy-flux / "world engine" arguments at *Elements* pp.357–358, **not** to an endo/exo definition:

[VERBATIM: Lotka 1945 footnote, citing *Elements* | p.188 | "(A. J. Lotka, Elements of Physical Biology, 1925, pp. 357–358)."]

**Consequence for citation:** Ch V should cite **Lotka 1945, p.188** (`lotkaLAWEVOLUTIONMAXIMAL1945`) as the source of *exosomatic*. The secondary literature that pairs "exosomatic" with "endosomatic" and attributes the *pair* to *Elements* (1925) is repeating a common mis-attribution; Lotka himself introduces the *exosomatic* coinage in 1945 against the *native* (endosomatic) apparatus. If a 1925 anchor is wanted, it can only support the underlying **receptor-effector "correlating apparatus"** idea, not the term.
> Do NOT generate an endosomatic/exosomatic *contrastive-definition* quotation — none exists verbatim in either Lotka text. The contrast is carried by the word "native … apparatus" (= endosomatic) vs. "'artificial' aids … exosomatic" in the p.188 passage above.

## 2. UEXKÜLL — receptor organ (Merkorgan) + effector organ (Wirkorgan) + the functional cycle (Funktionskreis)

**Primary-confirmed.** **Same edition Ch I/II already cite** → Zotero `uexkullForayWorlds2010` (item key **92M346FN**; PDF attachment **V3PMNURW**; itemID 24354). Re-acquisition unnecessary — edition matched per cascade.
Source: Jakob von Uexküll, *A Foray into the Worlds of Animals and Humans, with A Theory of Meaning*, trans. Joseph D. O'Neil (Minneapolis: University of Minnesota Press, 2010 [orig. *Streifzüge durch die Umwelten von Tieren und Menschen*, 1934]). PDF at `…\Zotero\storage\V3PMNURW\von Uexkull - 2010 - A Foray into the Worlds of Animals and Humans With A Theory of Meaning.pdf`.

> **Terminology bridge (translator's note, p.36).** Uexküll's prefixes **Merk-** (perception/receptor) and **Wirk-** (effect/action) are rendered by Schiller as "**receptors**" and "**effectors**"; Uexküll also uses **Rezeptor / Effektor** directly, which O'Neil renders "receptor"/"effector." So **Merkorgan = perception/receptor organ** and **Wirkorgan = effect/effector organ** in this translation.
[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.36 | "The terms of this cycle are differentiated by their prefix, Merk- or Wirk-, and connected by their stem: Mal or Zeichen… as in Schiller's translation in terms of 'effectors' and 'receptors'… Moreover, Uexküll also uses the terms Rezeptor and Effektor… which I translate as 'receptor' and 'effector.'"]

### 2.1 Receptor organ + effector organ defined (p.46)

Uexküll has the physiologist state the organism-model that the rest of the book re-animates — the two organ-types joined by a central control apparatus:

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.46 | "…one can differentiate receptors, i.e., sensory organs, and effectors, i.e., activity organs, in the tick. These are connected with one another through a control apparatus in the central nervous system."]

The **receptor** defined as a *selective* organ (admits only certain stimuli — the germ of the Umwelt's selectivity):

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.46 | "It begins with a receptor, i.e., with an apparatus that admits only certain external influences, such as butyric acid and heat, and disregards all others."]

The **effector** defined as the motor/action organ:

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.46 | "The arc ends with a muscle which sets an effector into motion, whether this is the apparatus for locomotion or for boring."]

### 2.2 Perception organ + effect organ (the organs *named*, p.47)

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.47 | "The organism uses brain cells… grouping half of them… as 'perception cells' in the part of the brain that is affected by stimuli, the 'perception organ.'… The organism uses the other half of the brain cells as 'effect cells' or impulse cells and arranges them in groups by means of which it controls the movements of the effectors… The groups of perception cells fill up the 'perception organs' of the brain, and the groups of effect cells form the 'effect organs' of the brain."]

### 2.3 The pincer of perception-mark + effect-mark (p.48–49)

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.48 | "Figuratively speaking, every animal subject attacks its objects in a pincer movement—with one perceptive and one effective arm. With the first, it imparts each object a perception mark [Merkmal] and with the second an effect mark [Wirkmal]."]

### 2.4 The functional cycle (Funktionskreis) itself (p.49)

The closing of the loop — subject and object bound into "an orderly whole." This is the **Funktionskreis** (Figure 3):

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.49 | "The connection of subject to object can be most clearly explained by the schema of the functional cycle (Figure 3). The schema shows how subject and object are interconnected with each other and form an orderly whole."]

Figure 3's own labels (verbatim, OCR — confirm against the printed diagram) name the two organ-poles of the cycle:
[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010), Figure 3 "Functional cycle" labels | p.49 | "Perception organ / Counterstructure / Effect organ / Receptor / Perception-mark carrier / Counterstructure / Effect-mark carrier"]
> [VERIFY-PAGE] — the Figure-3 caption + labels straddle printed pp.48–49 in the PDF text layer; the schema is universally cited as the §"functional cycle" diagram of the Introduction. Body sentence at p.49 is solid.

The cycle's upshot — every organism is "inserted into" its Umwelt to the same degree of perfection (the bridge back to Ch I's Umwelt usage):

[VERBATIM: Uexküll, *A Foray* (2010) | p.50 | "All animal subjects, from the simplest to the most complex, are inserted into their environments to the same degree of perfection. The simple animal has a simple environment; the multiform animal has an environment just as richly articulated as it is."]

> **Edition-consistency note.** Ch I already cites this exact translation for the **Umwelt / Umgebung** distinction (P0090, P0094, P0097, P0103: "approximate to what Uexküll calls Umgebung…"; "modified by author from Uexküll, 1934/2010"). The functional-cycle material above is from the **Introduction** of the *same* volume — no edition conflict. Ch II §1 (`chII-sim_v4`) likewise tags `uexkullForayWorlds2010` (92M346FN). Use `uexkullForayWorlds2010` throughout.

## 3. How this grounds Ch V's "exosomatic organs" mapping

The two sources interlock cleanly; the mapping Ch V wants is a *literal exteriorization* of Uexküll's functional cycle, licensed by Lotka's "exosomatic":

| Uexküll's *endosomatic* functional cycle (grown within the body) | Lotka: exteriorized *exosomatically* (made/used outside the body) | Ch V's apparatus term |
|---|---|---|
| **Receptor organ / Merkorgan** — "admits only certain external influences… disregards all others" (p.46) | "'artificial' aids to our native receptor-effector apparatus" (Lotka p.188) | **IoT sensors** = the apparatus's receptor organs |
| **Effector organ / Wirkorgan** — "a muscle which sets an effector into motion… locomotion or boring" (p.46) | same exosomatic exteriorization (Lotka p.188) | **IoT actuators / robotics** = the apparatus's effector organs |
| **Control apparatus in the CNS** binding receptor↔effector (p.46); the **functional cycle** that "form[s] an orderly whole" (p.49) | the "correlating apparatus" now built *outside* the body | **AI + big-data** = the exosomatic correlating apparatus / nervous system of the cycle |
| The functional cycle **builds the Umwelt** (p.50) | exosomatic evolution builds a *new* layer over the given | The IoT-cycle builds the **metaverse layer** over the world (squeeze hypothesis) |

**The argument Ch V can now make, on primary warrant:**
1. **Lotka (p.188):** human adaptation proceeds *exosomatically* — by "artificial aids to our native receptor-effector apparatus." The technical apparatus is, definitionally, the human's organs grown *outside* the body. (This is the endo→exo move: the *native* apparatus = endosomatic; its artificial extensions = exosomatic.)
2. **Uexküll (pp.46–49):** what is being exteriorized has a determinate form — **receptor organ + effector organ joined by a control apparatus into a functional cycle** that constitutes a subject's Umwelt.
3. **Therefore (thesis):** the **IoT** is the receptor *and* effector organs of a planetary apparatus; **AI/big-data** is its control/correlating apparatus; together they close an **exosomatic functional cycle** that constitutes the **metaverse** as a new Umwelt-layer — the human's perceptual-effective organology fully actualized outside the body. This is precisely the "neo-artificiality" that squeezes the world (cf. `[[chV-sim_v2]]`, squeeze hypothesis).
4. **Lineage to cite:** Lotka 1945 → Stiegler/Montévil *exosomatization* (`stieglerNeganthropocene2018`, `MontévilAnthropoceneExosomatization2020`) → thesis. The thesis's contribution is to give exosomatization a *Uexküllian organ-structure* (receptor/effector/cycle), which neither Lotka nor Stiegler spells out.
