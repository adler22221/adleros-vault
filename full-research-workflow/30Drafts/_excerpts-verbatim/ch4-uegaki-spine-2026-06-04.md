### (1b) [VERBATIM] The two presuppositions — (i) 時空間的自立性, (ii) 約束された本来性 (Rousseau "born free / everywhere in chains") — 下 print pp.110–112 (PDF p.118–120)

Reconstructed: 着想 (若想), 言葉 (盲葉). **EN gloss:** "Yet this modern concept of freedom in fact contained, from its very beginning, an important idea leading toward 〈infinite life〉. ## (4) ★ THE EMANCIPATORY-THEORY CRITIQUE ★

> **FOUND.** Uegaki does turn his diagnosis onto the emancipatory tradition itself. He does **not** use the exact words「フェミニズム」or「批判理論（フランクフルト学派）」; the load-bearing terms he names are **「権力論」(power-theory, i.e. Foucault)、「ジェンダー論」(gender-theory — the feminist lineage)、「ポストコロニアル論」(postcolonial-theory)、「ポストモダン論」**, and the **マルクス／ヘーゲル emancipation-narrative**. His thesis: because these "抑圧からの解放 (liberation-from-oppression)" projects demand the *limitless* removal of every「鉄鎖 (chain)」, they are themselves an expression of 〈無限の生〉=「意のままになる生」 and therefore **fuel, rather than counter, the pursuit of life-as-willed** — culminating in the「無間地獄 (endless hell)」 and a "先祖返り (atavistic reversion)" back into the very 自立した個人 they set out to criticise.
>
> The single most on-point statements are **(4d) Note (90), 下 print p.80** ("these frameworks themselves embody the 〈無限の生〉 world-view") and **(4e) 補論二, 下 print p.207** ("power-theory, because it desires liberation from all oppression, necessarily reaches a dead end … and reverts to the autonomous individual"). These are the passages to cite.
