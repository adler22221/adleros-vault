# IV-1_v2: Causal Efficacy Precedes Completion — Real-Level Mechanism Applied

## Annotation Addressed

A1 (high priority) demanded that the causal-efficacy argument articulated in response to Mi's "fully actualized?" question be re-grounded in the critical-realist stratified ontology rather than left as an informal Bhaskarian gesture. The original Ch-4-1 draft invoked the real/actual distinction analogically — a fault-line exerts pressure before the earthquake — but did not deploy the three-domain schema (real / actual / empirical) as a *systematic* descriptor of how the squeeze operates. A1 also flagged that the geo-cultural variability of the squeeze's empirical signatures (psychosomatic symptoms, livelihood singularization, algorithmic governance) needs to be located *at* the actual/empirical level so as to defend the real-level mechanism against the "you are universalising from a parochial case" objection. This v2 makes those moves explicit, names the squeeze a *transfactual* mechanism in Bhaskar's sense [VERIFY: bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008], and uses Archer's analytical-dualist defence of the Cultural System as a real stratum [VERIFY: archerCultureAgencyPlace1996] to secure the ontological standing of the *world* — a precondition for the squeeze argument to be coherent at all, since one cannot squeeze that which has no causal weight.

## 1. The Stratified Schema Applied to the Squeeze

Critical realism, following Bhaskar, distinguishes three domains of reality nested by strict inclusion: the *real* (`dr`) — generative mechanisms, structures, powers, and tendencies that exist whether or not they are activated and whether or not they are observed; the *actual* (`da`) — events and states of affairs that occur when mechanisms are triggered, regardless of observation; and the *empirical* (`de`) — the subset of those events that are perceived, recorded, or experienced by some subject [VERIFY: bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008 | p.13]. The relation among them is `de ⊆ da ⊆ dr`. Bhaskar's foundational critique of empiricism is that it commits the *epistemic fallacy* of collapsing `dr` into `de`, treating reality as exhausted by what can be observed.

The squeeze hypothesis advanced in this thesis is, properly stated, a claim at the level of `dr`. It asserts the existence of a generative mechanism — the progressive constriction of the human mode of existence between two real strata, the universe (the realist substrate of physical and biological mechanisms) and the metaverse (a neo-artificial stratum of computational, infrastructural, and algorithmic mechanisms) — under whose joint operation the *world*, in the thesis's specific sense of a human-constructed meaning-space nested within the universe, is displaced or restructured. The squeeze is a *tendency* (in Bhaskar's technical sense): a structured causal disposition that operates whether or not its effects, in any given conjuncture, are visibly registered.

This recasting is not cosmetic. It changes what kind of evidence the squeeze claim requires and what kind of objection can defeat it. A `dr`-level claim is not falsified by the absence of uniform `de`-level signatures across cases, because mechanisms in open systems are co-determined by countervailing mechanisms whose interaction yields highly variable empirical patterns. The claim is also not confirmed by any single empirical demonstration; what it requires is *retroductive* warrant — the kind of inference to a generative cause that best explains a heterogeneous body of phenomena. The remainder of this section follows that warrant.

## 2. Transfactuality: Why a Mechanism Need Not Be Empirically Complete to Be Causally Operative

Bhaskar's term for the mode of existence proper to real-level mechanisms is *transfactual*: mechanisms persist and operate as tendencies *across* the variation of actual conditions, including conditions in which their characteristic effects are countervailed, suppressed, or unobserved [VERIFY: bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008 | p.13]. A geological fault-line is a serviceable analogy precisely because the fault is causally operative — accumulating strain, reorganising the surrounding lithology, conditioning the probability and form of any future seismic event — long before any earthquake is registered at the empirical level. The "earthquake" is the actualisation; the *fault* is the mechanism; the *seismograph trace* is the empirical experience. Each is real in its own register, but only the first two have causal standing, and only the first persists across all states of the system.

The metaverse-universe squeeze should be understood transfactually. To insist, against Mi's challenge, that the squeeze's "causal efficacy precedes completion" is therefore not an exception to ordinary causation but an instance of how `dr`-level mechanisms always relate to `da`-level events. The mechanism does not wait for full empirical actualisation to begin doing causal work; it does causal work as soon as the structural conditions for its operation are in place. Those conditions — a human organism shaped by long evolutionary adaptation to the universe-stratum, and a rapidly thickening metaverse-stratum that increasingly mediates that organism's access to livelihood, sociality, and meaning — already obtain. What we observe at the empirical level (depressive psychosomatics in heavy-platform cohorts, the increasing prerequisite-status of digital infrastructure for economic participation, the de-facto governance of public discourse by content-moderation algorithms) are the *signatures* of a mechanism already in operation, not the mechanism itself.

This dissolves the threshold objection. Mi's question — "is the metaverse fully actualised, self-complete?" — implicitly treats the squeeze as a binary state-property of the system that obtains only at terminal completion. The transfactual reading reframes the squeeze as a mechanism whose causal powers do not depend on completion at all; what depends on the degree of completion is the *intensity, scope, and concrete form* of the actualisations the mechanism produces. "Not yet fully severe" is not the same as "not yet real."

## 3. Geo-Cultural Variation Belongs to the Actual and Empirical Layers

The most pointed objection that A1 anticipates is: if the squeeze were a real mechanism, why is its empirical signature so uneven across geographies and cultures? The Japanese hikikomori configuration is not the American gig-precarity configuration is not the South Korean PC-bang ecology is not the urban-Chinese Social-Credit assemblage. Doesn't this heterogeneity show that what is being described is a *situated* set of phenomena, not a transcultural mechanism?

The stratified schema answers this objection directly. The mechanism (`dr`) is the same: a real-level pressure exerted on the world-stratum by the joint operation of universe and metaverse strata. The events (`da`) are the locally specific actualisations of that mechanism under particular conjunctural conditions — historical timing of platform diffusion, regulatory regime, prior cultural configuration of the world-stratum, demographic structure, infrastructural inheritance. The experiences (`de`) are the still narrower subset of those events that get registered and reported as such — and `de` is heavily filtered by what each cultural context counts as a *symptom* worth naming.

This is the standard critical-realist account of why open-system social phenomena exhibit empirical variation without thereby failing to instantiate underlying mechanisms; Andrew Sayer makes the same point in operational terms when he distinguishes *necessary* relations among objects from *contingent* relations among the conjunctures in which those objects are activated [VERIFY: sayerMethodSocialScience2010]. The hikikomori pattern and the gig-precarity pattern and the Social-Credit pattern are not three different mechanisms; they are three contingent conjunctural actualisations of the same necessary relation between an organism adapted to one environmental stratum and a second environmental stratum that is rapidly absorbing the conditions of its livelihood.

To put the point in the form A1 demands: the *exact form* (empirical level) of psychosomatic symptoms, livelihood singularisation, and algorithmic governance differs depending on geo-temporal-cultural context (actual level), but the underlying causal power (real level) persists whether activated or not, and regardless of which particular form its activation takes. This forestalls the universalising-from-parochia objection without weakening the thesis. The thesis does not claim that everyone everywhere is being squeezed in the same way; it claims that a real mechanism is operating whose actualisations vary precisely as the stratified ontology predicts they should.

## 4. The World Must Be Real for the Squeeze to Be Coherent

A subtler objection lurks behind the threshold question. If the "world" — the human-constructed meaning-space — is *merely* the empirical layer, then talk of squeezing it is category-confused: one cannot exert real causal pressure upon what has no real causal standing. This objection has a long pedigree in pragmatist and constructivist traditions that treat the world-as-meaning-space as a useful interpretive frame rather than as an ontologically weighty stratum. If those traditions are right, the squeeze hypothesis dissolves into a description of changing experiences, not of changing realities.

Margaret Archer's defence of culture as a real-level emergent stratum is the decisive answer. In *Culture and Agency*, Archer distinguishes the *Cultural System* (CS) — the set of intelligible items (propositions, doctrines, theories, beliefs, symbolic complexes) standing in logical relations of contradiction or complementarity with one another — from *Socio-Cultural* (S-C) interaction, the empirical stratum of who-believes-what-when-with-whom [VERIFY: archerCultureAgencyPlace1996 | p.xv-xviii]. The CS is not reducible to S-C. It is an emergent property of past human activity that, once emergent, has its own causal powers: its internal contradictions and complementarities condition (without determining) the situations agents face and the strategic options available to them. As Archer puts it, culture has "causal efficacy in its own right." [VERIFY: archerCultureAgencyPlace1996 | p.xvi]

In the thesis's vocabulary, the *world* names precisely such a real-level cultural-symbolic stratum: a human-emergent but irreducible meaning-space nested within the universe and possessing its own generative mechanisms (the world's metabolic logics, its institutional grammars, its modes of meaning-stabilisation). The world is real — `dr`-level — even though it is human-constructed, because emergent properties are real properties. To squeeze the world is to apply countervailing real-level pressure on a real-level stratum; the description is ontologically licit. Without Archer's analytical-dualist defence, the squeeze would be merely a description of subjective dislocation; with it, the squeeze is a causal claim about a real interaction across three real strata.

This is also why the thesis can speak of *world-completeness* and the *disposition to domination* without metaphor. World-completeness names the condition in which the world-stratum can sustain the human mode of existence on its own internal resources, without requiring the human to inhabit the metaverse-stratum as a precondition for participation in livelihood, sociality, or meaning. The disposition to domination names the tendency — a real causal disposition, in Bhaskar's sense — of the metaverse-stratum to absorb and then condition the operations of the world-stratum, eroding world-completeness from within. Both terms refer to real-level features of real-level strata; neither is reducible to the empirical patterns through which they are made visible.

## 5. Recovering the Three Empirical Witnesses Within the Schema

With the stratification in place, the three classes of empirical witness adduced in the original draft can be restated more precisely. *Psychosomatic symptoms* — disrupted sleep architecture, attentional fragmentation, the depressive constellations associated with heavy platform use — are `de`-level experiences whose `da`-level events (specific neurochemical and behavioural cascades in specific cohorts under specific platform regimes) are themselves co-produced by the squeeze mechanism interacting with neurobiological mechanisms, attentional-economy mechanisms, and culturally specific norms of self-report. The squeeze does not produce these symptoms directly; it produces a structural pressure on the world-stratum's ordinary capacities for attentional and somatic regulation, the failure of which then becomes empirically visible in symptom patterns that vary by context.

*Singularisation of livelihood* — the increasing prerequisite-status of platform participation for access to work, finance, healthcare, education, and civic identity — is the `da`-level domain in which the squeeze is most legibly actualised, because the structural integration of the metaverse-stratum into the conditions of basic livelihood is exactly the mechanism by which the world-stratum loses its capacity to support the human mode of existence on its own. The COVID acceleration was not the appearance of the mechanism; it was a particularly conspicuous actualisation that compressed into months changes that were already underway.

*Algorithmic governance* — content-moderation regimes, algorithmic welfare-fraud detection, predictive policing, social-credit systems — represents the actualisation of the metaverse-stratum's regulatory capacity over the world-stratum's institutions of public reason and collective adjudication. The empirical forms differ markedly across jurisdictions; the mechanism is the same.

In each case the structure of the explanation is identical: a real-level mechanism (the squeeze) operates transfactually, its actualisations are co-determined by other concurrently active mechanisms, and the empirical signatures we observe are partial and culturally filtered registrations of those actualisations. Mi's question — at what completion-threshold does severity obtain? — is well-posed only at the `da`/`de` levels, where severity does indeed scale with the degree to which the mechanism has been actualised under local conditions. At the `dr` level, the mechanism either exists and operates, or it does not. The argument of this thesis is that it does, and that the available empirical witnesses are exactly what one would expect to observe if it does.
