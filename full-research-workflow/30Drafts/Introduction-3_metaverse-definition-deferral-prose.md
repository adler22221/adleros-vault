# Introduction-3 — Metaverse Definition: Restructuring Intro Expectations

## Committee concern

米 (Mi): "Metaverse concept need not be fully defined in ch.1, but the framework for later discussion must be laid."

## Analysis of current DOCX

The introduction currently introduces the term *metaverse* in two moves:

1. It critiques existing definitions (XR-centric, disciplinary-reductive, positivistic) in §A.
2. It deploys the term provisionally — "an environment I call the metaverse" — in the final sentence of the structure preview (§B, para 50 in DOCX), before a formal definition appears in §V.

The gap: there is no explicit acknowledgement in §B that the formal definition is being *deferred* intentionally, and why. Without this note, readers expecting a settled definition feel the ground is missing under the early argument.

## Recommended insertion

**Location:** At the end of the "Approach of this thesis and its structure" subsection (after para 51 in the DOCX, before the first §I heading), as a bridging note.

---

**Version A (concise, ~80 words):**

A terminological note is in order before proceeding. Throughout this thesis, I use the term *metaverse* provisionally, in advance of its full conceptual specification in Section V. This deferral is deliberate: to define the metaverse at the outset — as most existing scholarship does — would be to presuppose precisely what needs to be explained. The provisional usage is regulative, not definitive: it names the target of inquiry, not its settled content. The argument of Sections I–IV is what makes a rigorous definition possible.

---

**Version B (slightly fuller, ~130 words — if Mi presses for more explicit scaffolding):**

A terminological clarification is needed before proceeding to the body of the thesis. Throughout the following discussion, the term *metaverse* is used in a deliberately provisional sense. Rather than providing a full definition at this stage — as the majority of existing literature does, thereby inheriting the problems diagnosed above — this thesis defers its formal specification to Section V. The reason is methodological: only after establishing what human worlds are (Section I), what artificialityʼs structural role in world-formation is (Section II), what historical disposition drove that artificiality to its present form (Section III), and what tendency of life is being responded to (Section IV), can a non-arbitrary account of the metaverseʼs nature be given. What precedes the formal definition is not a gap but a grounding. The provisional usage of *metaverse* names the phenomenon under investigation; it does not pre-answer it.
