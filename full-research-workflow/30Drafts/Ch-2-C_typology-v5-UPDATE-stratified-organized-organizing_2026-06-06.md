## 3. Organized → organizing inorganic (the Ch V deployment)

- **Organized inorganic** (e.g., steam engine): a **Cell 1** material structure whose **real-level organizing principle is the *universe's*** (thermodynamics). It is *aided by* **Cell 3** (conventional nonmaterial — fallible knowledge, institutions) and **Cell 4① transitive** (math, *descriptive*, a tool). The made is *arranged by the human using the universe's principles*; the structural nonmaterial (math) **describes/aids**, it does not compel.
- **Organizing inorganic** (e.g., smart gadgets + platform): **Cell 1** material substrate + **Cell 4② structural nonmaterial** (code/AI) where the **real-level organizing principle is now the *artificial* structural nonmaterial** (code's compelling-against-contingency), and **the universe's principles are relevant only to the material substrate** (IoT/server). The structural nonmaterial (code) **propels/enforces** — it has *gained* the real-level compelling capacity.
- **The shift organized→organizing = the real-level organizing principle migrating from the universe's (natural) to the artificial structural nonmaterial (code, made-become-compelling).** That migration *is* self-completion (自足完成化), and at the typology level it *is* "tekhne becoming phusis" (and code-as-law: "nomos becoming phusis").

It **describes/aids**; it does **not** compel action against contingency. **Ancient — always occupied.** [ADLER: codified grammar should be conventional-becoming-structural?, parenthesized in cell 3: it doesn't propel itself without human action, but as the structure (once established) is followed without conventional enforcement, but still can be violated; mathematical formulas and algorithms should be structural-still-conventional?, parenthesized in cell 4: it doesn't propel itself without human action, but the structure (once established) is followed without conventional enforcement and cannot be violated -- both illustrative of the migration from conventional to structural]
