## 3. 400-Word Abstract *(reference-exclusive)*

Even as ministries pledge climate literacy and universities declare sustainability missions, education systems remain locked in productivist trajectories that the planetary crisis renders untenable. This chapter reads that persistence not as a funding gap or a values deficit but as the symptom of a deeper structural condition. Building on the editors' own diagnosis that more schooling is not the answer (Silova, Rappleye & Komatsu, 2025), the chapter advances a *modes-of-dependencies* framework, developed through dialogue with Daoist 有所待 (Zhuangzi) and Buddhist dependent origination, as the macro-architecture for explaining this lock-in. Within that frame, it specializes to *allocation dependence* (Yang, 2021, 2023) as the dominant educational pathology: the transfactual, fractal mechanism by which learners and institutions trim themselves to fit externally imposed metrics of validation. Read as a governance assemblage, education's resource flows and accreditation logic foreclose value regeneration in favour of value redemption. This extends the field's anti-productivist critique (Andreotti, Stein & Ahenakew, 2022) with a generative mechanism it currently lacks. Against this diagnosis the chapter proposes *Learning by Caring* (德中學) as an agent-based counter-pathway — a regenerative relational cycle in which the learner's caring act becomes the organizing principle of pedagogy, resisting both algorithmic and neoliberal capture because care cannot be fully metricized or automated. The practice is grounded in the pre-moralized sense of 德 (de) as inner power and generative virtue, a cross-traditional common ground spanning Confucian, Daoist, and Buddhist thought — answering, substantively rather than gesturally, the call to think beyond the Western horizon (Silova, Rappleye & You, 2020) and linking care to political economy (Tronto, 2013). Methodologically, the argument proceeds through retroductive critical-realist case analysis with autoethnographic and practitioner-inquiry components: a seven-year critical autoethnography of an alternative high school in Taiwan grounds the allocation-dependence mechanism (Yang, 2023), and practitioner action research at the Awakening Cooperative Lab traces how Learning by Caring emerges across a cohort of youth, including learners from disadvantaged backgrounds, in lived mentoring practice. The method does not test a prior intervention but retroduces the generative mechanisms operative in actual educational practice. The chapter positions *Education AS Sustainable Development* (Yang & Pi, 2022/23) as the institutional horizon such a practice requires — academia not teaching about sustainability but living as the regenerative system it cultivates. The contribution is threefold: a structural-causal account of educational climate inaction, a regenerative counter-pathway, and a non-Western theoretical voice currently absent from the planetary-education conversation.

## 4. Author Biography *(150 words)*

Adler Yang is an antidisciplinary scholar-practitioner working across comparative education, philosophy of education, synthetic anthropology, and critical realism. He is founder and director of the Awakening Cooperative Lab (2012–present), a think-and-do tank developing theory and praxis for transforming wicked problems through prosocial reciprocity, and of the International Research and Action Alliance for Experimental Higher Education (IRAEEH, 2019–present). He serves as a Council Member of the International Association for Critical Realism (2025–2027), a Board Member of the Japan Association of Synthetic Anthropology, and an inaugural Fellow of The Institutional Architecture Lab (Demos Helsinki / UCL / The Rockefeller Foundation). His research on allocation dependence, Learning by Caring, and Education AS Sustainable Development appears in *Synthetic Anthropology* (2021), KAS *Panorama* (2022/23), and IACR conference proceedings (2023). His documentary *If There Is a Reason to Study* (2009–2016), recognized at more than ten international festivals, contributed to discontinuing Taiwan's standardized high-school entrance examination.
