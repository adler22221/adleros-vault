# Ch-1-8 DECISION MEMO: Does the Epimetheus mythology still earn its place?

## 1. Recommendation (one line)

**KEEP the Epimetheus/Prometheus myth in compact form; DROP the seven-tradition comparative-cosmogony survey; RELOCATE the kept material from Chapter I to Chapter II §II.A ("Technology as exosomatization"), tied explicitly to the premature-birth / neoteny rationale.**

## 2. What the finished DOCX already does (the reason the question is live)

The finished Chapter I does **not** contain a mythology-corroboration section. Its argument for the necessity of the made world runs entirely through analytic/empirical channels:
- niche construction (beaver, ant, coral) → organisms reside in two environments;
- Obara's dual ecosystems, auto-artificial selection (自己人為淘汰), self-domestication (自己家畜化);
- the AOS five criteria;
- the world/universe definitions, Brague's *kosmos*, Uexküll's Umwelt/Umgebung.

Chapter I closes on the analytic claim that needs no myth: "Artificiality — my analytical unit that replaces culture — is what makes human human, and a world world" (DOCX §E, [P0109]).

Chapter II §II.A ("Technology as exosomatization") then makes the necessity-of-artificiality argument explicitly and in the canonical idiom:
- artificiality develops as "extensions or enhancements of our functions to compensate for our biological weaknesses or vulnerabilities" ([P0113]);
- Lotka's exosomatic evolution / exosomatization distinguishes *Homo sapiens* ([P0113]);
- Stiegler's ever-exteriorization of organs and memories, the localizing ("locally trans-forms the cosmic order") of universe-laws into techno-geographical milieus ([P0115]).

This is decisive for the decision: **the analytic point the mythology was recruited to corroborate — world-making as compensation for natural deficiency — is now stated outright in §II.A, without myth.** The comparative-cosmogony survey is therefore no longer carrying an argument; it is carrying *atmosphere*. Under Liu's scope discipline (what is argued vs. merely mentioned; section architecture), a seven-tradition tour (Enuma Elish, Purusha, Ymir, Popol Vuh, ma'at, Nüwa/Hou Yi/Yu, Eden…) is exactly the kind of breadth-without-load-bearing material that invites the "merely mentioned" objection. **Drop it.**

## 3. Why the Epimetheus myth, specifically, still earns its place

Three reasons the *one* myth survives the cut while the survey does not:

**(a) It is already philosophically thematized in the literature §II.A cites.** §II.A is built on Stiegler. The *défaut d'origine* (the fault/default of origin) is *the* Stiegler concept that grounds the exosomatization claim — it is the reason the human "compensate[s] for our biological weaknesses." The Epimetheus myth is not a decorative parallel to §II.A's argument; it is the originary scene from which Stiegler derives the very concept §II.A uses. To cite Stiegler's exosomatization while suppressing the Epimethean fault that motivates it is to use the conclusion without the premise.

**(b) It discharges the author's own flagged intuition (premature birth / neoteny).** The author's annotation asks whether "the necessity of artificiality due to homo sapien's premature birth can be corroborated by mythology." The Epimetheus myth corroborates exactly this, and tightly: the human "naked, unshod, unbedded, and unarmed" is the mythic figuration of secondary altriciality — *Homo sapiens* born neurologically and physiologically unfinished (the "extra-uterine year," Portmann's secondary altriciality / neoteny). The *défaut d'origine* IS the premature-birth thesis in philosophical dress: the human has no instinct-complete niche and so must be finished exosomatically. This makes the myth a genuine corroboration of a load-bearing §II.A claim, not ornamentation. (This is the single strongest reason to keep it.)

**(c) It is the only one of the seven that adds an argument §II.A doesn't already make.** §II.A says artificiality compensates for biological weakness. The Epimetheus myth adds the stronger, constitutive form of that claim — that there is *no pre-technical human to be supplemented*; the human is constituted by the lack ("Humans only occur through their being forgotten," Stiegler 1998, p. 188). That is a sharper thesis than "tools help us," and it is precisely the anti-instrumentalist point the canonical stance needs (it forecloses "technology is just a tool we added"). The comparative cosmogonies, by contrast, only re-assert the weaker "world is built, not given" point already secured analytically in Ch1.

## 4. Why relocate to Chapter II §II.A rather than keep in Chapter I

- In Chapter I the myth would now be orphaned: Ch1's argument is complete without it, and inserting a mythological excursus into the niche-construction → AOS spine would be the "slightly odd transition" the author already worried about, with no analytic payoff.
- In §II.A the myth lands on its natural host claim — exosomatization-as-compensation — and reads as the originary illustration of the *défaut d'origine* that §II.A's Stiegler citation already invokes. It deepens an existing argument rather than opening a new section.
- This is also exactly where the author's own undecided note pointed: "perhaps Ch2 is close." It is. §II.A specifically.

## 6. Recalibrated passage for §II.A (compact, myth-only)

Drop-in after the exosomatization paragraphs ([P0113]–[P0115]), before the §II.B turn to virtuality. ~190 words. All Stiegler page references carried from the verified Ch-1-8 lookup; preserve [VERIFY] until cross-checked against the print/PDF.

> The constitutive — rather than merely supplementary — character of this exosomatic compensation is encoded in the oldest stratum of Greek myth. In Plato's *Protagoras*, Epimetheus distributes natural powers to every creature — speed, claws, wings, shells — but forgets the human, leaving it "naked, unshod, unbedded, and unarmed," so that Prometheus must steal fire and *technē* to compensate (Stiegler, 1998, pp. 188–189) [VERIFY]. Stiegler reads this not as narrative accident but as structure: "humans are the forgotten ones; they only occur through their being forgotten" — there will have been "nothing at the origin but the fault… the *de-fault of origin*" (p. 188) [VERIFY]. The myth figures what paleoanthropology states as secondary altriciality: *Homo sapiens* is born biologically unfinished, without an instinct-complete niche, and must be finished from outside itself. Artificiality is therefore not an instrument added to an already-complete creature; it is "the constitution of this body *qua* 'human'" (pp. 152–153) [VERIFY]. The human does not *have* a world as a mole has its burrow; it must *make* one — because, lacking a given niche, it has no other way to be at all.

**[VERIFY note]** All four Stiegler quotations were page-confirmed in the Ch-1-8 lookup via PDF line-number analysis (attachment QS6TCGH4); re-confirm against the print edition before final submission. The secondary-altriciality / neotery claim ("born biologically unfinished") is standard paleoanthropology (Portmann's "extra-uterine year"); if a citation is wanted, add Portmann or a current developmental-anthropology source to Zotero — currently uncited here.
