**Placement:** **Expand existing P0176 (B) singularization section**. NOT a new section.

**Load-bearing claims to preserve:**
1. **Distinguish commercial from radical monopoly** (Illich 1973, pp. 64–65). Commercial = one firm controls a market. Radical = one industrial production process exercises exclusive control over satisfaction of a pressing need, *excluding nonindustrial activities from competition*.
2. **The automobile illustration**: cars achieve radical monopoly by reshaping the environment so foot/bicycle travel becomes practically impossible. *Environmentally achieved rather than commercially imposed.*
