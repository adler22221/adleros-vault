## Ch-2-2 rule-vs-law footnote — quickest packet

**Placement**: footnote attached to first occurrence of "laws of artificial environments" — P0126 heading itself.

**Decision**: Mi suggested "rule" is more appropriate. Three options:
- (a) **Global replace** "law" → "rule" throughout §IIC. Cost: ~20 instances; downstream cascades to Ch III/V/VI.
- (b) **Footnote justifying retention of "law"**. Recommended. Argue: "law" preserves the structural-emergent property that "rule" doesn't (rules are made and unmade; laws-of-an-environment-once-established operate independently of any rule-maker's intention).
- (c) **Inline parenthetical at first occurrence** + footnote. Most explicit.

**Recommended footnote text (your voice, 80–120w):**
> *I retain "laws" rather than "rules" of artificial environments deliberately. Rules are made and unmade by enforcement parties; once established, the laws of an artificial environment operate independently of any maker's intention, regulating the conditions of possibility for action within them. The terminology preserves the structural-emergent character of the property — a feature lost if "rules" is substituted, given that "rule" connotes deliberate prescription. The choice is parallel to Polanyi's usage in describing dual-control systems (see §I/Ch I criterion (iii) Polanyi footnote): the boundary conditions of a higher-level system constitute laws for the lower-level operation, not rules.*
