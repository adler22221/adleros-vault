## Recommended footnote text — draft for your voice rewriting

> *The irreducibility claim at (iii) is the application of Polanyi's dual-control thesis to adaptational operating systems. Polanyi (1958; 1966; 1968) argues that complex hierarchical systems exhibit "dual control": at every level, the higher organizing principle operates *through* the lower-level laws without being *determined by* them. The classic illustration is the machine: gears obey friction, electromagnetism, and thermodynamics, but what counts as the machine functioning is set by the boundary conditions of its design, which the physics of its parts cannot generate. Polanyi extends the structure across all levels of hierarchical organization — chemistry → biology → psychology → culture — at each step, the higher principle "harnesses" the lower without reducing to it. An adaptational operating system, on the account this thesis develops, is the supra-organismal level of this hierarchy: it operates through universe-level laws (gravitation, thermodynamics, biology) but introduces organizing conditions — material configurations, behavioural traditions, social arrangements, technical infrastructure — whose causal role within the operating system is not entailed by the underlying laws. The artificial-laws extension of this argument (the case in which the operating-system level is human and the organizing conditions are themselves products of cumulative cultural and technical evolution) is developed in §IIC.*

### If you want the main-text paragraph version instead (~180w, place after criterion (v))

> *The irreducibility claim at (iii) rests on a structural feature of all hierarchical complex systems that Michael Polanyi (1958, 1966) named dual control. At every level of organization, a higher principle operates through the laws of the lower level without being determined by them: a machine's function is not derivable from its physics; a cell's biological organization is not derivable from its chemistry; an institution's organizing structure is not derivable from the cognition of its individual members. The higher principle imposes boundary conditions on the lower level, and these boundary conditions are themselves irreducible — there is no derivation of the higher from the lower, even though the lower remains the substrate on which the higher operates. An adaptational operating system is the supra-organismal application of this structure: its organizing conditions (material configurations, behavioural traditions, social arrangements, technical infrastructure) are boundary conditions on the universe-level laws it operates through. The artificial-laws extension — in which the operating-system level is human and the organizing conditions are themselves products of cumulative cultural and technical evolution — is developed in §IIC.*
