# §VI.4: The Metaverse/Universe Squeeze Proper

## §0 — Framing

[SCAFFOLD] §VI.1 defined the squeeze structurally. §VI.2 established F1+F2 as empirically grounded in the animal record. §VI.3 situated the existing critical-theoretic literature as anthropological-experiential precursors to F1+F2 in formation. §VI.4 now states the metaverse/universe squeeze proper as the *consolidated* F1+F2 condition reached at the metaverse-stage — what the precursor signatures have been responding to, and what the structural-ontological diagnosis identifies as the contemporary human condition.

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The metaverse/universe squeeze proper is the structural-ontological condition that obtains when the metaverse — established in §V as a real-level background environment with its own laws — and the universe — the prior real-level background environment in which all human life had previously been situated — are *jointly* operative as environments within which humans must satisfy incommensurable selection criteria. This is the consolidated form of F1+F2 reached at the metaverse-stage.
>
> The previous three sections established the framing within which this statement is now made. §VI.1 defined the squeeze structurally and distinguished it from collapse. §VI.2 grounded F1+F2 empirically in the fifty-thousand-year animal record. §VI.3 situated the canonical critical-theoretic literature as describing the human-experiential signatures of F1+F2 in formation. This section states the structural condition itself, specifies its mechanism, articulates the contributions of three theorists (Uegaki, Hui, Lessig) whose work provides hints toward this specifically-novel condition, and deploys the three empirical signatures from the viva-passed §VI as evidence of the condition becoming operative in contemporary life.

## §1 — The squeeze stated structurally

The consolidated form of F1+F2 at the metaverse-stage:

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The human is now situated between two real-level background environments — the universe and the metaverse — whose organizing laws are not mutually translatable. The universe selects for capacities calibrated to biological-ecological-physical conditions: embodied resilience, autonomic regulation, inter-corporeal presence, vernacular competence in local ecosystems, biological adaptation to planetary manifestations of universe-laws. The metaverse selects for capacities calibrated to computational-algorithmic-architectural conditions: platform legibility, data-generativity, algorithmic compliance, credential-mediated identity, behavioral compatibility with predictive systems. Each set of criteria is operative as a real selection regime; each makes ongoing demands that the human must satisfy as the condition of continued participation in the relevant environment. The two sets of criteria do not share a common measure: F2 obtains. The two environments are not nested but parallel: F1 obtains. The structural-ontological condition F1+F2 names is the contemporary human condition under the metaverse-stage.
>
> This is not a metaphor. The metaverse, per §V, is not a figure of speech for "the contemporary digital landscape." It is a real-level background environment with its own organizing laws — established through the bidirectional coupling of human self-finishing (Uegaki's 自己完結化) and apparatus self-completion (自足完成化), actualized via the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention, instantiated empirically through the joint operational sufficiency of AI, big data, IoT, and code-as-law. The squeeze is the structural condition of being situated between this real-level environment and the universe-as-real-level-environment, with no possible single optimization that would satisfy both simultaneously.

## §2 — The Uegaki hint: the apparatus-side becomes a second environment

### §2.1 — What Uegaki's analysis provides

Uegaki's analysis of 自己完結社会 (self-completing society) and the normative project of 〈意のままになる生〉 (life-as-willed) names the apparatus-side mechanism by which a second environment becomes possible. The argument, developed through Uegaki's source manuscripts and grounded at Tier 0 in the NLM-Uegaki retrieval (per `20Wiki/squeeze-ontology.md` §3), runs as follows.

Humans collectively pursue life-as-willed — the reduction of 〈意のままにならない〉 states, conditions where things do not proceed as the human wills. This pursuit operates not as an isolated preference but as a *normative ought-to-be claim*: 〈意のままになる生〉こそが、人間存在のあるべき生の形だと考えるようになっているのである [Uegaki, T0 cit. 1]. The pursuit drives the construction of an artificial ecosystem (人為的生態系) capable of progressively eliminating as-not-willedness. Each successive technical and institutional addition to this artificial ecosystem extends its capacity to deliver as-willedness.

The structural cost of this pursuit, also developed by Uegaki, is the inversion of the human's dependency structure. The more thoroughly humans entrust matters to control means and pursue 〈意のままになる生〉, the smaller the human role in the world's operation becomes; the control means progressively automate; the human's portion of functionality contracts. *Self-completing person as "user" of social devices, operating under "ethics of non-intervention"* [Uegaki, T0 cit. 6]. At the limit, the human becomes a node in the artificial ecosystem the human's species has constructed, rather than an originator of activity within it.

### §2.2 — Why Uegaki's analysis is a hint toward the squeeze

Uegaki's analysis does not name the universe-metaverse squeeze. Uegaki's framework is structurally bifold: humans on one side, the social apparatus / 人為的生態系 on the other; the analytical concern is the *coupling* between the two sides as the apparatus develops toward self-completion. Uegaki does not explicitly frame this as two-environment between-structure.

But Uegaki's analysis specifies the apparatus-side mechanism the squeeze requires. The squeeze (F1) requires a *second* real-level environment, not just a modified or intensified version of the universe-substrate. Uegaki provides the bidirectional-coupling mechanism by which such a second environment becomes possible: the apparatus self-completes (自足完成化) as humans self-finish (自己完結化), and the apparatus's self-completion is what gives it the structural status of a background environment — capable of operating, maintaining, and extending itself without continuous human noetic involvement. Once the apparatus has reached this threshold, F1 is satisfied: there is now a *second* real-level environment with its own organizing laws.

What Uegaki contributes to §VI.4 is the *generative mechanism* by which the metaverse comes to exist as a second environment. The bidirectional coupling Uegaki specifies is what produces the F1 condition the squeeze requires.

### §2.3 — The Uegaki contribution in summary

In summary, Uegaki's analysis answers the question: *how does a second environment come into existence that humans must satisfy as a separate selection regime from the universe?* The answer: through the bidirectional coupling of human pursuit of life-as-willed and apparatus self-completion, reaching the threshold at which the apparatus operates as a real-level background environment in its own right. Uegaki's analysis is therefore upstream of F1 — it explains the conditions under which F1 can be satisfied.

## §3 — The Hui hint: the recursive mechanism by which the apparatus loop closes

### §3.1 — What Hui's analysis provides

Hui's analysis of recursive technological evolution, *organizing inorganic*, and tertiary protention names the *mechanism* by which the apparatus side closes its operational loop and thereby acquires environmental status. The argument, developed across *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019) and grounded at Tier 0 in the NLM-Hui retrieval (per Ch-5-3 v3 §2.3), runs as follows.

Stiegler's analysis of technical-historical grammatization extensively developed *tertiary retention* — the artificial, externalized, technically-recorded memory that prosthetizes consciousness. Without artificial memory supports, there is no human temporality at all. But Stiegler did not formalize a corresponding category for anticipation — *tertiary protention* as a category distinct from tertiary retention. Hui argues that this missing element is structurally critical: *the lack of discussion of tertiary protention in Stiegler's work seems very paradoxical* [Hui, R&C §36]. Hui's positive contribution is to develop tertiary protention as a distinct philosophical category, parallel to and irreducible to tertiary retention.

The decisive structural claim: *this missing element — tertiary protention — is crucial to understanding the evolution of technical systems, meaning their becoming organic* [Hui, R&C §36]. Without tertiary protention, the inorganic merely stores; with the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention, the inorganic begins to organize itself. Hui describes the cybernetic circuit: smart objects play the role of organs of motor-perception (capturing approximate sense perceptions), organs of analysis (calculation based on existing data), and *organs of synthesis (constitution of the tertiary protention)* [Hui, R&C §37].

And: *the preemption of the tertiary protention is possible only because of the computational hermeneutics, which is essentially recursive: it constantly evaluates the past in order to anticipate the future, which in turn determines the present. Human beings are reintegrated into the temporality of machines* [Hui, R&C §39].

### §3.2 — Why Hui's analysis is a hint toward the squeeze

Hui's analysis does not name the universe-metaverse squeeze. Hui's framework is what Ch-5-3 v3 §1.4 distinguished as *organicist-fused* — the contemporary cybernetic system is described as a superorganism humans live inside of, not as a second environment parallel to the universe. Hui explicitly reassigns the Schellingian/Lovelockian *general organism* term from nature to the technological system; for Hui, the universe ceases to be the relevant gigantic organism in favor of the cybernetic system that has supplanted it.

But Hui's analysis specifies the mechanism that the squeeze requires for the metaverse to function as a second environment. The squeeze (F2) requires that the metaverse have *its own organizing laws* that operate as a selection regime — not merely a set of regulatory affordances, but a *self-organizing* structure capable of constituting the conditions of action for its inhabitants. The recursive retention/protention coupling Hui specifies is the mechanism by which the apparatus side achieves this self-organization. The cybernetic system *closes its loop* through recursive past-evaluation and future-anticipation; once the loop closes, the system operates without requiring human noetic input; the system has acquired the structural status of a self-organizing environment with its own organizing logic.

What Hui contributes to §VI.4 is the *mechanism of metaverse-as-environment*. Without the recursive retention/protention coupling, the metaverse would be a regulatory layer — a set of constraints that humans navigate while remaining within the universe-substrate. With the coupling, the metaverse becomes self-organizing — an environment with its own operational logic, its own selection criteria, its own temporal structure. F2 is satisfied because the resulting environment's organizing laws (recursive, computational, optimizing) are not commensurable with the universe's organizing laws (biological, ecological, thermodynamic).

### §3.3 — The Hui contribution in summary

In summary, Hui's analysis answers the question: *what makes the metaverse's organizing laws incommensurable with the universe's?* The answer: the recursive structure of the cybernetic system, by which past-evaluation and future-anticipation are coupled in a self-organizing loop that operates by computational hermeneutics indifferent to biological-ecological criteria. Hui's analysis is therefore upstream of F2 — it specifies the mechanism by which the metaverse's organizing laws come to be incommensurable with the universe's.

## §4 — The Lessig hint: code-as-law as the regulatory mechanism of F2 incommensurability

### §4.1 — What Lessig's analysis provides

Lessig's analysis of *code is law* in *Code 2.0* names a regulatory mechanism that has the structural form of physical architecture but the medium of digital code. Lessig's foundational claim is that *four constraints regulate this pathetic dot — the law, social norms, the market, and architecture — and the "regulation" of this dot is the sum of these four constraints* [Lessig, *Code 2.0*, p. 122]. Architectural regulation operates not by command but by physical/structural constraint: walls constrain movement; gravity constrains action; the speed of light constrains communication. *Architectural constraints, then, work whether or not the subject knows they are working* [Lessig, *Code 2.0*, p. 344].

The contribution Lessig identifies is that *code in cyberspace functions as architecture* — it constrains by structuring the possibility space of action rather than by commanding behavior. Code-as-law is automatic in its operation; it does not require enforcement parties; it does not require the regulated subject's awareness; it operates deterministically across all subjects in the regulated space.

### §4.2 — Why Lessig's analysis is a hint toward the squeeze

Lessig's analysis does not name the universe-metaverse squeeze. Lessig's framework is regulatory: he is analyzing how regulation works in cyberspace, with the broader claim that cyberspace illuminates how all regulation works. The framework remains within a regulatory paradigm — concerned with how authority structures shape behavior.

But Lessig's analysis specifies the mechanism by which the metaverse's *law-character* differs structurally from the universe's law-character — and this is what makes the squeeze structurally novel rather than merely intense. Universe-laws are *given* — they are causal-generative structures of the real world, transfactually operative, not subject to human authorship. Metaverse-laws are *engineered* — they are written, mutable, recursively re-issued, and deterministic in their application. Both kinds of law operate deterministically (no enforcement party needed, no awareness required); both kinds of law structure the possibility space of action. But they differ in kind: universe-laws are the unauthored substrate of reality; metaverse-laws are authored architectural constraints.

§VI.5 will develop this distinction through the four-cell typology of constraint. Here, in §VI.4, Lessig's contribution is to name what makes the metaverse's organizing laws structurally distinctive: they are *deterministic-but-decoupled-from-universe-substrate*. They borrow the determinism of physical architecture (automatic operation; no enforcement party) and the decoupling of pure virtuality (no universe-substrate as constraining ground). This combination is what makes the metaverse's laws incommensurable with the universe's laws in the F2 sense.

### §4.3 — The Lessig contribution in summary

In summary, Lessig's analysis answers the question: *what kind of law operates in the metaverse, structurally distinct from any prior kind of law?* The answer: code-as-law — deterministic in operation, decoupled from universe-substrate as constraining ground, engineered rather than given, mutable rather than stable. Lessig's analysis is therefore upstream of F2 — it specifies the structural character of metaverse-law that makes it incommensurable with universe-law.

## §5 — The three hints together: how F1+F2 is satisfied at the metaverse-stage

The three theorists provide three distinct contributions that, taken together, complete the structural specification of the metaverse/universe squeeze proper.

- **Uegaki specifies F1**: through the bidirectional coupling of human self-finishing and apparatus self-completion, the apparatus side acquires the structural status of a real-level background environment. Two environments now exist where previously there was one.
- **Hui specifies the mechanism of F2**: the recursive coupling of tertiary retention and tertiary protention is the self-organizing process by which the apparatus environment generates its own incommensurable selection criteria — computational hermeneutics that operate indifferent to biological-ecological criteria.
- **Lessig specifies the character of F2**: the resulting metaverse-laws are deterministic-but-decoupled — they have the form of physical architecture without inheriting universe-laws as constraints. Code-as-law is the legal-ontological form of metaverse-organizing-logic.

The three contributions are not redundant; they specify different aspects of the same structural condition. Uegaki specifies *that* the apparatus becomes a second environment; Hui specifies *how* it does so mechanistically; Lessig specifies *what kind of law* operates within the resulting environment. Together they constitute the structural specification of F1+F2 at the metaverse-stage that this thesis has been developing.

None of the three names the universe-metaverse squeeze as such. The squeeze, as the structural-ontological condition of a human jointly subject to two such environments, is the thesis's distinctive contribution. The three theorists provide the elements; the thesis assembles them into the structural diagnosis.

## §6 — The universe-side characterized [PENDING LOCK REVISION ON F3]

> *Note: this subsection depends on the proposed F3 refinement distinguishing universe-LAWS (stable) from universe-MANIFESTATIONS (dynamic). Drafting proceeds on this revised framing.*

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> If the metaverse-side of the squeeze is constituted as developed in §§2–5, what about the universe-side? The squeeze-ontology lock F3 specifies the *law-change asymmetry* as one of the operational features of the squeeze: universe-laws are stable on civilizational timescales while metaverse-laws are deliberately engineered to change. This is correct at the laws level. But it is incomplete at the *manifestation* level, and the metaverse-stage condition reveals the incompleteness.
>
> Universe-laws — thermodynamic, biological, ecological, chemical, gravitational — are stable on the timescale of human civilization. They are transfactually so, in Bhaskar's sense: they hold whether or not their manifestations vary in particular events. The universe's *laws* are not what the metaverse-stage human contends with as a moving target.
>
> But the *planetary manifestations* of universe-laws are dynamic on civilizational timescales, and the dynamism is increasingly produced by the human worlds whose modes of artificiality §III–§V have analyzed. Climate is changing because of anthropogenic emissions. Biosphere composition is being altered through habitat conversion and species translocation. Ocean chemistry is shifting due to carbon dioxide absorption. Atmospheric composition is being engineered through fossil-fuel combustion. The universe whose *laws* stay constant is also a universe whose *manifestations* are increasingly the product of human worlds.
>
> This stratification is consistent with the thesis's existing critical-realist methodology. Real-level laws are stable; actual-level events through which laws operate are dynamic; empirical-level experiences track the dynamism. The universe-laws stay at the real level; the universe-manifestations occupy the actual level; the human experience of those manifestations occupies the empirical level. Acknowledging the manifestation-dynamism does not destabilize the universe's status as a real-level background environment; it specifies that the universe-side of the squeeze involves dynamic manifestation patterns that the human worlds have themselves disturbed.
>
> The implication for the squeeze: the universe-side is not stable in the way prior thinkers' frameworks typically assumed. The body is asked to maintain biological adaptation to a planetary manifestation that is itself in motion (temperatures rising, growing seasons shifting, disease vectors expanding, pollen seasons lengthening) — while also being asked to maintain metaverse-compliance with algorithmic systems whose laws are themselves engineered to be mutable. *Both sides of the squeeze are moving.* The universe-side moves at the manifestation level under anthropogenic disturbance; the metaverse-side moves at the laws level under deliberate engineering. The two move at different paces and for different reasons, but the human is squeezed between two dynamic environments, not between one stable environment and one dynamic one.

## §7 — The three empirical signs as evidence of F1+F2 operative

The viva-passed §VI identified three empirical signs of the squeeze. With the structural diagnosis now in hand, each can be read as evidence of F1+F2 operating at the contemporary moment. The function of this subsection is to *deploy* the empirical signs as evidence, not to introduce new empirical phenomena.

### §7.1 — Psychosomatic malfunctions: the body's signal

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The body is a universe-substrate creature: its rhythms, vulnerabilities, and requirements are governed by biological law. When behavior is adapted to metaverse-side demands — extended screen time, sedentary posture, disrupted sleep, attentional fragmentation, social media comparison dynamics, persistent low-grade arousal from notification expectancy — the body registers the incommensurability as dysfunction.
>
> Contemporary empirical evidence is extensive. Adolescent and young-adult mental health indicators have deteriorated across multiple OECD populations from 2010 onward, correlating with smartphone-and-social-media saturation. Sleep duration has declined; sedentary behavior has increased; postural musculoskeletal complaints associated with screen use have emerged as new clinical categories. Attention-deficit phenomena have been documented at rates exceeding plausible diagnostic-inflation explanations; the World Health Organization's gaming disorder category (ICD-11, 2018) represented a formal recognition of behavioral-pathology phenomena that did not exist before the metaverse-stage technologies. [VERIFY: pending — empirical psych/public-health literature; coordinate with §VI.2's empirical-paper acquisition queue]
>
> The psychosomatic signs are not a failure of individual willpower. They are the *biological signature* of cross-environmental incommensurability operative within a single subject. The universe-side selection criteria — embodied movement, undistracted attention, regulated sleep, ecological exposure — cannot be satisfied within metaverse-compliant behavior; the metaverse-side selection criteria — algorithmic legibility, attention-economy participation, credential-mediated identity — cannot be satisfied within universe-side embodied competence. The body is the part of the human that cannot be adapted to the metaverse without cost to universe-fitness, and the body speaks first.

### §7.2 — Singularization of livelihood: the metaverse-side advancing

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The second empirical sign is the singularization of pathways to livelihood under platform-mediated economic organization. Where prior generations had multiple universe-anchored pathways to economic participation (smallholder agriculture, regional craft production, locally-mediated services, vernacular exchange), the metaverse-stage has tended toward the elimination of all non-platform-mediated pathways through the restructuring of the conditions under which any livelihood can succeed.
>
> Concrete examples are abundant. Banking-without-platform-identification has become structurally impossible in most jurisdictions. Identity verification for civic participation, professional credentialing, and access to government services increasingly requires platform-mediated authentication. Professional visibility — for writers, artists, teachers, scholars, craftspeople — increasingly requires algorithmically-legible presence on platforms whose criteria for visibility are themselves opaque and mutable. Small-business viability in many sectors is increasingly conditional on algorithmic ranking on review platforms, search engines, or distribution platforms (Amazon, Apple App Store, Google Maps, TikTok, etc.). [VERIFY: pending — Ch-6-10 empirical evidence on KDP, TikTok, Google Business Profile; coordinate with Phase 3 Q2 acquisition]
>
> This is what Illich's *radical monopoly* names at the institutional-professional stratum, extended to the metaverse-stage via the platform-architectural mechanism. The radical monopoly is not over a specific industrial product (cars, schools, hospitals) but over the *architectural conditions of livelihood* — what counts as legible economic participation, identifiable selfhood, civically-recognized presence. §VI.5 will develop this as F5 (radical monopoly over livelihood) intensified by the metaverse-stage cell-unification.
>
> The singularization is the metaverse-side selection regime advancing into territory previously occupied by universe-anchored alternatives. F1 obtains because both selection regimes remain operative (universe demands biological-ecological functioning; metaverse demands platform-legibility); F2 obtains because the criteria are not commensurable; the singularization is the human-experiential signature of F2 operative under conditions where the alternatives the F2 incommensurability would require are being structurally eliminated.

### §7.3 — Disturbed lives under opaque and changing laws

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> The third empirical sign is the condition of living under laws that are opaque, mutable, and unaccountable. Universe-laws, however demanding, are stable and legible through long observation; metaverse-laws are deliberately obscured and periodically changed.
>
> The empirical record is extensive. Algorithmic recommendation systems and content moderation systems operate by criteria their developers do not disclose and that often their developers themselves do not fully understand (mechanistic-interpretability research notwithstanding). Platform terms of service are changed unilaterally and retroactively; appeals processes for algorithmic decisions are minimal or absent. Workers in algorithmically-managed industries (gig work platforms, warehouse logistics, content creation) face performance evaluation systems whose criteria are themselves dynamic and opaque. Citizens of jurisdictions with algorithmic government services (algorithmic welfare allocation, algorithmic criminal-risk assessment, algorithmic immigration triage) face state decisions whose basis they cannot inspect. [VERIFY: pending — Rouvroy 2013 on algorithmic governmentality; recent regulatory evidence; coordinate with Ch-5-3 v3 §4 LOOKUP queue]
>
> This is what Ch-5-3 v3 §4 identified as the *operational signatures* of self-completion at the empirical level: opacity to inhabitants (Lessig-side); designed mutability (Lessig-side); structural compulsion (Lessig-side); extraction (Hui-side); optimization-driven governance (Hui-side); temporal acceleration (cross-cutting). Each operational signature is a manifestation of metaverse-side organizing laws that are deterministic-decoupled (Lessig's code-as-law character) and self-organizing (Hui's recursive retention/protention). The human-experiential signature is the condition of living under laws one cannot inspect, anticipate, or appeal — a condition structurally different from prior conditions of being subject to social, institutional, or political authority.

### §7.4 — The three signs together

The three empirical signs converge on a common diagnosis: the F1+F2 condition is operative across multiple registers of contemporary human life. Psychosomatic malfunctions are the biological signature; singularization of livelihood is the economic signature; disturbed lives under opaque laws are the political-governance signature. Each register is a distinct stratum of the human-experiential signature of F1+F2; none alone constitutes the squeeze; together they evidence the structural-ontological condition this thesis identifies.

## §8 — What the squeeze is and is not, in light of §VI.4

[PROSE-TENTATIVE]
> Recapping the structural diagnosis with the metaverse/universe squeeze proper now stated: the squeeze is the condition of a human jointly subject to two incommensurable real-level background environments — the universe (with biological-ecological-physical selection criteria, dynamic at the manifestation level under anthropogenic disturbance) and the metaverse (with computational-algorithmic-architectural selection criteria, dynamic at the laws level under deliberate engineering). The condition is structural-ontological; it would obtain even if no particular subject experienced distress, although it produces empirically-documented distress patterns across psychosomatic, economic, and political-governance registers.
>
> The squeeze is not technology-anxiety; it is not nostalgia for the pre-digital era; it is not a moral position about the metaverse's existence. It is the structural condition of being a creature whose biological substrate retains universe-originated selection criteria while whose social-cognitive participation increasingly satisfies metaverse-side criteria, with no single optimization that would satisfy both simultaneously and no third option available in principle.
