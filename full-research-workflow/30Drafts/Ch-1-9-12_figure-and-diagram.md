## Figure 1: The Operating-System Spectrum (Ch-1-9)

### What the Figure Should Show

The "operating-system" spectrum is an analogy: every organism operates within an environment according to governing principles — an "operating system" that determines what inputs are recognized, what outputs are possible, and what constitutes fitness. The spectrum runs from the simplest organisms (operating systems with minimal complexity) to human worlds (operating systems of maximal complexity and cultural specificity).

**Spectrum layout (left to right):**

```
SIMPLE ORGANISMS ←————————————————————————→ HUMAN COMMUNITIES
[Bacterium]   [Ant colony]   [Primate group]   [Agricultural world]   [Industrial world]
OS: metabolic  OS: pheromonal  OS: social          OS: seasonal/ritual    OS: market/technical
    chemistry     signaling      hierarchy           calendar + subsistence  + legal + symbolic
```

**Key properties illustrated by the spectrum:**

1. **Increasing complexity of the operating system**: As organisms become more complex, the operating system governing their relationship to the environment becomes more elaborate. A bacterium's OS is its metabolic chemistry; an ant colony's OS includes pheromonal signaling and caste differentiation; a primate group's OS includes social hierarchy and proto-symbolic communication; a human world's OS includes language, law, ritual, built environment, and technical inheritance.

2. **Increasing mediation**: The more complex the OS, the more mediated the organism's relationship to the raw physical environment. A bacterium interacts with its environment through direct chemical contact. A human community interacts with the universe through a dense medium of artifice — the world. The world is the OS through which the universe's inputs are processed and human outputs are organized.

3. **The OS is the world**: For human communities, the operating system IS the world — the human-made meaning-space that mediates all contact with the universe. The world is not an optional accessory; it is the condition of biological survival for a world-making species (Stiegler's Epimetheus argument: the human was "forgotten" in the distribution of natural capacities and compensates through technical inheritance).

4. **The metaverse as OS update**: The metaverse represents an attempted update to the human OS — one that is not initiated by the human community from within its existing world but is being installed from outside, by platforms and algorithmic systems, on terms the community did not collectively choose. The metaphor of an OS update captures both the disruption (the new OS overrides existing functionality) and the asymmetry (the update is imposed by external actors rather than arising from the community's own developmental logic).

### Explanatory Prose for the Figure

> Every organism operates within its environment according to governing principles that determine what inputs it recognizes, what responses are possible, and what constitutes fitness. For the simplest organisms — bacteria, archaea, unicellular eukaryotes — these governing principles are metabolic: the chemistry of the cell membrane, the energy conversion pathways, the mechanisms of reproduction. For social organisms, the governing principles extend to behavioral repertoires: the pheromonal signaling of ant colonies, the social hierarchy of primate groups, the territorial and communicative conventions of complex animal societies.
>
> For human communities, the governing principles constitute what this thesis calls the world: an operating system of maximal complexity and cultural specificity, organized not only through biological inheritance but through the accumulated technical, linguistic, institutional, and symbolic inheritance of previous generations. The world is the medium through which the universe's inputs are processed and human outputs are organized. It is the condition under which biological survival is possible for a species that was, as Stiegler following the Epimetheus myth observes, "forgotten" in the original distribution of natural capacities — a species whose constitutive lack is compensated through the prosthetic inheritance of technical objects and cultural practice.
>
> [FIGURE 1: Operating-system spectrum from bacterium → ant colony → primate group → agricultural world → industrial world. Each position on the spectrum is labeled with its governing OS characteristics. Below the spectrum: a gradient bar showing "increasing mediation between organism and universe."]
>
> The significance of this spectrum for the thesis's argument is that the metaverse represents a qualitative shift in the nature of the update: not the organic development of the human OS from within an existing world (as agriculture updated the hunter-gatherer OS, and as industrialization updated the agricultural OS), but the installation of a new OS by external actors on terms the community did not collectively determine. All prior OS updates were endogenous: they arose from within the existing world as adaptive responses to changed conditions in the universe. The metaverse's OS update is exogenous: it is imposed by platform architecture and algorithmic design on a community that has not chosen it and cannot fully audit its parameters.

## Figure 2: World Nested in Universe — With Virtuality Located (Ch-1-12)

### The Committee's Specific Question

Mi asked: "world在universe裡的話，virtuality在哪裡？" — if world is nested inside universe, where does virtuality sit? This question is philosophically important because:
- If virtuality is inside the world (which is inside the universe), then virtual things are fully real (in the universe) via their material substrate
- If virtuality somehow exits the universe, that is ontologically impossible by the thesis's own framework
- The question is whether the metaverse introduces a *new* virtual layer alongside the world, or whether it is a *part* of the existing world structure

### Where Virtuality Sits: The Answer to Mi's Question

**Before the metaverse (the standard world picture):**

Virtuality is *inside the world*, which is *inside the universe*. Virtuality — ritual, symbol, fiction, game, imagination, protocol — is a dimension of the world: the dimension in which the world operates beyond the merely physical, organizing existence according to principles of meaning and value that are not physically necessary but are materially embodied. Gorilla chest-beating is virtual in this sense: it stands in for combat, substituting symbolic action for physical contact. Human language is virtual: the word "water" is not water but stands in for it within a system of meaning. All of this virtuality is materially grounded: it exists through the physical acts of bodies, the material substrate of symbolic objects, the biological capacity for imagination.

Virtuality in this pre-metaverse sense is *world-immanent* and *universe-anchored*: it is always operated by embodied beings on the universe's physical substrate, under the universe's biological and ecological constraints. The virtual is the meaningful dimension of the world, not an exit from the universe.

**After the metaverse's emergence:**

The metaverse is not simply an expansion of virtuality within the world. It is an attempt — so far successful — to *extract* the management of virtuality from the world and transfer it to algorithmically controlled platforms. The key transformation:

- *Previously*: virtuality is managed by the community within the world (ritual is community-maintained; symbol is culturally transmitted; fiction is communally produced)
- *Now*: virtuality is managed by platforms outside the community's control (the social archive is algorithmic; cultural memory is platform-curated; shared fiction is IP-controlled)

What the metaverse does is not add a new layer of virtuality *beside* the world (within the universe) but *displace* the community's management of its own virtuality and transfer that management to a second environment with its own governing logic. The diagram shows this as the world *shrinking* and the metaverse *expanding* — not because the universe shrinks, but because the world's capacity to organize virtuality on its own terms is being progressively transferred to the metaverse's platform administration.

**Answer to Mi's question ("world在universe裡的話，virtuality在哪裡？"):**

> Virtuality is always inside the universe — it cannot exit the universe, because the universe is the totality of what exists. In the standard picture, virtuality is managed by the world: the human community's organized dimension of meaning-making, which exists within the universe as the meaningful dimension of human habitation. The metaverse does not create a new *outside* the universe; it creates a new *second environment* within the universe — an environment in which the management of virtuality is administered by platform architecture and algorithmic design rather than by the community's own world-making practice. Virtuality remains in the universe; what changes is *who administers it* and *under what governing logic*.

### Explanatory Prose for Figure 2

> The thesis's central ontological claim — that the world is nested within the universe — can be made spatially precise. The universe is the totality of what exists: the physical, chemical, and biological substrate of all possible existence. Within the universe, human communities construct the world: the meaning-space through which they organize existence into habitable form. The world is smaller in extent than the universe but not in functionality: everything the world contains is materially supported by the universe, but the world organizes that support according to principles of meaning and value that are not derivable from physical laws alone.
>
> Within the world, there is a further dimension: *virtuality* — the world's capacity for symbolic, ritual, fictional, and imaginative action. The gorilla's chest-beat, the human ceremony, the shared narrative, the legal fiction, the digital game: all of these are virtual in the sense that they stand in for something else, operate according to rules that exceed physical necessity, and are maintained through communal practice rather than physical compulsion. Virtuality is the meaningful dimension of the world — the dimension in which the world exceeds mere physical constraint. It is always materially grounded: virtuality is performed by bodies, encoded in material objects, transmitted through biological and cultural inheritance.
>
> [FIGURE 2a: Three concentric rings: Universe (outermost) → World (middle) → Virtuality (innermost, within World). Labels: Universe = "physical/biological laws; given"; World = "human-made meaning-space; nested in Universe; maintained through practice"; Virtuality = "symbolic/ritual/fictional/imaginative dimension of World; materially grounded".]
>
> The metaverse does not add a new ring to this diagram. It is not a fourth, outermost layer beyond the universe (which is ontologically impossible: the universe contains everything) nor simply a new form of virtuality within the world. The metaverse is a second environment emerging *alongside* the world — within the universe, but not nested within the world's governing structure. Its virtuality is not managed by the community through world-internal practice but by platform architecture through algorithmic administration. This produces a structural split:
>

>
> To Mi's question — "if world is nested inside universe, where does virtuality sit?" — the answer is: virtuality is always inside the universe. What the metaverse changes is not the ontological location of virtuality but its *administrative governance*: who manages what communities share, remember, and imagine, and under what logic.
