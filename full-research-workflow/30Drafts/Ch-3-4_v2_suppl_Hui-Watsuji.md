## Paragraph A: Hui *Recursivity and Contingency* — The Posthuman Reflexive Extension

*[Insert into §A3 after the Hui 2016 paragraph; before the Viveiros de Castro paragraph.]*

Hui's subsequent work *Recursivity and Contingency* extends the cosmotechnics thesis into the contemporary AI era and supplies what the *Question Concerning Technology in China* leaves underdeveloped: an account of how the Modern Western technospheric mode reproduces and accelerates itself in algorithmic form [VERIFY: T0 | b324ee9a | Hui notebook 47ee1eac | nlm-excerpt: "organized inorganic — organizing inorganic" formulation, algorithmic governmentality extending Stiegler's tertiary protention into AI governance; locate exact pages in Zotero PDF — [LOOKUP-ZOTERO: Hui 2019, *Recursivity and Contingency*, Rowman & Littlefield International, ISBN to confirm]]. The decisive conceptual move in *Recursivity and Contingency* is the shift from what Hui calls the **organized inorganic** — the classical cybernetic and computational medium: fixed-architecture hardware processing representations — to the **organizing inorganic**: a computational medium that reorganizes its own architecture in response to feedback, instantiated in machine learning systems, adaptive algorithms, and AI-driven infrastructure. The thesis's metaverse-stratum is paradigmatically an *organizing inorganic* system: not merely a tool that processes inputs but a medium that restructures the conditions under which humans generate inputs. This is the posthuman reflexive turn that the thesis's Ch-3-4 argument requires and that neither the 2016 cosmotechnics book nor Stiegler's *tertiary retention* framework (developed prior to the deep-learning era) fully captures.

Hui frames this transition through what he terms **algorithmic governmentality** — extending Foucault's biopolitical analysis and Stiegler's *tertiary protention* into the AI governance context: machine learning systems do not merely capture existing intentional orientations (*secondary retentions*) but actively pre-configure the temporal horizons within which human intention forms [VERIFY: T0 | b324ee9a | page TBD]. The cosmopolitical implication — the one that makes *Recursivity and Contingency* directly relevant here — is the proposal for **技術多様性 (technodiversity)**: a positive programme of multiple, cosmologically differentiated computational architectures in place of a single algorithmic universalism. Hui's technodiversity is the AI-era correlate of his cosmotechnics plurality thesis: just as there have been and could be multiple cosmotechnics rather than one, there could be multiple species of organizing-inorganic medium rather than one dominant architecture. This positive programme converges structurally with Obara Hideo's **高次の自然さ / 第四世界** (higher-order naturalness / fourth-world) proposals as a corrective to 自己ペット化 within the artificial ecosystem — both refuse the rejection of technology as such in favour of insisting on adequate *plurality* within it. That convergence is significant: it is reached from entirely independent analytical starting points (evolutionary ecology vs. philosophy of technology), strengthening the thesis's claim that the pathology is real and that the corrective direction is not merely one thinker's preference.

## Paragraph B: Watsuji + Husserl Option β — The Phenomenological Grounding

*[Insert into §A3 after the Islamic science / Poskett paragraph; before the counter-evidence sub-section.]*

The comparative cosmotechnics argument — that different civilisational modes embed distinct metaphysical orientations into their technical practices — requires a phenomenological complement: an account of how these different orientations are *lived* rather than merely proposable from the philosophical outside. The phenomenological complement is needed to answer an objection that the cosmotechnics frame does not by itself resolve: even if one grants that different traditions have different cosmological orientations toward technology, how does the *particular human being embedded in a particular climate-habitat* experience and sustain that orientation? This question is Watsuji Tetsurō's contribution.

Watsuji's *A Climate: A Philosophical Study* (1961 [VERIFY: confirmed | watsujiClimatePhilosophicalStudy1961 | p.TBD | nlm-excerpt: "Climate, then, is the agent by which human life is objectivised" and *fūdo* distinguished from "natural environment" as "expressions of subjective human existence" — NLM source UUID 5c9f4410 (A-Sources c68ca15e); see also Janz commentary, UUID fce95d0b, for the 1943 revision risk]) develops **風土 (fūdo)** — climate or climate-habitat — not as a naturalistic concept (not mere weather or geography) but as the field within which human existence discovers and objectivises itself. *Fūdo* is the *between* in which self-understanding takes shape: not the environment as external given, but the environment as the medium through which the self becomes legible to itself.^[The 1943 revision of the original 1935 text introduced nationalist inflections that Watsuji scholars flag as compromising; Brian Janz's commentary establishes which passages belong to which stratum. The *fūdo* concept itself precedes and survives the revision; cite with care using Janz as companion.] This is philosophically significant for the thesis in two ways. First, it shows that the *world* — the human-constructed meaning-space — is not an abstract cultural system hovering above material life but is always already a *climatically situated* world: condensed, particular, irreducibly localised. Second, the plurality of *fūdo* types (Watsuji's own analysis distinguishes monsoon, desert, and meadow climates as conditioning different modes of self-understanding) provides an *experienced* basis for the cosmotechnics plurality that Hui argues at the philosophical level — the different cosmotechnics are different because they emerge from, and are sustained by, different *fūdo*, different fields of human self-discovery.

The connection is not merely coincidental. Watsuji's *fūdo* analysis was written in Berlin in 1927, the same year he encountered Heidegger's *Being and Time* — and he developed the *fūdo* concept partly as a corrective to what he found missing in Heidegger: the *spatial* and *climatic* situatedness of Dasein, which Heidegger subordinates to temporality.^[This 1927-Berlin convergence is also the moment of Husserl's *Crisis* response: Husserl was beginning the series of lectures that would become *The Crisis of European Sciences* (published posthumously 1954), in which he developed the concept of the *Lebenswelt* (lifeworld) as the pre-theoretical ground that scientific abstraction presupposes and forgets. The convergence of Watsuji's climatic situatedness and Husserl's Lebenswelt critique in 1927 Berlin is a strategic citation-link available to the thesis if needed — both diagnose the same erasure of the lived-experiential world by formal-universal abstraction, from different philosophical approaches. The thesis does not need to develop this link at length; one sentence in a footnote is sufficient to signal it for a philosophically informed reader.] The *fūdo* concept is therefore both a standalone philosophical contribution and a bridge between the phenomenological tradition Husserl was developing in German-language philosophy and the non-European modes of world-construction that the cosmotechnics argument needs to ground in experienced rather than merely proposed plurality.

## Connecting Note: Obara's 風土 and Watsuji's 風土

Both Obara and Watsuji use 風土 as a normative concept, with slightly different but convergent senses:

| | Obara 風土 | Watsuji 風土 |
|--|-----------|------------|
| **Level** | Ecological — the locally differentiated, co-evolved form of healthy 人工生態系 | Phenomenological — the climate-habitat as field of human self-objectification |
| **Function** | Criterion of adequacy: 人工生態系 should remain adequate to local 風土 | Constitution of world: 風土 is how the world becomes particular |
| **Normative use** | Contrast with カプセル化 (capsulization that severs 風土 connection) | Contrast with universal Enlightenment abstraction (which erases 風土 particularism) |
| **Thesis use** | Supplies evolutionary-ecological warrant for geo-cultural variation in squeeze signatures | Supplies phenomenological warrant for why each 人工生態系 has irreducibly local character |

The convergence of these two independent uses of the same concept (Obara's biology-ecological and Watsuji's phenomenological) strengthens the thesis's claim that *world* as a category is irreducibly pluriform and situated — a claim the squeeze hypothesis needs in order to resist the "you are universalising from a parochial case" objection that Mi's challenge (A1 in Ch-4-1) and the comparative-breadth requirement (A3 in Ch-3-4) both raise.
