# Disciplinary Positioning Matrix — Locked Reference

> **Question this lock answers:** Where does this thesis sit in the academic landscape? For each adjacent field — what does it take, contest, supplement, displace, or remain agnostic toward?
>
> **Status:** `revision-staging`. Promotion to `canonical` happens after Phase C intro integrates these positioning claims into prose and D6 positioning-pass confirms no field-level gap remains.
>
> **Drives:** Phase C intro positioning prose; D6 positioning-pass audit; oral-defense answers to "where does this fit?"

## 1. Stance taxonomy (definitions used in this matrix)

| Stance | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Takes from** | The thesis adopts substantive content (concepts, evidence, analytical apparatus) from this field. Citation in maintext or substantive footnote. |
| **Supplements** | The thesis adds something this field does not currently have, but does so as a friendly extension. The field is broadly correct on its own terms; the thesis identifies what it does not yet reach. |
| **Contests** | The thesis disagrees with a substantive claim or framing of this field. The disagreement is principled, not merely scope-based. |
| **Displaces** | The thesis offers an alternative whose adoption would render the field's central framing inadequate. Stronger than contests. |
| **Agnostic toward** | The thesis neither adopts nor rejects this field's framings; the field operates at a scale or on a question the thesis does not engage. Disclosed for transparency. |
| **Refuses methodologically (uses substantively)** | The thesis uses the field's empirical observations or local concepts substantively but rejects its methodological commitments. |
| **Background ancestor only** | The field/figure is genealogically present in resources the thesis does adopt, but not a methodological co-equal. |

A single field can have multiple stances simultaneously (e.g., Critical Theory: takes-from substantively + supplements + contests anthropological framing). The matrix below records all stances per field.

## 3. Detailed entries by family

### Family I — Continental philosophy of modernity & technology

#### 1. Frankfurt School / Critical Theory (Adorno, Horkheimer, Marcuse)

**Stance:** Takes from substantively + Supplements + Contests anthropological framing.

**Takes:** The original totalization thesis — Enlightenment rationality, when fully industrialized, becomes its own opposite, producing closed-circle administrative reason that absorbs critique. Marcuse's *One-Dimensional Man* on technological rationality. Bhaskar in *Plato Etc.* (2009) reads the Frankfurt School through critical realism, identifying the Platonic-Aristotelian fault line as the deeper philosophical structure that the Frankfurt School diagnosed at the level of culture and political economy.

**Supplements:** With the *environmental / evolutionary* dimension. Critical Theory analyzes the *content* of social relations within one environment (the late-modern industrial-administrative one); the thesis adds a second-environment claim — the metaverse imposes adaptational pressures incommensurable with those of the universe-and-world environment Critical Theory analyzes.

**Contests:** Critical Theory's *anthropological* framing of domination-as-power-relation. The squeeze is *not* a power relation; it is an *ontological position* — being between two kinds of background environment with incommensurable laws. Following Uegaki, the diagnostic is structural-ecological, not subject-experiential. (See `squeeze-ontology.md` §4 anti-metaphorical safeguards: squeeze ≠ alienation; squeeze ≠ domination.)

**Authors / Citations:** Adorno & Horkheimer 1944; Marcuse 1964, 1998 ed. Kellner; Bhaskar *Plato Etc.* 2009.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 1 (§3); squeeze-ontology §4 anti-metaphorical safeguards; III-6 (Bhaskar reading of Frankfurt fault line).

#### 2. Stiegler (general organology and pharmacology)

**Stance:** Takes from substantively.

**Takes:** Human form of life is constituted by its technical inheritance ("the fault of Epimetheus"). Tertiary retention; grammatization; generalized proletarianization (savoir-faire → savoir-vivre → savoir-théoriser); disruption.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 1; §II on technical inheritance; §V on grammatization; VI on disruption / fragility.

**Citation:** Stiegler, *Technics and Time, 1* (1998 — `YPJ94B8W`); later works cited as needed.

#### 3. Illich (radical monopoly / conviviality)

**Stance:** Takes from substantively (principal normative resource for the conclusion).

**Takes:** Institutions become radical monopolies when they make alternative ways of satisfying a need impossible — not when they outcompete alternatives but when they restructure the environment so alternatives no longer exist. *Tools for conviviality* as a positive program.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 1; VI-8 (singularization of livelihood); conclusion (tools for conviviality as alternative trajectory; cosmotechnics of co-becoming / Kyosei).

**Citation:** Illich, *Tools for Conviviality* (1973 — acquisition queued); *Deschooling Society* (1971); *Medical Nemesis* (1976); *Shadow Work* (1981 — acquisition queued).

#### 4. Hui (cosmotechnics / recursivity)

**Stance:** Takes from substantively.

**Takes:** No "technology in general"; technologies are always already cosmotechnical, embedded in particular cultural-cosmological frames. The metaverse as the *organizing inorganic* — an inorganic system that has acquired its own self-organizational dynamics. Negentropy / cosmotechnical framing of the diagnostic register.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 1; §II on cosmotechnical plurality; §V on the organizing inorganic.

**Citation:** Hui, *The Question Concerning Technology in China* (2016 — verify Zotero); *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019 — verify).

#### 5. Heidegger

**Stance:** Background ancestor only.

**Position:** Heidegger is genealogically present in Stiegler and Hui, both of whom the thesis adopts. But the thesis's methodology is *not* Heideggerian: no phenomenological-ontological method; no history-of-Being framing; no anti-realism about structures; no anti-naturalism about the human. Heidegger appears only as background to Stiegler's lineage in §II. Squeeze-ontology §4 explicitly distinguishes the squeeze from Heideggerian *Enframing* / *Gestell* (anthropological focus on Dasein vs. squeeze's structural-environmental focus).

**Where in thesis:** Footnote-level mention only; squeeze-ontology §4 anti-metaphorical safeguards.

#### 6. Baudrillard

**Stance:** Takes from substantively (in §IIB on virtuality) + Refuses methodologically.

**Takes:** The third-order simulacrum supports the thesis's rejection of "virtual = simulation of real." Useful local conceptual apparatus.

**Refuses:** Baudrillard's anti-realism — incompatible with Developmental Emergent Realism. The third-order simulacrum is read as *describing a phenomenon at the empirical level*, not as supplanting the real/actual/empirical stratification.

**Where in thesis:** §IIB on virtuality.

### Family II — Critical realism and its neighbours

#### 7. Critical Realism (Bhaskar tradition)

**Stance:** **The position itself.** "Developmental Emergent Realism" is critical realism, renamed for legibility (per A.4 round-2 declutter). Native vocabulary preserved as Version A footnote translation.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §1 position; A.4 §2 Version A footnote (native CR vocabulary); III-6 (Platonic-Aristotelian fault line via Bhaskar); V-2 (real/actual distinction); the entire methodology operates on CR.

**Citations:** Bhaskar, *A Realist Theory of Science* (1975 / 2008 ed. — `bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008`); *The Possibility of Naturalism* (1979 — `WAX2KNPQ`); *Plato Etc.* (2009 — `88YLH48Y`); *Dialectic* (2008 — `bhaskarDialecticPulseFreedom2008`).

#### 8. Morphogenetic theory (Archer)

**Stance:** Takes from as part of the position (the *developmental* commitment in §1).

**Takes:** Structural conditioning → action → reproduction or transformation → morphogenesis (or morphostasis) → next round of conditioning. This is the temporal-developmental complement to Bhaskar's stratified ontology and what makes the thesis's CR genuinely diachronic.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §4 Move 1 (diachronic retroduction).

**Citations:** Archer, *Realist Social Theory* (1995 — VERIFY/acquire); *Structure, Agency, and the Internal Conversation* (2003 — VERIFY/acquire).

#### 9. Polanyian emergence / boundary-condition theory

**Stance:** Takes from as part of the position (the *emergentist* commitment in §1).

**Takes:** Boundary conditions and dual control as the formal mechanism by which higher-level organizations emerge from and govern lower-level dynamics without reducing to them. This permits universe / world / metaverse to be hierarchically nested while remaining causally distinct.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §4 Move 1; S1-3, S1-4.

**Citations:** Polanyi, *Personal Knowledge* (1958); *The Tacit Dimension* (1966 — three block-quotes already in DOCX, acquisition needed).

### Family III — Eco-anthropological history & thought

#### 10. Japanese Synthetic Anthropology (`総合人間學 / sōgō ningengaku`)

**Stance:** Takes from substantively as anchor of A.4 Resource 2.

**Takes:** Obara's self-domestication thesis (`自己家畜化論`) carrying the evolutionary dimension; Uegaki's structural typology of self-completing society (`自己完結社会`), the Drifter (`漂流人`), and the ethics of non-intervention (`不介入の倫理`) carrying the civilizational-philosophical dimension; Miura's intellectual history of environmental thought as a distinct register from Western environmental philosophy.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 2; §I (Obara on self-domestication); §IV (Uegaki on self-completing society and ethics of non-intervention); §III (Miura on comparative intellectual history).

**Citations:** Obara — `MWBGTIFF` (1963), `2Q8KYMHC` (2000), `6L278LXZ` (1995, with Hani); Uegaki — `VESTLJGF` (2022a), `CYAAA3IK` (2022b); Miura — `NUF7WCJD` (2006).

#### 11. Comparative anthropology (Descola, Viveiros de Castro)

**Stance:** Takes from substantively.

**Takes:** Descola's four ontologies (animism, totemism, analogism, naturalism) — Modern Western "naturalism" is one option, not the default. Viveiros de Castro's Amazonian perspectivism / multinaturalism as a genuine alternative to Western multiculturalism.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 2; §III (comparative breadth, addressing Ma's concern).

**Citations:** Descola, *Beyond Nature and Culture* (2013 — VERIFY/acquire); Viveiros de Castro, *Cannibal Metaphysics* (2014 — VERIFY/acquire); DOCX already cites Viveiros de Castro at line 179.

#### 12. Big History / Civilizational Studies

**Stance:** Takes from substantively for the deep-time evidence base.

**Takes:** Graeber and Wengrow on plural deep histories of *Homo sapiens* — refuting linear-stage accounts and demonstrating wide variety of social, political, economic forms across deep time. Scott on the deep history of agrarian state-formation and the archaeology of state-modernist legibility.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 Resource 2; §I (deep-time anchor); §III (state-modernist legibility critique parallels squeeze analysis).

**Citations:** Graeber & Wengrow, *The Dawn of Everything* (2021 — VERIFY/acquire); Scott, *Seeing Like a State* (1998), *Against the Grain* (2017 — VERIFY/acquire).

#### 13. Decolonial / Global South theory

**Stance:** Aligned (supplements). The thesis's commitments are broadly compatible with decolonial thought; not adopted as a *primary* methodological frame because the thesis works through anthropological pluralism rather than through colonial-epistemic critique specifically.

**Position note:** The thesis explicitly refuses Western-Modernity-as-default through Resource 2 (Japanese Synthetic Anthropology + plural anthropology). Mignolo, Quijano, Dussel are aligned interlocutors but not load-bearing citations. The thesis's distinctive contribution is *ontological-civilizational* rather than *anti-colonial-epistemic*: the squeeze affects Western and non-Western alike, but its origins are in Western Modernity's specific disposition to dominate.

**Strategic note:** This positioning needs explicit articulation in the body to forestall the "why not decolonial?" question from any reader trained in that field. A short footnote in §III suffices.

#### 14. Environmental Humanities

**Stance:** Aligned (supplements).

**Takes implicitly:** Anthropocene framing; multi-species attention; planetary-scale ecological consciousness.

**Supplements:** Environmental Humanities focuses predominantly on the human-natural-environment relation; the thesis adds the human-metaverse-environment relation as a structurally analogous but ontologically distinct second case. The squeeze is positioned *between* (not against) Anthropocene critique and digital-environment critique.

**Where in thesis:** Resource 2 carries this implicitly through Obara (eco-anthropology) and Scott (state-environmental history); §VI on fragility-theoretic register has Anthropocene affinities.

### Family IV — Technology-and-society fields

#### 15. STS / Science and Technology Studies

**Stance:** Takes from + Supplements + Contests strong constructivism / flat ontology.

**Takes:** Path-dependency analysis of technological-historical contingency (David, Arthur). Social construction of technology (SCOT — Pinch, Bijker) as a useful local framing.

**Supplements:** STS is predominantly synchronic and microscopic in scale (specific technologies, specific moments, specific controversies). The thesis supplements with diachronic-civilizational analysis: dispositions across deep time at the level of background environments, not specific artifacts.

**Contests:**
- *Strong constructivism* (the squeeze is real, not socially constructed all the way down — Developmental Emergent Realism takes a non-relativist position about the structure being analyzed)
- *ANT's flat ontology* (the thesis maintains stratification: universe, world, metaverse are not symmetrically networked actors; they are nested kinds of background environment with distinct generative dispositions)

**Where in thesis:** A.3 diagnosis (A) path-dependency; I-4 contemporary evidence (Apple Vision Pro, Meta Horizon Worlds); §V on metaverse ontological status.

**Note:** STS readers should find the path-dependency analysis recognizable; the supplement is the structural-civilizational scale; the contest is the realist commitment.

#### 16. Media Theory / Communication Studies

**Stance:** Takes from substantively + Supplements.

**Takes:** Baudrillard on the third-order simulacrum (substantively, with methodological refusal of his anti-realism). McLuhan as background ancestor on technology-as-environment, but not load-bearing.

**Supplements:** Media theory's content-and-form focus with environmental-ontological framing — the metaverse is not a *medium* in McLuhan's sense; it is a *background environment* in the sense Resource 1 (Hui's organizing inorganic) and Resource 2 (Obara's self-domestication continuum) jointly articulate.

**Where in thesis:** §IIB (virtuality); §V (metaverse ontological status).

#### 17. Virtual Worlds / Metaverse Studies (emerging field)

**Stance:** Contests + Displaces.

**Contests:** Substrate-bound definitions (XR = metaverse). The thesis's central argumentative move is the empirical/real distinction in the 3×3 stratification (`method-frame.md` §5): the metaverse at the empirical level is XR, but the metaverse at the real level is the underlying generative disposition of self-completing artificiality. Substrate-bound definitions misidentify the empirical with the real (this is precisely the (C) actualistic-inquiry foreclosure named in `diagnoses-set.md`).

**Displaces:** The industry-academia-regulator closure that treats the metaverse as a specimen of technology to be analyzed by interlocking disciplines (this is the (B) reductionism+fragmentation foreclosure named in `diagnoses-set.md`). The thesis's reframing — metaverse as structural reorganization of the universe-world relation that *uses* technology as one of its substrates — would render specimen-frame analysis inadequate.

**Where in thesis:** A.3 diagnoses (A), (B), (C); I-3 (metaverse framework deferred); I-4 (April 2026 retrospective on Meta Horizon Worlds collapse); §V (metaverse ontological status).

#### 18. AI Ethics / Algorithmic Studies

**Stance:** Takes from + Contests ethics-as-deployment-rules framing + Supplements.

**Takes:** Empirical observations about algorithmic governance harms; documentation of AI deployment (industrial digital twins, AI-spatial platforms, recommendation systems).

**Contests:** The framing of AI as a tool with ethical guardrails to be designed in. The thesis's claim is structural: AI is part of self-completing artificiality, not a separable ethical object. "AI ethics" within the existing paradigm cannot reach the squeeze claim because it presupposes the specimen-frame foreclosure (B) — AI as one technology among others to be made compliant.

**Supplements:** AI ethics's mostly procedural-deontological framing with civilizational-structural analysis. The thesis is not against AI ethics; it is positioned upstream of it (the squeeze is the structural condition under which AI ethics is a downstream concern).

**Where in thesis:** §IV / §V on algorithmic governance; cross-reference to A.3 (B) specimen-frame closure.

### Family V — Philosophy of mind and humanity

#### 19. Posthumanism

**Stance:** Takes from + Contests posthuman triumphalism + Supplements with squeeze framing.

**Takes:** Anti-humanist commitment to decentering human exceptionalism (Haraway, Hayles, Braidotti); attention to multi-species and human-tech entanglements; non-anthropocentric framings.

**Contests:** Posthuman *triumphalism* — the celebratory reading of the dissolution of the human-nature boundary. The thesis is *not* celebrating the becoming-all-encompassing of artificiality; it is diagnosing fragility, asymmetric risk transfer, and the loss of variety. Where posthumanism often welcomes the breakdown of ontological boundaries, the thesis names that breakdown as the squeeze and analyzes its costs.

**Supplements:** Posthumanist attention to technical-ontological questions with the retroductive analysis of dispositional structures. Posthumanism describes the dissolution; the thesis explains it (as the dispositional logic of self-completing artificiality) and names what is at stake (the squeeze).

**Where in thesis:** §II (self-domestication continuum and posthuman becoming); §IV (transhumanism and self-completion); I-1 partially engages this.

**Note:** This positioning needs explicit articulation in I-1 or §II; currently implicit.

#### 20. Transhumanism

**Stance:** Contests as *symptom* of self-completing artificiality, not as enemy.

**Position:** The thesis is critical of transhumanism, but not as an external opponent. Transhumanism — the project of human enhancement through technical augmentation, life-extension, mind-uploading, etc. — is read as a *characteristic expression* of the same disposition (self-completing artificiality) that the thesis diagnoses. Transhumanism is a *symptom*, not a separate position to refute.

**Contests:** The transhumanist self-understanding that augmentation is a project of liberation from biological constraint. The thesis reads it as continuous with the squeeze — the artificial environment progressively absorbing the biological substrate to which it should adapt.

**Where in thesis:** §IV / §V; in dialogue with §VI fragility-theoretic register (transhumanism as the maximal expression of fragility-transfer).

#### 21. Cognitive Science / 4E Cognition (embodied / embedded / enacted / extended)

**Stance:** **Agnostic toward** (disclosed for transparency).

**Position:** The thesis's "emergentist" commitment in the position name does NOT claim a 4E-cognition lineage; the thesis works at civilizational-systemic scale, not at the scale of individual cognition. There is no engagement with cognitive science literature on embodiment / enaction in this thesis.

**Why disclose:** Readers familiar with 4E cognition may expect the thesis to engage that field given the "emergent" branding. Explicit agnosticism prevents misreading. The thesis's emergence is Polanyi-Bhaskar-Archer-flavored (multi-level structural emergence), not Varela-Maturana-Thompson-flavored (autopoietic-cognitive emergence).

**Possible bridge (not pursued):** Stiegler's tertiary retention has cognitive-science-adjacent claims about distributed cognition, but the thesis uses Stiegler at the cultural-evolutionary level, not the individual-cognitive level.

### Family VI — Epistemology and philosophy of inquiry

#### 22. Pragmatism (Dewey, Peirce, James)

**Stance:** Takes from + Aligned.

**Takes:** Ideal-types as kin to Dewey's working hypotheses and Peirce's abductive constructs (per A.4 §4 Move 3). The pragmatist orientation — concepts evaluated by analytical fruits, not by correspondence — is operationalized in the thesis's ideal-typical commitment.

**Aligned:** The thesis's declared revisability of conceptual constructions is broadly pragmatist. No core methodological disagreement.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §4 Move 3 (ideal-typical analysis).

#### 23. Virtue Epistemology (Sosa, Greco; Mi's tradition)

**Stance:** Aligned (compatible) + Strategic affordance.

**Aligned:** The thesis's declared revisability and methodological humility are compatible with virtue epistemology's commitments to intellectual humility and reflective endorsement. Retroduction is not claimed as certainty-yielding but as inference-to-the-best-explanation, open to revision on the strength of evidence — straightforwardly virtue-epistemically apt.

**Strategic affordance** (rhetorical, per A.4 §10): elevating ideal-typical analysis surfaces the thesis's epistemic discipline in a register Mi's training engages without prior commitment to the CR vocabulary. **No unique-virtue claim is made** — this is display vs. enact, not substance vs. lack thereof.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §10 Strategic Notes.

**Citation candidates (verification needed):** Sosa, *Knowing Full Well* (2011); Greco; Mi's published work on reflective intellectual virtues.

#### 24. Confucian philosophy

**Stance:** Aligned (Mi's third tradition) + Disclosed strategic.

**Position:** Mi works at the intersection of analytic virtue epistemology, pragmatism, and Confucian ethics. The thesis does not adopt Confucian methodology but is broadly compatible with Confucian commitments to: (a) cultivated reflective practice (kin to virtue-epistemic reflective endorsement), (b) the relational situatedness of the inquirer (kin to the thesis's rejection of view-from-nowhere), (c) the productive role of self-cultivation in inquiry. These compatibilities are noted strategically but not load-bearing.

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §10 Strategic Notes (Mi-strategic considerations).

**Where in thesis:** A.4 §1 position; A.4 §3 Resource 1 (Hui's organizing inorganic bridges to CAS framing); throughout the squeeze argument.

**Note:** Complexity theory is *part of* the position (CAS framing in A.4 §1), not an external resource. This entry exists to position the thesis legibly to systems-thinking readers.
