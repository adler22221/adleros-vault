## 1. Position name and one-paragraph statement

**Developmental Emergent Realism.**

The thesis adopts a position that is simultaneously **realist** (real structures and dispositions are causally efficacious independent of observation), **stratified** (real, actual, and empirical can be distinguished as access modes for any level of being), **emergentist** (higher levels of organization are not reducible to lower ones; new generative dispositions appear at each level), and **developmental** (the dispositions of interest are themselves processes that have unfolded across deep historical time, iteratively reshaping the environments to which they then must adapt).

The thesis argues from observable phenomena (the cataloged signs of human malaise under late artificiality, set out in §VI) to a hypothesis about the underlying generative structure that best explains them — what philosophers of science call *inference to the best explanation* (and what critical realism calls *retroduction*). Because the hypothesized structure is itself a developmental process, the analysis is necessarily *diachronic*: the historical trajectory by which a mechanism has come to operate is itself part of what the analysis must reconstruct, since the *direction* of that trajectory grounds the diagnostic claims about where it is heading.

### Version B — Main text (~400 words, analytic-friendly common-sense continental)

> This thesis adopts a multi-level, developmental, systems-oriented approach to its object. I call the position **Developmental Emergent Realism**: it is realist about generative structures, stratified about the modes by which we access them, emergentist about the irreducibility of higher-level organizations, and developmental about the deep-historical trajectories by which dispositions have come to operate. At every layer of analysis I distinguish between the *underlying structures and dispositions* that produce phenomena (whether or not they are observed), the *events* those structures generate (whether or not they are recorded), and the *observations* through which we come to know about any of this. This is a familiar distinction in the philosophy of science: it underwrites the difference between, say, the laws of thermodynamics (which obtain regardless of observation), the heat events those laws produce, and the temperature readings by which we come to know about them. The argumentative strategy is to argue from observed phenomena (the cataloged signs of human malaise under late artificiality, set out in §VI) to a hypothesis about the underlying structure that best explains them — what philosophers of science call inference to the best explanation. Because the hypothesized structure is itself a developmental process — a disposition that has unfolded across deep historical time, iteratively reshaping the environments to which it then has to adapt — the analysis is necessarily diachronic. The objects of analysis (organisms, worlds, civilizations, the metaverse) are treated as complex adaptive systems whose characteristic dynamics — self-organization, emergence at higher levels of organization, scale-recurrent patterns — are not artifacts of description but features of the phenomena themselves. The mode of critique is *forward-looking* rather than nostalgic: structurally fragile dispositions are criticized for what their continued operation will produce (risk transferred to weaker actors and to the future; loss of variety and adaptive capacity), not for falling short of an idealized past. The position's central concepts — universe, world, metaverse, squeeze, self-completing artificiality — are *ideal-typical constructions* in the Weberian sense: one-sided accentuations of points of view, formed by synthesizing concrete phenomena into unified analytical constructs designed to illuminate, not to exhaustively describe, the empirical multiplicity from which they are drawn. The synthesis draws on two substantive resources: (i) Continental philosophy of modernity and technology — Frankfurt School on instrumental reason; Stiegler on technical inheritance and proletarianization; Illich on radical monopoly; Hui on cosmotechnical plurality and the organizing inorganic — for the analysis of how technical artifacts and systems *constitute* (rather than merely supplement) the human form of life; and (ii) eco-anthropological history and thought — principally Japanese Synthetic Anthropology (`総合人間學 / sōgō ningengaku`: Obara on self-domestication; Uegaki on the structure of post-war Japanese society; Miura on the intellectual history of the human-environment relation), with corroboration from comparative anthropology and deep-time history (Graeber and Wengrow on plural deep histories; Descola on plural ontologies; Viveiros de Castro on perspectivism; Scott on the archaeology of state-modernist legibility) — for the empirical-historical specificity that the analysis applies *to*. The synthesis is unapologetically transdisciplinary because the squeeze cannot be analyzed within any single inherited discipline: no inherited discipline takes the universe-world-metaverse relation as its object.

### Version A — Footnote (~400 words, native critical-realist vocabulary, preserved verbatim from v4)

> The position articulated in the main text can be stated in its native critical-realist vocabulary as **diachronic critical realism oriented to complex civilizational emergence.** Three commitments organize the inquiry. *Ontologically*, the thesis adopts critical realism (Bhaskar, 2008; 2009): there are real structures, mechanisms, and dispositions whose causal powers operate transfactually — whether or not they are activated, and whether or not they are observed. At any level of being — the physical universe, the human world, the emerging metaverse — three domains of access can be distinguished: the *real* (structures and dispositions), the *actual* (events that occur), and the *empirical* (events as observed). The empirical is a subset of the actual, and the actual a subset of the real. This stratification of *access* is to be distinguished from any stratification of *being*: every level of being has its own real, actual, and empirical aspects (see §5 below for the corrected mapping that v1 of this thesis got wrong). *Methodologically*, the inquiry follows the morphogenetic approach (Archer, 1995) in treating its objects — worlds, modes of artificiality, the metaverse — as developmental *processes* rather than standing structures: structures *condition* action, action *reproduces or transforms* structures, the resulting morphogenesis (or morphostasis) constitutes the next round of structural conditioning. Polanyi's account of boundary conditions and dual control (Polanyi, 1966) supplies the formal model of how higher-level organizations emerge from and govern lower-level dynamics without reducing to them; this is what permits the universe / world / metaverse to be hierarchically nested without being hierarchically collapsed. *Inferentially*, the thesis proceeds by retroduction (Bhaskar) extended diachronically — from observable phenomena to the underlying disposition that, if real, would produce them, and from the disposition's reconstructed history to the structural condition the disposition continues to generate. Two further commitments organize the diagnostic register: fragility-theoretic analysis (Taleb's account of how complex systems accumulate hidden risks and asymmetrically transfer fragility downstream and downscale) and entropy-theoretic analysis (drawing on Stiegler and Hui's contemporary articulations of negentropy in technical and cosmotechnical registers). Both orient critique forward — toward what the disposition's continued operation will produce — rather than backward toward an idealized lost order, in deliberate refusal of the nostalgic-regressive pattern that mars much modernity-critique. The position deliberately omits the language of dialectics: the squeeze has dialectical *content* (it is a real contradiction without a guaranteed resolution mechanism) but the thesis does not use dialectical *form* (no triadic synthesis, no necessary supersession), and using weaker terms — *structural double-bind*, *incommensurability*, *adaptational contradiction* — preserves the analytical content while avoiding jurisdictional disputes between Hegelian, Marxist, and Bhaskarian dialectical traditions.

## 3. Two substantive resources

The position itself is realist-stratified-emergentist-developmental (§1). Beyond its own commitments, the thesis draws on two substantive resources for the *content* of its analysis. Critical realism, morphogenetic theory, and complex-systems thinking are *not* listed here as resources because they are the position itself, not external sources it draws on.

### Resource 1 — Continental philosophy of modernity and technology

Four streams, all taking the relationship between Western modernity and its technical apparatus as their primary diagnostic object:

- **Frankfurt School critical theory** (Adorno & Horkheimer 1944; Marcuse 1964, 1998 ed. Kellner): the original totalization thesis — Enlightenment rationality, when fully industrialized, becomes its own opposite, producing closed-circle administrative reason that absorbs critique. Used substantively in §III; Bhaskar in *Plato Etc.* (2009) reads the Frankfurt School through critical realism, identifying the Platonic-Aristotelian fault line as the deeper philosophical structure that the Frankfurt School diagnosed at the level of culture and political economy.
- **Bernard Stiegler's general organology and pharmacology** (*Technics and Time, 1*, 1998 — `YPJ94B8W`; later works on tertiary retention, grammatization, generalized proletarianization, disruption): the human form of life is constituted by its technical inheritance ("the fault of Epimetheus"); the metaverse is the contemporary form of the industrialization of tertiary retention.
- **Ivan Illich on radical monopoly and conviviality** (*Tools for Conviviality* 1973; *Deschooling Society* 1971; *Medical Nemesis* 1976; *Shadow Work* 1981 — acquisition needed): institutions become radical monopolies when they make alternative ways of satisfying a need impossible — not when they outcompete alternatives but when they restructure the environment so alternatives no longer exist. Used in §VI (singularization of livelihood, VI-8) and as the principal normative resource for the conclusion (tools for conviviality).
- **Yuk Hui on cosmotechnics and recursivity** (*The Question Concerning Technology in China* 2016; *Recursivity and Contingency* 2019 — VERIFY): there is no "technology in general"; technologies are always cosmotechnical, embedded in particular cultural-cosmological frames. The metaverse, on this account, is the *organizing inorganic* — an inorganic system that has acquired its own self-organizational dynamics.

**Heidegger** is the genealogical ancestor of Stiegler and Hui but not a methodological co-equal here (no phenomenological-ontological method; no history-of-Being framing; no anti-realism about structures). He appears in §II as background in Stiegler's lineage. **Baudrillard** is used substantively in §IIB on virtuality (the third-order simulacrum supports the rejection of "virtual = simulation of real") but not methodologically; his anti-realism is incompatible with Developmental Emergent Realism.

### Resource 2 — Eco-anthropological history and thought

This resource absorbs (i) the comparative-civilizational dimension Ma asked for, (ii) the deep-time evolutionary anchor that the dissolved 4E "Evolutionary" orientation carried, and (iii) the environmental-ecological registers that the dissolved "Environmental" and "Ecological" orientations carried. They are not separate tiers; they are dimensions of how this resource reads its objects.

#### Japanese Synthetic Anthropology (`総合人間學 / sōgō ningengaku`)

A transdisciplinary tradition combining biological-ecological, sociological-anthropological, and intellectual-historical-philosophical perspectives on the human-environment relation. Three figures:

- **Obara Hideo** (`小原秀雄`, `MWBGTIFF`, `2Q8KYMHC`, `6L278LXZ` — zoologist/ecologist): self-domestication thesis (`自己家畜化論`) — *Homo sapiens* is the species that creates its own habitats and adapts to them, in a continuum from animal niche-construction through agricultural domestication to contemporary self-petification (`自己ペット化`). Carries the **evolutionary** dimension of the analysis — the deep-time arc of human exosomatization.
- **Uegaki Takahide** (`上柿崇英`, `VESTLJGF`, `CYAAA3IK` — environmental philosopher): post-war Japanese subjectivity through the historical typology of self-completing society (`自己完結社会`), the dilution of relationality, the Drifter (`漂流人`), and the ethics of non-intervention (`不介入の倫理`).
- **Miura Nagamitsu** (`三浦永光`, `NUF7WCJD` — intellectual historian): comparative intellectual history of environmental thought as a distinct register from Western environmental philosophy.

#### Comparative anthropology and deep-time history

- **Graeber and Wengrow**, *The Dawn of Everything* (2021 — VERIFY): refutes the linear-stage account of human prehistory; *Homo sapiens* has experimented with a wide variety of social, political, and economic forms across deep time. Carries the **historical** dimension of the analysis at the civilizational scale.
- **Descola**, *Beyond Nature and Culture* (2013 — VERIFY): four ontologies (animism, totemism, analogism, naturalism) as plural human ontological commitments, of which Modern Western "naturalism" is one, not the default.
- **Eduardo Viveiros de Castro** on Amazonian perspectivism (VERIFY): multinaturalism (one culture, many natures) as a genuine alternative to Western multiculturalism.
- **James C. Scott**, *Seeing Like a State* (1998), *Against the Grain* (2017 — VERIFY): deep history of agrarian state-formation; archaeology of state-modernist legibility.

#### Environmental and ecological registers (now distributed across this resource)

The thesis attends to its objects environmentally (organism-environment relation; what counts as habitat for whom) and ecologically (structural feedback loops; cross-level co-adaptation). These registers are not a separate "orientation tier" but are how this resource reads its material: Obara's self-domestication is environmental-evolutionary; Uegaki's self-completing society is ecological-civilizational; Scott's archaeology of state-legibility is environmental-historical. In every case, the registers are diagnostic *attentions* the analysis brings to its material, not separate sets of commitments.

This resource supplies what Resource 1 cannot: empirical-historical specificity about *which* mode of artificiality is being analyzed, against *which* alternatives, with *what* deep-time evidence base. The choice of these particular figures over more familiar Western anthropological authorities reflects the thesis's commitment to the plurality the analysis claims to find.

## 4. Three analytical moves

The position (§1) is what the thesis IS philosophically; the resources (§3) are what it draws on substantively; the analytical moves below are what the thesis DOES. Three moves, each load-bearing.

### Move 1 — Diachronic retroduction

Retroduction is inference to the underlying structure that, if real, would produce the observed phenomena (Bhaskar, *A Realist Theory of Science* 1975 — `bhaskarRealistTheoryScience2008`; *Plato Etc.* 2009 — `88YLH48Y`). The thesis extends retroduction *diachronically*: from observable phenomena to the underlying disposition that, if real, would produce them, **and from the disposition's reconstructed history to the structural condition the disposition continues to generate**. The historical trajectory is not background context for a synchronic causal claim; it is itself part of the inferential structure. The direction of the trajectory grounds the diagnostic claims about where it is heading.

This is the move that makes the position *developmental* rather than merely realist-stratified. Standard textbook retroduction infers a synchronic mechanism from contemporary phenomena; diachronic retroduction infers a *developmental disposition* whose past trajectory is part of the explanatory apparatus. Margaret Archer's morphogenetic approach (*Realist Social Theory* 1995; *Structure, Agency, and the Internal Conversation* 2003 — VERIFY) supplies the formal articulation: structures condition action; action reproduces or transforms structures; morphogenesis (or morphostasis) constitutes the next round of conditioning.

Polanyi's account of boundary conditions and dual control (*Personal Knowledge* 1958; *The Tacit Dimension* 1966 — acquisition needed) supplies the formal model of how higher-level organizations emerge from and govern lower-level dynamics without reducing to them. This is what permits the universe / world / metaverse to be hierarchically nested without being hierarchically collapsed in the retroductive inference.

### Move 2 — Stratified-emergent reading (the 3×3 mapping)

The thesis distinguishes two stratifications that cut across each other:

- A stratification *of being*: **universe / world / metaverse** as distinct kinds of background environment that humans inhabit
- A stratification *of access*: **real / actual / empirical** as distinct modes in which any level of being can be related to (real = generative structures and dispositions; actual = events; empirical = observed events)

The 3×3 mapping (§5 below) is the formal scaffolding for this move. Its load-bearing function is to enable the central argumentative claim: the metaverse at the *empirical* level (XR, headsets, virtual worlds — what consumer-tech discourse and the *Reality+* style of philosophy take to *be* the metaverse) is distinct from the metaverse at the *real* level (the underlying generative disposition: a self-completing artificiality that has become, or is becoming, a second background environment).

Stratified-emergent reading is what the position (§1) *does* with phenomena. It is a methodological move because it determines which level of which stratification a given claim operates at, and therefore what kind of evidence is responsive to it.

### Move 3 — Ideal-typical analysis

The thesis's central concepts — **universe, world, metaverse, squeeze, self-completing artificiality, dependency mode, radical monopoly, neo-artificiality** — are **ideal-typical constructions** in Weber's sense (*Idealtypus*): one-sided accentuations of points of view, formed by synthesizing concrete individual phenomena into unified analytical constructs (`Gedankenbild`). Ideal-types are not found in empirical reality (they are utopian in Weber's technical sense) but serve as analytical reference points by which empirical material can be ordered, compared, and interrogated.

This is a load-bearing methodological commitment, not a heuristic. Three implications.

**(a) Concept-as-construction.** The thesis takes responsibility for the construction of its concepts. "World" is not a discovered entity that the thesis describes; it is an ideal-typical construct that abstracts from the empirical multiplicity of human meaning-spaces to enable the universe / world / metaverse stratification to do its argumentative work. The same is true of "metaverse," "squeeze," and the other central terms. None claim exhaustive empirical coverage; each claims analytical productivity.

**(b) Declared revisability.** Ideal-types are explicitly held open to revision: when the construction stops illuminating, it is replaced. This is a methodological commitment, not a personal trait. The thesis's central concepts are therefore presented as productive constructions, not as final ontological inventories.

**(c) Pragmatist kinship.** Weber's ideal-types are kin to Dewey's working hypotheses and Peirce's abductive constructs: concepts are tools for inquiry, evaluated by their analytical fruits (do they illuminate? do they organize the material? do they generate testable claims?), not by their correspondence to a pre-given empirical taxonomy. This kinship situates the thesis's conceptual apparatus within a recognizable pragmatist register of philosophy of inquiry.

**What this commits the thesis to.** Less than full descriptive coverage of every empirical instance. The thesis does not claim that every actually-existing virtual environment is or is not "the metaverse"; it claims that *the metaverse-as-ideal-type* identifies a real generative disposition whose effects can be retroductively inferred from the observable signs of human malaise.

**What this shields the thesis from.** The demand that conceptual constructions match observed reality exhaustively. Critics who say "Apple Vision Pro is not the metaverse you describe" or "Horizon Worlds is not what you call self-completing artificiality" are misreading the methodological commitment: the empirical instances are tested against the ideal-type, not the other way around. The ideal-type's purpose is to make the underlying disposition visible across heterogeneous empirical instances.

This move is not virtue-signalling or audience-strategic positioning; it is an analytical commitment with substantive consequences for what counts as a successful refutation of the thesis. (Audience-strategic considerations belong in §10.)

## 6. Deliberate omissions (locked)

1. **Dialectics (the language of)**. The squeeze has dialectical *content* — it is a real contradiction without a guaranteed resolution mechanism — but the thesis does not use dialectical *form* (no triadic synthesis, no necessary supersession, no *Aufhebung*). Weaker terms — *structural double-bind*, *incommensurability*, *adaptational contradiction* — preserve the analytical content while avoiding jurisdictional disputes between Hegelian, Marxist, and Bhaskarian dialectical traditions. This is a deliberate methodological choice, not an oversight.
2. **Heideggerian methodology**. Heidegger is the genealogical ancestor of Stiegler and Hui but does not appear as a methodological co-equal: no phenomenological-ontological method, no history-of-Being framing, no anti-realism about structures, no anti-naturalism about the human. Heidegger is background to Stiegler's lineage in §II.
3. **Baudrillard as method**. Baudrillard is used substantively in §IIB on virtuality (third-order simulacrum supports the rejection of "virtual = simulation of real") but not methodologically; his anti-realism is incompatible with Developmental Emergent Realism.
4. **Logical-positivist or empiricist framing**. By the (C)-rename in `diagnoses-set.md`, the thesis explicitly avoids "positivistic" and "empiricistic" as labels for what it critiques; the critical target is *actualism* (in Bhaskar's technical sense — the conflation of the actual with the real).

## 10. Strategic Notes (audience-strategic considerations)

These are rhetorical-strategic considerations for how the methodology is presented to specific committee readers. They are **not** load-bearing for the methodology itself; the methodology stands on its analytical merits in §1–§9.

### The combined approach

Version B (Developmental Emergent Realism, in everyday philosophical English) is in the main text because Mi's analytic-virtue-epistemology + pragmatist + Confucian training does not include critical realism or morphogenetic theory as familiar reference points, and demanding methodological reciprocity from Mi at the level of jargon would be an unforced rhetorical error. Version A (native critical-realist vocabulary) is in the footnote because the position *is* a critical-realist position and the thesis should be honest about that — the analytical-friendly translation in the main text is a translation, not a substitute. A determined reader who follows the footnote will find the position fully articulated in its native vocabulary; a reader who does not will find the same content articulated in vocabulary they can engage without prejudice.

### The Mi strategy

Mi will press on the realist commitment ("how do you know the disposition is real if you only observe its effects?"). The retroductive answer — that *all* causal explanation in the empirical sciences proceeds this way — is straightforwardly compatible with virtue epistemology's commitments to intellectual humility and openness. The squeeze, on this view, is not claimed as known with certainty; it is hypothesized as the underlying structure that best explains the observable phenomena, and the hypothesis is open to revision on the strength of evidence. This is good intellectual virtue, not bad ontology.

### Ideal-type elevation as Mi-strategic affordance (rhetorical, not substantive)

Elevating ideal-typical analysis to Move 3 surfaces the thesis's epistemic discipline — its self-conscious construction of concepts, declared revisability, and pragmatist orientation — in a register that Mi's analytic-virtue-epistemology + pragmatist + Confucian training engages without requiring prior commitment to the critical-realist vocabulary. The *display-vs-enact* distinction is the honest version of this affordance: ideal-types do not make the thesis *more* virtue-epistemically disciplined than CR-native methods would (Bhaskar's stratified retroduction enacts the same discipline). They make the discipline *visible* in Mi's idiom. The substantive analytical commitments are in §4 Move 3; this paragraph is the strategic note about why the move is also rhetorically apt. **No unique-virtue claim is made.**

### The Liu strategy

Liu's scope discipline is best served by keeping the methodological apparatus light in the main text (Version B) and reserving the heavier machinery (the native CR vocabulary, the morphogenetic apparatus, Polanyian boundary conditions) for footnotes. The thesis demonstrates scope discipline by *using* the methodology rather than displaying it. The round-2 declutter (collapsing the four-tier structure into 1 position + 2 resources + 3 moves) directly answers Liu's likely concern about methodological inflation.

### The Ma strategy

(Ma will not read the revised draft, but the considerations remain useful.) The explicit comparative-civilizational dimension is what Ma asked for. Resource 2 (Eco-anthropological history and thought — Japanese Synthetic Anthropology + comparative anthropology + deep-time history) is now substantively integrated into the methodology, not added as a token gesture toward "non-Western perspectives."

## 11. Deferred-integration notes (POSWID / DSRP / LMAO)

Three potentially useful systems-thinking apparatuses have been moved here from earlier drafts pending author decision on whether to integrate. Each entry maps (a) what it is, (b) where it would land if integrated, (c) what it would buy, (d) what it would cost, (e) status.

### POSWID (Stafford Beer)

- **What:** "The purpose of a system is what it does." A methodological prophylactic against intentionalism: systems should be analyzed by their actual effects and outputs, not by their stated or intended purposes.
- **Where it would land:** Inside Move 1 (diachronic retroduction) as a methodological constraint — when retroducing a disposition from observed phenomena, do not import stated intentions of platform-builders, regulators, or designers as part of the explanation. Or in the "forward-looking diagnostic register" footnote in Version A.
- **What it would buy:** Strong defense against critics who say "the metaverse builders did not intend the squeeze." POSWID lets the thesis say: stated intent is irrelevant; the system is what it does, the squeeze is what it is producing, and the dispositional analysis of its production is the thesis's diagnostic move.
- **What it would cost:** Adds another named principle to the methodology (after the round-2 declutter). May appear gratuitous if the dispositional argument is already doing the work POSWID would re-articulate.
- **Status:** Deferred. If integrated, two-line addition to Move 1 + acquire Beer source. Otherwise, drop.

### DSRP (Cabrera, Cabrera, & Midgley)

- **What:** Distinctions / Systems / Relationships / Perspectives — a meta-cognitive framework for systems thinking. Each letter names a fundamental cognitive operation that systems thinking deploys.
- **Where it would land:** Could organize the conceptual framing in I-2 (Distinctions = universe/world/metaverse; Systems = the squeeze as nested system; Relationships = the productive dimension; Perspectives = the comparative anthropology). Or could appear in Move 2 (stratified-emergent reading) as the explicit articulation of what the 3×3 mapping is doing cognitively.
- **What it would buy:** A scaffolding that makes the thesis's conceptual architecture explicit and pedagogically navigable. Already cited in `diagnoses-set.md` as the lens through which the three A.3 diagnoses are read; integrating it into the methodology would create a unified conceptual idiom across A.3 and A.4.
- **What it would cost:** DSRP is a niche framework outside Cabrera's circle. Some readers will not engage with it sympathetically. Heavy citation could read as methodological eclecticism.
- **Status:** Deferred. If integrated, would be in Move 2 + I-2. Already used in `diagnoses-set.md` for (B) "specimen-of-XR" closure naming. Decision pending: integrate into A.4 or restrict to A.3 only.

### LMAO (Cabrera, Cabrera, & Midgley)

- **What:** Linear / Mechanistic / Anthropocentric / Overly-ordered — the failure pattern that DSRP corrects. Inherited cognitive habits that prevent genuinely systems-thinking analysis.
- **Where it would land:** Could appear in §6 Deliberate Omissions as a positive characterization of what the methodology refuses (the LMAO pattern of inquiry). Already used in `diagnoses-set.md` to characterize each A.3 diagnosis as a different LMAO failure-profile.
- **What it would buy:** Symmetry with DSRP — if DSRP is integrated, LMAO names what's being avoided. Provides a concise vocabulary for the thesis's anti-reductionist commitments.
- **What it would cost:** Acronym fatigue. Adds a fourth term (after DSRP) that some readers will find faddish.
- **Status:** Deferred, dependent on DSRP decision. If DSRP integrates, LMAO follows. If DSRP stays in A.3 only, LMAO does too.
