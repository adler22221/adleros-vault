## §1 — The squeeze defined (the constitutive thesis)

The universe-metaverse squeeze is **the condition of a being that must simultaneously satisfy the selection criteria of two environments whose organizing laws are not mutually translatable**.

Two features constitute the squeeze as such:

### F1. Two-environment between-structure

The subject is *between* two environments — not within one environment subject to new pressures. Each environment has its own organizing principles, selection criteria, and conditions for adaptation/success. The subject's existence is jointly constrained by both, with no single optimization possible.

**Why this matters:** In a within-environment squeeze (e.g., agrarian-industrial transition), the subject is adapting to a transformed version of the same environment — the laws are continuous, the substrate persists. In a between-environment squeeze, the subject must operate under two distinct rule-sets simultaneously.

### F2. Incommensurability of the laws

The laws governing the two environments are not mutually translatable. What succeeds in one does not automatically succeed in the other; competencies developed for one are not fully transferable to the other; the value-currencies of each are not exchangeable.

The universe selects for: embodied resilience, biological attunement, ecological sensitivity, vernacular competence in local environments, inter-corporeal presence — capacities isomorphic with organic life.

The metaverse selects for: platform legibility, data-generativity, algorithmic compliance, credential-mediated identity, attention-economy participation — capacities isomorphic with computational and market logics.

**This is incommensurability in the strict sense** (after Kuhn/Feyerabend, but applied to environments rather than scientific paradigms): not merely difference, but the absence of a common measure that would allow a single trade-off calculation.

## §2 — How this squeeze differs from prior squeezes (the differentiating thesis)

This addresses Liu's question directly: 那個squeeze跟過去的有何不同？

Prior technological transitions imposed real adaptational pressures (foraging→agriculture, craft→industry, rural→urban, analog→digital communication). All were *within-world* or *within-universe* transformations — they changed what the world contained, not what kind of thing the world was. The factory, the railroad, the city, the radio: each transformed the universe-substrate locally without introducing a second environment with its own organizing logic.

The universe-metaverse squeeze is categorically distinct because it satisfies F1 + F2 — it is genuinely *between* two environments rather than within one.

This categorical distinction manifests through three operational features:

### F3. Law-change asymmetry

The laws of the universe (thermodynamic, biological, ecological, geographic) are stable on the timescale of human civilization — legible through long observation, unchanged within a generation. The laws of the metaverse (algorithmic governance, platform logics, recommendation systems, data policies) are deliberately engineered, periodically updated, and often deliberately obscured. The adaptation rate the metaverse demands exceeds the response cycles of biological evolution, cultural transmission, and even legislative deliberation.

Prior squeezes imposed stable new pressures whose contours could eventually be learned. The universe-metaverse squeeze imposes *unstable* new pressures whose instability is itself a design feature.

### F4. Substrate-decoupling

Prior technologies operated on the universe-substrate — the steam engine, the telegraph, the assembly line all altered material conditions while remaining tethered to thermodynamics, geography, and biology. The metaverse's organizing logic is designed to be **substrate-independent**: geographically placeless, somatically disembedded, indifferent to embodied presence. This decoupling is what makes it a genuinely new environment rather than a transformed feature of the universe.

(Note: substrate-independence is *aspirational and partial*, not absolute — the metaverse still requires servers, electricity, rare earths, embodied users. The point is that its organizing logic does not regulate or acknowledge these dependencies. This itself produces the squeeze: the universe-substrate continues to make demands the metaverse refuses to register.)

### F5. Radical monopoly over livelihood

(After Illich, *Tools for Conviviality*, 1973.) Prior technological transitions created new dependencies but left alternative universe-anchored pathways viable in principle. Rural livelihood remained possible after urbanization; craft remained possible after industrialization. The metaverse's radical monopoly tends toward the **elimination of all non-platform-mediated pathways** — not through prohibition but through the restructuring of the conditions under which any livelihood can succeed (banking, identity verification, civic participation, professional credentialing, social belonging, all increasingly platform-gated). This makes the present squeeze uniquely total: it offers no refuge even in principle.

## §3 — Productive dimension of the squeeze (the qualification)

> **Derivation tier:** [T1: source-ms-kaikan §2.4, §6] for structural argument and 依存転回 framing; **[T0: NLM-Uegaki-A-Sources, conv 15df9238]** for 〈意のままになる生〉 normative characterization, structural costs, and ecological/evolutionary framing. See `.research/uegaki-tier0-retrieval.md` for full retrieved passages.

This addresses Ma's and Liu's pushback that the squeeze isn't necessarily negative ("squeeze不一定都是負面的 / 正面的更多?"). The answer must acknowledge productive dimensions without dissolving the analytical force of the squeeze.

### The benefit/cost framing (not positive/negative)

The productive dimension of the squeeze is not simply a "positive side" to set against a "negative side." Such framing would dissolve the analytical force of the squeeze into a trade-off calculation — which is precisely what F2 (incommensurability) forbids. Instead, the productive dimension must be understood through the logic of **benefit/cost asymmetry of life-as-willed**.

The modern normative project, crystallized in what Uegaki (2017/2021) calls 〈意のままになる生〉 (life-as-willed) and 〈自己完結社会〉 (self-completing society), pursues the reduction of 〈意のままにならない〉 states — conditions where things do not proceed as willed. Uegaki characterizes this as the "worldview of infinite life" (〈無限の生〉) — a normative claim that a life entirely under one's control is the *ought-to-be* state of human existence. He calls this an "ideal that denies reality" (現実を否定する理想): it starts from an imagined "perfect human" and attempts to alter flesh-and-blood reality to fit this standard. The structural costs are internal to the ideal: the "Endless Hell" (無間地獄) of escalating dissatisfaction, the "Confusion of Life" (〈生の混乱〉) when uncontrollable realities are encountered, and the ultimate self-cancelling endpoint where fully achieving life-as-willed would require discarding biological humanity itself ("brain humans," 脳人間).

This project has genuine benefits: reduction of material scarcity, protection from ecological unpredictability, extension of human capabilities through technical systems. The source manuscript acknowledges these directly — none of the five dependency modes (天地 / 配分 / 貨幣 / 人工性 / 制御性) is intrinsically harmful; each arose as an adaptive response to real pressures.

But as the author argues [T1: source-ms-kaikan §2.4, citing 上柿 2021]: **the more thoroughly humans entrust all matters to control means and pursue 〈意のままになる生〉, the smaller the human role in the world's operation becomes, and control means progressively automate.** When artificiality (人工性) functions self-sufficiently without human involvement and humans become those who adapt to it rather than the reverse, that artificiality transcends its role as survival means and stands up as a new background environment — the metaverse/偽境.

The **structural costs** of life-as-willed are not merely contingent failures or side effects but necessary consequences of the inversion [T1: source-ms-kaikan §3]:
- The positive pyramid (根本性順位: 天地 > 配分 > 貨幣 > 制御性) and the inverted pyramid (生活依存構造: 制御性 > 貨幣 > 配分 > 天地) diverge further as the pursuit of 〈意のままになる生〉 intensifies
- The more thoroughly adapted to the inverted structure, the harder it becomes to return to more fundamental dependency modes
- The result is the very squeeze condition described in §1-§2: existence jointly constrained by universe and metaverse with no single optimization possible

The squeeze is, in this sense, the self-organized outcome of humanity's collective pursuit of life-as-willed — not an external imposition but an emergent consequence of humanity's participation in the progressive automation of control.

### The 〈依存転回〉 (dependency turn) as precondition of navigation [T1: source-ms-kaikan §6]

The productive dimension emerges not from the squeeze itself but from a specific *reframing* of one's relation to it. The author calls this 〈依存転回〉 (dependency turn):

When power and oppression are framed as evils that distort from outside, the subject is fixed in the position of victim. But when framed as *dependency*, the question becomes: **in what dependency structures am I participating, and how are they organized?** This reframing opens the question of how dependency modes can be reorganized (別の代謝回路の作り方 — the creation of different metabolic circuits) rather than merely suffered.

As the source manuscript notes [T1: source-ms-kaikan §6], analogous to the Buddhist 〈十二因縁〉 analysis: the chain of desire (愛) → attachment (取) → fixation of existence (有) is not severed from outside but is reorganized only through *awareness of the process itself* (連鎖のプロセスそのものへの気づき). And as Zhuangzi's 〈有所待〉 formulation cautions: there is no absolute starting point that can end all dependency chains — which is precisely why tracing and understanding the dependency chains becomes the fundamental honest path toward transformation.

The productive dimension of the squeeze is therefore not three generic "benefits" that can be listed. It is specifically the possibility of **reorganizing one's dependency modes** that opens when one recognizes oneself as a *participant in self-organized dependency structures* rather than a victim of external forces. This recognition is the precondition of navigation — and the analytical work of the thesis is what makes that recognition possible.

### Difference from the VI-3/VI-4 scaffold formulation

The three-item productive-dimension list in the prior scaffold (VI-3-VI-4: individual hybrid competencies / cultural revitalization / civilizational orientation) is a rough approximation that has now been superseded by this benefit/cost/dependency-turn analysis. The three items remain useful as **illustrative cases** of what dependency-mode reorganization looks like in practice, but they should not be presented as *constitutive* of the productive dimension.

**Do not use the VI-3-VI-4 scaffold formulation in Phase C.** Derive §VI-3 and §VI-4 prose from this document's §3 instead.

## §4 — What the squeeze is NOT (anti-metaphorical safeguards)

> **Derivation tier:** [T1: source-ms-kaikan §2.4, §6] for ecological/evolutionary framing; [T1: CLAUDE.md + viva comments] for metaphor-watering-down risk; **[T0: NLM-Uegaki-A-Sources, conv 15df9238]** for Uegaki's ecological/evolutionary framing confirmed.
> **⚠️ Caveat [T0-confirmed]:** Uegaki does NOT explicitly name Frankfurt School / Heidegger / Stiegler as his contrast. He engages with alienation theory (疎外論) and Scheler, but not those thinkers by name. The contrast in the table below represents the thesis author's positioning of Uegaki's framing relative to those traditions — not Uegaki's direct critique. Adjust §4 prose accordingly in Phase C.

To prevent the squeeze from being watered down to metaphor (the risk flagged in plan v3 §3 re: I-4), the following non-uses must be avoided in the revised thesis:

### Hard prohibitions (what the squeeze is NOT)

- **Not "pressure from technology"** — that's a metaphor. The squeeze is the structural condition F1+F2 satisfies: being between two environments whose laws are incommensurable.
- **Not "stress" or "anxiety"** — these are *experiential correlates* of the squeeze condition, not the squeeze itself. The squeeze can be fully constituted before any individual experiences psychological distress.
- **Not "trade-offs" or "tensions"** — these occur within a single environment under a common measure. F2 is precisely the absence of such a common measure. "Tension between X and Y" dissolves the squeeze's analytical force.
- **Not a moral position about technology** — the squeeze is a structural-ontological diagnosis. The thesis does not argue the metaverse should not exist; it describes the structural condition of those who must inhabit both environments.

### The key conceptual boundary: ecological/evolutionary vs. anthropological

The squeeze must be distinguished from the major existing critical-theory concepts it might superficially resemble:

| Concept | Tradition | What it names | Why the squeeze is different |
|---------|-----------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Alienation** (Entfremdung) | Marx, Frankfurt school | Human subject's separation from species-being, product, activity, other humans | Starts from human subject and their authentic relation to labor/world. The squeeze is a between-environment structural condition prior to any subject's experience. |
| **Domination** (Herrschaft) | Frankfurt school (Horkheimer, Adorno, Marcuse) | Human subject's subjugation to the technical-rational apparatus; loss of autonomy | Names a *relation of power* between human subjects and systems. The squeeze names a *structural position between two environments* — not a power relation but an ontological condition. |
| **Enframing** (Gestell) | Heidegger | The essence of modern technology as a challenging-forth (Herausforderung) that reveals beings only as standing-reserve (Bestand) | Starts from the question of Being and its concealment; fundamentally anthropological (concerned with the human Dasein's relation to Being). The squeeze starts from the self-organization of dependency modes — a process that is indifferent to any particular human Dasein. |
| **Grammatization** | Stiegler | The discretization of continuous human flows into calculable grammes; the pharmacological logic of technical systems | Names a *mechanism internal to the metaverse's logic* — what makes the metaverse operate as it does. The squeeze names the *structural condition* of being between universe and metaverse. Grammatization is part of what makes the metaverse a distinct environment with its own laws (F2); it does not name the squeeze itself. |

**The key distinction [T1: source-ms-kaikan §2.4, §6]:** The dependency modes (天地依存 / 配分依存 / 貨幣依存 / 人工性依存 / 制御性依存) are described as "自己組織化された歴史的産物" — historically self-organized products. The squeeze is the emergent condition of these self-organizing processes reaching a stage where a second background environment (偽境) has become operationally distinct from the universe. This is an **ecological/evolutionary** description: it concerns the structural niche conditions organisms face, not the *experience* of those conditions by human subjects.

Uegaki's own framing confirms this: he treats human civilization as an "artificial ecosystem" (人為的生態系) within which the biological organism Homo is shaped. He diagnoses the modern crisis not as loss of "authentic being" but as an **ecological mismatch** — the biological human reaching "limits of adaptation" (適応の限界) to the environment it created. He rejects alienation theory's premise that technology distorts an original "pure" human nature, treating technology instead as a direct extension of human evolutionary strategy.

The contrast table above frames the thesis author's positioning of Uegaki relative to those traditions. In Phase C prose, this should read as: "Unlike the anthropological critical traditions (Frankfurt school, Heidegger, Stiegler), which begin from the human subject and their relationship to technical systems, the ecological-evolutionary analysis following Uegaki begins from structural niche conditions that would obtain even if no particular human subject experienced them as distress."

Frankfurt school, Heidegger, and Stiegler begin from the *human subject* and their *relationship* to technical systems — they are fundamentally anthropological diagnoses. The squeeze begins from *structural niche conditions* that would obtain even if no particular human subject experienced them as distress, alienation, or loss. Alienation, domination, enframing, and grammatization name *consequences* or *correlates* of the squeeze that manifest at the level of human experience — they are downstream effects. The squeeze names the structural-ontological cause.

### Operational test

If a passage uses "squeeze" without (implicitly or explicitly) invoking F1+F2, it is using the term **metaphorically** and must be either:
- Rewritten to invoke the structural condition (between-environment, incommensurable laws), OR
- Have "squeeze" replaced with a less load-bearing word ("pressure," "tension," "challenge")

The I-4 retrospective (contemporary evidence section) is the highest-risk zone. Meta's struggles with Horizon Worlds are NOT "the squeeze." They are empirical evidence that a distinct metaverse environment is forming with its own selection criteria (mapping to F3-F5). The squeeze is the structural condition of humans who must navigate both. Contemporary corporate failures map to the *diagnoses* (path-dependency, disciplinary-reductionism, positivist boundary-setting) — not directly to "the squeeze." See plan §3 and cross-reference targets §6 above.

## §5 — Total feature count and rationale

**5 distinguishing marks total**: 2 constitutive (F1, F2) + 3 operational (F3, F4, F5).

| Feature | Type | What it does |
|---------|------|--------------|
| F1. Two-environment between-structure | Constitutive | Distinguishes from within-environment squeezes |
| F2. Incommensurability of laws | Constitutive | Distinguishes from commensurable trade-offs |
| F3. Law-change asymmetry | Operational | Distinguishes from stable-law adaptations |
| F4. Substrate-decoupling | Operational | Distinguishes from substrate-altering technologies |
| F5. Radical monopoly over livelihood | Operational | Distinguishes from coexistence-permitting transitions |

**Rationale for 5 (not 3 or 7):**
- 3 would conflate F1+F2 (constitutive) with F3-F5 (operational), losing the analytical separation between *what the squeeze is* and *how it works*.
- 7 would invite over-generation; the operational features F3-F5 are clearly distinct, and adding more (e.g., "epistemic opacity," "temporal acceleration") risks deriving sub-features of F3 as if they were independent.
- 5 = the minimum sufficient to distinguish the universe-metaverse squeeze from all prior structural pressures while preserving the analytical separation between definition and manifestation.

**Alternative framings considered and rejected:**
- ❌ 2 features only (F1+F2 alone): doesn't answer Liu's "how is this different from prior squeezes" with sufficient specificity.
- ❌ 3 features (F3+F4+F5 alone, dropping the constitutive layer): leaves the squeeze undefined; readers must infer F1+F2 themselves.
- ❌ Combining F4 and F5 into "totalization": loses the distinction between the metaverse's *internal* logic (F4 substrate-independence) and its *external* expansion (F5 monopoly over livelihood). They are connected but not identical.
