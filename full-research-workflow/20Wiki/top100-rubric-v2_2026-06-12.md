# Top-100 PhD-program readiness rubric (v2)
> Recreated 2026-06-12 for the pre-submission review run; supersedes [[top100-rubric-notes]] (revision-staging, 2026-05-04). Applied result for this thesis is in [[master-thesis_editorial-review_2026-06-12]] §6 (verdict: competitive top-20, 85.7%).

**Purpose.** Grade the manuscript as evidence for PhD admission at top-100 (and, as a stretch line, top-20) programs in the fields this project touches (philosophy of technology, critical theory, comparative philosophy, STS, philosophical anthropology). Two scored objects, because committees read a **~7–9k-word excerpt** (the *writing sample*; current norm ~15–25 double-spaced pp / ~5,000–7,000 words, Princeton firm ceiling 10,000) but infer *research promise* from the whole.

**Scale (per dimension, 0–4).** 0 = absent/disqualifying · 1 = weak (a committee would note it as a problem) · 2 = adequate (competent, unremarkable) · 3 = strong (a clear plus) · 4 = decisive (top-of-pile; the reason to admit). Score the **Excerpt** and the **Whole** separately where they diverge.

| # | Dimension | Wt | What a 4 looks like |
|---|---|----|---------------------|
| 1 | **Single claim + stakes** | ×3 | One position, stated early; a non-specialist knows what's claimed and why it matters by p.2 of the sample. |
| 2 | **Argument architecture** | ×3 | Sustained build, not a survey; every move motivated; no "tour of positions." |
| 3 | **Originality / contribution** | ×3 | Advances *past* its named interlocutors; the gap reads as invisible-until-seen, not "a gap to fill." |
| 4 | **Engagement w/ live scholarship** | ×2 | Primary-text-grounded, charitable, precise; no strawman; contemporary debate, not only historical. |
| 5 | **Self-containedness of the excerpt** | ×3 *(excerpt)* | Evaluable standalone; no load-bearing dependence on un-included chapters. |
| 6 | **Methodological reflexivity** | ×2 | Declares + defends the lens; owns its standpoint; ties method to the diagnoses. |
| 7 | **Trans/antidisciplinary legibility** | ×2 | "Frame once, position multiply": legible to several fields without pretending to be each; signposted, not blurred. |
| 8 | **Prose, clarity, signposting** | ×2 | Literate, controlled; reader never lost; abstruse material doesn't clog the body. |
| 9 | **Program / standpoint positioning** | ×1 | Declares theoretical standpoint; maps onto identifiable faculty/communities. |

**Scoring & thresholds.** Weighted max = 63. Report per dimension: Excerpt / Whole score + 1-line anchored evidence + the single highest-leverage upgrade. **≥0.80 = competitive at top-20; 0.65–0.80 = solid top-100; <0.65 = revise before using as a sample.** Always separate "the thesis is excellent" from "this excerpt is admissible" — they are not the same judgment. Name the strongest self-contained excerpt and the specific edits that make it free-standing.

**Why v2 is more precise than the 2026-05-04 notes:** explicit 0–4 anchors and dimension weights (vs. a flat criteria list); excerpt-vs-whole dual scoring (the actual unit a committee reads); a numeric decision threshold; and a forced excerpt recommendation. Calibrated to a trans/antidisciplinary philosophy-of-technology thesis.
