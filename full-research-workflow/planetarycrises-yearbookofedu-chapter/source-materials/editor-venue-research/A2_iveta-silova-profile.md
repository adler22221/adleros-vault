## Theoretical Lineages

- **More-than-human / common worlds pedagogy** — pedagogy "with" not "about" the more-than-human; multispecies vulnerability; learning through relations between children, place, materials, other species. ([Taylor & Pacini-Ketchabaw](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/14681366.2015.1039050))
- **Post-qualitative & post-humanist** inquiry, particularly via the Common Worlds Collective. ([UNESCO ROE](https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000374032))
- **Decolonial / postcolonial / postsocialist triangulation** — "interrupting the coloniality of knowledge production"; border-thinking; envisioning education *beyond the Western horizon*. ([CER 2017](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/690458); [ASU profile](https://search.asu.edu/profile/2911122))
- **Ecofeminism & environmental humanities** — Haraway's Chthulucene, Stengers on the eradication of "other worlds." ([ASU profile](https://search.asu.edu/profile/2911122); [CIES 2020 plenary](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Iveta-Silova))
- **Critique of culturally-specific "ontological individualism"** in education for sustainable development — student-centered learning critiqued as a Western export. ([Komatsu/Rappleye/Silova SAGE Open 2022](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/21582440221079836))
- **Memory studies / autoethnography** of (post)socialist childhoods as a route into ecomemory and intergenerational ecological learning. ([Academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu/39992098/Thinking_through_memories_of_post_socialist_childhoods))

## Methodological Signature

Multi-modal, narrative, collective. Auto/biographical memory work; multispecies ethnography; "arts of noticing" (Tsing-inflected); visual and arts-based methods; collaborative authorship across geopolitical sites; meta-analyses of ILSA (PISA/TIMSS) cross-referenced with environmental indicators (the Komatsu/Rappleye/Silova line — finding that "better" education correlates with *higher* per-capita CO2). ([GTI Forum](https://greattransition.org/gti-forum/pedagogy-transition-silova-komatsu-rappleye))

## Editorial Style — What Past Volumes Tell Us

Silova's edited collections (*Globalization on the Margins*; *Childhood and Schooling in (Post)Socialist Societies*; *(An)Archive*) consistently mix: (i) theoretically ambitious framings, (ii) **geopolitically peripheral** sites (Caucasus, Central Asia, post-Soviet Europe, Global South), (iii) **methodological pluralism** including memory-work, autoethnography, visual essays alongside conventional empirical pieces, and (iv) explicit decolonial/anti-Western-centric stance. She is comfortable with first-person, narrative, and arts-based chapters; she resists chapters that reproduce Eurocentric universalism or treat "comparison" as neutral measurement.

## What She Values (positioning cues for the proposal)

- **Unlearning, refusal, and "education beyond the Western horizon"** — chapters that critique business-as-usual SDG / ESD / student-centered orthodoxy will land. ([2025 SDG paper](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/09734082251355100))
- **Interdependence over individualism** — onto-relational framings preferred over autonomous-learner frames. ([SAGE Open 2022](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/21582440221079836))
- **Comparison as *relational* not metric** — see her 2024 keynote "Reclaiming Interdependence."
- **More-than-human / multispecies learning** as ethical-political stance, not aesthetic flourish.
- **Memory, childhood, and intergenerational ecological time** as legitimate units of analysis.
- **Sites usually excluded from the Anglosphere ESD canon** (post-Soviet, post-socialist, Indigenous, Asian cosmologies).
- **Hopeful but unflinching** — "planetary emergency" framing without techno-optimism.
