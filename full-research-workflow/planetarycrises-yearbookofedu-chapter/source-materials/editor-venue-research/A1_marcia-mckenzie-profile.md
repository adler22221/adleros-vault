## Recurring Theoretical Commitments / Lineages
- **Critical education policy studies** — emphasis on *policy mobility*, why/how policies travel, and the *affective* infrastructures that move them ([T&F 2017](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17508487.2017.1308875); [T&F 2022](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/02680939.2020.1852605)).
- **Geographies of education / place and land** — critical pedagogy of place expanded via Indigenous, postcolonial, decolonizing perspectives; *land* (not just place) as relational ([Routledge Land Education](https://www.routledge.com/Land-Education-Rethinking-Pedagogies-of-Place-from-Indigenous-Postcolonial-and-Decolonizing-Perspectives/McCoy-Tuck-McKenzie/p/book/9781138309050)).
- **Sociomaterial / new materialist theory** — narration, place, sociality co-constituted ([*Critical Education and Sociomaterial Practice*](https://books.google.com/books/about/Critical_Education_and_Sociomaterial_Pra.html?id=_iJBjgEACAAJ)).
- **Affect theory** in policy analysis (Massumi/Stewart lineage) ([T&F 2017](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17508487.2017.1308875)).
- **Infrastructural / data-as-governance analysis** — climate vs. digital infrastructures in schooling ([Sage 2023](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00345237231208658)).
- **Comparative and international education** — cross-national policy monitoring (MECCE country profiles methodology) ([MECCE data platform](https://mecce.ca/data-platform/climate-change-country-profile/)).

## Methodological Signature
- **Qualitative + critical policy analysis**, scaled up to *cross-national mixed-methods monitoring* (MECCE: 31 measures across 80 country profiles covering ~75% of global population) ([MECCE COP28](https://mecce.ca/cop28/); [World Ed Blog](https://world-education-blog.org/2023/12/01/greening-education-approaches-worldwide-learning-from-80-countries/)).
- Strong **methodological reflexivity** on place, materiality, and decolonization in qualitative inquiry ([Tuck & McKenzie 2015](https://www.routledge.com/Place-in-Research-Theory-Methodology-and-Methods/Tuck-McKenzie/p/book/9781138639683)).
- **Indicator design / SDG measurement** (greening curriculum indicator endorsed in SDG 4.7.1 2025 Comprehensive Review) ([World Ed Blog 2025](https://world-education-blog.org/2025/11/14/get-to-know-the-greening-curriculum-indicator-part-of-sdg-global-indicator-4-7-1/)).

## What She Values (from her own framing)
- Empirical work that treats **education policy as mobile, affective, and infrastructural** — not as a neutral lever ([T&F 2022](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/02680939.2020.1852605)).
- **Decolonizing and land-based** approaches that take Indigenous sovereignty as a serious epistemic claim, not a metaphor ([Land Education](https://www.routledge.com/Land-Education-Rethinking-Pedagogies-of-Place-from-Indigenous-Postcolonial-and-Decolonizing-Perspectives/McCoy-Tuck-McKenzie/p/book/9781138309050)).
- **Cross-national comparative work** that produces actionable data/indicators *without* flattening local specificity ([MECCE](https://mecce.ca/cop28/)).
- **Critical engagement with sustainability/ESD/GCED** — wary of techno-solutionist or curriculum-add-on framings; attention to *resistance* and *futurities* ([Sage 2023](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00345237231203073)).
- Per the WYoE Vol 29 CFP: chapters "confronting tensions directly," foregrounding **policy and possibility**, comparative scope, planetary scale ([CFP](https://edforplanetaryfutures.substack.com/p/education-and-planetary-crises-comparative)).
