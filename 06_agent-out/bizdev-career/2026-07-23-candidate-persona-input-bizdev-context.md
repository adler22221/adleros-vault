## What he stated (his words, lightly paraphrased — not yet coded/confirmed)

1. **On method, not just content**: he does not want coaching pre-anchored on a curated book/course reading list. His explicit ask: an adaptive approach where each coaching round checks current best practices tailored to his specific context *as needed*, rather than building on a fixed prior curriculum — because two rounds of book/course research (Western business classics, then East-Asia-specific titles, then "voluntary org" titles) each still weren't well fitted to his case.
2. **On why ~2010-era Western business classics didn't work for him**: Business Model Generation, Good to Great, Start with Why, and general MBA textbooks helped him strategize/plan but never converted into revenue. His diagnosis: these are strongly Western-cultural/market-context-bound. Concretely, Good to Great's "get the right people on the bus first, then decide where to drive" has not worked in Taiwan, and he doubts it works in Japan/Korea.
3. **What has worked instead, in his own practice**: starting from tangible, small, bounded goals — yields better results and team formation in an East Asian context than "who before what" macro-strategy-first approaches.
4. **His two persistent core bizdev problems**, both tied to East-Asia-vs-Western differences in market dynamics/personnel/workplace culture: (a) inability to convert planning/strategy into actual revenue; (b) team-building/organizational construction.
5. **A structural distinction he was explicit about**: his organizations (the [[East Asia Dojo for Transdisciplinary Research and Praxis]] chief among them) run on **voluntary, associative participation — work is a choice, not an obligation**. This makes top-down/KPI-driven corporate management models (even East Asian ones) a poor fit, since their motivation mechanics assume an obligated workforce. He named this explicitly as different from e.g. a company like Huawei.
6. **Market sequencing**: East Asian markets/teams as the near-term focus, Western markets "as soon as possible" after — but he wants tools/methods for understanding Western/global markets *now*, from an East Asia base, not deferred until later.
