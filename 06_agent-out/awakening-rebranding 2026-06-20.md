# Rebranding "Awakening" — working notes
*Discussion 2026-06-20 (Adler Yang × Claude). Status: **brainstorming, no decision yet.** Resumable.*

## Context
Adler branded his initiative **Awakening** (青醒 / 青醒人共生文化智庫 / Awakening Cooperative Lab) since **2012**. Fourteen years on, with **dependence** crystallizing as the central theme of all his work (Allocation Dependence → *modes of dependency* → the adaptational squeeze), he's weighing a rebrand.

## The core realization
**The concept is right; the English signifier is the problem.**
- Adler's original meaning of "awakening" — which he still holds — is **awakening ≠ enlightenment**:
  - *Awakening* = an **unfolding, a return, a flourishing of what is already there.**
  - *Enlightenment* = **overcoming / getting rid of** what is "formerly immature, bad, irrational."
- This is not merely branding rationale — **it IS the thesis.** The metaverse book critiques the **"Enlightenment mode"** (the sovereign subject reading the given as a wrong to be overcome) in favour of **attunement, re-embedding, recomposition** — letting what is already there flourish. So the *concept* under "Awakening" is load-bearing across everything Adler does and must be **preserved**.
- **The problem is the English word "Awakening":** (1) **crowded** (countless initiatives use it); (2) **New-Age-coded** in English — which **mis-signals the audience**. Adler wants serious transdisciplinary / critical-yet-constructive scholars, systems and policy people; he isn't against New Age, but it's the wrong tone/crowd for his work. So the legitimate target of change is the **word**, not the idea.

## Preferences so far
- **Scope:** leans "whole umbrella" (unite org + program + person) — but wants to **brainstorm before deciding.**
- **Author / theory / umbrella relationship:** undecided (asked Claude to advise — see below).

## Recommendations (Claude)
1. **Keep the concept; change at most the signifier.** Find a word worthy of the awakening-as-unfolding/return/flourishing concept.
2. **Most surgical fix — decouple the languages.** The complaint is English-only. Keep the distinctive, non-New-Age **Chinese** coinage (青醒 / 青醒人 / 共生文化 — 14 yrs of equity, already encodes 共生); design a **fresh English master-brand** carrying the same concept without "Awakening's" baggage. Bilingual brands need not be literal translations.
3. **Do NOT brand on bare "dependence."** In English, *dependence/dependency* reads as weakness/addiction/deficiency — the exact misreading the thesis fights. Lead with the **constructive pole**: interdependence, co-becoming (共生), attunement (和). "Awakening" sidesteps this trap; any new name should too.
4. **Three-layer brand architecture** (distinct but mutually reinforcing):
   - **You** — *Adler Yang* (楊逸帆): the scholarly credit line. Books/citations attach to a name, not a brand.
   - **Your ideas** — Allocation Dependence · the squeeze · modes of dependency: free-standing, citable theory (a theory that travels wins).
   - **The umbrella** — the org/community/praxis (today "Awakening"): the vehicle, kept **sober and substantive, not movement-y / guru-ish.**
   - **Model:** Elinor Ostrom (scholar / free-standing IAD–commons framework / sober Bloomington Workshop). **Anti-model:** the founder-fused, mildly-mystical "[Concept] Institute" — the New-Age drift to avoid.
5. **Timing:** fine, even advantageous, to settle the rebrand **before** the book publishes (the book hard-codes the author bio/affiliation; launch is a natural debut), **provided** it runs in parallel and does not gate the outreach (which keys off name + theories, not the org brand). Identity is already settled, so this isn't forcing an unsettled identity.

## Brainstorm — conceptual territories for the umbrella
*(From Adler's own East–West vocabulary — serious lineages, not wellness tropes. Directions, not names.)*
1. **Dependent co-arising — 緣起 (pratītyasamutpāda).** Everything arises in dependence on conditions; literally interdependence, rigorous (the thesis engages it). *Caution:* keep philosophical (conditions / co-arising), not devotional "interbeing."
2. **Having-something-to-depend-on — 有所待 / 待 (Zhuangzi).** The Daoist articulation of constitutive dependence, *against* the fantasy of 無待 (total independence) — the awakening-*from-the-illusion-of-independence* move in classical form. *Caution:* 待 = "await"; frame as reliance, not passivity.
3. **Return to the root / self-so — 復歸 · 歸根 · 自然.** Flourishing/return of the already-there. *Caution:* "return" can read backward; means forward-flourishing.
4. **Attunement / accord — 和.** Adler's own constructive keyword (attunement = prevention of the squeeze). *Caution:* "harmony" reads bland / politically loaded; "attunement / accord / concinnity" sharper.
5. **Symbiosis / co-becoming — 共生.** Already in the Chinese name; ecological, relational. *Caution:* "symbiosis" clinical; "co-becoming" fresher.
6. **Re-embedding / weaving.** "Ecosystem weaving" / re-embedding the human in its relations. *Caution:* weave / mycelium / regenerative is crowded in systems-change circles.

**Claude's instinct:** 有所待 (2) and 緣起 (1) are the most *distinctively Adler's* — about dependence itself, philosophically serious, unmistakably not New-Age. 3 and 4 carry the flourishing/attunement pole best.

## Design brief (for the eventual name)
- **Preserve:** awakening-as-unfolding/return/flourishing + constitutive interdependence (the thesis).
- **Read:** grounded, rigorous, East–West, constructive.
- **Avoid:** New-Age/wellness tone; "enlightenment/overcoming"; generic-uplift; crowded terms.

## Next step
Adler reacts to the six territories (which pull / which feel wrong / what's missing) → once a direction or two crystallizes, run a name-generation pass (bake-off + judging) against the brief, then stress-test candidates together. **Settle in parallel with — not ahead of — the book outreach.**
