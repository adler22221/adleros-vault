>“No one should have claimed that moving workers from low-productivity jobs to unemployment would either reduce poverty or increase national incomes. Believers in self-regulating markets implicitly believed in a kind of Say's law, that the supply of labor would create its own demand. For capitalists who thrive off of low wages, the high [[unemployment]] may even be a benefit, as it puts downward pressure on workers' wage demands^[This is very dark... But very convincing, given the way the economy is run today...]. But for economists, the unemployed workers demonstrate a malfunctioning economy, and in all too many countries we see overwhelming evidence of this and other malfunctions. Some advocates of the self-regulating economy put part of the blame for these malfunctions on government itself; but whether this is true or not, the point is that the myth of the self-regulating economy is, today, virtually dead.”
\- [[Stiglitz, Joseph E.]]

Excerpt From: Karl Polanyi. “The Great Transformation: The Political and Economic Origins of Our Time.” Apple Books.
