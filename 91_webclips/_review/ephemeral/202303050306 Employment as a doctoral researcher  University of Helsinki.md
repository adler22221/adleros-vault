> Employed, grant-funded or self-funded? Doctoral students may receive funding from the University of Helsinki or one of its departments, institutes or faculties, a research project, foundation, association or an external funder. They may also self-fund their studies. About a third of all doctoral students are in an employment relationship with the University and are members of University staff.

# Employment as a doctoral researcher | University of Helsinki
The Doctoral Programme in School, Education, Society and Culture (SEDUCE) invites applications for **5 salaried doctoral researcher positions** for a 4-year period starting from January 1, 2023. Preference will be given to applicants who are in the early stages of their doctoral degree and can be granted a full 4-year period. Applicants can receive salaried positions for shorter periods than four years **only in exceptional cases.**

Previous allocated doctoral grants or working contracts do not prevent you from receiving a 4-year position. You are eligible to apply for a salaried position, if you have not already spent a total of four years in a salaried doctoral researcher position.

Please prepare your application **in English** and pay attention to the maximum number of characters for each section. The study plan needs to follow the curriculum of the doctoral programme, which you can find in the [Instructions for Students](https://studies.helsinki.fi/instructions/article/scope-and-structure-doctoral-degrees). Also note that the research plan must fit the [research profile](https://www.helsinki.fi/en/admissions-and-education/apply-doctoral-programmes/doctoral-schools-and-doctoral-programmes/doctoral-school-humanities-and-social-sciences/doctoral-programme-school-education-society-and-culture/profile-activities) of SEDUCE.

Please include a signed supervision contract or your main supervisor’s written agreement for supervision, if you do not yet have the right to pursue a doctoral degree at the University of Helsinki.

**NB!** It is strongly recommended to apply for the right to study in the autumn 2022 application round (1.-15.9.2022), if an applicant does not yet have study rights.

**Please note: the application form asks for a mandatory attachment of an “Approval of the supervisors and the coordinating academic” (attachment 4). In the Doctoral Programme in School, Education, Society and Culture this attachment is not mandatory, but you have to attach something at this point. You can just upload an empty PDF-file instead of a statement of your supervisors and the coordinating academic.**

The evaluation process of the applications has two phases. The steering committee of the doctoral programme first shortlists applications, which will then be sent to three international outside evaluators for an external review.

The decisions will be announced latest on November 15, 2022.

You can find the application form during the open call period (29.8.-16.9.2022) on the [University of Helsinki Open Positions](https://jobs.helsinki.fi/job/Doctoral-Programme-in-School%2C-Education%2C-Society-and-Culture%2C-doctoral-researcher/753397502/) -webpages.
