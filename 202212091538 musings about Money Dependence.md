About what: [[Money Dependence 貨幣依存]]

Anyone with business-involved experience knows that when you're developing a business model, you're ultimately developing a way to create others' dependence on you/your business.

As long as people are dependent on money to make a living, 消費 is inevitable. As long as 消費 exists, the a) need to constantly generate/induce b) dependence and c) the supply to fulfill such dependence exist. 

a) Forces us to negate the possibility of self-sufficiency/self-reliance/self-dependence/self-completion 自給自足/自我完整. Thus all of us must be in a status of insufficiency/deficiency/inbalance for 消費 to work. We must be incomplete, or in other words, unhealthy (in a 身心靈 sense). 

b) 

c) Supply can be material or immaterial. In the case of material supply, the need to constantly generate material supply necessitates the exhaustion of resources; even in the case of circular economy, the law of entropy also guarentees loss in the resource circulation process. In the case of immaterial supply, money alienates the "personal," such as the "will" and "relationality" in such supply, translating it into an "impersonal," "indifferent" "buyer-seller relationship." Since the 
buyer-seller relationship renders the "buyer" and "seller" in unequal grounds of rights and obligations, the experience and co-generation of the "commons" and the daily act of mutual aid becomes difficult. 

While [[universal basic income]] may seem to be a viable solution against the problems of forced money-earning endeavors, a) people receiving the money are still dependent on the money-givers, thus the dependent/power-imbalanced structure doesn't change. Also, b) the money received has no substance (?) or substantial/intrinsic value -- It does not sustain people's livelihood per se, as it only serves as an intermediary to buy goods and services, thus not immune to inflations/deflations. 

The only solution I can see today is the synthetic return to agriculture (in the broad sense). In other words, disentagle people's survival with money. 

But the problem is, to achieve more, people might still hire others to farm for them; and with more and more life utilities (e.g., technology products) and personal agency in society still requiring money, the disparity of power still exist and may continue to grow, thus reproducing inequality. However, there might be solutions to ensure that poverty does not follow inequality, as long as the livelihood is self-sufficient and the access to basic infrastructures is free.
