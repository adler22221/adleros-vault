Ayache (2008) wrote an article, “I am a creator” (from the Coen brothers’ movie, Barton Fink). His
approach to the standard Black-Scholes-Merton model for pricing options a reflexive one. What matters
is to ==make the market while being in it==, to be a “dynamic trader”. You can be an original author and yet
be in-the-market. Market-makers are thinkers and creators. Because they make markets, they need to
produce prices as outputs of their models. But since the market is the outside they are reaching for, its
prices must be inputs to their models. A market-maker sets a price only if the market makes it.

Three stories shape money speculation: the economists’ claim that you can’t beat the markets; you can
with inside knowledge (which is illegal); and scientific methods guarantee steady profits from betting.
Poundstone shows that the rich rely on personal knowledge and contacts. Social science disciplines
assume that impersonal forces govern society. Middle-class parents shield their children from direct
experience of money (see below). But poor parents can’t afford to.

Samuelson introduced his textbook with a question. Ten million New Yorkers go to sleep every night,
confident that the economy will be there tomorrow. How do they know? J. K. Galbraith (1975) tells of a
member of Kennedy’s administration paid off with a bank directorship. After his first meeting, he was
seen staggering a little in the street, muttering “I never knew.” What had he not known? Galbraith
thinks that he learned the first principle of modern banking. ==Take money from one party and lend it to
another, then persuade both that they still have it.==

In Paul Baum’s The Wonderful Wizard of Oz (1900), money is a phantom conjured up by unscrupulous
wizards. Most of us would rather not know. We believe that we are standing on solid ground, that
money is real and durable. Or, reassured by their impenetrable jargon, ==we let experts handle the
problem.==^[According to Ilich, we're all "schooled" to let experts handle the problems. ] In either case, understanding is unnecessary. Inflation undermines us because, when money
values refuse to stand still, what else can we rely on? For all classes, fear of the unknown begets a
search for certainty in monetary affairs.

Most people cling to their own views about money, however uninformed. They won’t jeopardise
working for wages and pensions. Scientific betting and do-it-yourself trading circuits are the most
popular alternatives. ==Capitalists have large funds and make small bets often.== Most punters try to win a
lot with a little occasionally. So they lose, confirmed in the dogma that bookies and casinos always win.
