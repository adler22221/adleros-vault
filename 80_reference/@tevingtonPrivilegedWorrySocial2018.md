title: Privileged to Worry: Social Class, Cultural Knowledge, and Strategies toward the Future among Young Adults
authors: Patricia Tevington
year: 2018
abstract: How do differences in cultural capital operate in conditions of uncertainty? Interviews with 106 young adults of different social class backgrounds demonstrate significantly different knowledge of and attitudes about the Great Recession. Upper middle class youth are the most concerned about their long-term futures in light of the economic downturn and use distinctive strategies to combat these anticipated negative consequences. In contrast, working and middle-class youth are less concerned about the recession long term and more inclined to express hopeful and individualistic attitudes about their futures. This article demonstrates that threats to the reproduction of status bring about a disposition of anxiety among the most privileged. This discomfort is the result of greater levels of cultural knowledge. Importantly, it also leads to the activation of cultural capital to ensure the reproduction of their class standing.
