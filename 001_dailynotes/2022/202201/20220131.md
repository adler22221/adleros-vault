I realized that if I really decide to run a publishing business, there will be so many things for me to learn and do and that will greatly decrease my energy and focus to do other important things, especially my own academic publishing. 

So as I took a shower I deeply reflected on why do I insist so much on doing publishing on my own?

I think the main motivation is that: 
- there are so many important works out there yet they're unknown and unread by Taiwanese people because there isn't a Chinese version of them. 
- However, even with the opportunity to translate them, publishers won't publish them because it's not going to "sell."

and the secondary motivation is that:
- I want to build authority in specific fields
- I want to build deeper relationships (e.g., some sort of "spokesman") with the authors

If the necessary condition for publishing (or selling any product) is to provide irreplaceable value so we have a strong customer base, shouldn't that be the first thing to do?

Also, aren't there any alternative pathways to achieve the above goals?
--> blogging
--> vloging
--> podcasting

if these are the necessary conditions for publishing (or selling anything), but these actions in themselves can also achieve my primary goals of publishing, shouldn't these be my first steps?
