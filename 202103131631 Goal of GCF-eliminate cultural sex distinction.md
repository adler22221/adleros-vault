[[Pilgrim, David]]

Gender-critical feminists have a long-term goal of gender abolitionism in society. The
latter is implied by the eventual elimination of patriarchy as a primary feminist objective, typified by the claim from Firestone (1970) that the ‘ … end goal of feminist revolution must be, unlike that of the first feminist movement, not just the elimination of male privilege but of the sex distinction itself: ==genital differences between human beings would no longer matter culturally==’ (11, emphasis added). Thus, while ‘gender non-conformity’ is defended by all feminists, some of them consider that the ideology of transgenderism is not only misaligned with that fundamental goal, it may even undermine it.^[As it appeals to the reassignment of sex to resolve sex issues that arise from cultural factors, which at the same time affirms of the innate cultural properties of sex, also alleviates the demand to make sex culturally agnostic.]
