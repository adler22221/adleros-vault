## Defining Key Terms

- **Integration Expert(ise):** In the context of this workshop, an "integration expert" is a person who specializes in the process of bringing together different academic disciplines, methods, and stakeholders (like community members or policymakers) to address complex problems. Their expertise is not in a single subject, but in the skills of synthesis, facilitation, and creating a shared understanding across different groups. The workshop aims to find ways to formally recognize and reward this often invisible work.
    
- **The Landscape:** The agenda's reference to "the landscape of recognition" asks participants to describe the current situation in their professional environments. This involves mapping out the existing systems, challenges, and opportunities for valuing integration expertise. For example, are there formal job titles for this work, how is it considered in promotions, and what are the main obstacles to its recognition?

## Key Terms Explained

An **integration expert** specializes in bridging gaps between disciplines, stakeholders, and knowledge systems to tackle complex issues. This role involves skills like synthesis, facilitation, and co-creation rather than domain-specific knowledge. The workshop's "Value Project" seeks to elevate this often-overlooked expertise through recognition mechanisms, such as career credits or institutional policies.

The **landscape** in the agenda refers to the current ecosystem of recognition practices in your field or institution. It maps formal and informal ways integration work is valued—like in evaluations, funding, or promotions—along with barriers and gaps. Participants will share their views to identify patterns and opportunities for change.
