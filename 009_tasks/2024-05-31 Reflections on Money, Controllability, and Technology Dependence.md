further reflections on the question about why some people gets paid more while others paid less, which was raised during the [[2024-05-30 中平國中紀錄片映後座談]]. 

technology has an advantage, as the development and deployment of technology are, ultimately, artificial transformations of our habitat.
to survive, we need to constantly adapt to our habitat, which includes adapting to artificial ones. 
But, given the human's limited biological capacity, external tools are almost always needed (unless we create/opt-in for alternative, less artificial habitats).
So technology changes our habitat that increases survival pressure to us, which pushes us to equip ourselves with more technologies to help us adapt to the changed habitat. However, the technologies we adopt and our relations with them, again, formulate new habitat that we live in. 
The result of this is an [[acceleration]] (cf. [[Hartmut, Rosa]]) of our living tempo, increasing dependence on technology. And such dependence on technology is intertwined with our dependence on money. 
The production of technology requires work -- whether from [[labor]] or from [[automation]], and such work has a cost. [[capitalism]] has been the most successful system in facilitating the development, production, and deployment of (interventionist? Promethean?) technology. From the customer's perspective, the purchase of technological goods and services through money, has an advantage in efficiency. 
When Pre-modern non-Western ordinary people follow guides to slowly make their own watermills and other technologies, modern people enjoys the utility of technology just in a click of purchase -- the making of the technology is outsourced to companies and their labors/robots/codes by paying them. 
Coming back to our adaptation to a technology-induced change in our habitat -- Those who have the money to purchase, or skill to develop new technologies for our adaptation, have an edge in thriving in the new habitat -- Those who have the skills can be paid by those who have money to develop/produce/deploy new technologies, and with that income, increase the money they have. Whereas those who don't have the money or skill for new technologies for our adaptation, tend to be disadvantaged and fall back into economic disadvantageous situations. 
[[essential workers]] help us as individuals and the societies as a whole to maintain its basic survival or operation. Those in the applied sciences and technology develop/produce/deploy new technologies that aid our adaptation to new habitats. 
Problem of monoculture: We need essential workers (be them human or robots)
