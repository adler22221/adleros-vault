About the event: National Security Collective Intelligence conference

In April-May 2022, the UK government’s Deputy Chief Scientific Advisor for National Security commissioned Policy Lab, the government's policy innovation team, to run a Collective Intelligence debate to determine how UK civil servants, subject matter experts, and members of the public view issues of National Security and foreign policy.

The debate used Pol.is to involve experts and the public. This conference is designed to create a space for the rest of the UK government to understand the potential for using these methods to change the way they make policy. 

Given your experience and subject matter expertise in the field of digital democracy, Policy Lab and Demos would be delighted if you were able to join us as a speaker at this event, which will host around 150 foreign embassy staff, other collective intelligence subject matter experts, and government officials.

This particular National Security case study builds on an initial experiment that demonstrated value in the Pol.is approach. Run in early 2021, this initial experiment was a collaboration between Policy Lab, Demos, and the National Security Secretariat (UK Cabinet Office), focusing on policy issues related to the Integrated Review of Security, Defence, Development and Foreign Policy. Since then, Policy Lab has developed its own Pol.is capability, and Demos has pioneered its use in research projects covering a range of topics, from the impact of digital technology on society, to the role of a ‘civic university’ within a region and to issues around privacy and safety online.
