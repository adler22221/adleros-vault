About what: 

Money allows us to move fluently between our intimate lives and more inclusive forms of society. To
focus on how a household controls its money is to stay in the everyday world. We need ways of
reaching parts of the economy we don’t know. Money separates economic spheres and fragments
human experience. It also joins together what it has divided. Money is central to both home and work.
Markets are not just about abstraction. Abstract and concrete interact there. If you have some money,
there is no limit to what you can do with it. As soon as you buy something, payment lends finality to
your choice. Money’s significance lies in its synthesis of impersonal abstraction and personal meaning,
objectification and subjectivity, analytical reason and synthetic narrative. Its social power comes from
mediating infinite potential and finite determination.

Money is linked to democracy because its impersonality dissolves differences between people. We vote
with our money whenever we buy something.^[[[202104291530 money, democracy, community]]] Thinking of money as a durable ground to stand on may
anchor identity in collective memory. It also helps us to generate the personal credit linking us to
society. Money is an aspect of our personality, like language. It allows us to widen our horizons. To be
human puts us in the widest circles of humanity, world society. Money opens up local societies to
interdependence with foreigners. But the pressure to assert local control persists. ==A human economy
must seek to build bridges between different levels of association. Money’s social value lies not in
separating local and global spheres, but in moving between them. Money reflects our universal
humanity while anchoring us in the everyday. All markets are world markets, but not all money is world
money.==

We tend to think of money as one thing. We don’t know that this situation is recent. Money has taken
many historical forms. Several of them have usually circulated together in a territory. Now a world of
plural currencies has returned. We can join monetary experiments at all social levels. We could learn
how to make more egalitarian societies.

A human economy mediates the two pairs that define national capitalism – state and market, home and
world. Today’s crisis is the collapse of that system. Instead of contrasting its poles, we should synthesize
them.^[[[202104291522 Money’s power of synthesis]]] Always keep in mind what people do and want. We should not reject machines and money, but
develop ourselves through them. Money is a great equalizer, but it also fuels inequality. As memory,
money links individuals to their communities. We can join together past, present and future; fact and
fiction; local and global. We should not perch on one pole, but learn to combine them in society.
Exchange of meanings through language and of goods and services through money are now converging
on the internet. The digital revolution could advance the human conversation about a better world.
Approaching money with open minds and social purposes is the strategic way to learn how to be more
fully human.
