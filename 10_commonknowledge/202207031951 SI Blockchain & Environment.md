Blockchain & Environment 區塊鏈與環境

Typically when talking about applications of blockchain people start by talking about currencies, finance and then more general business applications such as supply chain, then possibly learn about its potential use for the Internet of Things. What is rarely talked about is its applications to the environment, but as we will try to illustrate in this video this could turn out to be one of its most important potentials. 

通常情況下，當談論區塊鏈的應用時，人們首先談論的是貨幣、金融，然後是更普遍的商業應用，如供應鏈，然後可能了解其在物聯網方面的潛在用途。很少有人談到它在環境方面的應用，但我們將在本影片試圖說明，環境應用可能成為區塊鏈最重要的潛在發展方向。

To understand why we need to appreciate that the reason we are in an environmental crisis is largely a function of the design of our economic system. We developed our modern economic framework in a world that was industrializing with abundant natural resources and limited industrial infrastructure. The system was set up to grow GDP by producing and consuming more products with limited interest to the effects this had on the environment. As a consequence natural capital was not accounted for in the system creating a strong incentive for people to simply overuse natural resources, converting what was not accounted for into increase GDP and profits. 

為了理解這個原因，我們需要明白，我們處於環境危機的原因主要來自我們的經濟體系設計的方式。現代經濟框架來自剛開始工業化的時代，當時世界仍有豐富的自然資源和有限的工業基礎設施。這個體系的建立是為了通過生產和消費更多產品來增加GDP，不太關心這一過程對環境的影響。因此，自然資本在這個系統中沒有被計算在內，這就產生了一個強烈的動機，使人們簡單地過度使用自然資源，將沒有被計算在內的東西轉化為增加的GDP和利潤。

Today, of course, we realize that this whole linear economic framework is unsustainable and one of the most effective ways to alter the model is to try and incorporate the costs and benefits that accrue from ecosystems into economic accounting. This means essentially placing a value on the ecosystems so that people factor the full environmental cost of their actions into the cost-benefit equation under which they are acting, this is called full cost accounting or natural capital accounting. The idea is not new, indeed it is becoming ever more mainstream, in a recent Ernst and Young report entitled, “Accounting for Natural Capital — the elephant in the boardroom”. The executive summary starts by saying, “Natural capital will become as prominent a business concern in the 21st Century as the provision of adequate financial capital was in the 20th Century… We are already ‘drawing down’ on 50% more natural capital a year than the earth can replenish — and the rate of depletion is accelerating. All too soon, businesses will face a stark choice: adapt or fail.” 

當然，如今我們已意識到，整個線性經濟框架是不可持續的，改變這一模式的最有效方法之一是嘗試將生態系統的成本和效益納入經濟核算。這意味著從本質上為生態系統設定價值，以便人們將其行為的全部環境成本納入其行為的成本效益計算中，這被稱為「全部成本會計」或「自然資本會計」。這個想法並不新鮮，事實上，它正變得越來越主流。在安永會計師事務所最近一份題為「自然資本會計——會議室裡的大象」的報告中，執行摘要一開始就說：「在21世紀，自然資本將成為一個突出的商業問題，就像在20世紀金融資本的充分提供一樣......我們每年『消耗』的自然資本已經超過了地球能夠補充的50%，而且消耗的速度正在加快。很快，企業將面臨一個嚴峻的選擇：『適應或失敗』。」

This powerful capacity of blockchain token economies to design new incentive structures has the potential to fundamentally alter the current dynamic. When designed properly token economies can provide powerful incentive structures for people to collaborate on growing some underlying source of value to the community. This same capacity can be harnessed to develop and grow the natural capital of a community to build their ecosystem. 

區塊鏈代幣經濟得以設計新獎勵結構的強大能力，有可能從根本上改變目前的動態。如果設計得當，代幣經濟可以為人們提供強大的獎勵結構，使他們在社群價值的來源上進行合作。這種能力也可以被用來發展和增長社群的自然資本，以建立他們的生態系統。

Unlike our traditional industrial age economic system that simply quantifies and incentivizes people to consume products, by creating token systems we can actually incentivize and reward people for any action that is of benefit to growing ecological resources. Tokens can be gained by choosing less consumptive options that conserve and replenish resources. 

與我們傳統的工業時代，簡單地量化和激勵人們消費產品的經濟體系不同，通過創建代幣系統，我們實際上可以誘導和獎勵人們任何有利於增長生態資源的行動。代幣可以通過選擇保護和補充資源的低消耗方案而獲得。

For example, think about a small town in rural Australia that has a limited amount of water to go round, a finite amount of water tokens could be issued and earnt for any activity that conserves on water. For example, if you replace your plumbing with a system that leeks less you would earn water tokens that could be used elsewhere or traded for fiat currency, meaning that people can earn by conserving creating an economy around it and an incentive system. ^[Would it be possible that some day there would be a poverty in water tokens, as some are still more capable of centralizing capital?]

例如，想像澳大利亞農村的一個小鎮，它的水是有限的，可以發行有限數量的水代幣，並為任何節約用水的活動賺取代幣。例如，如果你把你的水管換成了一個可以減少漏水的系統，就可以獲得水代幣。這些代幣可以在其他地方使用，也可以用法幣進行交易，這意味著人們可以通過節約用水來獲得收益，從而形成一個經濟和激勵系統。

Or with respect to carbon emissions a token economy could likewise be created to manage this in a distributed fashion, every time you emit carbon you pay for it with a kind of carbon coin. Every time you sequester carbon, you get paid in carbon coins. When all carbon coins have been redeemed, the world is running at net zero carbon. 

或者在碳排放方面，同樣可以創建一個代幣經濟，以分散式的方式管理這個問題：每次你排放碳，你就用一種碳幣來支付。每當你封存碳時，你就會得到碳幣的報酬。當所有的碳幣都被贖回時，世界將以淨零碳運行。

Likewise, any community could take account of its stock of natural capital and issue a finite amount of tokens to the community members, if someone does something that is ecologically beneficial to grow that underlying stock they could earn more tokens. Likewise, if someone wanted to chop down lots of trees they would have to purchase tokens from others, this would incentivize people to go and plant trees or perform other activities that contribute to their ecosystem in order to earn tokens. In such a way ecological services would not have to be managed and performed by a central authority as anyone could clean the lake or switch to a renewable energy to earn carbon tokens and thus provide environmental services. 

同樣，任何社群都可以考慮其自然資本的存量，並向社群成員發行有限數量的代幣，如果有人做了對生態有益的事情來增加基礎存量，他們可以獲得更多的代幣。同樣，如果有人想砍伐大量的樹木，他們將不得不從其他人那裡購買代幣，這將激勵人們去植樹或進行其他有助於生態系統的活動，以獲得代幣。這樣一來，生態服務就不需要由中央機構來管理和執行，因為任何人都可以清理湖泊或改用可再生能源來賺取碳代幣，從而提供環境服務。^[some are still capable of providing environmental services than others --> they get more carbon tokens than others]

Of course closed organizations have always seen the benefit of conserving in their production because it directly affects their balance sheets, but what is happening with token economies though is that we can push that capacity to benefit from conserving out to the end user and the edges of the network so that we can harness everyone's incentives to conserve, which is potentially a much more powerful mechanism than centralized organizations simply telling people to be nice and environmentally friendly which doesn't really work because it lacks real incentives. 

當然，封閉式組織一直都有看到在生產中保護環境的好處，因為這直接影響到他們的資產負債表，但代幣經濟所促成的是，將這種從保護環境中獲益的能力推向終端用戶和網路邊緣，這樣我們就可以利用每個人的誘因來保護環境，這可能是一個更強大的機制，而不是中心化組織簡單地告訴人們要善良和環保，因為它缺乏真正的誘因。

Of course, the move towards a full cost economy will not happen overnight, already it has been growing over the past decades with such things as organic food, green bonds, carbon trading etc. Incentivised recycling systems are already made possible on the blockchain via the Ethereum based token-based Dapp RecycleToCoin. The token platform allows the Public to exchange harmful plastic at their reverse vending machines and central collection charity shops for tokens. This cryptographic reward is then exchangeable on participating crypto exchanges for other currencies. 

向全成本經濟的轉變雖然不會在一夜之間發生，在過去的幾十年裡，它已經隨著有機食品、綠色債券、碳交易等事情而不斷增長。通過基於以太坊的代幣Dapp RecycleToCoin，激勵性的回收系統已經在區塊鏈上成為可能。該代幣平台允許公眾在他們的反向售貨機和中央收集慈善商店將有害塑料換成代幣。然後，這種加密獎勵可以在參與的加密貨幣交易所兌換成其他貨幣。

The recognition and increasing quantification of ecosystems services coupled with the development of a fully fledged blockchain natural capital accounting system could support a fundamental reorganizing of the incentive structures in our economy towards one that is systematically more sustainable. 

對生態系統服務的認可和日益量化，再加上一個成熟的區塊鏈自然資本會計系統的發展，可以支持我們經濟中的誘因結構進行根本性的重組，以實現系統性的更永續的發展。

As Prince Charles noted "The ultimate bank on which we all depend — the bank of natural capital — is in the red; the debt is getting ever bigger and that is reducing Nature’s resilience and considerably impeding her ability to re-stock. It leaves us dangerously exposed.” 

Fixing that equation requires a new accounting system and the blockchain could be a critical enabling technology.

  

正如查爾斯王子所指出的，「我們所依賴的最終銀行--自然資本銀行--處於虧損狀態；債務越來越大，降低了大自然的複原力，大大阻礙了她重新儲存的能力。這使我們處於危險的境地。」

修復這個等式需要一個新的會計系統，而區塊鏈可能是一個關鍵的促成技術。

  
  

Supply Chains 供應鏈

The blockchain would not only be important in enabling a token economy for managing ecosystem capital but also as a system for tracking, authenticating and making transparent the underlining workings of supply chains and business activity. 

  

區塊鏈不僅在實現管理生態系統資本的代幣經濟方面很重要，而且還可以作為一個追蹤、驗證和透明化供應鏈和商業活動幕後運作的系統。

At present when we go buy something, we have little idea where it comes from or how it's made. Supply chains are conventionally held secret, limiting the stakeholders who can prevent environmental, social and health and safety problems. There are so many intermediaries, and it's very easy to be deceitful. Companies can perform all sorts of actions without it being revealed to the end user who gets greenwashed by massive amounts of advertising so that they don't know what to think when they walk down the supermarket aisle. 

目前，當我們去買東西時，我們幾乎不知道它來自哪裡或如何製造。供應鏈傳統上是保密的，限制了能夠防止環境、社會、健康和安全問題的利益相關者。有這麼多的中間商，而且很容易被欺騙。公司可以採取各種行動，而不向終端用戶透露，使他們被大量的廣告洗腦，以至於當他們走在超市時不知道該怎麼想。

Blockchain could enable environmental transparency along the supply chain of products. A blockchain could ensure that a grouper fish being tossed through Tokyo's Tsukiji Market actually came from a sustainable fisherman in Indonesia or to track the price that villages in Kenya were paid for their coffee beans. The Provenance blockchain has been used to verify proof of payment to 55 farmers whilst tracking coconuts from South East Asia to Europe. The initiative has demonstrated the possibility of using blockchain technology to track ethical claims and digitally prove fair trading practices. Working with NGO Fairfood, Provenance tracked ethics claims on 1000 coconuts. Using a grassroots certification approach, they tracked the moment of payment one way and product the other way, registering the harvest via SMS, and verifying chain of custody along the supply chain. 

區塊鏈可以實現產品供應鏈上的環境透明度。例如，區塊鏈可以確保在東京築地市場拋出的石斑魚實際上來自印尼的永續漁民，或者追蹤肯亞村莊為其咖啡豆支付的價格。Provenance區塊鏈已經被用來驗證對55個農民的付款證明，同時追蹤從東南亞到歐洲的椰子。這一舉措證明了使用區塊鏈技術來檢核道德主張和數位認證公平貿易做法的可能性。與非政府組織Fairfood合作，Provenance追蹤了1000個椰子的道德主張。使用基層認證方法，他們一方面追蹤付款時刻，另方面追蹤產品，通過簡訊登記收穫情況，並核實供應鏈上的監管鏈。

As discussed previously the blockchain is not magic, it can only authenticate what is within the network, in order to ensure that the information that is inputted into the system is authentic and correct it needs to work in tandem with other technologies. Here again, the blockchain will have to intersect with IoT systems and big data advanced analytics if we are going to be able to automatically ensure that the fish was fished at the date and location inputted to the network. Data automatically inputted from tamper-proof IoT systems and cross-correlated with data points from a multiplicity of other sources could work to create truly trustworthy inputs and reliable information to someone on the other end of the supply chain automatically without human intervention via the distributed database. For example, an IoT device in a warehouse might scan the RFID tags on pallets when they come in to verify the date of entry. 

如前所述，區塊鏈不是魔術，它只能驗證網路中的內容，為了確保輸入系統的信息是真實和正確的，它需要與其他技術協同工作。如果我們要能夠自動確保魚是在輸入網路的日期和地點捕撈的，區塊鏈將不得不與物聯網系統和大數據進階分析交互使用。從防竄改的物聯網系統中自動輸入的數據，並與來自其他多種來源的數據點交叉關聯，可以通過分散式資料庫自動建立真正可信的輸入和可靠的信息給供應鏈的另一端的人，而不需要人工干預。例如，倉庫中的物聯網設備可以在托盤進入時掃描其上的RFID標籤，以驗證進入日期。

This is one of the advantages of big data, it is also decentralized not relying on a single data source, but multiple varied sources cross-correlated. The blockchain itself can't provide a trustworthy supply chain it will have to be integrated with these other technologies to enable trusted, automatic and distributed oracles for data input that are not dependent upon corruptible third-party institutions. This is of course particularly relevant in parts of the world where government organizations are not trustworthy. Knowing that the apple you bought has been certified organic by a country at the bottom of the list of corrupt nations doesn't really tell you very much.

這是大數據的優勢之一，它也是分散的，不依賴單一數據源，而是多個不同的數據源交叉關聯。區塊鏈本身不能提供一個值得信賴的供應鏈，它必須與這些其他技術整合，以實現可信的、自動的和分散式的神諭，使數據輸入不依賴於可能腐敗的第三方機構。當然，這與世界上政府組織不值得信任的地區尤其相關。如果你買的蘋果由腐敗程度高的國家認證為有機產品，其實並不能說明什麼。

  

Distributed Systems 分散式系統

On a broader level, we can identify the centralization of many existing systems as a huge source of vulnerability in a world of climate and environmental change. In a world of stability centralization appeared to create efficiencies and stability, but today we are increasingly recognizing these large centralized components create critically vulnerable single points of failure with huge dependencies. Large centralized components like large financial institutions, electrical power stations, or factories also create inertia within the system and reduce adaptive capacity and the agility required to deal with systemic changes. 

  

更廣泛而言，我們可以肯定地說，現今許多中心化系統是氣候和環境變遷下，世界巨大的脆弱性來源。在一個穩定的世界裡，中心化似乎創造了效率和穩定性，但今日我們越來越認識到這些大型的中心化組件創造了極其脆弱的單點故障和巨大的依賴性。像大型金融機構、發電站或工廠這樣的大型中心化組件也在系統內產生了慣性，並降低了應對系統性變化所需的適應能力和敏捷性。

By supporting peer-to-peer distributed systems the blockchain can enable greatly more agile, adaptive and potentially more resilient networks with lower dependencies on critical centralized elements. Removing the need for the centralization of resources can also greatly increase the efficiency of these networks as resources can be exchange locally peer to peer without having to be always routed to a centralized component. Blockchain platforms can enable local food systems, where the food is grown locally disintermediating the long complicated supply chains of supermarkets, by matching producers and consumers peer-to-peer locally. 

通過支持分散式對等系統，區塊鏈可以使網路的靈活性、適應性和潛在的彈性大大增強，對關鍵的中心化元素的依賴性降低。消除對資源集中化的需求也可以大大提高這些網路的效率，因為資源可以在本地對等式交換，而不必總是被送到一個集中化的組件。區塊鏈平台可以實現當地的食品系統，通過在當地匹配生產者和消費者，使食品在當地種植，而不是通過超市的冗長複雜的供應鏈。

Or as another example blockchain based Power Ledge provides a platform for people to trade energy peer to peer within a local smart grid. With this people simply trade electricity with one another and receive payment in real-time from an automated and trustless reconciliation and settlement system. There are many other immediate benefits such as being able to select a clean energy source, trade with neighbors, receive more money for excess power, benefit from the transparency of all trades on a blockchain with very low-cost settlement costs. Likewise, power ledger supports carbon trading via smart contracts.

基於區塊鏈的Power Ledge為人們提供了一個平台，在當地智能電網中進行對等式能源交易。有了這個平台，人們只需相互交易電力，並從一個自動和無信任的對賬和結算系統中實時收到付款。還有許多其他直接的好處，例如能夠選擇清潔能源，與鄰居交易，為多餘的電力收到更多的錢，從區塊鏈上所有交易的透明度中受益，結算成本非常低。同樣，power ledger也支持通過智能合約進行碳交易。
