In the Research for Impact approach, research impact is more than dissemination; it is the good that researchers and the people we work with can do in the world, the benefits that arise from both the process and outcomes of our research.

Research impact is often thought of as ‘beneficial change’, but for us to claim any responsibility for that change those benefits need to be clearly linked to our research processes and/or outcomes. Demonstrating and attributing impact is increasingly becoming an important and established part of the research process. To demonstrate research impact there are two things we need to do; i) show there has been an impact, and ii) show that our research made a ‘contribution’ toward achieving that impact.

The quality of the impact we make is also important, we need to think about the significance and reach of the impact we make; how meaningful, valuable or beneficial is the impact to the people we’re working with? And can or has that impact been scaled out to other people or groups that might benefit from similar change?

## Types of impact

There are many types of impact, and ‘spaces’ that we can change. The list below covers the main areas where we can make an impact, but it is not always easy to define impact, and it is rare that an impact is confined to a single type or space. The key to achieving impact is understanding the nature of impact, and planning what impact you want to see at the beginning of your research process.

-   **Understanding and Awareness** \- working with people to get a better understanding of an issue or problem.
-   **Attitudinal and Cultura**l - a change in beliefs, attitudes, values, discourse and patterns of behaviour that improves lives.
-   **Economic** \- money saved, costs reduced, spending avoided and increases in profit, turnover, funding or benefits.
-   **Environmental** - benefits to genetic diversity, conservation, ecosystems, and the benefits we get from a healthy environment.
-   **Health and Wellbeing** \- improved public health and/or better health outcomes for people we work with.
-   **Policy** \- impact and/or influence we can have on new or amended laws, public policy and governmental practice.
-   **Capacity or Preparedness** - new or more developed capacity and resilience that will help people be more prepared and better able to cope with changes that will impact them negatively.
