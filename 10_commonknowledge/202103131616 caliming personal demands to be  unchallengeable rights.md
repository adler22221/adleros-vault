One mixed blessing of the emergence of human language is that the discourse about
gender means we can now talk ourselves into being a woman, when we are in fact a man (or vice versa). Beyond its use in the study of grammar, the word ‘gender’ was simply missing, until the second half of the twentieth century, when it emerged as an ambiguous or even *misleading proxy* for ‘sex’ in social science (Money 1955)^[Would like to look into this as I feel the same.]. It has now acquired a rhetorical or ideological status in identity politics, despite second wave feminists cautioning against the conflation of sex and gender, with the first being a factual biological regularity and the second a flexible socially-negotiated state.

If we push the rhetoric really hard we can even demand that others must accept all the conduct implied in its wake. ==We can also demand medical cooperation to interfere in our *healthy bodies* to resemble the opposite sex.== These demands can then be elevated into a civil right, which others *must not query*^[The unchallengeable status of certain ideas is terrifying, as it resembles the authoritarianism/tyranny of thought/mind?]. Variations in how we experience ourselves as human beings can be converted into entitlements and so biology becomes axiology.

Money, John. 1955. “Hermaphroditism, Gender and Precocity in Hyperadrenocorticism: Psychologic Findings.” Bulletin of the Johns Hopkins Hospital 96 (6): 253
