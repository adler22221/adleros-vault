Moreover, child referrals to gender-identity clinics have increased and the preponderant ratio of self-identification is now reversing from male-to-female in the past, to femaleto-male in the present. For example, at the Tavistock Clinic in London last year 72% of referred children were girls. The most parsimonious explanation for this trend is faddish social contagion in the young (medicalized tautologically as ‘rapid onset gender dysphoria’), rather than the mysteries of placental sex hormones allegedly creating a rare morbid condition in the foetus. If the latter was plausible then we would expect a routine low incidence, and stable sex-ratio, of clinical presentations of gender dysphoria. Instead, what we are witnessing are rising levels of referral and fluxing patterns in relation to the sex of patients.
